'''Paramaribo''' is a district of [[Suriname]], coextensive with the capital city of [[Paramaribo]].

Paramaribo district has a [[population]] of 240,924, almost half the population of the entire country, and an [[area]] of 182&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.statistics-suriname.org/index.php/statistieken/downloads/category/8-censusstatistieken-2004 | title=Census profile at District level 2004 (in Dutch) | publisher=Stichting Algemeen Bureau voor de Statistiek in Suriname | access-date=2013-04-24 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131113144128/http://www.statistics-suriname.org/index.php/statistieken/downloads/category/8-censusstatistieken-2004 | archive-date=2013-11-13 | url-status=dead }}</ref>

The area was first [[colonisation|colonised]] by the [[United Kingdom|British]] in the 17th century with the construction of [[Fort Willoughby]]. This fort was later taken by the [[Netherlands]] and renamed [[Fort Zeelandia (Paramaribo)|Fort Zeelandia]]. The area, and the city of Paramaribo, switched between Dutch and British control until the [[Treaty of Breda (1667)|Treaty of Breda]] at the end of the [[Second Anglo-Dutch War]] ceded all of Suriname to the Dutch.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.suriname.nu/301ges/aparbo01.html|title=Paramaribo Suriname 1|access-date=29 May 2020|language=nl|website=Suriname.nu}}</ref>
