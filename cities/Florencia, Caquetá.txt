'''Florencia''' ({{IPA|es|floˈɾensja}}, {{langx|en|Florence}}) is a [[Municipalities of Colombia|municipality]] and the capital city of the [[Departments of Colombia|Department]] of [[Caquetá Department|Caquetá]], [[Colombia]]. It is  the most populous city in the [[Amazon Region of Colombia]]. It lies on the [[Orteguaza River]] which flows into the [[Caqueta River]]. Its population is 177,946 in 2023.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Telefónicas |first=Telencuestas-Encuestas |title=Cuántos habitantes tiene Florencia, Caquetá en 2023 |url=https://telencuestas.com/censos-de-poblacion/colombia/2023/caqueta/florencia |access-date=2023-10-28 |website=Telencuestas |language=es}}</ref><ref>https://www.dane.gov.co/files/varios/informacion-capital-DANE-2019.pdf {{Bare URL PDF|date=March 2022}}</ref>

== Etymology ==
The name of the city pays homage to an Italian businessman dedicated to the rubber trade, Paolo Ricci, from the city of [[Florence]].<ref>{{Cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=b9hRAQAAMAAJ&q=Paolo+Ricci+Florencia+Caquet%C3%A1 |title=Caquetá: características geográficas |date=2010 |publisher=Instituto Geográfico Agustín Codazzi, Subdirección de Geografía y Cartogafia |isbn=978-958-8323-37-4 |language=es}}</ref>
[[File:Plaza de Toros Santo Domingo.JPG|thumb|Santo Domingo bullring]]

==History==
The area where Florencia is located was inhabited by tribes of the [[Andaquí people|Andaquí]] ethnic group.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Bustos |first=Mauricio |title=La Red Cultural del Banco de la República |url=https://www.banrepcultural.org/ |access-date=2022-07-31 |website=Banrepcultural |language=spanish}}</ref> The territory where the city is located was also inhabited by [[Witoto]] and {{Ill|Coreguaje people|lt=Coreguaje|es|Coreguaje}} tribes. In 1542, the territory was explored for the first time by the Spanish conquistador, [[Hernán Pérez de Quesada]].<ref>{{Cite book |last= |first= |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=3pwxDwAAQBAJ&dq=Paolo+Ricci+Florencia+Caquet%C3%A1&pg=PT382 |title=Diez festivales en Colombia: Valores e impacto |date=2013-02-22 |publisher=Edtitorial Pontificia Universidad Javeriana |isbn=978-958-716-785-6 |language=es}}</ref> Florencia was founded on December 25, 1902 by Capuchin Friar Doroteo De Pupiales (1876-1959) Born in [[Pupiales]], [[Nariño]], [[Colombia]]. Rubber plantations around several areas of what is now known as the department of Caqueta encouraged the creation of the city next to the {{Ill|Hacha River|lt=Hacha River|es|Río Hacha}}.<ref name=":0" /> The city became an important site after the creation of the trail between the settlement and the town of [[Guadalupe, Huila|Guadalupe]] in what is now the department of Huila.<ref name=":0">{{Cite book |last1=Niño |first1=Oscar Arcila |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=LAlJAwAAQBAJ&dq=Fundacion+de+Florencia%2C+Caqueta&pg=PA36 |title=Caquetá, Construcción de un territorio amazónico en el siglo XX |last2=León |first2=Gloria González |last3=Rey |first3=Franz Gutiérrez |last4=Salazar |first4=Adriana Rodríguez |last5=Salazar |first5=Carlos Ariel |date=2002-04-02 |publisher=Instituto Amazónico de Investigaciones Científicas "SINCHI" |isbn=978-958-96878-1-9 |language=es}}</ref>

==Geography==
Florencia is located at the foot of the [[Cordillera Oriental (Colombia)|Cordillera Oriental mountain range]]. The municipality of Florencia limits to the north with the [[Huila Department]] and the municipality of [[El Paujil]], to the east with the municipalities of El Paujil and [[La Montañita]], to the south with the municipalities of [[Milán, Caquetá|Milán]] and [[Morelia, Caquetá|Morelia]], and to the west with the municipality of [[Belén de Andaquies]] and the Huila Department.

==Climate==
Florencia has a [[tropical rainforest climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification|Köppen]] ''Af''), and experiences significant rainfall during the course of the year. There is still a large amount of rainfall, even in the driest month. The average temperature in Florencia is {{convert|26.1|°C|1|disp=or}}. In Florencia, January is the driest month, while June tends to be the wettest month. About {{convert|3840|mm|in|-1|disp=or}} of rain falls annually during the year.

== Economy ==
According to a report of the first half of 2022 by the [[Ministry of Commerce, Industry and Tourism (Colombia)|MinCIT]], the main economic activities of the city were Trade and repair of vehicles with 22.6%, followed by Public Administration and Defense with 18.8% and third, Artistic Activities with 10.9%.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022 |title=Perfiles Económicos Departamentales - Caquetá |url=https://www.mincit.gov.co/CMSPages/GetFile.aspx?guid=5f834ed5-8943-4317-897d-9b047844ceb2 |access-date=2022-08-02 |website=Colombian Ministry of Commerce |language=es}}</ref>

== Heritage Sites ==

* '''El Encanto Petroglyphs''': These petroglyphs are located to the left of the Hacha River near Barrio Torasso. The engravings are approximately 16 meters long.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Arqueología - Caquetá |url=https://www.sinic.gov.co/SINIC/ColombiaCultural/ColCulturalBusca.aspx?AREID=3&SECID=8&IdDep=18&COLTEM=211 |access-date=2022-07-31 |website=Sistema Nacional de Información Cultural |language=es}}</ref> They were first described by archaeologist Eliécer Silva Celis in 1963. Among the petroglyphs are Zoomorphic and Human figures.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Lizarazo |first=María |date=2022-05-10 |title=El arte rupestre de la Amazonia que está en riesgo de perderse |url=https://www.elespectador.com/ambiente/amazonas/el-arte-rupestre-de-la-amazonia-que-esta-en-riesgo-de-perderse/ |access-date=2022-07-31 |website=[[El Espectador]] |language=spanish}}</ref>
* '''Curiplaya Building''': A historic building of colonial architecture built in the 1950s.  This building served as a hotel, currently it houses some cultural entities of the municipality and the department.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Guía departamental del Caquetá (CITUR) |url=https://www.citur.gov.co/upload/publications/documentos/37.Guias_departamentales_Caqueta.pdf |access-date=2022-08-05 |website=Centro de Información Turística de Colombia |page=42 |language=es}}</ref> In addition it is the headquarters of the Palace of Culture and Fine Arts of the Amazon (Spanish: ''Palacio de la Cultura y Bellas Artes de la Amazonía'').<ref name=":1">{{Cite web |last=Arias |first=Gloria |date=2016-07-27 |title=Conozca las maravillas naturales del Caquetá |url=https://www.eltiempo.com/archivo/documento/CMS-16655898 |access-date=2022-08-05 |website=[[El Tiempo (Colombia)|El Tiempo]] |language=spanish}}</ref> The building was declared a national cultural property in 2000 by the [[Ministry of Culture (Colombia)|Colombian Ministry of Culture]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2020 |title=Lista de Bienes declarados Bien de Interés Cultural de Ámbito Nacional |trans-title=List of Properties declared of Cultural Interest at the National Level |url=https://www.mincultura.gov.co/prensa/noticias/Documents/Patrimonio/BIENES%20DE%20INTER%C3%89S%20CULTURAL%20DEL%20%C3%81MBITO%20NACIONAL_mayo%202020.pdf |access-date=2022-08-05 |website=[[Ministry of Culture (Colombia)|Colombia Ministry of Culture]]}}</ref> The name "Curiplaya" means "Golden Beach" in the [[Witoto language]].<ref name=":1" />
