'''Jalna''' {{audio|Jalna.ogg|pronunciation}} is a city in [[Jalna district]] in the [[Aurangabad Division]], or [[Marathwada]] region, of the [[Indian state]] of [[Maharashtra]].<ref name="census" /> It was part of [[Hyderabad State]] as a [[tehsil]] of [[Aurangabad district, Maharashtra|Aurangabad district]], before Jalna district was formed effective 1 May 1981.<ref>[https://www.newsclick.in/maharashtras-jalna-steel-industry-trapped-overall-market-slowdown Maharashtra’s Jalna is one of the biggest hubs of steel industry in Central India.]</ref>

Jalna become Maharashtra's 29th Municipal Corporation.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Jalna, steel and mosambi hub, becomes Maha's 29th Municipal Corporation |url=https://www.daijiworld.com/news/newsDisplay?newsID=1078448 |access-date=2023-08-15 |website=daijiworld.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-05-17 |title=Maha: Jalna municipal council to become 29th corporation |url=https://urbanupdate.in/maha-jalna-municipal-council-to-become-29th-corporation/ |access-date=2023-08-15 |website=Urban Update}}</ref>

== History ==
Jalna (formerly Jalnapur) is one example. Like [[Aurangabad]]'s predecessor Khirki, prior to the [[Mughal Empire|Mughal]] conquests Jalna had been ruled by the [[Ahmadnagar Sultanate|Nizam Shahs]] of Ahmadnagar, during whose reign their governor Jamshid Khan built a mosque [[Kali Masjid, Jalna|Kali Masjid]], travellers' sara’i, and reservoir in around 1557.<ref name="Nile Green">{{Cite book |title=Making Space: Sufis and Settlers in Early Modern India |publisher=Nile Green |year=2012 |isbn=9780198077961 |location=New Delhi, India |page=154}}</ref> The town was
taken by the Mughals during the reign of [[Akbar]], and during the reigns of
[[Aurangzeb]] and [[Nizam-ul-Mulk, Asaf Jah I|Nizam al-Mulk]] it saw numerous improvements to its civic and military infrastructure, its fort ([[Jalna Fort]] or Mastgad) being constructed in 1725 in response to Maratha incursions.<ref name="Nile Green"/>
Jalna is home to many religions and people from different backgrounds. One of the main religions is Jainism, as there are many Jain temples. There is also a large Hindu population, and one of the most popular temples in Jalna is the Sri Ganesh Temple. There are also many Muslim mosques and Christian churches in Jalna.

Jalna is located at {{Coord|19.8410|N|75.8864|E|}}. It has an average elevation of {{Convert|508|m|abbr=on}}, on the banks of the [[Kundalika River]].<ref name="geo" />

===Language===
Marathi is the official language of the city. Marathi is also the most commonly spoken language in the city, Urdu is the second most spoken language followed by Hindi.

== Economy ==
The first [[Cotton gin|cotton-ginning]] & [[Cottonseed oil|oil]]-pressing factory was established in the year 1863 by Mr. Pestonji Meherwanji. There is so many problems in jalna like electricity, water shortage, road, etc.

In 1889 a [[Cotton mill|cotton-spinning and weaving mill]] was erected in [[Aurangabad]] city, which employed 700 people. With the opening of the [[Hyderabad–Godavari Valley Railways]] in the year 1900, several ginning factories were started. In Jalna alone, there were 9 cotton-ginning factories and 5 cotton presses, besides two ginning factories at Aurangabad and [[Kannad]], and one oil press at Aurangabad. The total number of people employed in the cotton presses and ginning factories in the year 1901 was 1,016.<ref name=Khan1909>{{cite book |last=Khan |first=Mirza Mehdy |title=Hyderabad |series=Imperial Gazetteer of India |year=1909 |location=Calcutta |publisher=Government Printing Press}}</ref>

Jalna is the biggest producer of sweet lemons and oranges in Maharashtra.{{citation needed|date=December 2019}}

Jalna is the biggest steel producer in Maharashtra state, with many steel companies located in the [[Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation]] (MIDC) area.{{citation needed|date=September 2020}}

== Transport ==
Jalna is primarily connected with the rest of India by railway and road.

===Air===
[[Aurangabad Airport]] is the closest airport to Jalna, it is about {{convert|55|km}} away from Jalna city center, about one hour travel time by road.

===High speed rail===
Jalna is going to be an important station in the middle of the bullet train project proposed between [[Mumbai]] and [[Nagpur]]. So in the future, Jalna is going to get a boost of high speed transport connectivity.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/nagpur/ngp-mum-bullet-train-dpr-to-be-ready-by-feb-end-danve/articleshow/89533877.cms|title=Mumbai-Nagpur Bullet Train Project: Overview|newspaper=The Times of India |date = 13 February 2022}}</ref>

=== Rail ===
{{stnlnk|Jalna}} is a station located on the [[Secunderabad–Manmad line]] of the newly created Nanded Division of [[South Central Railway]]. Formerly, it had been a part of [[Hyderabad railway division|Hyderabad Division]], before divisional adjustments in 2003, which saw HYB's bifurcation.

=== Road ===
Jalna is connected to major towns of the state by state highways. Road connectivity is excellent, roads connecting to [[Aurangabad Maharashtra|Aurangabad]], [[Pune]], [[Ahmednagar]], [[Nagpur]], [[Nanded]], [[Parbhani]], [[Beed]], [[Khamgaon]], [[Jalgaon]] and [[Mumbai]] having been widened to four-lane highways. The new [[Mumbai-Nagpur Expressway]] (Samruddhi Mahamarg) passes through Jalna.

=== Dry port ===
Recently, a [[Jalna Dry Port|new dry port project]] has been set up in the MIDC Phase 3 area near the city. This is the first [[dry port]] project in the country. A similar, smaller project is also in progress in [[Wardha]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Dry port near Jalna to be ready by end of 2021: Danve |url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/aurangabad/dry-port-near-jalna-to-be-ready-by-end-of-2021-danve/articleshow/80912373.cms |date=15 February 2021 |newspaper=The Times of India |agency=TNN |access-date=1 April 2022}}</ref>
