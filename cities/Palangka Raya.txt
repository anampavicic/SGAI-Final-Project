'''Palangka Raya''' is the capital and largest city of the Indonesian [[provinces of Indonesia|province]] of [[Central Kalimantan]]. The city is situated between the [[Kahayan River|Kahayan]] and the [[Sabangau River|Sabangau]] rivers on the island of [[Borneo]]. As of the 2020 census, the city has a population of 293,500;<ref name="Badan Pusat Statistik 2021">Badan Pusat Statistik, Jakarta, 2021.</ref> the official estimate as at mid 2023 was 305,797 (comprising 154,205 males and 151,592 females).<ref name="Badan Pusat Statistik 2024">Badan Pusat Statistik, Jakarta, 28 February 2024, ''Kota Palangkaraya Dalam Angka 2024'' (Katalog-BPS 1102001.6271)</ref> Palangka Raya is the largest city by land area in Indonesia (approximately four times the size of [[Jakarta]]); however, most of the area is forested (particularly in the northern districts of Rakumpit and Bukit Batu) including protected forests, nature conservation areas, and Tangkiling Forest.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kemendagri.go.id/pages/profil-daerah/kabupaten/id/62/name/kalimantan-tengah/detail/6271/kota-palangka-raya |title=Kabupaten - Kementerian Dalam Negeri - Republik Indonesia |access-date=20 September 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130921054125/http://www.kemendagri.go.id/pages/profil-daerah/kabupaten/id/62/name/kalimantan-tengah/detail/6271/kota-palangka-raya |archive-date=21 September 2013 }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Simorangkir|first=Eduardo|title=5 Hal Ini Hanya Anda Temukan di Palangka Raya|url=https://finance.detik.com/berita-ekonomi-bisnis/d-3569210/5-hal-ini-hanya-anda-temukan-di-palangka-raya|access-date=7 May 2021|website=detikfinance|language=id-ID|archive-date=7 May 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210507072353/https://finance.detik.com/berita-ekonomi-bisnis/d-3569210/5-hal-ini-hanya-anda-temukan-di-palangka-raya|url-status=live}}</ref>

The city is the center of economic, governance, and education of Central Kalimantan province. It is a relatively new city, founded from a small Dayak village of Pahandut in 1957. The city was planned from scratch and the construction was assisted by the [[Soviet Armed Forces|Soviet military]] due to [[Sukarno]]'s relation to [[Eastern Bloc]] at the time. Despite relatively developed infrastructure and high Human Development Index rating, the city suffers from environmental problems such as haze, forest fires, and floods.<ref name="apahabar.com">{{Cite web|date=2019-06-24|title=Palangka Raya: Jejak Rusia dan Kandidat Ibu Kota dari Masa ke Masa|url=https://apahabar.com/2019/06/palangka-raya-jejak-rusia-dan-kandidat-ibu-kota-dari-masa-ke-masa/|access-date=2021-09-26|website=apahabar.com|language=id-ID}}</ref>

== Etymology ==
[[File:Sungai Kahayan Palangkaraya.jpg|thumb|The [[Kahayan River]] through the city of Palangka Raya.|alt=|left]]
The name Palangka Raya is a combination of two words from two languages: ''palangka'' ([[Dayak language|Dayak Ngaju]]), meaning sacred site, and ''raya'' ([[Malay language|Malay]]), meaning vast; therefore, Palangka Raya means ''a vast sacred site''. The city stands on the site of a small Dayak village called Pahandut that was surrounded by rainforest.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Profil Palangka Raya|url=https://palangkaraya.go.id/wp-content/uploads/2018/10/PROFIL2017-final.pdf|url-status=live|access-date=23 April 2021|archive-date=23 April 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210423012845/https://palangkaraya.go.id/wp-content/uploads/2018/10/PROFIL2017-final.pdf}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Febriyana|first=Wahyu|title=Website Kalimantan Tengah|url=https://kalteng.go.id/|access-date=2021-06-30|website=kalteng|language=id|archive-date=7 June 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210607230325/https://www.kalteng.go.id/|url-status=live}}</ref>

According to a local legend, ''Bayuh'' and ''Kambang'', a married Dayak couple, founded the village because the surrounding region was not suitable for building a house, as its soil was made up of peat and swamps. Over time, the village grew into a small Dayak outpost with its surroundings being used for farming and as a small plantation. At first, it was believed that the name of the village was Dukuh Bayuh. Dukuh Bayuh was thought to be derived from ''Eka Badukuh'', meaning "place to settle" in Dayak (with daily usage called ''Dukuh ain Bayuh,'' shortened Dukuh Bayuh). Pahandut on the other hand, according to local stories, was a respected elder and cultural figure from the village. He was often called ''Bapa Handut'', which literally means Mr. Handut. Handut passed away because of an illness, and to respect his death as an important figure from the village, it was named Pahandut.<ref>{{Cite book|last=Rahman Hakim|first=Arif|title=Sejarah kota Palangka Raya|publisher=Palangka Raya : Pemerintah Kota Palangka Raya|year=2003|isbn=979-97978-0-2|location=Palangka Raya|pages=17–19}}</ref>

== History ==
[[File:Manuscript map of the Banjarmasin region.jpg|left|thumb|Map of Kalimantan under [[Dutch East Indies|Dutch colonial rule]]]]

=== Colonial Era ===
According to documents from the [[Dutch East Indies]], Pahandut village's existence was discovered by Zacharias Hartman, a Dutch explorer, on his journey around [[Kahayan River|Kahayan]] and [[Kapuas River]] in 1823. This conflicts with the statements that claim the village was previously known under the Sultanate of [[Sultanate of Banjar|Banjar]], which became a Dutch protectorate under an agreement with Sultan Tahmidullah II in 1787. The region was leased to the Dutch East Indies by Sultan Sulaiman in 1817, and was ceded by Sultan Adam of [[Sultanate of Banjar|Banjar]] under a contract signed on 4 May 1826, written in Dutch on second point which reads in English as:<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|title=Perjanjian-Perjanjian Antara Kesultanan Banjarmasin dengan Pemerintah Inggris, Republik Batavia, dan Hindia Belanda|url=http://lib.ui.ac.id/file?file=digital/20393402-Surat-surat%20perdjandjian%20antara%20kesultanan%20%20bandjarmasin%20dengan%20pemerintah2%20V.O.C.,%20bataafse%20republik,%20Inggris%20dan%20hindia~1.pdf|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210831074354/http://lib.ui.ac.id/file?file=digital%2F20393402-Surat-surat+perdjandjian+antara+kesultanan++bandjarmasin+dengan+pemerintah2+V.O.C.%2C+bataafse+republik%2C+Inggris+dan+hindia~1.pdf|archive-date=31 August 2021|access-date=31 August 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|last=Rahman Hakim|first=Arif|title=Sejarah kota Palangka Raya|publisher=Pemerintah Kota Palangka Raya|year=2003|isbn=9799797802|location=Palangka Raya|pages=15–18}}</ref>

It was integrated into ''Zuid Ooster Afdeeling'' in accordance with ''Bêsluit van den Minister van Staat, Gouverneur-Generaal van Nederlandsch-Indie'' ''No. 8'' , 27 August 1849. ("Decision of the Minister of State, Governor-General of Netherlands-India No. 8) <ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=KJFBAAAAYAAJ&q=Verdeeling+van+het+Eiland+Borneo+in+tteee++afdeelingen%2C+onder+de+benaming+van+Wester+afdeeling+en+Zuid+en+Ooster+afdeeling.&pg=PA55-IA22|title=Staatsblad van Nederlandisch Indië|date=1849|language=nl|access-date=4 May 2021|archive-date=31 August 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210831074317/https://books.google.com/books?id=KJFBAAAAYAAJ&q=Verdeeling+van+het+Eiland+Borneo+in+tteee++afdeelingen%2C+onder+de+benaming+van+Wester+afdeeling+en+Zuid+en+Ooster+afdeeling.&pg=PA55-IA22|url-status=live}}</ref> In 1898, it became part of ''Afdeeling Dajaklandeen'' in accordance with ''Staatblad 1898 No. 178''. (Official Gazette 1898 No. 178)<ref>{{Cite book|last=Harmsen|first=Leendert Karel|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Drqm_YG3PIsC&q=Groot-Dajak&pg=PA61|title=Bahoewa ini ilmoe boemi ija-itoe pada menjatakan perihal tanah kepoelauwan hindija di sabelah Timor jang di bawah perentah goewernemen Holanda|date=1875|publisher=Ogilvie|language=id|access-date=4 May 2021|archive-date=31 August 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210831074317/https://books.google.com/books?id=Drqm_YG3PIsC&q=Groot-Dajak&pg=PA61|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name=":5">{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=TVZVAAAAcAAJ&q=G%27roote+Dajak+%28standplaats+Penglok.%29&pg=PA179|title=Almanak van Nederlandsch-Indië voor het jaar ....|date=1870|publisher=Lands Drukkery|pages=83|language=nl|access-date=4 May 2021|archive-date=31 August 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210831074318/https://books.google.com/books?id=TVZVAAAAcAAJ&q=G%27roote+Dajak+%28standplaats+Penglok.%29&pg=PA179|url-status=live}}</ref> Dutch rule remained weak in this region and several revolts from native rulers took place, such as under Raden Djaija and Pambakal Sulil in 1849.<ref>{{Cite book|last=Mayur|first=Gusti|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=vBEtAQAAIAAJ&q=Raden+Kasuma+Raga|title=Perang Banjar|date=1979|publisher=Rapi|language=id|access-date=4 May 2021|archive-date=31 August 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210831074352/https://books.google.com/books?id=vBEtAQAAIAAJ&q=Raden+Kasuma+Raga|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name=":5" /> The region saw a conflict over succession and against Dutch rule called the [[Banjarmasin War]]. There has been debate about whether the fighting constituted one war or two, with the potential auxiliary war called the "Barito War".<ref name=":7" /> The village of Pahandut was also used by German [[Missionary|missionaries]] as an outpost for the region around the Kahayan river. In the Tumbang Anoi Peacemaking Agreement between Dayak tribes in 1894 to end the practice of [[headhunting]], it was noted that Pahandut village consisted of eight [[longhouse]]s and was inhabited by around 40 families.<ref>{{Cite book|last=Rahman Hakim|first=Arif|title=Sejarah kota Palangka Raya|publisher=Palangka Raya : Pemerintah Kota Palangka Raya|year=2003|isbn=979-97978-0-2|location=Palangka Raya|pages=18–20}}</ref>

Later, during [[Indonesian National Revolution]], there were armed clash within the region between Indonesian nationalists under unit TNI/MN 1001 and [[Royal Netherlands East Indies Army]] on 17 January 1946.<ref name=":7">{{Cite book|last=Rahman Hakim|first=Arif|title=Sejarah kota Palangka Raya|publisher=Palangka Raya : Pemerintah Kota Palangka Raya|year=2003|isbn=979-97978-0-2|location=Palangka Raya|pages=8–13}}</ref>

=== Establishment ===
The province of Central Kalimantan was established on 23 May 1957. At first, the capital for the Central Kalimantan province was not agreed and it was debated where it should be located. R.T.A. Milono, acting governor of Central Kalimantan, discussed the matter with several native organizations and cultural figures from the region. This however evoked tension from several native tribes and organizations that each wanted their own region to be chosen as capital. On 23 January 1957, a committee to discuss the matter was created, consisting mostly of Dayak cultural figures, in addition to military figures such as [[Tjilik Riwut]] and Dutch-descendant architect D.A.W. Van Der Pijl who also later designed the then-proposed new capital of [[South Kalimantan]], [[Banjarbaru]]. From the committee's meeting, it was decided that Pahandut village would be the capital to avoid cultural association within tribes in the province, hence avoiding conflicts.<ref name=":7" /> At the time, the village only had an estimated population of around 900.<ref name=":8">{{Cite web|last=Fikrie|first=Muammar|title=Tjilik Riwut, sang legenda Kalimantan Tengah|url=https://lokadata.id/artikel/tjilik-riwut-sang-legenda-kalimantan-tengah|access-date=2022-01-02|website=Lokadata.ID|language=id}}</ref>

The first pillar in the development of Palangka Raya City was erected by [[Sukarno]], the [[President of Indonesia|President of the Republic of Indonesia]], on 17 July 1957, marking the inauguration of the monument to the provincial capital of Central Kalimantan in Pahandut.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://kaltim.tribunnews.com/2017/07/05/tugu-di-palangkaraya-ini-dibangun-soekarno-saat-menggagas-rencana-ibu-kota-negara-indonesia|title=Tugu di Palangkaraya Ini Dibangun Soekarno saat Menggagas Rencana Ibu Kota Negara Indonesia|year=2017|work=Kaltim Tribun News|access-date=19 October 2017|archive-date=19 October 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171019215708/http://kaltim.tribunnews.com/2017/07/05/tugu-di-palangkaraya-ini-dibangun-soekarno-saat-menggagas-rencana-ibu-kota-negara-indonesia|url-status=live}}</ref> Then, in accordance with Law No. 21 of 1958, Pahandut was renamed to Palangka Raya City, maintaining its status as the provincial seat.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.kemendagri.go.id/pages/profil-daerah/kabupaten/id/62/name/kalimantan-tengah/detail/6271/kota-palangka-raya |title=Kabupaten - Kementerian Dalam Negeri - Republik Indonesia |access-date=20 September 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130921054125/http://www.kemendagri.go.id/pages/profil-daerah/kabupaten/id/62/name/kalimantan-tengah/detail/6271/kota-palangka-raya |archive-date=21 September 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The construction of the city was directly led by Tjilik Riwut, whom by then had become governor of the Central Kalimantan province. The city was claimed by him to be "free from colonial legacies". At first however, he was rumoured to be insane by residents of the province for trying to build a city on top of nothingness.<ref name=":8" /> It was officially made the capital of Central Kalimantan on 1 January 1960, relocated from temporary seat in [[Banjarmasin]]. By late 1962, the city had by this point largely finished construction of basic infrastructure such as schools, government offices, inner city roads, and residential areas, with an estimated 500 buildings having been constructed.<ref name=":9" />

On 23 July 1965, it gained official city status (''kotapraja'') and formed its own municipal government.<ref name=":9">{{Cite book|last=Rahman Hakim|first=Arif|title=Sejarah kota Palangka Raya|publisher=Palangka Raya : Pemerintah Kota Palangka Raya|year=2003|isbn=979-97978-0-2|location=Palangka Raya|pages=57–70}}</ref> The city was constructed with the assistance of Soviet military, especially regarding road construction due to the terrain of the region - dominated by swamps and peat.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://beritagar.id/media/galeri-foto/dari-mosow-untuk-borneo|access-date=2021-09-26|website=beritagar.id|title=Dari Moskow untuk Borneo |date=19 August 2017 }}</ref> The road which today is named Jl. Tjilik Riwut, was previously planned to be constructed from the city to [[Pangkalan Bun]] and [[Sampit]]. This however, was never realized due to [[30 September Movement]], coup d'état against Sukarno, and subsequent [[Indonesian mass killings of 1965–66|communist purge]] that followed.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Handayani|first=Maulida Sri|title=Jalan Rusia di Kalimantan Timur|url=https://tirto.id/jalan-rusia-di-kalimantan-timur-cip3|access-date=2021-09-26|website=tirto.id|date=6 February 2017 |language=id}}</ref> Many local workers resigned and abandoned their work due to fear of being accused as communist and the road construction stopped at the town of Tangkiling.<ref name="apahabar.com"/>

== Geography ==
Palangka Raya borders [[Gunung Mas Regency]] to the north, [[Katingan Regency]] to the west, and [[Pulang Pisau Regency]] to the south and east. The area's soil consists mostly of [[peat]] and [[alluvium]] in its southern part and [[podzol]] on the northern part of the city. Overall, podzol soil dominates the city soil with 218,486 out of 285,349 hectares of city's landscape. Most of the city's topography is flat, with a general slope of less than 40&nbsp;percent.<ref name=":6">{{Cite web|date=3 June 2016|title=Geografis|url=https://palangkaraya.go.id/selayang-pandang/geografis/|access-date=22 April 2021|website=Pemerintah Kota Palangka Raya|language=id-ID|archive-date=23 April 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210423032124/https://palangkaraya.go.id/selayang-pandang/geografis/|url-status=live}}</ref> Palangka Raya is mostly located around 35 meters above sea level.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Badan Pusat Statistik Provinsi Kalimantan Tengah|url=https://kalteng.bps.go.id/statictable/2016/09/21/264/ketinggian-beberapa-kota-dari-permukaan-laut-menurut-kabupaten-kota-di-provinsi-kalimantan-tengah.html|access-date=22 April 2021|website=kalteng.bps.go.id|archive-date=22 April 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210422231420/https://kalteng.bps.go.id/statictable/2016/09/21/264/ketinggian-beberapa-kota-dari-permukaan-laut-menurut-kabupaten-kota-di-provinsi-kalimantan-tengah.html|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name=":2">{{Cite web|title=BPS Kota Palangka Raya|url=https://palangkakota.bps.go.id/publication/2021/02/26/55e738988cf6f9dfcc99fed5/kota-palangka-raya-dalam-angka-2021.html|access-date=22 April 2021|website=palangkakota.bps.go.id|archive-date=22 April 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210422231418/https://palangkakota.bps.go.id/publication/2021/02/26/55e738988cf6f9dfcc99fed5/kota-palangka-raya-dalam-angka-2021.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

10,105.34 hectares of land in the city is designated as protected forest, while 90,722.15 hectares are production forest. In addition, there are also 63,816.40 hectares of national park, 1,771.13 hectares of nature reserve, and 726.20 hectares of preserved nature.<ref name=":6" />

=== Climate ===
Palangka Raya has a [[tropical rainforest climate]] (Af) with heavy rainfall year-round. According to [[Meteorology, Climatology, and Geophysical Agency]], average temperature in Palangka Raya is {{Convert|27.28|C|F}} , with minimum temperature {{Convert|21.4|C|F}}, and highest temperature {{Convert|35.2|C|F}}. [[Moisture]] is between 65 – 95% with yearly average of 82.89%. Wind usually flows between 2 and 22 knots, with yearly average of 5.3 knots. Increase of wind speed is expected in August and November.<ref name=":6" />

== Demographics ==
Population growth was 2.84% as of 2019, with sex ratio of 106 males per 100 females. As of 2020, the city's population is dominated by younger demographics above the age of 15, making up an estimated 222,285 people of reproductive age. There are estimated to be 199,140 Muslims, 73,641 Protestants, 5,511 Catholics, 3,453 Hindus, 485 Buddhists, 8 Confucians, and 27 followers of other religions such as folk religions.<ref name=":3" /><ref name=":2" /> The population of age 20 – 24 keeps increasing; this is thought to be caused by students from other regions in Central Kalimantan moving to the city to pursue higher education.<ref name=":3" /> In addition, the city has the highest human development index and standard of living in the entire Kalimantan, making it an attractive destination for migrants.<ref>{{Cite web|title=BPS Kota Palangka Raya|url=https://palangkakota.bps.go.id/subject/26/indeks-pembangunan-manusia.html|access-date=30 April 2021|website=palangkakota.bps.go.id|archive-date=30 April 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210430073547/https://palangkakota.bps.go.id/subject/26/indeks-pembangunan-manusia.html|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=RRI 2021|first=LPP|title=Palangka Raya Tertinggi Indeks Pembangunan Manusia di Kalimantan|url=https://rri.co.id/palangkaraya/daerah/1013304/palangka-raya-tertinggi-indeks-pembangunan-manusia-di-kalimantan|access-date=30 April 2021|website=rri.co.id|language=en|archive-date=30 April 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210430073548/https://rri.co.id/palangkaraya/daerah/1013304/palangka-raya-tertinggi-indeks-pembangunan-manusia-di-kalimantan|url-status=live}}</ref> Majority of city population are [[Dayak people|Dayak ethnic]], accounts more than half of city's population. Other ethnics such as [[Banjar people|Banjar]], [[Malays (ethnic group)|Malay]], [[Buginese people|Buginese]], and [[Javanese people|Javanese]] also exist in the city.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Febriyana|first=Wahyu|title=Keragaman Agama di Kalimantan Tengah|url=https://mmc.kalteng.go.id/berita/read/2025/keragaman-agama-di-kalimantan-tengah|access-date=30 April 2021|website=mmckalteng|language=id|archive-date=30 April 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210430081831/https://mmc.kalteng.go.id/berita/read/2025/keragaman-agama-di-kalimantan-tengah|url-status=live}}</ref> More than half of the city population are from Jekan Raya District.<ref>{{Cite web|date=3 June 2016|title=Jumlah Penduduk|url=https://palangkaraya.go.id/selayang-pandang/jumlah-penduduk/|access-date=1 May 2021|website=Pemerintah Kota Palangka Raya|language=id-ID|archive-date=28 April 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210428175725/https://palangkaraya.go.id/selayang-pandang/jumlah-penduduk/|url-status=live}}</ref>

== Economy ==
Economic growth was 7% in 2019, above the national average, and inflation rate in the same year was 0.85%. Unemployment rate was 6% in 2020. The service sector is the largest contributor to city's gross regional product, contributing 28.36% of it in 2020, in contrast with neighboring regions which still rely mainly on agriculture and extraction. The next largest contributor to city's economy in 2020 was trade, with figure of 17.85%, followed by construction with 11.06%. The financial and banking sector provides around 6% of the city's economy. Agriculture, forestry, plantation, and fishery all combined contributed to 2.45% of the city's economy in 2019. Crop yields decreased rapidly from year-on-year; production of [[Chili pepper|red chili]] dropped from 2,864 tons in 2018 to just more than 200 tons in 2020. Fish catch also decreased by 19.8% from 2018 to 2019. Tourism is a small but growing sector, with 389,235 domestic tourists and 3,865 foreign tourists visiting the city in 2019.<ref name=":3" /><ref name=":2" /> The city government owns 2.47% of the shares of [[Central Kalimantan Bank]] together with provincial government.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Profil Perusahaan|url=http://www.bankkalteng.co.id/info/Profil-Perusahaan|access-date=23 April 2021|website=www.bankkalteng.co.id|archive-date=23 April 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210423041801/http://www.bankkalteng.co.id/info/Profil-Perusahaan|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Blog – BANK KALTENG BAGI DIVIDEN TAHUN BUKU 2017|url=http://www.bankkalteng.co.id/blog/BANK-KALTENG-BAGI-DIVIDEN-TAHUN-BUKU-2017|access-date=23 April 2021|website=www.bankkalteng.co.id|archive-date=23 April 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210423041803/http://www.bankkalteng.co.id/blog/BANK-KALTENG-BAGI-DIVIDEN-TAHUN-BUKU-2017|url-status=live}}</ref>

There are 56,430 small and medium enterprises in 2017, from which 72% are on trade, restaurant, and hotel sector. On the same year, there are more than 250 registered cooperatives in the city.<ref>{{Cite web|date=8 June 2016|title=Industri Kecil dan Menengah|url=https://palangkaraya.go.id/potensi-daerah/industri-kecil-dan-menengah/|access-date=30 April 2021|website=Pemerintah Kota Palangka Raya|language=id-ID|archive-date=4 May 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210504172003/https://palangkaraya.go.id/potensi-daerah/industri-kecil-dan-menengah/|url-status=live}}</ref>

=== Local government ===
[[File:Kantor Wali Kota Palangka Raya.JPG|thumb|Palangka Raya city hall building]]
As with all [[City status in Indonesia|Indonesian cities]], it is a second-level administrative division run by a mayor and a vice mayor together with the city parliament, and it is equivalent to a regency.<ref>{{Cite web|title=UU 22 1999|url=https://www.dpr.go.id/dokjdih/document/uu/UU_1999_22.pdf|url-status=live|access-date=22 April 2021|archive-date=16 April 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210416012225/https://www.dpr.go.id/dokjdih/document/uu/UU_1999_22.pdf}}</ref> Executive power is vested in the mayor and vice mayor, while legislative duties are vested in the local parliament. The mayor, vice mayor, and parliament members are directly elected by the people of the city in an election.<ref>{{Cite web|title=UU 8 2015|url=https://www.dpr.go.id/dokjdih/document/uu/1627.pdf|url-status=live|access-date=22 April 2021|archive-date=26 February 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210226055828/https://www.dpr.go.id/dokjdih/document/uu/1627.pdf}}</ref> Heads of districts are appointed by the mayor on the recommendation of the city secretary.<ref>{{Cite web|title=PP No. 17 Tahun 2018 tentang Kecamatan [JDIH BPK RI]|url=https://peraturan.bpk.go.id/Home/Details/77921/pp-no-17-tahun-2018|access-date=16 April 2021|website=peraturan.bpk.go.id|archive-date=17 April 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210417073426/https://peraturan.bpk.go.id/Home/Details/77921/pp-no-17-tahun-2018|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>Government Law No.19 1998</ref>

=== Politics ===
Palangka Raya is part of 1st Central Kalimantan electoral district in provincial level, together with [[Katingan Regency]] and [[Gunung Mas Regency]] have representatives of 10 out of 45 in provincial parliament. In city level, Palangka Raya is divided into three electoral districts. City parliament in total has 30 representatives that are democratically elected by people of the city in an election. Jekan Raya District is divided into two in electoral district distribution, with Jekan Raya A consist of Bukit Tunggal and Petuk Katimpun Subdistrict, and Jekan Raya B consist of Menteng and Palangka Subdistrict.  Last election was in 2019 and the next one would be in 2024.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Keputusan KPU Kalteng 284|url=https://jdih.kpu.go.id/data/data_kepkpu/284_Kalteng.pdf|url-status=live|access-date=23 April 2021|archive-date=2 October 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201002054040/https://jdih.kpu.go.id/data/data_kepkpu/284_Kalteng.pdf}}</ref> As Palangka Raya is also provincial capital, it also has governor office and provincial parliament of Central Kalimantan located in the city.

=== Military ===
The city is headquarter of Kodim 1016/Palangka Raya inside [[:id:Komando Resor Militer 102|Korem 102/Panju Pujung]], which was historically under [[Regional Military Command|Kodam XI/Tambun Bungai]] but currently is under [[Kodam XII/Tanjungpura]]. The unit was created on 9 December 1975 and formalized on 23 March 1976.<ref>{{Cite web|title=SEJARAH|url=https://korem102panjupanjung.com/?page_id=41|access-date=7 May 2021|website=korem102pjg|language=en-US|archive-date=13 May 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210513164034/https://korem102panjupanjung.com/?page_id=41|url-status=live}}</ref> The unit has one [[Indonesian Army infantry battalions|raider battalion]] attached to it as of 2021, which is Raider Battalion 631st Antang Elang.<ref>{{Cite web|title=YONIF 631/ATG Archives|url=https://korem102panjupanjung.com/?cat=211|access-date=7 May 2021|website=korem102pjg|language=en-US|archive-date=10 May 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210510142432/https://korem102panjupanjung.com/?cat=211|url-status=live}}</ref>

== Infrastructure ==

===Education===
[[File:GEDUNG SMP IT AL GHAZALI.jpg|thumb|A junior highschool in Palangka Raya]]
There are 134 kindergartens, 146 elementary schools, 60 junior highschools, 34 senior highschools, and 16 vocational highschools in the city. Palangka Raya is a home to number of universities and higher education institutions. [[University of Palangka Raya]] is the only public university in the city and the most notable one in the city. Other higher education institutions are Palangka Raya Christian University, Tampung Penyang Hindu Institute, Palangka Raya Muhammadiyah University, among many others.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Daftar Perguruan Tinggi Negeri & Swasta di Kalimantan Tengah – PENDAFTARAN MAHASISWA|url=http://www.pendaftaranmahasiswa.web.id/2019/07/daftar-perguruan-tinggi-negeri-swasta_88.html|access-date=23 April 2021|website=www.pendaftaranmahasiswa.web.id|archive-date=23 April 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210423001944/http://www.pendaftaranmahasiswa.web.id/2019/07/daftar-perguruan-tinggi-negeri-swasta_88.html|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|title=Universitas Palangka Raya|url=https://campus.quipper.com/directory?location=Kalimantan%20Tengah|url-status=live|access-date=23 April 2021|website=campus.quipper.com|archive-date=23 April 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210423001945/https://campus.quipper.com/directory?location=Kalimantan%20Tengah}}</ref> School enrollment rate as of 2020 was 99%.<ref name=":0" />

===Healthcare===
Palangka Raya city has eight hospitals (including two maternity hospitals), five [[polyclinic]]s, 35 [[puskesmas]], and eight pharmacy. One of the biggest public hospital, Doris Sylvanus Regional Hospital, is owned by provincial government and has been rated by [[Ministry of Health (Indonesia)|Ministry of Health]] as B-class hospital. == Transportation ==
The city is served by [[Tjilik Riwut Airport]], which is also home to an aviation school operated by Lion Air.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://nasional.tempo.co/read/461538/lion-air-bangun-sekolah-pilot-di-palangkaraya|title=Lion Air Bangun Sekolah Pilot di Palangkaraya|first=Abdul Djalil|last=Hakim|date=15 February 2013|website=Tempo|access-date=29 April 2019|archive-date=11 April 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190411142728/https://nasional.tempo.co/read/461538/lion-air-bangun-sekolah-pilot-di-palangkaraya|url-status=live}}</ref> The airport served more than 8,000 aircraft and approximately 800,000 passengers in 2020.<ref name=":0" /> Palangka Raya has total of 911.83 kilometers of road, of which 563.73 kilometers have been paved with [[Asphalt concrete|asphalt]].<ref name=":0" />

The [[Kahayan River]] bisects the city and is also used for means of transportation. == Media ==
