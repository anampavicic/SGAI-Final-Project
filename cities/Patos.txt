'''Patos''' is a [[Municipality (Brazil)|municipality]] of the [[States of Brazil|state]] of [[Paraíba]] in the [[Northeast Region, Brazil|Northeast Region]] of [[Brazil]]. It is classified by the [[Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics]] as a [[Urban hierarchy in Brazil#Sub-regional centers|sub-regional center A]].<ref name="ibge_regic">{{Cite web |url=http://biblioteca.ibge.gov.br/visualizacao/livros/liv40677.pdf |title=Regions of Influence of Cities 2007|date=October 10, 2008 |accessdate=April 3, 2017 |publisher=Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics (IBGE)|last=|first=}}</ref><ref name="ipardes_diana">{{Cite web |url=http://www.ipardes.gov.br/pdf/revista_PR/100/diana.pdf |title=Configuration of the Urban Network of Brazil |date=June 2001 |accessdate=December 3, 2020 |publisher=Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics (IBGE)}}</ref>

It is located in the [[Espinharas River]] valley, surrounded by the [[Borborema Plateau]] to [[east]] and [[south]], and by the [[pediplain]] [[Sertão|Sertanejo]] to the [[west]]. It originated from the village of Patos, spun off from the [[Pombal, Paraíba|Parish of Nossa Senhora do Bom Sucesso de Pombal]] on October 6, 1788.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://biblioteca.ibge.gov.br/visualizacao/dtbs/paraiba/patos.pdf |publisher=Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics (IBGE) |format=PDF |title=History - Patos-PB |accessdate=June 20, 2019}} </ref>

The city is 306&nbsp;km from the city of [[João Pessoa, Paraíba|João Pessoa]], the center of its [[List of Intermediate and Immediate Geographic Regions|immediate and intermediate geographic regions]]. It stands out as an educational, commercial, banking, religious and health center, both in the [[Sertão|back country of Paraíba]], and in areas of [[Pernambuco]] and [[Rio Grande do Norte]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sinatran-pb.com/post.php?codigo=81#.Ujzd98Y3saA|title=CITIZEN SUGGESTION: Integration Terminal of buses and Alternatives in Patos|accessdate=September 20, 2013|author=Sinatran-PB}}</ref> It is the third most important municipality in the state considering the economic, political and social aspects (behind João Pessoa and [[Campina Grande]]).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.folhapatoense.com/2019/09/02/patos-ou-santa-rita-qual-e-o-terceiro-municipio-mais-importante-da-paraiba/|title=Patos or Santa Rita? What is the third most important municipality in Paraíba?|author=|date=September 2, 2019|accessdate=September 4, 2019|publisher=Folha Patoense}}</ref> According to IBGE estimates for 2021, it is the fourth most populous municipality in the state with 108,766 inhabitants. <ref name="IBGE_Pop_2021">{{cite web |url=https://cidades.ibge.gov.br/brasil/pb/patos/panorama|title=Estimativa populacional 2021 IBGE|publisher=Instituto Brasileiro de Geografia e Estatística (IBGE)|date= August 28, 2021 |accessdate = August 28, 2021}}</ref>

[[Esporte Clube de Patos|Esporte]] and [[Nacional Atlético Clube (Patos)|Nacional]] are the city's two [[Association football|football]] (soccer) clubs. They play at the José Cavalcanti Municipal Stadium. There are four multisport arenas: Rivaldão, AABB, SESC and SESI.

The city is served by [[Patos Airport|Brig. Firmino Ayres Airport]].

==Climate==
The city of Patos experiences a [[tropical]] [[Semi-arid climate#Hot semi-arid climates|hot semi-arid climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification|Köppen]]: ''BSh''), just short of a [[tropical savanna climate]], with a short rainy season from February to April. During this period, when the equatorial rainband associated with the highly seasonalized positioning of the [[Intertropical Convergence Zone]] is over the city, warm to hot temperatures and abundant equatorial rainfall prevail. Conversely, the dry season dominates the remaining majority of the year, with abundant sunshine prevalent from May to January. The wettest month is March, with an average monthly precipitation of 213&nbsp;mm (8.38&nbsp;in), while the driest month is September, with an average precipitation of only 1&nbsp;mm (0.04&nbsp;in).
