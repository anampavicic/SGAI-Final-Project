'''Marab√°''' is a [[Municipalities of Brazil|municipality]] in the state of [[Par√°]], [[Brazil]]. Its greatest geographic reference is the confluence of two large rivers near the historic city center, the [[Itacaiunas River]] and the [[Tocantins River]], forming a "Y" if seen from space. It basically consists of six urban centers linked by five highways.

Marab√° is the fourth-most-populous municipality in the state of Par√°, approximately 283,542 inhabitants according to the [[Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics|IBGE]]/2020, and the fourth largest [[GDP]] in the state of Par√°, with US$1.543.254,34 according to the IDESP/2010.<ref>[http://www.idesp.pa.gov.br/pdf/pib/PIBMunicipal2010.pdf Produto Interno Bruto dos Munic√≠pios do estado do Par√° em 2010] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131110012212/http://www.idesp.pa.gov.br/pdf/pib/PIBMunicipal2010.pdf |date=2013-11-10 }} - Instituto de Desenvolvimento Econ√¥mico, Social e Ambiental do Par√° - 13 de mar√ßo de 2012</ref> It's the main center of political, social, and economic development in southern of Par√° and one of the most dynamic municipalities in Brazil.

Marab√° has a strategic position and is crossed by five highways. It also has a large logistics infrastructure, with a port, airport, and railway. The municipality has a growing industrial park. The [[steel industry]] is especially important to Marab√°'s vast agricultural frontier. Marab√° also has a strong trade and services sector.

Marab√° is characterized by its broad mix of peoples and cultures that do justice to the meaning of the town's nickname: "Son of Miscegenation."

== Etymology ==
The word "Marab√°" derives from the [[Indigenous peoples in Brazil|indigenous]] vocable "Mayr-Ab√°", which simply means "son of the indigenous woman with a [[Caucasian race|white man]]."

A poem written by poet [[Gon√ßalves Dias]] inspired by the merchant Francisco Silva to its commercial name of "Casa Marab√°" (Maraba House). This was located the banks of the Tocantins River, and served as a strategic business point for exchanging all kinds of products and services.

== History ==
The settlement of the river basin Itacaiunas played an important factor in shaping the city, because even though this region was later colonized by the [[Portuguese Empire]] in the sixteenth century, remained without a permanent settler occupation for almost 300 years. Only in 1894 is, in fact, the space was occupied by settlers.

=== Colonization ===
The first to participate in the colonization of the territory of Marab√°, in 1894, were the political leaders who had escaped from political guerrillas in the northern [[Goi√°s Province|Province of Goi√°s]], specifically the city of [[Tocantin√≥polis|Boa Vista]].<ref>[http://www.marabaonline.com.br/maraba/links/gomesleitao.php Carlos Gomes Leit√£o] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140515015200/http://www.marabaonline.com.br/maraba/links/gomesleitao.php |date=2014-05-15 }} - Maraba Online. {{in lang|pt}}</ref> One of those leaders was Carlos Leitao, who traveled with his group to the southeast of the province of Gr√£o-Par√° and established its first camp in downstream Itacaiunas River, in December 1894. A definitive village was established on the left bank of the Tocantins River, about 10&nbsp;km downstream from the other camp, the place that Carlos Leitao called "Burgo do Itacay√∫na" (Burgo's Itacay√∫na). After a few months, the extraction of rubber latex began in the "Burgo's Itacay√∫na" region.

In 1895 Carlos Gomes Leit√£o asks the President of the [[Gr√£o-Par√° Province|Province of Gr√£o-Par√°]], Jos√© Paes de Carvalho, funding for the extraction and commercialization of rubber in addition to getting drugs to be used specifically to combat [[tropical diseases]]. To get funding, Carlos Leit√£o disseminating information on the extraction of rubber in small colony of Burgo's Itacay√∫na and its surroundings. Immediately there is the first population explosion of the locality where the rubber tappers, fishermen and hunters settle in the village of Burgo.<ref name="Clipping Liberal">[http://sib.iesam-pa.edu.br/downloads/hemeroteca/ciencias_humanas/HST/HST1579.pdf ''Clipping de not√≠cias do jornal "O Liberal" sobre o centen√°rio de Marab√°] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140514235144/http://sib.iesam-pa.edu.br/downloads/hemeroteca/ciencias_humanas/HST/HST1579.pdf |date=2014-05-14 }} - Instituto de Estudos Superiores da Amaz√¥nia</ref>

=== Foundation of Pontal do Itacay√∫na ===
The merchant Francisco Coelho da Silva would have been the first to settle in the area downstream of Rio Itacaiunas, crucial to the foundation of the village of "Pontal do Itacay√∫na" (Pontal of Itacay√∫na). The goal of Francisco Silva, while riding the village of Pontal of Itacay√∫na, was to negotiate with the rubber tappers who sought to sell the rubber collected in the region.<ref name="Clipping Liberal"/>

The official historiography Francisco Silva assigns the authorship of the current name of the city. He had installed in the village of Pontal of Itacay√∫na a commercial point called "Casa Marab√°" (House Marab√°), which years later would be used to name the until then village Pontal of Itacay√∫na.<ref name="Clipping Liberal"/>

With the death of Colonel Carlos Leit√£o in 1903, the village of Burgo's Itacay√∫na is disabled and Leit√£o family along with other residents move to the village of Pontal do Itacay√∫na. In 1904 the village of the Pontal shall be called "Marab√°" and host the borough, with the installation of the police detachment.<ref name="sobre marab√° pmm">[http://maraba.pa.gov.br/sobre-maraba/ Hist√≥ria] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140515021115/http://maraba.pa.gov.br/sobre-maraba/ |date=2014-05-15 }} - Prefeitura de Marab√°. {{in lang|pt}}</ref>

=== Formation of the municipality ===
[[File:Embarque de castanha em Marab√°.jpg|thumb|left|250px|Shipment of [[Brazil nuts]] in small boats.]]
The economy of the village of Marab√° grew considerably supported by the extractive base, anchored in exports of [[rubber]] and [[Brazil-nut]] to [[United States|U.S.]] and [[Europe]]an markets. The economic importance gained, influenced the political aspects, so that in the years 1908 to 1909 a major rebellion occurred in Maraba, called of "Revolta dos Galegos" (Revolt of the Galicians).<ref>MONTARROYOS, Heraldo Elias. [http://historiaehistoria.com.br/materia.cfm?tb=artigos&id=222 Hist√≥ria Social e Econ√¥mica da Casa Marab√°: Reconstruindo o Cotidiano de um Barrac√£o na Amaz√¥nia Oriental entre 1898 e 1906] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140515062622/http://historiaehistoria.com.br/materia.cfm?tb=artigos&id=222 |date=2014-05-15 }} - Revista Hist√≥ria ''e''-Hist√≥ria, 2013</ref> The rebellion was demanding political autonomy from the county of [[Bai√£o, Par√°|Bai√£o]], and the low prices paid for rubber and the highest [[interest rate]] on loans for collecting nuts and rubber.<ref name="Souza">TEIXEIRA, M. [http://aurbe.blogspot.com.br/2013/10/maraba-100-anos-declacao-de-emancipacao.html Marab√° 100 anos: A revolta dos galegos e a declara√ß√£o de emancipa√ß√£o] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402113215/http://aurbe.blogspot.com.br/2013/10/maraba-100-anos-declacao-de-emancipacao.html |date=2015-04-02 }}. Blog A Urbe, 2013</ref>

Retaliating against the rebellion of Maraba the government of the province of Gr√£o-Par√° does not meet demands by emancipation. However the enormous pressure and influence of the local society has made the provincial government gave in and finally initiate negotiations for the creation of the municipality of Marab√°.<ref name="Souza"/>

That culminated on February 27, 1913 Maraba achieved political autonomy and became a municipality. The first mayor of Maraba was the military Antonio Maia, who took office as appointed by the President of the Province of Gr√£o-Par√°.<ref name="Souza"/>

In subsequent years the migratory flow to the region of Marab√° increased considerably. During the 1920s, immigrants moved to Maraba mainly to participate in the extraction and sale of Brazil nuts, [[andiroba]], [[Copaifera langsdorffii|copaiba oils]] and rubber. And from the 1930s moved to Maraba, in order to work in the diamond mines that were on the banks of the Tocantins River.<ref name="sobre marab√° pmm"/>

In 1929, Marab√° becomes illuminated by a power plant that operated the base from burning wood. In November 1935 the local airport is opened and the first plane lands in this. Maraba in this period consists of 450 houses and 1.500 inhabitants fixed.<ref name="sobre marab√° pmm"/>

=== The 1970s ===
With the construction of Highway PA-70, in 1969, Maraba is connected to the [[Bel√©m-Bras√≠lia Highway]]. The implementation of road infrastructure was part of the [[Brazilian military government]]'s strategy to integrate the [[Amazon region]] to the rest of the country. All this was part of the integration strategy of the Amazon territory, especially the region of Marab√°, where was undertaken mainly the official plan of agricultural colonization, the construction of the [[Tucuru√≠ Dam|Tucuru√≠ Hydroelectric Power]], the implementation of "Projeto Grande Caraj√°s" ([[Greater Carajas Project]]), and even the discovery of large gold deposits of the [[Serra Pelada]]. All these projects were centered in Marab√°, and contributed to the economic and population boom that occurred in the city between 1970 and 1980.<ref name="Idelma">DA SILVA, Idelma Santiago. [http://www.ufg.br/this2/uploads/files/112/Idelma_Santiago_da_Silva.pdf Migra√ß√£o e Cultura no Sudeste do Par√°]{{dead link|date=June 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} - Universidade Federal de Goi√°s, 2010</ref>

In 1970 the city was declared a National Security Area (legal position allowing direct intervention of the Brazilian central government), a condition that lasted until the end of the Brazilian military dictatorship in 1985.<ref name="sobre marab√° pmm"/> Besides having strategic importance to the policy of national integration, Marab√° region was the only region of Brazil where there was fighting between the army and the [[PCdoB#The Araguaia Guerrilla .281969-1976.29|guerrillas of the PCdoB party]] who want to topple the military regime and establish a communist regime in Brazil. Then performed an [[Araguaia guerrilla]] movement, which forced the Brazilian government to Marab√° to send a large contingent of troops of the [[Brazilian Army]]. The city has become since then one of the bases for military operations troops of the [[Brazilian Armed Forces]].<ref>NASCIMENTO, Durbens. [http://www.obed.ufpa.br/pdfs/dissertacao_guerrilha_do_araguaia.pdf A Guerrilha do Araguaia: Paulistas e Militares na Amaz√¥nia] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120106013951/http://www.obed.ufpa.br/pdfs/dissertacao_guerrilha_do_araguaia.pdf |date=2012-01-06 }} - Universidade Federal do Par√°</ref>

In 1970 it launched the "Programa de Integra√ß√£o Nacional" (National Integration Program), which, among other things, provided for the construction of the [[Trans-Amazon Highway]], whose first section opened in 1971, along with the creation of a post of the [[INCRA|National Institute of Colonization and Agrarian Reform]] in Marab√°.<ref name="Idelma"/> With the completion of construction of the Trans-Amazon Highway, the flow of immigrants towards Maraba became very large, and in less than a decade the city has been radically transformed, from a small urban center on the banks of the Tocantins River (in Maraba time had only 20,000 inhabitants), a main city in the region with over 100,000 inhabitants.<ref name="pin rodovias">[http://www.usp.br/fau/cursos/graduacao/arq_urbanismo/disciplinas/aup0270/6t-alun/2010/m10/10-loureiro.pdf O Plano de Integra√ß√£o Nacional de 1970 e as rodovias na Amaz√¥nia] - FAU/Universidade de S√£o Paulo</ref>

=== The 1980s ===
In 1980 the city was devastated by the biggest flood in its history, when the Tocantins River rose 17.42 meters. As a result, there was a redesign of the city's expansion, with the construction of urban areas planned of the "Cidade Nova" (New City) and "Nova Marab√°" (New Marab√°),  to the detriment of traditional urban area of the "Velha Marab√°" (Old Marab√°). In 1984, comes into operation the [[Caraj√°s Railway]], which has Marab√° as center of operations; in 1988 and come into operation the first two [[steel mill]]s for the production of [[pig iron]].<ref name="beneficiosgusa">[http://www.sindiferpa.com.br/polo/polo20080506.pdf Sider√∫rgicas aquecem economia regional] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121029104830/http://www.sindiferpa.com.br/polo/polo20080506.pdf |date=2012-10-29 }} - Revista P√≥lo Sustent√°vel</ref>

== Geography ==

Occupying an area of 15,092,268&nbsp;km¬≤, Marab√° counts, in 2017, with 271,594 inhabitants, being the tenth most populous municipality of the North region of Brazil. The municipal seat has the following geographical coordinates: 05¬∫ 21 '54 "South latitude and 049¬∫ 07' 24" longitude WGr. Located in southeastern Par√°, in the Marab√° microregion, it is bordered by the municipalities of: [[Novo Repartimento]], [[Itupiranga]], [[Nova Ipixuna]] and [[Rondon do Par√°]] (to the north); [[S√£o Geraldo do Araguaia]], [[Eldorado dos Caraj√°s]], [[Curion√≥polis]] and [[Parauapebas]] (to the south); [[Bom Jesus do Tocantins, Par√°|Bom Jesus do Tocantins]], [[S√£o Jo√£o do Araguaia]] and [[S√£o Domingos do Araguaia]] (to the east); and [[S√£o F√©lix do Xingu]] (to the west).

The topography of the municipality of Marab√° presents the highest altitudes in the Southeast region of Par√°, through the [[Caraj√°s Mountains|Caraj√°s]], Sereno, Buritirama, Pared√£o, Encontro, Gray and Misteriosa mountains. Of this complex, stands out the Caraj√°s mountain range, like the one of greater size. However, it is in the Serra do Cinzento that the maximum altitude of the municipality of Marab√° is found, with 792 meters. The Caraj√°s, Cinzento and Buritirana mountains are located in conservation areas, under federal jurisdiction, called the [[Tapirap√©-Aquiri National Forest]] ( {{convert|196,504|ha}} sustainable use conservation unit created in 1989.<ref>{{citation|ref={{harvid|FLONA do Tapirap√©-Aquiri ‚Äì ISA}}|language=pt|title=FLONA do Tapirap√©-Aquiri|publisher=ISA: Instituto Socioambiental |url=https://uc.socioambiental.org/uc/6634|access-date=2016-05-30}}</ref>) and the [[Tapirap√© Biological Reserve]], where several caves are found. Its forms of relief are encompassed by the morphostructural unit denominated Peripheral Depression of the South of Par√°, where they dominate the amazonian plateaus.

The vegetation cover of the municipality of Marab√° is quite diverse. The fitofisionomy of the forests of the municipality of Marab√° is characterized by three types: the open ombrophylous forest, the dense ombrophylous forest and the anthropic areas. In the urban area of Marab√°, the anthropic forests predominate. Because of its diverse nature, the municipality holds one of the largest natural heritage sites in Brazil, housing large forest reserves such as the [[Tapirap√© Biological Reserve]], with 103,000 ha (1,030&nbsp;km¬≤), and the [[Tapirap√©-Aquiri National Forest]] with 190,000 hectares ha (1,900&nbsp;km¬≤), in addition to the M√£e Maria Indigenous Land, with 64,488,416 ha (644.88&nbsp;km¬≤), which is close to the municipal seat of Marab√°, belonging to the municipality of Bom Jesus do Tocantins.

Marab√° is located in a low altitude area in the connexion of two rivers - [[Itacai√∫nas River|Itacai√∫nas]] and [[Tocantins River|Tocantins]] - and suffers from annual floods due to the topography and direct influence of four rivers: Itacaiunas, Tocantins, Tauarizinho and [[Soror√≥ River|Soror√≥]]. In addition to the basins related to these rivers, the municipality is inserted in the basins of the rivers Aquiri, Tapirape, Gray, Black, Parauapebas and Red. Of these, the basins of the Tapirap√©, Cinzento and Preto rivers are included totally in the area of the municipality. The basin of Itacai√∫nas stands out for bathing the whole municipality, in whose mouth is the municipal headquarters of Marab√° and covers the largest area, that is, 5,383.4&nbsp;km¬≤.

== Economy ==
{{Unreferenced section|date=March 2023}}
[[File:Jardins da Orla do Tocantins, em Marab√°, em 2011.jpg|right|thumb|200px|trade in Maraba.]]
The municipality of Maraba experienced various economic cycles. Until the early 1980s economy was based on the extraction plant. At first revolved around the extraction of [[latex rubber]], whose lucrative attracted large numbers of the [[Northeast Region, Brazil|Northeast]]. Since the late nineteenth century (1892) until the late 1940s, was marked by the extraction of the [[rubber boom]] that has contributed greatly to the economy of the city and the region, however, the rubber crisis led the council to a new cycle . This time, the cycle of Brazil nuts, which for years led the municipal economy. There was also the cycle of diamonds between 1920 and 1940, which were mainly found on the river Tocantins. With the discovery of [[Serra Pelada]] Maraba went through cycles of  mining mostly the extraction of gold.

Since the early 1970s installation of the Grande Caraj√°s project and steel industries diversified and boosted the local economy.

=== Primary sector ===
Today, Maraba is the economic center and a vast administrative region of the Amazon agricultural frontier ", the city has one of the most significant economic growth in the country. Livestock based on cattle ranching is an activity of great importance for the municipality, and ensure the livelihoods of the population, provides the regional and local development by creating large scale, and is marketed in different Brazilian regions and abroad. The herd area is highlighted by its superior quality, being one of the most important cattle herds of the state, a result arising from the use of advanced technology in the selection and fertilization. It has also herds of pigs, horses, sheep and poultry

The fishing sector also has a key role in local economic base by exporting its surplus for the entire north and northeast. Agriculture is diversified, with production cereals, pulses and oilseeds such as corn, rice and beans, fruits such as bananas and [[acai]], and logging

=== Secondary sector ===
[[File:SteelMill interior.jpg|right|thumb|200px|Inside a steel plant.]]
Through the Industrial Development Company of Para - CDI, was installed at the end of the eighties, in an area of 1,300 hectares, the industrial district of Maraba - DIM, to create the base of a steel pole targeting the Carajas iron ore, exploited by the mining company Vale.

Metallurgical Industries and intense livestock activity, accounted for a large environmental devastation in the region. The activity of the [[steel industry]] requires large amounts of coal, leading to a devastation of native forests. As a result of public pressure the industries were forced to change its production model, investing in reforestation and charcoal production through the babassu coconut palm.

Besides, to have more than 200 industries, and the steel (pig iron) most important, second is the timber industry and the manufacture of tiles and bricks. The city's economy also relies on the production of manganese and [[Agribusiness]]. In Maraba, Agribusiness works with pulp processing, [[cassava]] flour, rice processing, milk and [[Palm (plant)|palm]].

The installation of steel plant came boost the local economy even more, forming a metal pole-mechanical, with a view to verticalize the local mineral production. There are still projects that seen during and after the installation of steel works, among them: The [[gas pipeline]] A√ßail√¢ndia-Maraba and construction of new port city.

=== Education ===
Marab√° counts on schools in practically all the regions of the municipality, however, it is far from being among its best indicators.
