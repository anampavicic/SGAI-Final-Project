'''Garoua''' (also '''Garua'''; Fula: ðž¤ºðž¤¢ðž¤ªðž¤±ðž¤¢, Garwa) is a port city and the capital of the [[North Region (Cameroon)|North Region]] of [[Cameroon]], lying on the [[Benue River]]. A thriving centre of the textiles and cotton industries, the city has approximately 1,285,000 inhabitants in 2020, mostly Fulbe/Fulani people.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://worldpopulationreview.com/countries/cameroon-population/cities/|title=Population of Cities in Cameroon (2018)|website=worldpopulationreview.com|access-date=2018-09-06}}</ref>

==History==
Garoua was established by the [[Fulani]] emir [[Modibbo Adama]] in the first half of the 19th century.  During the steamship era, it developed into a major river port.<ref name="EB">{{cite encyclopedia|url=https://www.britannica.com/place/Garoua|title=Garoua|encyclopedia=EncyclopÃ¦dia Britannica|access-date=29 October 2016}}</ref>

The population of the city was 30,000 in 1967.<ref>Mark Dike DeLancey, Rebecca Neh Mbuh, Mark W. Delancey, ''Historical Dictionary of the Republic of Cameroon'', Scarecrow Press, USA, 2010, p. 189</ref>

==Geography==
Garoua is situated in northern Cameroon, and lies on the [[Benue River]].<ref name="GM"/> It serves as the gateway to [[Benoue National Park]].<ref name="Ham2009">{{cite book|last=Ham|first=Anthony|title=West Africa|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=1HapewBo3c0C&pg=PA207|year=2009|publisher=Lonely Planet|isbn=978-1-74104-821-6|page=207}}</ref> Neighborhoods include Commercial Centre, Lopere, Quartier de Marouare, Poumpoumre, Roumde Adjia and the northwestern suburb of Yelwa, near Garoua International Airport.<ref name="GM">{{Google maps | url =https://www.google.com/maps/@9.3280221,13.4041372,3258m/data=!3m1!1e3?hl=en  | access-date =29 October 2016}}</ref>

==Climate==
Garoua has a [[tropical savanna climate]] ([[KÃ¶ppen climate classification|KÃ¶ppen]] ''Aw''), with a wet season and a dry season and the temperature being hot year-round. The average temperature in Garoua ranges from {{convert|26.0|C|F|abbr=on}} in December and January, the coolest months, to {{convert|33.0|C|F|abbr=on}} in April, the hottest month. The hottest time of year is in March and April, just before the wet season starts. March has the highest average high at {{convert|39.8|C|F|abbr=on}}, while the highest average low is {{convert|26.4|C|F|abbr=on}} in April. August has the lowest average high at {{convert|30.7|C|F|abbr=on}}, while December has the lowest average low at {{convert|17.3|C|F|abbr=on}}.

Garoua receives {{convert|997.4|mm}} of rain over 88 precipitation days, with a distinct wet and dry season like most tropical savanna climates. December, January and February receive no precipitation at all. August, the wettest month, receives {{convert|247.9|mm}} of rainfall on average. September has 24 precipitation days, which is the most of any month. Garoua receives 2927.1 hours of sunshine annually on average, with the sunshine being distributed evenly across the year, although it is lower during the wet season.

==Demographics==
In 2005, Garoua had a population of 495,996 people. The city is inhabited by the [[Fulbe]] ethnic group.<ref name="DrÃ¸nen2009">{{cite book|last=DrÃ¸nen|first=Tomas Sundnes|title=Communication and Conversion in Northern Cameroon: The Dii People and Norwegian Missionaries, 1934-1960|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=PT-xCQAAQBAJ&pg=PA40|date=31 August 2009|publisher=BRILL|isbn=978-90-474-3098-8|page=40}}</ref> Due to a significant number of Chadian expatriates in the city, there is a Chadian Consulate here.<ref name="Ham2009"/>

==Economy==
As the fourth largest port in Cameroon,<ref name="Trillo2008">{{cite book|last=Trillo|first=Richard|title=The Rough Guide to West Africa|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=clU65BAfOmUC&pg=PT3028|date=2 June 2008|publisher=Rough Guides Limited|isbn=978-1-4053-8068-3|page=3028}}</ref> Garoua is a major center of trade in the country.<ref name="Publications2005">{{cite book|title=Georgia (Republic) Export-Import Trade and Business Directory|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=qlArJ3yV4rUC&pg=PA97|date=October 2005|publisher=International Business Publications|isbn=978-0-7397-3267-0|page=97}}</ref> It developed as a centre for trade in petroleum, cement, hides, peanuts, and cotton, transported on ships along the Benue River, between [[Burutu]] in Nigeria.<ref name="EB"/> It has long been a thriving centre of the cotton industry and houses several [[textile]] processing facilities and factories.<ref name="Ndenecho2011">{{cite book|last=Ndenecho|first=Emmanuel Neba|title=Decentralisation and Spatial Rural Development Planning in Cameroon|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=6KkL0YxFxPsC&pg=PA76|year=2011|publisher=African Books Collective|isbn=978-9956-717-66-8|page=76}}</ref> The cotton company [[Sodecoton]] has one of its major centres in the city.<ref name="Raimond2013">{{cite book|last=Raimond|first=Christine|title=Ressources vivriÃ¨res et choix alimentaires dans le bassin du lac Tchad|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=-B4VObZQ11gC&pg=PA202|date=8 October 2013|publisher=IRD Editions|isbn=978-2-7099-1576-2|page=202}}</ref> Leatherwork and fishing are also notable industries.<ref name="EB"/>
