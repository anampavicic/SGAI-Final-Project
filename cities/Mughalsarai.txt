'''Mughalsarai''' ({{IPAc-en|ˌ|m|uː|g|h|æ|l|'|s|ɑːr|eɪ|ɪ}}; [[English language|English]]: ''[[Mughal tribe|Mughal]] [[Tavern]]''<ref name="Sarai2">{{cite web|title=Roads and Sarais of Sher Shah Suri|url=http://www.barmazid.com/2016/10/roads-and-sarais-inns-of-sher-shah-suri.html|website=www.barmazid.com|access-date=31 December 2018}}</ref>), officially known as '''Pandit Deen Dayal Upadhyaya Nagar''',<ref>{{cite news |last1=Dikshit |first1=Rajeev |title=Mughalsarai town becomes Pt Deen Dayal Upadhyaya Nagar |url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/varanasi/mughalsarai-town-becomes-pt-deen-dayal-upadhyaya-nagar/articleshow/60518236.cms |access-date=4 July 2023 |work=The Times of India |date=14 September 2017}}</ref> is a city and a [[municipal board]] in the [[Chandauli district]] of [[Uttar Pradesh]]. Located around {{convert|16|km|miles|0|abbr=}} from [[Varanasi]], it is an important [[Mughalsarai Junction railway station|railway junction]].<ref>{{cite book|title=The National Geographical Journal of India, Volume 10|year=1964|publisher=National Geographical Society of India|language=en|page=136|quote=Its importance as a railway junction can well be understood by the fact that more than 50 passenger trains and over 150 goods trains pass through it within 24 hours. The amount of coal passing through Mughalsarai was about 39,000 tons daily in 1960 and the wagons carrying coal from this end to other stations numbered 1,729 per day.}}</ref>

==History==
Mughalsarai is located along the [[Grand Trunk Road]] ([[National Highway 19 (India)|NH 19]]), also called Sadak-e-Azam by [[Sher Shah Suri]], was one of the corridors connecting [[North India]] with the east during the [[Mughal period]]. In past centuries, it has been variously known as Mughalchak, Mangalpur and Oven Nagar. The township was named Mughalsarai when Indian railways established a [[Junction (rail)|junction]] here in 1883.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Dikshit |first1=Rajeev |title=The many names of Mughalsarai |url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/varanasi/the-many-names-of-mughalsarai/articleshow/59924547.cms |access-date=4 July 2023 |work=The Times of India |date=5 August 2017}}</ref>

In August 2018, the city was renamed as ''Pandit Deen Dayal Upadhyaya Nagar'', after [[Deendayal Upadhyaya]], a politician and leader of the [[Bharatiya Jana Sangh]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Raghuvanshi |first1=Umesh |title=After Allahabad became Prayagraj, clamour grows for name changes in UP |url=https://www.hindustantimes.com/india-news/after-allahabad-became-prayagraj-clamour-grows-for-more-name-changes-in-up/story-lHZlrH5ch8n9X31aVXVDcP.html |access-date=4 July 2023 |work=Hindustan Times |date=27 October 2018}}</ref>

==Geography==
Mughalsarai is bisected by NH 19 into three parts. The southern part mainly consists of railway colonies like Vasant Vihar, New Central Colony, Diesel Colony, Haper Colony, European colony, Shubhash Nagar, Loco Colony, and Roza Colony, Plant-Depot Colony and Saresar, Alampur. It starts after the Parao Road in Varanasi and continues until Alinagar Road which comes after Railway Colony on the south. A few mohallas are near the railway station like Qassab Mohal, and Gram Panchayats like Amoghpur, Taranpur, Pashurampur, Dharamshala, Mainatali and Galla Mandi. However, situated on the west side are the Kailashpuri and Ravinagar colonies which compose the poshest area in the city.

==Demographics==
As of [[2011 Indian Census]], Mughalsarai had a total population of 109,650, of which 57,682 were males and 51,968 were females. Population within the age group of 0 to 6 years was 14,864. The total number of literates in Mughalsarai was 76,936, which constituted 70.2% of the population with male literacy of 76.0% and female literacy of 63.7%. The effective literacy rate of 7+ population of Mughalsarai was 81.2%, of which male literacy rate was 87.9% and female literacy rate was 73.7%. The [[Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes]] population was 17,943 and 2,093 respectively. Mughalsarai had 16,796 households in 2011.<ref name="Census2011Gov">{{cite web |title=Census of India: Mughalsarai |url=http://www.censusindia.gov.in/pca/SearchDetails.aspx?Id=235717 |website=www.censusindia.gov.in |access-date=20 December 2019}}</ref>
