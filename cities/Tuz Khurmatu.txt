== Etymology ==
The name of the city is in the local [[Iraqi Turkmen#Language|Iraqi Turkmen dialect]], meaning ''salt and dates''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Iraq's Tuz Khurmatu: A town rich in history and conflict {{!}} Nermeen Mufti|url=https://thearabweekly.com/iraqs-tuz-khurmatu-town-rich-history-and-conflict|access-date=2020-10-07|website=AW|language=en|archive-date=2020-10-08|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201008181650/https://thearabweekly.com/iraqs-tuz-khurmatu-town-rich-history-and-conflict|url-status=live}}</ref>

== History ==
[[File:Turkish army irak.jpg|250px|thumb|right|A U.S. Army Soldier and a [[Turkish Air Force]] member transport an Iraqi child to safety during a multinational humanitarian airlift effort on [[Kirkuk Air Base]], Iraq, July 8, 2007. The victim was hurt in an attack in Tuz Khurmatu.]]

[[Naphtha]], oil and [[Bitumen|asphalt]] was found in the town in the 18th century.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Rasoul|first=Rasoul Muhammed|date=2017|title=History of Kirkuk from the Beginning of the Nineteenth Century until Becoming Part of the Iraqi Monarchy in 1925|url=https://core.ac.uk/download/pdf/224744195.pdf|publisher=[[University of Erfurt]]|pages=188|journal=|access-date=2020-11-15|archive-date=2020-10-26|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201026215908/https://core.ac.uk/download/pdf/224744195.pdf|url-status=live}}</ref>

The city was populated by both [[Kurds]] and [[Iraqi Turkmen|Turkmens]] in the 19th century. [[Claudius Rich]] visited the town in 1820 and stated that the town had a population of 50,000. In 1882 Major General Gerard visited the town and stated that the town had a [[bazaar]], 300 houses, 100 regulars and 30 ''zaptiyehs.''<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Rasoul|first=Rasoul Muhammed|date=2017|title=History of Kirkuk from the Beginning of the Nineteenth Century until Becoming Part of the Iraqi Monarchy in 1925|url=https://core.ac.uk/download/pdf/224744195.pdf|publisher=[[University of Erfurt]]|pages=8|journal=|access-date=2020-11-15|archive-date=2020-10-26|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201026215908/https://core.ac.uk/download/pdf/224744195.pdf|url-status=live}}</ref> The town was captured by [[United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland|United Kingdom]] in May 1918 and were met with joy from the locals. The local [[Hamawand (tribe)|Hamawand]] tribe would offer their assistance to secure the area.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Rasoul|first=Rasoul Muhammed|date=2017|title=History of Kirkuk from the Beginning of the Nineteenth Century until Becoming Part of the Iraqi Monarchy in 1925|url=https://core.ac.uk/download/pdf/224744195.pdf|publisher=[[University of Erfurt]]|pages=137|journal=|access-date=2020-11-15|archive-date=2020-10-26|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201026215908/https://core.ac.uk/download/pdf/224744195.pdf|url-status=live}}</ref>

In 1925, Tuz Khurmatu’s population was entirely [[Iraqi Turkmen|Turkmen]], except for some Jewish families (35 out of 405 families).<ref>{{Cite web|date=20 August 1925|title=Question of the Frontier between Turkey and Iraq - League of Nations|url=https://biblio-archive.unog.ch/Dateien/CouncilMSD/C-400-M-147-1925-VII_BI.pdf|access-date=14 December 2020|archive-date=10 January 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180110175408/https://biblio-archive.unog.ch/Dateien/CouncilMSD/C-400-M-147-1925-VII_BI.pdf|url-status=live}}</ref>

40% of the population was [[Kurds|Kurdish]] in the 1947 census.<ref>{{cite book|author1=C. J. Edmonds|page=438|title=Kurds, Turks and Arabs, Politics, Travel and Research in North-Eastern Iraq, 1919-1925|date=1957|publisher=Oxford University Press|access-date=17 November 2019|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Qm26AAAAIAAJ|archive-date=13 October 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211013230416/https://books.google.com/books?id=Qm26AAAAIAAJ|url-status=live}}</ref>

In 1976, [[Tooz District]] was separated from [[Kirkuk Governorate]] and attached to [[Saladin Governorate]] for [[Arabization]] purposes. The population of the town was 75,737 the subsequent year, and decreased to 51,998 in 1987.<ref>{{citation|last=Ihsan|first=Mohammad|title=Administrative Changes in Kirkuk and Disputed Areas in Iraq 1968-2003|pages=26–27}}</ref> The town participated in the [[1991 Iraqi uprising]] before being suppressed by the [[Ba'athist Iraq]]i army.<ref>{{cite book |last=Goldstein |first=Eric |date=June 1992 |title=Endless Torment: The 1991 Uprising in Iraq and Its Aftermath |url=https://www.hrw.org/reports/1992/Iraq926.htm |location=U.S. |publisher=Human Rights Watch |page=58 |isbn=1-56432-069-3 |access-date=2016-01-12 |archive-date=2010-06-15 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100615171955/http://www.hrw.org/reports/1992/Iraq926.htm |url-status=live }}</ref>

=== Operation Iraqi Freedom ===
* On June 2, 2005, at least 12 people were killed and at least 40 wounded in an explosion targeting a restaurant.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/Iraq/Story/0,,1497737,00.html|title=20 dead in Iraq bombings|work=The Guardian|date=2 June 2005 |access-date=15 May 2016|archive-date=13 October 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211013230418/https://www.theguardian.com/world/2005/jun/02/iraq1|url-status=live}}</ref>
* On June 23, 2005,  a car bomb detonated by remote control hit an Iraqi police patrol, killing one policeman and wounding 7 civilians.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.iht.com/articles/2005/06/23/africa/web.0623iraq.php |title=Car bombs kill nearly 40 people in Baghdad – ''International Herald Tribune''<!-- Bot generated title --> |access-date=2007-03-14 |archive-date=2007-05-21 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070521015417/http://www.iht.com/articles/2005/06/23/africa/web.0623iraq.php |url-status=live }}</ref>
* On September 20, 2005, insurgents detonated a car bomb targeting Shiite worshippers as they were exiting the Hussainiyat al-Rasoul al-Azam mosque, killing at least 10 and wounding 21 others.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.defendamerica.mil/downloads/eyeoniraq220_20050920.pdf |title=Microsoft PowerPoint – Eye on Iraq Sep 20, 2005 – English<!-- Bot generated title --> |access-date=March 14, 2007 |archive-date=March 15, 2007 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070315110217/http://defendamerica.mil/downloads/eyeoniraq220_20050920.pdf |url-status=live }}</ref>
* On March 14, 2007, a [[suicide bombing|suicide bomber]] struck a market and killing 8 and wounding 25.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://news.yahoo.com/s/ap/20070314/ap_on_re_mi_ea/iraq |title=Iraqi president returns after treatment - Yahoo! === Operation New Dawn ===
* On September 7, 2010, the first US casualties after President [[Barack Obama]] declared an end to US combat operations took place near the city when Iraqi insurgents killed 2 US military personnel.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://news.yahoo.com/s/ap/20100907/ap_on_re_mi_ea/ml_iraq_us_casualties |title=Iraqi soldier fires on US troops, kills 2 |access-date=2017-01-15 |archive-date=2010-09-14 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100914105956/http://news.yahoo.com/s/ap/20100907/ap_on_re_mi_ea/ml_iraq_us_casualties |url-status=live }}</ref>

=== Post-U.S. withdrawal and Iraqi Civil War ===
* On October 27, 2012, a car bombing next to a building owned by a [[Shi'ite]] [[Financial endowment|endowment]] killed two civilians and injured ten others.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.ninanews.com/english/News_Details.asp?ar95_VQ=GEFGKI |title=Killing and wounding 12 civilians east of Tikrit (NINA News Agency) |access-date=2013-01-24 |archive-date=2012-10-31 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121031081148/http://www.ninanews.com/english/News_Details.asp?ar95_VQ=GEFGKI |url-status=live }}</ref>
* On December 17, 2012, two consecutive car bombings hit a residential area near the city's General Hospital, killing 11 civilians and injuring 45 others. The attacks were part of a country-wide wave of violence that killed almost 100 people in a single day.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.ninanews.com/english/News_Details.asp?ar95_VQ=GFEDDH |title=BREAKING NEWS. 25 people killed and wounded in bombings series in Tuz district. * On January 16, 2013, a suicide car bombing at the offices of the [[Kurdistan Democratic Party]] killed 5 and injured 40 others.<ref>{{cite web |agency=Reuters |url=http://www.trust.org/alertnet/news/bombers-kill-more-than-35-across-iraq/ |title=Bombers kill more than 35 across Iraq |publisher=Trust.org |date=2013-01-15 |access-date=2013-01-15 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130221164850/http://www.trust.org/alertnet/news/bombers-kill-more-than-35-across-iraq/ |archive-date=2013-02-21 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |author=Margaret Griffis |url=http://original.antiwar.com/updates/2013/01/16/iraq-slaughter-55-killed-288-wounded/ |title=Iraq Slaughter: 55 Killed, 288 Wounded |publisher=Antiwar.com |date=2013-01-16 |access-date=2013-01-24 |archive-date=2013-01-21 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130121023625/http://original.antiwar.com/updates/2013/01/16/iraq-slaughter-55-killed-288-wounded/ |url-status=live }}</ref>
* On January 23, 2013, a suicide bomber blew himself up during a funeral for a politician's relative in the city, killing 42 and leaving 75 others wounded.<ref>{{cite web |author=Marwan Ibrahim |url=https://www.google.com/hostednews/afp/article/ALeqM5jQy57ea_bmclo1OnOLDPWxnw_3vg?docId=CNG.c8e0480540a829085a3a9eee42c89e47.361 |title=Iraq suicide bomb at Shiite mosque kills 42 |date=2013-01-23 |access-date=2013-01-24 |archive-date=2013-02-16 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130216041843/http://www.google.com/hostednews/afp/article/ALeqM5jQy57ea_bmclo1OnOLDPWxnw_3vg?docId=CNG.c8e0480540a829085a3a9eee42c89e47.361 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |author=Margaret Griffis |url=http://original.antiwar.com/updates/2013/01/23/at-least-51-killed-98-wounded-in-iraq-attacks/ |title=At least 51 Killed, 98 Wounded in Iraq Attacks |publisher=Antiwar.com |date=2013-01-23 |access-date=2013-01-24 |archive-date=2020-05-04 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200504220558/http://original.antiwar.com/updates/2013/01/23/at-least-51-killed-98-wounded-in-iraq-attacks/ |url-status=live }}</ref>
