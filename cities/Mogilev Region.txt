'''Mogilev Region''', also known as '''Mogilev Oblast'''{{efn|{{langx|ru|Могилёвская область|Mogilyovskaya oblast'}}.}} or '''Mahilyow Voblasts''',{{efn|{{langx|be|Магілёўская вобласць|Mahilioŭskaja voblasć}}.}} is one of the [[regions of Belarus]]. Its administrative center is the city of [[Mogilev]].

Important cities within the region include [[Mogilev]], [[Asipovichy]] and [[Babruysk]].

==Geography==
Within Belarus, Mogilev Region borders [[Vitebsk Region]] (to the north), [[Minsk Region]] (to the west), and [[Gomel Region]] (to the south). It has international borders with the [[Russian Federation]] ([[Smolensk Oblast]] to the east and [[Bryansk Oblast]] to the south-east).

Mogilev Region covers a total area of {{convert|29100|km2|sqmi}},<ref name=statistics>{{cite web |url = http://landofancestors.com/travel/statistics/geography/236-territory-and-population-density-by-region-as-of-january-1-2011.html |title = Main Geographic Characteristics of the Republic of Belarus. Territory and population density of Belarus by region as of January 1, 2011 |publisher = The Scientific and Production State Republican Unitary Enterprise "National Cadastre Agency" of the State Property Committee of the Republic of Belarus | year = 2011 | website = Land of Ancestors | access-date = 24 September 2013 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20130927131905/http://landofancestors.com/travel/statistics/geography/236-territory-and-population-density-by-region-as-of-january-1-2011.html | archive-date = 27 September 2013 | url-status = dead }}</ref> about 14% of the national total. The region's greatest extent from north to south comprises {{convert|150|km|mi}}, from east to west – {{convert|300|km|mi}}, while the highest point is {{convert|239|m|ft}} [[Above mean sea level| above sea level]] and the lowest at {{convert|126|m|ft}} above sea level.

Many rivers flow through the Mogilev Region, including the [[Dnieper River| Dnieper]] (Dniapro), [[Berezina River| Berezina]], [[Sozh River |Sozh]], [[Druts River |Druts]], [[Pronia]] and [[Ptsich]]. The oblast also has small lakes, the largest being the Zaozerye Lake with a surface area of {{convert|0.58|km2|sqmi}}. The {{Ill|Chigirin Reservoir (Belarus)|lt=Chigirin Reservoir|ru|Чигиринское_водохранилище_(Белоруссия)}} on the Druts River has an area of {{convert|21.1|km2|sqmi}}.

The extreme eastern point of Belarus is situated within the Mogilev Region to the east of the [[Khotsimsk Raion| Khotimsk District]].<ref>{{cite web|url= http://landofancestors.com/travel/statistics/geography/235-coordinates-of-the-extreme-points-of-the-state-frontier.html |title = Main Geographic Characteristics of the Republic of Belarus |publisher = The Scientific and Production State Republican Unitary Enterprise "National Cadastre Agency" of the State Property Committee of the Republic of Belarus | year = 2011 | website = Land of Ancestors | access-date = 20 September 2013 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20130921053717/http://landofancestors.com/travel/statistics/geography/235-coordinates-of-the-extreme-points-of-the-state-frontier.html |archive-date = 21 September 2013 |url-status = dead }}</ref>

===Climate===
Mogilev Region has a temperate continental climate, with cold winters and warm summers. January's average temperature ranges from {{convert|-8.2|°C|°F|abbr=on}} in the northeast to {{convert|-6.5|°C|°F|abbr=on}} in the southwest. July's average temperature ranges from {{convert|17.8|°C|°F|abbr=on}} in the northeast to {{convert|18.7|°C|°F|abbr=on}} in the southwest. The region's average yearly vegetative period lasts around 183–194 days. The average [[Precipitation (meteorology)| precipitation]] is {{convert|575|-|675|mm|in}} a year, with approximately 70% falling during the warm season (April–October).{{citation needed|date=September 2022}}

==Demographics==
With a total population of 1,088,100 (2011),<ref name=statistics /> 353,600 inhabitants live in rural areas and 855,000 live in cities or towns. There are 639,300 women and 567,300 men in the region, of which 288,100 are under 18 while 267,300 are elderly people.

Of the major nationalities living in the Mogilev Region, 1,044,000 inhabitants are Belarusians, 132,000 are Russians, 3,500 are [[Jews|Jewish]], 2,800 are [[Polish people|Poles]], 2,110 are [[Ukrainians]], 1,700 are [[Tatars]], 1,300 are [[Lithuanians]], 1,100 are [[Armenians]], and 1,070 are [[Romani people|Romani]].
