'''Dili Municipality''' ({{Langx|pt|Município Díli}}, {{Langx|tet|Munisípiu Dili}}) is one of the 14 [[Municipalities of East Timor|municipalities]], formerly districts, of [[Timor-Leste]], and includes the national capital [[Dili]]. The municipality had a population of 277,279 {{as of|2015|lc=y}},<ref>{{cite web |title=2015 Timor-Leste Census |url=http://www.statistics.gov.tl/wp-content/uploads/2016/11/1_2015-V2-Population-Household-Distribution.xls |website=Statistics Timor-Leste}}</ref> most of whom live in the capital city. 
==Etymology==
Several explanations have been proffered for the origin of the municipality's name. It is often explained, including by the municipality's own website, as being derived from the [[Tetum language|Tetum]] word {{lang|tet|ai-dila}}, which means [[Papaya|pawpaw]].<ref name="hull 2006-06">{{cite journal |last1=Hull |first1=Geoffrey |authorlink1=Geoffrey Hull |title=The placenames of East Timor |journal=Placenames Australia: Newsletter of the Australian National Placenames Survey |date=June 2006 |pages=6–7 |url=http://www.anps.org.au/documents/June_2006.pdf |accessdate=4 June 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170214220045/http://www.anps.org.au/documents/June_2006.pdf |archive-date=14 February 2017 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref name="di toponymy">{{cite web |title=Perfil: 2. Toponímia |trans-title=Profile: 2. Toponymy |url=https://dili.gov.tl/ |website=Dili Municipality |access-date=18 July 2022 |language=tet |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220717085927/https://dili.gov.tl/ |archive-date=17 July 2022 |url-status=dead }}</ref> According to Australian [[linguistics|linguist]], [[ethnology|ethnologist]] and [[history|historian]] [[Geoffrey Hull]], however, that explanation is [[Phonology|phonologically]] and historically implausible, as the language spoken in the area now known as Dili before the Portuguese established their seat of administration there in 1769 was [[Mambai language (Timor)|Mambai]], not Tetum. Hull describes such explanations as "folk etymology".<ref name="hull 2006-06"/>

In Hull's view, the word Dili appears to be cognate with the [[Bunak language|Bunak]] word ''zili'' ({{translation|'cliff'}}), a reference to the escarpment behind the city; he comments that a Papuan language, of which Bunak is an example, was spoken in the Dili area before the spread of the [[Austronesian languages|Austronesian]]-based Mambai and Tetum languages.<ref name="hull 2006-06"/>

Another "folk etymology" explanation suggested by the municipality is that its name is derived from the Portuguese word {{lang|pt|Dali}}, which, in turn, is an approximation of the Tetum expression {{lang|tet|tali akar}} ({{translation|'[[hemp rope]]'}}). According to the municipality, there were lots of [[Hemp|hemp trees]] in what is now the [[Sucos of East Timor|suco]] of {{ill|Caicoli|de}} at the centre of the then {{lang|pt|Reino Motaain}}, which covered present day Dili. Every year, the local community held a traditional dance ceremony, and also offered sacrifices for the hemp tree. The local people believed that the Portuguese word {{lang|pt|Dali}} meant 'sacred eye', and so they adopted it as the area's name.<ref name="di toponymy"/>

==Geography==
[[File:Dili Reisfelder.jpg|thumb|left|200px|Rice fields in Dili district]]
Since 1 January 2022, Dili has been the second smallest municipality in East Timor by area, at {{cvt|224.0|km2}}. It also has the highest population.<ref name="dge datasheet">{{Citation |year=2015 |title=Timor-Leste Population and Housing Census - Data Sheet |publisher=General Directorate of Statistics |publication-place=Dili |url=https://www.statistics.gov.tl/wp-content/uploads/2016/11/Wall-Chart-Poster-Landscape-Final-English-rev.pdf |access-date=18 April 2022 |archive-date=9 July 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220709163707/http://www.statistics.gov.tl/wp-content/uploads/2016/11/Wall-Chart-Poster-Landscape-Final-English-rev.pdf |url-status=dead }}</ref> It lies on the north coast of the island of [[Timor]] facing [[Ombai Strait]],<ref name="jica 2016-10">{{cite report |date=October 2016 |title=The Project for Study on Dili Urban Master Plan in the Democratic Republic of Timor-Leste Final Report Part I: Current Conditions |url=https://openjicareport.jica.go.jp/pdf/12268603.pdf |publisher=[[Japan International Cooperation Agency]] (JICA) |page= |docket=EI JR 16-132 |access-date=13 April 2022}}</ref>{{rp|((ES I-4)),((2-13))}} and borders the municipalities of [[Manatuto Municipality|Manatuto]] to the east, [[Aileu Municipality|Aileu]] to the south, and [[Liquiçá Municipality|Liquiçá]] to the west.

[[Atauro Island]], to the north of the municipality opposite the capital, was one of Dili's [[Administrative posts of East Timor|administrative posts]] until the end of 2021, when it became a separate municipality.<ref name="tatoli 2022-03-0">{{cite news |last1=Piedade da Freitas |first1=Domingos |title=Governo nomeia Domingos Soares para Administrador Municipal de Ataúro |trans-title=Government appoints Domingos Soares as Municipal Administrator of Atauro |url=http://tatoli.tl/pt/2022/03/09/governo-nomeia-domingos-soares-para-administrador-municipal-de-atauro/ |access-date=28 April 2022 |agency=[[Tatoli]] |date=9 March 2022 |language=pt |archive-date=7 July 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220707063928/http://tatoli.tl/pt/2022/03/09/governo-nomeia-domingos-soares-para-administrador-municipal-de-atauro/ |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref name="l 2022-03-09">{{cite news |title=Governo timorense nomeia primeiro administrador do novo município de Ataúro |trans-title=Timorese government appoints first administrator of the new municipality of Ataúro |url=https://www.rtp.pt/noticias/mundo/governo-timorense-nomeia-primeiro-administrador-do-novo-municipio-de-atauro_n1390006 |access-date=28 April 2022 |work=[[Rádio e Televisão de Portugal|RTP Notícias]] |date=9 March 2022 |language=pt}}</ref>

Dili is the economic and political centre of East Timor.

== Administrative posts ==
The municipality's administrative posts (formerly sub-districts) are:<ref>{{HD of East Timor|title=Dili District|page=73}}</ref>

* [[Cristo Rei Administrative Post|Cristo Rei]]
* [[Dom Aleixo Administrative Post|Dom Aleixo]]
* [[Metinaro Administrative Post|Metinaro]]
* [[Nain Feto]]; and
* [[Vera Cruz Administrative Post|Vera Cruz]]

The administrative posts are divided into 31 sucos ("villages") in total.
