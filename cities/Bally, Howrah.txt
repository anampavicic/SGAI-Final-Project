'''Bally''' is a neighbourhood in the city of [[Howrah]] of [[Howrah district]] in the [[States and territories of India|Indian state]] of [[West Bengal]]. It is a part of the area covered by [[Kolkata Metropolitan Development Authority]] (KMDA).<ref name=tel>{{cite news | url=https://www.telegraphindia.com/1151002/jsp/calcutta/story_45726.jsp#.VhIB7jMckjM | archive-url=https://archive.today/20151106050417/http://www.telegraphindia.com/1151002/jsp/calcutta/story_45726.jsp%23.VhIB7jMckjM | url-status=dead | archive-date=6 November 2015 | title=Bally battles merger blues | date=2 October 2015 | first=Anasuya | last=Basu | newspaper=The Telegraph | location=India | access-date=18 August 2018 }}</ref><ref name=toi>[https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/kolkata/Merge-Bally-Howrah-civic-bodies-Mamata-Banerjee/articleshow/47336577.cms Merge Bally, Howrah civic bodies: Mamata Banerjee]. ''timesofindia.com''. 19 May 2015.</ref>

Bally is under the jurisdiction of Bally Police Station of [[Howrah Police Commissionerate|Howrah City Police]]. It is governed by the [[Bally Municipality]].

==Geography==
Bally is located at {{Coord|22.65|N|88.34|E|}} and also the northernmost part of [[Howrah]] city.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fallingrain.com/world/IN/28/Bali.html|title=Maps, Weather, and Airports for Bali, India|website=www.fallingrain.com}}</ref> It has an average elevation of 15&nbsp;metres (49&nbsp;[[foot (length)|feet]]). It is more elevated in the west than the east (where flows the Hoogly river), thus leading to a problem of water logging.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://en-in.topographic-map.com/maps/ao3a/Bally/|title = Bally topographic map, elevation, relief}}</ref> Bally is separated from [[Uttarpara|Uttarpara, Hooghly]] by Bally Canal (Bally Khal) at its north. A Bridge was constructed over Bally Canal in 1846 to join the two localities.<ref>{{cite web|title=Importance of Bally Khal|url=https://www.ballyonline.in/city-guide/bally-khal-the-historic-canal}}</ref>

==Transport==
An important transportation hub of Kolkata, Bally is connected to [[Dakshineswar|Dakshineswar, Kolkata]] via [[Vivekananda Setu]] and [[Nivedita Setu]]. [[State Highway 6 (West Bengal)|State Highway 6]]/ [[Grand Trunk Road]] and [[Belghoria Expressway]] also pass through Bally. From here, people can avail a number of buses to go to [[Kolkata]] (via [[Vivekananda Setu]]), [[Howrah district|Howrah]] (via [[National Highway 16 (India)|National Highway 16]]) and [[Hooghly district|Hooghly]] (via [[National Highway 19 (India)|National Highway 19]]).

Bally also has three railway stations of [[Kolkata Suburban Railway]]. They are [[Bally railway station]] (on [[Howrah-Bardhaman main line]] and [[Howrah-Bardhaman chord|Howrah-Bardhaman chord line]]), [[Bally Ghat railway station]] and [[Bally Halt railway station]] (on [[Chord link line (Kolkata Suburban Railway)|Sealdah-Dankuni line]]).

Nearest Airport of Bally is [[Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport]] in [[Dum Dum|Dumdum, Kolkata]].
