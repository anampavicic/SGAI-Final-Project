'''Pavlodar Region''' ({{langx|kk|Павлодар облысы|translit=Pavlodar oblysy}}; {{langx|ru|Павлодарская область|translit=Pavlodarskaya oblast}}) is a [[Regions of Kazakhstan|region]] of [[Kazakhstan]]. The population of the region was {{Kz-census2009|742,475|punct=;}} and {{Kz-census1999|806,983|punct=.}} The latest official estimate (as at the start of 2022) was 756,511.<ref>Agency of Statistics of the Republic of Kazakhstan.</ref> Its capital is the city of [[Pavlodar]], which had a population of 360,014 at the start of 2018.<ref>Agency of Statistics of the Republic of Kazakhstan.</ref> Many people, especially [[Ukrainians]], migrated to Pavlodar in [[Nikita Khrushchev]]'s [[Virgin Lands Campaign]]. 

The [[Bayanaul National Park]], a [[protected area]] of the [[Kazakh Uplands]], is located in the [[Bayanaul Range]], within 100&nbsp;km of [[Ekibastuz]].

==Geography==
Pavlodar borders [[Russia]] ([[Altai Krai]], [[Omsk Oblast]] and [[Novosibirsk Oblast]]) to the north, and also borders the following Kazakh regions: [[Akmola Region|Akmola]] (to the west), [[East Kazakhstan Region|East Kazakhstan]] (to the south-east), [[North Kazakhstan Region|North Kazakhstan]] (to the north-west), and [[Karaganda Region|Karaganda]] (to the south). The southern part of the district is in the [[Kazakh Uplands]], while the northern part falls within the [[Baraba Plain]] and [[Kulunda Plain]]. The highest point of the region is [[Akbet]], a {{convert|1022|m|sp=us}} high summit located in the [[Bayanaul Range]].<ref name="STMa"/>

The [[Irtysh River]] flows from the [[Altay Mountains]] in [[People's Republic of China|China]] to Russia through the region; the [[Irtysh–Karaganda Canal]] crosses the western part, taking some of the river's water to [[Ekibastuz]] and [[Karaganda]]. The [[Sileti]] river also flows through the region.
There are many lakes in the region, most of them [[Salt lake|saline]], such as [[Siletiteniz]], [[Kyzylkak]], [[Koryakovka (lake)|Koryakovka]], [[Lake Jasybay|Zhasybai]], [[Zhalauly]], [[Shureksor]], [[Kudaikol]], [[Karasor (lake, Ekibastuz)|Karasor]], [[Zhamantuz, May District|Zhamantuz]], [[Kalkaman (lake)|Kalkaman]], [[Tuzdysor]], [[Maraldy (lake)|Maraldy]], [[Zharagash]] and [[Ulken Tobylzhan]], among others.<ref name="STMa">{{cite web|url=https://maps.vlasenko.net/smtm1000/n-43.jpg|title=N-43 Topographic Chart (in Russian)|access-date=5 July 2022}}</ref><ref>[https://www.researchgate.net/publication/326783739_Lakes_in_the_Central_Kazakhstan Lakes in the Central Kazakhstan]</ref>
