'''Larkana''' ({{langx|sd|{{naskh|لاڙڪاڻو}}|translit=lāṛkāṇo}}; {{langx|ur|{{Nastaliq|لاڑکانہ}}|translit=lāṛkāna}}) is a city located in the [[Sindh]] province of Pakistan. It is the [[List of most populous cities in Pakistan|15th largest city of Pakistan]] by population.<ref name="Population city">{{Cite web|url=http://www.tageo.com/index-e-pk-cities-PK.htm|title=Pakistan City & Town Population List|access-date=29 September 2017|publisher=Tageo.com website}}</ref> It is home to the [[Indus Valley civilization]] site [[Mohenjo-daro]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Indus|first1=Valley |title=Archeology|url=http://www.harappa.com/har/moen0.html|access-date=14 October 2014|url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141025145352/http://www.harappa.com/har/moen0.html|archive-date=25 October 2014 |df=dmy-all}}</ref> The historic [[Indus River]] flows in east and south of the city.<ref name="rivers">{{cite news|author=Staff reporter |title=Sindh CM visits bridge on River Indus in Larkana district |work=Dawn news |url=http://www.dawn.com/news/550918/sindh-cm-visits-bridge-on-river-indus-in-larkana-district|date=3 August 2010 |access-date=15 October 2014}}</ref>

The city is located within [[Larkana District]]. Formerly known as "[[Chanduka|Chandka]]", Larkana is located on the south bank of the Ghar canal, about {{convert|40|mi|km}} south of the town [[Shikarpur, Sindh|Shikarpur]], and {{convert|36|mi|km}} northeast of [[Mehar Taluka|Mehar]].<ref name="IGI">{{cite web|url=https://dsal.uchicago.edu/reference/gazetteer/pager.html?objectid=DS405.1.I34_V16_150.gif|title=Imperial Gazetteer2 of India, Volume 16, page 144 -- Imperial Gazetteer of India -- Digital South Asia Library|work=uchicago.edu|access-date=23 March 2016}}</ref> According to the [[2017 Census of Pakistan]], its population is 490,508.<ref name="census 2017"/> Therefore, it is the fourth most populated city of [[Sindh]] province after [[Karachi]], [[Hyderabad, Sindh|Hyderabad]], and [[Sukkur]]. Moreover, Larkana city has been placed as fifteenth largest of Pakistan during Census 2017.

== Geography ==
Larkana is situated at Latitude 24 56' 00' and Longitude 67 11' 00'.<ref>{{cite news |title=Larkana lines |website=Tageo.com |url=http://www.tageo.com/index-e-pk-v-05-d-m3841727.htm |access-date=14 October 2014}}</ref> It is situated in northwest part of Sindh and it has its own division. It is also included in Upper Sindh.

== Climate ==
Larkana has a [[hot desert climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification]] ''BWh''), bordering a hot [[semi arid]] climate (Koppen: BSh), with sweltering summers and mild winters. The highest recorded temperature is {{convert|53.0|°C|°F|1}}, recorded On 26 May 2010 and the lowest recorded temperature is {{convert|-4|°C|°F|1}}, recorded in January 1929. The average annual rainfall is {{cvt|237.4|mm}}, and mainly occurs in the monsoon season (July to September). The highest annual rainfall ever is {{cvt|988.7|mm}}, [[2022 Pakistan floods|recorded in 2022]] (some areas of the city got more than 1000 mm of annual rainfall in 2022) and the lowest annual rainfall ever is {{cvt|7.1|mm}}, recorded in 1938.

==Famous Crops==
Rice is the most famous crop of Larkana. Peas, Grape, Mustard, and Sunflowers are also grown there.

== Education ==
Several old and new schools, colleges, as well as university are functioning in the city for both boys and girls. Many are private and public institutions. IBA has takeover Public School and from 2018, Agha Khan University Education Board will takeover Sindh Board

=== Schools, colleges and training institutes ===
Local schools include [[Government Pilot School, Larkana]] (founded in 1926 by the British colonists), Girls High School, Public School Larkana, Deeni Madersa High School, and Government Degree College. Technical institutes include Polytechnic Institute and Teachers' Training College.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Apna|first1=Larkana|title=Schools and colleges|url=http://www.apnalarkana.com/education.html|access-date=23 March 2016}}</ref>

([[List of organizations with consultative status to the United Nations Economic and Social Council|UN ECOSOC accredited organization]]) has established [[Sindh Technical Education & Vocational Training Authority|STEVTA]] registered [https://dessi.org.pk/TVET.html technical and vocational training institute] for girls in 2017. [https://dessi.org.pk DESSI International] imparts free trainings along with giving monthly stipends through [[National Vocational and Technical Training Commission|NAVTTC]] and [https://board.bbsydpsindh.gov.pk BBSHRRDB]/[[Benazir Bhutto Shaheed Youth Development Program|BBSYDP]] projects in Larkana since 2017. [https://dessi.org.pk DESSI International] also provides training programs of [[Cosmetology|Beautician]], Diploma in IT, Computers, Dress Making, [https://dessi.org.pk/rilli-applique.html Rilli Applique], Spoken and Business English, [[Chinese language]] and other different short and professional courses in Larkana.

=== University and campuses ===

* [[University of Larkano]]
* [[Shaheed Mohtarma Benazir Bhutto Medical University]]
* [[Shaheed Zulfikar Ali Bhutto Institute of Science and Technology|Shaheed Zulfiqar Ali Bhutto Institute of Science and Technology]] campus
* [[Chandka Medical College]]

[[SZABIST]] opened its first campus in Larkana in 2004. It offers degrees in [[Bachelor of Business Administration|BBA]] (day), [[Bachelor of Arts|BA]] (day), [[Master of Business Administration|MBA]] (evening), [[Master of Business Administration#Programs|EMBA]]. A few years later the SZABIST Trust established two school and college in the city: Montessori to Class VII (Junior School) and Class VIII to XII (School and College).<ref>{{cite web|last1=School & College|title=SZABIST Larkana|url=http://larkana.szabist.edu.pk/aboutus.asp|access-date=23 March 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160322191048/http://larkana.szabist.edu.pk/aboutus.asp|archive-date=22 March 2016|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref>
[[Chandka Medical College]] (CMC) was established and inaugurated by the former prime minister of Pakistan [[Zulfiqar Ali Bhutto]] on 20 April 1973. It was the fourth public medical college to be established in the province of Sindh. Chandka Medical College is recognised by [[Pakistan Medical & Dental Council]] (PMDC). The [[College of Physicians & Surgeons Pakistan]] (CPSP) has also established its regional center at the campus of CMC Larkana. CMC is recently upgraded to [[Shaheed Mohtarma Benazir Bhutto Medical University]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Chandka|first1=University|title=SMBB|url=http://www.cmc.edu.pk/history.aspx|website=CMC|access-date=15 October 2014}}</ref> In 2009, Quaid-e-Awam University College of Engineering Science & Technology (QUCEST), Larkano, was established in the outskirt of Larkana, under the directives of [[Government of Sindh]] in collaboration with [[Quaid-e-Awam University of Engineering, Science and Technology|Quaid-e-Awam University of Engineering Science & Technology]] (QUEST), Nawabshah, Sindh. The campus offers bachelor of engineering degrees in the technologies of [[civil engineering]], [[mechanical engineering]], [[electronic engineering]] and [[electrical engineering]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=QUCEST|first1=Larkana|title=Engineering Campus Larkana|url=http://quest.edu.pk/qucest/aboutqucest.html|access-date=23 March 2016}}</ref> 

In 2024, [[University of Larkano]], established a merger of Sindh University Campus, Quaid-e-Awam University College of Engineering Science & Technology (QUCEST) and Shaheed Zulfiqar Ali Bhutto Agricultural College (SZBAC) to its faculties.

== Transport ==
[[Larkana Railway Station]] is located in the center of the city. It connects Larkana to the rest of Sindh and Pakistan. Pakistan Railway also assists in the transportation of agricultural products to provincial capital [[Karachi]] from Larkana.  [[Moenjodaro Airport]] is located near [[Mohen-jo-daro]], 28&nbsp;km away to the south of the city of Larkana, about 5 kilometres away from [[Dokri]].

Larkana is connected via land buses to most major cities in the country, including [[Karachi]], [[Islamabad]], [[Hyderabad, Sindh|Hyderabad]] and [[Quetta]].

== Sport ==
Larkana was the birthplace of the Sindh Games. It also hosted the 12th Sindh Games in 2009 which included sports such as football, gymnastics, hockey, judo, karate, squash, table tennis, tennis, volleyball, weightlifting, and wushu. It also included traditional games such as kodi kodi, malakhara, shooting and wanjhwatti.<ref name="games">{{cite news|last1=Larkana|first1=Sports|title=Sindh Games|url=http://www.dawn.com/news/989726/sindh-games-open-in-larkana-today|access-date=22 March 2016|publisher=Dawn}}</ref> Larkana was also home to cricket team [[Larkana Bulls]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pcb.com.pk/averages-reports.php?action=team_averages&tournament_id=979&match_type=&team_id=12362&match_type_id=8|title=Team Larkana Bulls T20 Batting Bowling Stats - Live Cricket Scores - PCB|work=pcb.com.pk|access-date=23 March 2016}}</ref>

The Larkana Tennis Association provides the only [[tennis court]] in Larkana, and coaches boys coming from various other cities.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tribune.com.pk/story/869867/promoting-tennis-in-larkana/|title=Promoting tennis in Larkana|date=14 April 2015|work=The Express Tribune|access-date=23 March 2016}}</ref>
