'''San José del Cabo''' ({{IPA|es|saŋ xoˈse ðel ˈkaβo}}, ''[[Saint Joseph]] of the Cape'') is a coastal city located on the [[Gulf of California]] coast, near the southern tip of [[Baja California Sur]] (state), [[Mexico|México]]. San José del Cabo is situated on the edge of a shallow bay, some {{convert|32|km|mi}} northeast of [[Cabo San Lucas]], a city with which it shares the title of ''Los Cabos''.

San José del Cabo is the seat of the [[Los Cabos Municipality]]. The city had a population of 136,285 at the 2020 census.<ref name="CENSOCABOS2020">{{Cite web|url=https://biblioteca.setuesbcs.gob.mx/administrador/biblioteca/publicaciones/pdf/Estrategico_2023_Los_Cabos.pdf|title = CENSO Los Cabos 2020}}</ref> 

The two cities are served by [[Los Cabos International Airport]].

== People and history ==
The 2020 census found that, at the time, 136,285 people lived in the city.<ref name=CENSOCABOS2020/> Together with neighboring Cabo San Lucas, it forms a major tourist hub, with over 900,000 hotel guests in 2011.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ranking of World Tourism|url=http://www.siimt.com/work/sites/siimt/resources/LocalContent/2014/10/Numeralia_Junio2012.pdf|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220224223337/http://www.siimt.com/work/sites/siimt/resources/LocalContent/2014/10/Numeralia_Junio2012.pdf|url-status=dead|archive-date=February 24, 2022|publisher=Consejo de Promoción Turística de México|access-date=3 September 2012|page=2|language=es|year=2011}}</ref>

The ''[[Misión Estero de las Palmas de San José del Cabo Añuití]]'' was founded in 1730 on the west bank of the nearby Río San José, which flows into the largest body of freshwater in Baja California Sur, an estuary, after flowing largely subterraneanly for {{convert|39.1|km|mi}} from its origin in the ''[[Sierra de la Laguna]]'' (Laguna Mountains). For centuries, the river has provided a source of freshwater for the locals and animals of this coastal desert we know today as San José del Cabo; it was once used as a source of freshwater for Spanish [[galleon]]s traveling back from the [[Philippines]].<ref>Encyclopædia Britannica, Second edition, 1778, Edinburgh, page 1580. Scan of page can be found at http://www.hyzercreek.com/britannica.htm</ref> The river once flowed above-ground until the beginning of the 20th century, due to [[Human impact on the environment|anthropogenic causes]].<ref>{{cite journal |title=Floristic Composition of the San José del Cabo Oasis, Baja California Sur, México |author1=José Luis León de la Luz |author2=Raymundo Domínguez Cadena |author3=Miguel Domínguez León |author4=José Juan Pérez Navarro |journal=SIDA, Contributions to Botany |volume=17 |number=3 |date=September 1997 |pages=599–614 |jstor=41967252 }}</ref> A one-kilometer long [[Shoal|sandbar]] separates the estuary from what early Spanish explorers, including [[Sebastián Vizcaíno]], called the ''Bahía de San Bernabé'' (Bay of Saint Burnaby), now known as Bahía de San José del Cabo.<ref>{{cite book|title=Peter Masten Dunne|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=LeZLxC3_iaUC&pg=PA4|publisher=University of California Press|pages=4–|id=GGKEY:FK94TH92Q55}}</ref>

San José del Cabo is one of two places where the critically endangered [[Oryzomyini|rice rat]] ''[[Oryzomys peninsulae]]'' has survived.<ref>Carleton,  M.D. and Arroyo-Cabrales,  J. 2009. [http://digitallibrary.amnh.org/dspace/bitstream/2246/6035/4/331-03-carleton.pdf Review of the ''Oryzomys couesi''  complex (Rodentia: Cricetidae: Sigmodontinae) in Western Mexico]. Bulletin of the American Museum of Natural History 331:94–127.</ref>

==Geography==
===Climate===
San José del Cabo, like almost all of the Baja California peninsula, has a [[tropical desert climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification|Köppen]] ''BWh''). The area, however, does receive slightly more rainfall than most areas further north, mainly due to [[tropical cyclone]]s occasionally approaching from the south. These storms can bring very heavy precipitation events, such as the roughly {{convert|340|mm|in|1}} on 1 September 1998, or the {{convert|316|mm|in|1}} on 3 November 1993. Overall, rainfall events here are some of the most spontaneous anywhere in the world due to the cyclone influence, and many years can pass without significant rain, at all.

The sea experiences lows of {{cvt|72|–|73|F}} in winter, and highs of {{cvt|77|–|84|F}} during the summer months.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.myweather2.com/City-Town/Mexico/San-Jose-Del-Cabo/climate-profile.aspx?month=12 |title=December Climate History for San Jose Del Cabo &#124; Local &#124; Mexico |publisher=Myweather2.com |access-date=2018-07-21}}</ref>

Many people from around the world live here, mostly from the USA, Canada, New Zealand, Australia, China, Korea, Japan, Spain, the United Kingdom, and other Latin American countries, among others. Some 80% of the population, however, are originally from other parts of Latin America (Mexico, [[Central America|Central]] and [[South America]]) thus enabling one to hear and experience several dialects of Spanish being spoken.
