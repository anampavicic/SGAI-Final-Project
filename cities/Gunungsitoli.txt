'''Gunungsitoli''' is a city<ref>{{Cite web|title=Jokowi Terbitkan PP Pemindahan Ibu Kota Kabupaten Nias dari Gunungsitoli|url=https://www.tribunnews.com/nasional/2016/08/02/jokowi-terbirkan-pp-pemindahan-ibo-kota-kabupaten-nias-dari-gunungsitoli|access-date=2021-12-27|website=Tribunnews.com|language=id|archive-date=2021-12-27|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211227133256/https://www.tribunnews.com/nasional/2016/08/02/jokowi-terbirkan-pp-pemindahan-ibo-kota-kabupaten-nias-dari-gunungsitoli|url-status=live}}</ref> located in [[North Sumatra]] province, [[Indonesia]], on the [[Indian Ocean]] island of [[Nias]], west of [[Sumatra]]. Gunungsitoli is the island's only city and is the main hub for the island and surrounding smaller islands. Located on the north-eastern side of Nias island, the city was historically a series of fortifications made by the [[Netherlands East Indies|Dutch colonial administration]] in the 1600s to defend against frequent raids from Nias tribes, especially those from southern parts of the island. Until 1914, it was the only part of the island that was effectively controlled by the Dutch.

Gunungsitoli had a population of 126,202 at the 2010 Census<ref name="Biro Pusat Statistik 2011">Biro Pusat Statistik, Jakarta, 2011.</ref> and 136,017 at the 2020 Census;<ref name="Badan Pusat Statistik 2021">Badan Pusat Statistik, Jakarta, 2021.</ref> in mid 2023, Gunungsitoli had a population of 137,518,<ref name="Badan Pusat Statistik 2024">Badan Pusat Statistik, Jakarta, 26 September 2024, ''Kota Gunungsitoli Dalam Angka 2024'' (Katalog-BPS 1102001.1278)</ref> which makes it the seventh-most-populous city in North Sumatra. It had a population density of 304 people per square kilometre, making it the most-densely populated place on Nias island. Being the only city in the island, Gunungsitoli is the economic hub of the island and the surrounding Nias archipelago, as well as the only place with significant, non-agriculture industries, on the island. The city was previously part of the larger [[Nias Regency]] but was separated on 29 October 2008.

== History ==
=== Precolonial ===
[[File:Nias Ahnenfiguren Museum Rietberg RIN 403.jpg|left|thumb|Adu Zatua statues from Nias]]

Nias island, together with groups of other islands off the western coast of Sumatra,  was mentioned by [[Ptolemy]] in 150 CE as "Barus islands".<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|title=Sejarah Nias|url=https://museum-nias.org/sejarah-nias/|access-date=2022-01-12|website=Museum Pusaka Nias|language=id|archive-date=2022-01-12|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220112202249/https://museum-nias.org/sejarah-nias/|url-status=live}}</ref> Nias had well-established trade contacts with Arab and Chinese traders since around the seventh century.<ref name=":4" /> In 1154, the island was mentioned by [[Muhammad al-Idrisi]] as "Niyan" and described as "densely populated, with one big town, and inhabited by many tribes".<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|title=Orang Nias|url=https://museum-nias.org/orang-nias/|access-date=2022-01-12|website=Museum Pusaka Nias|language=id|archive-date=2021-10-19|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211019071553/https://museum-nias.org/orang-nias/|url-status=live}}</ref>

Archeological evidence shows humans have inhabited the island for 12,000 years. Remains of tools were found in Tögi Ndrawa cave by Indonesian archeologists from [[Medan]] in August 1999. The excavation shows sign of [[mesolithic]] culture and that the cave was still inhabited until around 700 years ago.<ref name=":4" /> According to folk stories of the Nias people, the island was settled by six ancestor tribes but the current Nias people or ''Ono Niha''—which means "human" in the Nias language—arose more recently, according to records compiled by German missionary [[Wilhelm Heinrich Sundermann]].<ref name=":5" /> Migration of ''Ono Niha'' people from mainland Sumatra occurred in around 1350; they brought with them knowledge of metallurgy, agriculture, husbandry, and woven clothing. It is unknown whether previous inhabitants of the island were assimilated or out-competed with the arrival of the ''Ono Niha''.<ref name=":4" />

In 1416, [[Ming treasure voyages]] led by [[Zheng He]] occupied a portion of mainland Sumatra that directly faces Nias island and constructed a port town named ''Singkuang'' (New Land). The occupation led to a significant presence of Chinese communities on the island.<ref name=":4" /> Around the 1500s, the island was subject to frequent slave raids by ships from [[Aceh Sultanate]], which at the time was under [[Ali Mughayat Syah|Sultan Ali Mughayat Syah]], who sought to conquer the western coast of Sumatra.<ref name=":4" /> In 1642, seven ships from Aceh Sultanate were stranded on the eastern coast of the island, resulting in a significant presence of [[Acehnese people|Acehnese]] communities, which are known locally as ''Polem'' people.<ref name=":5" />

=== Contact with Europeans ===
First contact between Nias people and Europeans came on 2 July 1664 when Dutch traders and the king of [[Luaha Laraga]] made a trade agreement and tariffs for Dutch ships that were using the port in modern-day [[Idanoi district]].<ref name=":9">{{Cite web|title=niaskab.go.id - Portal Resmi Pemerintah Kabupaten Nias|url=https://niaskab.go.id/sjkabnias|access-date=2022-01-12|website=niaskab.go.id|archive-date=2021-11-21|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211121110021/https://niaskab.go.id/sjkabnias|url-status=live}}</ref> In 1668, the [[Dutch East India Company]] made agreements with village chiefs around the location of modern-day Gunungsitoli city and the [[Hinako islands]]. The company settled the region and built several warehouses<ref name=":4" /> but the Dutch traders left the region and abandoned the settlement in 1740 due to decreasing Dutch influence over the region.<ref name=":4" />

=== Colonial era ===
[[File:COLLECTIE TROPENMUSEUM Kerkdienst Goenoeng Sitoli Nias TMnr 10013687.jpg|left|thumb|A church scene in Gunungsitoli, around 1900]]

In 1776, British traders tried to settled the region but soon also abandoned it because the region was not profitable. For several decades, there was no significant European presence on the island. The British again tried to settle the region in 1821 but the settlement was taken over by the Dutch in 1825.<ref name=":4" /> In 1840, the Dutch tried to gain control of the entire island following the [[Anglo-Dutch Treaty of 1824]] but were unable to establish military presence on the island outside a small area of  Gunungsitoli, which was then known as Rapatgebied.<ref name=":4" /> Frequent raids by Nias tribes against Dutch fortifications concentrated the settlement in the area that would become the city.<ref name=":4" /><ref name=":6">{{Cite web|date=2016-11-16|title=Desa Orahili Fau, Benteng Terakhir Nias Melawan Belanda|url=http://niassatu.com/2016/11/16/desa-orahili-fau-benteng-terakhir-nias-melawan-belanda/|access-date=2022-01-12|website=Nias Satu|language=id|archive-date=2021-07-17|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210717140247/http://niassatu.com/2016/11/16/desa-orahili-fau-benteng-terakhir-nias-melawan-belanda/|url-status=live}}</ref>

The Dutch were only able to start a significant military campaign against Nias tribes in 1900 and subjugated the entire island by 1914. Nias was one of the last regions of the Indonesian archipelago by to be conquered by the [[Dutch East Indies]].<ref name=":6" /> Missionary activities on the island grew after the Dutch established control over the island. In 1916, a mass conversion to Christianity known as ''Fangesa Sebua'' (The Great Repentance) occurred on the island. The event started in Gunungsitoli from Idanoi and later spread throughout the island.<ref>{{Cite book|last=Beatty|first=Andrew|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=qVN-DwAAQBAJ&dq=fangesa+sebua&pg=PA70|title=Emotional Worlds|date=2019-02-07|publisher=Cambridge University Press|isbn=978-1-107-02099-3|language=en|access-date=2022-01-12|archive-date=2022-01-13|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220113092918/https://books.google.co.id/books?id=qVN-DwAAQBAJ&pg=PA70&lpg=PA70&dq=fangesa+sebua&source=bl&ots=wvOWOB4NIk&sig=ACfU3U3923GYtRLoik_6ZiC0Qpmq6q4d2A&hl=id&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwi5s6ivl631AhUkT2wGHZ28CRMQ6AF6BAgNEAM#v=onepage&q=fangesa%20sebua&f=false|url-status=live}}</ref>

=== Mid-to-late 20th century===
Gunungsitoli was one of two locations on Nias where Dutch authorities held German prisoners during [[World War II]]. The prisoners were part of formerly bigger German prisoner groups abroad [[SS Van Imhoff|SS ''Van Imhoff'']], which was sunk by Japanese bombers off the west coast of Sumatra.<ref>{{Cite web|title=» Todeskampf im Indischen Ozean: Der Untergang der Van Imhoff|url=http://www.orden-online.de/news/2012/01/11/todeskampf-im-indischen-ozean-der-untergang-der-van-imhoff/|access-date=2022-01-12|language=de|archive-date=2021-05-17|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210517171551/http://www.orden-online.de/news/2012/01/11/todeskampf-im-indischen-ozean-der-untergang-der-van-imhoff/|url-status=live}}</ref> Upon hearing news about Japanese attacks on Sumatra, the German prisoners planned a coup against the Dutch colonial authority in the city. The prisoners tried to persuade native police, known as ''Veldpolities'', to revolt.<ref name=":7">{{Cite web|last=Salam|first=Fahri|title=Kudeta Orang-Orang (Nazi) Jerman di Pulau Nias|url=https://tirto.id/kudeta-orang-orang-nazi-jerman-di-pulau-nias-clyj|access-date=2022-01-12|website=tirto.id|date=26 March 2017 |language=id|archive-date=2021-07-19|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210719171855/https://tirto.id/kudeta-orang-orang-nazi-jerman-di-pulau-nias-clyj|url-status=live}}</ref> At the time, the city was home to around 60 German prisoners. On 29 March 1942, the native police revolted by shooting Dutch residents and imprisoning Dutch officials, and the city was quickly occupied.<ref name=":7" /> Once occupied German prisoners established the unrecognized state of the Free Republic of Nias.<ref>[[Rosihan Anwar|Anwar, Rosihan]] (2004), Sejarah kecil "Petite Histoire" Indonesia (in Indonesian), vol. 1, Jakarta: Penerbit Buku Kompas</ref> On 17 April 1942, the Japanese military landed in the city and was welcomed by the German prisoners, who took over the city. By 24 April 1942, all German prisoners had left the island and the administration was handed over to the Japanese until the end of World War II.<ref name=":7" />
[[File:Beringin Market in Gunungsitoli, Sumatera Utara Membangun, p330.jpg|left|thumb|Beringin Market in Gunungsitoli, 1975. In 1975, the city and Nias island seen a short-lived tourist boom followed by brief improvement of infrastructures.]]

During the [[Indonesian National Revolution]], Gunungsitoli and Nias came under blockade from the [[Dutch Navy]] to cut off the island from the western coast of Sumatra. Due to the blockade, the city printed its own banknotes because Republican banknotes from [[Bukittinggi]] could not be transported.<ref name=":8">{{Cite web|title=Berjuang dengan uang mempertahankan dan memajukan Republik Indonesia : semangat juang otoritas dan masyarakat Sumatera Utara/ penulis: Darsono, Siti Astiyah, Ichwan Azhari, Enny Tin Suryanti, Allan Akbar {{!}} OPAC Perpustakaan Nasional RI.|url=https://opac.perpusnas.go.id/DetailOpac.aspx?id=1220585|access-date=2022-01-12|website=opac.perpusnas.go.id|archive-date=2022-01-13|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220113092917/https://opac.perpusnas.go.id/DetailOpac.aspx?id=1220585|url-status=live}}</ref> The banknotes were known as ORIPDA-Nias (Regional Money of Republic Indonesia-Nias).<ref name=":8" /> The Dutch military landed in the city in November 1945. The relationship between the newly arrived troops and the city's residents was tense, especially because of constant patrols of Republican youths armed with [[bambu runcing]]. Small armed clashes occurred around the city when Dutch military tried to take back control of governmental buildings that were taken over by Indonesian nationalists following the end of the war.<ref name=":14">{{Cite book|last=Penerangan|first=Indonesia Departemen|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=unoSAAAAMAAJ&dq=gunungsitoli&pg=PA491|title=Propinsi sumatera Utara|date=1953|publisher=Kementerian Penerangan|language=ms|access-date=5 February 2022|archive-date=18 March 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220318043322/https://books.google.com/books?id=unoSAAAAMAAJ&dq=gunungsitoli&pg=PA491|url-status=live}}</ref>

The republican government of Nias was evacuated from Gunungsitoli in the aftermath.<ref name=":14" /> In November 1946, the [[Indonesian National Committee]] or (''Komite Nasional Indonesia'') (KNI) led by [[Roos Telaumbanua]] attempted to establish armed elements of Republicans such as a branch of the [[People's Security Agency]] and the [[Indonesian National Police]] around the island. The committee also established a ''Fonds Kemerdekaan'' (Independence Fund) to give economic support to the Indonesian republic in Tapanuli, mainland Sumatra, and ''Kongsi Pelajaran'' (Shipping Union).<ref name=":14" /> The economic support was in form of the sale of [[copra]] to [[Singapore]] and donating pigs and oil to Tapanuli.<ref name=":14" /> Gunungsitoli was one of the main centers of Republican support on the island. Due to [[Operation Kraai]], communication between Nias and Tapanuli was cut off so the island tried to establish communication with [[Kutaraja]], [[Aceh]]. On 19 November 1949, officials from North Sumatra visited Gunungsitoli to establish a local government council (''Dewan Pemerintah Daerah'') on Nias, which was realized in 1950.<ref name=":14" />

[[File:US Navy 050411-N-1485H-005 Ens. Mat Nolan, front center, and Cmdr. Karen McDonald, back left, help unload patients that have been medically evacuated.jpg|thumb|US Navy together with Indonesian Army on Binaka Airport in the aftermath of 2005 Nias earthquake]]

In 1975, Nias experienced a tourist boom, especially with Australian tourists, and became destination for surfers. The tourist boom was followed by general improvements to infrastructure in Gunungsitoli and the building of markets and roads.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2014-08-11|title=Nias Bangkit.COM {{!}} News and articles from Nias Island|url=http://www.nias-bangkit.com/2013/08/dalimen-peletak-fondasi-pembangunan-nias-bagian-i/|access-date=2022-01-13|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140811190610/http://www.nias-bangkit.com/2013/08/dalimen-peletak-fondasi-pembangunan-nias-bagian-i/|archive-date=2014-08-11}}</ref> Despite these improvements the boom was short-lived.<ref name=":4" />

===21st century===

Nias, including Gunungsitoli, was devastated by the [[2004 Indian Ocean earthquake and tsunami]] and the [[2005 Nias–Simeulue earthquake]]. Much of the infrastructure was destroyed, and between 2005 and 2010, the residents became reliant on aid from nonprofit organizations to recover.<ref name=":4" />

== Geography ==

Gunungsitoli borders [[North Nias Regency]] in the north, [[Nias Regency]] in the south and west, and the [[Indian Ocean]] in the east. The city has many hills with heights of up to {{Convert|800|meters|feet|abbr=out}} above sea level. The city's soil is mostly unstable, and often causes landslides and damage to roads.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Badan Pusat Statistik Kota Gunungsitoli|url=https://gunungsitolikota.bps.go.id/publication/2020/11/19/9453945d0c184a9d0c89332c/statistik-daerah-kota-gunungsitoli-2020.html|access-date=2021-05-03|website=gunungsitolikota.bps.go.id|archive-date=2021-05-03|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210503051553/https://gunungsitolikota.bps.go.id/publication/2020/11/19/9453945d0c184a9d0c89332c/statistik-daerah-kota-gunungsitoli-2020.html|url-status=live}}</ref> Soil composition varies from [[alluvium]] and limestone to corals and is generally prone to compaction.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=Profil Gunungsitoli|url=https://sippa.ciptakarya.pu.go.id/sippa_online/ws_file/dokumen/rpi2jm/DOCRPIJM_1506492990BAB_4_OK.pdf|url-status=live|access-date=2021-05-03|archive-date=2021-05-03|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210503051553/https://sippa.ciptakarya.pu.go.id/sippa_online/ws_file/dokumen/rpi2jm/DOCRPIJM_1506492990BAB_4_OK.pdf}}</ref> Limestone often makes underground water undrinkable.<ref name=":0" />

The slope in the city interior varies from 8% to 25%. Coastal areas are mostly flatter with a slope of less than 8%. Gunungsitoli is located between the [[subduction zone]] of the [[Eurasian Plate]] and [[Indo-Australian Plate]], making it extremely prone to earthquakes. The city was devastated by the 2005 Nias–Simeulue earthquake. According to the [[Meteorology, Climatology, and Geophysical Agency]], on average, Gunungsitoli experiences more than 300 earthquakes per month. Due to its coastal location, the city is also prone to [[tsunami]]s.<ref name=":1" />

=== Climate ===
Gunungsitoli is located close to the [[equator]], and has a tropical rainforest climate with an average of 21 days of rain per month. The average rainfall per month in 2019 was 250.21&nbsp;mm<sup>3</sup> but this can vary widely from 100 and 300&nbsp;mm<sup>3</sup> per month. Moisture in the city is usually between 87% and 95%, and the average temperature is {{Convert|26|C|F|abbr=on}}. Wind speed on average per month reaches {{Convert|5.17|knot|m/s|sp=us}} per hour.<ref name=":0" />

== Demographics ==
[[File:Baju adat Nias.jpg|thumb|Nias traditional attire. Nias people are the majority of the city's inhabitants]]

The annual population growth in Gunungsitoli was 0.73% in 2020, with sex ratio of 94 males to 100 females. As with other Indonesian cities, the population is young, with 95,147 of the total population of 136,017 above the age of 15, of reproductive age and considered part of the workforce. In 2020, 46.8% of the city population lived in Gunungsitoli District. Despite the high birthrate and expansive structure of its [[population pyramid]], the population growth was slow because of internal migration to bigger cities such as [[Padang]] and Medan.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|title=Badan Pusat Statistik Kota Gunungsitoli|url=https://gunungsitolikota.bps.go.id/publication/2021/02/26/76c3756dbe5fcf120f9ea3da/kota-gunungsitoli-dalam-angka-2021.html|access-date=2021-05-03|website=gunungsitolikota.bps.go.id|archive-date=2021-05-03|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210503053741/https://gunungsitolikota.bps.go.id/publication/2021/02/26/76c3756dbe5fcf120f9ea3da/kota-gunungsitoli-dalam-angka-2021.html|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name=":0" /><ref>{{cite journal |last1=Mimi Citra Sari Citra Sari, Isjoni ', Tugiman ' |title=MIGRASI SUKU NIAS KE KOTA PADANG SUMATERA BARAT (Studi Kasus di Kecamatan Padang Selatan) |journal=Jurnal Online Mahasiswa Bidang Keguruan Dan Ilmu Pendidikan |volume=2 |issue=2 |url=https://jom.unri.ac.id/index.php/JOMFKIP/article/view/6008 |access-date=2022-02-28 |publisher=Fakultas Keguruan dan Ilmu Pendidikan Universitas Riau |archive-date=5 February 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220205015021/https://jom.unri.ac.id/index.php/JOMFKIP/article/view/6008 |url-status=live }}</ref>

[[File:Cap Go Meh Gunungsitoli.png|thumb|[[Cap go meh|Cap Go Meh]] festival celebrated by Chinese Indonesians in Gunungsitoli, February 2020. The city has significant population of Chinese Indonesians.]]

The majority of the city's population is [[Protestantism|Protestant]] with a minority of Muslims, Catholics, and Buddhists. The Protestant population was 116,435 in 2020, followed by 21,979 Muslims, 10,363 Catholics and 382 Buddhists.<ref name=":2" /><ref name=":0" /> The majority of city's residents are [[Nias people]], and there are significant minorities of other ethnicities such as [[Batak]], [[Minangkabau people|Minangkabau]], [[Javanese people|Javanese]], [[Chinese Indonesians]] and Acehnese people.<ref name=":12">{{Cite web|title=PU-net|url=http://perkotaan.bpiw.pu.go.id/v2/kota-sedang/92|access-date=2022-01-12|website=perkotaan.bpiw.pu.go.id|archive-date=2022-01-13|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220113092920/http://perkotaan.bpiw.pu.go.id/v2/kota-sedang/92|url-status=live}}</ref> The Chinese population are mainly descendants of traders in the precolonial era while the Acehnese people, who are found mostly around Mudik village on Idanoi, are descendants from Acehnese ships' crews.<ref name=":5" /> Most of the Acehnese and Chinese population have been assimilated into Nias society and can fluently speak the [[Nias language]]. There were historically populations of [[Bugis people]], especially around the region close to the [[Hinako Islands]], who were killed by raids from Acehnese ships during the precolonial era.<ref name=":5" /> Other ethnicities are known by Nias people as "Orang Seberang" (Indonesian: people from across).<ref name=":12" />

Most people in the city speak the Nias language, which is also taught at schools as a regional language.<ref>{{Cite web|title=BAHAN MUATA LOKAL/ MULOK BAHASA NIAS UNTUK RPP|url=https://ayoguruberbagi.kemdikbud.go.id/rpp/bahan-muata-lokal-mulok-bahasa-nias-untuk-rpp/|access-date=2022-01-12|website=GURU BERBAGI|language=id|archive-date=2022-01-13|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220113092913/https://ayoguruberbagi.kemdikbud.go.id/rpp/bahan-muata-lokal-mulok-bahasa-nias-untuk-rpp/|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Mata Pelajaran|url=http://10258938.siap-sekolah.com/kurikulum-akademik/kurikulum-pelajaran/|access-date=2022-01-12|website=SDN 075021 OMBOLOTA SALOO|language=id|archive-date=2022-01-13|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220113092908/http://10258938.siap-sekolah.com/kurikulum-akademik/kurikulum-pelajaran/|url-status=live}}</ref> [[Indonesian language|Indonesian]] is also well-understood in the city.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Rahayu|first=Sri|date=2022-01-12|title=Interferensi morfologis bahasa Nias dalam bahasa Indonesia pada karangan siswa SMP Gunungsitoli|url=http://mulok.library.um.ac.id/index3.php/73353.html|journal=SKRIPSI Mahasiswa UM|language=id|access-date=2022-01-12|archive-date=2020-06-26|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200626032006/http://mulok.library.um.ac.id/index3.php/73353.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

As with all [[City status in Indonesia|Indonesian cities]], the local government of Gunungsitoli is a second-level administrative division that is run by a mayor and vice-mayor together with the City Council, and is equivalent in status to a regency.<ref>{{Cite web|title=UU 22 1999|url=https://www.dpr.go.id/dokjdih/document/uu/UU_1999_22.pdf|url-status=live|access-date=2021-05-03|archive-date=2021-04-16|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210416012225/https://www.dpr.go.id/dokjdih/document/uu/UU_1999_22.pdf}}</ref> Executive power lies in the mayor and vice-mayor while legislation duties are carried on by the City Council. == Economy ==
[[File:Market scene in Gunungsitoli.jpg|thumb|Market scene in Gunungsitoli. Trade dominates the city's economic activities]]

The biggest contributor to Gunungsitoli's gross regional product is the trade sector with a figure of 25.49%, followed by construction with 21.82%, and fisheries and agriculture with 14.6%. Economic growth was 6.05% in 2019. The city's [[gross regional product]] (GRP) in 2021 was 5,776.11 billion [[Indonesian rupiah|rupiahs]], which was the second-highest in the island after South Nias Regency.<ref name=":11">{{Cite web|title=Badan Pusat Statistik Kota Gunungsitoli|url=https://gunungsitolikota.bps.go.id/publication/2021/09/27/e3f3d64f51d1cbe03941e4dc/statistik-daerah-kota-gunungsitoli-2021.html|access-date=2022-01-12|website=gunungsitolikota.bps.go.id|archive-date=2022-01-13|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220113092911/https://gunungsitolikota.bps.go.id/publication/2021/09/27/e3f3d64f51d1cbe03941e4dc/statistik-daerah-kota-gunungsitoli-2021.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

=== Agriculture and fisheries ===
Despite it being smaller than other sectors in terms of contribution to the GRP, agriculture employs around 31% of the city's workforce. In 2019, {{Convert|2313|ha|acre sqmi|1|abbr=on}} of the city was cultivated for paddy with a crop yield of 12,997 tons. Copra is among the island's main exports and is shipped from Gunungsitoli after being harvested from neighbouring regencies.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-03-28|title=Tol Laut 2021. KSOP Gunungsitoli Kerja Keras Tingkatkan Muatan Balik Trayek T-2|url=https://jurnalmaritim.com/tol-laut-ksop-gunungsitoli-kerja-keras-tingkatkan-muatan-balik-trayek-t-2/|access-date=2022-01-14|website=Jurnal Maritim|language=id|archive-date=15 January 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220115062701/https://jurnalmaritim.com/tol-laut-ksop-gunungsitoli-kerja-keras-tingkatkan-muatan-balik-trayek-t-2/|url-status=live}}</ref> Other cultivated crops in Gunungsitoli are maize with a crop yield of 655.54 tons, cassava with 1,456 tons, and sweet potatoes with production of 634.25 tons. Most of the city's population planted cassava without harvesting it, and instead used its leaves to feed pigs. The pig population in Gunungsitoli as of 2020 was 2,699.  The city's egg production was 268 tons in 2020.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":2" /> The same year's fish catch was 6,284 tons from the sea and 129 tons of freshwater fish.<ref name=":0" />

The shipyard was built in 2017 and started operating in 2019, mostly repairing and painting ships.<ref name=":10">{{Cite web|title=PROFIL POTENSI DAERAH KOTA GUNUNGSITOLI TAHUN 2020|url=https://www.northsumatrainvest.id/data/pdf/publication/3.%20(Gunungsitoli)%20PROFIL%20POTENSI%20DAERAH%20KOTA%20GUNUNGSITOLI%20TAHUN%202020%20(2).pdf|url-status=live|access-date=2022-01-12|archive-date=2022-01-13|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220113092912/https://www.northsumatrainvest.id/data/pdf/publication/3.%20%28Gunungsitoli%29%20PROFIL%20POTENSI%20DAERAH%20KOTA%20GUNUNGSITOLI%20TAHUN%202020%20%282%29.pdf}}</ref> The city government runs an ice factory mainly to support the city's fisheries. The factory has capability to produce around 300 blocks of ice per day.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Agency|first=ANTARA News|title=Wali Kota Bagi Es Balok Gratis Kepada Nelayan - ANTARA News Sumatera Utara|url=https://sumut.antaranews.com/berita/159422/wali-kota-bagi-es-balok-gratis-kepada-nelayan|access-date=2022-01-12|website=Antara News|archive-date=2022-01-13|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220113092939/https://sumut.antaranews.com/berita/159422/wali-kota-bagi-es-balok-gratis-kepada-nelayan|url-status=live}}</ref> Other industries in the city include production of foods for livestock.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Bahan Produksi Jagung Butuh 20 Ton Sehari|url=https://m.riausidik.com/read-116472-2016-11-05-bahan-produksi-jagung-butuh-20-ton-sehari.html|access-date=2022-01-12|website=m.riausidik.com|archive-date=2022-01-13|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220113092918/https://m.riausidik.com/read-116472-2016-11-05-bahan-produksi-jagung-butuh-20-ton-sehari.html|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Agency|first=ANTARA News|title=Wali Kota Resmikan Usaha Pakan Ternak - ANTARA News Sumatera Utara|url=https://sumut.antaranews.com/berita/162265/wali-kota-resmikan-usaha-pakan-ternak|access-date=2022-01-12|website=Antara News|archive-date=2022-01-13|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220113092911/https://sumut.antaranews.com/berita/162265/wali-kota-resmikan-usaha-pakan-ternak|url-status=live}}</ref> Gunungsitoli has significant [[tofu]] industry; There are also fisheries-related industries such as fish processing and production of canned fish.<ref name=":10" />

=== Tourism ===
Gunungsitoli is the main gateway to Nias and a hub for tourists before reaching their destinations elsewhere on the island. According to the city government, there are 110 tourist spots inside the city. Despite the decline of the tourist industry following earthquakes, there are still significant international tourist visits, mostly by Australians.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-07-06|title=Wisatawan Australia Dominasi Turis Berkunjung ke Pulau Nias|url=http://niassatu.com/2015/07/06/wisatawan-australia-dominasi-turis-berkunjung-ke-pulau-nias/|access-date=2022-01-12|website=Nias Satu|language=id|archive-date=2021-01-17|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210117195122/http://niassatu.com/2015/07/06/wisatawan-australia-dominasi-turis-berkunjung-ke-pulau-nias/|url-status=live}}</ref> Tourist potential includes Nias culture, as well as beaches and natural spots such as cave and waterfalls.<ref name=":10" /> In 2019, 64,767 tourists—mostly domestic—visited the city. The tourist sector is supported by 23 hotels in the city as of 2019.<ref name=":10" />

=== Finance ===
There are several banks in Gunungsitol such as [[North Sumatra Bank]], [[Bank Rakyat Indonesia]], [[Bank Negara Indonesia]], [[Bank Mandiri]], and [[Bank Danamon]]. There are also several insurance companies—mostly state-owned—such as Jiwasraya and Putra Muda.<ref name=":10" /> The finance sector contributed 3.29% to the city's GRP as of 2021.<ref name=":11" />

The hospital is operated by the Nias Regency government because the city was previously part of that regency. It underwent an expansion in early 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Media|first=Kompas Cyber|date=2021-01-21|title=RSUD Gunungsitoli Berganti Nama Jadi RSUD dr M Thomsen Nias|url=https://regional.kompas.com/read/2021/01/21/21244551/rsud-gunungsitoli-berganti-nama-jadi-rsud-dr-m-thomsen-nias|access-date=2021-05-03|website=KOMPAS.com|language=id|archive-date=2021-05-03|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210503065242/https://regional.kompas.com/read/2021/01/21/21244551/rsud-gunungsitoli-berganti-nama-jadi-rsud-dr-m-thomsen-nias|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=RSUD dr. Convenience store chains such as [[Alfamart]] and [[Indomaret]] opened shops in Gunungsitoli in mid-2020. This received harsh criticism and rejection from many locals. Incumbent mayor Lakhomizaro said he was threatened by an unknown person when attending a Christmas celebration in the city's main church because he had issued convenience store permits.<ref>{{Cite web|title=5 Gerai Alfamidi Beroperasi di Gunungsitoli, Wali Kota Lakhomizaro: Saya Dihujat|url=https://medanbisnisdaily.com/news/online/read/2020/12/31/125955/5_gerai_alfamidi_beroperasi_di_gunungsitoli_wali_kota_lakhomizaro_saya_dihujat/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-05-03|archive-date=2021-01-24|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210124150735/https://medanbisnisdaily.com/news/online/read/2020/12/31/125955/5_gerai_alfamidi_beroperasi_di_gunungsitoli_wali_kota_lakhomizaro_saya_dihujat/}}</ref>

Gunungsitoli's internet connectivity is mostly provided by [[Telkomsel]] though both [[Mobile internet|cellular]] and [[fiber optic]] for its service [[IndiHome]]. The fiber optic service is available in Gunungsitoli, South Gunungsitoli, and Gunungsitoli Idanoi districts. Other providers in the city are [[XL Axiata]] and [[Indosat]]. As of 2019, all the providers are in [[4G]].<ref name=":10" />

== Transportation ==
[[File:A road scene in Gunungsitoli.jpg|thumb|Road scene in Gunungsitoli]]

Gunungsitoli has {{Convert|490.68|km|miles|1|abbr=in}} of roads, most of which are paved with asphalt. Road quality varies, however, because of frequent earthquakes and poor soil condition. According to [[Statistics Indonesia]], in 2019, more than 30% of city's roads were considered damaged. Gunungsitoli is served by [[Binaka Airport]] with regular flights to Medan and [[Jakarta]].<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":2" /><ref name=":10" />

The city has two ports; Angin Port and Roro Siwalubanua II Port, both of which provide service for passengers and container freight. There are regular ferry routes to [[Sibolga]], [[Singkil, Indonesia|Singkil]], and Padang. The city is also served by [[Sea Toll Program]], which has routes to Padang and Jakarta.<ref name=":10" />

Like other Indonesian cities, Gunungsitoli has [[angkot]]s (shared taxis), which are regulated by the city government and use Faekhu Passenger Terminal located at South Gunungsitoli. The terminal is intended both for angkot and buses.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Terminal Faekhu Kota Gunungsitoli Akhirnya Difungsikan|url=https://www.wartanias.com/2017/06/terminal-faekhu-kota-gunungsitoli.html|access-date=2022-01-13|website=Warta Nias|archive-date=2018-04-25|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180425085548/http://www.wartanias.com/2017/06/terminal-faekhu-kota-gunungsitoli.html|url-status=live}}</ref> [[Perum DAMRI]] has a bus route to the town Telukdalam, South Nias.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Sayuti|first=Uspan|date=2021-02-01|title=Bus Damri Telukdalam-Gunungsitoli, Pemkab Nias Selatan dapat Bantuan Bus dari Pemerintah Pusat|url=https://kliksajasumut.co/bus-damri-telukdalam-gunungsitoli-pemkab-nias-selatan-dapat-bantuan-bus-dari-pemerintah-pusat/|access-date=2022-01-13|website=Kliksaja Sumut|language=id|archive-date=2021-05-18|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210518211507/https://kliksajasumut.co/bus-damri-telukdalam-gunungsitoli-pemkab-nias-selatan-dapat-bantuan-bus-dari-pemerintah-pusat/|url-status=live}}</ref>
