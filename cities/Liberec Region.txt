'''Liberec Region''' ({{lang-cs|Liberecký kraj}}) is an administrative unit ([[Czech language|Czech]]: ''[[kraj]]'') of the [[Czech Republic]], located in the northernmost part of its historical region of [[Bohemia]]. It is named after its capital [[Liberec]]. The region shares international borders with Germany and [[Poland]]. Domestically the region borders the [[Ústí nad Labem Region]] to the west, the [[Central Bohemian Region]] to the south and the [[Hradec Králové Region]] to the east.

==Physical geography==
The region's landscape includes the [[Jizera Mountains]], part of the [[Krkonoše]] Mountains and part of the [[Lusatian Mountains]].<ref name=czechcz>{{cite web|url=http://www.czech.cz/en/Discover-CZ/Facts-about-the-Czech-Republic/Geography/The-Liberec-Region|title=The Liberec Region|work=czech.cz|date=13 March 2011|access-date=22 April 2014|archive-date=24 April 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140424180954/http://www.czech.cz/en/Discover-CZ/Facts-about-the-Czech-Republic/Geography/The-Liberec-Region|url-status=dead}}</ref>

The region was affected by flash floods in August 2010, with swelling of the river [[Smědá]] being one of the factors to prompt evacuation efforts in the region.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.praguepost.cz/news/5920-flood-hit-area-in-another-close-call.html|title=Flood-hit area in another close call|work=[[Prague Post]]|date=6 October 2010|access-date=22 April 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://archive.today/20140424130135/http://www.praguepost.cz/news/5920-flood-hit-area-in-another-close-call.html|archive-date=24 April 2014|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

==Demographics==
As of 1 January 2019, the population of the Liberec Region was 442,356 with 217,791 men and 224,565 women, accounting for 49.2% and 50.8% of the population respectively.<ref name=csu/>

==Economy==
The Liberec Region hosts manufacturing, glassmaking, mechanical engineering and jewellery production industries among others.<ref name=czechcz/> The region is part of the so-called [[Black Triangle (region)|Black Triangle]], an area of heavy industrialization and environmental damage on the three-way border of Poland, Germany, and the Czech Republic.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.umwelt.sachsen.de/umwelt/download/luft/Bericht_Luftqualitaet_Schwarzes_Dreieck_2002.pdf |title=Archived copy |access-date=16 April 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140413033921/http://www.umwelt.sachsen.de/umwelt/download/luft/Bericht_Luftqualitaet_Schwarzes_Dreieck_2002.pdf |archive-date=13 April 2014 }} page 9</ref>

==Culture==
[[File:Ještěd.jpg|thumbnail|upright|Ještěd Tower]]
The Liberec Region is home to 11 national cultural monuments including [[Bezděz Castle]], [[Dlaskův statek]] in [[Dolánky u Turnova]] and the [[Ještěd Tower]] which transmits television signals as well as being a hotel.<ref name=cultural-monuments>{{cite web|url=http://www.rozhlas.cz/liberec/informacezliberecka/_zprava/liberecky-kraj-zrejme-ziska-dve-nove-narodni-kulturni-pamatky--1296913|title=Liberecký kraj zřejmě získá dvě nové národní kulturní památky|language=cs|work=[[Czech Radio]]|date=28 December 2013|access-date=22 April 2014}}</ref> 2013 saw the proposal of an additional two sites to the list, those being a glass grinding plant in [[Harrachov]] dating from 1895 and the [[Janatův Mlýn]] [[watermill]] in [[Buřany]], [[Jablonec nad Jizerou]] part of which remains from 1767.<ref name=cultural-monuments/>

A [[Neolithic]] site dating to around 4,500 BC was uncovered in 2007 near the village of [[Příšovice]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.praguepost.cz/archivescontent/3380-neolithic-settlement-found.html|title=Neolithic settlement found|work=[[Prague Post]]|date=18 April 2007|access-date=22 April 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140424162912/http://www.praguepost.cz/archivescontent/3380-neolithic-settlement-found.html|archive-date=24 April 2014|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

[[Lake Mácha]] near the town of [[Doksy]] is an important regional centre for leisure, attracting around 30,000 visitors annually.<ref name=machovo1>{{Cite news|url=http://www.denik.cz/liberecky-kraj/odemceno-u-jezera-zacina-letni-sezona-voda-bude-asi-cistsi-20130520-yuw0.html|title=Odemčeno. U Máchova jezera začíná letní sezóna. Voda bude asi čistší|work=[[Deník]]|date=20 May 2013|access-date=22 April 2014}}</ref> The vicinity of the lake has a caravan park and hosts an annual music festival called ''{{lang|cs|Mácháč}}'', which was attended by approximately 8,000 people in 2013.<ref name=machovo1/><ref name=machovo2>{{Cite web|url=http://zpravy.idnes.cz/loupezna-prepadeni-na-festivalu-machac-dyw-/krimi.aspx?c=A130824_105426_liberec-zpravy_mav|title=Na festivalu Mácháč řádili lupiči. U vstupu přepadli a okradli mladíky|work=[[Mladá fronta DNES]]|date=24 August 2013|access-date=22 April 2014}}</ref>

Since the region was for a long time part of the [[Holy Roman Empire]], and later (in 1938–1945) the major part of the region was a part of [[History of Germany|Germany]] as part of [[Sudetenland]], the local buildings and the culture in general have been influenced by the [[Germans]], also considering the fact that parts of the region were populated by a majority of [[Sudeten Germans]], prior to their [[Expulsion of Germans from Czechoslovakia|expulsion after World War II]].

==Transport==
The region is linked to Prague by the [[D10 motorway (Czech Republic)|D10 motorway]], which terminates in the town of Turnov. The length of operated railway lines in the region is {{convert|551|km|mi|abbr=on}}.<ref name=transport2013>{{cite web|url=http://www.czso.cz/csu/2013edicniplan.nsf/t/3E0036B975/$File/511011131701.xls|title=Doprava|trans-title=Transport|publisher=[[Czech Statistical Office]]|access-date=22 April 2014|format=XLS}}</ref> Three airports are in the region; [[Hradčany Airport]], a disused military airport near the town of [[Ralsko (Česká Lípa District)|Ralsko]], is the most significant. Other minor airports exist in Česká Lípa and [[Hodkovice nad Mohelkou]].

==Education==
The region is home to the [[public university|public]] [[Technical University of Liberec]], which was founded in 1953. The university, which originally specialized in Mechanical and Textile Engineering, added a further four faculties in the 1990s, namely those specialising in Education, Economy, Architecture and [[Mechatronics]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ceskatelevize.cz/ct24/regiony/199069-technicka-univerzita-v-liberci-otevrela-specialni-vyvojove-centrum/|title=Technická univerzita v Liberci otevřela speciální vývojové centrum|language=cs|publisher=[[Czech Television]]|date=10 October 2012|access-date=22 April 2014}}</ref>
