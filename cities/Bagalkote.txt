'''Bagalkote''', is a city in the state of Karnataka, India, which is also the headquarters of [[Bagalkot district]]. It is situated on branch of [[Ghataprabha River|River Ghataprabha]], The population of the urban agglomeration was 111,933<ref name="ReferenceA">"District Census Handbook – Guntur"</ref> according to the provisional results of 2011 national census of India, the city is spread over an area of {{convert|49.06|km2}}<ref name="ReferenceA" /> with an average elevation of {{cvt|532|m}} above MSL.

==History==

===Existence of the city===
According to stone inscriptions in the surrounding area, the town's name was formerly '''Bagadige'''. According to legend, the town was given to the Bhajantries (musicians) by [[Ravana]], the king of Lanka who ruled this area. One of the [[Adil Shahi dynasty|Bijapur Adil Shahi]] king [[Ibrahim Adil Shah II]] is said to have presented the town to his daughter as ''bangle money'' (a tradition in which the daughter is given money to buy bangles, sarees, and gold ornaments from her parents after the marriage).<ref name="ReferenceB">Busateenus-Salateen a Persian Manuscript of Mirza Ibrahim Zubairi.</ref>

===Rulers===
Bagalkot remained under successive dominions of Vijayanagar emperors, [[Adil Shahi dynasty|Bijapur Adil Shahi]], [[Maratha Empire|Peshwas]], [[Kingdom of Mysore]], Maratha rulers, and finally the British in 1818. In 1865, it was established as a municipality and civic amenities were provided to the residents of Bagalkot.

==Geography==
Bagalkot is located at {{Coord|16.18|N|75.7|E}}.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.fallingrain.com/world/IN/19/Bagalkot.html|title=Maps, Weather, and Airports for Bagalkot, India|website=www.fallingrain.com}}</ref> It has an average elevation of {{convert|533|m|ft}}. It is situated on the bank of the river [[Ghataprabha River|Ghataprabha]].

At the time of the 2011 census, Bagalkot had a population of 111,933. Bagalkot city has a sex ratio of 985 females to males and a literacy rate of 85.40%. Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes made up 10.33% and 3.78% of the population respectively.<ref name="districtcensus">{{cite web |year=2011 |title=District Census Handbook: Bagalkot |url=https://censusindia.gov.in/nada/index.php/catalog/582/download/1976/DH_2011_2902_PART_A_DCHB_BAGALKOT.pdf |access-date= |website=censusindia.gov.in |publisher=[[Registrar General and Census Commissioner of India]]}}</ref>
