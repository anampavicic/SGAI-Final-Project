'''Cabinda''', also known as '''Chioua''',<ref name="LaGamma">{{cite book|title=Kongo – Power and Majesty|author=LaGamma, Alisa|location=New York|publisher=The Metropolitan Museum of Art |date=2015}}</ref> is a city and a municipality located in the [[Cabinda Province]], an [[exclave]] of [[Angola]]. Angolan sovereignty over Cabinda is disputed by the [[secession]]ist [[Republic of Cabinda]]. The city of Cabinda had a population of 550,000<ref>[https://www.citypopulation.de/en/angola/cities/ Citypopulation.de] Population of the major cities in Angola</ref> and the municipality a population of 624,646, at the 2014 Census. The residents of the city are known as ''Cabindas'' or ''Fiotes''. Cabinda, due to its proximity to rich oil reserves, serves as one of Angola's main oil ports.<ref name="brit"/><ref name="info"/>

==History==
The city was founded by the [[Portuguese Empire|Portuguese]] in 1883 after the signing of the [[Treaty of Simulambuco]], in the same period as the [[Berlin Conference]].
Cabinda was an embarkation point for slaves to Brazil.

There are considerable offshore [[oil reserves]] nearby.

==Geography==
Cabinda is located on the [[Atlantic Ocean]] coast in the south of [[Cabinda Province]], and sits on the right bank of the [[Bele River]].<ref name="brit"/>

According to the [[Köppen climate classification]], Cabinda is a [[tropical savanna climate]].

It is {{Convert|56|km|mi}} north of [[Moanda, Democratic Republic of the Congo|Moanda]] ([[Democratic Republic of the Congo|Congo-Kinshasa]]), {{convert|70|km|mi}} north of [[Congo River]] estuary and {{convert|137|km|mi}} south of [[Pointe-Noire]] ([[Republic of the Congo|Congo-Brazzaville]]).<ref name="maps" />

==Districts==
The city of Cabinda is divided into three districts, or ''comuna'':

*Cabinda, the city seat, with 88.6% of the population the city
*Malembo, with 3.1%
*Tando-Zinze, with 8.3%<ref name="post"/>

== Education and science ==
Cabinda is home to two public higher education institutions, namely the [[11 de Novembro University]] and the Higher Institute of Education Sciences of Cabinda.
In addition, it has campuses of the [[Lusíada University]] and the [[Private University of Angola]].

==Culture==
The city's population has a distinctive culture from its way of dressing and eating to traditional rituals, especially Chicumbe and celebrated ceremonies of ''Bakamas do Tchizo'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.gpcabinda.com/noticiasnsimbote.cfm?id=23|title=Cidade de Cabinda comemora 52 anos|publisher=Governo da Província de Cabinda|access-date=2019-03-19|trans-title=Cabinda city celebrates 52 years|language=pt}}{{Dead link|date=June 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> a traditional ritual that enables the interaction between the living and the occult spirits of the gods and the ancestors, thus ensuring the reconciliation between the dead and the living.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.teiaportuguesa.com/cabinda/cabinda.htm |title=Uma excursão virtual a Cabinda |publisher=Teia Portuguesa |access-date=2019-03-19 |trans-title=A virtual tour to Cabinda |language=pt |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080725021328/http://www.teiaportuguesa.com/cabinda/cabinda.htm |archive-date=2008-07-25 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Language==
Since Portugal colonized Cabinda later than the rest of Angola, Portuguese, the official language of Angola, is not yet widely spoken, although Portuguese speakers are rapidly growing in number. Portuguese is used mostly in official or administrative settings. It is [[Ibinda]], a [[Bantu languages|Bantu language]], that is the primary language of both the city and province of Cabinda.<ref name="info"/>
