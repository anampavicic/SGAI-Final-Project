'''Hawalli Governorate''' ({{langx|ar|محافظة حولي}} {{translit|ar|Muḥāfaẓat Ḥawalli}}) is one of the six [[governorates of Kuwait]], consisting of the following [[Areas of Kuwait|areas]]:<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kuwait-info.com/map/tou_hawalli.htm|title=Kuwait Info - Information About Kuwait Tourism and Organizations|publisher=kuwait-info.com}}</ref>
*[[Hawally, Kuwait|Hawalli]]
*[[Bayan, Kuwait|Bayan]]
*[[Mishref District|Mishref]]
*[[Maidan Hawalli]]
*[[Jabriya]]
*[[Rumaithiya]]
*[[Salmiya]]
*[[Salwa, Kuwait|Salwa]]
* Sha'ab
* Al-Salam
* Hattin
* Al-Zahra
* Mubarak Al-Abdullah
* Al-Jaber
* Al-Shuhada
* Al-Badae
* Al-Siddiq
[[Nawaf Al-Ahmad Al-Jaber Al-Sabah]] became governor in 1962.<ref name="Rush1987">{{cite book|author=Alan Rush|title=Al-Sabah: History & Genealogy of Kuwait's Ruling Family, 1752-1987|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=6Yk4yJvaLSQC|year= 1987|publisher=[[Ithaca Press]] |location=London |isbn=978-0-86372-081-9}}</ref> A more recent [[governor]] of the Hawalli governorate is [[Ahmad Nawaf Al-Ahmad Al-Sabah]], who later became prime minister.

A 2005 estimate reported the population of Hawalli to be 393,861.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.geohive.com/cd/link.php?xml=kw&xsl=neo1 |title=GeoHive : Kuwait |accessdate=9 August 2005 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20050823065428/http://www.geohive.com/cd/link.php?xml=kw&xsl=neo1 |archivedate=23 August 2005 }}</ref>

A 31 December 2007 estimate reported Hawalli's population to be 714,876.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.paci.gov.kw/Sttc/tblanalis02.aspx?Page=tblpopbygovnatsex|title=الهيئة العامة للمعلومات المدنية|publisher=paci.hov.kw|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080505220320/http://www.paci.gov.kw/Sttc/tblanalis02.aspx?Page=tblpopbygovnatsex|archivedate=5 May 2008}}</ref>

As of June 2014, the population of Hawalli is estimated to be 890,533.<ref name="stat.paci.gov.kw"/>
