'''Cuautitlán Izcalli''' ({{IPA|es|kwawtiˈtlan isˈkaʎi}}) is a city and one of the [[Municipalities of the State of Mexico|125 municipalities]] that make up the [[State of Mexico]]. Its municipal seat is Cuautitlán Izcalli. It is located in the [[Valley of Mexico]] area, and is part of the [[Metropolitan area of the Valley of Mexico|Metropolitan area of Mexico City]]. It borders to the north and northwest with [[Tepotzotlán]], to the northeast and to the east with [[Cuautitlán]], to the south with [[Tlalnepantla de Baz]], to the southeast with [[Tultitlán]], to the southwest with [[Atizapán de Zaragoza]] and to the west with the municipality of [[:es:Municipio de Nicolás Romero|Nicolás Romero]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mapnall.com/es/Mapa-Cuautitl%C3%A1n-Izcalli_1144785.html|title=Mapa - Cuautitlán Izcalli (Cuautitlán Izcalli)|access-date=19 April 2023|work=MapNall}}</ref><ref name="EncMuc">{{cite web|url=http://www.e-local.gob.mx/work/templates/enciclo/mexico/mpios/15121a.htm |title=Enciclopedia de los municipios de Mexico Estado de Mexico Cuautitlán Izcalli |access-date=2008-03-07 |language=es-MX |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070930032603/http://www.e-local.gob.mx/work/templates/enciclo/mexico/mpios/15121a.htm |archive-date=2007-09-30}}</ref>

== Identity elements ==

=== Shield ===
It is formed by a red triangle, fragmented in the center of each of its sides and with rounded corners, which represents the conjunction of the municipalities of Cuautitlán, [[Tepotzotlán]] and [[Tultitlán]]; Inside the triangle, a white circle stands out with ramifications towards each of the sides that form it. Forming a single image, a letter «C», in green, and a letter «I», in black, appear integrated.<ref name="símbolos">{{Cite web|url=https://izcalli.info/conoces-los-simbolos-dan-identidad-cuautitlan-izcalli/|title=¿Conoces los símbolos que dan Identidad a Cuautitlán Izcalli?|access-date=19 April 2023|work=Izcalli.info|language=es-MX|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220521182026/https://izcalli.info/conoces-los-simbolos-dan-identidad-cuautitlan-izcalli/|archive-date=21 May 2022|url-status=live}}</ref>

=== Glyph ===
The [[glyph]] is formed by a tree with a tooth open in the center of the trunk, and means "abundance between the heads". The [[Spindle (textiles)|spindles]] or [[Spindle whorl|malacates]] with the [[cotton]] are the glyph that represented the goddess [[Tlazolteotl]], protector of the [[loom|weavers]], and ''Izcalli'' is represented by the glyph ''calli'', “house”.<ref name="símbolos"/>

==History==
There is proof that the place that the municipality occupies today has been important for more than two thousand years, when indigenous groups, including hunters [[nomadic]]s and settled peoples, such as the [[Teotihuacán|teotihuacanos]], the [[Toltecs]], [[Chichimecas]] and [[Olmecs]], occupied the territory. With the defeat and [[Fall of Tenochtitlan|fall of Mexico-Tenochtitlan]] in 1521, the [[kingdom of Spain]] exercised its power in the newly conquered regions. [[Hernán Cortés]] entrusted the town of Cuautitlán to [[:es:Alonso de Ávila|Alonso de Ávila]] and this, to his brother [[Gil González Dávila]]. During this time, Cuautitlán was one of the first places where the [[Franciscan friars]] taught their [[doctrine]], because a nephew of [[Moctezuma Xocoyotzin]], the ''Señor de Tenayuca'' ({{langx|en|Lord of Tenayuca}}), reigned there.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://arqueologiamexicana.mx/mexico-antiguo/la-vida-de-don-martin-cortes-moctezuma-nezahualtecolotzin-uno-de-los-hijos-de |title=La vida de don Martín Cortés Moctezuma Nezahualtecolotzin, uno de los hijos de Moctezuma II |access-date=20 April 2023 |first=María |last=Castañeda de la Paz |language=es-MX |work=arqueologiamexicana |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221204010745/https://arqueologiamexicana.mx/mexico-antiguo/la-vida-de-don-martin-cortes-moctezuma-nezahualtecolotzin-uno-de-los-hijos-de |archive-date=4 December 2022}}</ref>

In the period of the [[Mexican War of Independence|independence war]], in order to control by the Spanish the participation of the “rebels” in the war, a proclamation was issued to the sub-delegate of Cuautitlán, offering the grace of pardon to all those who belonged to its jurisdiction and who participated in the movement. Already promulgated, in 1827, the Political Constitution of the Free and Sovereign State of Mexico, the Departmental Board decreed on December 23, 1837, that the department of Mexico be divided into 13 districts, of which the fourth was Cuautitlán.<ref name="lugares">{{cite web|url=https://lugaresquever.com/wiki/cuautitlan-izcalli?spmchkbj=spmprvbj4crh2YYOvdAHd45TWgFktCXOh8 |title=Cuautitlán Izcalli |access-date=21 April 2023 |work=lugaresquever |language=es-MX |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/archive/Lh6jn |archive-date= 20 April 2023 |url-status=live}}</ref>

In 1855, nine districts and 33 districts were delimited, and the fifth was named [[Tlalnepantla de Baz|Tlalnepantla]], made up of that locality, Cuautitlán, [[Tlalpan]] and [[Zumpango]]. The history and strategic location gave rise to the original towns of the municipality: [[:es:Axotlán|Axotlán]], [[:es:San Juan Atlamica|San Juan Atlamica]], La Aurora, Santa Bárbara Tlacatecpan, [[:es:San José Huilango|San José Huilango]], San Lorenzo Riotenco, [[:es:San Mateo Ixtacalco|San Mateo Ixtacalco]], San Martín Tepetlixpan, Santa María Tianguistengo, San Francisco Tepojaco, Santiago Tepalcapa, El Rosario,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.digitalmex.mx/cultura-y-espectaculos/story/1502/preservan-tradiciones-en-el-rosario-pueblo-fundador-de-cuautitlan-izcalli |title=Preservan tradiciones en El Rosario, pueblo fundador de Cuautitlán Izcalli |first= Juan |last=Guerrero |date=9 October 2017 |access-date=20 April 2023 |work=DigitalMex |language=es-MX |url-status=live |archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20220330074945/https://www.digitalmex.mx/cultura-y-espectaculos/story/1502/preservan-tradiciones-en-el-rosario-pueblo-fundador-de-cuautitlan-izcalli |archive-date=30 March 2022}}</ref> and San Sebastián Xhala.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://experiencia.edomex.gob.mx/recursos_turisticos/mostrarDetalleRecursos/2417 |title=Pueblo de San Sebastián Xhala, Cuautitlán Izcalli |access-date=20 April 2023 |work=experiencia.edomex.gob |language=es-MX |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220821063423/https://experiencia.edomex.gob.mx/recursos_turisticos/mostrarDetalleRecursos/2417 |archive-date=21 August 2022}}</ref>

To keep the workers close to the various factories that flourished in the city, especially in the town of La Aurora, 225 [[adobe]] or [[tepetate]] houses were built around the facilities known as [[ranchería]]s. They had services such as: [[drinking water]], [[drainage]], bathrooms, a [[Company store|tienda de raya]] ({{langx|en|raya store}}) called "La Vizcaya", a school (they were provided with supplies such as notebooks, pencils, books), streets and passenger cars for their transportation. La Vizcaya was the first factory that existed in this direction of Cuautitlán and that shipped products by rail.<ref name="dup-0">{{cite web|url=http://soloizcalli.com/pueblo-la-aurora/ |title=Pueblo la Aurora |access-date=20 April 2023 |work=soloizcalli |language=es-MX |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221207060644/https://soloizcalli.com/pueblo-la-aurora/ |archive-date=7 December 2022}}</ref>

In 1965, Gonzalo Monroy Ortega, then municipal president of [[Cuautitlán]], asked the local legislature to make the colony of ''La Aurora'' disappear to become part of the town of La Aurora, but this request was not answered positively, because the two towns are only separated by a canal. On April 1, 1961, La Vizcaya closed due to the competition that arose at that time with the production of products derived from [[petroleum]], which reduced the sale and distribution of [[coastal]]s of [[natural fiber]]s.<ref name="dup-0" />

On July 31, 1971, the then President of the Republic, [[Luis Echeverría Álvarez]], laid the first stone of the city, located next to what is now the [[Charlotte Yazbek|Parque de las Esculturas]].<ref name="planos">{{cite web|url=http://periodicodeizcalli.com.mx/2016/2-historia/ |title=Historia de Cuautitlán Izcalli |access-date=20 April 2023 |work= periodicodeizcalli |language=es-MX |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230420214155/http://periodicodeizcalli.com.mx/2016/2-historia/ |archive-date=20 April 2023 |url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://soloizcalli.com/piedra-angular-de-cuautitlan-izcalli-el-inicio-de-una-historia/ |title=Piedra angular de Cuautitlán Izcalli, el inicio de una historia |date=3 May 2013 |access-date=20 April 2023 |work=soloizcalli |author=Soulengi |language=es-MX |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230420214504/https://soloizcalli.com/piedra-angular-de-cuautitlan-izcalli-el-inicio-de-una-historia/ |archive-date=20 April 2023 |url-status=bot: unknown }}</ref> Originally, Cuautitlán Izcalli was planned to be the first self-sufficient city in the [[Greater Mexico City|metropolitan area of the Valley of Mexico]], and for this, a large part of the territory of existing municipalities was taken, such as [[Cuautitlán]], [[Tepotzotlán]], [[Tultitlán]] and [[Atizapán de Zaragoza]]. Like many other projects of the time, one of the objectives of building this city was the regulation of population growth and the reduction of congestion in the metropolitan area.<ref name="llano">{{cite web|url=https://www.eluniversal.com.mx/galeria/metropoli/cdmx/la-ciudad-en-el-tiempo-cuautitlan-izcalli-cumple-45-anos/ |title=La Ciudad en el Tiempo: Cuautitlán Izcalli cumple 45 años |date=7 July 2018 |access-date=20 April 2023 |work= El Universal |language=es-MX |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220925193506/https://www.eluniversal.com.mx/galeria/metropoli/cdmx/la-ciudad-en-el-tiempo-cuautitlan-izcalli-cumple-45-anos |archive-date= 25 September 2022}}</ref> The city was created using the plans of European and American cities as a base,<ref name="planos"/> which helped to transform the land into appropriate areas for the establishment of work, service and housing centers, with the idea of having sports areas and industrial, residential and green areas.<ref name="llano"/>

On May 1, 1972, President Luis Echeverría, together with the then Governor of the State of Mexico [[Carlos Hank González]], made the symbolic delivery of 225 houses, and also began to operate the first office of [[Telegraphy|telegraphs]] of Cuautitlán Izcalli; but it was not until August 21 of the same year, when the keys to the first 227 houses were handed over to normal school teachers from the entity and the first “Conasuper” shopping center was inaugurated in the vicinity of Plaza Isidro Fabela, in Cumbria.<ref name="inició">{{cite web|url=https://www.periodicopuntomedio.com/asi-lo-recuerdo-colaboracion-especial-por-46-aniversario-de-izcalli/ |title=ASÍ LO RECUERDO…. Colaboración especial por 46 Aniversario de Izcalli |date=28 June 2019 |access-date=20 April 2023 |language=es-MX |work=periodicopuntomedio |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230201033407/https://www.periodicopuntomedio.com/asi-lo-recuerdo-colaboracion-especial-por-46-aniversario-de-izcalli/ |archive-date=1 February 2023 |url-status=live}}</ref>

Decree Number 71 of the H. XLV Legislature of the [[State of Mexico]], signed on June 22, 1973,<ref name="cita">{{cite web|url=https://legislacion.edomex.gob.mx/sites/legislacion.edomex.gob.mx/files/files/pdf/gct/1973/nov243.pdf |title=Gaceta del Gobierno |date=24 November 1973 |access-date=20 April 2023 |language=es-MX |work=legislacion.edomex |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230420210640/https://legislacion.edomex.gob.mx/sites/legislacion.edomex.gob.mx/files/files/pdf/gct/1973/nov243.pdf |archive-date=20 April 2023}}</ref> stipulates that the official name of [[municipality]] number 121 of the State of Mexico is Cuautitlán Izcalli:<ref>{{cite web|url=https://legislacion.edomex.gob.mx/node/30885 |title=Periódico Oficial "Gaceta del Gobierno" Sección Tercera
|date=24 November 1973 |access-date=20 April 2023 |language=es-MX |work=legislacion.edomex |url-status=live  |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230420211016/https://legislacion.edomex.gob.mx/node/30885 |archive-date=20 April 2023}}</ref>

({{langx|en|"The Municipality of Cuautitlán Izcalli, Mexico, is created and the polygonal is delimited that, based on the reference points in the aforementioned discharge decree, serve as limits to this municipality."}}).<ref name="cita"/>|Congreso del Estado de México, XLV Legislatura}}

Once the official designation of the city was finalized, the first political administration of the municipality was headed by the lawyer Gabriel Marcelino Ezeta Moll, who began his activities with a limited budget that amounted to 3 million 103 thousand 166 pesos until the end of 1973.<ref name="inició"/> Serving in the role of ruler from 1973 to 1976,<ref name="Ezeta">{{cite web|url=https://www.elsoldetoluca.com.mx/local/fallece-el-notario-publico-gabriel-ezeta-moll-por-covid-19-6185400.html |title=Fallece el notario público Gabriel Ezeta Moll por Covid-19 |date=29 December 2020 |access-date=20 April 2023 |first=Sofía |last=San Juan |quote=Participó en la creación del municipio de Cuautitlán Izcalli, lo cual le permitió ser su primer presidente municipal en los años 1973 a 1976, cuando sólo tenía 28 años de edad |trans-quote=He participated in the creation of the municipality of Cuautitlán Izcalli, which allowed him to be its first municipal president in the years 1973 to 1976, when he was only 28 years old |work= El Sol de Toluca |language=es-MX |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201229200018/https://www.elsoldetoluca.com.mx/local/fallece-el-notario-publico-gabriel-ezeta-moll-por-covid-19-6185400.html |archive-date=29 December 2020 |url-status=live}}</ref> the first seats of government were established first in the "Calmecac" secondary school in Cumbria, then in four houses on Jocotitlán street, later in some offices installed in the upper part of a sub-commercial center located on Jilotzingo street, and Finally, it was not until 1976 that a government building was established that began to function as the municipal presidency. The headquarters was inaugurated by Luis Cuauhtémoc Riojas Guajardo, who was elected as the second municipal president of Izcalli after the end of the term of governance of Gabriel Marcelino.<ref name="inició"/>

=== Religion ===
Cuautitlán Izcalli is one of the municipalities with the largest number of patron saint festivities of Catholic worship in the region.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://soloizcalli.com/festividades_cizcalli-php/|title=Calendario anual de festividades cívicas|work=soloizcalli|date=11 September 2013|access-date=21 April 2023|language=es-MX|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211104060406/https://soloizcalli.com/festividades_cizcalli-php/|archive-date=4 November 2021|url-status=live}}</ref>

On June 9, 2014, Pope [[Pope Francis|Francis]] created the [[Roman Catholic Diocese of Izcalli|Diocese of Izcalli]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.20minutos.com.mx/noticia/14697/0/papa-francisco/nueva-diocesis/izcalli-estado-mexico/|title=El papa Francisco crea una diócesis en Izcalli en el Estado de México y designa obispo|work=20minutos|date=9 June 2014|access-date=21 April 2023|language=es-MX|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211104060616/https://www.20minutos.com.mx/noticia/14697/0/papa-francisco/nueva-diocesis/izcalli-estado-mexico/|archive-date=4 November 2021|url-status=live}}</ref>

Cuautitlán Izcalli has several temples, two of which date from the XVII century:

* San Lorenzo Río Tenco (also part of the XVI century)<ref>{{cite web|url=https://experiencia.edomex.gob.mx/recursos_turisticos/mostrarDetalleRecursos/2388|title=Pueblo de San Lorenzo Río Tenco, Cuautitlán Izcalli|work=Edomex|language=es-MX|access-date=21 April 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220225012821/https://experiencia.edomex.gob.mx/recursos_turisticos/mostrarDetalleRecursos/2388|archive-date=25 February 2022|url-status=live}}</ref> 
* Santa Bárbara Tlacatecpan<ref>{{cite web|url=https://experiencia.edomex.gob.mx/recursos_turisticos/mostrarDetalleRecursos/2416|title=Pueblo de Santa Bárbara Tlacatecpan, Cuautitlán Izcalli|work=Edomex|language=es-MX|access-date=21 April 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220821063430/https://experiencia.edomex.gob.mx/recursos_turisticos/mostrarDetalleRecursos/2416|archive-date=21 August 2022|url-status=live}}</ref>

And two from the XVIII century:

* San Juan Atlamica<ref>{{cite web|url=https://experiencia.edomex.gob.mx/recursos_turisticos/mostrarDetalleRecursos/2372|title=Pueblo de San Juan Atlamica, Cuautitlán Izcalli|work=Edomex|language=es-MX|access-date=21 April 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220221014605/https://experiencia.edomex.gob.mx/recursos_turisticos/mostrarDetalleRecursos/2372|archive-date=21 February 2022|url-status=live}}</ref>

== Geography ==
It has a territorial extension of 109.54 square kilometers, it represents 0.5% of the surface of the State of Mexico.<ref name="geo">{{cite web|url=http://www.inafed.gob.mx/work/enciclopedia/EMM15mexico/municipios/15121a.html|title=Cuautitlán Izcalli|work=inafed|access-date=22 April 2023|language=es-MX|archive-date=25 July 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210725175817/http://www.inafed.gob.mx/work/enciclopedia/EMM15mexico/municipios/15121a.html|url-status=dead}}</ref>

The highest portions are located to the south of the municipality at a maximum height of 2430 m s. n. m. (metros sobre el nivel del mar, English: meters above sea level) and the lowest is to the west with 2200 m s. n. m., the municipal seat is at 2280 m s. n. m.<ref name="geo"/>

=== Location and access ===
The municipality of Cuautitlán Izcalli is located in the northwestern part of the [[:es:Cuenca de México|Mexico Basin]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://caem.edomex.gob.mx/sites/caem.edomex.gob.mx/files/files/AtlasInundaciones/Atlas2018/ValleMexico/MapaVM.pdf |title=Mapa de la Cuenca de México |access-date=22 April 2023 | work=caem.edomex.gob.mx |language=es-MX |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230422184111/http://caem.edomex.gob.mx/sites/caem.edomex.gob.mx/files/files/AtlasInundaciones/Atlas2018/ValleMexico/MapaVM.pdf |archive-date=22 April 2023}}</ref> It has a territorial extension of 109,924&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.municipios.mx/mexico/cuautitlan-izcalli/ |title=Municipio de Cuautitlán Izcalli |access-date=22 April 2023 |language=es-MX | work=Municipios.MX |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220816094054/http://municipios.mx/mexico/cuautitlan-izcalli/ |archive-date=16 August 2022}}</ref>

Its location is along the infrastructure chain formed by the [[:es:Autopista México-Querétaro|Mexico-Querétaro highway]], México-Pachuca, México-Puebla, Chamapa-Lechería, and Peñón-Texcoco, with which a large number of areas are connected conurbations through high-specification highways, and integrated in turn, towards the regions of [[Toluca]], [[Puebla]], [[Hidalgo (state)|Hidalgo]] and [[Querétaro]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://saascaem.edomex.gob.mx/circuito-exterior-mexiquense |title=Circuito Exterior Mexiquense
|access-date=22 April 2023 | work=saascaem.edomex.gob.mx |language=es-MX |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230204062501/http://saascaem.edomex.gob.mx/circuito-exterior-mexiquense |archive-date=4 February 2023}}</ref> Izcalli is also connected to the [[Tren Suburbano]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://t21.com.mx/ferroviario/2005/08/29/tren-suburbano-incluira-amplio-centro-comercial |title=El tren suburbano incluirá un amplio centro comercial |language=es-MX |date=29 August 2005 |access-date=22 April 2023 |first=José |last=Iniesta | work=T21 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230422184856/https://t21.com.mx/ferroviario/2005/08/29/tren-suburbano-incluira-amplio-centro-comercial |archive-date=22 April 2023}}</ref>

It has four entrances: the entrance of Constitución avenue, Chalma avenue, Adolfo López Mateos avenue, México Cuautitlán avenue.<ref name="lugares"/> The authorities of Cuautitlán Izcalli, in coordination with the government of the State of Mexico, built the James Watt bridge, which increased by 75 percent the capacity of vehicles at the entrance of the Mexico-Cuautitlán highway.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dictec.com.mx/concluidos_Puente_vehicular_James_Watt/ |title=Puente vehicular James Watt |access-date=22 April 2023 | work=Dictec |language=es-MX |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221006213506/https://www.dictec.com.mx/concluidos_Puente_vehicular_James_Watt/ |archive-date=6 October 2022}}</ref>

=== Hydrography ===
[[File:Laguna de Axotlán y Sierra de Tepotzotlán.JPG|thumb|Laguna de Axotlán in 2011]]
The principal water currents in Izcalli are the [[:es:Río Cuautitlán|río Cuautitlán]], which crosses an approximate extension of 40&nbsp;km of the municipal territory, and the Hondo de Tepotzotlán River. The main bodies of water in the municipality are:<ref name="geo"/>

* Presa de Guadalupe ({{langx|en|Guadalupe Dam}})
* Laguna de la Piedad ({{langx|en|Lagoon of Mercy}})
* Espejo de los Lirios ({{langx|en|Mirror of the Lilies}})
* Presa de Angulo ({{langx|en|Angulo Dam}})
* Laguna de Axotlán ({{langx|en|Axotlan Lagoon}})

=== Lago de Guadalupe ===
El lago de Guadalupe ({{langx|en|lake of Guadalupe}}) is a dam that was built between 1936 and 1943, for flood control and irrigation. It extends over 348 hectares, being 2,200 meters above sea level. It belongs to the basin of the [[Moctezuma River]].<ref name="presa">{{cite web|url=https://riosdelplaneta.com/lago-de-guadalupe/|title=Conoce todo acerca del Lago de Guadalupe en México|work=riosdelplaneta.com|date=27 May 2020|access-date=22 April 2023 |language=es-MX |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220927094518/https://riosdelplaneta.com/lago-de-guadalupe/|archive-date=27 September 2022|url-status=live}}</ref>

The dam is the second most important body of water in the Valley of Mexico, and is supplied by the Cuautitlán, Xinte and San Pedro rivers.<ref name="presa"/> Starting in 2006, as a result of the death of hundreds of fish between the months of May 2004 and February 2005, the Guadalupe Dam Basin Commission was created, with the aim of restoring the natural resources of this sub-basin, in addition to promoting sustainable development, having as its main axis this water resource.<ref name="presa"/>

=== Ecosystems ===
[[File:Caballos alimentándose..JPG|thumb|Horses around lago de Guadalupe]]
The municipality has a great variety of vegetation, mainly made up of forests and grasslands. The former occupy a land area of approximately 451 hectares. The main reason why the municipality has several green areas is because the initial construction lands were covered by several [[plains]].<ref name="llano"/>
