'''Ixtapaluca''' is a city and a [[municipalities of Mexico|municipality]] in the eastern part of the [[State of Mexico]] in [[Mexico]]. It lies between [[Mexico City]] and the western border of the state of [[Puebla]]. The name Ixtapaluca means "Where the salt gets wet".<ref name="EncMuc">{{cite web|url=http://www.e-local.gob.mx/work/templates/enciclo/mexico/mpios/15039a.htm |title=Enciclopedia de los Municipios de Mexico Estado de Mexico Huixquilucan |access-date=2008-03-08 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070927210055/http://www.e-local.gob.mx/work/templates/enciclo/mexico/mpios/15039a.htm |archive-date=2007-09-27 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

As of 2006, Ixta included part of the world's largest mega-[[slum]], along with [[Valle de Chalco|Chalco]] and [[Ciudad Nezahualcóyotl|Neza]].<ref>[[:en:Mike Davis (scholar)|Mike Davis]], ''Planet of Slums'', La Découverte, Paris, 2006 ({{ISBN|978-2-7071-4915-2}}), p. 31.</ref>

==The city==

At the census of 2005 the city had a population of 290,076.<ref name="INEGI">{{cite web|url=http://www.inegi.gob.mx/est/contenidos/espanol/sistemas/conteo2005/localidad/iter/default.asp?s=est&c=10395 |title=Principales resultados por localidad 2005 (ITER) |access-date=2008-03-08 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110613185456/http://www.inegi.gob.mx/est/contenidos/espanol/sistemas/conteo2005/localidad/iter/default.asp?s=est&c=10395 |archive-date=2011-06-13 }}</ref>

The parish of Ixtapaluca was founded in 1531 and had great prominence in the area. The municipal palace was built in 1973.<ref name="EncMuc"/>

==The municipality==
As municipal seat, the town of Ixtpaluca has governing jurisdiction over the following communities: Acozac, Ampliación San Francisco, Cabaña de los Medina, Camino a Mina Milagro (El Potrero), Camino Mina Rosita, Cerro de la Abundancia, Coatepec, Colonia Julio Chávez López (UPREZ), Colonia Tetitla, Ejido el Capulín, Ejido San Francisco (Las Joyas), Ejidos de Xalpa (Camino de los Alcanfores), El Campamento (Las Cocinas)  El Carmen, El Corazón, El Cuarenta, El Jaral (El Capulín), El Patronato del Maguey (Santa Rosa), El Pozo del Venado, El Treinta y Nueve (Dos Jagüeyes), El Treinta y Siete (Kilómetro Diecinueve), General Manuel Ávila Camacho, Huertas de Canutillo, Jorge Jiménez Cantú, La Cañada, La Cañada, La Espinita (Parque Industrial la Espinita), La Granja, La Guadalupana, La Mesa, Linderos de Ixtapaluca (El Tablón), Llano Grande (Rancho Viejo), Loma del Rayo (Chocolines Segunda Sección), Lomas de Coatepec, Los Cedros, Los Hornos, Los Lavaderos, Los Vergeles, Piedra Grande (Las Cabañas), Pueblo Nuevo (San Isidro Labrador), Puente del Tablón, Puente el Mezquite, Rancho el Guarda, Rancho Francisco Santillán (Atzizintla Tres), Rancho la Pastoría, Rancho la Peña, Rancho Loma Ancha, Rancho los Gavilanes, Rancho San Isidro, Rancho Venta Nueva, Rancho Verde, [[Río Frío de Juárez]], Ricardo Calva, [[San Buenaventura, México|San Buenaventura]], [[San Francisco Acuautla]], [[San Jerónimo Cuatro Vientos]], Santa Ana, Santa Bárbara, Tierras Comunales (Cerro de Ayotla), Villas de Escalerillas (Bellavista), and Zoquiapan.

The municipality had a population of 429,033 in 2005. The area of the municipality is 315.10&nbsp;km² (121.66 sq mi).<ref name="INEGI"/> The municipality is part of the Mexico City [[metropolitan areas of Mexico|metropolitan area]]. It is an important suburb because many people that work in Mexico City live in Ixtapaluca, and it has developed recent commercial interest due to this. The first "fraccionamiento" or subdivision called San José de la Palma, Municipio de Ixtapaluca, was built in 1989. The municipality is bordered by [[Chalco de Díaz Covarrubias|Chalco]], [[Valle de Chalco Solidaridad]], [[Chicoloapan]], [[La Paz, State of Mexico|La Paz]], [[Texcoco, México|Texcoco]], [[Tlalmanalco]], and the state of [[Puebla]].<ref name="EncMuc"/>

The area was first settled in 1286 by the [[Mexica]]s. After the Spanish conquest, in 1543 Ixtapaluca was given to the control of  Juan de Cuellar, who continued the indigenous practice of demanding tribute every 80 days from the people.<ref name="EncMuc"/>
