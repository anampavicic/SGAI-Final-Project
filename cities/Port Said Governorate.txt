'''Port Said Governorate'''<ref>{{cite web|title=Muḩāfaz̧at Būr Sa'īd|url=http://www.geonames.org/maps/google_31.225_32.177.html|website=Geonames|access-date=20 October 2016|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161020110306/http://www.geonames.org/maps/google_31.225_32.177.html|archive-date=20 October 2016}}</ref> ({{langx|ar|محافظة بورسعيد|Muḩāfaz̧at Būr Sa'īd}}) is a subdivision located at the northeast end of the [[Nile Delta]], on the coast of the [[Mediterranean Sea]] at the northern mouth of the [[Suez Canal]]. It is wholly urban,<ref name="pop22">{{cite web |title=Population Estimates By Governorate ( Urban /Rural ) 1/1/2018 |url=https://www.capmas.gov.eg/Pages/StaticPages.aspx?page_id=5035 |access-date=10 October 2018 |website=www.capmas.gov.eg}}</ref> comprising the original [[Port Said|Port Said city]] on the west bank of the Suez Canal, and the town of [[Port Fuad]] on the eastern bank, which makes it one of the few transcontinental metropolitans in the world, as it spans across two [[continent]]s ([[Africa]]/[[Asia]]).<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |title=About - Administrative Division |url=https://portsaid.gov.eg/StaticContent/1 |access-date=2024-01-03 |website=portsaid.gov.eg}}</ref>

[[Port Said]] is one of six governorates that make up the [[Economic regions of Egypt|Suez Canal economic region]], and is the home of the [[Suez Canal Authority]] historical administrative building and the [[Lighthouse of Port Said]].

==Economy==
{{see also|Port Said|Port Fuad}}
To promote trade, during his presidency, [[Anwar Sadat]] declared it a duty-free zone. Port Said Governorate is a major transit point for trade, importing and exporting millions of tons of goods each year. Tourism is also promoted for the region.<ref>{{cite web|title=Port Said Gobernorate|url=http://www.ask-aladdin.com/egypt-governorates/port-said-governorate.html|website=Ask-Aladdin|access-date=18 October 2016|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161019080411/http://www.ask-aladdin.com/egypt-governorates/port-said-governorate.html|archive-date=19 October 2016}}</ref>

==Important sites==
{{see also|Port Said|Port Fuad}}
There are several museums in this area. For cultural tourism one may want to visit the [[Port Said National Museum of Antiquities]] which opened in 1987 and is located at the confluence of the Suez Canal waters and the Mediterranean Sea. It houses about 9,000 artifacts from all eras, ranging from the Pharaonic, Greek, Roman, and modern eras, as well as Coptic and Islamic eras.<ref>{{cite web|title=Port Said National Museum|url=http://www.sca-egypt.org/eng/MUS_Port-Said.htm|website=Supreme Council of Antiquities|access-date=18 October 2016|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161002192623/http://www.sca-egypt.org/eng/MUS_Port-Said.htm|archive-date=2 October 2016}}</ref>
[[The Military Museum of Port Said]], established in 1964, commemorates the 1956 the tripartite aggression on the city. It features armory and military artifacts, equipment used in the wars fought between 1956 - 1967 and in 1973.<ref name="MilitaryMuseum">{{cite web|title=Military Museum of Port Said|url=http://www.ask-aladdin.com/Egypt-museums/Port_Said_National_Museum.html|website=Ask Aladdin|access-date=18 October 2016|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161019142916/http://www.ask-aladdin.com/Egypt-museums/Port_Said_National_Museum.html|archive-date=19 October 2016}}</ref> [[El Nasr Museum of Modern Art in Port Said]] opened on December 25, 1995, and includes artwork by Egyptian artists in various branches of Fine Art.<ref name="FineArt">{{cite web|title=Al-Nasr Museum of Modern Art in Port Said|url=http://www.fineart.gov.eg/Eng/musem/Musem.asp?IDS=4|website=Fine Art Gov Egypt|access-date=18 October 2016}}</ref><ref name="FirneArtMuseum">{{cite web|title=History of Al-Nasr Museum of Modern Art in Port Said|url=http://www.fineart.gov.eg/Eng/musem/Musem.asp?IDS=4|website=Fine Art Government of Egypt|access-date=20 October 2016}}</ref>

[[The Statue de Lesseps]], honoring [[Ferdinand de Lesseps]], the developer of the Suez and Panama Canals, has its base at the entrance to the Suez Canal, along Palestine Street, where passing ships come from all over the world. This makes it a favorite with tourists.<ref name="AA">{{cite web|title=Port_Said|url=http://www.ask-aladdin.com/Egypt_cities/Port_Said.html|website=Ask Aladdin|access-date=20 October 2016|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161020103946/http://www.ask-aladdin.com/Egypt_cities/Port_Said.html|archive-date=20 October 2016}}</ref> Nearby, is the [[Suez Canal Authority]] building built on the banks of Port Said at the start of the project.<ref name="AA" />

The [[Lighthouse of Port Said|lighthouse and jetties at Port Said]], located at the northern terminus of the Suez canal, and completed in 1869, were built of concrete of [[Teil tree|Teil lime]] and Port Said sand. The lighthouse is a monolith 180 feet high. In the construction 120,000 tons of Teil hydraulic lime were used. It was an important engineering feat involving 25,000 blocks, each weighing twenty-five tons.<ref name="Engineering">{{cite book|last1=Frost|first1=G.H.|title=Engineering News and American Contract Journal, Volumen 13|date=June 27, 1885|location=University of Iowa|page=406|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=eChKAQAAMAAJ&q=concrete+iron+and+cement%2C+egypt%2C+port+said&pg=PA406|access-date=20 October 2016}}</ref>

[[Pelusium|Tall al Faramah]], also called Pelusium, is of Ecclesiastical and archeological importance.<ref name="Pelusium">{{cite journal|last1=Grzymski|first1=Krzysztof A.|title=Pelusium: Gateway to Egypt|journal=Pelusium: Gateway to Egypt|date=1997}}</ref> It is an archeological, prehistoric site with ruins and a Byzantine church.<ref>{{cite web|title=Tall al Faramah|url=http://www.geonames.org/maps/google_31.043_32.54.html|website=Geonames|access-date=20 October 2016|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161020110357/http://www.geonames.org/maps/google_31.043_32.54.html|archive-date=20 October 2016}}</ref>

In 2004, the marine sector of Egypt had 3,013 fishermen who were categorized in the recreational fishing sector, fishing in and around the Mediterranean Sea and Red Sea. "Recreational fishing is widely practised along the Mediterranean Coast", where Port Said is located.<ref name="MarineFisheries">{{cite book|title=FAO Fishery Country Profile - The Arab Republic of Egypt|date=2004|url=http://www.fao.org/fi/oldsite/FCP/en/EGY/profile.htm|access-date=20 October 2016|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161023151751/http://www.fao.org/fi/oldsite/FCP/en/EGY/profile.htm|archive-date=23 October 2016}}</ref>
[[Tennis, Egypt|Tel Tennis]] is an island (also called Tinnis or Thenessus) located {{convert|7|km|mi|abbr=on}} southwest of Port Said on [[Lake Manzala]] and can be reached from Port Said by boat.<ref name="JMG">{{cite journal|last1=Stanley|first1=Jean-Daniel|title=Submergence and burial of ancient coastal sites on the subsiding Nile delta margin, Egypt|journal=Journal of Mediterranean Geography|date=2015|issue=104 |pages=65–73 |doi=10.4000/mediterranee.2282 |url=https://mediterranee.revues.org/2282|access-date=18 October 2016|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161023175957/http://mediterranee.revues.org/2282|archive-date=23 October 2016}}</ref>

One can find historic churches in Port Said Governorate. There is a Roman Church, established in 1926,<ref name="Catholic">{{cite web|title=Apostolic Vicariate of Port Said|url=http://www.gcatholic.org/dioceses/former/port7.htm|website=GC Catholic|access-date=18 October 2016|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170129082656/http://www.gcatholic.org/dioceses/former/port7.htm|archive-date=29 January 2017}}</ref> a Melkite Greek Catholic Church called Saint Elias Greek Catholic Church,<ref name="GreekCatholic">{{cite web|title=Melkite Greek Catholic Church Information Center|url=http://www.mliles.com/melkite/indexmelkiteotheregypt.shtml|website=Melkite Greek Catholic Churches|access-date=18 October 2016|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161218104323/http://www.mliles.com/melkite/indexmelkiteotheregypt.shtml|archive-date=18 December 2016}}</ref> and an old French Cathedral.<ref>{{cite web|title=French Cathedral|url=http://www.geonames.org/8636358/french-cathedral.html|website=Geonames|access-date=20 October 2016|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161020104517/http://www.geonames.org/8636358/french-cathedral.html|archive-date=20 October 2016}}</ref>
