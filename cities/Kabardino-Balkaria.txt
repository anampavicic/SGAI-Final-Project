'''Kabardino-Balkaria''' ({{langx|ru|Кабарди́но-Балка́рия}}), officially the '''Kabardino-Balkarian Republic''',{{efn|{{langx|ru|Кабарди́но-Балка́рская Респу́блика|Kabardino-Balkarskaya Respublika}}; {{langx|kbd|Къэбэрдей-Балъкъэр Республикэ|Ķêbêrdej-Baĺķêr Respublikê}}; {{langx|krc|Къабарты-Малкъар Республика|Qabartı-Malqar Respublika}}|group=note}}<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://en.kremlin.ru/events/president/news/55504|title=Head of Kabardino-Balkarian Republic reported to the President on the situation in Mount Elbrus region|website=President of Russia|date=September 2, 2017 |language=en|access-date=2020-02-17}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://council.gov.ru/en/structure/regions/KB/|title=Russia's federal constituent entities|website=Federation Council of the Federal Assembly of the Russian Federation|language=en|access-date=2020-02-17}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|last=Skutsch|first=Carl|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=yXYKAgAAQBAJ&pg=PA313 |title=Encyclopedia of the World's Minorities|date=2013-11-07|publisher=Routledge|isbn=978-1-135-19388-1|language=en}}</ref> is a [[Republics of Russia|republic]] of [[Russia]] located in the [[North Caucasus]]. As of the [[Russian Census (2021)|2021 Census]], its population was&nbsp;904,200.<ref name="2021Census">{{ru-pop-ref|2021Census}}</ref> Its capital is [[Nalchik]]. The area contains the highest mountain in Europe,  [[Mount Elbrus]], at {{convert|5642|m|abbr=on}}. Mount Elbrus has 22 glaciers that feed three rivers — [[Baksan (river)|Baksan]], [[Malka (river)|Malka]] and [[Kuban (river)|Kuban]]. The mountain is covered with snow year-round.

==Geography==
[[File:Абай къала.jpg|thumb|Cherek-Balkarskaya gorge]]
The republic is situated in the North Caucasus mountains, with plains in the northern part. The republic shares an international border with [[Georgia (country)|Georgia]].
*''Area'': {{convert|12500|km2|sp=us}}
*''Borders'':
**''internal'': [[Stavropol Krai]] (N/NE), [[North Ossetia–Alania]] (E/SE/S), [[Karachay–Cherkessia]] (W/NW)
**''international'': [[Georgia (country)|Georgia]] ([[Racha-Lechkhumi and Kvemo Svaneti]], [[Svaneti|Zemo Svaneti]]) (S/SW)
*''Highest point'': [[Mount Elbrus]] (5,642&nbsp;m)
*''Maximum N->S distance'': {{convert|167|km|sp=us}}
*''Maximum E->W distance'': {{convert|123|km|sp=us}}
<!--*''Average elevation'': no data-->

Kabardino-Balkaria is traversed by the northeasterly line of equal latitude and longitude.

There are about 100 lakes in the Republic, none of which is large. Just over half (55) are located between the Baksan and Malka Rivers, the largest each of an area of no more than {{convert|0.01|km2|sp=us}}. Some of the lakes are:

*[[Upper Tserikkel Lake]] (depth 18&nbsp;m)
*[[Sekretnoye Lake]]
*[[Tambukan Lake]] (area 1.77&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>; depth 1.5 to 2&nbsp;m), partially within Stavropol Krai.

===Natural resources===
{{Expand section|date=March 2022}}
Kabardino-Balkaria's natural resources include [[molybdenum]], [[tungsten]], and [[coal]].

===Climate===
The republic has a continental-type climate.
*''Average January temperature'': {{convert|-12|C}} (mountains) to {{convert|-4|C}} (plains)
*''Average July temperature'': {{convert|+4|C}} (mountains) to {{convert|+23|C}} (plains)
*''Average annual [[precipitation]]'': 500–2,000&nbsp;mm.

==History==
{{Main|History of Kabardino-Balkaria}} 
{{Expand section|date=March 2022}}
It is known that modern-day Circassians also called Kassogs were inhabiting Kabardino Balkaria since at least the 6th century BCE, then known as Zichia.<ref>{{Cite book |url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/22733550 |title=The Oxford dictionary of Byzantium |date=1991 |publisher=Oxford University Press |editor-first1=A. P. |editor-last1=Kazhdan |editor-first2=A-M. M. |editor-last2=Talbot |editor-first3=A. |editor-last3=Cutler |editor-first4=T. E. |editor-last4=Gregory |editor-first5=N. P. |editor-last5=Ševčenko |isbn=0-19-504652-8 |location=New York |oclc=22733550}}</ref> On 1 July 1994 Kabardino-Balkaria became the second republic after Tatarstan to sign a power-sharing agreement with the federal government, granting it autonomy.<ref name="asymmetries">{{Cite journal|last=Solnick|first=Steven|date=29 May 1996|title=Asymmetries in Russian Federation Bargaining|url=https://www.ucis.pitt.edu/nceeer/1996-810-08-Solnick.pdf|journal=The National Council for Soviet and East European Research|pages=12}}</ref>

==Politics==
The head of government in Kabardino-Balkaria is the [[Head of the Kabardino-Balkarian Republic|Head]]. The current Head is [[Kazbek Kokov]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://meduza.io/paragraph/2018/09/26/putin-opyat-uvolnyaet-gubernatorov-a-na-ih-mesto-naznachaet-odnofamiltsev-ili-byvshih-gubernatorov-a-a-a-kak-ne-zaputatsya|script-title=ru:Путин опять увольняет губернаторов, а на их место назначает однофамильцев или бывших губернаторов. А-а-а! Как не запутаться? Вот шпаргалка|work=Meduza|access-date=2018-09-26|language=ru-RU}}</ref> The legislative body of the Republic is the [[Parliament of Kabardino-Balkaria|Parliament]] comprising 72 deputies elected for a five-year term.<ref name="HeadLegis" /><ref name="Parliament">Constitution, Article&nbsp;94.</ref>

The republic adopted a new constitution in 2001 which prevents the republic from existing independently of the Russian Federation.{{sfn|Bell|2003|p=78}}

===Ethnic groups===
Kabardino-Balkaria includes two major ethnic communities, the [[Kabarday|Kabardins]] (Circassians), who speak a [[Northwest Caucasian languages|North-West Caucasian language]], and the [[Balkars]] who speak a [[Turkic language]]. According to the [[Russian Census (2021)|2021 Census]],<ref name=census2021>{{cite web|title=Национальный состав населения|url=https://rosstat.gov.ru/storage/mediabank/Tom5_tab1_VPN-2020.xlsx|publisher=[[Federal State Statistics Service (Russia)|Federal State Statistics Service]]|accessdate=30 December 2022}}</ref> Kabardins make up 57.1% of the republic's population, followed by [[Russians]] (19.8%) and Balkars (13.7%). Other groups include [[Cherkess]] (3.0%), [[Turkish people|Turks]] (1.9%), [[Ossetians]] (0.8%), [[Romani people|Romani]] (0.5%), and a host of smaller groups, each accounting for less than 0.5% of the total population.

According to a 2012 survey which interviewed 56,900 people,<ref name="2012ArenaAtlas"/> 70.8% of the population of Kabardino-Balkaria adhered to [[Islam]], 11.6% to the [[Russian Orthodox Church]], 3.8% were non-Orthodox [[Christians]], and 1.8% followed [[Adyghe people|Adyghe]] (Kabardian) folk religion and other indigenous faiths. In addition, 12% of the population declared to be "[[spiritual but not religious]]" and 5.6% was [[Atheism|atheist]] or followed other religions, including [[Jehovah's Witness]]es.<ref name="2012ArenaAtlas"/>
