'''Nonthaburi''' ({{langx|th|นนทบุรี}}, {{IPA|th|nōn.tʰá(ʔ).bū.rīː|pron}}) is the principal city of the [[Mueang Nonthaburi district|district]] and [[Nonthaburi province|province]] of the same name in [[Thailand]].

On 15 February 1936, Nonthaburi town municipality (''[[Thesaban#Town municipality|thesaban mueang]]'') was established, which only covered Suan Yai subdistrict (''[[tambon]]''), just {{cvt|2.5|km2}}.<ref>{{cite journal |date=14 February 1936 |title=พระราชกฤษฎีกา จัดตั้งเทศบาลเมืองนนทบุรี จังหวัดนนทบุรี พุทธศักราช ๒๔๗๙ |trans-title=Royal Decree Establishing of Nonthaburi town municipality, Nonthaburi province, Buddhist Era 2479 (1936) |url=http://www.ratchakitcha.soc.go.th/DATA/PDF/2479/A/1196.PDF |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200319083211/http://www.ratchakitcha.soc.go.th/DATA/PDF/2479/A/1196.PDF |url-status=dead |archive-date=19 March 2020 |access-date=10 December 2019 |journal=Royal Thai Government Gazette |volume=53 |pages=1196–1200 |postscript=, effectively on 15 February 1936}}</ref>
On 25 September 1995, Nonthaburi was upgraded to city municipality (''[[Thesaban#City municipality|thesaban nakhon]]'') by enlargement of the city with four more subdistricts, Talat Khwan ({{cvt|8.2|km2|disp=sqbr}}), Bang Khen ({{cvt|9.0|km2|disp=sqbr}}), Bang Kraso ({{cvt|11.2|km2|disp=sqbr}}) and Tha Sai ({{cvt|8.0|km2|disp=sqbr}}), covering a total of {{cvt|38.9|km2}}.<ref>{{cite journal |date=24 September 1995 |title=พระราชกฤษฎีกา จัดตั้งเทศบาลนครนนทบุรี จังหวัดนนทบุรี พ.ศ.๒๕๓๘ |trans-title=Royal Decree Establishing Nonthaburi city municipality, Nonthaburi province, B.E.2538 (1995) |url=http://www.ratchakitcha.soc.go.th/DATA/PDF/2538/A/040/29.PDF |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110917112537/http://www.ratchakitcha.soc.go.th/DATA/PDF/2538/A/040/29.PDF |url-status=dead |archive-date=17 September 2011 |access-date=10 December 2019 |journal=Royal Thai Government Gazette |volume=112 |issue=40 Kor |pages=29–32 |postscript=, effectively on 25 September 1995}}</ref> As of 31 December 2019, it has a registered population of 254,375,<ref name="TDD"/> making it the most populous [[Thesaban|city municipality]] in Thailand (excluding Bangkok). There are 93 communities (''chumchon''), although not directly chosen by the local citizens, which provides advice and recommendations to local administrative organizations. Due to its proximity to [[Bangkok]], the city is considered a suburb of Bangkok, a part of the [[Bangkok Metropolitan Region]], or Greater Bangkok.

Nonthaburi is served by public transport systems including the [[Bangkok Mass Transit Authority]] bus system, the [[Chao Phraya Express Boat]], and the [[Purple Line (Bangkok)|MRT Purple Line]], inaugurated in 2016.

Nonthaburi is known for its temple and market such as Wat Chaloem Phra Kiat, Wat Chomphuwek, Wat Chotikaram, Wat Khema Phirataram, Wat Prasat, Wat Sangkhathan and so on.
