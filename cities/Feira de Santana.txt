'''Feira de Santana''' ({{IPA|pt|ˈfejɾɐ d(ʒi) sɐ̃ˈtɐ̃nɐ}}; [[Portuguese language|Portuguese]] for "[[Saint Anne]]'s Fair") is a city in [[Bahia]], [[Brazil]]. It is the second-most populous city in the state, with a population of 616,272 according to [[Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics|IBGE]]'s census in 2022.<ref name="IBGE2" /> It is located 100&nbsp;km northwest of [[Salvador, Brazil|Salvador]], [[Bahia]]'s capital city. These cities are connected by [[BR-324]], a four-lane divided highway.

{{anchor|Etymology}}

== Name ==
Feira de Santana, formerly spelled '''Feira de Santa Anna''',{{sfnp|''EB''|1911}} is named in honor of the [[cattle farmer|cattle]] fairs{{sfnp|''EB''|1911}} held at the St-Anne-of-the-Fountains Plantation (''{{lang|pt|Fazenda Sant'Anna dos Olhos D’Água}}'') in the 19th century.

== History ==
[[File:Feira de Santana (BA).tif|thumb|290px|left|Feira de Santana, 1963.]]
The St-Anne-of-the-Fountains Plantation was established in the 18th century by [[Domingos Barbosa de Araujo]] and his wife [[Anna Brandoa]]. Located at the edge of [[Bahia]]'s "backcountry" (''{{lang|pt|sertão}}''), it became a center for the cowboys on their way from the pastures there to the port of [[Cachoeira]]. The cowboys' practice of starting annual fires to clear old brush eventually worsened the area's natural aridity to the point where the cattle industry collapsed,{{sfnp|''EB''|1911}} but by then Brazilians and foreigners had begun adopting the area as their home. A city began to develop with wide streets which were bordered by commercial houses serving the area's growing population. It was incorporated as the second city in Bahia after [[Salvador, Bahia|the capital]] and the 31st in the entirety of [[colonial Brazil]].

Today, Feira de Santana remains the second largest city in Bahia and an important stopping point for travelers.

==Culture==
Feira de Santana is famous for its parties and festivals, particularly St Anne's Day (''{{lang|pt|Senhora Sant'Ana}}'') in the last week of July, which features activities such as bumba-meu-boi, segura-a-véia, and burrinha.{{clarify|date=January 2016}} The [[Micareta]] carnival party is held 15 days after [[Passover]]; the Festival of Violeiros in September; and the donkey race in November. The city boasts of several interesting tourist destinations including a market for local artists, a museum of contemporary art, and the [[Antares Astronomical Observatory]]. The city is the seat of the [[Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Feira de Santana]].

==Economy and transport==
The city is served by [[Feira de Santana Airport|Gov. João Durval Carneiro Airport]]. With a major junction of Northeastern Brazil's highways nearby, Feira de Santana functions as a crossroads for the traffic coming from the South and West-Central portions of Brazil bound for [[Salvador, Brazil|Salvador]] and other important cities of the northeast. Feira de Santana, thanks in part to its longstanding importance as a crossroads and its proximity to Salvador, is now an important and diverse commercial and industrial center.

==Education==
The [[Universidade Estadual de Feira de Santana]] is located in the city.
