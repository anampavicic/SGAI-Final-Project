'''Gashua''' is a community in [[Yobe State]] in northeastern [[Nigeria]], on the [[Yobe River]] a few miles below the convergence of the [[Hadejia River]] and the [[Jama'are River]]. Average elevation is about 299 m. The population in 2006 was about 125,000.<ref>{{cite web

==Town==

Gashua is one of the largest and most developed towns in Yobe State. Since 1976, it has been headquarters of the [[Bade, Nigeria|Bade]] [[Local Government Areas of Nigeria|Local Government Area]]. The town lies near the [[Nguru-Gashua Wetlands]], an economically and ecologically important ecological system.<ref>{{cite web |author=A.E. Neiland, B.M.B. Ladu |title=Enhancement of inland fisheries in Nigeria: the institutional context provided by traditional and modern systems of fisheries management |url=http://www.fao.org/docrep/005/W8514E/W8514E26.htm |publisher=Food and Agriculture Organization |accessdate=2010-03-11}}</ref>
The town is the location of the court of Mai Bade, the [[Bade Emirate|Emir of Bade]].<ref>{{cite web |title=ASSALAMU ALAIKUM (Welcome) |url=http://bade-emirate.com/ |url-status=dead |publisher=Bade Emirate council |accessdate=2010-09-11 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20101122070849/http://bade-emirate.com/ |archivedate=22 November 2010 |df=dmy-all}}</ref>

== Language ==
The [[Bade language]] is spoken in Gashua and in an area fanning out east and south of Gashua. Bade is one of seven languages of the [[Chadic]] family indigenous to Yobe State.<ref name="trib1" /><ref>{{cite web
