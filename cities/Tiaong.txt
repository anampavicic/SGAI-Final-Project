'''Tiaong''' ({{IPA|tl|ˈtjaʔɔŋ|}}), officially the '''Municipality of Tiaong''' ({{lang-tgl|Bayan ng Tiaong}}), is a [[municipality of the Philippines|municipality]] in the [[Philippine Province|province]] of [[Quezon]], [[Philippines]]. According to the 2020 census, it has a population of 106,265 people.{{PH census|current}}

Tiaong is {{convert|35|km|sp=us}} from [[Lucena]] and {{convert|95|km|sp=us}} from [[Manila]].

==Etymology==
According to local legends, Tiaong derived its name from the [[portmanteau]] of two words:
* ''Tiya'' (''tia''), [[Tagalog language|Tagalog]] for aunt in reference to Doña Tating, a well-loved woman in the area; and 
* ''Ong'' (''oong'') from the sound of her bull that signalled her nearby presence.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://lifestyle.inquirer.net/38143/tiaong-has-its-come-ons/s|title=Tiaong has its come-ons|first=Annelle S.|last=Tayao|date=March 10, 2012|accessdate=August 15, 2023|website=INQUIRER.net}}</ref> 

==History==
Historically, [[Dolores, Quezon|Dolores]] was once consolidated with the municipality of Tiaong by virtue of Act No. 402 dated May 17, 1902.<ref>{{cite PH act|chamber=Act|number=402|title=An Act Consolidating the Municipalities of Tiaon, Dolores, Candelaria, and Sariaya in the Province of Tayabas Into Two Municipalities|url=https://issuances-library.senate.gov.ph/legislative-issuance/act-no-402|date=May 17, 1902|accessdate=September 21, 2024|publisher=Senate of the Philippines Legislative Digital Library}}</ref> <!-- split date unknown -->

On June 21, 1957, barrios Matipunso, Behia, and Bucal were established out of barrios Niing, Callejon, and Buha, respectively.<ref>{{cite PH act|chamber=RA|number=1765|date=June 21, 1957| url=https://lawphil.net/statutes/repacts/ra1957/ra_1765_1957.html|title=An Act Creating the Barrio of Matipunso in the Municipality of Tiaong, Province of Quezon|access-date=August 15, 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite PH act|chamber=RA|number=1766|date=June 21, 1957| url=https://www.lawphil.net/statutes/repacts/ra1957/ra_1766_1957.html|title=An Act Creating the Barrio of Behia in the Municipality of Tiaong, Province of Quezon}}</ref><ref>{{cite PH act|chamber=RA|number=1767|date=June 21, 1957| url=https://lawphil.net/statutes/repacts/ra1957/ra_1767_1957.html|title=An Act Creating the Barrio of Bucal in the Municipality of Tiaong, Province of Quezon|access-date=August 15, 2023}}</ref> On October 4, 1957, barrios Buliran, Callejon, Niing, and Pury were excised from Tiaong to form the new municipality of [[San Antonio, Quezon|San Antonio]].<ref>{{cite PH act |title=Creating the Municipality of San Antonio in the Province of Quezon |chamber=EO |number=270 |folio = |language= |date=October 4, 1957 |year= |article= |section= |url=https://www.officialgazette.gov.ph/1957/10/04/executive-order-no-270-s-1957/ |accessdate=January 5, 2022 |archiveurl= |archivedate= |url-status= |ref=}}</ref>
