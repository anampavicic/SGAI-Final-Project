'''Nan'an''' ({{zh|s={{linktext|南安}}|p=Nán'ān|poj=Lâm-oaⁿ |l=southern peace}}) is a [[county-level city]] of [[Minnan region|southern]] [[Fujian|Fujian Province]], People's Republic of China. It is under the administration of [[Quanzhou]] City and as of 2023, had a total population of 1,663,542.<ref name="Nan'an 321">{{cite web|title=Nan'an City Government Official Website|url=http://www.nanan.gov.cn/view.aspx?id=321}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=14 August 2023 |title=人口民族 |url=http://www.nanan.gov.cn/zjna/rkmz/ |access-date=7 February 2024 |website=南安市人民政府 |language=zh}}</ref> More than 4,000,000 [[overseas Chinese]] trace their ancestry to Nan'an.<ref name="Nan'an 321"/>

==History==
[[File:Qianban - Lianhe Church - DSCF9147.JPG|thumb|left|Lianhe Church in Qianban Village, Shijing Town, Nan'an]]
Inhabited since the [[Neolithic|Neolithic Era]], the first incarnation of Nan'an was established as a [[Commandery (China)|commandery]] in the early 500s by the division of Jin'an County under [[Emperor Wu of Liang]]. It was under the jurisdiction of the [[Qingyuan Circuit]] during the [[Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms period|Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms Period]], and was incorporated into Quanzhou Prefecture (泉州府) during the [[Qing dynasty]], when it was considered one of the Three Wu Kingdoms alongside [[Jinjiang, Quanzhou|Jinjiang]] and [[Hui'an County|Hui'an]].

Following the establishment of the [[Republic of China (1912–1949)|Republic of China]], Nan'an was a part of Jinjiang Prefecture until its incorporation into [[Quanzhou|Quanzhou City]] in 1986; it was the site of military engagements during the [[Chinese Communist Revolution]]. Nan'an was established as a [[county-level city]] in its own right in 1993.

== Geography and climate ==
Nan'an experiences [[Humid subtropical climate|subtropical monsoonal humid climate]]. It has an average temperature of {{convert|20.9|°C}}.

The city covers an area of {{convert|2036|km2}}.

Nan'an is situated below [[Anxi County]], adjacent to [[Jinjiang, Fujian|Jinjiang City]] to the east and [[Tong'an, Xiamen|Tong'an District]] of [[Xiamen]] to the West. Nan'an is {{convert|97|km}} from Xiamen. It is {{convert|30|km}} from downtown of Quanzhou and {{convert|220|km}} from the provincial capital, Fuzhou.

== Economy ==
[[File:Anhai Bay - DSCF8881.JPG|thumb|left|An [[Aquaculture in China|aquaculture]] establishment in [[Anhai Bay]], near [[Shijing, Nan'an, Fujian|Shijing Town]]. Note the use of the local stone for construction.]]
Nan'an is fast developing into an economic and industrial hub for Quanzhou prefecture. Its close proximity to regional economic centres such as Xiamen, Guangzhou, Fuzhou, Shanghai, Guangzhou and Shenzhen makes it an important investment location for foreign investments and Taiwan businessmen.

Nan'an has very strong rail infrastructure and road links making it a gateway for tourism as well as commerce.{{citation needed|date=January 2020}}

== Culture ==
Nan'an is closely connected to the history of overseas Chinese, having been a hub of the [[Maritime Silk Road]] and a longtime economic and cultural centre for the [[Hoklo people|Minnan people]], with over 4 million overseas Chinese tracing their ancestry to the area<ref>{{Cite web |title=南安概况 |trans-title=Overview of Nan'an |url=http://www.nanan.gov.cn/zjna/ |access-date=7 February 2024 |website=南安市人民政府}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Nan'an City Government Official Website History |url=http://www.nanan.gov.cn/view.aspx?id=324}}</ref> [[Nanguan music]] has its origins in the area in and surrounding Nan'an. It is known as the location of [[Anping Bridge]], a stone [[beam bridge]] dating back to the [[Song dynasty]], as well as the burial place of [[Southern Ming|Ming]] general Zheng Chenggong (better known internationally as [[Koxinga]].)

The local language of Nan'an is a variety of [[Hokkien]], part of the [[Quanzhou dialects|Quanzhou]] family of Hokkien dialects. The surname [[Hong (surname)|Hong]] (洪; ''Ang'' in Hokkien and [[Teochew dialect|Teochew]] romanizations) has one of its origins in Nan'an.

== Education ==
The Shishan campus of [[Quanzhou Normal University]] is located in Nan'an. Schools located in Nan'an include Nan'an Experimental Middle School.
