'''Tanjungbalai{{refn|The city's name is correctly and officially spelled as Tanjungbalai|group=fn}}''', also colloquially written as '''Tanjung Balai''', is a [[Cities of Indonesia|city]] in [[North Sumatra]] province, [[Indonesia]], on the estuary of the [[Asahan River]]. It has an area of {{convert|60.52|sqkm|sqmi|sp=us}} and the sixth largest ''kota'' (urban) population in North Sumatra with 154,445 inhabitants in the 2010 census<ref name="Biro Pusat Statistik 2011">Biro Pusat Statistik, Jakarta, 2011.</ref> and 176,027 at the 2020 Census;<ref name="Badan Pusat Statistik 2021">Badan Pusat Statistik, Jakarta, 2021.</ref> the official estimate as at mid 2023 was 183,170 - comprising 92,715 males and 90,455 females.<ref name="Badan Pusat Statistik 2024">Badan Pusat Statistik, Jakarta, 28 February 2024, ''Kota Tanjungbalai Dalam Angka 2024'' (Katalog-BPS 1102001.1274)</ref> The town has a ferry terminal with services to [[Port Klang]], [[Malaysia]], and the city-island of Singapore.

Formerly known as Tanjungbalai-Asahan, this area was once a district within [[Asahan Regency]] until it was granted [[City status in Indonesia|city status]] in 1984. However, the identifier "Asahan" is still used to distinguish it from the port of the same name on [[Great Karimun|Great Karimun island]]. After the separation of Tanjungbalai from Asahan Regency, it technically became an [[enclave and exclave|enclave]] within the regency.

==Tanjungbalai Asahan Port (Teluk Nibung Port)==
The Port (which actually lies in Tanjungbalai District of [[Asahan Regency]], between the city and the coast) has warehouse facilities and stacking fields. This port also carries out cargo and passenger services with activities mainly in exporting vegetables and fish to Malaysia. The port has passenger services to Port Klang and Port of Melintang Forest, Perak.
Teluk Nibung passenger terminal currently has an area of about 2,500 square metres, consisting of two floors with a capacity of around 800 people. 1st floor for check-in, customs, immigration for arrivals, VIP rooms, quarantine rooms and 2nd floor for immigration rooms for departures, passenger waiting rooms, food court and coffee shops.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.medanbisnisdaily.com/news/online/read/2018/11/14/57665/terminal_internasional_teluk_nibung_dioperasikan_jelang_tahun_baru/|title=Terminal Internasional Teluk Nibung Dioperasikan Jelang Tahun Baru|newspaper=Medan Business Daily|accessdate=11 January 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://medan.tribunnews.com/2018/11/14/pelindo-1-lakukan-modernisasi-pelabuhan-tanjungbalai-asahan|title=Pelindo 1 Lakukan Modernisasi Pelabuhan Tanjungbalai Asahan|newspaper=Tribun News|accessdate=11 January 2019}}</ref>
