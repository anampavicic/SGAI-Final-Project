'''Cachoeiro de Itapemirim''' ({{IPA|pt|kaʃuˈe(j)ɾu dʒi itapemiˈɾĩj}}) is a [[Municipalities of Brazil|municipality]] and a major town, located in the south of [[Espírito Santo]], [[Brazil]], on the banks of the [[Itapemirim River]]. It is the economic hub of southern Espírito Santo, being the most important producer of [[marble]] and [[granite]] in [[Brazil]]. Home to one of the biggest intercity bus companies, it is a very well-connected city. The population is about 210,000. Location: 138&nbsp;km south of [[Vitória, Brazil|Vitória]].

==History==
Cachoeiro de Itapemirim's first commercial establishment opened in 1853, and in 1889 its first [[post office]] and [[telegram]] services were begun. Since its early days, transport has played a major role in this town. Since it was established by a waterfall, where it would be impossible to continue the journey up the river, it became an important stop for gold prospectors traveling to [[Minas Gerais]]. Many [[European immigration to Brazil|immigrant families]] which travelled to southern Espírito Santo established in the region.{{cn|date=March 2023}}

== Transport ==
The railway line to [[Rio de Janeiro]] was completed in 1900, allowing both cultural and economical influence from the latter.  In 1911 the railway to [[Vitória, Brazil|Vitória]] was completed, strengthening links to the north.{{cn|date=March 2023}}

The city is served by [[Cachoeiro de Itapemirim Airport]].

==Attractions==

The municipality contains the {{convert|451|ha}} [[Pacotuba National Forest]], created in 2002.<ref>{{citation|location=Vila Vilha|date=June 2011|language=pt|page=3|last1=Gonçalves|first1=Augusta Rosa|first2=José Luiz|last2=Helmer|title=Plano De Manejo Da Floresta Nacional De Pacotuba|volume=I – Diagnóstico|publisher=ICMBio|url=http://www.icmbio.gov.br/portal/images/stories/imgs-unidades-coservacao/Volume_I_Pacotuba_Junho_2011.pdf|access-date=2016-10-27|archive-date=March 3, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303200604/http://www.icmbio.gov.br/portal/images/stories/imgs-unidades-coservacao/Volume_I_Pacotuba_Junho_2011.pdf|url-status=dead}}</ref>
The ''quilombola'' community of Monte Alegre offers ethnic, cultural and [[ecotourism|environmental tourism]] in partnership with the forest and the ministry of tourism.
This includes crafts and gastronomy, expression of traditions and community lifestyle, and guided tours of the forest.<ref>{{citation|ref={{harvid|Floresta Nacional de Pacotuba – Via Rural}}|language=pt|title=Floresta Nacional de Pacotuba|publisher=Via Rural|url=http://br.viarural.com/servicos/turismo/florestas-nacionais/floresta-nacional-de-pacotuba/default.htm|access-date=2016-10-27|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161028083819/http://br.viarural.com/servicos/turismo/florestas-nacionais/floresta-nacional-de-pacotuba/default.htm|archive-date=October 28, 2016|url-status=usurped}}</ref>
The municipality contains part of the protected area of the [[Frade e a Freira Natural Monument]].<ref>{{citation|language=pt|title=Monumento Natural O Frade e a Freira|publisher=Secretário de Meio Ambiente, Espírito Santo|url=http://www.meioambiente.es.gov.br/default.asp?pagina=16710|access-date=2016-05-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160603133900/http://www.meioambiente.es.gov.br/default.asp?pagina=16710|archive-date=June 3, 2016|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref>
It contains the {{convert|452|ha}} [[Itabira Natural Monument]].<ref>{{citation|ref={{harvid|Official Record ... Protected Planet}}
 |title=Official Record for Monumento Natural Do Itabira|publisher=Protected Planet
 |url=https://www.protectedplanet.net/monumento-natural-do-itabira-natural-monument|access-date=2016-10-24}}</ref>
The city is nicknamed ''A capital oculta do mundo'', meaning "the secret capital of the world".
