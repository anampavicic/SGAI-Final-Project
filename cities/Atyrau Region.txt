'''Atyrau Region''' ({{langx|kk|Атырау облысы|translit=Atyrau oblysy}}; {{langx|ru|Атырауская область}}) formerly known as '''Guryev Region''' ({{Langx|ru|Гурьевская область}}) until 1991, is one of the [[regions of Kazakhstan]], in the west of the country around the northeast of the [[Caspian Sea]]. Its capital is the city of [[Atyrau]], with a population of 355,117; the region itself has a population of 681,241, of which [[Kazakhs]] make up more than 90%.

==History==
The [[Turan]] lowlands of Atyrau Region were the central homeland for the [[Malkar]] "Hun" dynasties of late antiquity.{{cn|date=December 2023}}

==Geography==
{{no sources|section|date=December 2023}}
With an area of 118,600 square kilometers, it is the second-smallest region in Kazakhstan ([[Turkistan Region]] is the smallest). It borders [[Russia]] ([[Astrakhan Oblast]]) to the west, as well as the fellow Kazakh regions [[Aktobe Region|Aktobe]] to the east, [[Mangystau Region|Mangystau]] to the south, and the [[West Kazakhstan Region]] to the north. The [[Ural River]] is the border between [[Asia]] and [[Europe]] and flows from Russia to the [[Caspian Sea]] through the region, meaning western Atyrau Region lies in Europe. 

A large part of the region is situated in the [[petroleum|oil]]-rich [[Caspian Depression]]. Many oil wells have been drilled in the [[Tengiz Field]] and [[Kashagan Field]] areas. An [[oil pipeline]] runs from Atyrau to [[Samara, Russia]], where it joins the Russian pipeline system. A separate oil pipeline runs from the [[Tengiz field]] to the Russian Black Sea port of [[Novorossiisk]].

Atyrau is traversed by the northeasterly line of equal latitude and longitude.

==Demographics==
As of 2021, the Atyrau Region has a population of 659,074.{{cn|date=December 2023}}

*Others: 1.30%

==Administrative divisions==
The region is divided into seven districts and the city of [[Atyrau]]. The districts are:<ref>{{cite web|url=http://atyrau.gov.kz/ru/apparat-akima-zhylyojskogo-rajona-1/ |archive-url=https://archive.today/20121219165732/http://atyrau.gov.kz/ru/apparat-akima-zhylyojskogo-rajona-1/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=19 December 2012 |script-title=ru:Аппарат акима Жылыойского района |publisher=Электронный Акимат Атырауской области |access-date=5 June 2012 |language=ru }}</ref>
# [[Inder District]], with the administrative center in the urban-type settlement of [[Inderbor]];
# [[Isatay District]], the [[Village#Central and Eastern Europe|selo]] (village)  of [[Akkystau]];
# [[Kurmangazy District]], the selo of [[Ganyushkino]];
# [[Kyzylkoga District]], the selo of [[Miyaly]];
# [[Makat District]], the urban-type settlement of [[Makat]];
# [[Makhambet District]], the selo of [[Makhambet]];
# [[Zhylyoi District]], the town of [[Kulsary]].

<sup>*</sup> Two localities in Atyrau Region have town status. These are Atyrau and [[Kulsary]]. Isatay and Kurmangazy districts are in Europe, Makat, Kyzylkoga and Zhylyoi in Asia, while Inder and Makhambet straddle the Ural River which marks the boundary.
