'''Kaech'ŏn''' or '''Kaechon''' ({{IPAc-en|US|ˈ|ɡ|eɪ|tʃ|ʌ|n}} {{respell|GAY|chun}},<ref>{{Cite Merriam-Webster|Kaech'ŏn|access-date=7 September 2019}}</ref> {{IPA|ko|kɛtsʰʌn|lang}}; [[Hangul]]: {{lang|ko|개천시}}, [[Hanja]]: {{lang|ko|价川市}}) is a city in [[South Pyongan Province|South P'yŏngan]] province, [[North Korea]].

==Geography==
The [[Myohyangsan]], [[Changansan]], [[Chonsongsan|Ch'ŏnsŏngsan]], and [[Chongryongsan|Ch'ŏngryongsan]] mountain ranges come together in Kaech'ŏn.  The highest peak is Paekt'apsan.  The most important rivers are the [[Chongchon River|Ch'ŏngch'ŏn River]] and the [[Taedong River]].  The area of the city is 61% forested.

==Economy==
Water resources are abundant, and several reservoirs are located in Kaech'ŏn.

Agriculture has been extensively developed, including livestock and fruit orchards.  Machining and metalworking are the dominant industries,  mining has also become more prominent.

==Transportation==
Kaech'ŏn is served by the [[Korean State Railway]]'s [[Kaechon Line|Kaech'ŏn Line]] and the [[Manpo Line|Manp'o Line]] trunk lines, as well as the [[Choyang Tangwang Line|Choyang Colliery Line]] and [[Chunhyok Line|Chunhyŏk Line]] secondary lines.

==Tourism==
Tourist sites in Kaech'ŏn include [[Songam Cavern]], Taeripsa Temple with its 9-level stone [[pagoda]], the fortresses of Changhamsŏng, T'osŏng, and Kosasansŏng, Namsa [[dolmen]], and the group of dolments at Mukpangsan.  There are also Yŏnpung Lake, which was constructed in 1956, and Yongwŏn Cavern, which was discovered in 1966 and is preserved as North Korea's Natural Monument No. 43.

The Kaechon Revolutionary Site is associated with [[Kim Il Sung]]'s [[250 Mile Journey for National Liberation]]. A statue was erected there on the occasion of the [[Day of the Sun]], April 15, in 2001.<ref>{{Cite news |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141012062514/http://www.kcna.co.jp/item/2007/200707/news07/09.htm |url= http://www.kcna.co.jp/item/2007/200707/news07/09.htm#6 |title=Statues of Kim Il Sung Erected in Different Places |date=8 July 2007 |archive-date=12 October 2014 |agency=KCNA }}</ref>

==Prison camps==
There are two large prison camps in Kaech'ŏn, both known for very harsh conditions:
* [[Kaechon internment camp|Political Prison Camp No. 14]] is a prison labour colony around {{convert|20|km|mi|abbr=on}} southeast of the city centre at the banks of [[Taedong River]]. [[Shin Dong-hyuk (human rights activist)|Shin Dong-hyuk]] was born in the camp, tortured there, and finally saw his mother and brother executed before he escaped.<ref>{{ cite web | url = http://www.iht.com/articles/2007/07/09/news/korea.php?page=2 | title = Born and raised in a North Korean gulag | author = Choe Sang-hun | author-link = Choe Sang-hun | date = July 9, 2007 | publisher = International Herald Tribune}}</ref>
* [[Kaechon concentration camp|Re-education Camp No. 1]] is a prison building complex around {{convert|2.5|km|mi|abbr=on}} east of the city centre. [[Lee Soon Ok|Lee Soon-ok]] was imprisoned for six years in the camp and gave testimony before the United States Senate.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://judiciary.senate.gov/hearings/testimony.cfm?id=292&wit_id=665|title=United States Senate Hearings: Testimony of Ms. Soon Ok Lee, June 21, 2002|access-date=July 2, 2010|archive-date=November 9, 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101109021741/http://judiciary.senate.gov/hearings/testimony.cfm?id=292&wit_id=665|url-status=dead}}</ref>
