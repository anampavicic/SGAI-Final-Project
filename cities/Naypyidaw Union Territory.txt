The '''Naypyitaw Union Territory''' ({{langx|my|ပြည်‌ထောင်စုနယ်မြေ နေပြည်တော်}}), also called '''Naypyitaw Council Territory''' ({{my|နေပြည်တော်ကောင်စီနယ်မြေ}}) ('''Naypyitaw''' also spelled '''Nay Pyi Taw''', '''Naypyidaw''' or '''Nay Pyi Daw''') is an [[Administrative divisions of Myanmar|administrative division]] in central [[Myanmar]] (Burma).<ref>[http://www.irrawaddy.org/article.php?art_id=19671] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101116043806/http://www.irrawaddy.org/article.php?art_id=19671|date=November 16, 2010}}</ref> It contains [[Naypyidaw]], the capital city of Myanmar.

==Administrative divisions==
The Naypyidaw Union Territory consists of the following districts and townships:
* [[Ottara District]] (also known as North Naypyidaw)
** [[Ottarathiri Township]] ({{my|ဥတ္တရသီရိမြို့နယ်}})
** [[Pobbathiri Township]] ({{my|ပုဗ္ဗသီရိမြို့နယ်}})
** [[Tatkone Township]] ({{my|တပ်ကုန်းမြို့နယ်}})
** [[Zeyathiri Township]] ({{my|ဇေယျာသီရိမြို့နယ်}})
* [[Dekkhina District]] (also known as South Naypyidaw)
** [[Dekkhinathiri Township]] ({{my|ဒက္ခိဏသီရိမြို့နယ်}})
** [[Lewe Township]] ({{my|လယ်ဝေးမြို့နယ်}})
** [[Pyinmana Township]] ({{my|ပျဉ်းမနားမြို့နယ်}})
** [[Zabuthiri Township]] ({{my|ဇမ္ဗူသီရိမြို့နယ်}})

==Administration==
Naypyidaw Union Territory is under the direct administration of the [[President of Myanmar|President]]. Day-to-day functions are carried out on the President's behalf by the [[Naypyidaw Council]] led by a Chairperson. The Chairperson and members of the Naypyidaw Council are appointed by the President and include both civilians and Armed Forces representatives.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a1/Constitution_of_Myanmar_of_2008.pdf|title=Constitution of the Republic of the Union of Myanmar|access-date=13 October 2014|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110427121736/http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a1/Constitution_of_Myanmar_of_2008.pdf|archivedate=27 April 2011}}</ref>

On 30 March 2011, President [[Thein Sein]] appointed [[Thein Nyunt]] as chairman of the Naypyidaw Council, along with 9 chair members: Than Htay, Colonel Myint Aung Than, Kan Chun, Paing Soe, Saw Hla, Myint Swe, Myint Shwe and Myo Nyunt.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.burmalibrary.org/docs11/NLM2011-03-31.pdf|title=Notification No. 7/2011: Formation of Nay Pyi Taw Council|author=Thein Sein|date=31 March 2011|work=New Light of Myanmar|pages=15|access-date=21 August 2011}}</ref>

===Chairmen of the Naypyidaw Council===

*[[Myo Aung]] (30 Mar 2016–  1 Feb 2021) 
*Maung Maung Naing (2 Feb 2021 - 19 Aug 2022)

The [[2014 Myanmar Census]] reported that Naypyidaw Union Territory had a population of 1,160,242.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://themimu.info/sites/themimu.info/files/documents/Census_Nay_Pyi_Taw_Report_Eng_2015.pdf|title=Nay Pyi Taw Union Territory Report|date=May 2015|website=2014 Myanmar Population and Housing Census|access-date=2018-10-28|archive-date=2018-08-20|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180820181109/http://themimu.info/sites/themimu.info/files/documents/Census_Nay_Pyi_Taw_Report_Eng_2015.pdf|url-status=dead}}</ref> The [[population density]] was 164.4 people per km<sup>2</sup>.<ref name=":0" /> The census reported that the median age was 26.8 years, and 95 males per 100 females.<ref name=":0" /> There were 262,253 households; the mean household size was 4.1.<ref name=":0" />

=== Religion ===
According to the [[2014 Myanmar Census]], [[Buddhism|Buddhists]] make up 96.8% of Naypyidaw Union Territory's population, forming the largest religious community there.<ref name="TUR2">{{cite book|author=|url=https://www.dop.gov.mm/sites/dop.gov.mm/files/publication_docs/union_2-c_religion_en_0.pdf|title=The 2014 Myanmar Population and Housing Census Census Report Volume 2-C|publisher=Department of Population Ministry of Labour, Immigration and Population|date=July 2016|pages=12–15}}</ref> Minority religious communities include [[Christianity|Christians]] (1.1%), [[Islam|Muslims]] (2.1%), and [[Hinduism|Hindus]] (0%) who collectively comprise the remainder of Naypyidaw Union Territory's population.<ref name="TUR2" />

According to the [[State Sangha Maha Nayaka Committee]]’s 2016 statistics, 10,956 Buddhist monks were registered in Naypyidaw Union Territory, comprising 2% of Myanmar's total [[Sangha]] membership, which includes both novice [[samanera]] and fully-ordained bhikkhu.<ref name="mahana">{{Cite web|date=2016|title=The Account of Wazo Monks and Nuns in 1377 (2016 year)|url=http://www.mahana.org.mm/en/religious-affairs/the-account-of-wazo-monks-and-nuns-in-1377-2016-year/|access-date=2021-01-19|website=[[State Sangha Maha Nayaka Committee]]|language=en-US}}</ref> The majority of monks belong to the [[Thudhamma Nikaya]] (98.2%), followed by [[Shwegyin Nikaya]] (1.8%), with the remainder of monks belonging to other small [[Nikāya|monastic orders]].<ref name="mahana" /> 923 [[thilashin]] were registered in Naypyidaw Union Territory, comprising 1.5% of Myanmar's total thilashin community.<ref name="mahana" />
