'''Gimhae''' ({{korean|김해}}, {{IPA|ko|kim(ɦ)ɛ|pron}}) is a [[Administrative divisions of South Korea|city]] in [[South Gyeongsang Province]], South Korea, situated near the [[Nakdong River]].

It is the seat of the large [[Gimhae Kim]] clan, one of the largest [[Kim (Korean name)|Kim]] clans in Korea, claiming descent from the ancient royal house of [[Geumgwan Gaya]].<ref>''[스포츠]김해시청 축구단 창단 '초읽기''' Newsmaker</ref> Gimhae is also the birthplace of the late [[Roh Moo-hyun]], former [[president of South Korea]].

The city has a [[K3 League]] soccer club called [[Gimhae FC]]. The largest foreign sports club in Gimhae is the Gimhae Semi-Athletic Club (G-SAC) located in the Nae-dong neighbourhood.

==Climate==
Gimhae has a [[humid subtropical climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification|Köppen]]: ''Cwa'', bordering on ''Cfa'') with very warm summers and cold winters. Due to the influence of its coastal climate, Gimhae is the mildest region in Korea. The annual average temperature is around 15&nbsp;°C, and annual average precipitation is 1,200&nbsp;mm, which is very close to the Korean average of 1,274&nbsp;mm.<ref>[http://engtour.gimhae.go.kr/01/01.jsp?mmain=1&pagenum=1 Welcome To GimHae: Home > Gimhae Travel Guide > Introducing Gimhae<!-- Bot generated title -->]{{dead link|date=October 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

==Transportation==
[[File:Gimhae National Museum Station.jpg|thumb|300px|[[Busan-Gimhae Light Rail Transit]] opened to the public on September 9, 2011]]

===Airport===
[[Gimhae International Airport]] is just east of Gimhae (in Gangseo District, Busan) and has 9.17&nbsp;million annual users. Various conveniences, especially those for the disabled, will be expanded step by step.<ref>[http://engtour.gimhae.go.kr/01/03_01.jsp?mmain=1&pagenum=3&subnum=1 Welcome To GimHae : Home > Gimhae Travel Guide > Transportation<!-- Bot generated title -->]{{dead link|date=October 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

===Railways===
Gimhae has two stations on the [[Gyeongjeon Line]]; [[Hallimjeong station]] ({{Korean|hangul=한림정역|labels=no}}) and [[Jinyeong station]] ({{Korean|hangul=진영역|labels=no}}). The [[Busan–Gimhae Light Rail Transit]], which opened in September 2011, connects the city to Busan via [[Gimhae International Airport|the airport]]. It connects to the [[Busan Metro]] network {{rint|busan|3}} at [[Daejeo LRT Station|Daejeo]] ({{Korean|hangul=대저|labels=no}}), and {{rint|busan|2}} at [[Sasang LRT Station|Sasang]] ({{Korean|hangul=사상|labels=no}}). The closest [[KTX]] station from Gimhae is [[Gupo station]] in Busan.

==Floricultural industry==
Gimhae has spacious flower gardens. Busan and its neighbor cities have exported large amounts of flowers.
