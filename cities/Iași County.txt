'''Iași County''' ({{IPA|ro|ˈjaʃʲ}}) is a county (județ) of [[Romania]], in [[Western Moldavia]], with the administrative seat at [[Iași]]. It is the most populous county in Romania, after the Municipality of [[Bucharest]] (which has the same administrative level as that of a county).

==Geography==
[[File:Hanul Trei Iazuri2.jpg|thumb|200px|Three Lakes Area]]
This county has a total area of {{cvt|5476|km2}}. It lies on a plain between the [[Siret (river)|Siret River]] and the [[Prut|Prut River]]. Two other rivers run through the county: the [[Bahlui|Bahlui River]] (on the banks of which lies the city of [[Iași]]) and the [[Jijia|Jijia River]].

===Neighbours===
{{Romanian counties map|width=250px}}
*[[Moldova|Republic of Moldova]] to the east - [[Ungheni District]].
*[[Neamț County]] to the west.
*[[Botoșani County]] and [[Suceava County]] to the northwest.
*[[Vaslui County]] to the south.

At the [[2021 Romanian census|2021 census]] Iași County had a population of 760,774. ==Economy==
This county is predominantly agricultural, due to its topography. Industry is concentrated in the cities. 
The principal industries are:
* Software
* Pharmaceuticals
* Automotive
* Metallurgy and heavy-equipment manufacturing
* Electronics & Electrotechnics
* Textiles
* Food production

==Tourism==
[[File:Mănăstirea Hadâmbu24.jpg|thumb|200px|[[Hadâmbu Monastery]]]]
[[File:Castelul Sturdza din Miclăușeni.jpg|thumb|200px|Sturdza Palace in [[Butea, Iași|Miclăușeni]]]]
The city of [[Iași]] is the most important city in [[Moldavia]] and one of the most important social, cultural and business centres in Romania. It has the oldest [[Alexandru Ioan Cuza University|University]] in the country, and, until the formation of the [[United Principalities]], it was the capital of Moldavia.

Some of the tourist destinations in the county:
* City of [[Iași]] and its environs (the [[Seven hills of Iași]]);
* [[Alexandru Ioan Cuza]] Memorial Palace in [[Ruginoasa, Iași|Ruginoasa]];
* [[Cucuteni]] - Neolithic archeological site;
* [[Grasă de Cotnari|Cotnari]] and [[Busuioacă de Bohotin|Bohotin]] vineyards; 
* Museum of Vineyard and Wine in [[Hârlău]];
* [[Hadâmbu Monastery|Hadâmbu]] and [[Dobrovăț Monastery|Dobrovăț]] Monasteries;
* [[Butea, Iași|Miclăușeni]] Castle and Monastery; 
* [[Vasile Alecsandri]] Memorial House in [[Mircești]];
* [[Constantin Negruzzi]] Museum in [[Trifești, Iași|Hermeziu]];
* [[Cezar Petrescu]] Museum in [[Cotnari]];
* City of [[Pașcani]], and towns of [[Târgu Frumos]] and [[Hârlău]];
* [[Strunga]] health resort.

The county was located in the northeastern part of [[Greater Romania]], in the northeast of the region of [[Moldavia]]. Today, most of the territory of the former county is part of the current Iași County. In the eastern part of the county, the county included a part of the left bank of the [[Prut River]], now in the territory of the [[Moldova|Republic of Moldova]]. It was bordered to the north by the counties of [[Botoșani County#Historical county|Botoșani]] and [[Bălți County (Romania)|Bălți]], to the east by [[Lăpușna County (Romania)|Lăpușna County]], to the south by the counties of [[Fălciu County|Fălciu]] and [[Vaslui County#Historical county|Vaslui]], and to the west by the counties of [[Roman County|Roman]] and [[Baia County|Baia]].

Iași County included two urban localities: Iași (county seat) and urban commune Târgu Frumos, located at the western border of the county.

=== Population ===
According to the 1930 census data, the county population was 275,796 inhabitants, 81.6% Romanians, 14.6% Jews, 0.6% Russians, 0.5% Hungarians, 0.4% Germans, as well as other minorities.<ref name=Rec_1930_246>Recensământul general al populației României din 29 decemvrie 1930, Vol. II, pag. 246-247</ref> From the religious point of view, the population was 82.0% Eastern Orthodox, 14.9% Jewish, 2.3% Roman Catholic, as well as other minorities.

==== Urban population ====
In 1930, the county's urban population was 107,804 inhabitants, 102,872 in Iaşi and 4,932 in Târgu Frumos, comprising 60.8% Romanians, 33.6% Jews, 0.9% Germans, 0.9% Russians, as well as other minorities. In the urban area, languages were Romanian (72.5%), followed by Yiddish (22.2%), Russian (1.8%), German (0.9%), as well as other minorities.<ref name=Rec_1930_246/> From the religious point of view, the urban population was composed of Eastern Orthodox (61.4%), Jewish (34.4%), Roman Catholic (3.0%), as well as other minorities.
