'''Pingtung County''' ({{zh|t=屏東縣|p=Píngdōng Xìan}}) <ref>[[Taiwanese Mandarin|Mandarin]] [[pinyin]]: ''Píngdōng Xiàn'', [[Wade-Giles]]: ''Pʻing²-tung¹ Hsien⁴''; [[Taiwanese Hokkien|Hokkien]] [[Pe̍h-ōe-jī|POJ]]: ''Pîn-tong-koān''; [[Taiwanese Hakka|Hakka]] [[Pha̍k-fa-sṳ|PFS]]: ''Phìn-tûng-yan''; [[Paiwan language|Paiwan]]: ''Akaw''/''Qakaw''</ref> is a [[County (Taiwan)|county]] located in [[southern Taiwan]]. It has a warm [[tropical monsoon climate]] and is known for its agriculture and tourism. [[Kenting National Park]], Taiwan's oldest national park, is located in the county. The [[county seat]] is [[Pingtung City]].

==Name==
The name ''Pingtung'' means "east of Banping mountain",<ref>{{Cite web|last=Cultural Heritage of Pingtung|first=County Cultural Center|title=A-Hou City Gate|url=https://www.cultural.pthg.gov.tw/Son/Landmark/page02_2.aspx?ID=11#/PhotoData/1051030195011.jpg|access-date=2021-03-24}}</ref> referring to a nearby [[Mount Banping|mountain]] known as Banping mountain ({{zh|c={{linktext|半屏山}}|poj={{linktext|Pòaⁿ-pêng-soaⁿ}}}}).

==History==
===Early history===
Aboriginal inhabitants of [[Liuqiu Island]] ({{convert|13|km|mi|abbr=on|disp=sqbr}} southwest of Taiwan, and now part of Pingtung County) killed Dutch sailors on two occasions. In response, in the spring of 1636, Dutch sailors carried out a punitive campaign that became known as the [[Lamey Island Massacre]].

Modern-day Pingtung County and [[Kaohsiung City]] were part of Banlian-chiu (萬年州; ''Bān-liân-chiu'') during the [[Kingdom of Tungning]] (1661–1683) and Fongshan Prefecture (鳳山縣; ''Hōng-soaⁿ-koān'') during [[Taiwan under Qing rule|Qing dynasty rule]] (1683–1895).

Until the seventeenth century, this area of Taiwan was a place of exile for Chinese criminals and the occasional landing point for international [[Sailor|mariners]]. Only the settlements near present-day [[Checheng, Pingtung|Checheng Township]] existed. In 1664, the [[Hakka people|Hakka]] settlers arrived from [[mainland China]] and farmed under a homesteading system introduced by [[Zheng Jing]].

Pingtung City, the biggest city in Pingtung County, also known as "A-Kau" (阿猴; ''A-kâu'', English: the forest), was the home of [[Taiwanese Plains Aborigines]].

In 1684, settlers from China's southern [[Fujian]] region created the first Han Chinese villages near Pingtung. By 1734, most of the [[Pingtung Plain]] was cultivated, and Pingtung was expanded in 1764. In 1836, the government and locals worked together to build the city's four walls (the North Gate, the East Gate, the West Gate, and the South gate), and the roads were completed.

In March 1867, fourteen American sailors were killed near [[Kenting]] by local aborigines in the [[Rover incident|''Rover'' incident]], which lead to the failed American [[Formosa Expedition]] three months later. In 1871, local aborigines killed 54 sailors from Ryukyu in the [[Mudan incident|Mudan Incident]]. The Japanese carried out a punitive campaign against the local aborigines in the [[Japanese invasion of Taiwan (1874)|1874 Japanese invasion of Taiwan]].

===Empire of Japan===
{{main|Takao Prefecture}}
Under [[Taiwan under Japanese rule|Japanese rule]] (1895–1945), {{nihongo|Hōzan Subprefecture|鳳山支廳|lead=yes}}; {{zh|poj=Hōng-soaⁿ Chi-thiaⁿ}} was initially under {{nihongo4||臺南縣|Tainan Ken}}; {{zh|poj=Tâi-lâm-koān}}, but political divisions frequently changed between 1895 and 1901. In 1901, {{nihongo4||阿猴廳|Akō Chō}}; {{zh|poj=A-kâu-thiaⁿ}} was established. In 1909, the name changed to {{nihongo4||阿緱廳|Akō Chō}}; {{zh|poj=A-kâu-thiaⁿ}}. In 1920, the name was changed to {{nihongo4|Heitō City|屏東市}}; {{zh|poj=Pîn-tong-chhī}} and was under [[Takao Prefecture]] administration, which consisted of modern-day Pingtung County and [[Kaohsiung]].

===Republic of China===
Following the [[Retrocession Day|handover of Taiwan from Japan]] to the [[Republic of China (1912–1949)|Republic of China]] on 25 October 1945, the area of present-day Pingtung County was incorporated into [[Kaohsiung County]] on 25 December 1945. On 16 August 1950, Pingtung County was established after being separated from Kaohsiung County.<ref>{{cite news|date=1 February 2011|url=https://taiwantoday.tw/news.php?unit=4,29,31,45&post=6187|title=Rezoning Taiwan|publisher=Taiwan Today|access-date=9 December 2020}}</ref> On 1 December 1951, [[Pingtung City]] was downgraded from [[Provincial city (Taiwan)|provincial city]] to [[county-administered city]] and made the [[county seat]] of Pingtung County.

Pingtung was the site of a [[2006 Hengchun earthquakes|7.1 magnitude earthquake]] on 26 December 2006. In 2009, due to [[Typhoon Morakot]], Pingtung received over {{convert|2500|mm|in}} of rainfall, breaking records for any place in Taiwan struck by a single typhoon.

==Geography==
With a land area of over {{convert|2775|km2|abbr=on}}, Pingtung is the fifth-largest county in Taiwan, and the second-largest of [[Southern Taiwan]] after [[Kaohsiung City]]. Geographically, it borders Kaohsiung City to the north, [[Taitung County]] to the east, the [[Taiwan Strait]] to the west and the [[Bashi Channel]] to the south. Islands administered by the county include [[Hsiao Liuchiu]] (Lamay Islet; 琉球嶼) and [[Qixingyan (Taiwan)|Qixingyan]] (Seven Star Reefs; {{linktext|七星岩}}).

===Climate===
Located in the southernmost part of Taiwan, Pingtung County is known for one of the warmest climates within the country. It has a [[tropical monsoon climate]] bordering on a [[tropical wet and dry climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification]]s: Am bordering on Aw). The climate differs across the large county due to its varying geography.

Northern Pingtung, where [[Pingtung City]] is located, is characterized by high daytime temperatures year-round with average daytime highs of {{convert|30|–|40|C|F}} from April to November, and {{convert|25|–|28|C|F}} from December to March. The lowest nighttime temperatures are around {{convert|16|°C|°F|abbr=on}}, due to distance from the sea.

Central Pingtung, such as the coastal [[Fangliao|Fangliao Township]], has a lower daytime temperatures and warmer nights due to the regulating effect of the ocean, which is especially noticeable during winter.

The mildest climate of Pingtung is at its southern tip, the [[Hengchun Peninsula]], which is nearly surrounded by the Pacific Ocean. Daily highs reach {{convert|29|–|32|C|F}} during summer and {{convert|23|–|26|C|F}} during winter. Nighttime temperatures remain warm throughout the year with lows of around {{convert|25|°C|°F|abbr=on}} during summer and {{convert|19|°C|°F|abbr=on}} during the winter.

===Politics===
[[Pingtung City]] is the county seat of Pingtung County which houses the [[Pingtung County Government]] and [[Pingtung County Council]]. The county head is Magistrate [[Pan Men-an]] of the [[Democratic Progressive Party]]. Pingtung County elected three [[Democratic Progressive Party]] legislators to the [[Legislative Yuan]] during the [[2016 Taiwanese general election|2016 legislative election]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://vote2016.cec.gov.tw/en/T1/n707010000000000.html|title=2016 The 14th Presidential and Vice Presidential Election and The 9th Legislator Election|access-date=17 January 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304122924/http://vote2016.cec.gov.tw/en/T1/n707010000000000.html|archive-date=4 March 2016|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://vote2016.cec.gov.tw/en/T1/n707020000000000.html|title=2016 The 14th Presidential and Vice Presidential Election and The 9th Legislator Election|access-date=17 January 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304121444/http://vote2016.cec.gov.tw/en/T1/n707020000000000.html|archive-date=4 March 2016|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://vote2016.cec.gov.tw/en/T1/n707030000000000.html|title=2016 The 14th Presidential and Vice Presidential Election and The 9th Legislator Election|access-date=17 January 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304131322/http://vote2016.cec.gov.tw/en/T1/n707030000000000.html|archive-date=4 March 2016|url-status=live}}</ref>

Pingtung is home to the [[Taiwanese indigenous peoples|indigenous]] [[Rukai people|Rukai]] and [[Paiwan people|Paiwan tribes]], which makes up 7% of the population.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pthg.gov.tw/en/cp.aspx?n=2E1F32592C9629E5|title=PINGTUNG County Government|access-date=6 January 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160203044009/http://www.pthg.gov.tw/en/cp.aspx?n=2E1F32592C9629E5|archive-date=3 February 2016|url-status=live}}</ref> {{As of |2023|January|post=,}} the total population of Pingtung County was 798,940. ===Religion===
{{As of |2015|post=,}} Pingtung County had 1,101 registered [[Chinese temple|temples]], the third highest amongst Taiwan's counties after [[Tainan]] and [[Kaohsiung]].{{citation needed|date=January 2020}}
[[Donglong Temple]] and [[Checheng Fu'an Temple]] are some of the most prominent temples in the county. Indigenous tribes like [[Makatao people]] also have their own places of worship like [[Jiaruipu Temple]].

==Economy==
The agriculture and fishing industries dominate the county's economy. In recent years, the county has also promoted the tourism sector, which constituted 30% of [[Tourism in Taiwan|Taiwan's tourism industry]] in 2015.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.pthg.gov.tw/en/cp.aspx?n=A0061DD41F5FEABF |title=About Pingtung |access-date=20 July 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160828140557/http://www.pthg.gov.tw/en/cp.aspx?n=A0061DD41F5FEABF |archive-date=28 August 2016 |url-status=live }}</ref>

In February 2014, the county government announced a plan to develop an industrial center that consists of a service center, workshop, and performance venue. The aim is to promote the development of industries that leverage the unique cultural attributes of the Linali tribe.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.chinapost.com.tw/taiwan/local/pingtung/2014/02/17/400793/Industry-development.htm |title=Industry development center almost ready in Pingtung County: Gov't - the China Post |access-date=14 February 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150214164901/http://www.chinapost.com.tw/taiwan/local/pingtung/2014/02/17/400793/Industry-development.htm |archive-date=14 February 2015 |url-status=live }}</ref> On 22&nbsp;September 2015, the [[National Development Council (Taiwan)|National Development Council]] revealed a three-year large-scale development plan to boost the economy of the county by transforming [[Dapeng Bay]], [[Donggang, Pingtung|Donggang Township]], and [[Xiaoliuqiu|Lamay Island]] into a fishery, recreational, and tourism hub.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.taiwantoday.tw/ct.asp?xItem=236995&ctNode=2182 |title=NDC rolls out Pingtung County development plan - Taiwan Today |access-date=20 July 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160916111850/http://www.taiwantoday.tw/ct.asp?xItem=236995&ctNode=2182 |archive-date=16 September 2016 |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Energy==
Pingtung County houses Taiwan's third nuclear power plant, the [[Maanshan Nuclear Power Plant]]. The power plant is located in [[Hengchun|Hengchun Township]]. It is Taiwan's second-largest nuclear power plant in terms of its capacity at {{nowrap|2 × 890 MW.}}

==Transportation==
===Rail===
[[File:Pingtung Railway Station New20150901.jpg|thumb|[[Pingtung Station]]]]

The [[Pingtung Line]] and [[South-Link Line]] of the [[Taiwan Railways Administration]] cross Pingtung County. These lines service [[Central Signal Station|Central Signal]], Chaozhou, Donghai, [[Fangliao Station|Fangliao]], [[Fangshan Station|Fangshan]], [[Fangye Signal Station|Fangye Signal]], Guilai, Jiadong, [[Jialu Station|Jialu]], Kanding, Linbian, Linluo, [[Liukuaicuo Station|Liukuaicuo]], Nanzhou, [[Neishi Station|Neishi]], [[Pingtung Station|Pingtung]], Xishi, Zhen'an and Zhutian Station. The Pingtung Line links Pingtung County with [[Kaohsiung City]], while the South-Link Line links Pingtung County with [[Taitung County]].

===Ferries===
Ferry service operates between [[Donggang Township]] and [[Baisha Port]] and [[Dafu Port]] on the offshore [[Lamay Island]].
