'''Chapecó''' ({{IPA|pt|ʃapeˈkɔ}}) is a municipality in the state of [[Santa Catarina (state)|Santa Catarina]], in the Southern Region of [[Brazil]]. Being a major industrial, financial and educational center, it is a major producer of industrialized food products. Considered a medium city, with a population estimated at 224,013 inhabitants, it is among the four most important cities in the state. It belongs to the Meso-region of Western [[Santa Catarina (state)|Santa Catarina]] and to the Microregion of Chapecó.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ibge.gov.br/geociencias-novoportal/organizacao-do-territorio/estrutura-territorial/15761-areas-dos-municipios.html?&t=destaques|title=Áreas dos Municípios {{!}} Geociências {{!}} IBGE :: Instituto Brasileiro de Geografia e Estatística|last=Rego|first=Rodrigo Faria De Almeida|website=www.ibge.gov.br|language=pt-br|access-date=2018-07-05}}</ref>

Distant 550&nbsp;km from the state capital, [[Florianópolis]], it is Headquarters of the Metropolitan Region of Chapecó, and exerts significant influence not only in the Catarinense West but also in the Northwest [[Rio Grande do Sul]] and Southwest of [[Paraná (state)|Paraná]], from an economic, cultural, or political point of view.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ub.edu/geocrit/-xcol/257.htm|title=A influência do capital agroindustrial na distribuição sócio-espacial urbana do município de Chapecó no sul do Brasil|website=www.ub.edu|access-date=2018-07-05}}</ref>

Chapecó became known worldwide through its [[association football]] club [[Associação Chapecoense de Futebol|Chapecoense]] and the aftermath of [[LaMia Flight 2933]] which killed 71, including most of the team's roster and staff.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.independent.ie/world-news/lack-of-fuel-caused-chapecoense-plane-crash-tragedy-probe-finds-36852688.html|title=Lack of fuel caused Chapecoense plane crash tragedy, probe finds - Independent.ie|work=Independent.ie|access-date=2018-07-05|language=en}}</ref>

With the titles of "Brazil's [[agribusiness]] capital" and "Brazil's [[business tourism]] capital", the [[planned town]] constructed in the form of a chess grid also has universities attracting students from all over [[Brazil]]. The main educational institutions are [[Universidade Federal da Fronteira do Sul|UFFS]], UCEFF, UNOCHAPECÓ, UNOESC and [[Santa Catarina State University|UDESC]].<ref name="ibge-2016">{{Cite web |url=http://ftp.ibge.gov.br/Estimativas_de_Populacao/Estimativas_2016/estimativa_dou_2016_20160913.pdf |title=Estimativas da população residente no Brasil e unidades da federação com data de referência em 1º de Julho de 2016 |publisher=Instituto Brasileiro de Geografia e Estatística |page=81 |language=pt |access-date=30 December 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.cidades.ibge.gov.br/xtras/perfil.php?lang=&codmun=420420 |title=Chapecó |publisher=Instituto Brasileiro de Geografia e Estatística |access-date=30 December 2016|language=pt}}</ref>

== History ==

Until the 19th century, the area around Chapecó land was under [[Indigenous peoples in Brazil|indigenous]] (mainly [[Kaingang]]) and [[Spain|Spanish]] control, mostly unoccupied by [[Ethnic groups in Europe|European]] people and subject to [[Argentina-Brazil border|border issues]] and armed conflicts. Brazilian control was in fact established as a [[:pt:Colônia Militar de Chapecó|military colony]] in 1882. Its official status as a [[Municipalities of Brazil|municipality]] was established by state government decree after the [[Contestado War]] on August 25, 1917. Its area was then given to a private colonization company<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.unochapeco.edu.br/en/ceom/noticias/ceom-unochapeco-recebe-acervo-da-colonizadora-bertaso |title=CEOM/Unochapecó recebe acervo da colonizadora Bertaso |date=28 February 2014 |publisher=Unochapecó}}</ref> which began selling land to people coming mainly from the neighboring state of [[Rio Grande do Sul]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://nilson-historia.blogspot.com.au/2009/02/ocupacao-do-territorio-contestado-apos.html |title=A Ocupação do Território Contestado após 1917 |last=Thomé |first=Nilson |website=História do Contestado |access-date=30 December 2016|language=pt}}</ref> Most of its inhabitants are descended from [[Italian Brazilians|Italian]], [[German Brazilians|German]] and [[Polish Brazilians|Polish]] immigrants.

== Demography ==
[[File:Centro chapeco.jpg|left|thumb|320x320px|Buildings in the central part of the city in 2017]]
The region has always been the subject of disputes because of its strategic importance as a border region. During the [[Contestado War]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://educacao.uol.com.br/disciplinas/historia-brasil/|title=UOL Educação: Pesquisa escolar, vestibular e dicas para alunos, pais e professores - UOL Educação|website=UOL Educação|language=pt-BR|access-date=2018-07-05}}</ref> airplanes were used for the first time in the history of [[Americas|America]] for the purpose of recognition and support for war operations. Colonized by [[Italy|Italian]] and [[Germans|German]] immigrants, mainly from [[Rio Grande do Sul]], [[Paraná (state)|Paraná]], [[São Paulo (state)|São Paulo]] and the coast of [[Santa Catarina (state)|Santa Catarina]], the city today attracts several inhabitants thanks to its development.

It is one of the cities with the highest number (in percentage) of buildings in [[Santa Catarina (state)|Santa Catarina]]. According to the [[Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics|IBGE]], Chapecó has almost 16% of its population living in apartments, gaining in percentage of cities such as [[Joinville]], [[Itajaí]] and [[Blumenau]].

== Economy ==
[[File:Chapeco01.png|left|thumb|327x327px]]
This region of the state is home of some of the largest meat processing and exporting industrial enterprises such as [[Sadia]] and [[Seara Foods]]; the farmers are organized in [[agricultural cooperatives]]. Chapecó is known as a Brazilian agro-industrial capital, specialized in [[pork]], [[poultry]] and technology involved.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.acichapeco.com.br/links/chapeco---sc |title=Conheça mais sobre Chapecó |publisher=Associação Comercial e Industrial Chapecó |access-date=30 December 2016|language=pt}}</ref> The city is headquarters of [http://www.auroraalimentos.com.br/export/aurora.php Aurora Central Cooperative] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161123095108/http://www.auroraalimentos.com.br/export/aurora.php |date=2016-11-23 }} and has a plant of [[BRF S.A.]] since 1973.

Other major economic sectors are metal mechanics specialized in equipment for [[slaughterhouses]] and [[transportation]], plastics and [[packaging]], [[furniture]], [[beverages]], [[software development]] and [[biotechnology]]. [[Civil construction]] and [[trade]] are also important source of income.

Chapecó is served by [[Chapecó Airport|Serafin Enoss Bertaso Airport]].

==Culture==

===Sports===

Football is the great passion of the Chapecoenses. The [[Associação Chapecoense de Futebol|Chapecoense Football Association]], known among its fans as a "Hurricane from the west", Chape and verdão, a team from Santa Catarina, has as its last title the Campeonato Catarinense de Futebol de 2020 - Série A. Its headquarters is in Arena Condá, with a capacity of over 22,000 people and located in the East Zone of the city. The team had spent three consecutive years in the C Series of the Brazilian Championship (2010, 2011 and 2012), before beginning a rapid rise through the Brazilian football pyramid by reaching the second division in 2013 and entering the Serie A Brasileirão in 2014, where it currently resides.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://odia.ig.com.br/esporte/2013-11-20/os-segredos-do-sucesso-a-ascensao-meteorica-da-chapecoense-rumo-a-elite.html|title=Os segredos do sucesso: A ascensão meteórica da Chapecoense rumo à elite|date=2013-11-19|work=O Dia - Esporte|access-date=2018-07-05|language=pt-BR}}</ref> The team was among the best teams in South America, reaching the quarterfinals of the [[2015 Copa Sudamericana]] and reaching the final of the [[2016 Copa Sudamericana]].

On November 29, 2016, Chapecoense team members and staff boarded [[LaMia Flight 2933]] to play the first leg of the [[2016 Copa Sudamericana Finals]] against [[Atlético Nacional]] in [[Medellín]], [[Colombia]].  The plane crashed in the mountains a few miles south of the city. Seventy-one of the seventy-seven people on board died, including all of the club's coaching staff and all but three of the players. A wave of commotion abated in the football world in the days and weeks after, playing tribute to the club; Atlético Nacional requested that [[CONMEBOL]], the governing football association for South America, award the 2016 title to Chapecoense, which was granted.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://g1.globo.com/mundo/noticia/2016/11/aviao-com-equipe-da-chapecoense-sofre-acidente-na-colombia.html|title=Avião com equipe da Chapecoense cai na Colômbia e deixa mortos|date=2016-11-29|work=Mundo|access-date=2018-07-05|language=pt-BR}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Romero |first1=Simon | author1-link =Simon Romero |last2=Casey |first2=Nicholas |date=29 November 2016 |title=Brazil's Chapecoense Soccer Team Devastated as Plane Crash Kills Dozens |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/11/29/world/americas/plane-carrying-brazilian-soccer-team-crashes-in-colombia.html |work=[[The New York Times]] |access-date=29 November 2016}}</ref>
