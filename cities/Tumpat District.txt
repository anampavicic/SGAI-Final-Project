'''Tumpat''' ([[Kelantan-Pattani Malay|Kelantanese]]: ''Ttupak'') is a town, a [[Districts of Malaysia|district (jajahan)]] and [[Dewan Rakyat|parliamentary constituency]] in northern [[Kelantan]], [[Malaysia]], at the mouth of [[Kelantan River]]. 

Tumpat is situated at the end of the ''[[East Coast Line (Malaysia)|East Coast Line]]'' railway line operated by [[Keretapi Tanah Melayu]] (Malayan Railways) which links Kelantan to the southern and western part of [[Peninsular Malaysia]]. This strategic location makes it a transportation hub of Kelantan. Tumpat town is approximately 15&nbsp;km from the state capital, [[Kota Bharu]].

==History==
Tumpat district was established on 1 January 1949, detaching from [[Kota Bharu District|Kota Bharu metropolitan area]].

[[File:Map of Tumpat District, Kelantan.svg|thumb|Map of Tumpat District]]

==Geography==
Tumpat is the northernmost constituency in Kelantan, bordering [[Thailand]] across the [[Golok River]] to the west, [[Kota Bharu]] across the [[Kelantan River]] to the east, and [[Pasir Mas]] to the south, and the [[Thailand|Thai]] district of [[Tak Bai District|Tak Bai]] to the west.

Tumpat has a population about 152,168 (2010), with the majority being the [[Malaysian Malays|Malays]] with significant [[Malaysian Siamese|Siamese]], [[Malaysian Indian|Indian]] and [[Malaysian Chinese|Chinese]] population.{{my10|2}}

Generally in Kelantan, the populations of Malaysian Indians are low but Tumpat is known to be the area where most Indians reside in Kelantan. During any Indian festivals, Indians around Kelantan gather there for community celebration events like Ponggal, Thaipusam & Deepavali.

==Transportation==
===Public transport===
Tumpat is perhaps famous for being the northern terminal of the [[KTM East Coast Line]], which began in [[Gemas]] in [[Negeri Sembilan]]. Bus options to Kota Bharu and [[Kuala Terengganu]] are available.

===Car===
The main federal highway serving Tumpat constituency is [[Malaysia Federal Route 134|Federal Route 134]], going from downtown Kota Bharu, crossing the Kelantan River and terminating at Pengkalan Kubur. The main roads serving downtown Tumpat are Kelantan state routes {{JKR(D)|174}} and {{JKR(D)|23}}.
