'''Berbérati''' is the [[List of cities in the Central African Republic|third-largest city]] in the [[Central African Republic]], with a population of 105,155 (2013 census). Located in the south-west of the country, near the border with [[Cameroon]], it serves as capital of the [[Mambéré-Kadéï]] [[Prefectures of the Central African Republic|Prefecture]] and the Berbérati [[Sub-prefectures of the Central African Republic|sub-prefecture]].

== History ==
[[File:Africa. French Equatorial Africa. The zebu herd (oxen) arrive at Compagnie Miniere de l' Oubanghi headquarters in... - NARA - 541651.tif|thumb|right|The zebu herd (oxen) arrive at Compagnie Miniere de l' Oubanghi headquarters in Berberati, a little weary and thin after the 260 kilometer hike. ca. 1950]]
The city was founded in 1893.<ref> Richard Bradshaw, Juan Fandos-Rius, ''Historical Dictionary of the Central African Republic'', Rowman & Littlefield, USA, 2016, p. 115</ref> In the early 20th century Berbérati was part of [[Oubangui-Chari]], one of the four territories comprising [[French Equatorial Africa]]. In 1911 it was ceded to the [[German Empire]] under the terms of the [[Morocco–Congo Treaty]] and the [[Treaty of Fez]], becoming part of the German colony of [[Neukamerun]], until it was reconquered by the French in 1916 following the defeat of German forces in western Africa during [[World War I]].

== Health ==
The state-owned [[Teaching hospital|university hospital]] of Berbérati is an unfenced complex of several bungalows near the town center. The hospital was constructed in the 1950s and operated by French military doctors until the 1980s. The French hospital administrators were succeeded by an expatriate Italian Catholic nun, although the hospital also receives Protestant support.<ref>{{cite web|last=Beesley|first=Mark|title=The Bottom of the Sack: Health Service Provision in the Central African Republic|url=http://www.sph.uq.edu.au/docs/ACITH/Health%20Provision%20in%20the%20Central%20African%20Republic%20latest%20draft.pdf|publisher=[[University of Queensland]]|accessdate=6 December 2013|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140204090105/http://www.sph.uq.edu.au/docs/ACITH/Health%20Provision%20in%20the%20Central%20African%20Republic%20latest%20draft.pdf|archivedate=4 February 2014}}</ref>

== Places of worship ==  
[[File:Église Évangélique Baptiste de Berberati-centre.jpg|thumb|Evangelical Baptist Church of Berbérati]]
[[Places of worship]] in Berbérati are predominantly [[Christianity|Christian]] churches and temples: [[Evangelical Lutheran Church of the Central African Republic]] ([[Lutheran World Federation]]), [[Evangelical Baptist Church of the Central African Republic]] ([[Baptist World Alliance]]), [[Roman Catholic Diocese of Berbérati]] ([[Catholic Church]]).<ref> J. Gordon Melton, Martin Baumann, ‘‘Religions of the World: A Comprehensive Encyclopedia of Beliefs and Practices’’, ABC-CLIO, USA, 2010, p. 531-532</ref> There are also [[Islam|Muslim]] mosques.
