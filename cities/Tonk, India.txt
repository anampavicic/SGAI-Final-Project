'''Tonk''' is a town in Tonk district of the Indian state of [[Rajasthan]]. The town of Tonk is situated 95&nbsp;km (60&nbsp;mi) by road south from [[Jaipur]], near the right bank of the [[Banas River]]. It is the administrative headquarters of [[Tonk District]]. Tonk was also the capital of the [[Tonk (princely state)|eponymous]] [[princely state]] of [[British India]] from 1817 to 1947. Kamal Amrohi's movie ''[[Razia Sultan (film)|Razia Sultan]]'' were shot in Tonk in 1981–82. Famous places in Tonk include: Shahi Jama Masjid, [[Bisalpur Dam]], [[Rajasthan Arabic and Persian Research Institute|Arabic Persian Research Institute]], [[Sunehri Kothi, Tonk|Sunhari Kothi]], Hathi Bhata, Annapurna Dungri Ganesh Temple, Rasiya Ki Tekri, Kidwai Park, Ghantaghar, Kamdhenu Circle, Nehru Garden, Chaturbhuj Talab Lake. It is also known as [[Rajasthan|Rajasthan's]] ''Nawabo ka shahar.''<ref>{{Cite web |title=पोर्टल, राजस्थान सरकार |url=https://tonk.rajasthan.gov.in/home/dptHome |access-date=2022-12-30 |website=tonk.rajasthan.gov.in}}</ref>

==Demographics==

In the [[2011 Indian census]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.census2011.co.in/census/city/85-tonk.html|title= Census of India 2011: Data from the 2011 Census, including cities, villages and towns (Provisional)|access-date=2021-01-01|publisher= Census Commission of India}}</ref> Tonk had a population of 165,294, with 48% being female. 14% of the population is age six and under. Tonk has an average literacy rate of 68.62%: 77.68% in males, and 59.18% in females. In terms of religion, 48% were Muslim, 50% were Hindu, 1.8% were Jain and 0.2% others.

[[Muhammad Amir Khan]] (1769–1834), a leader of Pashtun descent from Salarzai sub-clan of [[Yusufzai]] Tribe, Village Jawarai (Jowar) of District [[Buner]], [[Khyber Pakhtunkhwa]]. In 1806, Khan conquered the area, taking it from a retreating regime [[Yashwant Rao Holkar]]. The British government captured it in turn. Khan then received the [[Tonk State|state of Tonk]] from the British Government who returned it.<ref>{{Cite book | url=https://books.google.com/books?id=7iOsNUZ2MXgC&q=holkar+tonk&pg=PA542 | title=The Golden Book of India: A Genealogical and Biographical Dictionary of the Ruling Princes, Chiefs, Nobles, and Other Personages, Titled or Decorated of the Indian Empire| isbn=9788187879541| last1=Lethbridge| first1=Sir Roper| year=2005| publisher=Aakar Books}}</ref> In 1817, after the [[Third Anglo-Maratha War]], Amir Khan submitted to the British [[British East India Company]] and kept his territory of Tonk while receiving the title of [[Nawab]].<ref>[http://www.worldstatesmen.org/India_princes_K-W.html Princely States of India]</ref> Tonk was founded one year later after Khan was granted land by the ruler of [[Indore State|Indore]].

A municipality was established at Tonk in 1885.<ref>{{cite book |author=Rima Hooja |title=A History of Rajasthan |year=2006 |publisher=Rupa |isbn=9788129108906 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=tosMAQAAMAAJ |page=1166}}</ref>
