'''Holguín''' ({{IPAc-en|ɔː|l|ˈ|g|iː|n}},<ref>[http://www.dictionary.com/browse/holguin "Holguín"]. ''[[Random House Webster's Unabridged Dictionary]]''.</ref> {{IPA|es|olˈgin}}) is a municipality-city in [[Cuba]]. After [[Havana]], [[Santiago de Cuba]], and [[Camagüey]], it is the [[List of cities in Cuba|fourth largest city]] in Cuba.

==History==
{{See also|Timeline of Holguín}}
Before [[Christopher Columbus|Columbus]], the [[Taino]] people settled in huts made from [[royal palm]] in the Holguin area later urbanized by the [[Spanish people|Spanish]]; their artifacts are shown at the local Holguin La Periquera [[museum]]. The settlement was founded in 1523 on land donated by [[Diego Velázquez de Cuéllar]] to Captain Francisco García Holguín, a Spanish military officer. Holguin added his maternal surname to the name of the town, giving it the name '''San Isidoro de Holguín'''.<ref>{{Cite web| url= http://www.guije.com/pueblo/aoriente/holguin/index.htm | title= Ciudad de Holguin| author= Guije.com |date=1 September 2004 | access-date= 21 June 2020}}</ref>  Prior to 1976, Holguín was located in the province of [[Oriente Province|Oriente]]. Before [[Pope Francis's visit to the United States]], in September 2015, he visited Cuba, and one of his stops was at the Diocese of Holguín to, among other things, commemorate the location where [[Christopher Columbus]] landed.

==Geography==
The municipality is divided into ''repartos'' or [[barrio]]s. The old municipality was more extense, and in 1940 included: Aguarás, Aguas Claras, Alcalá, Arroyo Blanco del Sur, Báguanos, Cabezuelas, Cacocum, Calderón, Camazán, Cauto del Cristo, Corralillo, Cruces de Purnio, Damián, Floro Pérez, Gibara, Guabasiabo, Guayabal, Guirabo, Haticos del Purial, La Aguada, La Cuaba, La Palma, La Rioja, Las Calabazas, Managuaco, Melones, Norte, Omaja, Purnio, San Agustín, San Andrés, San Francisco, San Juan, San Lorenzo, Santa Rita, Sur, Tacámara, Tasajeras, Uñas, Uñitas, Velasco, Yareyal and Yayal.<ref name=guije>{{Cite web|url=http://www.guije.com/pueblo/municipios/oholguin/index.htm|title=Holguín|author=Guije.com|access-date=2007-10-05|language=es}}</ref>

At present time, some of the above are part of other municipalities, but the following still belong to Holguín: Aguas Claras, Guirabo, La Cuaba, Las Calabazas, Purnio, San Andrés, Yareyal.

==Economy==
[[File:Holguín Street.jpg|thumb|left|Frexes Street in Holguin]]
The brewery ''[https://www.google.com/maps/place/F%C3%A1brica+de+Cerveza+Mayabe,+Holgu%C3%ADn,+Cuba/@20.8640669,-76.2715955,886m/data=!3m1!1e3!4m5!3m4!1s0x8ecded80bbb01403:0x33b4b98950775bcb!8m2!3d20.8639549!4d-76.2699974 Cervecería Bucanero]'' is based in the city.  It makes three brands of beer: Bucanero, Cristal and Mayabe.

There are two small hotels (or villas) focusing on health tourism in the outskirts of the city, very close to the [[Frank País Airport|airport]]: ''[https://www.google.com/maps/place/Hotel+Villa+Quinqu%C3%A9/@20.8335902,-76.2820059,1773m/data=!3m1!1e3!4m5!3m4!1s0x0:0x9262f098546b369c!8m2!3d20.8360518!4d-76.2789237 Villa El Quinque]'' and ''[https://www.google.com/maps/place/Hotel+Villa+El+Cocal/@20.8038261,-76.2933999,1773m/data=!3m1!1e3!4m5!3m4!1s0x8ecdf1eba8090dd9:0x2cc2e31f98d65d04!8m2!3d20.8040316!4d-76.2912941 Villa El Cocal]''. Patients like Argentine football (soccer) star [[Diego Maradona]] have been treated there for substance addiction.

There are also two established city hotels (''[https://www.google.com/maps/place/Islazul+Pernik/@20.8877716,-76.2373908,16z/data=!4m5!3m4!1s0x0:0xd271d45983dd6915!8m2!3d20.8894521!4d-76.240489 Pernik]'' and ''[https://www.google.com/maps/place/Islazul+El+Bosque/@20.8877716,-76.2373908,16z/data=!4m5!3m4!1s0x0:0x1b56ea59a38b77e7!8m2!3d20.8888219!4d-76.2345023 El Bosque]''). More recently, a few small (boutique) hotels, managed by Hoteles E, have opened (''[https://www.google.com/maps/place/Hotel+Saratoga/@20.8862856,-76.2586446,19z/data=!4m5!3m4!1s0x0:0x654949064afe1ab8!8m2!3d20.886051!4d-76.2594494 Caballeriza]'', one block away from ''[https://www.google.com/maps/place/Las+Flores+Park/@20.8858203,-76.258045,17z/data=!4m5!3m4!1s0x8ecded6f4b73eaab:0xa5a4b574fd2dfb63!8m2!3d20.885177!4d-76.2583418 Parque San Isidoro (Las Flores)]'', and ''[https://www.google.com/maps/place/Hotel+Esmeralda/@20.8892451,-76.2609379,21z/data=!4m5!3m4!1s0x8ecded67c7886889:0x176d185b164c871e!8m2!3d20.8892448!4d-76.2609382 Esmeralda]'', right across from ''[https://www.google.com/maps/place/San+Jos%C3%A9+Park/@20.8858203,-76.2602337,16z/data=!4m5!3m4!1s0x8ecded67c485b0af:0x31d55e73b38f2a95!8m2!3d20.8892517!4d-76.2604031 Parque San José]''). As of May, 2020, and after a major restoration, there's a third boutique hotel about to be inaugurated (''[https://www.google.com/maps/place/Hotel+Saratoga/@20.8862856,-76.2586446,19z/data=!4m5!3m4!1s0x0:0x654949064afe1ab8!8m2!3d20.886051!4d-76.2594494 Saratoga]''), right across one of the corners of the main plaza and heart of the city (''[https://www.google.com/maps/place/Calixto+Garc%C3%ADa+Park/@20.8858203,-76.2602337,16z/data=!4m5!3m4!1s0x8ecded689f16305b:0xe74756b5aa7ce551!8m2!3d20.886975!4d-76.2593168 Parque Calixto Garcia]'').

==Demographics==
In 2004, the municipality of Holguín had a population of 326,740.<ref name=atenas>{{Cite web|url=http://www.atenas.inf.cu/todo/Estadisticas/TABLA%20No_3balance.htm |title=2004 Population trends, by Province and Municipality |author=Atenas.cu |year=2004 |access-date=2007-10-05 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070927111826/http://www.atenas.inf.cu/todo/Estadisticas/TABLA%20No_3balance.htm |archive-date=2007-09-27 |language=es |url-status=dead }}</ref> With a total area of {{convert|658|km2|sqmi|abbr=on}},<ref name=statoids>{{Cite web|url=http://www.statoids.com/ycu.html|title=Municipios of Cuba|author=Statoids| access-date=2007-10-05}}</ref> it had a population density of {{convert|497|/km2|/sqmi|abbr=on}}. By  2012, the population had increased slightly to 346,195<ref name=census2012>{{Cite web|url=http://www.onei.gob.cu/sites/default/files/informe_nacional_censo_0.pdf |page=110 |title=National Census Report |author=Government of Cuba |year=2012 |access-date=2020-05-21 |language=es }}</ref> for a population density of {{convert|520|/km2|/sqmi|abbr=on}}.

==Infrastructure==
[[File:Iglesia de San José 2022.jpg|thumb|right|San José Church]]
There are several small city parks such as ''[https://www.google.com/maps/place/Parque+Infantil/@20.8931375,-76.2755008,15z/data=!4m5!3m4!1s0x8ecded5d6da9e201:0xd4af4f22383da9da!8m2!3d20.8926221!4d-76.2614894 Parque Infantil]'', ''[https://www.google.com/maps/place/San+Jos%C3%A9+Park/@20.8858203,-76.2602337,16z/data=!4m5!3m4!1s0x8ecded67c485b0af:0x31d55e73b38f2a95!8m2!3d20.8892517!4d-76.2604031 Parque San José]'', ''[https://www.google.com/maps/place/Las+Flores+Park/@20.8858203,-76.258045,17z/data=!4m5!3m4!1s0x8ecded6f4b73eaab:0xa5a4b574fd2dfb63!8m2!3d20.885177!4d-76.2583418 Parque San Isidoro (Las Flores)]'', ''[https://www.google.com/maps/place/Parque+Jose+Marti,+Holgu%C3%ADn,+Cuba/@20.8804323,-76.2569399,18z/data=!4m5!3m4!1s0x8ecded6fb359bd17:0x7ec1bdb6da5664f2!8m2!3d20.8811993!4d-76.2566923 Parque Martí]'', among others, most central the ''[https://www.google.com/maps/place/Calixto+Garc%C3%ADa+Park/@20.8859937,-76.259068,18z/data=!4m5!3m4!1s0x8ecded689f16305b:0xe74756b5aa7ce551!8m2!3d20.8869747!4d-76.2593171 Calixto García]'' in the downtown area. Close by one finds the [[Art gallery|galleries]] ''Centro Provincial de Arte'' and ''Bayado'', a [[library]], the club ''Casa de la Trova'', the Martí [[movie theater|cinema]], the ''Theatre Eddy Suñol'', the [https://www.google.com/maps/place/Provincial+Museum+of+History/@20.8874738,-76.2595593,20z/data=!4m5!3m4!1s0x0:0x787b0aa1ff87d77c!8m2!3d20.8878175!4d-76.2595886 Province Museum ''La Periquera''], a science museum, and a history museum.

One of the most famous landmarks of the city is the [https://www.google.com/maps/place/Loma+de+la+Cruz/@20.8986836,-76.272067,17z/data=!4m5!3m4!1s0x0:0xfd00ff32baf1f868!8m2!3d20.8999597!4d-76.2656488 hill] [[Loma de la Cruz (Cuba)|Loma de la Cruz]] (English: Hill of the Cross), which can be ascended by climbing its +450 stairs, and from where the whole city can be admired. There, in the early years of the city, a large cross was erected with the belief that it would protect it from evil coming down from the North.

Holguín has a [[Calixto García Íñiguez Stadium|baseball stadium]], named after [[Calixto García]].

===Transportation===
Holguín is served by [[Frank País Airport]] (HOG/MUHG), located at 20°47´08"N, 076°18´54"W, with flights to [[Havana]] and several other world destinations, mostly in Canada and Europe.

==Education==
The main post-secondary education institution is the [[University of Holguín]] "Oscar Lucero Moya" (Spanish: [https://www.google.com/maps/place/Universidad+de+Holgu%C3%ADn+Oscar+Lucero+Moya,+Holgu%C3%ADn,+Cuba/@20.8945719,-76.2404272,443m/data=!3m1!1e3!4m5!3m4!1s0x8ecded3b7a990287:0x37ea90571cebbee5!8m2!3d20.8957011!4d-76.2388596 Universidad de Holguín] "Oscar Lucero Moya", UHO).

Other post-secondary educational centers are: [https://www.google.com/maps/place/Universidad+de+Ciencias+Medicas+De+Holguin+Mariana+Grajales+Coello/@20.885593,-76.2696208,18z/data=!4m5!3m4!1s0x8ecded617cfaf81f:0x93cd8674a0aec944!8m2!3d20.8862283!4d-76.2676095 Universidad de Ciencias Médicas] (University of Medical Sciences) "Mariana Grajales", [https://www.google.com/maps/place/Universidad+Pedagogica,+Holgu%C3%ADn,+Cuba/@20.8771326,-76.2305464,15z/data=!4m5!3m4!1s0x8ecdece17b7a82e9:0x35c94028495dbca9!8m2!3d20.8844052!4d-76.2211141 Universidad Pedagógica] (Pedagogical University of Holguin, formerly Instituto Superior Pedagógico, ISPH) "José de la Luz y Caballero", and [https://www.google.com/maps/place/Escuela+de+Cultura+F%C3%ADsica+Piti+Manuel+Fajardo,+Holgu%C3%ADn,+Cuba/@20.8899877,-76.2461874,443m/data=!3m1!1e3!4m5!3m4!1s0x8ecded158011f92d:0xb8df6c6a2ef4310a!8m2!3d20.8903385!4d-76.2446532 Universidad Deportiva] (formerly Facultad de Cultura Física) "Manuel Piti Fajardo".
