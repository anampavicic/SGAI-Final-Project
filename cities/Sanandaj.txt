'''Sanandaj''' ({{langx|fa|سنندج}}; {{IPA|fa|sænænˈdædʒ|pron|Sanandaj.ogg}}){{efn|{{langx|ku|سنە}}, [[romanize]]d as '''Senneh''' and '''Sine'''<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.britannica.com/art/Senneh-rug |title=Senneh rug |access-date=12 November 2019 |archive-date=12 November 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191112232027/https://www.britannica.com/art/Senneh-rug |url-status=live }}</ref>}} is a city in the [[Central District (Sanandaj County)|Central District]] of [[Sanandaj County]], in the [[Kurdistan province]] of [[Iran]], serving as capital of the province, the county, and the district.<ref name="Kurdistan Province Structure">{{cite web |title = Approval of the organization and chain of citizenship of the elements and units of the national divisions of Kurdistan province, centered in the city of Sanandaj |language = fa |website = Islamic Parliament Research Center of the Islamic Republic of Iran |url = https://rc.majlis.ir/fa/law/show/113045 |archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20181023230624/https://rc.majlis.ir/fa/law/show/113045 |publisher = Ministry of Interior, Defense Political Commission of the Government Board |last = Habibi |first = Hassan |archive-date = 23 October 2018 |date = 21 June 1369 |access-date = 22 January 2024}}</ref> With a population of 414,069,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sanandaj.gov.ir/Default.aspx?TabID=2|title=آمار جمعیتی شهرستان سنندج :: فرمانداری سنندج|access-date=8 August 2016|archive-date=7 August 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170807030402/http://sanandaj.gov.ir/Default.aspx?TabID=2|url-status=dead}}</ref> Sanandaj is the 23rd largest city in Iran and the second largest [[Kurds|Kurdish]] city.{{cn|date=June 2024}}

== History ==
Sanandaj's founding is fairly recent, (about 250 years ago), yet in its short existence it has grown to become one of the centers of [[Kurdish culture]].<ref name="auto">Geoffrey Khan, The Jewish Neo-Aramaic Dialect of Sanandaj, Piscataway NJ: Gorgias Press, p. 1.</ref><ref>[http://www.britannica.com/EBchecked/topic/521720/Sanandaj Sanandaj] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130615085220/http://www.britannica.com/EBchecked/topic/521720/Sanandaj |date=15 June 2013 }} ''[[Encyclopædia Britannica]]'', retrieved 16 July 2014.</ref>  During the [[Iran–Iraq War]] the city was attacked by Iraqi planes and saw disturbances.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.britannica.com/place/Sanandaj|title=Sanandaj &#124; Iran &#124; Britannica|access-date=30 November 2020|archive-date=27 November 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201127182821/https://www.britannica.com/place/Sanandaj|url-status=live}}</ref> Since 2019, UNESCO has recognized Sanandaj as Creative City of Music.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://en.unesco.org/creative-cities/events/sanandaj-one-year-anniversary-unesco-creative-city-music |title=Sanandaj one-year anniversary as a UNESCO Creative City of Music {{!}} Creative Cities Network |website=en.unesco.org |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201207155108/https://en.unesco.org/creative-cities/events/sanandaj-one-year-anniversary-unesco-creative-city-music |archive-date=2020-12-07}} </ref>

The name "Sinna" first appears in records from the 14th century CE.<ref name="Minorsky Sisar">{{cite book |last1=Minorsky |first1=Vladimir |editor1-last=Bosworth |editor1-first=C.E. |editor2-last=van Donzel |editor2-first=E. |editor3-last=Heinrichs |editor3-first=W.P. |editor4-last=Lecomte |editor4-first=G. |title=The Encyclopaedia of Islam, Vol. IX (SAN-SZE) |date=1997 |publisher=Brill |location=Leiden |isbn=90-04-10422-4 |pages=680–1 |url=https://ia600603.us.archive.org/14/items/EncyclopaediaDictionaryIslamMuslimWorldEtcGibbKramerScholars.13/09.EncycIslam.NewEdPrepNumLeadOrient.EdEdComCon.BosDonHeinLec.etc.UndPatIUA.v9.San-Sze.Leid.EJBrill.1997..pdf |access-date=13 June 2022 |chapter=SĪSAR}}</ref> Before this, the main city in the region was '''[[Sisar]]''', whose exact location is unknown.<ref name="Minorsky Sisar"/> Sisar was also called "Sisar of Sadkhaniya", or "Sisar of the hundred springs", and it has been proposed that the current name of "Sinna" is a contracted form of "Sadkhaniya".<ref name="Minorsky Sisar"/>

The name "Sisar" disappears in the 14th century and the name "Sinna" replaces it, for example in the works of [[Hamdallah Mustawfi]] who refers to a mountain and a pass with this name.<ref name="Minorsky Sisar"/> Then the Kurdish historian [[Sharaf al-Din Bitlisi]] mentions that in 1580 an [[Ardalan]] ruler named Timur Khan had a land grant including Sinna and the earlier Ardalan capital of [[Hasanabad, Sanandaj|Hasanabad]].<ref name="Minorsky Sanandaj"/> However, the local historian Ali-Akbar Munshi Waqayi-Nigar wrote in 1892/3 that Sinna was founded later, by the ruler [[Soleyman Khan Ardalan]], on the site of an earlier settlement; the [[chronogram]] he gives for this event corresponds to 1046 [[Hijri year|AH]], or 1636-7 CE.<ref name="Minorsky Sanandaj"/>

Sinna was developed significantly under the reign of Aman Allah "the Great" (from 1797-1825).<ref name="Minorsky Sanandaj"/> 19th-century Sinna was "a lively commercial center, exporting [[oak gall]]s, [[tragacanth]], furs, and carpets".<ref name="Minorsky Sanandaj"/> Its population was mostly [[Kurds|Kurdish]], with a significant [[Jewish]] minority and smaller numbers of [[Armenians|Armenian]] and [[Chaldean Catholic]] Christians.<ref name="Minorsky Sanandaj"/>

==Demographics==
===Ethnicity===
The population of Sanandaj is mainly [[Kurds|Kurdish]]. The city also had an [[Armenian people|Armenian]] minority who gradually emigrated from the city. Until the [[Iranian Revolution]] (1979), the city had a small Aramaic-speaking Jewish community of about 4,000 people.<ref name="auto"/> The city boasted a sizable [[Assyrian people|Assyrian]] community that spoke a unique dialect of Aramaic called [[Senaya language|Senaya]], they are mostly members of the [[Chaldean Catholic Church]].<ref>{{cite book |last1=Khan |first1=Geoffrey |title=The Jewish Neo-Aramaic Dialect of Sanandaj |date=2009 |publisher=Gorgias Press |isbn=978-1-60724-134-8 |pages=3–4 |language=en}}</ref>

===Religion===
Most of the people of Sanandaj follow the [[Shafi‘i]] branch of [[Sunni Islam]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.iranhumanrights.org/2017/11/intelligence-ministry-invites-rouhani-campaign-manager-to-stop-advocating-for-sunni-muslim-rights/|title=Intelligence Ministry "Invites" Rouhani Campaign Manager to Stop Advocating for Sunni Muslim Rights|date=17 November 2017|access-date=23 January 2018|archive-date=23 January 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180123131638/https://www.iranhumanrights.org/2017/11/intelligence-ministry-invites-rouhani-campaign-manager-to-stop-advocating-for-sunni-muslim-rights/|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>

===Population===
At the time of the 2006 National Census, the city's population was 311,446 in 81,380 households.<ref name="2006 census">{{cite web | title = Census of the Islamic Republic of Iran, 1385 (2006) | page = 12 | language = fa | publisher = The Statistical Center of Iran | website = AMAR | url = http://www.amar.org.ir/DesktopModules/FTPManager/upload/upload2360/newjkh/newjkh/12.xls | access-date = 25 September 2022 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20110920092008/http://www.amar.org.ir/DesktopModules/FTPManager/upload/upload2360/newjkh/newjkh/12.xls | format = Excel | archive-date = 20 September 2011}}</ref> The following census in 2011 counted 373,987 people in 106,771 households.<ref name="2011 census">{{cite web | title = Census of the Islamic Republic of Iran, 1390 (2011) | page = 12 | language = fa | publisher = The Statistical Center of Iran | website = Syracuse University | url = https://irandataportal.syr.edu/wp-content/uploads/Kurdistan.xls | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20230119120701/https://irandataportal.syr.edu/wp-content/uploads/Kurdistan.xls | archive-date = 19 January 2023 | access-date = 19 December 2022 | format = Excel}}</ref> The 2016 census measured the population of the city as 412,767 people in 126,240 households.<ref name="2016 census">{{cite web | title = Census of the Islamic Republic of Iran, 1395 (2016) | page = 12 | language = fa | publisher = The Statistical Center of Iran | website = AMAR | url = https://www.amar.org.ir/Portals/0/census/1395/results/abadi/CN95_HouseholdPopulationVillage_12.xlsx | access-date = 19 December 2022 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20220508185635/https://www.amar.org.ir/Portals/0/census/1395/results/abadi/CN95_HouseholdPopulationVillage_12.xlsx | format = Excel | archive-date = 8 May 2022}}</ref>

==Geography==
===Location===
The city is between the [[Qishlaq river]], a tributary of the [[Diyala river|Diyala]], and Mount Awidar, which separates it from the old [[Ardalan]] capital of [[Hasanabad, Sanandaj|Hasanabad]].<ref name="Minorsky Sanandaj">{{cite book |last1=Minorsky |first1=Vladimir |editor1-last=Bosworth |editor1-first=C.E. |editor2-last=van Donzel |editor2-first=E. |editor3-last=Heinrichs |editor3-first=W.P. |editor4-last=Lecomte |editor4-first=G. |title=The Encyclopaedia of Islam, Vol. IX (SAN-SZE) |date=1997 |publisher=Brill |location=Leiden |isbn=90-04-10422-4 |pages=6–7 |url=https://ia600603.us.archive.org/14/items/EncyclopaediaDictionaryIslamMuslimWorldEtcGibbKramerScholars.13/09.EncycIslam.NewEdPrepNumLeadOrient.EdEdComCon.BosDonHeinLec.etc.UndPatIUA.v9.San-Sze.Leid.EJBrill.1997..pdf |access-date=13 June 2022 |chapter=SANANDADJ}}</ref> Carpet making is the biggest industry in Sanandaj.<ref name="Minorsky Sanandaj"/>

===Climate===
[[File:Sanandaj city center.jpg|thumb|left|Sanandaj city center in 2021]]
Sanandaj has a [[Mediterranean climate]] (''Csa'') according to the [[Köppen climate classification]], bordering a [[humid continental climate|dry-summer continental climate]] (''Dsa''), with cold and wet winters and very hot and dry summers.

==Economy==
The economy of Sanandaj is based upon the production of carpets, processed hides and skins, milled rice, refined sugar, woodworking, cotton weaving, [[metalware]] and cutlery.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.yjc.ir/fa/news/4806092/%DA%A9%D8%B1%D8%AF%D8%B3%D8%AA%D8%A7%D9%86-%D8%A7%D8%B2-%D9%86%D8%B8%D8%B1-%D9%86%D8%B1%D8%AE-%D8%A8%DB%8C%DA%A9%D8%A7%D8%B1%DB%8C-%D8%AF%D8%B1-%D8%AC%D8%A7%DB%8C%DA%AF%D8%A7%D9%87-%D8%AF%D9%88%D9%85-%DA%A9%D8%B4%D9%88%D8%B1-%D9%82%D8%B1%D8%A7%D8%B1-%DA%AF%D8%B1%D9%81%D8%AA|title=کردستان از نظر نرخ بیکاری در جایگاه دوم کشور قرار گرفت|website=yjc.ir|access-date=2016-05-14|archive-date=28 November 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151128015706/http://www.yjc.ir/fa/news/4806092/%DA%A9%D8%B1%D8%AF%D8%B3%D8%AA%D8%A7%D9%86-%D8%A7%D8%B2-%D9%86%D8%B8%D8%B1-%D9%86%D8%B1%D8%AE-%D8%A8%DB%8C%DA%A9%D8%A7%D8%B1%DB%8C-%D8%AF%D8%B1-%D8%AC%D8%A7%DB%8C%DA%AF%D8%A7%D9%87-%D8%AF%D9%88%D9%85-%DA%A9%D8%B4%D9%88%D8%B1-%D9%82%D8%B1%D8%A7%D8%B1-%DA%AF%D8%B1%D9%81%D8%AA|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.sanayenews.com/provinces/east-western/kordestan/3711|title=سرمایه‌گذاری در صنعت و معدن کردستان، 4 برابر کمتر از متوسط کشوری است|website=sanayenews.com|access-date=2016-05-14|archive-date=11 June 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160611164909/http://www.sanayenews.com/provinces/east-western/kordestan/3711|url-status=dead}}</ref>
