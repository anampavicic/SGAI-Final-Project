'''Tambaram''' is a city located within the [[Chennai metropolitan area|Chennai Metropolitan Area]] in the Indian State of [[Tamil Nadu]].<ref name="tcmc act" /> The city is governed by [[Tambaram City Municipal Corporation]].<ref name="tm ref5" />

== Etymology ==
Tambaram is an ancient town referred to as Taamapuram in an inscription of the 13th century. The word was inscribed on the walls around the [[sanctum sanctorum]] at [[Marundeeswarar Temple, Thirukachur|Marundeeswarar temple]] in Tirukachur village, near [[Chengalpattu]].<ref name=":02">{{cite web |title=Dr.Gift Siromoney's Home Page |url=https://www.cmi.ac.in/gift/Archeaology/arch_tambaramhistory.htm |access-date=2022-02-16 |website=Cmi.ac.in}}</ref>

==History==
The earliest mention of Tambaram dates back to the 13th century when the word 'Taamapuram' was inscribed on the walls around the [[sanctum sanctorum]] at [[Marundeeswarar Temple, Thirukachur|Marundeeswarar temple]] in Tirukachur village, near Chengalpattu.<ref name=":02"/>

=== Old Stone Age ===
The oldest locality in the city is Pallavapuram which is considered one of the oldest inhabited places in South Asia.<ref>{{Cite web |title=How Pallavaram changed archeologists’ understanding of burial in South Asia |url=https://www.newindianexpress.com/cities/chennai/2019/may/31/how-pallavaram-changed-archeologists-understanding-of-burial-in-south-asia-1983990.html |access-date=19 February 2022 |newspaper=[[The New Indian Express]]}}</ref> Pallavapuram is most commonly known today as [[Pallavaram]].<ref name="Muthiah_2014">{{cite book |last=Muthiah |first=S. |title=Madras Rediscovered |date=2014 |publisher=EastWest |isbn=978-93-84030-28-5 |location=Chennai |page=145 |author-link=S. Muthiah}}</ref>

On 13 May 1863, [[Robert Bruce Foote]], a British geologist with the [[Geological Survey of India]] (GSI), discovered a hand axe belonging to the [[Lower Paleolithic|Lower Palaeolithic Age]] at Pallavaram (Pallavapuram).<ref>{{Cite book |last1=Muthiah |first1=S. |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=tbR_LLkqdI8C&dq=stone+tool+5+lakh+year+old+discovered+in+pallavaram&pg=PA196 |title=Madras: The land the people & their governance |year=2008 |isbn=9788183794688}}</ref><ref name="muthiahp129">[[Tambaram Municipal Corporation#Muthiah|Muthiah]], Pg 129</ref> Since then, several Stone Age artefacts have been discovered.<ref name="muthiahp129" /> Most of these artefacts are currently lodged in the [[Government Museum, Chennai|Egmore museum]].<ref name="muthiahp129" />

=== Early Medieval Period ===
==== Pallava Dynasty ====
{{Main|Pallava dynasty}}
The oldest locality in the city, Pallavapuram, existed during the reign of [[Pallava dynasty|Pallava]] king [[Mahendravarman I]] (600–630 CE). The Pallavas have left titles in early Pallava script at the cave temple in Pallavaram neighbourhood, which dates back to 600 CE. The remains of a cave shrine constructed by the Pallava ruler have been found at Asthana-E-Moula Ali Dargah.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Venkat |first=Vaishali R. |date=2014-07-17 |title=Silent presence for 400 years |language=en-IN |work=The Hindu |url=https://www.thehindu.com/features/friday-review/silent-presence-for-400-years/article6221583.ece |access-date=2022-02-16 |issn=0971-751X}}</ref>

==== Chola Dynasty ====
{{Main|Chola dynasty}}
During the reign of [[Later Cholas]], from ninth to twelfth century CE, the region was called Churathur Nadu. Churathur Nadu was named after Thiruchuram, the present-day [[Tirusulam|Trisulam]]. The Churathur Nadu extended from Tambaram in the south to [[Adambakkam]] and [[Alandur]] in the north. The region included the areas of Pammal, Pallavaram, and Thiruneermalai.<ref>{{cite web |date=24 September 2018 |title=Now known for the airport, Tirusulam is actually an ancient, historical part of Puliyur Kottam, aka Chennai |url=https://inmathi.com/2018/09/24/now-known-for-the-airport-tirusulam-is-actually-an-ancient-historical-part-of-puliyur-kottam-aka-chennai/13200/}}</ref><ref name="cmi.ac.in">{{cite web |title=History of Chennai Southern Suburbs |url=https://www.cmi.ac.in/gift/Archeaology/arch_tambaramhistory.htm |access-date=2019-12-01 |website=Cmi.ac.in}}</ref>

=== Colonial Period ===
During the [[Carnatic wars]] in the late 17th century, the city was an entrenchment camp for the [[British East India Company]]. During the 17th century, Pallavaram neighborhood of the city remained dependent upon the Portuguese colony of [[San Thome]]. Later, the British established a cantonment at Pallavaram, supplementary to the one at [[St. Thomas Mount]].

A [[Wireless base station|wireless station]] was established in the early years of the 20th century. The Madras Aerodrome was opened at Pallavaram in 1929.

=== Post-Independence ===
In the 1951 census, Tambaram was classified as a [[Nagar panchayat|Town Panchayat]] for the first time.<ref name="census1961">{{Citation |last=Nambiar |first=P.K. |title=Madras District Census Handbook, Chingleput, Part X-VI, Vol-II, VoI-IX - Census 1961 |date=1965 |url=https://censusindia.gov.in/nada/index.php/catalog/31273/download/34454/22102_1961_CHI.pdf |work= |volume= |page=68 |type=Census Report |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230922224511/https://censusindia.gov.in/nada/index.php/catalog/31274/download/34455/22101_1961_CHI.pdf |chapter=General Population Tables—"A" Series |publication-place=Madras |publisher=The Director of Stationery and Printing |archive-date=22 September 2023}}</ref> In 1964, Tambaram Town Panchayat was incorporated as a Grade III Municipality by annexing the Village Panchayats of Pulikoradu, Kadapperi, Tambaram, Irumbliyur, and Selaiyur.<ref name=":0">{{Citation |last=<nowiki>Tamil Nadu Urban Development Fund, Government of Tamil Nadu</nowiki> |title=City Corporate cum Business Plan: Tambaram Municipality |date=October 2007 |url=http://tnuifsl.com/documents/ccpbp/Tambaram.pdf |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140801144944/http://tnuifsl.com/documents/ccpbp/Tambaram.pdf |archive-date=1 August 2014}}</ref><ref>{{Cite act|type=Government Order (Manuscript)|index=1756|date=1 December 1964|legislature=Department of Rural Development and Local Administration, [[Government of Tamil Nadu|State Government of Tamil Nadu]]}}</ref>

Due to rapid development and growth of the town commercially and residentially, the Municipality was classified as a 'Selection Grade Municipality'.<ref name=":0" /> The extent of the municipality was {{cvt|20.72|km2}}. The revenue villages under this municipality are Pulikoradu, Kadapperi, Tambaram, Irumbliyur, and Selaiyur. The number of households is 26,333, the number of notified slums is 17 and the number of unnotified slums is 7.<ref name="autogenerated12">{{cite web |date=1978-11-15 |title=Welcome to Tambaram Municipality Home Page |url=http://municipality.tn.gov.in/tambaram/gen_info.htm |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120309173119/http://municipality.tn.gov.in/tambaram/gen_info.htm |archive-date=2012-03-09 |access-date=2012-02-24 |publisher=Municipality.tn.gov.in}}</ref> The Tambaram range comprises forest lands in Nanmangalam, Madurapakkam, Tambaram, Pulikoradu, Kumili, Vandalur, Onnamancherry, Erumaiyur, Vattampakkam and Vadakupattu.<ref>{{cite web |title=After 14 years, encroachments in Nanmangalam reserve forest removed |url=http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2009-07-30/chennai/28183139_1_forest-officials-reserve-forest-encroachments |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120716003841/http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2009-07-30/chennai/28183139_1_forest-officials-reserve-forest-encroachments |archive-date=16 July 2012 |access-date=18 September 2011 |work=[[The Times of India]]}}</ref>

In 2009, Tambaram taluk was trifurcated into Tambaram, Sholinganallur and Alandur taluks. Clubbing all the three taluks, a new revenue division with Tambaram as headquarters was formed.<ref>{{cite news |title=Tambaram trifurcated into 3 taluks |newspaper=[[The Times of India]] |url=http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2009-07-01/chennai/28196600_1_taluks-tambaram-cuddalore |url-status=dead |access-date=18 September 2011 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120711171639/http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2009-07-01/chennai/28196600_1_taluks-tambaram-cuddalore |archive-date=11 July 2012}}</ref>

==== Incorporation as a city ====
The increasing population and rapid urbanisation increased the need for town planning, improved administration structure and human resource planning. These demands drove the creation of Tambaram City Municipal Corporation.<ref name="tm ref5">{{cite news |date=5 November 2021 |title=Tambaram, Chennai's southern gateway, now a municipal corporation |language=en |publisher=The News Minute |url=https://www.thenewsminute.com/article/tambaram-chennais-southern-gateway-now-municipal-corporation-157263 |access-date=3 December 2021}}</ref><ref name="tm ref4">{{cite news |date=5 November 2021 |title=Tambaram becomes the 20th municipal corporation of Tamil Nadu, Ordinance promulgated |publisher=The New Indian Express |url=https://www.newindianexpress.com/states/tamil-nadu/2021/nov/05/tambaram-becomes-the-20th-municipal-corporation-of-tamil-naduordinance-promulgated-2379839.html |access-date=3 December 2021}}</ref> The announcement to establish the Tambaram City Municipal Corporation was made by Minister for Municipal Administration [[K. N. Nehru]] in the state Assembly, on 24 August 2021<ref name=":3">{{cite web |date= |title=Ordinance issued for formation of Tambaram Corporation |url=https://www.dtnext.in/News/TamilNadu/2021/11/06062632/1327474/Ordinance-issued-for-formation-of-Tambaram-Corporation.vpf |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211202104857/https://www.dtnext.in/News/TamilNadu/2021/11/06062632/1327474/Ordinance-issued-for-formation-of-Tambaram-Corporation.vpf |url-status=dead |archive-date=2 December 2021 |access-date=2022-02-16 |website=DTNext.in}}</ref> by merging five municipalities, five town panchayats and fifteen village panchayats.<ref name="tm ref7">{{cite news |last1=Lopez |first1=Aloysius Xavier |date=30 August 2021 |title=Tambaram Corporation takes shape amidst great expectations |language=en-IN |work=The Hindu |url=https://www.thehindu.com/news/cities/chennai/tambaram-corporation-takes-shape-amidst-great-expectations/article36172796.ece |access-date=3 December 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |title=Left out of upgrade plans, village panchayats irked |newspaper=[[The New Indian Express]] |url=https://www.newindianexpress.com/cities/chennai/2021/sep/15/left-out-of-upgrade-plans-village-panchayats-irked-2358882.html |access-date=19 February 2022}}</ref>

Following a supreme court ruling to conduct rural local body elections by October 2021, the village panchayats elections for [[Pozhichalur]], [[Cowl Bazaar]], [[Tirusulam]], [[Moovarasampettai|Moovarasampattu]], [[Kovilambakkam]], [[:ta:நன்மங்கலம்_ஊராட்சி|Nanmangalam]], [[Medavakkam]], [[:ta:வேங்கைவாசல்_ஊராட்சி|Vengaivasal]], [[Perumbakkam]], [[Sithalapakkam]], [[:ta:ஒட்டியம்பாக்கம்_ஊராட்சி|Ottiyambakkam]], [[:ta:மதுரப்பாக்கம்_ஊராட்சி,_செங்கல்பட்டு|Madurambakkam]], [[Agaramthen]], [[:ta:திருவெஞ்சேரி_ஊராட்சி|Thiruvancheri]], [[Mudichur]] of the St.Thomas Mount panchayat union were held. As a result, the 15 village panchayats were excluded from the Government order issued by the State [[Government of Tamil Nadu]] on 11 September 2021.<ref>{{cite news |title=Rural local body polls in 9 districts on Oct. 6, 9 |newspaper=[[The Hindu]] |url=https://www.thehindu.com/news/national/tamil-nadu/rural-local-body-polls-in-9-districts-on-oct-6-9/article36444134.ece |access-date=2022-02-19}}</ref>


The five municipalities—[[Anakaputhur]], [[Pallavaram]], [[Pammal]], [[Sembakkam]] and Tambaram, and five town panchayats—[[Chitlapakkam]], [[Madambakkam]], [[Perungalathur|Perungaluthur]], [[Peerkankaranai|Peerkangaranai]] and [[Thiruneermalai|Tiruneermalai]], were incorporated as Tambaram City, with Tambaram City Municipal Corporation as governing body.<ref name="thehindu.com6">{{Cite news |date=5 November 2021 |title=Gazette notification on Tambaram Municipal Corporation |newspaper=[[The Hindu]] |url=https://www.thehindu.com/news/cities/chennai/gazette-notification-on-tambaram-municipal-corporation/article37339026.ece}}</ref> After the tenure of existing village panchayats ends in 2024, the proposed village panchayats may get annexed into corporation limits, and may accordingly be converted into urban wards.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Village panchayats’ merger only in 2026 |newspaper=[[The New Indian Express]] |url=https://www.newindianexpress.com/states/tamil-nadu/2021/nov/04/village-panchayats-merger-only-in-2026-2379475.html |access-date=19 February 2022}}</ref>

Agaramthen, Medavakkam, Kovilambakkam, Cowl Bazaar, Mudichur, Urapakkam, Vandalur, Vengadamangalam, Perumbakkam, Nanmangalam, Pozhichalur, Tirusulam, Ottiyambakkam, Thiruvanchery, Vengaivasal, Madurapakkam, Moovarasampattu and Sithalapakkam village panchayats are expected to be annexed to Tambaram Corporation from January 2025.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Chennai: Tambaram and Avadi corporations to expand from January 2025|newspaper=[[The Times of India]] |url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/chennai/chennai-tambaram-and-avadi-corporations-to-expand-from-january-2025/articleshow/113785157.cms |access-date=29 September 2024}}</ref>

== Geography ==

=== Climate ===
Tambaram features a [[Tropical savanna climate|tropical savanna climate with a dry winter]]. Tambaram lies on the [[thermal equator]] and is also coastal, which prevents extreme variation in seasonal temperature. For most of the year, the weather is hot and [[Humidity|humid]]. The hottest part of the year is late May and early June, known locally as ''Agni Nakshatram'' ("fiery star") or as ''Kathiri Veyyil'', with maximum temperatures around {{convert|38|–|42|C|lk=on|F}}. The coolest part of the year is January, with minimum temperatures around {{convert|18|–|20|C|F}}. The lowest temperature recorded is {{convert|15.8|°C|°F|abbr=on}} and highest {{convert|45|°C|°F|abbr=on}}(30 May 2003) both being recorded at the observatory at IMD Numgambakkam.<ref name="Singapore-temp">{{cite web |title=Climate of India |url=http://app.nea.gov.sg/cms/htdocs/article.asp?pid=1111 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20061006131405/http://app.nea.gov.sg/cms/htdocs/article.asp?pid=1111 |archive-date=6 October 2006 |access-date=4 August 2005 |work=National Environment Agency – Singapore}}</ref><ref name="hightemp">{{cite news |date=31 May 2003 |title=Highest temperature |work=The Hindu |url=http://www.hinduonnet.com/2003/05/31/stories/2003053104790101.htm |url-status=usurped |access-date=2007-04-25 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110711171303/http://www.hinduonnet.com/2003/05/31/stories/2003053104790101.htm |archive-date=11 July 2011}}</ref>

The average annual [[rainfall]] is about {{convert|1400|mm|in|abbr=on}}. The city gets most of its [[season]]al rainfall from the north-east [[monsoon]] winds, from mid-September to mid-December. [[Cyclone]]s in the Bay of Bengal sometimes hit Tambaram. The highest annual rainfall recorded is {{cvt|2,570|mm}} in 2005 for IMD Nungambakkam.<ref>{{cite news |author=T. === Water bodies ===
Some of the notable lakes in and around the suburb are [[Chitlapakkam Lake]], [[Pallavaram Lake]] (locally known as Periya eri), Thiruneermalai Lake, Thirupananthal Lake, Peerkankaranai Lake, and Perungalathur Lake.

== Governance ==
{{Main articles|Tambaram City Municipal Corporation}}

Tambaram is governed by the [[Tambaram City Municipal Corporation]], which was established on November 3, 2021.<ref name="thehindu.com6" /> The executive authority is vested in [[Municipal Commissioner (India)|Corporation Commissioner]].<ref name="thehindu.com6" /> 

The council of Tambaram City Municipal Corporation consists of elected [[councillors]] from each ward. The council is presided over by the mayor who is indirectly elected by the councillors.<ref name="thehindu.com6" /> The mayor is the head of the city government, but the role is largely ceremonial as executive powers are vested in the corporation commissioner. The office of the mayor combines a functional role of chairing the city government meeting as well as a ceremonial role associated with being the First Citizen of the city.<ref name="thehindu.com6" /> Deputy mayor is appointed by the mayor for a five-year term.<ref name="thehindu.com6" />

At the present, the corporation is governed by [[Secular Progressive Alliance]] which collectively holds 54 out of 70 seats in the council. The opposition is led by [[All India Anna Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam]] which holds 9 seats in the council. The current mayor is Vasanthakumari Kamalakannan, who assumed the position on 4 March 2022. The current deputy mayor is G Kamaraj, who assumed the position on 4 March 2022.

=== Administration divisions ===
{{See also|Tambaram City Municipal Corporation#Administrative Divisions|label 1=Administrative Divisions}}

When Tambaram City Municipal Corporation was established on 3 November 2021, it consisted of 70 wards under 5 zones.<ref name="thehindu.com6" />

=== Law and order ===
{{See also|Tambaram City Police|label 1=}}

In September 2021, the [[Government of Tamil Nadu|State Government of Tamil Nadu]] revealed its plans of reforming the [[Greater Chennai Police|Greater Chennai City Police]] and setting up two new commissionerates in Tambaram and Avadi. The new Police Commissionerates in Tambaram was formally inaugurated by the chief minister [[M. K. Stalin]] on 1 January 2022.

The Tambaram police commissionerate functions with two police districts—Tambaram and Pallikaranai, comprising 20 police stations. For ease of administration, Somangalam and Manimangalam police stations from Kancheepuram district along with Otteri, Guduvanchery, Maraimalai Nagar, Thalambur and Kelambakkam police stations from Chengalpattu district have been attached to the Tambaram Police Commissionerate.<ref>{{Cite news |date=January 2022 |title=New Police Commissionerates in Tambaram, Avadi opened |newspaper=The Hindu |url=https://www.thehindu.com/news/cities/chennai/new-police-commissionerates-in-tambaram-avadi-opened/article38085909.ece}}</ref>

=== Urban planning ===
{{See also|Chennai Metropolitan Development Authority}}

The [[Chennai Metropolitan Development Authority]] (CMDA) is the nodal agency responsible for planning and development of Tambaram. It is responsible for all the three [[Municipal corporation (India)|Municipal Corporation]] in the [[Chennai Metropolitan Area]]—[[Tambaram City Municipal Corporation]], [[Avadi Municipal Corporation]], [[Greater Chennai Corporation]] and [[Kancheepuram City Municipal Corporation]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite news |last=Srivathsan |first=A. |date=2013-09-19 |title=Indecisiveness keeps urban projects on hold |language=en-IN |work=The Hindu |url=https://www.thehindu.com/news/cities/chennai/indecisiveness-keeps-urban-projects-on-hold/article5142863.ece |access-date=2022-03-05 |issn=0971-751X}}</ref>

=== Madras Export Processing Zone ===
{{Main|Madras Export Processing Zone}}

Madras Export Processing Zone (MEPZ) is a [[special economic zone]] located on [[Grand Southern Trunk Road|GST Road]], {{convert|6|km|spell=in}} south of [[Chennai International Airport]]. It is one of the seven export processing zones in the country set up by the central government.<ref>{{cite news |last=Narasimhan |first=T. E. |date=23 June 2010 |title=Six new SEZs to come up in TN |newspaper=Business Standard |location=Chennai |url=http://www.business-standard.com/article/economy-policy/six-new-sezs-to-come-up-in-tn-110062300014_1.html |access-date=30 Mar 2013}}</ref> It was established in 1984 to promote [[foreign direct investment]], enhance [[International trade|foreign exchange]] earnings, and create greater employment opportunities in the region.<ref name="BS_MEPZNotToAllowMoreITFirms2">{{cite news |date=29 May 2010 |title=MEPZ not to allow more IT firms |newspaper=Business Standard |location=Chennai |url=http://www.business-standard.com/article/economy-policy/mepz-not-to-allow-more-it-firms-110052900023_1.html |access-date=30 Mar 2013}}</ref>

The zone is under the administrative control of the Ministry of Commerce and Industries and caters to the needs of units within the SEZ, in addition to monitoring the functions of 100-percent export-oriented units (EOUs) located in [[Tamil Nadu]], [[Pondicherry]], and [[Andaman & Nicobar Islands]].<ref name="BS_MEPZPosts25PCGrowth2">{{cite news |last=Menon |first=Ravi |date=8 October 2007 |title=MEPZ posts 25% growth in exports |newspaper=Business Standard |location=Chennai |url=http://www.business-standard.com/article/economy-policy/mepz-posts-25-growth-in-exports-107100801048_1.html |access-date=30 Mar 2013}}</ref> The zone is headed by a development commissioner.

Spread over an area of 265 acres (109 hectares),<ref name="BS_MEPZPosts25PCGrowth2" /> MEPZ SEZ is a multi-product zone housing 117 functional units.<ref name="MEPZ_AboutUs2">{{cite web |title=About us |url=http://www.mepz.gov.in/aboutus.asp |access-date=30 Mar 2013 |publisher=MEPZ.gov.in}}</ref> In addition, another 27 units are under various stages of implementation.<ref name="MEPZ_AboutUs2" /> The zone employs over 26,000 people.<ref name="BS_MEPZPosts25PCGrowth2" /> In the manufacturing front, there are 110 SME units in the zone. MEPZ's manufacturing sector employs nearly 20,000 people.<ref name="BS_MEPZPosts25PCGrowth2" /> IT companies housed in the zone include [[Cognizant Technology Solutions]], Computer Sciences Corporation, CSS and HTC Global Services among others.<ref name="BS_MEPZNotToAllowMoreITFirms2" /> About 50 container trucks arrive at and leave the zone every day.<ref name="Hindu_NewGateAtMEPZ2">{{cite news |date=24 March 2013 |title=New gate at Tambaram SEZ to end traffic hassles on GST Road |newspaper=The Hindu |location=Chennai |url=http://www.thehindu.com/news/cities/chennai/new-gate-at-tambaram-sez-to-end-traffic-hassles-on-gst-road/article4542360.ece |access-date=30 Mar 2013}}</ref>

=== Architecture ===

==== Dravidian ====

===== Medieval Cholas =====

====== Dhenupureeswarar Temple ======
{{Main|Dhenupureeswarar Temple (Madambakkam)}}
[[Dhenupureeswarar Temple (Madambakkam)|Dhenupureeswarar Temple]] is also known as Dhenupurisvara, and Thiripureeswarar temple. These names are colloquial name of [[Hindus|Hindu]] deity [[Shiva]]. The temple is located in the area of [[Madambakkam]] and it was built during the reign of [[Parantaka Chola II]] (r. 962–980 CE).<ref name=":4">{{Cite web |date=2003-12-06 |title=The Hindu : Ancient Chola temple at Madambakkam |url=http://www.hindu.com/fr/2003/09/26/stories/2003092601940800.htm |access-date=2022-03-06 |website= |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20031206191220/http://www.hindu.com/fr/2003/09/26/stories/2003092601940800.htm |archive-date=6 December 2003 |url-status=dead}}</ref>

The [[garbhagriha]], which is Sanskrit equivalent of [[sanctum sanctorum]],<ref>{{Cite book |last=Hardy |first=Adam |title=Indian Temple Architecture: Form and Transformation: the Karṇāṭa Drāviḍa Tradition |publisher=Abhinav Publications |year=1995 |isbn=9788170173120}}</ref> houses the primary deity Dhenupureeswara in linga form.<ref name=":4" /> The main [[garbhagriha]] is apsidal in shape and this is a characteristic feature of Shiva Temples built during [[Chola dynasty|Chola Period]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Several gods adorn the walls here |url=https://www.newindianexpress.com/cities/chennai/2018/jan/16/several-gods-adorn-the-walls-here-1755433.html |access-date=2022-03-06 |website=The New Indian Express}}</ref> This type of [[garbhagriha]] are known as gajaprishta [[Vimana (architectural feature)|vimana]], where "gajaprishta" literally means "back of an elephant" in [[Sanskrit|Samskrit]].<ref>{{Cite book |last=Grover |first=Satish |title=The Architecture of India (Buddhist and Hindu period) |publisher=Vikas Publishing House |year=1981 |location=New Delhi}}</ref> This type of [[Vimana (architectural feature)|vimana]] here is a separate south-facing [[garbhagriha]] for the Dhenupureeswarar's Consort Goddess Dhenukambal adjacent to the main garbhagriha.

The temple is one of the 163 [[Megalith|megalithic sites]] in the state of Tamil Nadu.<ref name="Hindu_NISModifiesExpansionPlan">{{cite news |last=Madhavan |first=D. |date=20 December 2012 |title=National Institute of Siddha modifies expansion plan |newspaper=The Hindu |publisher=The Hindu |location=Chennai |url=http://www.thehindu.com/news/cities/chennai/national-institute-of-siddha-modifies-expansion-plan/article4218676.ece |access-date=23 Dec 2012}}</ref> The temple has been declared a monument of national importance under the [[AMASR Act|Ancient Monuments and Archaeological Sites and Remains]] (amendment and validation) 2010 Act.<ref name="legend">{{cite news |author=J. V. Siva Prasanna Kumar |date=3 August 2012 |title=1,000-year-old Chola legacy draws devotees |work=The Asian Age |url=http://archive.asianage.com/chennai/1000-year-old-chola-legacy-draws-devotees-412 |access-date=2014-07-24}}</ref> The temple has been conserved and restored by [[Archaeological Survey of India]].<ref>{{cite news |author=T. Ramakrishnan |date=2 October 2001 |title=Restoring past glory |work=[[The Hindu]] |url=http://www.hindu.com/thehindu/2001/10/02/stories/1302126h.htm |url-status=dead |access-date=2014-07-23 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20020122111743/http://hindu.com/thehindu/2001/10/02/stories/1302126h.htm |archive-date=22 January 2002}}</ref>

== Infrastructure ==

=== Transportation ===

==== Rail ====
The [[Tambaram railway station|Tambaram Railway Station]] serves as one of the four primary terminals of the [[Indian Railways]] in the [[Chennai metropolitan area|Chennai Metropolitan Area]]. The other three terminals are [[Chennai Central]], [[Chennai Egmore]], [[Chennai Beach]]. The inter-state railway service is provided by [[Southern Railway zone|Southern Railway]] of Indian Railways. The inter-city railway service is provided by [[Chennai Suburban Railway]] operated by Indian Railways.

===== Inter-state =====
{{Main|Tambaram railway station|Southern Railway zone}}
Similar to [[Chennai Egmore|Chennai Egmore Station]], [[Tambaram railway station|Tambaram Railway Station]] provides access to destinations primarily within Tamil Nadu; however, it also handles a few interstate trains.

===== Inter-city =====
====== Chennai Suburban Railway ======
{{Main|Chennai Suburban Railway|South Line, Chennai Suburban}}
The [[Chennai Suburban Railway]] is a [[commuter rail]] system in the [[Chennai metropolitan area|Chennai Metropolitan Area]], operated by the [[Indian Railways]]. Every day, 160 train services are operated between Chennai Beach and Tambaram, 70 between Tambaram and Chengalpet and 16 between Tambaram and Kancheepuram.<ref name="FundShortageForGuageConversion">{{cite news |last=Madhavan |first=D |date=24 March 2012 |title=Shortage of funds derails gauge conversion works at Tambaram |newspaper=[[The Times of India]] |location=Chennai |url=http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2012-03-24/chennai/31233546_1_gauge-conversion-broad-gauge-tambaram-station |url-status=dead |access-date=31 Mar 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130215015618/http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2012-03-24/chennai/31233546_1_gauge-conversion-broad-gauge-tambaram-station |archive-date=15 February 2013}}</ref>

The City is served by the [[South Line, Chennai Suburban|South Line]] of [[Chennai Suburban Railway]] which runs between [[Chennai Beach railway station|Chennai Beach]] to [[Chengalpattu Junction railway station|Chengalpattu]]. The South Line connects Tambaram with seven major interchange stations out of the eight in Chennai Suburban Railway Network.

The longest circular train in India runs through Tambaram, connecting the City with [[Chennai Beach]], [[Chennai Egmore|Egmore]], [[Chengalpattu]], [[Kanchipuram]], [[Takkolam]], [[Arakkonam Junction]], [[Tiruvallur]], [[Avadi]], [[Ambattur]], [[Vyasarpadi Jeeva (Chennai Suburban Railway)|Vyasarpadi Jeeva]], [[Washermanpet (Chennai Suburban Railway)|Washermanpet]] and [[Royapuram Railway Station|Royapuram]].
