'''Nilópolis''' ({{IPA|pt|niˈlɔpoliʃ}}, {{IPA|pt|niˈlɔpɔliʃ|}}, {{IPA|pt|niˈlɔpuliʃ|}}) is a [[List of municipalities in Rio de Janeiro|municipality]] in [[Brazil]], located in the [[Rio de Janeiro (state)|Rio de Janeiro state]]'s southwestern region, bordering [[São João de Meriti]], [[Mesquita, Rio de Janeiro|Mesquita]] and [[Rio de Janeiro]]. It is the smallest municipality in Rio de Janeiro state, with a total area of 19.39&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>. The population of 162,693 inhabitants living in an area of 9&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> and the remaining area corresponds to the Gericinó's Natural Park. Officially, the population density exceeds 8,120 inhabitants/km<sup>2</sup>, but if calculated just 9&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> occupied, it becomes the most densely populated city in Brazil with more than 17.400 inhabitants/km<sup>2</sup>. Currently, [[São João de Meriti]] has the highest population density, with 12,897 inhabitants/km<sup>2</sup>.

The city was named after [[Nilo Peçanha]], a [[President of Brazil]].

==Beija Flor==

Nilópolis is famous for its [[GRES Beija-Flor de Nilópolis|''Grêmio Recreativo Escola de Samba Beija-Flor de Nilópolis'']], one of the most successful and luxurious [[Samba school]]s and usually a top contender for winning the carnival parade in Rio.{{Citation needed|date=August 2009}}
