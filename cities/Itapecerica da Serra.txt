'''Itapecerica da Serra''' ({{IPA|pt|itapeˈsɛɾikɐ dɐ ˈsɛʁɐ}}) is a municipality in the [[states of Brazil|Brazilian state]] of [[São Paulo (state)|São Paulo]]. It is part of the [[Metropolitan Region of São Paulo]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2014-03-01 |title=Lei Complementar 1139/11 {{!}} Lei Complementar nº 1.139, de 16 de junho de 2011, Governo do Estado de São Paulo |url=http://governo-sp.jusbrasil.com.br/legislacao/1028148/lei-complementar-1139-11 |access-date=2023-05-19 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140301003739/http://governo-sp.jusbrasil.com.br/legislacao/1028148/lei-complementar-1139-11 |archive-date=2014-03-01 }}</ref> The population is 177,662 (2020 est.) in an area of {{cvt|150.74|km2|sqmi}}.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2015-10-08 |title=IBGE {{!}} Cidades {{!}} São Paulo {{!}} Itapecerica da Serra |url=http://cidades.ibge.gov.br/xtras/perfil.php?codmun=352220 |access-date=2023-05-19 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151008115128/http://cidades.ibge.gov.br/xtras/perfil.php?codmun=352220 |archive-date=2015-10-08 }}</ref> It is located 23 miles southwest of São Paulo and at an altitude of {{cvt|920|m|ft}} above sea level. The name ''Itapecerica'' is believed to come from the [[Tupi language]] for ''slippery stone'', and ''da Serra'' means ''of the Mountains'' in [[Portuguese language|Portuguese]].

==History==
Itapecerica da Serra was founded in 1562  by the [[Company of Jesus|Jesuit]] missionaries.  It was one of several settlements  [[Christianity|Christianized]] [[Brazilian indians|indians]], established around the [[Pátio do Colégio|Colégio de São Paulo de Piratininga]] (later to become the city of [[São Paulo (city)|São Paulo]]) as a first line of defense against raids by hostile Indians.  Besides the local inhabitants, the village incorporated natives from the village of [[Carapicuíba]], brought by ''[[bandeirante]]'' [[Afonso Sardinha]] and priest [[Belchior Pontes]].

== Media ==
In telecommunications, the city was served by [[:pt:Companhia_Telefônica_Brasileira|Companhia Telefônica Brasileira]] until 1973, when it began to be served by [[:pt:Telecomunicações_de_São_Paulo|Telecomunicações de São Paulo]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.imprensaoficial.com.br/DO/BuscaDO2001Documento_11_4.aspx?link=/1973/ineditoriais/abril/24/p1/pag_0054_DUI6O3SN7V5CNe7E7ANSRTTG04F.pdf&pagina=54&data=24/04/1973&caderno=Ineditoriais&paginaordenacao=100054|title=Creation of Telesp - April 12, 1973|access-date=2024-05-27|website=www.imprensaoficial.com.br|language=Portuguese}}</ref> In July 1998, this company was acquired by Telefónica, which adopted the [[Vivo (telecommunications company)|Vivo]] brand in 2012.<ref name=":vivo" />

The company is currently an operator of cell phones, fixed lines, internet (fiber optics/4G) and television (satellite and cable).<ref name=":vivo">{{Cite web|url=https://ri.telefonica.com.br/en/the-company/our-history/|title=Our History - Telefônica RI|access-date=2024-05-27|website=Telefônica}}</ref>
