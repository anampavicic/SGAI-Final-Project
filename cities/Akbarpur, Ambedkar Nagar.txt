'''Akbarpur''' is a city, municipal corporation, tehsil,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Tehsil {{!}} Ambedkarnagar {{!}} India |url=https://ambedkarnagar.nic.in/tehsil/|access-date=2 March 2021|language=en-US}}</ref> and the administrative headquarters of [[Ambedkar Nagar district]] in the state of [[Uttar Pradesh]], [[India]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Ambedkarnagar {{!}} Birthplace Of Ram Manohar Lohiya {{!}} India |url=https://ambedkarnagar.nic.in/ |website=ambedkarnagar.nic.in |access-date=21 February 2021}}</ref> It is a part of [[Ayodhya division]].

== Mythology ==
According to the ''[[Ramayana]]'', Akbarpur is where King Dashratha shot Dhanush Shravan Kumar, at a place called Shravan Kshetra. The ashram of the sage [[Rishyasringa|Shringi Rishi]] was said to be situated here. According to the ''Ramayana'', [[Rama]]'s son Kusha ruled Shravasti. This area was part of the extended Ayodhya city.

== Geography ==
Akbarpur, is situated on the banks of the [[Tamsa River]] (also known as the Tons River). The Tamasa River divides the city of Ambedkarnagar into two parts, Akbarpur and Shahzadpur, with the latter being the commercial centre of the city. Lorepur, part of Akbarpur City, is noted as the location of the old palace and [[Hussainiya|imambargah]] of Lorepur.

== Demographics ==
As of [[2011 Indian Census]], Akbarpur had a total population of 111,447, of which 57,330 were males and 54,117 were females. Population within the age group of 0 to 6 years was 14,726. The total number of literates in Akbarpur was 72,049, which constituted 64.6% of the population with male literacy of 70.9% and female literacy of 58.1%. The effective literacy rate of 7+ population of Akbarpur was 74.5%, of which male literacy rate was 81.7% and female literacy rate was 66.9%. The [[Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes]] population was 15,310 and 50 respectively. Akbarpur had 17720 households in 2011.<ref name="Census2011Gov">{{cite web |title=Census of India: Akbarpur |url=http://www.censusindia.gov.in/pca/SearchDetails.aspx?Id=193937 |website=www.censusindia.gov.in |access-date=3 November 2019}}</ref>

== Economy ==
Major economic activities in the district are power looms and farming. Agricultural industries include rice milling and power and distribution transformer manufacturing. The district is noted for Tanda Terricot clothes based in nearby [[Tanda, Ambedkar Nagar|Tanda]]. The district has a sugar factory, the Akbarpur Sugar Mill, which is situated near [[Mijhaura]], about {{convert|10|km}} from the city. Many rice mills are present in Akbarpur. There is a cement manufacturing plant, the Jaypee Ayoudha Grinding Unit, which belongs to the [[Jaypee Group]]. The district has a [[thermal power station]] belonging to [[NTPC Limited]].<ref name=AmbedkarNagarEconomy>{{cite web |title=Economy |url=https://ambedkarnagar.nic.in/economy/ |website=Ambedkar Nagar |access-date=19 January 2019}}</ref>

==Transport==

Akbarpur is well connected with the nearby cities of [[Faizabad]], [[Ayodhya]], [[Lucknow]], [[Sultanpur, Uttar Pradesh|Sultanpur]], [[Raebareli]], [[Amethi]], [[Azamgarh]], [[Jaunpur, Uttar Pradesh|Jaunpur]], [[Varanasi]], [[Prayagraj]], [[Pratapgarh, Uttar Pradesh|Pratapgarh]]; and with the nearby towns of [[Tanda, Ambedkar Nagar|Tanda]], [[Baskhari]], [[Dostpur]], [[Hanswar]], [[Haiderganj]], [[Jalalpur]], [[Jahangirganj]], [[Goshainganj]], [[Tarun]], and [[Bikapur]].

The [[Purvanchal Expressway]] connects Akbarpur to the capital, [[New Delhi]], via the [[Agra-Lucknow Expressway|Agra-Lucknow]] and [[Yamuna Expressway|Yamuna]] expressways.<ref name="Ambedkar Nagar How to Reach">{{cite web |title=How to Reach |url=https://ambedkarnagar.nic.in/how-to-reach/ |website=Ambedkar Nagar |access-date=21 February 2021}}</ref>

== Education ==
The recent establishment of various local colleges has improved the availability of [[higher education]]. The [[Ramabai Government Women Post Graduate College]],<ref>{{cite web |title=Ramabai Government Women Post Graduate College |url=http://static.panoramio.com/photos/original/51149108.jpg |date=2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120902231102/http://static.panoramio.com/photos/original/51149108.jpg |archive-date=2 September 2012 |website=Panoramio.com |access-date=5 November 2018}}</ref> Akbarpur, was established in 1997 to provide opportunities in various disciplines to rural, as well as urban, students. BNKBPG College is a privately managed and government-aided co-educational degree college that offers several programs.<ref>{{cite web |title=Welcome to BNKBPG College |url=http://www.bnkbpg.org/bnkb/home.php |website=B.N.K.B.P.G. College, Akbarpur |access-date=5 November 2018}}</ref> There is one government engineering college, Mahamaya Engineering College, a medical college, and a recently opened agriculture college.
 
Many government secondary schools (junior high schools and ''Rajkiya'' intermediate colleges) have been opened in town to provide educational opportunities. St. Peter's Inter College is an [[Indian Certificate of Secondary Education|ICSE]]-accredited English medium inter college founded in 1984, and it celebrated 25 years of existence in 2009.<ref>{{cite web |title=About the School |url=http://www.spsakbarpur.org/ |website=St. Peter's Inter College, Akbarpur, Ambedkar Nagar (U.P.) |access-date=5 November 2018}}</ref> Radiant college is a [[Central Board of Secondary Education|CBSE]]-accredited college in [[Jalalpur]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Director's Message |url=http://radiantacademy.in/ |website=Radiant Central Children Academy, Jalalpur, Ambedkar Nagar, U.P.  |access-date=5 November 2018}}</ref> Narendra Dev Intermediate College is accredited to the U.P. board.

== Religious sites ==
'''1.'''[[Ashrafpur Kichhauchha]], a village in Ambedkar Nagar district about {{convert|25|km}} from Akbarpur city, is the location of the shrine of the illustrious 13th Century Chisti Sufi saint [[Ashraf Jahangir Semnani]],<ref>{{cite book |last=Ashraf |first=Waheed |title=Hayate Makhdoom Syed Ashraf Jahangir Semnani |year=2017 |orig-year=1975 |edition=Second |location=Shamshad Market, Aligarh, [[India]] |publisher=Maktaba Jamia, Ltd |language=ur |isbn=978-93-85295-54-6 |ol=26384309M }}</ref><ref>''Life and Times of Makhdoom Ashraf Jahangir Semnani (RAH)'' by Dr. Syed Nurusyyidyn Madani Ashraf {{Cite book|url=http://www.amazon.com/Life-Times-Makhdoom-Ashraf-Jahangir-ebook/dp/|title=Life and Times of Makhdoom Ashraf Jahangir Semnani (RAH)|last1=Ashraf|first1=Syed  |last2=Madani|first2=Nurusyyidyn|date=3 September 2019|publisher=Amazon|asin= B07XBYV1V8|language=en}}</ref>  which is visited by millions of devotees every year, irrespective of religion, ethnicity, and gender.

'''2.'''A popular pilgrimage site is the [[Hindu temple]] of Shiv Baba. It is situated {{convert|5|km}} from the city, on Faizabad Road, and {{convert|50|km}} from the holy city of [[Ayodhya]]. A fair is held here every Monday and Friday.
