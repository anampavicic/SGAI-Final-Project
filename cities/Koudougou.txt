'''Koudougou''' ({{langx|mos|Kʋdgo}}) is a city in [[Burkina Faso]]'s [[Boulkiemdé]] Province. It is located {{convert|75|km}} west of [[Ouagadougou]], the capital of [[Burkina Faso]]. With a population of 160,239 (2019),<ref name=pop/> it is the [[List of cities in Burkina Faso|third most populous city]] in Burkina Faso after [[Ouagadougou]] and [[Bobo Dioulasso]], and is mainly inhabited by the [[Gurunsi peoples|Gurunsi]] and [[Mossi people|Mossi]] ethnic groups.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.insd.bf/Documents/Publications/INSD/Publications/Resultats_enquetes/Autres%20enq/Rapport_preliminaire_RGPH06.pdf |title=Resultats préliminaires du recensement de 2006 |access-date=2008-07-02 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150721185435/http://www.insd.bf/Documents/Publications/INSD/Publications/Resultats_enquetes/Autres%20enq/Rapport_preliminaire_RGPH06.pdf |archive-date=2015-07-21 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Koudougou is situated on the [[Rail transport in Burkina Faso|only railway line]] in Burkina Faso and has some small industries, a market, a [[University of Koudougou|university]] and provincial government offices.

==Geography==
[[Image:Localisation de Koudougou.jpg|left|thumb|alt=plan de koudougou|Location of Koudougou]]
Situated on the Mossi Plateau, the city is {{convert|75|km}} west of [[Ouagadougou]]. In 1952 it was connected by rail to Ouagadougou and [[Abidjan]]. It is situated along the N13 road south of [[Sabou]] and north of [[Yako, Burkina Faso|Yako]]. The N21 road connects the city to [[Réo, Burkina Faso|Réo]] and the N14 connects it with [[Dédougou]].

Although the city's administrative borders used to extend further, the city currently encompasses 15 surrounding villages.

==Economy==
[[File:Rue de koudougou.jpg|thumb|305px|right|Street in Koudougou]]
[[File:Ruines à koudougou.JPG|thumb|305px|Ruins of the royal palace of the canton of Lalle]]
[[File:Marché de koudougou.jpg|thumb|right|305px|Koudougou's Market]]
The economy of the city is dominated by agriculture with annual GDP for the city amounting to around 17.5 billion CFA (apx. $36 million).<ref name="web.archive.org">Site officiel [archive] de Koudougou., (French), https://web.archive.org/web/20110224011339/http://www.mairie-koudougou.net/</ref> The city has small industries such as a soap, [[shea butter]], [[cotton]] and various textile factories. As of February 2007, the city had radio-television production and broadcasting installations for [[Tele-Yaka]], a clothing recycling and manufacturing workshop, an experimental mango and green-leaf vegetable plantation, a metal forge for construction and maintenance of farming tools and implements, a metal waste-management and metal engineering workshop geared towards machine parts and motor components, an electronics repair shop and an open-air exhibition space for local sculptors.{{citation needed|date=June 2014}}

==Transportation==
[[File:Koudougou-TrainStation.JPG|thumb|305px|right|Train station in Koudougou]]
The city has a railroad station along the [[Rail transport in Burkina Faso|Abidjan – Ouagadougou Railway]]. As of June 2014 ''Sitarail'' operated a passenger train along the line three times a week in each direction.<ref>European Rail Timetable, Summer 2014 Edition, (journey time is 43 to 48 hours)</ref> [[Ouagadougou Airport|Ouagadougou International Airport]] is approximately 141&nbsp;km southeast of central Koudougou and as of June 2014 had regularly scheduled flights to most major cities in West Africa as well as Paris, Brussels and Istanbul.

==Education==
Since 2005 the city is home to the [[University of Koudougou]] (there were around 5,600 students enrolled in 2010/2011)<ref>Government of France, MINISTERE DES AFFAIRES ETRANGERES ET EUROPEENNES, AMBASSADE DE FRANCE AU BURKINA FASO, FICHE BURKINA FASO, (French)http://www.diplomatie.gouv.fr/fr/IMG/pdf/BURKINA_18-5-11__2_.pdf</ref> and in 2012 a vocational school opened. Primary school enrollment in 2007-2008 was 79.2% and secondary school enrollment was 20.3%.<ref name="web.archive.org"/>

==Sports==
The city has a few [[soccer]] clubs including ''l'Association des Jeunes Sportifs de Koudougou'' (AJSK), ''l'AS des Employés de Commerce de Koudougou'' (ASEC-K), ''le Bouloumpoukou FC'' (BPFC), ''le Bouloumpoukou Stade'' (BPS), ''l'Association des jeunes footballeurs'' (AJF), and ''le Jeunesse Club Boulkiemdé''.
They all play at the [[Stade Balibiè]].
