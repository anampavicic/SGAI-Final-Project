The '''North Jutland Region'''<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.visitnordjylland.com/ln-int/north_jutland |title=Official Tourist information – ''Welcome to North Jutland!'' |website=www.visitnordjylland.com |access-date=29 April 2018 |archive-date=7 July 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190707032432/https://www.visitnordjylland.com/ln-int/north_jutland |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.statsforvaltning.dk/site.aspx?p=3325 |title=Danish state authorities |website=www.statsforvaltning.dk |access-date=12 October 2009 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20080327190152/http://www.statsforvaltning.dk/site.aspx?p=3325 |archive-date=27 March 2008 |url-status=dead }} use both the Danish name ''Region Nordjylland'' and the direct translation ''North Jutland Region'' in English texts. The latter is also widely used by others, e.g. by the former [https://web.archive.org/web/20101015114815/http://www.ft.dk/Folketinget/Medlemmer/findMedlem/~/media/biographies/pdfDocuments/SORHA_en%20pdf.ashx Region Council Chairman].</ref> ({{langx|da|Region Nordjylland}}), or in some official sources, the '''North Denmark Region''',<ref>[http://www.rn.dk/Service/English The North Denmark Region] ''www.rn.dk'', accessed 2 January 2021</ref> is an [[Regions of Denmark|administrative region]] of [[Denmark]] established on 1 January 2007 as part of the [[2007 Danish Municipal Reform|2007 Danish municipal reform]], which abolished the traditional [[counties of Denmark|counties]] ({{Lang|da|amter}}) and set up five larger regions. At the same time, smaller [[municipalities of Denmark|municipalities]] were merged into larger units, cutting the number of municipalities from 271 before 1 January 2006, when [[Ærø Municipality]] was created, to 98. North Jutland Region has 11 municipalities. The reform diminished the power of the regional level dramatically in favor of the local level and the central government in [[Copenhagen]].

==Geography==
The North Jutland Region consists of the former [[North Jutland County]] combined with parts of the former [[Viborg County]] (the former municipalities of [[Aalestrup Municipality|Aalestrup]], [[Hanstholm Municipality|Hanstholm]], [[Morsø Municipality|Morsø]], [[Sydthy Municipality|Sydthy]], and [[Thisted Municipality|Thisted]]), and the western half of [[Mariager Municipality]] (in the former [[Aarhus County]]). It includes islands of [[Mors (island)|Mors]], [[Læsø]], and [[North Jutlandic Island]].

Geologically, the region lies in the northern part of Denmark, which is rising because of [[post-glacial rebound]].

== Economy ==
The [[gross domestic product]] (GDP) of the region was €26.1 billion in 2018, accounting for 8.7% of Denmark's economic output. The GDP per capita adjusted for purchasing power was €33,200 or 110% of the EU27 average in the same year.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/documents/2995521/10474907/1-05032020-AP-EN.pdf/81807e19-e4c8-2e53-c98a-933f5bf30f58|title=Regional GDP per capita ranged from 30% to 263% of the EU average in 2018|website=Eurostat |date= 5 March 2020 |access-date=2 January 2021}}</ref>

== Culture and education ==
One of Denmark's five universities, [[Aalborg Universitet|Aalborg University]], is situated in the region. Most of the region's museums are situated in Aalborg such as [[the Historical Museum of Northern Jutland]],  [[KUNSTEN Museum of Modern Art Aalborg|KUNSTEN Museum of Modern Art]], [[Musikkens Hus]], [[Utzon Center]] and [[Aalborg Zoo]].

==Regional Council==
Each of the five regions of Denmark has a regional council of 41 members. These are elected every four years, during the local elections.
