'''Caála''' is a town and a municipality located 23&nbsp;km west of the [[Huambo|city of Huambo]], in the namesake [[Huambo Province|province]] in [[Angola]]. It was known as [[Vila Robert Williams]] while Angola was a [[Portugal|Portuguese]] colony, after the famous [[railroad]] developer and [[mining]] magnate, [[Sir Robert Williams, 1st Baronet, of Park|Sir Robert Williams]]. The town has a population of 130,000<ref>[https://www.citypopulation.de/en/angola/cities/ Citypopulation.de]  Population of the major cities in Angola</ref> and the municipality a population of 279,792 according to the 2014 census.<ref name =Censo2014>{{cite web |url=https://www.ine.gov.ao/images/Censo_2014/PUBLICACAO_HUAMBO_FINAL_21_09_16_rev_dg.pdf |title=Resultados Definitivos Recenseamento Geral da População e Habitação – 2014 Província do Huambo |access-date=3 May 2020 |website=Instituto Nacional de Estatística, República de Angola |df=dmy }}{{Dead link|date=November 2023 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

== History ==
[[Vila Robert Williams]] was the former name of Caála, in the then [[Portuguese Angola]] (before 1975). The town of Robert Williams was just west of Nova Lisboa (now [[Huambo]]) and was remarkable for huge outcroppings of [[boulder]]s that jutted from the fields just outside the town. In those outcroppings researchers could find pottery shards, primitive metal smelting pits, and other archeological detritus.

==Sports==
Caála is home to the local popular soccer club [[C.R. Caála|Clube Recreativo da Caála]] who plays in Angola's top league, the [[Girabola]].

==Transport==
Caála is served by a station on the [[Benguela Railway]] and is also the junction for a branch railway to [[Cuima]]. There are no airports found within 15 km around Caala, however one airport is within 50 kilometers.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Airports within 50 km around Caala - Angola.Places-in-the-world.com |url=https://angola.places-in-the-world.com/3351500-airports-within-50km-around-caala.html |access-date=2022-12-03 |website=angola.places-in-the-world.com}}</ref>
