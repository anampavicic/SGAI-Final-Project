'''Alcorcón''' ({{IPA|es|alkoɾˈkon|lang|Pronunciation of Alcorcón in Spanish.ogg}}) is a city and [[Municipalities of Spain|municipality]] of [[Spain]] located in the [[Community of Madrid]]. As of 2022, it had a population of 170,296.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Madrid: Población por municipios y sexo. (2881) |url=https://www.ine.es/jaxiT3/Datos.htm?t=2881 |access-date=2023-10-09 |website=INE |language=es}}</ref>

== Geography ==
[[File:(Alcorcón) Madrid ESA354454 (cropped).jpg|thumb|left|The city as seen by the [[European Space Agency]]'s [[Sentinel-2]].]]
The municipality, largely flat,<ref name=AyDU /> lies at an average altitude of 711 metres above sea level,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.aemet.es/es/eltiempo/prediccion/municipios/alcorcon-id28007|publisher=[[AEMET]]|year=1991|title=Alcorcón (Madrid)}}</ref> with the highest point being El Ventorro del Cano (741 m), located in the north of the municipality.<ref name=AyDU>{{Cite book|volume=I|title=Arquitectura y Desarrollo Urbano Comunidad de Madrid (zona centro)|page=66–91|publisher=Dirección General de Arquitectura Consejería de Política Territorial de la Comunidad Autónoma de Madrid & Colegio Oficial de Arquitectos de Madrid|isbn=84-451-0296-6|location=Madrid|url=http://www.madrid.org/bvirtual/BVCM004553.pdf}}</ref>

== History ==
The site appears to have been inhabited since prehistoric times. The name is of Arabic origin, but little is known of Alcorcón before the [[Reconquista]]. The first recorded written reference to Alcorcón is in a court document (''{{ill|privilegio rodado|es}}'') dated on 28 July 1208, in the context of the territorial strife over {{ill|El Real de Manzanares|es}} between the city councils of [[Madrid]] and [[Segovia]] that took place in the Middle Ages.<ref>{{Cite book|page=7|chapter-url=https://dialnet.unirioja.es/descarga/articulo/6202309.pdf
|title=V Congreso virtual sobre historia de las vías de comunicación: 15 al 30 de septiembre de 2017 |editor=Enrique Escobedo Molinos |editor2=Juan Antonio López Cordero |editor3=Manuel Cabrera Espinosa |year=2017|chapter=Caminos reales, cañadas, veredas y coladas a su paso por Alcorcón|first=María Isabel|last=Rodríguez Iglesias}}</ref> The parish church, [[Church of Santa María la Blanca (Alcorcón)|Santa María la Blanca]] dates back to the 16th and 17th centuries.<ref name=AyDU /> Alcorcón preserved an urban structure characteristic of a small rural settlement until the 1960s.<ref name=AyDU /> The centre is surrounded by modern developments, as Alcorcón has grown rapidly since that decade.

As announced in February 2013, Alcorcón was the tentative building site for the [[Eurovegas]] casino resort project,<ref>{{cite news
|url=http://www.nbcnews.com/id/50743178/ns/local_news-las_vegas_nv|title=Spain's Alcorcon town chosen for EuroVegas resort|date=8 February 2013}}{{dead link|date=August 2024|bot=medic}}{{cbignore|bot=medic}}</ref> officially canceled in December 2013.<ref>{{cite web|title=Sheldon Adelson cancels $30bn Eurovegas project in Spain|author=Tobias Buck (Madrid)|publisher=[[Financial Times]] (London)|date=13 December 2013|url=http://www.ft.com/cms/s/0/3783f5c8-63f7-11e3-98e2-00144feabdc0.html|access-date=13 December 2013}}</ref>

== Economy ==
Agriculture and animal husbandry are hardly performed, and only a 0.04% of the [[Gross domestic product|GDP]] of the municipality is collected from these activities. Mining, industry and power economy activities produce the 11.09% of the money collected of this economy measure. 8.17% of the registers to an organism of the Welfare System named ''Tesorería General de la Seguridad Social'' (in which people must be registered when being hired), were linked to the organism by jobs of this sector. Construction activities make 7.28% of the GDP, and 10.65% of the registers in ''Tesorería General de la Seguridad Social'' are due to posts in the sector.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Datos ficha municipal 0072 2|url=http://www.madrid.org/desvan/almudena/FichaMunicipal.icm?codFicha=1&codMunZona=0072&tipoFicha=M&codSolapaActiva=2|access-date=2020-08-31|website=Comunidad de Madrid|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Datos ficha municipal 0072 5|url=http://www.madrid.org/desvan/almudena/FichaMunicipal.icm?codFicha=1&codMunZona=0072&tipoFicha=M&codSolapaActiva=5|access-date=2020-08-31|website=Comunidad de Madrid|language=es}}</ref>10.57% of the inhabitants work in the trade and hotel industry sectors, and 12.37% have transportation and stock posts.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Datos ficha municipal|url=http://www.madrid.org/desvan/almudena/FichaMunicipal.icm?codFicha=1&codMunZona=0072&tipoFicha=M&codSolapaActiva=5|access-date=2020-08-31|website=Comunidad de Madrid|language=es}}</ref>

== Health ==
Alcorcón has two hospitals, the [[Fundación Hospital de Alcorcón]] (which is state owned, but run privately), and the [[Hospital Sur Madrid]] (a totally private hospital). The Alcorcón campus of the [[Universidad Rey Juan Carlos]] (URJC) teaches health-related courses.
