'''Southwest Finland''' is since 2019<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.stat.fi/uutinen/alueluokitusmuutokset-112019 |title=Alueluokitusmuutokset 1.1.2019 &#124; Tilastokeskus |language=fi |trans-title=Changes in regional classification on 1 January 2019 |publisher=Stat.fi |date=January 7, 2019 |accessdate=2022-03-23}}</ref> the official name in English for the region in the southwest of [[Finland]] hitherto known as '''Finland Proper'''. In Finnish and Swedish, the region still retains the previous name ({{langx|fi|Varsinais-Suomi}}, {{IPA|fi|ˈʋɑrs̠inɑi̯s̠ˌs̠uo̞̯mi|IPA}}; {{langx|sv|Egentliga Finland}}). It borders the regions of [[Satakunta]], [[Pirkanmaa]], [[Tavastia Proper|Tavastia Proper (Kanta-Häme)]], [[Uusimaa]], and [[Åland]]. The region's capital and most populous city is [[Turku]], which was the [[capital city]] of Finland before [[Helsinki]].

The area is largely the same as the historical province of [[Finland Proper (historical province)|Finland Proper]], so named because it is the original home of the tribe known as the Finns proper.

== Origin of the name Finland Proper ==
[[File:Finland Proper seal 1326.png|thumb|left|upright=0.7|The seal of Finland Proper from 1326]]
The name of Finland Proper has a historical function. In historic times, in the area of the present Southern Finland lived three tribes, which were the [[Finns proper]], the [[Tavastians]] and the [[Karelians (Finns)|Karelians]]. The southwestern part of the country, the province where the Finns proper lived, was simply called Finland (Finnish: ''Suomi''). In the 17th century the name began to be used to refer to the whole land and a specified name for the lesser Finland was required. The first notes ''Fennigia specialiter dicta'' and ''Fennigia presse dicta'' were recorded in Latin in the 1650s, and the Swedish ''Finland för sig sielft'' and ''Egenteliga Finland'' later in the 18th century. The modern form ''Egentliga Finland'' was in official use at the end of the century, and the Finnish term ''Varsinais-Suomi'' became established only around the 1850s.<ref>{{cite book |title=Suomalainen paikannimikirja |location=Jyväskylä |publisher=Gummerus |year=2007 |isbn=978-951-593-976-0}}</ref>

== Geography ==
[[File:Finland 2018-07-07 (29411848567).jpg|thumb|left|Turku Archipelago]]
Southwest Finland's nature differs from other regions. The most notable biotopes are the [[Archipelago Sea]] and groves. 80% of Finland's insect species can be found in Southwest Finland.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.vihreapolku.info/kestava_kehitys/parempia_valintoja_-_turkulaisen_toiminta-_ja_kulutusopas/kuntoillaan_kestavasti_ja_nautitaan_lahiluonnosta | title=Essay Writing Help at Its Finest }}</ref> There are around 20,000 islands near the coast.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.saaristoon.fi/ |title=Saaristoon – Saaristoon |access-date=4 June 2019 |archive-date=4 December 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201204105025/https://saaristoon.fi/ |url-status=dead }}</ref>

The southernmost point of Southwest Finland and the southernmost inhabited island is [[Utö (Finland)|Utö]].<ref>Turun Sanomat</ref> Its highest point is 164 meters in [[Kiikala]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.salo.fi/ymparistojaluonto/luontojaretkeily/luontokohteetjareitit/hyypparanharjukultalahdejasaramaki/ |title=Hyypp r nharju, Kultal hde ja S r m ki - Salon kaupunki |website=www.salo.fi |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130506111755/http://www.salo.fi/ymparistojaluonto/luontojaretkeily/luontokohteetjareitit/hyypparanharjukultalahdejasaramaki/ |archive-date=2013-05-06}} </ref>

As of 2020, Southwest Finland had a population of 481,403, making it the third most populated Finnish region after [[Uusimaa]] and [[Pirkanmaa]]. 86.45% speak [[Finnish language|Finnish]], 5.68% [[Swedish language|Swedish]] and 7.86% speak other languages, the most common being [[Russian language|Russian]], [[Estonian language|Estonian]], [[Arabic]], [[Kurdish language|Kurdish]] and [[Albanian language|Albanian]].

It has the most summer cottages out of any Finnish region, with 49,000 as of 2012.<ref>{{cite web| url = https://www.iltalehti.fi/mokkiextra/a/2013052417065336| title = Tiesitkö tätä kesämökeistä? 10 faktaa}}</ref>
