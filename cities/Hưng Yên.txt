'''Hưng Yên''' ({{audio|Hung Yen.ogg|listen|help=no}}) is a city in [[Vietnam]]. It is the provincial capital of [[Hưng Yên Province]] and is a third-graded city according to Vietnam's city classification table.

==Geography==
*Hưng Yên is a delta city. It is located in the south of [[Hưng Yên Province]], on the left bank (north bank) of the [[Red River (Asia)|Red River]] in the northern part of Vietnam. Hưng Yên is about 60&nbsp;km away from [[Hanoi]].
*Hưng Yên borders [[Kim Động District]] to the north and [[Tiên Lữ District]] to the east. The Red River is the natural boundary between Hưng Yên and [[Hà Nam Province]]'s [[Lý Nhân District]] and [[Duy Tiên District]].
*Area: 46.80&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> (4,685.51 ha)

==Demography==
*Population: 121,486 people (2008)

==Administration==
Hưng Yên administers 12 administrative divisions:

*7 [[ward (Vietnam)|wards]] (phường): Lê Lợi, Quang Trung, Minh Khai, Hiến Nam, Lam Sơn, Hồng Châu and An Tảo.
*5 [[Commune of Vietnam|communes]] (xã): Bảo Khê, Trung Nghĩa, Liên Phương, Hồng Nam and Quảng Châu.

==Tourism==

The city is well-known in the world (especially among historians) for [[Phố Hiến]] (an ancient port-city located in the area). There are still a lot of ancient Asian temples, pagodas and other religious buildings here.

[[Longan|Nhãn lồng]] (caged longan - a kind of fruit) is one of Hưng Yên's specialties.
