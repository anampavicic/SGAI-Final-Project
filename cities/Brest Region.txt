'''Brest Region''', also known as '''Brest Oblast''',{{efn|{{langx|ru|Брестская область|Brestskaya oblast'}}.}} '''Brest Voblasts'''{{efn|{{langx|be|Брэсцкая вобласць|Bresckaja voblasć}}; {{langx|be|Берасьцейская вобласьць|Bieraściejskaja vobłaść|label=[[Taraskievica]]}}.}} or '''Brestchyna''',{{efn|{{langx|be|Брэстчына|Brestčyna|links=no}}; also {{langx|be|Берасьцейшчына|Bieraściejščyna|label=none}}.}} is one of the six [[regions of Belarus]]. Its [[administrative center]] is [[Brest, Belarus|Brest]]. Other major cities in the region include [[Baranavichy]], and [[Pinsk]]. As of 2024, it has a population of 1,308,569.<ref name="pop"/>

==Geography==
It is located in the southwestern part of Belarus, bordering the [[Podlaskie Voivodeship|Podlasie]] and [[Lublin Voivodeship|Lublin]] voivodeships of [[Poland]] on the west, the [[Volyn Oblast]] and [[Rivne Oblast]] of [[Ukraine]] on the south, the [[Grodno Region]] and [[Minsk Region]] on the north, and [[Gomel Region]] on the east. The region covers a total area of 32,800&nbsp;km²,<ref name=statistics>{{cite web |url = http://landofancestors.com/travel/statistics/geography/236-territory-and-population-density-by-region-as-of-january-1-2011.html |title = Main Geographic Characteristics of the Republic of Belarus. Territory and population density of Belarus by region as of January 1, 2011 |publisher = The Scientific and Production State Republican Unitary Enterprise “National Cadastre Agency” of the State Property Committee of the Republic of Belarus |year = 2011 |website = Land of Ancestors |access-date = 24 September 2013 |archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20130927131905/http://landofancestors.com/travel/statistics/geography/236-territory-and-population-density-by-region-as-of-january-1-2011.html |archive-date = 27 September 2013 |url-status = dead }}</ref> about 15.7% of the national total.

The westernmost point of Belarus is situated in [[Kamyenyets District]] near the town of [[Vysokaye]].<ref>{{cite web |url = http://landofancestors.com/travel/statistics/geography/235-coordinates-of-the-extreme-points-of-the-state-frontier.html |title = Main Geographic Characteristics of the Republic of Belarus |publisher = The Scientific and Production State Republican Unitary Enterprise “National Cadastre Agency” of the State Property Committee of the Republic of Belarus |year = 2011 |website = Land of Ancestors |access-date = 20 September 2013 |archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20130921053717/http://landofancestors.com/travel/statistics/geography/235-coordinates-of-the-extreme-points-of-the-state-frontier.html |archive-date = 21 September 2013 |url-status = dead }}</ref>

2.7% of the territory is covered by [[Belovezhskaya Pushcha National Park]], 9.8% is covered by 17 wildlife preserves of national importance.<ref>{{cite web |url = http://landofancestors.com/travel/statistics/resources/culture-and-arts/297-nature-reserves-and-national-parks-wildlife-preserves-and-nature-sanctuaries.html |title = Nature reserves and national parks, wildlife preserves and nature sanctuaries |publisher = Data of the Ministry of Natural Resources and Environmental Protection of the Republic of Belarus |year = 2011 |website = Land of Ancestors |access-date = 29 November 2013 |archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20130929092527/http://landofancestors.com/travel/statistics/resources/culture-and-arts/297-nature-reserves-and-national-parks-wildlife-preserves-and-nature-sanctuaries.html |archive-date = 29 September 2013 |url-status = dead }}</ref>

It is often dubbed the ''Western gateway'' to [[Belarus]]. Geographically, the Brest Region belongs to the area known as [[Polesia]]. The area of the region was part of the [[Second Polish Republic]] from 1921 until 1939 largely as the [[Polesie Voivodeship]], when it was joined to the [[Byelorussian Soviet Socialist Republic]]. Northeastern part of it was administered as part of [[Nowogródek Voivodeship (1919–1939)|Nowogródek Voivodeship]].

==Demographics==
The Brest Region has a population of 1,380,391,<ref name=belstat.2019 /> about 14,7% of the national total. About 47.2% of the region's population are men, and the remaining 52.8% are women. Number of inhabitants per 1 km2 is 43.<ref name="statistics"/> Share of urban population is increasing continuously since the 1950s (17.1% in 1950, 70.5% in 2017).<ref name=dem.29 />

Of the major nationalities living in the Brest Region, 1,262,600 are [[Belarusians]] (85%), 128,700 (8.6%) are [[Russians]], 57,100 (3.8%) are [[Ukrainians]], and 27,100 (1.8%) are [[Polish people|Poles]]. 53.7% of the population speak [[Belarusian language|Belarusian]] and 42.6% speak [[Russian language|Russian]] as their native language.<ref>{{cite book|format=Zip archive|url=http://belstat.gov.by/homep/en/census/2009/volume3.zip|title=Ethnic Composition of the Population of the Republic of Belarus: Population Census 2009|publisher=National Statistical Committee of the Republic of Belarus|date=2011|volume=3|page=378|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120423092206/http://belstat.gov.by/homep/en/census/2009/volume3.zip|archive-date=23 April 2012}}</ref>

Brest is the province with the highest birth rate in all of Belarus. As of 2008, the birth rate was 12.0 per 1000 and death rate was 13.4 per 1000.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://belstat.gov.by/homep/ru/news/news85.php |title=Новости |access-date=2008-12-29 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090918013106/http://belstat.gov.by/homep/ru/news/news85.php |archive-date=2009-09-18 }}</ref> In 2017, 12.4% of live births were to unmarried women (average in Belarus — 18.1%).<ref name=dem.266>[http://www.belstat.gov.by/ofitsialnaya-statistika/publications/izdania/public_compilation/index_10769 Демографический ежегодник Республики Беларусь. As of 2018, share of the population under working age was 19.3% (average in Belarus — 17.9%), of working age — 55.7% (average in Belarus — 57.2%), over working age — 25% (average in Belarus — 25.1%).<ref>[http://www.belstat.gov.by/ofitsialnaya-statistika/publications/izdania/public_compilation/index_10769 Демографический ежегодник Республики Беларусь. Минск, 2018] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190714144405/http://www.belstat.gov.by/ofitsialnaya-statistika/publications/izdania/public_compilation/index_10769 |date=2019-07-14 }}. In 2015—2017, the region had a positive [[net migration rate]] for international migration (+3,209 in 2015, +1,771 in 2016, +1,357 in 2017) and negative — for internal migration (-6,294 in 2015, -3,659 in 2016, -1,836 in 2017). In 2017, 6,944 people which departed from the Brest Region arrived in Minsk, 2,219 — in the Minsk Region, 2,118 — in the Hrodna Region, less than 1,000 — in every other region.<ref>[http://www.belstat.gov.by/ofitsialnaya-statistika/publications/izdania/public_compilation/index_10769 Демографический ежегодник Республики Беларусь. ==Administrative territorial entities==

==Economy==

As of 2017, the industrial output of the region amounted to {{Currency|10.578 million|BYN|}} (~US$5,300 million), or 11.2% of the Belarusian industrial output.<ref>{{cite book|url=http://www.belstat.gov.by/ofitsialnaya-statistika/realny-sector-ekonomiki/promyshlennost/publikatsii_13/index_10633/|title=Промышленность Республики Беларусь [Industry in Belarus]|publisher=Minsk: National Statistical Committee of the Republic of Belarus|date=2018|page=38}}</ref>

Food industry is the leading economy sphere in the region (47.6% of the region's industrial output). The biggest industrial plants in the region are "Savushkin produkt" (dairy products), "Santa Bremor" (fish products), Brestgazoapparat (gas equipment, including "Gefest" cookers; all three are situated in [[Brest, Belarus|Brest]]), "Pinskdrev" in [[Pinsk]] (furniture, matches and other wood products), "Polesie" in [[Kobryn]] (toys and plastic products). Other major factories are machine tool plant "Atlant", cotton factory, military jet fighters repair plant (all three are situated in [[Baranavichy|Baranavichy (Baranovichi)]]), sugar plant in [[Zhabinka]], "Polesie" textile and garment factory in [[Pinsk]], "Ivacevichidrev" wood products plant in [[Ivacevichi]], "Granite" quarry in [[Mikashevichi]], "Belsolod" [[malt]] factory in [[Ivanava]] (the only such plant in Belarus).<ref name=prom>[http://brest-region.gov.by/index.php/ekonomika/promyshlennost Промышленность] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190717130155/http://brest-region.gov.by/index.php/ekonomika/promyshlennost |date=2019-07-17 }} {{in lang|ru}}</ref>

Food factories in the region produced 153.9 thousand tons of meat and offal in 2017, 90 thousand tons of sausage products, 29.7 thousand tons of meat semi-finished products, 71.8 tons of fish and semi-finished products (including canned fish), 551.9 thousand tons of whole milk products, 47.2 thousand tons of [[cottage cheese]], 66.1 thousand tons of [[cheese]], 21.3 thousand tons of [[butter]], 108.2 thousand tons of flour, 2.5 million [[decalitre]]s of [[beer]] and 4.3 million decalitres of distilled alcoholic beverages.<ref>{{cite book|url=http://www.belstat.gov.by/ofitsialnaya-statistika/realny-sector-ekonomiki/promyshlennost/publikatsii_13/index_10633/|title=Промышленность Республики Беларусь [Industry in Belarus]|publisher=Minsk: National Statistical Committee of the Republic of Belarus|date=2018|pages=89–96}}</ref> Textile and garment factories produced 56,361 thousand m<sup>2</sup> of fabrics in 2017, 994 thousand m<sup>2</sup> of carpets, 5.9 million pieces of all types of knitwear, 1.9 million pieces of outerwear (except knitwear), 28.4 million pairs of socks and similar hosiery, 623 thousand shoes.<ref>{{cite book|url=http://www.belstat.gov.by/ofitsialnaya-statistika/realny-sector-ekonomiki/promyshlennost/publikatsii_13/index_10633/|title=Промышленность Республики Беларусь [Industry in Belarus]|publisher=Minsk: National Statistical Committee of the Republic of Belarus|date=2018|pages=98–101}}</ref> Electrical plants in the region produced 242.2 thousand electric engines (alternating current), 4800 transformers, 899 thousand lamps.<ref>{{cite book|url=http://www.belstat.gov.by/ofitsialnaya-statistika/realny-sector-ekonomiki/promyshlennost/publikatsii_13/index_10633/|title=Промышленность Республики Беларусь [Industry in Belarus]|publisher=Minsk: National Statistical Committee of the Republic of Belarus|date=2018|page=133}}</ref> Brest lamp factory is producing [[incandescent light bulb]]s, 2 factories are engaged in production of [[diode]]s and [[printed circuit board]]s.<ref name=prom />

Byarozaŭskaja (Berezovskaya) thermal power plant in [[Byaroza District]] is one of the biggest power plants in Belarus (1095 [[Megawatt|MW]], 4.8 [[GW·h]] annually). As of 2018, it had 4 power generating units with [[Combined cycle power plant|combined cycle gas turbines]].<ref>[https://www.brestenergo.by/%D0%91%D0%B5%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%B7%D0%BE%D0%B2%D1%81%D0%BA%D0%B0%D1%8F_%D0%93%D0%A0%D0%AD%D0%A1 Березовская ГРЭС] {{in lang|ru}}</ref>

As of 2017, total cultivated area in the region was 930,000 [[hectare]]s, including 844,400 hectares of land used by agricultural organizations (mainly state-owned), 17,900 hectares — by the registered farmers, and 67,700 hectares — by personal farms of the population. 383,800 hectares of fields were used to grow [[cereal]]s, 52,800 — [[rapeseed]]s, 21,800 — [[sugar beet]]s, 5,700 — [[flax]], 56,300 — [[potato]]es, 12,700 — [[vegetable]]s, 392,800 — feed crops.<ref>{{cite book|url=http://www.belstat.gov.by/ofitsialnaya-statistika/publications/izdania/public_compilation/index_9394/|title=Сельское хозяйство Республики Беларусь [Agriculture in Belarus]|publisher=Minsk: National Statistical Committee of the Republic of Belarus|date=2018|pages=64–71}}</ref> Big agricultural organizations harvest almost all cereals, flax, sugar beets, rapeseeds and feed crops, while farmers (both registered and not) harvest more than 90% of potatoes (1,144,400 tons of 1,266,200 in the region) and vegetables (409,400 tons of 439,200 in the region).<ref>{{cite book|url=http://www.belstat.gov.by/ofitsialnaya-statistika/publications/izdania/public_compilation/index_9394/|title=Сельское хозяйство Республики Беларусь [Agriculture in Belarus]|publisher=Minsk: National Statistical Committee of the Republic of Belarus|date=2018|pages=97–101}}</ref> Average [[cereal]] yield in the region in 2017 was 3,480&nbsp;kg per [[hectare]] (average in Belarus — 3,330), [[sugar beet]] yield — 43,900&nbsp;kg per hectare (average in Belarus — 49,900), [[flax]] fiber yield — 1,160&nbsp;kg per hectare (average in Belarus — 920).<ref>[http://brest-region.gov.by/index.php/ekonomika/selskoe-khozyajstvo/8308-itogi-raboty-brestskoj-oblasti-2017-god Сельское хозяйство] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190504114638/http://brest-region.gov.by/index.php/ekonomika/selskoe-khozyajstvo/8308-itogi-raboty-brestskoj-oblasti-2017-god |date=2019-05-04 }} {{in lang|ru}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|url=http://www.belstat.gov.by/ofitsialnaya-statistika/publications/izdania/public_compilation/index_9394/|title=Сельское хозяйство Республики Беларусь [Agriculture in Belarus]|publisher=Minsk: National Statistical Committee of the Republic of Belarus|date=2018|pages=110–117}}</ref> Due to warm mild climate and personal activity, the region leads in the production of many fruits, vegetables and berries in Belarus. As of 2018, agricultural organizations and farmers kept 861,600 cattle (including 301,100 cows), 491,100 pigs, 10,000 horses, 19,500 sheep, 7,605,000 poultry. In 2017, all types of farms in the region produced 218,700 tons of livestock and poultry meat (in slaughter weight), 1,605,000 tons of milk (second place among the regions of Belarus) and 606.8 million eggs (second place).<ref>{{cite book|url=http://www.belstat.gov.by/ofitsialnaya-statistika/publications/izdania/public_compilation/index_9394/|title=Сельское хозяйство Республики Беларусь [Agriculture in Belarus]|publisher=Minsk: National Statistical Committee of the Republic of Belarus|date=2018|pages=131–155}}</ref>
