'''Sobral''' is a [[Municipalities of Brazil|municipality]] in the state of [[Ceará]], Brazil.

Sobral is the fifth largest municipality of Ceará, after [[Fortaleza]]. Its economy is based on agriculture, services and some manufacturing [[Industry (economics)|industries]]. The city has two public universities: [[Universidade Federal do Ceará]] and [[Universidade Estadual do Vale do Acaraú]]. It also has private universities, such as {{interlanguage link|Faculdade Luciano Feijão|lt=Faculdade Luciano Feijão|pt}}, [[Universidade Norte do Paraná|Unopar]], and {{interlanguage link|Instituto Superior de Teologia Aplicada|lt=UNINTA|pt}} − a theological institute. The city is the seat of the [[Roman Catholic Diocese of Sobral]].

The city is known for being the place where the [[Eddington experiment|astronomical observation]] of a [[solar eclipse]] on [[Solar eclipse of May 29, 1919|May 29, 1919]], by a team of [[United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland|British]] scientists led by [[Sir Frank Watson Dyson]] was offered as the [[tests of general relativity|first proof]] of [[Albert Einstein]]'s theory of [[general relativity]], which had been published in 1916. The town's {{interlanguage link|Museu do Eclipse|pt}} ("Museum of the Eclipse") celebrates this event. There is a monument in Patrocínio Square marking the location of this solar eclipse. A planetarium was also inaugurated in 2015 next to this monument.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://cultura.sobral.ce.gov.br/espaco/996/|title = Planetário de Sobral - Mapa Cultural do Ceará|date = January 27, 2015}}</ref>
