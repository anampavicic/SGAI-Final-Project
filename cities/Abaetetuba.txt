The city of '''Abaetetuba''' is located in [[Pará]] State, [[Brazil]]. The population of the municipality is 159,080 ([[Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics|IBGE]] figure 2020). The city is the seat of the [[Roman Catholic Diocese of Abaetetuba]], and home of the [[Our Lady of the Conception Cathedral, Abaetetuba|Our Lady of the Conception Cathedral]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=FRANCINETI MARIA RODRIGUES CARVALHO|url=https://cidades.ibge.gov.br/brasil/pa/abaetetuba/panorama|url-status=live|access-date=2021-07-12|website=cidades|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20180410134841/https://cidades.ibge.gov.br/brasil/pa/abaetetuba/panorama  |archive-date = 10 April 2018  }}</ref>

== Etymology ==
"Abaetetuba" comes from the [[Tupi language]] and means "group of true men", from the words ''abá'' ("man"), ''eté'' ("true") and ''tyba'' ("group").<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fflch.usp.br/dlcv/tupi/vocabulario.htm |title=Curso de Tupi Antigo |access-date=2012-09-10 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120809053512/http://www.fflch.usp.br/dlcv/tupi/vocabulario.htm |archive-date=9 August 2012 }}</ref>

== History ==

First settlements in the region date to 1635, when capuchin friars originating in the convent of Una in [[Belém]] joined a village of nomadic indigenous tribes in a settlement with the name of '''Samaúma''', though later baptized as the district of '''Beja''' by governor [[Francisco Xavier de Mendonça Furtado]].

Later, in 1724, [[Francisco de Azevedo Monteiro]], dedicated to the exploration of local amazonian spices and goods, acquired a [[Sesmaria]] in the region, founding a settlement on the shores of the river Maratauíra in a location protected from the tides by the island of Sirituba, which would later form the urban center of the current city.<ref>{{Cite web|title=History of the Municipality (Official City Website)|access-date=2022-01-25|url=https://www.abaetetuba.pa.gov.br/omunicipio.php|language=pt}}</ref>

Known then as '''Abaeté''', the settlement was granted the status of autonomous municipality and separated from the capital of [[Belém]] in 1880 through law nº 973, Abaeté would later be granted the status of city in 1895 through state law nº 324 and finally change its name to Abaetetuba in December 30 of 1943, through decree-law nº 4.505.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Comarcas in the State of Pará|access-date=2022-01-25|url=https://www.tjpa.jus.br/PortalExterno/indexComarcas.xhtml|language=pt}}</ref>
