'''Casablanca-Settat''' ({{langx|ar|الدار البيضاء - سطات|ad-dār al-bayḍāʾ - siṭṭāt}}) is one of the twelve [[regions of Morocco|administrative regions of]] [[Morocco]]. It covers an area of 20,166 km<sup>2</sup> and recorded a population of 6,861,739 in the [[2014 Moroccan census]],<ref name=statoids /> 69% of which lived in urban areas.<ref name=conjoncture>{{cite web |url=http://www.conjoncture.info/casablanca-settat-une-mega-region-a-fort-potentiel/ |title=Casablanca-Settat, une « méga-région » à fort potentiel |date=6 December 2015 |access-date=14 December 2015}}</ref> The capital of the region is [[Casablanca]].<ref name=Bulletin>{{cite web |url=http://www.pncl.gov.ma/fr/EspaceJuridique/DocLib/d%C3%A9cret%20fixant%20le%20nombre%20des%20r%C3%A9gions.pdf |title=Décret fixant le nom des régions |date=20 February 2015 |access-date=11 December 2015 |format=pdf |work=Portail National des Collectivités Territoriales |language=fr |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150518083233/http://www.pncl.gov.ma/fr/EspaceJuridique/DocLib/d%C3%A9cret%20fixant%20le%20nombre%20des%20r%C3%A9gions.pdf |archive-date=2015-05-18 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Geography==
Casablanca-Settat is located on the [[Atlantic Ocean|Atlantic]] coast. It borders the regions of [[Rabat-Salé-Kénitra]] to the northeast, [[Béni Mellal-Khénifra]] to the southeast, and [[Marrakesh-Safi]] to the south. Part of the border with Marrakesh-Safi follows the course of the [[Oum Er-Rbia River]], which flows northwest and empties into the Atlantic at [[Azemmour]].<ref>{{cite journal |last=Ouassou |first=A. |last2=Ameziane |first2=T. |last3=Ziyad |first3=A. |last4=Belghiti |first4=M. |date=2007 |title=Application of the Drought Management Guidelines in Morocco |url=http://om.ciheam.org/article.php?IDPDF=800547 |journal=Options Méditerranéennes, Series B |publisher=CIHEAM |issue=58 |pages=343–372 |access-date=13 December 2015}}</ref> The river divides the region into two plains, the [[Doukkala]] in the west and the [[Chaouia (Morocco)|Chaouia]] in the east. Several reservoirs provide water for the region, including that of the [[Al Massira Dam]] on the Oum Er-Rbia and one on the [[Oued Mellah]] south of [[Mohammedia]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.water.gov.ma/index.cfm?gen=true&id=13&ID_PAGE=46 |title=Ressources en eau par bassin – Côtiers atlantiques |language=fr |publisher=Ministry of Energy, Mines, Water and Environment, Morocco |access-date=14 December 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151222142133/http://www.water.gov.ma/index.cfm?gen=true&id=13&ID_PAGE=46 |archive-date=2015-12-22 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.water.gov.ma/index.cfm?gen=true&id=13&ID_PAGE=29 |title=Ressources en eau par bassin – Bassins d'El Jadida - Safi |language=fr |publisher=Ministry of Energy, Mines, Water and Environment, Morocco |access-date=14 December 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151222151423/http://www.water.gov.ma/index.cfm?gen=true&id=13&ID_PAGE=29 |archive-date=22 December 2015 }}</ref>

==History==
Casablanca-Settat was formed in September 2015 by merging [[Grand Casablanca]] with the provinces of El Jadida and Sidi Bennour in [[Doukkala-Abda]] region and the provinces of Benslimane, Berrechid and Settat in [[Chaouia-Ouardigha]] region.<ref name=Bulletin />

==Government==
[[Mustapha Bakkoury]], a member of the [[Authenticity and Modernity Party]], was elected as the first president of Casablanca-Settat's regional council on 14 September 2015.<ref>{{cite press release |date=14 September 2015 |title=Ministère de l'Intérieur : l'élection des présidents des Conseils des régions s'est déroulée dans de bonnes conditions et dans un climat de transparence |trans-title=Ministry of the Interior: the regional council presidential elections took place under good conditions and in an air of transparency |url=http://www.maroc.ma/fr/actualites/ministre-de-linterieur-lelection-des-presidents-des-conseils-des-regions-sest-deroulee |language=fr |publisher=[[Maghreb Arabe Press]] |access-date=11 December 2015}}</ref> Khalid Safir was appointed governor (''wali'') of the region on 13 October 2015.<ref>{{cite news |date=14 October 2015 |title=SM le Roi a procédé à la nomination les Walis des régions |trans-title=His majesty the King appointed the Walis of the regions |url=http://www.lavieeco.com/news/actualite-maroc/sm-le-roi-a-procede-a-la-nomination-les-walis-des-regions-35593.html |language=fr |newspaper=[[La Vie Éco]] |access-date=13 December 2015 |archive-date=22 December 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151222092322/http://lavieeco.com/news/actualite-maroc/sm-le-roi-a-procede-a-la-nomination-les-walis-des-regions-35593.html |url-status=dead }}</ref> He was succeeded by Abdelkébir Zahoud in 2017.<ref>{{cite news |date=27 June 2017 |title=Abdelkébir Zahoud: wali de Casablanca, businessman et ancien député |url=http://telquel.ma/2017/06/27/abdelkebir-zahoud-wali-casablanca-businessman-ancien-depute_1552032 |language=fr |newspaper=[[TelQuel]] |access-date=9 October 2017}}</ref>

==Economy==
Casablanca-Settat had a [[gross domestic product]] of 290 billion [[Moroccan dirham]]s in 2013, accounting for 32% of Morocco's GDP and ranking first among Moroccan regions.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Comptes régionaux: Deux pôles assurent la moitié du PIB |language=fr |url=http://www.leconomiste.com/article/976749-comptes-regionauxdeux-poles-assurent-la-moitie-du-pib |newspaper=[[L'Économiste]] |access-date=14 December 2015}}</ref> Its economy is primarily based on services and industry.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.moroccobusinessnews.com/Morocco/Casablanca.asp |title=Regions of Morocco: The Great Casablanca |publisher=Centre Marocain de Conjoncture |access-date=14 December 2015 |url-status=usurped |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120309082247/http://www.moroccobusinessnews.com/Morocco/Casablanca.asp |archive-date=9 March 2012 }}</ref> In addition, the Doukkala area in the west is noted for its agricultural output.<ref name=conjoncture/>

==Infrastructure==
The [[Casablanca–Rabat expressway|A3]], [[Casablanca–Safi expressway|A5]], and [[Casablanca–Marrakesh expressway|A7]] [[Autoroutes of Morocco|expressways]] connect Casablanca with [[Rabat]], [[Safi, Morocco|Safi]]<ref>{{cite news |last=Roudaby |first=Youssef |url=http://www.huffpostmaghreb.com/2016/08/04/autoroute-el-jadida-safi_n_11329336.html?1470303324 |title=Après 17 mois de retard, l'autoroute reliant El Jadida à Safi est opérationnelle |language=fr |newspaper=HuffPost Maroc |date=4 August 2016 |access-date=6 August 2016 |archive-date=15 August 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160815134020/http://www.huffpostmaghreb.com/2016/08/04/autoroute-el-jadida-safi_n_11329336.html?1470303324 |url-status=usurped }}</ref> (via [[El Jadida]]), and [[Marrakesh]] (via [[Berrechid]] and [[Settat]]) respectively. There is also an [[Berrechid–Beni Mellal expressway|expressway]] running from Berrechid to [[Beni Mellal]]. Railways link the region with Marrakesh to the south, [[Oued Zem]] to the southeast, and Rabat and other Moroccan cities to the northeast.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.oncf.ma/OffreDeTransport/Pages/TrainsGrandesLignes.aspx |title=Trains Grandes Lignes |language=fr |publisher=[[ONCF]] |access-date=13 December 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151127183541/http://www.oncf.ma/OffreDeTransport/Pages/TrainsGrandesLignes.aspx |archive-date=2015-11-27 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The ports at [[Port of Casablanca|Casablanca]], [[Jorf Lasfar]] and Mohammedia ranked second through fourth nationally by tonnage in 2014.<ref>{{cite report |title=Activite portuaire 2014 |language=fr |url=http://www.anp.org.ma/Services/Trafic%20portuaire/Trafic_2014.pdf |publisher=Agence Nationale des Ports |access-date=14 December 2015 |archive-date=9 August 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170809225542/http://www.anp.org.ma/Services/Trafic%20portuaire/Trafic_2014.pdf |url-status=dead }}</ref> [[Mohammed V International Airport]], located 20 km south of Casablanca in Nouaceur Province, is [[List of the busiest airports in Morocco|Morocco's busiest airport]], handling nearly eight million passengers in 2014.<ref name="2014traffic">{{cite press release|url=http://www.onda.ma/content/download/5887/46997/version/1/fichier/Communiqu%C3%A9+statiqtiques+CMN+n+04-15.pdf/|access-date=23 November 2015 |title=Aéroport Mohammed V Trafic aérien en  2014 |format=pdf |language=fr |trans-title=Mohammed V Airport Air Traffic in 2014 |date=30 January 2005|publisher=Office National Des Aéroports (ONDA)}}</ref> The country's only [[oil refinery]] in Mohammedia shut down in August 2015.<ref>{{cite news |last=Libby |first=George |date=25 November 2015 |title=Moroccan refinery's debts highlight oil sector lending dangers |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/morocco-refinery-debts-idUSL8N13F28320151125 |agency=[[Reuters]] |access-date=14 December 2015}}</ref>
