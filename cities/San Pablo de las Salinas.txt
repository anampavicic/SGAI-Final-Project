'''San Pablo de las Salinas''' is an [[exclave]] and the second-largest [[Colonia (Mexico)|colonia]] in [[Tultitlán de Mariano Escobedo|Tultitlán Municipality]] in the [[State of Mexico]], [[Mexico]].<ref>{{Cite book |last=Gibson |first=Charles |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=V76hPwilk04C |title=The Aztecs Under Spanish Rule: A History of the Indians of the Valley of Mexico, 1519-1810 |date=1964 |publisher=Stanford University Press |isbn=978-0-8047-0196-9 |pages=47–56 |language=en}}</ref> The neighborhood is part of the [[Mexico City]] [[metropolitan areas of Mexico|metropolitan area]] and had a 2010 census population of 189,453 inhabitants, or 36.15% of the municipal population.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2013-05-02 |title=Catálogo de claves de entidades federativas, municipios y localidades |url=http://mapserver.inegi.org.mx/mgn2k/?s=geo&c=1223 |access-date=2022-10-11 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130502025339/http://mapserver.inegi.org.mx/mgn2k/?s=geo&c=1223 |archive-date=2013-05-02 }}</ref> The town lies near the northern tip of [[Mexico City]].<ref>{{Cite journal |last1=Millhauser |first1=John K. |last2=Morehart |first2=Christopher T. |date=July 2018 |title=8 Sustainability as a Relative Process: A Long‐Term Perspective on Sustainability in the Northern Basin of Mexico |url=https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/apaa.12103 |journal=Archaeological Papers of the American Anthropological Association |language=en |volume=29 |issue=1 |pages=134–156 |doi=10.1111/apaa.12103 |s2cid=134709380 |issn=1551-823X}}</ref> It is the third-largest locality in Mexico that is not a municipal seat (after [[Ojo de Agua, Mexico State|Ojo de Agua, Tecámac Municipality]], and [[Buenavista, Mexico State|Buenavista]], also in Tultitlán Municipality). 

The neighborhood of Granjas San Pablo was founded in 1957, the first family that moved to Granjas San Pablo was Don Antonio Lopez-Araiza y Padilla and her wife Consuelo Villegas de Araiza, Daughters Guillermina Araiza Garcia, Nancy Araiza Martinez, Abel Araiza, Antonio Araiza y Carlos Araiza.
