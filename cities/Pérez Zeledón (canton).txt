'''Pérez Zeledón''' is the nineteenth [[Cantons of Costa Rica|canton]] of the province of [[San José Province|San José]] in [[Costa Rica]], located in the [[Brunca|Brunca region]].<ref name="divadm">{{cite web |language=es |title=Declara oficial para efectos administrativos, la aprobación de la División Territorial Administrativa de la República N°41548-MGP |url=http://www.pgrweb.go.cr/scij/Busqueda/Normativa/Normas/nrm_texto_completo.aspx?param1=NRTC&nValor1=1&nValor2=88416&nValor3=115607&param2=1&strTipM=TC&lResultado=1&strSim=simp |website=Sistema Costarricense de Información Jurídica |access-date=26 September 2020 |date=19 March 2019}}</ref><ref name="dta2017">{{cite book |language=es |title=División Territorial Administrativa de la República de Costa Rica|date=8 March 2017|publisher=Editorial Digital de la Imprenta Nacional|isbn=978-9977-58-477-5|url=https://www.imprentanacional.go.cr/editorialdigital/libros/historiaygeografia/division_17.pdf}}</ref> The capital city of the canton is [[San Isidro de El General]].

The Río Savegre establishes the canton's boundaries on the northwest, the Río Guabo and a series of coastal mountain ranges on the southwest and south, and the peaks of the highest mountains of the [[Cordillera de Talamanca]] (Talamanca Mountain Range) on the east. In the middle of the canton, the Río General, from which the canton's capital city takes its name, runs north to south.

== History ==
Pérez Zeledón was created on 9 October 1931 by decree 31.<ref name="dta2017" /> Originally with four initial districts: Ureña, El General, Daniel Flores, and Rivas, with Ureña being the capital.<ref name="History of Pérez Zeledón Canton">{{cite web|url=http://www.mpz.go.cr/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=85:historia-pz&catid=38|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110809095139/http://www.mpz.go.cr/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=85:historia-pz&catid=38|url-status=dead|archive-date=9 August 2011|title=Historia Pérez Zeledón|author=Administrator|website=Mpz.go.cr|access-date=18 September 2017}}</ref>

===Early Costa Rican Settlements===
Near the end of the 19th century, between 1870 and 1899, quite a few families lived in [[El General Valley|El General valley]]. The [[hamlet (place)|hamlet]]s ''([[Spanish language|Spanish]]: Caseríos)'' of El General, Palmares, Rivas, and Ureña were further populated from 1900 onward.<ref name="History of Pérez Zeledón Canton" />

The ''Caserío'' area of Ureña grew significantly. By 1911, during [[Ricardo Jiménez Oreamuno]]'s administration, the [[Mountain hut|Refuges]] of Ojo de Agua, El Cerro de la Muerte, and División were established. These refuges would further advance the growth of the region known today as Pérez Zeledón and El General Valley.<ref name="History of Pérez Zeledón Canton" />

===Costa Rican Civil War===
[[San Isidro de El General]], Pérez Zeledón's capital, was awarded the recognition of ''Ciudad Mártir'', regarding the civil war of 1948, as established by '''Decree Nº274'''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.elespiritudel48.org/docu/h110.htm|title=Decreto San Isidro de El General Ciudad Mártir – El Espíritu del 48|website=Elespiritudel48.org|access-date=18 September 2017}}</ref> This decree established official legal recognition of the city's participation in the civil war of 1948. A section of the decree states (in [[Spanish language|Spanish]]):

:''la Junta Fundadora de la Segunda República, apreciando el noble esfuerzo y la valiosa contribución de San Isidro de El General, se siente obligada a hacer un público y oficial reconocimiento de tan loable conducta para que de ninguna manera quede ella relegada al olvido, sino que constantemente sea exaltada como merecen los grandes hechos dignos de vida eterna en los fastos nacionales.''

In English, this would loosely translate into:

:'' The [[Founding Junta of the Second Republic]], appreciating the noble effort and valuable contribution of San Isidro de El General, feels compelled to make a public and official recognition of such praiseworthy conduct that by no means be it relegated to oblivion, but rather constantly be exalted as great deeds merit worth of eternal life in the national splendor.''

Pérez Zeledón was also the location of significant battles during the [[Costa Rican Civil War]]. The most notable battle was the Battle of San Isidro de El General. This battle was carried out at around 6:30&nbsp;a.m. when 200 government forces engaged the revolutionaries at the city's central plaza. The fight was won by [[José Figueres Ferrer]] and his revolutionary forces, which secured the southern Pacific region of Costa Rica in rebel hands.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.elespiritudel48.org/docu/h118.htm|title=Comunicado del Cuartel General del ELN – El Espíritu del 48|website=Elespiritudel48.org|access-date=18 September 2017}}</ref>

=== Municipal Council ===

==Economy==
[[File:Cerro Chirripó.jpg|thumbnail|left|[[Chirripó National Park]] 3820 meters above sea level]]
[[File:Picture 1191.jpg|thumbnail|left|Picture of [[Cerro Chirripó]]]]
The canton of Pérez Zeledón's economic development has been characterized mainly through agriculture, livestock, trade, industry, and tourism. One factor that has driven this economic development is the canton's unique geographic positioning, which makes it a sort of 'bridge' between [[Costa Rica|Costa Rica's]] capital ([[San José, Costa Rica|San José]]) and the southern areas of the country, especially those that border [[[Panama|Panamá]].

'''Important agricultural activities:'''

* [[Sugar Cane]] cultivation
* [[Coffee]]
* [[Xanthosoma sagittifolium|Tiquizque]] cultivation 
* [[Banana]] cultivation
* [[Blackberry]] cultivation

The first two products are the most important in the region.

Livestock activities are also economically relevant, with pigs and cattle being the most important. The latter is used for fattening and dairy.

Commercial activities have boomed in recent decades, mainly due to the establishment of [[credit unions]], banks, local companies, media, and various other companies of the Central Valley (San José) setting up operations in the canton.

===Tourism===
The area's main attraction is its ecological diversity. Tourist sites in the area include the [[Chirripó National Park]], one of the highest peaks in Central America and the Caribbean, at 3,820 meters above sea level.

Among other attractions are the journeys along the rapids.

Another prominent park in the area is the [[Parque Internacional La Amistad]], literally 'The Friendship International Park'.

==Education==
[[File:Escuela 12 de Marzo, Pérez Zeledón.JPG|thumbnail|12 de Marzo School, San Isidro de El General, Pérez Zeledón]]
The first [[elementary school]] in Pérez Zeledón was established during [[Ascensión Esquivel Ibarra|Ascensión Esquivel Ibarra's]] government (1902-1906). It was called '''Escuela Mixta de Ureña'''. It was in the Ureña district, south of [[San Isidro de El General|San Isidro de El General's]] central park. After the [[Costa Rican Civil War]], the school was moved to a new building 100 meters past San Isidro de El General's main entrance. It currently serves under the name ''"Escuela 12 de Marzo"'', in honor of the start of the Costa Rican Civil War (which had just ended when the school was re-inaugurated at its current location).<ref name="Escuela 12 de Marzo">{{cite web|url=http://www.circuito01pz.com/escuela-12-de-marzo-de-1948.html|title=Escuela 12 de Marzo de 1948 - Circuito 01 Pérez Zeledón|website=Circuito01pz.com|access-date=18 September 2017}}</ref>
