'''Roquetas de Mar''' ({{IPA|es|roˈketas de maɾ}}) is a [[municipality]] of [[Province of Almería|Almería province]], in the autonomous community of [[Andalucía]], Spain.<ref>{{cite book |last=McFee |first=James |title=City Maps Roquetas de Mar Spain |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=9nQ2DwAAQBAJ |publisher=Soffer Publishing |date=31 July 2017 |pages=24}}</ref> In 2016, the population was 91,965, the municipality with the second highest population in the province behind [[Almería|the capital]], the 14th in [[Andalusia|Andalucía]] and the 69th in [[Spain]].

In 1981, in this town, three young men from [[Santander, Spain|Santander]] were murdered by various members of the [[Civil Guard (Spain)|Spanish Civil Guard]], in what is known as [[Almería Case]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Emilio Pelayo |first=José |date=17 May 2016 |title=Caso Almería, aniversario de un horror |trans-title=Almería Case, anniversary of a horror |url=https://www.eldiariomontanes.es/cantabria/201605/17/caso-almeria-aniversario-horror-20160517104223.html |access-date=27 June 2023 |publisher=El Diario Montañés |language=es }}</ref>

== Geography and climate ==
Roquetas de Mar has a [[hot desert climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification|Köppen]]: ''BWh'') bordering on a [[hot semi-arid climate|hot semi-desert climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification|Köppen]]: ''BSh'')<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |title=Evolucion de los climas de Köppen en España en el periodo 1951-2020 |url=https://www.aemet.es/documentos/es/conocermas/recursos_en_linea/publicaciones_y_estudios/publicaciones/NT_37_AEMET/NT_37_AEMET.pdf |access-date=2024-05-18 |website=[[Agencia Estatal de Meteorologia]]}}</ref> with very mild winters and very hot and dry summers. During July and August, Roquetas de Mar has often southern winds coming from Africa, thus increasing the temperatures.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.diariodealmeria.es/opinion/tribuna/dichoso-vientecico-almeriense_0_1257774416.html|title=El dichoso vientecico almeriense|website=Diario de Almería|date=25 June 2018|access-date=24 June 2021|language=es}}</ref> Dust storms coming from the [[Sahara desert]] are uncommon, but they might happen from time to time.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.lavozdealmeria.com/noticia/12/almeria/101025/una-tormenta-de-arena-del-sahara-cubre-almeria-de-polvo-rojizo|title=Una tormenta de arena del Sáhara cubre Almería de polvo rojizo|website=La Voz de Almería|date=22 February 2016|access-date=24 June 2021|language=es}}</ref>

== Tourism ==
The 'urbanización' (tourist area with hotels and restaurants and shops) of Roquetas is predominantly a Spanish holiday resort for the majority of the summer months, but it also receives many visitors from the [[United Kingdom]], [[Ireland]] and [[Germany]]. It is one of the two major resorts in [[Costa de Almería]], the other being [[Mojácar]].

The 2018 padron data counted 94,956 inhabitants, 49,038 of which were men and 45,887 of which were women.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ine.es/jaxiT3/Tabla.htm?t=2857 |title=Almería: Población por municipios y sexo |website=www.ine.es|language=es|access-date=2019-01-26}}</ref> The population density is 1600/km<sup>2</sup>.

==Transport==
Roquetas is served by the [[Autovía A-7]] on its outskirts and is linked to the town itself by the A-1051. It is the second-largest town in Spain without a rail link behind [[Marbella]]; in 2020 a proposal was submitted to build a railway from [[Almería]] to [[Adra, Spain|Adra]] via Roquetas and [[El Ejido]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.diariodealmeria.es/provincia/mesa-ferrocarril-insiste-corredor-poniente_0_1470453297.html|title=La Mesa del Ferrocarril insiste con el corredor ferroviario del Poniente|website=Diario de Almería|date=3 June 2020|access-date=22 August 2020|language=es}}</ref>
