'''Kabal''' ([[Urdu]] and {{langx|ps|{{nq|کبل}}}}) is a town in [[Swat, Pakistan|Swat District]], [[Khyber Pakhtunkhwa|Khyber-Pakhtunkhwa]] province of  Pakistan located {{convert|10|km|mi|0}} from [[Mingora]] city. The old name of Kabal was ''Chendakhwara''. It is the [[List of cities in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa by population|tenth largest city in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa]] and [[Largest cities of Pakistan|82nd largest in Pakistan]] by population

Nearby settlements include the villages of [[Kotlai]] and [[Akhun Kalai]].

=== Civil unrest ===
After years of suffering and losses, the locals began to protest against the law enforcement agencies and local government resulting into violent riots and resistance from the government.

Following these incidents, Pashtun Tahaffuz Movement, a movement for the rights of Pashtun people in Pakistan, called protests in different areas of Swat, including Kabal Tehsil.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Khaliq |first=Fazal |date=2022-09-30 |title=‘We want peace’: Swat residents take to streets in Matta against wave of militancy |url=https://www.dawn.com/news/1712740 |access-date=2023-03-16 |website=DAWN.COM |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Reporter |first=The Newspaper's Staff |date=2018-03-27 |title=Hundreds respond to protest call by Pashtun movement |url=https://www.dawn.com/news/1397781 |access-date=2023-03-16 |website=DAWN.COM |language=en}}</ref>

Manzoor Pashteen, the PTM leader, stated that the movement's demands for peace and justice for Pashtuns in Pakistan were constitutional rights. He accused the government of denying these rights, particularly in the tribal areas, and criticized the use of force against PTM's peaceful protests.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Correspondent’ |first=Our |date=2023-03-14 |title=PTM chief says demanding peace our constitutional right |url=https://www.dawn.com/news/1742008 |access-date=2023-03-16 |website=DAWN.COM |language=en}}</ref>

Government considered their protests as violent and unlawful behavior against the state, and began to desist forcefully.<ref>{{Cite news |author= Shaheen Buneri|date=2018-04-26 |title=Pashtun Campaigners Complain Of Hurdles Ahead Of Protest In Pakistan |language=en |work=RFE/RL |url=https://gandhara.rferl.org/a/pashtun-campaigners-complain-of-hurdles-ahead-of-protest-in-pakistan/29194419.html |access-date=2023-03-16}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=PTM activists in Belgium stage protest against Pakistani soldiers firing on their leaders |url=https://www.aninews.in/news/world/europe/ptm-activists-in-belgium-stage-protest-against-pakistani-soldiers-firing-on-their-leaders20220214010501/ |access-date=2023-03-16 |website=ANI News |language=en}}</ref> PEMRA suspended the license of a private news channel due to their coverage of an opposition politician's press conference. The move was widely criticized by journalists and opposition parties as an attack on press freedom, and the channel's staff held protests against the suspension.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Khaliq |first=Fazal |date=2022-10-24 |title=Connecting the dots: What really went down in Swat |url=https://www.dawn.com/news/1715517 |access-date=2023-03-16 |website=DAWN.COM |language=en}}</ref> Two government officials were held in allegations of involvement with PTM.<ref>{{Cite news |date=2020-01-28 |title=Two Pakistani lawmakers held after protests by rights activists |language=en |work=Reuters |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-pakistan-arrest-activist-idUSKBN1ZR1UX |access-date=2023-03-16}}</ref>

=== Government efforts to stop terrorism ===
Police and paramilitary forces established security posts in the Matta and Kabal tehsils of Swat district to prevent terrorist activities. The posts were placed strategically, and their personnel were responsible for manning them. The government aimed to enhance the security situation in the region through this initiative.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Khaliq |first=Fazal |date=2022-09-21 |title=Security posts set up in Swat's Matta, Kabal tehsils |url=https://www.dawn.com/news/1711202 |access-date=2023-03-16 |website=DAWN.COM |language=en}}</ref>

The Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Chief Minister, Mahmood Khan, opened a police command and control centre in Swat to improve the security situation in the district. The centre helped the police and law enforcement agencies better handle security challenges and provide a more coordinated response to threats.<ref>{{Cite web |author=News Desk |date=2022-06-01 |title=CM inaugurates police Command, Control Centre in Swat |url=https://pakobserver.net/cm-inaugurates-police-command-control-centre-in-swat/ |access-date=2023-03-16 |website=Pakistan Observer |language=en-US}}</ref>

=== Qalagay Road ===
Government of Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf took necessary steps in order to maintain situation in those areas, and formulated plans for development projects to encourage local and non-Pakistani visitors for tourism.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2021-06-02 |title=Construction in Swat boosting tourism |url=https://dailytimes.com.pk/765904/construction-in-swat-boosting-tourism/ |access-date=2023-03-16 |website=Daily Times |language=en-US}}</ref>

The construction of roads, bridges, and hotels in Swat gave a boost to tourism in the region, as per local officials. The new infrastructure made it easier for visitors to explore the scenic valley, which was home to ancient Buddhist ruins and a rich cultural heritage.
