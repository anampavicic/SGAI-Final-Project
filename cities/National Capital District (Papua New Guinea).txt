'''The National Capital District''' of [[Papua New Guinea]] is the incorporated area around [[Port Moresby]], which is the capital of Papua New Guinea.  Although it is surrounded by [[Central Province (Papua New Guinea)|Central Province]], where Port Moresby is also the capital, it is technically not a part of that province. It covers an area of 240 km<sup>2</sup> and has a population of 364,125 (2011 census).<ref>{{cite book | title=Papua New Guinea 2000 Census: Western | publisher=National Statistical Office | pages=7}}</ref><ref name="nso2">{{cite web | url=https://www.nso.gov.pg/index.php/document-library?view=download&fileId=64 | title=Census Figures by Wards - Southern Region | publisher=National Population & Housing Census 2011 | access-date=31 March 2017}}</ref> It is represented by three open MPs and an NCD-wide representative in the [[National Parliament of Papua New Guinea]] who acts as Governor of the National Capital District; however, these MPs do not have the same powers as elsewhere in the country due to the role of the [[National Capital District Commission]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.onepng.com/2013/01/national-capital-district-port-moresby.html | title=National Capital District (Port Moresby) | publisher=One PNG | access-date=1 April 2017}}</ref>

==Governors==

The National Capital District did not have a decentralised administration with a Premier, as with the other provinces, prior to 1995; however, since the 1995 reforms of provincial governments it has been headed by a Governor, albeit one with more limited powers.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.rulers.org/pngprov.html | title=Provinces | publisher=rulers.org | access-date=31 March 2017}}</ref>
