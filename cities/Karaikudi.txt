'''Karaikudi''' is the [[List of cities in Tamil Nadu by population|largest city]] in [[Sivaganga district]] in the Indian [[States and territories of India|state]] of [[Tamil Nadu]]. '''[[Karaikudi Metropolitan Area|Karaikudi Urban Agglomoration]]''' is the 21st largest urban area of Tamil Nadu based on 2011 census data. It is part of the area commonly referred to as "[[Chettinad]]" and has been declared a City Municipal Corporation by the [[Government of Tamil Nadu]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Government Order on Heritage Towns|url=http://www.tn.gov.in/dtcp/gos/go_191_1994.pdf|publisher= Directorate of Town and Country Planning, Government of Tamil Nadu, India|access-date=31 July 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Service |first1=Express News |title=CM Stalin announces creation of 4 new corporations in TN, total goes up to 25 |url=https://www.newindianexpress.com/states/tamil-nadu/2024/Mar/16/cm-stalin-announces-creation-of-4-new-corporations-in-tn-total-goes-up-to-25 |access-date=29 April 2024 |work=The New Indian Express |date=16 March 2024 |location=Chennai |language=en}}</ref> on account of the palatial houses built with [[limestone]] called ''Kaarai veedu''. For this reason, the place is sometimes called '''Kaarai Nagar''' locally. The newly formed  '''[[Karaikudi Municipal Corporation]]''' spreads about {{convert|94.31|sqkm|sqmi|abbr=on}} which undergoes major developments as part of Government Schemes.<ref>{{cite web |title=New updates from Government of Tamilnadu for Karaikudi LPA |url=chrome-extension://efaidnbmnnnibpcajpcglclefindmkaj/https://karaikudilpa.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/VOL-2-FINAL-COPY-ADOBE-EDITEDorganized-KARAIKUDI.pdf}}</ref>

Karaikudi comes under the [[Karaikudi Assembly constituency]], which elects a member to the [[Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly]] once every five years, and it is a part of the [[Sivaganga Lok Sabha constituency]], which elects its member of parliament (MP) once in five years. The town is administered by the greater Karaikudi Municipal corporation. This metropolitan city had a population of 303,291 in 2023.  This metropolitan area consists of Karaikudi City Corporation which includes its neighbourhood towns like [[Kottaiyur]], [[Koviloor, Karaikudi|Koviloor]], [[Kandanur]], [[Puduvayal]], and [[Sankarapuram]] (census town) and villages like [[Amaravathiputhur]], [[Ariyakudi]], Iluppakkudi, Managiri, Mathur, Peyampatti, Thiruvelangudi and Soorakudi.
[[File:Karaikudi Corporation.jpg|thumb]]

This city is famous for [[Kandangi|Karaikudi Kandangi sarees]] a special type of sarees which come from and are woven in this area. The [[geographical indication]] presents the government of India of this Kandangi sarees.<ref>{{Cite web|date=November 12, 2019|first=Sruthi|last=Raman|title=TN's Kandangi sari gets GI tag. What does this mean for the state's handloom market?|url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/chennai/tns-kandangi-sari-gets-gi-tag-what-does-this-mean-for-the-states-handloom-market/articleshow/71092224.cms|access-date=2020-11-25|website=The Times of India|language=en}}</ref>&nbsp;

The [[Chettinad cuisine]] originated in the areas around Karaikudi.

It is one of the few towns and cities in [[List of AMRUT Smart cities in Tamil Nadu]] selected for AMRUT Schemes <ref>{{cite web | url=http://tufidco.in/amrut.aspx | title=Tufidco }}</ref> from central government and the developmental activities are taken care by the government of Tamil Nadu.<ref name="tcp.tn.gov.in">{{Cite web|url=https://tcp.tn.gov.in/amrutscheme|title=Directorate of Town and Country Planning, Government of Tamil Nadu|website=tcp.tn.gov.in}}</ref>

==History==
[[File:Kanadukathan Palace Chettinad Architecture near Karaikudi.jpg|left|thumb|Kanadukathan Palace Chettinad Architecture near Karaikudi]]
The city derives its name from the thorny plant ''[[Canthium coromandelicum|Karai]],'' referred to in ancient literature as ''Karaikudi'', which in modern times has become ''Karaikudi''. The town was established in the 19th century, and the oldest known structure is the Koppudaiya Nayagi Amman Temple.<ref name="history">{{cite web |title= Historical Moments |url= http://municipality.tn.gov.in/karaikudi/abcity_Historical%20Moments.htm |publisher= Karaikudi Municipality |year=2011 |access-date= 2013-08-08}}</ref> [[Mahatma Gandhi]] delivered two speeches in Karaikudi in 1927<ref>{{cite web |title= Mahatma Gandhis Message on Khadi |url= http://www.mkgandhi.org/gandhionkhadi/messageofkhadi.htm |publisher= Bombay Sarvodaya Mandal / Gandhi Book Centre, 299, Tardeo Road, Nana Chowk, Bombay 400007 India |access-date= 23 July 2013 }}</ref><ref>Pushpa Joshi (2002) Gandhi on Women: Collection of Mahatma Gandhi's Writings and Speeches on Women, Navajivan Publishing House, {{ISBN|8172293143}}</ref> and [[Bharathiyar]] visited Karaikudi in 1919 to participate in an event.<ref>Malarmannan article on Madhabhimana Sangam in Thinnai e-zine, 17 Jan 2011, [http://www.thinnai.com/index.php?module=displaystory&story_id=21101175&format=html]</ref> After independence, the city saw significant growth in the industrial sector.<ref name="history"/> Karaikudi and surrounding areas are generally referred as "Chettinad".<ref>{{cite book|title=Government of Burma 1930a, Report of the Burma Provincial Banking Enquiry Committee, 1929-30, Volume I: Banking and Credit in Burma, Rangoon|publisher= Superintendent of Government Printing|year=1930}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|first=Siegleman|last= P.|year= 1962|title= Colonial Development and the Chettyar: A Study in the Ecology of Modern Burma, 1850-1941, unpublished doctoral dissertation|publisher= University of Minnesota}}</ref> Chettinad comprises a network of 73 villages and 2 towns forming clusters spread over a territory of 1,550 km<Sup>2</sup> in the districts of Sivagangai and Pudukottai in the state of Tamil Nadu. Karaikudi is the largest town in Sivaganga district. Karaikudi and surrounding areas are very popular for their unique palaces, Chettinad Architecture based buildings and palatial mansions. Kannadasan Manimandapam, Kamban Manimandapam and Thousand windows house are other visitor attractions in Karaikudi.<ref>{{cite web|year=2011|title=Places of interest in Karaikudi|url=http://municipality.tn.gov.in/karaikudi/abcity_Place%20of%20interest.htm|access-date=2013-08-08|publisher=Karaikudi Municipality}}</ref>

The first [[Tamil Thai|Temple for Mother Tamil]], Tamil Thai Kovil is located in Karaikudi and was established in 1993.<ref>{{cite news|title=Dinamalar Jan 14, 2012|newspaper=Dinamalar|url=http://www.dinamalar.com/News_Detail.asp?Id=385854&Print=1|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140226134614/http://www.dinamalar.com/News_Detail.asp?Id=385854&Print=1|url-status=dead|archive-date=26 February 2014|access-date=31 July 2013}}</ref>

== Demographics ==
As calculated for the town extension processes, the district administration office confirmed the population of Karaikudi is 303,291 in 2023.<ref>{{cite web |title=Added population as per new portal created by government |url=chrome-extension://efaidnbmnnnibpcajpcglclefindmkaj/https://karaikudilpa.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/VOL-2-FINAL-COPY-ADOBE-EDITEDorganized-KARAIKUDI.pdf}}</ref> According to [[2011 census of India|2011 census]], Karaikkudi had a population of 181,125 with a sex-ratio of 1,000 females for every 1,000 males, much above the national average of 929.<ref name="dashboard">{{cite web|title=Census Info 2011 Final population totals|url=http://www.censusindia.gov.in/2011census/censusinfodashboard/index.html|publisher=Office of The Registrar General and Census Commissioner, Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India|year=2013|access-date=26 January 2014}}</ref> A total of 19,619 children were under the age of six, constituting 10,405 males and 10,214 females. The average literacy rate of the town was 81.48%, compared to the national average of 72.99%.<ref name="dashboard"/> The town had a total of 47504 households. There were a total of 60,069 workers, comprising 244 cultivators, 314 main agricultural labourers, 1,303 in household industries, 30,836 other workers, 17,372 marginal workers, 162 marginal cultivators, 2496 marginal agricultural labourers, 1345 marginal workers in household industries and 9,469 other marginal workers.<ref name="2011census">{{cite web|url=https://www.census2011.co.in/census/metropolitan/455-karaikkudi.html|title=Census Info 2011 Final population totals - Karaikkudi|year=2013|publisher=Office of The Registrar General and Census Commissioner, Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India|access-date=26 January 2014}}</ref>   In Karaikudi, Hinduism is the majority religion with 83.9% of the total population, followed by Islam, 11.39%, Christianity, 4.3%, Jainism, 0.05%, Sikism, 0.01% and Others, the rest.

Karaikudi is located in Sivagangai district of Tamil Nadu State. The [[Trichy]]–[[Rameswaram]] [[National Highway 210 (India)|Highway]] passes through Karaikudi. The [[Thennar River]] flows through South Karaikudi. Karaikudi is located at {{Coord|10.07|N| 78.78|E|}}. It has an average elevation of {{convert|82|m}}. The terrain of Karaikudi is predominantly flat.  Rocky areas are found in the surrounding areas of Karaikudi town with more rocks towards the western side of the town. The soil is of the hard red lateritic type and is not suitable for cultivation.<ref name="Karaikudi Municipality">{{cite web|title=Karaikudi Municipal Council|url=http://municipality.tn.gov.in/karaikudi/#|work=General info on the KMC|publisher=Department Of Municipal Administration And Water Supply|access-date=20 July 2011}}</ref> The [[water table]] in the area is generally at depths of {{cvt|3|to|6|ft}} and rises to nearly {{cvt|1|m}} below the ground level during rainy seasons. Since the 1970s, the water supply of the Karaikudi residents has depended on the deeper aquifers.<ref name="Karaikudi Municipality"/> The average maximum temperature is about {{cvt|34|°C}}, and average minimum temperature is about {{cvt|24|°C}}. The annual average rainfall in Karaikudi is about {{convert|920|mm |abbr=off}}.

* AAVIN Karaikudi Dairy in Sivaganga Union is supplying milk and milk products to most of the southern districts commercial and residential needs by collecting sources from Sivaganga and Ramanathapuram Districts. It is operating since 1983.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Unions - Tamilnadu Co-operative Milk Producers' Federation Limited |url=https://aavin.tn.gov.in/unions/-/categories/57440 |access-date=2024-04-12 |website=aavin.tn.gov.in}}</ref>
* Karaikudi has the Postal [[Sorting office|Sorting Office]] and serves as the Headquarters of the Postal [[Postal Index Number|Sorting District]] of the Madurai Sub-Zonal postal region since 1972, so the entire postage received for both Sivaganga district & Ramanathapuram district will be received & handled here from [[Madurai]] and then dispatched to the rest of areas in the postal circle. It also serving as the Postal Intra Circle Hub (ICH) of Sivaganga and Ramanathapuram Districts.

==Transport==
===By Air===
Nearby airports include [[Tiruchirapalli Airport|Tiruchirapalli International Airport]] ({{convert|95|km|mi}}) and [[Madurai Airport]] ({{convert|100|km|mi}}).

Though this city is well connected by [[roadways]] and [[railways]]. This town also has an unused airstrip called [[Chettinad Airport|Chettinad airport]] also known as Karaikudi airport. This old British era airfield had been used during the [[World War II|world war 2]]. The [[government of India]] is planning to reopen this airfield under the [[UDAN|UDAN scheme]].<ref>{{Cite news|date=2017-02-27|title=Chettinad Group ready to support efforts for mini airport|language=en-IN|work=The Hindu|url=https://www.thehindu.com/news/cities/Madurai/chettinad/article17378317.ece|access-date=2020-11-25|issn=0971-751X}}</ref>

===By Rail===
Karaikudi is well connected by railway network through [[Karaikudi Junction]] railway station, It is a major rail head serves the city and it has the suburban places around the city.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://southernrailway.gov.in/sr/passing.jsp/|title=Karaikudi Railway|access-date=25 September 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100410144604/http://southernrailway.gov.in/sr/passing.jsp/|archive-date=10 April 2010|url-status=dead}}</ref> Complete list of railway stations in Karaikudi city limits can be found in [[Railway stations in Karaikudi| Suburban Stations]] with the station code details.

===By Road===
The town has two bus terminals namely "Rajaji Bus-stand" aka [[Old Bus terminal]] and [[new bus terminal]]. Buses that connect the Karaikudi city, nearby villages and smaller towns terminate at the Old bus-stand. The State Transport Corporation Buses To:Chennai, Bangalore, Coimbatore, Madurai, Trichy and near by cities run moffusil buses terminate at the New bus-stand [[Karaikudi Bus Terminal]].{{Citation needed|date=November 2021}}

==Education==
It serves as one of the two headquarters of the Educational District in Sivaganga district. As of 2011, there were government schools and many private schools in Karaikudi and Alagappa university colleges, many private colleges in the city.<ref>{{cite web|title=Educational Institutions|url=http://municipality.tn.gov.in/karaikudi/srv_edu.htm|publisher=Karaikudi municipality|access-date=2012-12-29|year=2011}}</ref> [[Alagappa University]] is located in this town and [[Alagappa Chettiar College of Engineering and Technology]] is the oldest college here and was established in 1953.<ref>{{cite web|title=Karaikudi|url=http://www.accet.in/history.htm|publisher=Alagappa Chettiar College of Engineering and Technology|access-date=30 July 2013}}</ref>  [[Alagappa Government Arts College]] and [[Alagappa Polytechnic]] College are located in Karaikudi area.

Many educational institutions in the town benefited from donations made by the businessman and philanthropist [[Alagappa Chettiar]], who attended Sri Meenakshi Sundareswarar Vidyalaya.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Rajesh |first=Govindarajulu |title=Life of Dr. Alagappa Chettiar ( 1909 - 1957 ) - The Verandah Club |url=https://theverandahclub.com/article/life-of-dr-alagappa-chettiar-1909-1957-685 |access-date=2024-01-20 |website=theverandahclub.com |language=en}}</ref> The school, on Sivan Koil Street, dates back to 1913 and is now SMSV Higher Secondary School for Boys. Some of the known institutions in this area can be referred in [[List of educational institutions in Karaikudi]].

==Utility services==
Electricity supply to Karaikudi is regulated and distributed by the [[Tamil Nadu Electricity Board]] (TNEB). The town along with its suburbs forms the Sivaganga Electricity Distribution Circle.<ref>{{cite web|title=Important Address|url=http://www.windpro.org/pdf/MISC/TNEB%20Address.pdf|publisher=Indian Wind Power Association|access-date=2012-12-29|year=2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121016045943/http://www.windpro.org/pdf/MISC/TNEB%20Address.pdf|archive-date=16 October 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> Water supply is provided by the Karaikudi Municipality from seven borewells is located at Sambai Oothu ([[Aquifer|Artesian aquifer]]).<ref>{{cite web|title=Water Supply Details|url=http://municipality.tn.gov.in/karaikudi/sal_watersupply.htm|publisher=Karaikudi Municipality |year=2011|access-date=2012-12-29}}</ref> As per the municipal data for 2011, about 45 metric tonnes of solid waste were collected. The underground drainage system in the city is on board at process and the sewerage system for disposal of sullage is through septic tanks, open drains and public conveniences.<ref>{{cite web|title=Waste management programme|url=http://municipality.tn.gov.in/karaikudi/sal_solidwastemanagement.htm|publisher=Karaikudi Municipality|access-date=2012-12-29|year=2011}}</ref> The municipality maintained a total of {{convert|46.55|km|mi|abbr=on}} of storm water drains in 2011.<ref>{{cite web|title=Storm water drains|url=http://municipality.tn.gov.in/karaikudi/sal_stromwaterdrainage.htm|publisher=Karaikudi Municipality|access-date=2012-12-29|year=2011}}</ref> As of 2011, there was one government hospital and 13 private hospitals in the town.<ref>{{cite web|title=Hospitals in Karaikudi|url=http://municipality.tn.gov.in/karaikudi/srv_hospitals.htm|publisher=Karaikudi Municipality|access-date=2012-12-29|year=2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Karaikudi street lights|url=http://municipality.tn.gov.in/karaikudi/sal_streetlight.htm|publisher=Karaikudi Municipality, Government of Tamil Nadu|year=2011|access-date=2012-12-29 }}</ref> The municipality operates one daily market namely the Anna Daily Market and there are three weekly markets (Uzhavar Santhai) that cater to the needs of the town and the rural areas around it.<ref>{{cite web|title=Karaikudi markets|url=http://municipality.tn.gov.in/karaikudi/sal_markets.htm|publisher=Karaikudi Municipality, Government of Tamil Nadu|year=2011|access-date=2012-12-29 }}</ref>
