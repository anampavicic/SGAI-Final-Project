'''Kislovodsk''' ({{langx|ru|Кислово́дск}}; {{Langx|kbd|Нартсанэ}};<ref>{{Cite web|title=Нартсанэ - это... Что такое Нартсанэ?|url=https://ady_rus_qarden.academic.ru/19182/%D0%9D%D0%B0%D1%80%D1%82%D1%81%D0%B0%D0%BD%D1%8D|access-date=2021-03-01|website=Словари и энциклопедии на Академике|language=ru}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Нартсанэ in English - Kabardian-English Dictionary {{!}} Glosbe|url=https://en.glosbe.com/kbd/en/%D0%9D%D0%B0%D1%80%D1%82%D1%81%D0%B0%D0%BD%D1%8D|access-date=2021-03-01|website=en.glosbe.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Нартсанэ - старое черкесское названое современного Кисловодска|url=https://www.adygi.ru/index.php?newsid=20057|access-date=2021-03-01|website=Адыги.RU - Новости Адыгеи, история, культура и традиции адыгов (черкесов)|language=ru}}</ref> {{langx|krc|Ачысуу}}<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Салпагаров|first=Ахмад|title=Тюркские названия северо-кавказских городов|url=https://www.academia.edu/25919615|language=en}}</ref>) is a [[spa town|spa]] [[types of inhabited localities in Russia|city]] in [[Stavropol Krai]], in the [[North Caucasus]] region of Russia which is located between the [[Black Sea|Black]] and [[Caspian Sea]]s. It is part of the [[Caucasian Mineral Waters]] region. 

Population: {{ru-census|p2010=128,553|p2002=129,788|p1989=114,414}}

==Etymology==
The [[Russian language | Russian-language]] name of the city translates as "sour water" and originated due to the abundance of ''{{ill|narzan|ru|нарзан}}'' [[mineral water | mineral-water]] ({{langx |ru| нарзан | translit = narzan}}) springs in the area.<ref>{{cite book |last1= Pospelov |first1= Evgenij Mikhailovich |title= Geografičeskie nazvanija Rossii : toponimičeskij slovarʹ; bolee 4000 nazvanij geografičeskich obʺektov Rossii |date= 2008 |publisher= AST |location= Moskva | script-title = ru:Географические названия России: топонимический словарь : более 4,000 названий географических объектов России |isbn= 978-5-17-054966-5 |pages= 523 |edition= Spravočnoe izd | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=mqKTQQAACAAJ}}
</ref>{{qn|date=June 2023}}

==History==
The settlement gained town status in 1903.{{citation needed|date=May 2010}} Several of the events in [[Mikhail Lermontov]]'s 1840 novel ''[[A Hero of Our Time]]'' take place in Kislovodsk.

==Archaeology==
Numerous settlements of the [[Koban culture]] (ca. 1100 to 400 BC) are found in the Kislovodsk city and its surroundings.<ref>Sabine REINHOLD, Dmitri S. KOROBOV, [http://www2.muse.it/pubblicazioni/7/42/13_Reinhold.pdf ''The Kislovodsk basin in the North Caucasian piedmonts – archaeology and GIS studies in a mountain cultural landscape.''] 2007</ref> They include the sites of Industria I, Sultan-gora I, Berezovka I, Berezovka II, Berezovka III, Berezovka IV.

==Administrative and municipal status==
Within the [[subdivisions of Russia#Administrative divisions|framework of administrative divisions]], it is, together with seven [[types of inhabited localities in Russia|rural localities]], incorporated as the '''[[city of federal subject significance|city of krai significance]] of Kislovodsk'''—an administrative unit with the status equal to that of the [[administrative divisions of Stavropol Krai|districts]].<ref name="Ref372">Resolution #63-p</ref> As a [[subdivisions of Russia#Municipal divisions|municipal division]], the city of krai significance of Kislovodsk is incorporated as '''Kislovodsk Urban Okrug'''.<ref name="Ref893">Law #88-kz</ref>

==Geography==
The city is located in the North Caucasus region of Russia which is located between the Black and Caspian Seas.

==Notable people==
[[Nobel Prize]] winner [[Aleksandr Solzhenitsyn]] (1918-2008) was a native of Kislovodsk. A museum was planned in the house he was born. Renovations were to begin in 2011. [[Nikolai Yaroshenko]]'s (1846-1898) memorial house is open to the public.

The Ukrainian historian [[Mykhailo Hrushevskyi]] (1866-1934) died when on exile to Kislovodsk under circumstances which remain mysterious and controversial.

*[[Zuhra Bayramkulova]] (1940–2013), farmer and politician, was born there.
*[[Felix Feodosidi]] (born 1933), wine maker.
*[[Boris Parsadanian]], composer, was born there.
*[[Karine Shadoyan]], wrestler born in Kislovodsk but represented Armenia. Bronze medal winner at the European Championships.
