The '''Region of Maradi''' is one of seven [[Regions of Niger|regions]] of [[Niger]].  It is located in south-central Niger, east of the Region of [[Tahoua Region|Tahoua]], west of [[Zinder Region|Zinder]], and north of the Nigerian city of Katsina. The administrative centre is at [[Maradi, Niger|Maradi]]. The population of the Region is predominantly [[Hausa people|Hausa]].

== History ==
In 2021, a [[Niger gold mine collapse|large gold mine collapse]] in the region killed 18 miners.<ref>{{Cite news|date=2021-11-08|title=At least 18 killed in artisanal gold mine collapse in Niger|language=en|work=Reuters|url=https://www.reuters.com/world/africa/least-18-killed-artisanal-gold-mine-collapse-niger-2021-11-08/|access-date=2021-12-22}}</ref>

==Geography==
Maradi borders [[Agadez Region]] to the north, [[Zinder Region]] to the east, [[Nigeria]] to the south (specifically the states of [[Katsina State|Katsina]], [[Zamfara State|Zamfara]] and [[Sokoto State|Sokoto]]), and [[Tahoua Region]] to the west. Most of the 35,100&nbsp;km²<ref name="Decalo1997">Samuel Decalo, ''Historical Dictionary of Niger'' (3rd ed.). Scarecrow Press, Boston & Folkestone, (1997) {{ISBN|0-8108-3136-8}} pp.209–210</ref> of land is classified as "[[Sahel]]", though the northern parts merge into the [[Sahara]] desert, and the very southern edges along the border with Nigeria get almost 600&nbsp;mm a year in average rainfall, with some areas receiving as much as 650–700&nbsp;mm in better years.<ref name="Decalo1997" /> [[Lake Madarounfa]] lies to the south of Maradi, into which flow the seasonal [[Goulbi N'Gabi]] and [[Goulbi N'Maradi]] rivers.<ref name="Bradt"/>

==Economy==
The [[Niger-Nigeria border]] dips south below the Region's capital, forming an area sometimes called the "breadbasket" of Niger.{{citation needed|date=October 2019}} While [[tobacco]], [[mangoes]], [[wheat]], [[soy beans]] and even [[cotton]] are cultivated in some areas, the predominant crops are [[Bambara groundnut|groundnuts]], grown as a commercially, and [[millet]], [[sorghum]] and [[cow peas]], typically grown for domestic consumption.<ref name="Bradt">Geels, Jolijn, (2006) ''Bradt Travel Guide - Niger'', pgs. 203-212</ref>

==Demographics==
[[File:Hausa women near Maradi (2).jpg|thumb|Hausa women in a village]]

Maradi is most densely populated region of Niger,<ref name="Decalo1997" /> with a population of 3,117,810 as of 2011.<ref name="Annuaire statistique du Niger">[http://www.stat-niger.org/statistique/file/Annuaires_Statistiques/Annuaire_ins_2011/population.pdf Annuaire statistique du Niger]</ref> Culturally, Maradi Region forms the west central section of Nigerien [[Hausaland]], which extends along the Nigerian border west to [[Dogondoutchi]] and east to just beyond [[Zinder]]. Other groups include the [[Fula people]] (including the [[Wodaabe]], who predominate to the north and east of the region), and [[Tuareg people|Tuareg]] peoples, especially in the north of the region and along major trade routes.<ref name="Languages of Niger">{{cite web | url = https://www.ethnologue.com/country/ne/languages| title = Languages of Niger | publisher = [[Ethnologue]] | accessdate = 24 October 2019}}</ref> The [[Tagdal]] language, thought to be a mixed [[Songhai languages|Songhay]]-Tuareg language, is also spoken.<ref name="Languages of Niger"/>{{Historical populations|1977|949,747|1985|1,389,433|2001|2,235,570|2012|3,402,094|align=none|footnote=source:<ref>[http://www.citypopulation.de/en/niger/cities/ Niger: Administrative Division population statistics]</ref>}}
