'''Central Kalimantan''' ({{langx|id|Kalimantan Tengah}}) is a [[provinces of Indonesia|province of Indonesia]]. It is one of five provinces in [[Kalimantan]], the Indonesian part of [[Borneo]]. It is bordered by [[West Kalimantan]] to the west, [[South Kalimantan]] and [[East Kalimantan]] to the east, [[Java Sea]] to the south and is separated narrowly from [[North Kalimantan]] and [[Malaysia]] by East Kalimantan's [[Mahakam Ulu Regency]]. Its provincial capital is [[Palangka Raya]] and in 2010 its population was over 2.2 million,<ref name="Biro Pusat Statistik 2011">Biro Pusat Statistik, Jakarta, 2011.</ref> while the 2020 Census showed a total of almost 2.67 million;<ref name="Badan Pusat Statistik 2021">Badan Pusat Statistik, Jakarta, 2021.</ref> the official estimate as at mid 2023 was 2,774,747 (comprising 1,434,119 males and 1,339,628 females), and was projected to rise to 2,809,700 at mid 2024.<ref name="Badan Pusat Statistik 2024">Badan Pusat Statistik, Jakarta, 28 February 2024, ''Provinsi Kalimantan Tengah Dalam Angka 2024'' (Katalog-BPS 1102001.62)</ref>

The population growth rate was almost 3.0% per annum between 1990 and 2000, one of the highest provincial growth rates in Indonesia during that time; in the subsequent decade to 2010 the average annual growth rate slowed markedly to around 1.8%, but it rose again in the decade beginning 2010. More than is the case in other province in the region, Central Kalimantan is populated by the [[Dayak people|Dayaks]], the indigenous inhabitants of Borneo.

== History ==
Since the eighteenth century the central region of Kalimantan and its Dayak inhabitants were ruled by the Muslim [[Sultanate of Banjar]]. Following Indonesian independence after [[World War II]], Dayak tribes demanded a province separate from South Kalimantan province.<ref>{{cite book
|title= Profile Central Kalimantan Province
|publisher= Central Kalimantan Province Tourism and Culture Board
|date=September 2001 }}</ref>

In 1957 [[South Kalimantan]] was divided to provide the Dayak population with greater autonomy from the [[Muslim]] population in the province. The change was approved by the Indonesian Government on 23 May 1957 under Presidential Law No. 10 Year 1957, which declared Central Kalimantan the seventeenth province of Indonesia. President Sukarno appointed the Dayak-born national hero [[Tjilik Riwut]] as the first Governor and [[Palangkaraya]] the provincial capital.<ref>{{Cite book |last=Riwut |first=Nila |title=Maneser Panatau Tatu Huang |date=2003 |publisher=Pusaka Lima |isbn=979-97999-1-0 |location=Palangkaraya |display-authors=etal}}</ref>

== Geography ==
Central Kalimantan is the largest Indonesian province by area ([[Papua (province)|Papua]] held this position until the 2022 partition) with a size of {{cvt|153443.91|km2}}, about 1.5 times the size of the island of [[Java]] and it is slightly larger than [[Bangladesh]] or the U.S. state of [[Illinois]]. It is bordered by [[West Kalimantan]] and [[East Kalimantan]] provinces to the north, by the [[Java Sea]] to the south, by [[South Kalimantan]] and East Kalimantan provinces to the east, and by West Kalimantan province to west.

The [[Schwaner Mountain]]s stretch from the north-east of the province to the south-west, 80% of which is covered in dense forest, [[Borneo peat swamp forests|peatland swamps]], [[mangrove]]s, rivers and traditional agriculture land. Highland areas in the north-east are remote and not easily accessible. Non-volcanic mounts are scattered in this area including [[Kengkabang]], [[Samiajang]], [[Liang Pahang]] and [[Ulu Gedang]].

The centre of the province is covered with tropical forest, which produces [[rattan]], [[resin]] and valuable timber such as [[Ulin]] and [[Shorea|Meranti]]. The southern lowlands are dominated by peatland swamps that intersect with many rivers. [[Sabangau National Park]] is a protected peatland area internationally acknowledged as sanctuary for the endangered [[Orangutan]]. Recently the peat swamp forests have been damaged by the [[Mega Rice Project (Kalimantan)|Mega Rice Project]], which unsuccessfully sought to turn large areas into [[rice paddies]].

The province's climate is [[tropical rainforest climate|wet weather]] equatorial zone with an eight-month rainy season, and 4 months of dry season. Rainfall or precipitation is 2,776—3,393&nbsp;mm per year with an average of 145 rainy days annually.{{Citation needed|date=August 2008}}

=== Rivers ===
Central Kalimantan has numerous rivers from the catchment areas to the north in the [[Schwaner Mountains]], flowing to the [[Java Sea]]. The major rivers include:
* [[Barito River]] (900&nbsp;km)
* [[Kapuas River (Barito River)|Kapuas River]] (600&nbsp;km)
* [[Kahayan River]] (600&nbsp;km)
* [[Mendawai River|Katingan River]] (600&nbsp;km)
* [[Sampit River (Indonesia)|Mentaya (Sampit) River]] (400&nbsp;km)
* [[Pembuang River|Seruyan River]] (350&nbsp;km)
* [[Lamandau River]] (300&nbsp;km)
* [[Arut River]] (250&nbsp;km)
* [[Sabangau River]] (200&nbsp;km)
* [[Kumai River]] (179&nbsp;km)
* [[Jelai-Bila River|Jelai River]] (100&nbsp;km)

Rivers are an important mode of [[river transport|transportation]] and a primary location for settlement. With relatively undeveloped infrastructure, the province's economy relies heavily on the rivers.{{Citation needed|date=August 2008}}

== Ecology ==
Based upon the research of the Indonesian Ministry of Environment and Forestry, Central Kalimantan has approximately 10 different ecological regions and is dominated by two main ecological regions. Those two are peat ecological region which covered 26% of the total area of Central Kalimantan and the fluvial plain ecoregion which covered 21% of the whole area of Central Kalimantan.<ref name="ecoregion">{{cite web | url = http://182.253.224.163/assets/lampiran/DIKPLHD%20Kalteng.pdf | title = Informasi Kinerja Lingkungan Hidup Tahun 2019 | lang = id | date = 2020 | accessdate = 17 May 2023 | publisher = Environmental Services of Central Kalimantan}}</ref> Due to its high concentration of peat ecological region, Central Kalimantan is prone to drought and peat fires during the dry season and inundation during the rainy season within each episodes of the [[El Niño–Southern Oscillation|ENSO]] and the [[Indian Ocean Dipole|IOD]]. These situations worsen because of massive deforestation in the region and climate change.

Central Kalimantan is also home for many endemic floras and faunas like [[orangutan]]s, [[proboscis monkey]]s, [[hornbill]] birds, [[rattan]], [[Eusideroxylon|Bornean iron wood]], etc. This province currently has three national parks i.e. [[Sabangau National Park]], [[Tanjung Puting National Park]], and [[Bukit Baka Bukit Raya National Park]] which are home to the endemic floras and faunas of Kalimantan.<ref name="ecoregion"/>

== Government and administrative divisions ==
Central Kalimantan Province, when separated from South Kalimantan in 1958, comprised three [[Regencies of Indonesia|regencies]] (''kabupaten'') - '''Barito''', '''Kapuas''' and '''Kotawaringin'''. However on 26 June 1959 two of these were split - Barito Regency was divided into a '''South Barito''' Regency and a '''North Barito''' Regency, while Kotawaringin Regency was divided into a '''West Kotawaringin''' Regency and an '''East Kotawaringin''' Regency. On 14 June 1965 the provincial capital of '''[[Palangka Raya]]''' was split off from Kapuas Regency to form an independent city (''kotamadya''). 

On 10 April 2002 an additional eight regencies were created by splitting existing regencies - '''Sukamara''' and '''Lamandau''' from parts of West Kotawaringin Regency, '''Seruyan''' and '''Katingan''' from parts of East Kotawaringin Regency, '''Pulang Pisau''' and '''Gunung Mas''' from parts of Kapuas Regency, '''East Barito''' from part of South Barito Regency, and '''Murung Raya''' from part of North Barito Regency. Thus the province now is administratively divided into thirteen regencies (each headed by a regent) and the single city. In addition to the civil service, Central Kalimantan also recognises a traditional governing system led by traditional leaders known as ''Damang''. The province is divided into 67 traditional law areas known as ''Kedamangan'', headed by ''Damang''. The system is intended to culturally recognise and preserve the customs and heritage of the Dayak tribes.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://peraturan.bpk.go.id/Home/Details/89276/perda-prov-kalimantan-tengah-no-16-tahun-2008|title= PERDA Prov. Kalimantan Tengah No. 16 Tahun 2008 (Regional Regulation of Central Kalimantan Province No. 16 Of 2008)|lang=id|publisher=Provincial Government of Central Kalimantan|accessdate=8 August 2023|date=December 2008}}</ref>

The province now forms one of Indonesia's 84 national electoral districts to elect members to the [[Indonesian Parliament|People's Representative Council]]. The '''Central Kalimantan Electoral District''' consists of all of the 13 regencies in the province, together with the city of Palangkaraya, and elects 6 members to the People's Representative Council.<ref>Law No. 7/2017 (''UU No. 7 Tahun 2017'') as amended by Government Regulation in Lieu of Law No. 1/2022 and Regulation of General Elections Commission No. 6/2023.</ref>

Central Kalimantan is predominantly inhabited by the Dayak people that would further be divided into three major Dayak sub-ethnics. The three major Dayak tribes in Central Kalimantan are the [[Ngaju people|Ngaju]], Ot Danum and Dusun [[Ma'anyan people|Ma'anyan]] Ot Siang. The three major tribes extended into several branches of prominent Dayak tribes in Central Kalimantan such as [[Lawangan people|Lawangan]], Taboyan, Dusun Siang, Boyan, Bantian, Dohoi and Kadori.

In addition to the indigenous Dayak tribes, there are also ethnic groups from other areas of Indonesia, including [[Malays (ethnic group)|Malays]], [[Javanese people|Javanese]], [[Madurese people|Madurese]], [[Batak]], [[Toraja]], [[Ambonese]], [[Bugis]], Palembang, [[Minangkabau people|Minang]], [[Banjar people|Banjarese]], Makassar, Papuan, [[Balinese people|Balinese]], [[Acehnese people|Acehnese]] and also [[Chinese Indonesians|Chinese]].

==Culture==
[[File:Grup Musik Karungut.JPG|thumb|250px|A Karungut Music Group Performance in Isen Mulang Cultural Festival in 2018. To the left of the group it can be seen that the group played three main musical instruments of Dayak people i.e. Japen or Kacapi, Gandang Manca, and Garantung.]]

===Traditional music===
The most well-known traditional musical instrument from Central Kalimantan is Japen, Garantung, and Gandang Manca which are traditional Dayak musical instruments. These musical instruments are usually played during traditional procession or ritual.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.kompas.com/skola/read/2021/05/11/140932369/japen-dan-garantung-alat-musik-daerah-kalimantan-tengah | title = Japen & Garantung Alat Musik daerah Kalteng | accessdate = 16 May 2023 | lang = id }}</ref><ref name="Gandang">{{cite web | url = https://disbudpar.gunungmaskab.go.id/?p=476 | title = Alat Musik Gandang Manca | accessdate = 16 May 2023 | publisher = Pemkab Gunung Mas | lang = id}}</ref> 

Japen or Kecapi is a lute-like instrument that is honored by [[Ngaju people|Dayak]] people. This traditional musical instrument is usually played as an accompaniment for Central Kalimantan traditional music such as Karungut recitation, a Dayak oral literature in the form of musicalized [[rhyme scheme]]. Japen is mainly made of wood and nylon rope for its strings.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://staffnew.uny.ac.id/upload/132309934/pendidikan/Materi+Musik+Nusantara.pdf | title = Materi Musik Nusantara | accessdate = 1 May 2023 | lang = id | publisher = UNY}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url = https://e-journal.upr.ac.id/index.php/tambuleng/article/download/2047/1980/5043#:~:text=Kecapi%20adalah%20alat%20musik%20tradisional%20dari%20Kalimantan%20Tengah%2C%20Indonesia. | title = Kecapi, Alat Musik Tradisional Kalteng | lang = id | publisher = Palangka Raya University | accessdate = 15 May 2023}}</ref>

Garantung is a [[percussion]] instrument that is fairly similar with [[kempul]] in [[gamelan]]. It is a type of hanging gong that is normally played ensembly with Gandang Manca or Gandang Kembar. Gandang Manca itself is a two-headed drum of Dayak traditional musical instrument that is mainly made of wood for the body of the drum and leather membranes to cover the cavity at both ends that are tied by [[rattan]] rope.<ref name="Gandang"/> Both Garantung and Gandang Manca are commonly played ensembly as an accompaniment for Dayak traditional processions and rituals.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://digilib.isi.ac.id/6286/3/NASKAH%20PUBLIKASI%201510554015.pdf | title = Ensambel Sakepeng | publisher = ISI Yogyakarta | lang = id | accessdate = 17 May 2023 | date = 2020}}</ref>

=== Traditional architecture ===
The most notable [[fine arts]] of Dayak people is the traditional house. The traditional house of Dayak people in Central Kalimantan is commonly called Huma Betang. Huma Betang or simply called Betang like many other traditional houses in Indonesia is built on stilts. This traditional house structure is mainly made of iron wood which is commonly anti-termite and resistant to high humidity, thus the house can stand up to hundreds of years. Huma Betang is very well-known for its huge and long size. The length of this house structure is usually between 30 and 150 metres, the width is somewhere between 5 and 30 metres, and the height is approximately 3 to 6 metres. Due to its huge and long size, this house can be occupied by up to 150 people and usually inhabited by one big family.<ref>{{cite web | url  = https://lingua.soloclcs.org/index.php/lingua/article/download/237/219 | title = Huma Betang Masyarakat Dayak Kalimantan tengah | accessdate  = 17 May 2023 | lang = id | publisher = Centre of Language and Culture Studies in Surakarta }}</ref>

=== Traditional carving ===
[[File:Patung Sapundu Suku Dayak Ngaju.jpg|thumb|150px|Sapundu Sculptures of Dayak Ngaju in Central Kalimantan]]
[[File:Mandau blade hilt and other equipment.jpg|thumb|150px|Mandau blade, hilt, scabbard, and other equipments]]
[[File:Talawang perisai dayak.jpg|thumb|180px|Talawang shield used as a dance instrument]]
Central Kalimantan is well-known for its unique traditional carving. The three most famous carving from this province are the three dimensional wood carving sculpture of Sapundu and the traditional weapons i.e. Mandau and Talawang.

Sapundu sculpture is a three dimensional wood carving in the form of humanoid statue. This sculpture is commonly adorned with decorative motifs or even colors. Sapundu sculpture is mainly made of Bornean iron wood and usually presents during Tiwah procession. In ancient times, Sapundu sculpture symbolized the social status of its owner and was believed to possess magical powers.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://warisanbudaya.kemdikbud.go.id/?newdetail&detailTetap=258 | title = Warisan Budaya Tak Benda Patung Sapundu | publisher = Ministry of Education, Culture, Research, and Technology (Indonesia) | accessdate = 17 May 2023 | lang = id | date = January 2015}}</ref>

The ''[[Mandau]]'' and ''Talawang'' are the main traditional weapon of Dayak people in Central Kalimantan. The ''Mandau'' is a type of sword with a single edge blade i.e. one side is convex and the other side is concave. The ''Mandau'' consists of three different parts, which are (i) the ''Bilah'' or the blade that made of ''Mantikei'' iron plate with a sharp edge and is 70 cm long, (ii) the ''Pulang Gagang'' or the hilt that is usually made of wood, deer's horns, or even human bones, and (iii) the ''Sarung'' or the sheath which covers the blade and is typically made of wood and usually covered with bracelet-shaped bone on the upper part and wrapped by rattan rope.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://warisanbudaya.kemdikbud.go.id/?newdetail&detailCatat=932 | title = Warisan Budaya Tak Benda Mandau | publisher = Ministry of Education, Culture, Research, and Technology (Indonesia) | accessdate = 17 May 2023 | lang = id | date = January 2010}}</ref>

The ''Talawang'' is a shield that mainly made of Bornean iron wood that is carved with certain motifs and adorned with some bright colors. In ancient times, this weapon was used as a protection against wild animals and enemy's arrows and sword slashes. However, the function currently shifts to become a decorative ornament of [[Kaharingan]] temples, public buildings, or even people's houses mostly in Central Kalimantan.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://journal.isi.ac.id/index.php/sense/article/download/5849/2482|title=Pergeseran Nilai Guna Talawang, Perisai Suku Dayak|accessdate=17 May 2023|lang=id|date=May 2021}}</ref>

=== Traditional dance ===
Central Kalimantan is home for various sub-ethnics of [[Dayak people|Dayak]] tribe. Each of those Dayak sub-ethnics live spread out across all regencies in Central Kalimantan. This makes every regency have their own unique traditional dances. These are some of the traditional dances from Central Kalimantan:<ref>{{cite web | url = https://mmc.kalteng.go.id/berita/read/1936/tarian-tradisional-asal-kalimantan-tengah | title = Tarian Tradisional Asal Kalteng | lang = id | accessdate = 16 May 2023}}</ref>
# Kinyah Mandau Hatue Dance, originating from Kapuas Regency and depicting theatrical war and martial arts of Dayak tribes in ancient time.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://journal.unair.ac.id/download-fullpapers-bk924d942098full.pdf | title = Makna Tarian Kinyah Mandau Hatue | publisher = Airlangga University | accessdate = 17 May 2023 | lang = id}}</ref>
# Giring-Giring Dance, coming from East Barito Regency and usually performed in group of 6 to 10 people.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://kebudayaan.kemdikbud.go.id/bpnbkalbar/wp-content/uploads/sites/22/2017/06/deskripsi-TARI-GIRING-GIRING-Neni.pdf | title = Deskripsi Tarian Giring-giring | lang = id | publisher = Ministry of Education, Culture, Research, and Technology (Indonesia) | accessdate = 17 May 2023}}</ref>
# Walian Dadas Dance, originating from South Barito Regency and used to be performed as a way to heal a region from negativity.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://e-journal.upr.ac.id/index.php/parislangkis/article/download/1670/1531/3344#:~:text=Tari%20Balean%20Dadas%20adalah%20tarian,ada%20di%20Provinsi%20Kalimantan%20Tengah. | title = Tarian Dadas dalam Membentuk Kedisiplinan | lang = id | publisher = Palangka Raya University | accessdate = 17 May 2023}}</ref>
# [[Babukung|Babukung Dance]], originally from Lamandau Regency and performed during funeral especially among Dayak Tomun people.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://kumparan.com/infopbun/tarian-babukung-upacara-adat-kematian-suku-dayak-tomun-di-kalteng-1rspCMFo3Rz | title = Tarian babukung Upacara Adat Pemakaman Suku Dayak Tomun | lang = id | accessdate = 17 May 2023 | publisher = Kumparan}}</ref>
# Tambun Bungai Dance, originating from Palangka Raya city and depicting heroic action of Tambung and Bungai protecting the territory from enemy who tried to loot people's crops.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://kikomunal-indonesia.dgip.go.id/index.php/jenis/1/ekspresi-budaya-tradisional/567/tari-tambun-dan-bungai#:~:text=Tari%20tambun%20dan%20bungai%20merupakan,merampas%20hasil%20panen%20dari%20rakyat. | title = Tari Tambun Bungai | accessdate = 17 May 2023 | lang = id}}</ref>
