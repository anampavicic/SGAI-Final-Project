'''Mubende''' is a town in the [[Central Region, Uganda|Central Region of Uganda]]. It is the main municipal, administrative, and commercial center of [[Mubende District]] and the location of the district headquarters.<ref>{{cite web|accessdate=1 August 2015| 
url=http://www.monitor.co.ug/News/National/Municipal-status-excites-Mubende-residents/-/688334/2815640/-/ep6y51z/-/index.html| title=Municipal Status Excites Mubende Residents|date=1 August 2015 |first=Godfrey|last=Mayengo|newspaper=[[Daily Monitor]]|
location=Kampala}}</ref>

==Location==
Mubende is approximately {{convert|150|km}}, by road, west of [[Kampala]], the capital of Uganda and its largest city.<ref>{{google maps|title=Distance Between Kampala And Mubende With Map |url=https://www.google.com/maps/dir/Kampala/Mubende/@0.4372528,31.4249586,9z/data=!3m1!4b1!4m14!4m13!1m5!1m1!1s0x177dbc0f9d74b39b:0x4538903dd96b6fec!2m2!1d32.5825197!2d0.3475964!1m5!1m1!1s0x1763241c7be83eb1:0x4704f8c044e3382c!2m2!1d31.3884671!2d0.5539013!3e0
|access-date=23 January 2021}}</ref> The town is located about {{convert|145|km}} east of the city of [[Fort Portal]] along the [[Mubende–Kyegegwa–Kyenjojo–Fort Portal Road]].<ref>{{google maps |url=https://www.google.com/maps/dir/Fort+Portal/Mubende/@0.5780295,30.2715542,9z/data=!3m1!4b1!4m14!4m13!1m5!1m1!1s0x1763da423ccd41c1:0xb3115cac70684750!2m2!1d30.2801166!2d0.6546257!1m5!1m1!1s0x1763241c7be83eb1:0x4704f8c044e3382c!2m2!1d31.3884671!2d0.5539013!3e0 |title=Road Distance Between Fort Portal And Mubende |
access-date=23 January 2021}}</ref> The geographical coordinates of the town are 0°33'27.0"N, 31°23'42.0"E (Latitude:0.5575; Longitude:31.3950).<ref name="5R">{{google maps |access-date=23 January 2021 |title=Location of Mubende Town, Central Region, Uganda|url=https://www.google.com/maps/place/0%C2%B033'27.0%22N+31%C2%B023'42.0%22E/@0.5624699,31.3919654,1867m/data=!3m1!1e3!4m5!3m4!1s0x0:0x0!8m2!3d0.5575!4d31.395?hl=en}}</ref> Mubende Town sits at an average elevation of {{convert|1408|m}} above [[mean sea level]].<ref name="6R">{{cite web|publisher=Floodmap.net | url=https://www.floodmap.net/Elevation/ElevationMap/?gi=228971 | title=Elevation of Mubende Town, Central Region, Uganda |date=23 January 2021 |author=Floodmap |access-date=23 January 2021}}</ref>

==Overview==
{{See also|Empire of Kitara}}
[[File:The Nakayima tree 01.jpg|alt=The Nakayima tree found in Mubende Hill at Nakayima Shrine.|left|thumb|The Nakayima tree found in Mubende Hill at Nakayima Shrine.]]
Mubende is home to the [[Nakayima Shrine]], said to hold the spirit of [[Ndahura]], a former Bacwezi king.<ref name="Nakyma">{{cite web | url=http://www.speedwaysafaris.com/tourist-sites-in-uganda/nakayima-tree.html | title=Nakayima Tree Uganda | date=1 August 2014 | accessdate=1 August 2015 | publisher=Speedwaysafaris.com (SSC) | 
last=SSC}}</ref> The site, located about {{convert|4|km|mi}} outside of the town's central business district, is marked by the [[Nakayima Tree]]. Large root buttresses, which form nooks and fissures, lie at the base of the tree.

The shrine is located on top of [[Mubende Hill]], rising {{convert|213|m|ft}} above the surrounding terrain to a peak of {{convert|1533|m|ft}} above sea level. The hill has a flat top where an ancient palace once stood.<ref>{{cite web|title=Elevation of Mubende Hill | url=http://www.getamap.net/maps/uganda/uganda_(general)/_mubende/| accessdate=7 January 2016|date=7 January 2016|publisher=Getamap.net (GAM)|last=GAM}}</ref> The shrine is visited by people paying homage to the matriarch Nakayima of the Bachwezi, believed to be semi-gods, whose dynasty ruled this region in the period prior to 1,300 AD.<ref>{{cite web | title=Profile of Mubende Town | accessdate=1 August 2015 | url=http://www.ugandatravelguide.com/mubende-district.html |
publisher=Uganda Travel Bureau (UTB) | last=UTB | date=1 August 2015}}</ref>

==Population==
The 2002 national census estimated the population of Mubende to be 16,000. In 2010, the [[Uganda Bureau of Statistics]] (UBOS) estimated the population at 21,400. In 2011, UBOS estimated the mid-year population at 22,200.<ref name="10R">{{cite web |  url=http://www.ubos.org/onlinefiles/uploads/ubos/pdf%20documents/TP52010.pdf |  archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140707231502/http://www.ubos.org/onlinefiles/uploads/ubos/pdf%20documents/TP52010.pdf |  url-status=dead |  archive-date=2014-07-07 |title=Estimated Population of Mubende In 2002, 2010 & 2011 |publisher=[[Uganda Bureau of Statistics]] |date=2012 |
author=Uganda Bureau of Statistics |access-date=23 January 2021 |location=Kampala |format=Archived from the original on 19 January 2013}}</ref> In 2014, the national population census put the population of the town at 103,473.<ref name="Same">{{cite web | url=https://www.citypopulation.de/en/uganda/cities/ |title=The Population of The Regions of the Republic of Uganda And All Cities And Towns of More Than 15,000 Inhabitants |date=27 August 2014 |publisher=Citypopulation.de Quoting Uganda Bureau of Statistics |access-date=23 January 2021 |author=Uganda Bureau of Statistics}}</ref> In 2020 UBOS estimated the mid-population of Mubende Town at 121,600 people. The population agency calculated the annual population growth rate of Mubende at 2.8 percent, on average, from 2014 until 2020.<ref name="11R">{{cite web| 
url=https://www.citypopulation.de/en/uganda/cities/ |title=The population of all Ugandan cities and towns with more than 15,000 inhabitants according to census results and latest official projections |publisher=Citypopulation.de |date=14 June 2020 | author=Uganda Bureau of Statistics |access-date=23 January 2021 |format=Citypopulation.de Quoting [[Uganda Bureau of Statistics]]}}</ref>{{Historical populations| 2002|16,000|2010|21,000|2011|22,200|2014|103,473|2020|121,600|||align=none|footnote=source:<ref name="Same"/><ref name="10R"/><ref name="11R"/>}}

==Points of interest==
The following points of interest lie within the town limits or near its edges:<ref name="5R"/>

1. The offices of Mubende Town Council

2. [[Mubende Regional Referral Hospital]], a regional referral hospital administered by the [[Uganda Ministry of Health]]

3. Mubende Central Market

4. A branch of the [[National Social Security Fund (Uganda)|National Social Security Fund]]

5. The headquarters of the Tiger Battalion of the [[Uganda People's Defence Force]], located in the western part of the town

6. The southern end of [[Mubende–Kakumiro–Kibaale–Kagadi Road]]

7. The source of the [[River Muzizi]], in the hills northwest of the town

8. Mubende Campus of [[Muteesa I Royal University]].

9. Fliptown and Flippers. The Fliptown Hub, Home of the youths' New Hometown World Movement in Uganda.
