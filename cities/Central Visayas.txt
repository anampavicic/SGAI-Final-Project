'''Central Visayas''' ({{lang-ceb|Tunga-tungang Kabisay-an}}; {{lang-tl|Gitnang Kabisayaan}} or {{lang-fil|Gitnang Visayas}}) is an [[Regions of the Philippines|administrative region]] in the [[Philippines]], numerically designated as '''Region VII'''. With only two [[Provinces of the Philippines|provinces]]: [[Cebu]] and [[Bohol]], as well as three [[Cities of the Philippines#Independent cities|highly urbanized cities]]: [[Cebu City]], [[Lapu-Lapu City|Lapu-Lapu]], and [[Mandaue]], it has the fewest number of provinces out of any region in the country. Despite this, it is the most populous region in the [[Visayas]], with a population of 6,545,603. 

The regional center, as well as its largest city, is [[Cebu City]]. The [[Cebuano language]] is the region's [[lingua franca]]. The region is also dominated by the native speakers of three [[Bisayan languages|Visayan languages]]: [[Bantayanon language|Bantayanon]], [[Boholano dialect|Boholano]] and [[Porohanon language|Porohanon]]. 

In 2015, Central Visayas was redefined when it lost the province of [[Negros Oriental]] to the newly formed [[Negros Island Region]]. However, the Negros Island Region was dissolved in 2017, returning Negros Oriental to Central Visayas. After seven years, it lost Negros Oriental again, as well as the island province of [[Siquijor]], after the Negros Island Region was re-established in 2024.
{{TOC limit|3}}

==Etymology==
The name of the region, Central Visayas, was mostly chosen by American colonists to denote the centrality of the islands within the bigger Visayas area. 

There have been proposals to rename the current Central Visayas region, which is dominated by the [[Cebuano people|Cebuano]] (Sugbuanon) ethnic group, to ''Sugbu'', the former name of the region prior to Spanish colonization in the 16th century. The name refers to the former kingdom of the region, the [[Rajahnate of Cebu]], or ''Sugbu'' in Cebuano.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://opinion.inquirer.net/95645/change-name-will-good-philippines |title=Change in name will be good for Philippines |website=Inquirer Opinion|date=July 15, 2016 }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web | url=http://news.abs-cbn.com/news/06/13/17/should-the-philippines-be-renamed-historian-weighs-in | title=Should the Philippines be renamed? Historian weighs in}}</ref>

==History==
Regions first came into existence on September 24, 1972, when the provinces of the Philippines were organized into 11 regions by ''Presidential Decree No. 1'' as part of the ''Integrated Reorganization Plan'' by President [[Ferdinand Marcos Sr.]]. The provinces of Cebu, Bohol, and Negros Oriental (including its then-subprovince of Siquijor) were grouped together to form the Central Visayas region.

By virtue of ''Executive Order No. 183, s. 2015'', issued on May 29, 2015, by President [[Benigno Aquino III]], moved Negros Oriental to the newly formed [[Negros Island Region]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.officialgazette.gov.ph/2015/05/29/executive-order-no-183-s-2015/ | title=Executive Order No. 183, s. 2015 | publisher=[[Official Gazette (Philippines)]] | date=May 29, 2015 | access-date=June 5, 2015 | archive-date=July 29, 2017 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170729100608/http://www.officialgazette.gov.ph/2015/05/29/executive-order-no-183-s-2015/ | url-status=dead }}</ref> 

On August 9, 2017, President [[Rodrigo Duterte]] dissolved the Negros Island Region, revoking ''Executive Order No. 183, s. 2015'' through the signing of ''Executive Order No. 38''.<ref>{{cite news|title=Duterte dissolves Negros Island Region|url=http://www.rappler.com/nation/178175-duterte-dissolves-negros-island-region|access-date=August 10, 2017|publisher=[[Rappler]]|date=August 9, 2017}}</ref> This returned Negros Oriental to Central Visayas. 

In 2024, Negros Oriental was again moved to the re-established Negros Island Region. Siquijor was also moved to the NIR through Republic Act No. 12000 signed by President [[Bongbong Marcos]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Marcos signs law creating Negros Island Region |url=https://news.abs-cbn.com/regions/2024/6/13/marcos-signs-law-creating-the-negros-island-region-1535 |access-date=13 June 2024 |work=ABS-CBN |date=13 June 2024 |language=en}}</ref>

==Geography==
[[Image:Central Visayas in June 2024.png|thumb|200px|Political map of Central Visayas (since June 2024)]][[Image:Ph central visayas.png|thumb|200px|Former map of Central Visayas prior to the revival of Negros Island Region in June 2024]]{{See also|Cebu#Geography|Bohol#Geography}}
Central Visayas consists of the island provinces of Cebu and Bohol, as well as the three independent cities of [[Cebu City]], [[Lapu-Lapu City]], and [[Mandaue City]]. The region also includes the straits of [[Cebu Strait|Cebu]] and parts of the [[Tañon Strait]]. Its land area is {{convert|10,144.52|km2|sqmi|abbr=on}}, 3.3% of the country's total land area.

Central Visayas is bounded on the north by the [[Visayan Sea]], west by the provinces of [[Negros Occidental]] and [[Negros Oriental]] in [[Negros (Philippines)|Negros]] island, south by the [[Bohol Sea]] and the province of [[Siquijor]], and east by the [[Camotes Sea]] and the island of [[Leyte (island)|Leyte]] in [[Eastern Visayas]].

According to the 2020 census, it has a population of 6,545,603. Before Negros Oriental and Siquijor separated from the region in 2024, it was 8,081,988.

The [[population density]] was {{convert| {{sigfig|8,081,988/15872|2}}|PD/km2|abbr=on}}. The 2015 census showed an average annual population growth rate of 1.76% from 2010 to 2015, slightly higher than the national average of 1.72%.{{PH census|2015}}

===Languages===
The native languages of Central Visayas are:
* [[Bantayanon language|Bantayanon]], spoken in [[Bantayan Island]] of Cebu.
* [[Boholano dialect|Boholano]], a Cebuano dialect spoken in Bohol.
* [[Cebuano language|Cebuano]], spoken in Cebu and Bohol. It is the regional lingua franca.
* [[Porohanon language|Porohanon]], spoken in [[Camotes Islands]] of Cebu.

==Economy==
[[File:Cebu Business Park and IT Park skyline, Kamputhaw (Cebu City; 09-07-2022).jpg|thumb|A skyline view of [[Cebu City]]. ]]
{{PH poverty incidence}}
Central Visayas has the fourth-largest economy in the Philippines.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Palaubsanon |first=Mitchelle L. |title=Central Visayas remains Philippine's 4th largest economy |url=https://www.philstar.com/the-freeman/cebu-news/2023/04/28/2262281/central-visayas-remains-philippines-4th-largest-economy |access-date=2024-02-07 |website=Philstar.com}}</ref> [[Cebu City]] serves as the region's economic hub.

==Transportation==
===Ports===
[[Image:Santander port.jpg|thumb|Fastcraft terminal for ferrying passengers from Cebu to Negros Island.]]
The [[Port of Cebu]] is the region's main gateway by sea. Other seaports in the region include the Liloan Port in [[Santander, Cebu]] and the Port of [[Tagbilaran]] in Bohol. Inter-island shipping is served by numerous shipping lines, including [[Roll-on/roll-off|ro-ro]] companies [[Montenegro Lines]] and [[Lite Shipping Corporation|Lite Ferries]] and [[fastcraft]] companies [[Ocean Fast Ferries|OceanJet]] and [[Supercat Fast Ferry Corporation|Supercat]].

===Airports===
[[Image:Mactan Cebu International Airport.jpg|thumb|[[Mactan–Cebu International Airport]].]]

[[Mactan–Cebu International Airport]], located on [[Mactan|Mactan Island]] in [[Metro Cebu]], is the country's second-busiest airport and a gateway to the region by air. It is the secondary hub of [[Cebu Pacific]] and [[Philippine Airlines]] (and their subsidiaries), with flights to locations throughout the country. It also serves international flights to other Asian and intercontinental destinations.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mactan-cebuairport.com.ph/ |title=Mactan Cebu International Airport - Cebu Pacific - Philippines |publisher=mactan–cebuairport.com.ph |access-date=2013-04-22 |archive-date=August 6, 2009 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090806055453/http://www.mactan-cebuairport.com.ph/ |url-status=dead }}</ref>

[[Bohol–Panglao International Airport]] serves Bohol and is the region's newest airport.

== Mass media ==
Cebu City is the main media hub for the region. Large media networks – [[ABS-CBN]], [[GMA Network]], [[TV5 (Philippine TV channel)|TV5]], [[People's Television Network]], [[CNN Philippines]], and [[Intercontinental Broadcasting Corporation|IBC 13]] – maintain their respective local stations and branches for viewership, commercial and news coverage purposes. Most of these stations broadcast local news and public affairs as well as entertainment and dramas to cater to the local viewers.

Aside from the 24 national daily newspapers available, Cebu City also has 20 local newspapers. Among the widely read are ''[[SunStar Cebu]]'', ''Cebu Daily News'', and ''[[The Freeman (newspaper)|The Freeman]]''.
