'''Jimeta''' is a town in [[Yola, Adamawa State]], [[Nigeria]]. The town's population was 73,080 in 1991.<ref name="eb">{{cite encyclopedia | encyclopedia =Encyclopædia Britannica | title = Jimeta Yola | url = http://www.britannica.com/eb/article-9043648/Jimeta | accessdate = 2006-12-31| edition =  | date =  | publisher = Encyclopædia Britannica, Inc }}</ref> The elevation of Jimeta is 135 m,<ref name="fallingrain">{{cite web| url = http://www.fallingrain.com/world/NI/0/Jimeta3.html| title = Jimeta, Yola Nigeria| accessdate = 2006-12-31| publisher = Falling Rain Genomics, Inc}}</ref> and it lies along the [[Benue River]].<ref name="eb"/>

Jimeta and nearby [[Yola, Nigeria|Yola]], the state capital of Adamawa have interconnected histories, and between 1935 and 1955, it were unified as one town. Jimeta also serves as Yola's port<ref name="eb"/>  and it also contains [[Yola Airport|Yola's airport]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Yola Airport profile|url=http://aviation-safety.net/database/airport/airport.php?id=YOL|website=Aviation Safety Network|accessdate=12 April 2015}}</ref> Some refer to the two as "Jimeta Yola."<ref name="worldbank">{{cite web| url = http://siteresources.worldbank.org/INTSOCIALCAPITAL/Resources/Social-Capital-Integrated-Questionnaire/AdamawaStatePilotRptShortVersionAug02.pdf | title = Report of Social Capital Household Survey Pilot in Adamawa State, Nigeria| accessdate = 2006-12-31| last = Onu| first = Justice I| date = July 2005| publisher = [[World Bank]]}}</ref>

== Climate ==

Jimeta located at [[sea level]] and has a [[Tropical savanna climate]] with dry winter ([[Köppen climate classification]] Aw). The region's yearly average [[temperature]] is 32.71&nbsp;°C (90.88&nbsp;°F) and it is 3.25% higher than [[Nigeria|Nigeria's]] [[Midpoint (astrology)|midpoints]]. Jimeta commonly gets around 122.5 mm (4.82 inches) of [[precipitation]] and has 147 [[blustery]] days (40% of the time) every year.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Jimeta, Adamawa, NG Climate Zone, Monthly Averages, Historical Weather Data |url=https://weatherandclimate.com/nigeria/adamawa/jimeta#:~:text=Located%20at%20an%20elevation%20of,3.25%25%20higher%20than%20Nigeria's%20averages. |access-date=2023-10-19 |website=weatherandclimate.com}}</ref>

== Serious flooding in Jimeta, August, 2012 ==
Serious [[Flood|flooding]] has hit Jimeta - [[Yola, Nigeria|Yola]] in [[Adamawa State|Adamawa]] State after a weighty [[storm]] in the [[North East (Nigeria)|North-Eastern]] [[Territory]] of [[Nigeria]] yesterday.

Many individuals have been rendered destitute by the assaulting floods. The most recent flooding impacted the Doubeli area of Jimeta and the water level was all the while ascending as at the hour of documenting this report.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Flooding Hits Jimeta In Adamawa State {{!}} Sahara Reporters |url=https://saharareporters.com/2012/08/26/flooding-hits-jimeta-adamawa-state |access-date=2023-10-19 |website=saharareporters.com}}</ref>

The flood has reached knee-level in certain areas and [[Occupancy|occupants]] are battling to adapt to the staggering effect on their lives.  

The brief [[Asylums and Care for the Insane|asylums]] set up to give help have made cleanliness challenges, 

The flooding has additionally destroyed numerous [[Agricultural land|farmlands]] nearby, leaving occupants without their essential means of revenue.  

The continuous flooding has dislodged many residents who are now battling to adapt to the effect on their livelihood.

As per the [[National Emergency Management Agency (Nigeria)|National Emergency Management Agency]] (NEMA), in a press explanation gave to [[Journalist|newsmen]] on Saturday, the floods have lowered [[Ranch|ranches]] and houses and great many occupants have been compelled to empty for wellbeing.

Following weighty storm on Friday in Adamawa State, which brought about floods, the circumstance has provoked for critical national government mediation.

The flood was occasioned by the progressive arrival of water from the Lagdo Dam in the [[Cameroon]] Republic.

The NEMA added that cooperative endeavors with its Yola office and the Adamawa State Emergency Management Agency (ADSEMA) and pertinent partners have been set up to screen the heightening water levels.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Adamawa residents seek help as floods wreck havoc - Daily Trust |url=https://dailytrust.com/adamawa-residents-seek-help-as-floods-wreck-havoc/ |access-date=2023-10-19 |website=dailytrust.com}}</ref>
