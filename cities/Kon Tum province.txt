'''Kon Tum''' is the northernmost mountainous [[Provinces of Vietnam|province]] in the [[Central Highlands (Vietnam)|Central Highlands]] region, the [[Central Vietnam|Central]] of [[Vietnam]]. It borders [[Quảng Nam]] to the north, [[Gia Lai]] to the south, [[Quảng Ngãi province|Quảng Ngãi]] to the east, [[Sekong province|Sekong]], [[Attapeu province|Attapeu]] of [[Laos]] and [[Ratanakiri]] of [[Cambodia]] in the west.

Kon Tum shares borders with [[Laos]] and [[Cambodia]]. It has an area of {{Convert|9677.30|km2|mi2|abbr=on}}<ref name="area_2022"/> and a population of approximately 530,000. The economy is primarily agricultural.

==Name==
The name Kon Tum comes from the name of a village of the [[Bahnar people|Bahnar ethnic group]]. In the [[Bahnar language]], ''kon'' means "village" and ''tum'' means "pool". Prior to the adoption of the [[Vietnamese alphabet]] in the 1920s, the name was rendered as {{linktext|崑|嵩}}.

==Geography==
The capital city is [[Kon Tum]], located on the [[Đăk Bla River]].

The southern border with [[Gia Lai Province|Gia Lai]] follows the [[Tonle San|Sê San]] river up to [[Yali Falls Dam]], as well as part of the Dak Bla, Dak Pokei and Dak Poe rivers. The [[Sa Thầy River]], a tributary of the Sê San, also runs through the province.<ref>{{cite web|title=Khảo sát tính đa dạng sinh học của lưu vực sông Sa Thầy|url=http://baochinhphu.vn/Tin-khac/Khao-sat-tinh-da-dang-sinh-hoc-cua-luu-vuc-song-Sa-Thay/39416.vgp}}</ref>

The province is primarily located in the Sê San river basin, though parts are also in the headwaters of the [[Vu Gia River|Vu Gia]], [[Trà Khúc River|Trà Khúc]] and [[Sekong River]]s.

==Demographics==
Ethnic groups in Kon Tum province include the [[Viet]], [[Bahnar people|Bahnar]], [[Brau people|Brau]], [[Giẻ Triêng]], [[Jarai people|Jarai]], [[Rơ Măm people|Rơ Măm]], and [[Xo Dang people|Xo Dang]].
