'''Yeongnam''' ([[Hangul]]: 영남, {{IPA|ko|jʌŋ.nam}}; literally "south of the ridge") is a region that coincides with the former [[Gyeongsang Province]], one of the ancient [[Eight Provinces of Korea|Eight Provinces]], in what is now [[South Korea]].

The region includes the modern-day provinces of [[North Gyeongsang|North]] and [[South Gyeongsang]] and the autonomous [[List of provincial-level cities of South Korea|Metropolitan cities]] of [[Busan]], [[Daegu]], and [[Ulsan]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Hyeong-woo |first1=Kan |title=[Us and Them] Korea’s division runs deeper than South and North |url=https://www.koreaherald.com/view.php?ud=20211115000625 |website=The Korea Herald |access-date=1 November 2024 |language=en |date=15 November 2021}}</ref> The regional name is used (with a slightly different spelling) as the name of [[Yeungnam University]].
