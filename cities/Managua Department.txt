'''Managua''' ({{IPA|es|maˈna.ɣwa}}) is a [[Departments of Nicaragua|department]] in [[Nicaragua]]. It covers an area of 3,465&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> and has a population of 1,559,774 (2021 estimate), making it the country's most populated department. The capital is the city of [[Managua]], which is also the capital of Nicaragua. The department has two coastlines, on the [[Pacific Ocean]] and on [[Lake Managua]], but does not border [[Lake Nicaragua]]. The [[Nahuas|Nahua]] chiefdom of Tekwantepet was located in the Managua department. According to Spanish [[conquistador]] and historian Gonzalo Fernández de Oviedo y Valdés, Tekwantepet one of the last chiefdoms in present-day Nicaragua to fall to the conquistadors and their central Mexican allies.<ref>Vida de González Dávila, Gil. Ávila, c. 1480 – 21.IV.1526. Descubridor y conquistador. et al., 2012</ref><ref>Historia general y natural de las Indias, islas y tierra-firme del mar océano; por el Capitán Gonzalo Fernández de Oviedo y Valdés; Primer Cronista del Nuevo Mundo; publícala la Real Academia de la Historia.- Tomo IV.- Libro XLII.- Capitulo XIII.</ref><ref>Colonización de américa, cuando la historia marcha, de Oviedo y Valdés, Gonzalo c. 1480 - 1557, 2006</ref> The chiefdom's name is a combination of the [[Nawat language|Nawat]] words ''tēkwani'' ([[jaguar]]),<ref name="Campbell1985">{{cite book|last=Campbell|first=Lyle|title=The Pipil Language of El Salvador|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=N5bmUh7jproC&pg=PA10|date=January 1, 1985|publisher=Walter de Gruyter|isbn=978-3-11-088199-8|pages=10–12}}</ref> and ''tepet'' (hill),<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.revistas.unam.mx/index.php/antropologia/article/view/22958|title= El Nawat de Cuzcatlan}}</ref> therefore the translation of Tekwantepet is "hill of jaguars" or "jaguar hill". The municipality of [[Ticuantepe]] located in the department is likely named after this chiefdom. The [[Indigenous peoples of the Americas|Indigenous]] inhabitants of Managua are the [[:es: Chorotegas|Chorotegas]] and [[Nicarao people|Nahuas]].
