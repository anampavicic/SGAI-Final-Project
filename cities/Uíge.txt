'''Uíge''' ({{lang-kg|Wizidi}}<ref name=Angon >{{cite web|url= https://www.angonoticias.com/Artigos/item/47348/cidade-do-uige-celebra-98-anos-de-existencia-na-quarta-feira |language=pt|title=Cidade do Uíge celebra 98 anos de existência na quarta-feira |work= ANGONOTÍCIAS |date= 27 June 2015 |access-date=15 December 2020}}</ref>), formerly '''Carmona''', is a provincial capital city in northwestern [[Angola]], with a population of 322,531 (2014 census),<ref>[https://www.citypopulation.de/en/angola/cities/ Citypopulation.de] Population of the major cities in Angola</ref> and a municipality, with a population of 519,196 (2014 census), located in the province of the [[Uíge Province|same name]]. It grew from a small market centre in 1945 to become a city in 1956.  It is serviced by the [[Uíge Airport]] with daily flights to [[Luanda]].

==Name==
Uíge was renamed<ref name="Eribo1997">{{cite book|last=Eribo|first=Festus|title=Press freedom and communication in Africa|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=aMM2qKU9bVUC&pg=PA330|date=1997|publisher=Africa World Press|isbn=978-0-86543-551-3|page=330}}</ref> '''Vila Marechal Carmona''' in 1955 after the former Portuguese President [[Óscar Carmona]], renamed simply Carmona after it became a city, but changed back to Uíge in 1975.

==History==
During Portuguese occupation it was a major center for coffee production in the 1950s.<ref name=Brit>{{Cite web|url=http://www.britannica.com/EBchecked/topic/612771/Uige|title=Uige|access-date=21 January 2011|publisher=Encyclopædia Britannica}}</ref> The city was the nerve center of rebel activity against Portuguese occupation. Consequently, the city faced frequent guerrilla war between Portuguese forces and the [[National Front for the Liberation of Angola]] (Frente Nacional de Libertação de Angola; FNLA).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.statoids.com/yao.html|title=City councils of Angola|publisher=Statoids|access-date=9 April 2009}}</ref>

It had the worst known ever outbreak of the [[Marburg virus]] in 2005.{{citation needed|date=March 2018}}

==Demographics==
In 2010 it had a population of 119,815.<!--<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://bevoelkerungsstatistik.de/wg.php?x=&men=gcis&lng=de&des=wg&srt=npan&col=abcdefghinoq&msz=1500&geo=-19|title=bevoelkerungsstatistik.de - This website is for sale! -  Resources and Information. |website=bevoelkerungsstatistik.de |language=en|access-date=2017-07-06 |url-status=usurped |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160522172005/http://bevoelkerungsstatistik.de/wg.php?x=&men=gcis&lng=de&des=wg&srt=npan&col=abcdefghinoq&msz=1500&geo=-19 |archive-date=22 May 2016}}</ref>--> In 2014 the population was 322,531.  Projected to be the fourth fastest growing city  on the African continent between 2020 and 2025, with a 5.92% growth.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.visualcapitalist.com/ranked-the-worlds-fastest-growing-cities/|publisher=virtual capitalist| access-date=2021-08-17|title=Ranked: The World’s Fastest Growing Cities| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210813174630/https://www.visualcapitalist.com/ranked-the-worlds-fastest-growing-cities/|archive-date=13 August 2021}}</ref>
