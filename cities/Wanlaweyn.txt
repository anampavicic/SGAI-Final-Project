'''Wanlaweyn''' ({{lang-ymm|Wanliwiing}}) is a town in the northern [[Lower Shebelle]] region of [[Somalia]]. Situated around 90&nbsp;km (50 miles) northwest of the capital [[Mogadishu]], it is the center of the [[Wanlaweyn District]]. [[File:2012 10 27 AMISOM WanlaWeyn A (8134729906).jpg|thumb|265px|Street in Wanlaweyn|alt=]]
The Shanta Alemo (Somali: Shanta Caleemo), (Italian: Shanta Alemo), (Arabic: شانتا أليمو
shanta 'alemo means 5 leaves means 5 clan consist of hubeer clan , erdho clan , hifmage clan , jambulul clan 
) or shan aleen  is one of the largest Somali clans, whose origins trace back to 5 clans leaving together . The Shanta Alemo clan belong to the Digil and mirifle family of clans.

==Demographics==
Wanlaweyn has a population of around 26,700 inhabitants.<ref name="Tsctp">{{cite web|title=Somalia City & Town Population|url=http://www.tageo.com/index-e-so-cities-SO.htm|publisher=Tageo|access-date=4 October 2013}}</ref> The broader Wanlaweyn District has a total population of 250,643 residents.<ref name="Rdatp">{{cite web|title=Regions, districts, and their populations: Somalia 2005 (draft)|url=https://docs.unocha.org/sites/dms/Somalia/UNDP-POP-RURAL-URBAN%202005.pdf|publisher=UNDP|access-date=21 September 2013}}</ref> The town is predominantly inhabited by the Shanta Caleemood clan sub-division of the [[Digil]] [[Rahanweyn]] [[Somalis]].

== History ==
During the [[1961 Somali constitutional referendum]], in order to secure a substantial "Yes" vote for southerners, the small town reported 100,000 votes.<ref name=":0">{{Cite book|last=Abdullahi|first=Mohamed Diriye|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=2Nu918tYMB8C&q=wanlaweyn+vote&pg=PA27|title=Culture and Customs of Somalia|date=2001|publisher=Greenwood Publishing Group|isbn=978-0-313-31333-2|language=en}}</ref> This was higher than total ballots cast in the North ([[Somaliland]]), therefore Northerners became suspicious of the political nature of Southerners.<ref name=":0" /> As a result, coining the new term "Wanlaweyn" for Southerners, which is still used today.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite book|last=Adam|first=Hussein Mohamed|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=lF93AAAAMAAJ&q=Wanlaweyn|title=From Tyranny to Anarchy: The Somali Experience|date=2008|publisher=Red Sea Press|isbn=978-1-56902-288-7|language=en}}</ref>
