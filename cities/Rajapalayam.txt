'''Rajapalayam''' ({{IPA-ta|ɾaːdʑabaːɭaɪjam|lang}}) is a town in the Indian state of [[Tamil Nadu]]. It is the largest municipality in the [[Virudhunagar district|Virudhunagar]] district{{Citation needed|date=April 2023}}. Rajapalayam is located on the [[Madurai]] to [[Quilon]] National Highway at a distance of 562&nbsp;km south of the state capital [[Chennai]]. The economy is primarily industrial with several mills for spinning and weaving. The town is known for its [[mango]] and the [[Rajapalayam dog|Rajapalayam breed of dogs]].

== Climate ==
The climate of the region is of the [[Semi-arid climate|semi-arid]] [[Tropical monsoon climate|tropical monsoon]] type. The average temperature range is 20&nbsp;°C to 37&nbsp;°C.

==Demographics==

According to the [[2011 census of India|2011 census]], Rajapalayam had a population of 130,442 with 1,014 females for every 1,000 males, far above the national average of 929.<ref name="dashboard">{{cite web|title=Census Info 2011 Final population totals|url=http://www.censusindia.gov.in/2011census/censusinfodashboard/index.html|publisher=Office of The Registrar General and Census Commissioner, Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India|year=2013|access-date=26 January 2014}}</ref> A total of 11,604 individuals (5,927 males and 5,677 females) were under the age of six. [[Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes]] accounted for 13.51% and 0.09% of the population respectively. The average literacy of the town was 77.87%; the national average was 72.99%.<ref name="dashboard"/> The town has a total of 37,797 households. There are 53,913 workers, comprising 380 cultivators, 3,676 main agricultural labourers, 1,375 in household industries, 45,223 other workers, 3,259 marginal workers, 34 marginal cultivators, 326 marginal agricultural labourers, 286 marginal workers in household industries and 2,613 other marginal workers.<ref name="2011census">{{cite web|title=Census Info 2011 Final population totals - Rajapalayam|url=http://www.censusindia.gov.in/pca/SearchDetails.aspx?Id=689062|publisher=Office of The Registrar General and Census Commissioner, Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India|year=2013|access-date=26 January 2014}}</ref> According to the religious census of 2011, the population is 94.53% [[Hindus|Hindu]], 3.48% [[Muslims|Muslim]], 1.75% [[Christians|Christian]], 0.02% [[Sikhs|Sikh]] and 0.21% other.<ref name="religion2011">{{cite web|url=http://www.censusindia.gov.in/2011census/C-01.html|title=Population By Religious Community - Tamil Nadu|year=2011|publisher=Office of The Registrar General and Census Commissioner, Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India|format=XLS|access-date=13 September 2015}}</ref>

==Geography==
The mountains of the [[Western ghats]] are to the west of the town (12&nbsp;km from the town), and the [[Sanjeevi]] hills are to the east. These moderate the climate, and several water catchment areas in the hills provide water for the town. Water is stored in the 6th mile water reservoir. There are several major lakes around the town, including Kondaneri, Karungulam, Pirandaikulam, and Sengulam. The soil in the area is classified by the government as [[Loam|red loam]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://virudhunagar.tn.nic.in/statistics_handbook/Agriculture.pdf|title=Soil in Rajapalayam|website=Virudhunagar district}}</ref>
The area of the municipality<ref>https://www.adb.org/sites/default/files/linked-documents/49107-003-ieeab-04.pdf {{Bare URL PDF|date=August 2024}}</ref> is about 11.36 sq.kms which makes the town the biggest municipality in Virudunagar district.<ref>https://www.tnurbantree.tn.gov.in/rajapalayam/about-us/ {{Bare URL inline|date=August 2024}}</ref>

==Industries==
The principal industry was initially agriculture. In 1936, Thiru P. A. C. Ramasamy Raja started the first cotton spinning mill, called Rajapalayam Mills Ltd. Later, more [[textile mill]]s were started. Today, Rajapalayam is a major hub of the textile industry, famous for bandages, woven fabric, [[nighties|nightwear]], and other products. The neighboring town of [[Chatrapatti]] (Virudhunagar Dist.) is a large player in producing surgical cotton [[gauze]], and nearby [[Dhalavaipuram]] and Muhavoor manufacture women's nightwear.

==Transportation==

===By Land===

====Road====
The National Highway NH-744 from [[Madurai]] to [[Kollam]] passes through the town. State highway SH-41 (Rajapalayam-Sankarankovil-Tirunelveli) links the major parts of the extreme south of Tamil Nadu, including [[Tirunelveli]], [[Thoothukudi|Tuticorin]] and [[Kanyakumari]]. The road also offers good freight connectivity to Tamil Nadu's second largest seaport, [[Tuticorin Port Trust|Tuticorin]].  State highway SH-186 (Rajapalayam-Vembakottai) connects the town to the east part of [[Virudhunagar]] district and passes through the fast-growing industrial towns of [[Chatrapatti]] and [[Alangulam, Tirunelveli|Alangulam]].

====Bus====
[[File:Rajapalayam Old Bus Stand.jpg|thumb|Rajapalayam Old Bus Stand|right|250px]]                

Rajapalayam's long-distance bus station, located on Sankarankovil Road, is served by buses from the government of Tamil Nadu's TNSTC (Tamil Nadu State Transport Corporation) and private transport companies. Several private and government operated services connect to [[Chennai]], [[Bangalore]], [[Coimbatore]], [[Hosur]], [[Pondicherry]], [[Salem, Tamil Nadu|Salem]], [[Tirupur|Tiruppur]], [[Tiruchirappalli|Trichy]] and other major cities. 

Rajapalayam old bus station is the boarding point for buses connecting nearby villages. It is also called Old bus stand or Town bus stand. [[Sankarankovil]] Mukku road connects the new bus stand.{{Citation needed|date=October 2021}}

====Rail====
{{Main|Rajapalayam railway station}}
The Rajapalayam railway station is in the Southern Railways zone/Madurai division of [[Indian Railways]] and falls on the [[Virudhunagar]] to [[Kollam]] line. The station code is RJPM.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://indiarailinfo.com/departures/rajapalayam-rjpm/2680|title=Rajapalayam Station - 16 Train Departures SR/Southern Zone - Railway Enquiry|last=P|first=Arpit|website=indiarailinfo.com|access-date=2019-09-12}}</ref>
