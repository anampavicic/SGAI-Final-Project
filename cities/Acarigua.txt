'''Acarigua''' ({{IPA|es|akaˈɾiɣwa}}), founded as '''San Miguel de Acarigua''' in 1620,<ref>{{Cite web |date=29 September 2015 |title=La bella Acarigua cumple 395 años |trans-title=The beautiful Acarigua turns 395 years old |url=https://www.conelmazodando.com.ve/la-bella-acarigua-cumple-395-anos |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230716031146/https://www.conelmazodando.com.ve/la-bella-acarigua-cumple-395-anos |archive-date=16 July 2023 |access-date=10 August 2024 |website=Con El Mazo Dando |language=es}}</ref> is a city in northwestern [[Venezuela]], in the northern part of the state of [[Portuguesa (state)|Portuguesa]] and its former capital.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |date=December 2014 |title=XIV CENSO NACIONAL DE POBLACIÓN YVIVIENDA |url=http://www.ine.gob.ve/documentos/Demografia/CensodePoblacionyVivienda/pdf/portuguesa.pdf |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231120035946/http://www.ine.gob.ve/documentos/Demografia/CensodePoblacionyVivienda/pdf/portuguesa.pdf |archive-date=20 November 2023 |access-date=10 August 2024 |website=Instituto Nacional de Estadística (INE)}}</ref> It encompasses the Páez municipality.

It is a major commercial center for the northern [[Llanos]] region of [[South America]]. It is contiguous with the neighboring city of [[Araure]].

== Etymology ==
The name Acarigua comes from the indigenous word ''Hacarygua'', given to the land by the indigenous [[Gayon language|Gayón]] people. The name ''San Miguel de Acarigua,'' given to the area by Spanish [[Conquistador|conquistadores]], follows the Spanish Catholic tradition of naming cities after saints.

== History ==
The first accounts of the ''Hacarygua'' territory was in the writings of German colonizer [[Nikolaus Federmann|Nicolás Federmann]], who arrived in one of the area's [[Indigenous peoples in Venezuela|indigenous]] villages on December 15, 1530. He was headed to southwestern Llanos territory to search for the mythical city of [[El Dorado]]. In the winter of 1535, German colonizer Jorge Spira sought shelter and food during his travels in what is now Acarigua.<ref>{{Citation |last=Donís Ríos |first=Manuel Alberto |title=LA FORMACIÓN TERRITORIAL DEL OCCIDENTE VENEZOLANO: Discurso de incorporación como Individuo de Número a la Academia Nacional DE LA HISTORIA |date=20 October 2011 |work=BOLETÍN DE LA  ACADEMIA NACIONAL  DE LA HISTORIA |issue=376 |pages=49,110 |trans-title=THE TERRITORIAL FORMATION OF WESTERN VENEZUELA: Discourse of incorporation as a Number Individual to the National Academy of History |url=http://anhvenezuela.org.ve/sites/default/files/boletines/BANH_376.pdf |access-date=10 August 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170407233045/http://anhvenezuela.org.ve/sites/default/files/boletines/BANH_376.pdf |archive-date=7 April 2017 |url-status=dead |place=Caracas, Venezuela |publisher=Academia Nacional de la Historia |issn=0254-7325}}</ref>

It was established as the state capital of Portuguesa in 1927, but ten years later in 1937 Guanare took its place.<ref name=":0" />

== Demographics ==
The city's population was 116,551 in 1990 and was estimated at 208,495 in 2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.world-gazetteer.com/wg.php?x=1145571938&men=gpro&lng=en&dat=32&geo=-228&srt=npan&col=aohdq&pt=c&va=&geo=358499080|title=www.world-gazetteer.com|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071001032130/http://www.world-gazetteer.com/wg.php?x=1145571938&men=gpro&lng=en&dat=32&geo=-228&srt=npan&col=aohdq&pt=c&va=&geo=358499080|archive-date=2007-10-01}}</ref> In 2012 the population was 143,704.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web |title=Acarigua, Municipio Páez, Estado Portuguesa, Venezuela |url=https://www.mindat.org/feature-3649833.html |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240810234330/https://www.mindat.org/web/20240810234330/https://www.mindat.org/feature-3649833.html |archive-date=10 August 2024 |access-date=10 August 2024 |website=mindat.org}}</ref> In 2020 the population of the Páez municipality was 216,827.<ref>{{Cite web |date=10 July 2020 |title=ELECCIONES A LA ASAMBLEA NACIONAL 2020 CIRCUNSCRIPCIONES ELECTORALES DIPUTADOS o DIPUTADAS A LA ASAMBLEA NACIONAL |url=https://sumate.org/AN_2020/circunscripciones_electorales_2020_tablas_y_mapas.pdf |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240802103046/https://sumate.org/AN_2020/circunscripciones_electorales_2020_tablas_y_mapas.pdf |archive-date=2 August 2024 |access-date=10 August 2024 |website=Junta Nacional Electoral{{!}} Oficina Nacional de Infraestructura Electoral |page=35}}</ref>

== Religion ==
Its [[Cathedral]] ''Catedral de Nuestra Señora de la Corteza'' is the episcopal see of the [[Roman Catholic Diocese of Acarigua–Araure]].

===Fauna===
Fauna of the area includes [[Macaw|macaws]], [[Jaguar|jaguars]], [[Howler monkey|howler monkeys]], [[Puma (genus)|pumas]], and [[Moth|moths]]. The ''[[Thysania agrippina]]'' is one of the world's largest moths, with wings {{convert|32|cm|in}} in size, and can be found in Acarigua.

===Flora===
The saman or ''[[Samanea saman]]'' is the most widespread tree throughout the city.

===Protected areas===
* Parque Musiu Carmelo
* Parque Mittar Nakichenovich
* Balneario Sabanetica
* Balneario el Mamón
* Parque Curpa, popularly known as [[José Antonio Páez]] Park.

== Agriculture ==
Acarigua is a principal commercial center of the northern portion of the [[Llanos]] (plains), in which [[cattle]], [[Peanut|peanuts]], [[sorghum]], [[Cashew|cashews]], [[Bean|beans]], [[cotton]], [[Maize|corn]] (maize), and [[rice]] are the principal products.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Acarigua, Venezuela|url=https://www.britannica.com/place/Acarigua|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150614114346/http://www.britannica.com/place/Acarigua |archive-date=2015-06-14 }}</ref>

== Media ==
Acarigua is home to [[List of newspapers in Venezuela#Portuguesa State|3 regional newspapers]], and the community TV station [[Siguaraya TV]].

== Transport ==
[[File:Centro Occidental 2.JPG|thumb|210x210px|Map of the Central Western Railway System ({{Langx|es|Sistema Ferroviario Centro Occidental de Venezuela}})]]
Acarigua is served by the [[Oswaldo Guevara Mujica Airport]] and the Central Western Railway System. The main access road into the city is the José Antonio Páez Highway.

== Sports ==
{{Unreferenced section|date=January 2023}}
It is home to [[Portuguesa FC]], whose home stadium is the [[Estadio General José Antonio Paez]]. Other sports facilities in the city include the Estadio Bachiller Julio Hernández Molina, the Wilbaldo Zabaleta Indoor Gym, and the Cancha Techada 19 de Abril de la Urb. La Goajira.
