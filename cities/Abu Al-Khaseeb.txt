'''Abu Al-Khaseeb''' (sometimes spelled Abu Al-Khasib) is a town in [[Abu Al-Khaseeb District]], [[Basra Governorate]], southern [[Iraq]]. Its name means "The rich area - agricultural", referring to the fertile [[Shatt Al-Arab]] river. It is an agricultural town, well known for its date palm farms on the river. Its population is predominantly [[Shia]] [[Arab]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://gulf2000.columbia.edu/images/maps/Iraq_Ethnic_lg.jpg |title=Archived copy |access-date=2011-11-18 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120121050718/http://gulf2000.columbia.edu/images/maps/Iraq_Ethnic_lg.jpg |archive-date=2012-01-21 }}</ref> Abu Al-Khaseeb has long been a traditional center for boat-building of the [[mashoof]] canoes that are widely used by the [[Marsh Arabs]].<ref>{{Cite book| publisher = Trans Pacific Press| isbn = 9780863723339| last = Kubba| first = Sam| page=68| title = The Iraqi Marshlands and the Marsh Arabs: The Ma'dan, Their Culture and the Environment| date = 2011| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=GoiR2Jy57LwC&q=The%20Marsh%20Arabs&pg=PA36}}</ref>
