'''Behbahan''' ({{langx|fa|بهبهان}}){{efn|Also [[romanize]]d as '''Behbahān''' and '''Behbehān'''<ref>{{GEOnet3|-3055917}}</ref>}} is a city in the [[Central District (Behbahan County)|Central District]] of [[Behbahan County]], [[Khuzestan province|Khuzestan]] province, [[Iran]], serving as capital of both the county and the district.<ref name="Khuzestan Structure">{{cite web |title =  Approval of the organization and chain of citizenship of the elements and units of the national divisions of Khuzestan province, centered in the city of Ahvaz |language = fa |website = Islamic Parliament Research Center |url = https://rc.majlis.ir/fa/law/show/113029 |archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20140717093724/https://rc.majlis.ir/fa/law/show/113029 |publisher = Ministry of Interior, Political and Defense Commission of the Government Board |last = Habibi |first = Hassan |archive-date = 17 July 2014 |access-date = 25 January 2024}}</ref> 

== Etymology ==
[[File:Coin of either Hormizd V or Hormizd VI, minted in Veh-az-Amid-Kavād.jpg|alt=Coin of either Hormizd V or Hormizd VI, minted in Veh-az-Amid-Kavād|thumb|[[Sasanian dynasty|Sassanid]] coin of either [[Hormizd V]] or [[Hormizd VI]], minted in present-day Behbahan]]
The origin of word 'Behbahan' can be traced back to two distinct ideas. According to some scholars like Nowban,<ref>{{Cite book |last=Nowban |first=Mehrozaman |title=Names of Geographical Places across Time |publisher=Mah |year=1998 |isbn=964-6497-00-4 |pages=118 |language=fa}}</ref> while the first part of the word, 'beh', means 'good', the latter, 'bahan', means palace or a very big house surrounded by orchards and gardens. This is well-documented that the region was an agricultural center/hub producing mainly olive, dates, citruses as well as flower gardens.<ref>{{Cite book |last=Qubadiani-Balkhi |first=Naser Khosrow |title=Book of Travels |publisher=Amirkabir |year=2007 |editor-last=Vazinpour |editor-first=Nader |edition=10th |location=Tehran |publication-date=2007 |language=fa}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=گنجور » ناصرخسرو » سفرنامه » بخش ۹۲ - ارجان |url=https://ganjoor.net/naserkhosro/safarname/sh92 |access-date=2024-05-31 |website=ganjoor.net}}</ref> This means, the term Behbahan means a nice living area surrounded by gardens and farms.

Alternatively, it is suggested that the latter component of the word, namely 'bahan', might had been used to refer to a type of tent used in old times. In other words, after the downfall<ref>{{Cite web |last=Iranica Encyclopedia |title=Arrjan |url=https://iranicaonline.org/articles/arrajan-medieval-city-and-province-in-southwestern-iran-between-kuzestan-and-fars |access-date=May 31, 2024 |website=Encyclopedia Iranica}}</ref> of the ancient city of [[Arrajan]] due to a series of devastating earthquakes,<ref>{{Cite book |last=Ebn-e Athir |first=Ezzoddin |title=The Full History |publisher=Asatir |year=2005 |edition=3rd |location=Tehran |language=fa |translator-last=Roohani |translator-first=Hossein}}</ref> survivors unsurprisingly had to live in tents for some time. As they started to reconstruct the city, the name 'Behbahan' was used to mean it is better than tents.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2011-07-14|title=Meaning of Behbahan - معنى بهبهان |url=http://www.marafie.com/port10/index.php/behbahan-long/meaning-of-behbahan.html|access-date=2021-02-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110714033012/http://www.marafie.com/port10/index.php/behbahan-long/meaning-of-behbahan.html|archive-date=14 July 2011}}</ref> However, the use of the word 'beh' as 'better' rather than 'good' is rarely, if any, reported in [[Persian language|Persian]] language.<ref>{{Cite book |last=Dehkhoda |first=Ali Akbar |title=Dehkhoda Dictionary of Persian Language |publisher=Tehran University Publications |year=2007 |isbn=978-964-0393857 |location=Tehran |language=fa}}</ref> Also, there is no actual document of people using 'bahan' tents after the above-mentioned earthquakes. Resultantly, there needs to be more research on the etymology of the word Behbahan.

==History==

However, Arjan saw its downfall as it was hit by a series of earthquakes destroying almost all of its infrastructure. In the cross-road of [[Elam|Elamite]] and [[History of Iran|Persian]] empires, as claimed by Alvarez-Mon,<ref name=":0">{{Cite book |last=Alvarez-Mon |first=Javier |title=The Arjan Tomb: At the Crossroads of the Elamite and the Persian Empires |publisher=Peeters |year=2010 |isbn=9789042922006 |language=en}}</ref> the city had to be rebuilt.

The reconstruction was done by [[Kavadh I]] who was the [[Sasanian]] [[King of Kings]]<ref>{{Cite journal |last=Tyler-Smith |first=Susan |title=The Kavād Hoard |journal=The Numismatic Chronicle |volume=164 |pages=308–312}}</ref> of Iran from 488 to 531, with a two or three-year interruption.<ref name=s1>{{Cite web |title=Kavadh I {{!}} Sasanian Dynasty, Zoroastrianism & Revolt |url=https://www.britannica.com/biography/Kavadh-I |access-date=2024-06-01 |website=www.britannica.com |language=en}}</ref> In 502-503,<ref>{{Cite web |title=Kavad I - Livius |url=https://www.livius.org/articles/person/kavad-i/ |access-date=2024-06-01 |website=www.livius.org}}</ref> the king launched a campaign as part of the [[Anastasian War]] against northern [[Roman Mesopotamia]],<ref name=s1/> and [[Deportation|deported]] 80,000 prisoners from [[Amida (Mesopotamia)|Amida]], [[Erzurum|Theodosiopolis]], and possibly [[Martyropolis]] to [[Pars (Sasanian province)|Pars]], some of whom are thought to have helped rebuild the city of [[Arrajan]].<ref>{{Cite news |last=profilbaru.com |title=Amida (Mesopotamia) |url=https://profilbaru.com/article/Amida_(Mesopotamia) |access-date=2024-06-01 |language=en}}</ref> This is why the names 'Ram-Qobad', 'Beram-Qobad', 'Abar-Qobad' and 'Beh az Amed-e Kavad'<ref name=":0" /> all used by later historians to refer to the reconstructed [[Arrajan|Arjan]], include an element referring to king [[Kavad I]].<ref>{{Cite book |last=Kavir |first=Mahmood |title=The Phoenix Millennium: from the Sassanid to the Samanid |publisher=H & S Media |year=2017 |location=London |publication-date=2017 |language=fa}}</ref>

Through time, Arrajan experienced ups and downs of the history and managed to survive even the Arab Muslim invasion albeit at the cost of almost all residents having to convert from [[Zoroastrianism|Zoroasterianism]] to Islam. The name, as discussed above, was changed to Behbahan in the Islamic period.  

==Demographics==
===Population===
At the time of the 2006 National Census, the city's population was 99,204 in 24,204 households.<ref name="2006 census">{{cite web | title = Census of the Islamic Republic of Iran, 1385 (2006) | page = 06 | language = fa | publisher = The Statistical Center of Iran | website = AMAR | url = http://www.amar.org.ir/DesktopModules/FTPManager/upload/upload2360/newjkh/newjkh/06.xls | access-date = 25 September 2022 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20110920093911/http://www.amar.org.ir/DesktopModules/FTPManager/upload/upload2360/newjkh/newjkh/06.xls | format = Excel | archive-date = 20 September 2011}}</ref> The following census in 2011 counted 107,412 people in 29,280 households.<ref name="2011 census">{{cite web | title = Census of the Islamic Republic of Iran, 1390 (2011) | page = 06 | language = fa | publisher = The Statistical Center of Iran | website = Syracuse University | url = https://irandataportal.syr.edu/wp-content/uploads/Khuzestan.xls | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20230118191739/https://irandataportal.syr.edu/wp-content/uploads/Khuzestan.xls | archive-date = 18 January 2023 | access-date = 19 December 2022 | format = Excel}}</ref> The 2016 census measured the population of the city as 122,604 people in 35,826 households.<ref name="2016 census">{{cite web | title = Census of the Islamic Republic of Iran, 1395 (2016) | page = 06 | language = fa | publisher = The Statistical Center of Iran | website = AMAR | url = https://www.amar.org.ir/Portals/0/census/1395/results/abadi/CN95_HouseholdPopulationVillage_06.xlsx | access-date = 19 December 2022 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20201021081917/https://www.amar.org.ir/Portals/0/census/1395/results/abadi/CN95_HouseholdPopulationVillage_06.xlsx | format = Excel | archive-date = 21 October 2020}}</ref>
