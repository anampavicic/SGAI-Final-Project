'''Northern Mindanao''' ({{lang-ceb|Amihanang Mindanao}}; [[Maranao language|Maranao:]] ''Pangotaraan Mindanao''; {{lang-tl|Hilagang Mindanao}}) is an [[Regions of the Philippines|administrative region]] in the [[Philippines]], designated as '''Region X'''. It comprises five [[Provinces of the Philippines|provinces]]: [[Bukidnon]], [[Camiguin]], [[Misamis Occidental]], [[Misamis Oriental]], and [[Lanao del Norte]],  and two cities classified as ''highly urbanized'', all occupying the north-central part of [[Mindanao]] island, and the island-province of Camiguin. The regional center and largest city is [[Cagayan de Oro]]. Lanao del Norte was transferred to Northern Mindanao from [[Central Mindanao|Region XII]] (then called ''Central Mindanao'') by virtue of ''Executive Order No. 36'' in September 2001.<ref name="GovPH-EO36">{{cite web|title=Executive Order No. 36; Providing for the Reorganization of the Administrative Regions in Mindanao and for Other Purposes|url=https://www.officialgazette.gov.ph/2001/09/19/executive-order-no-36-s-2001/|website=Official Gazette of the Republic of the Philippines|access-date=March 29, 2016|location=Malacañang, Manila, Philippines|date=September 19, 2001|quote=SECTION 2. Region X, known as Northern Mindanao, is hereby reorganized and shall be composed of the following provinces and cities: a. Provinces i. Bukidnon ii. Camiguin iii. Lanao del Norte iv. Misamis Occidental v. Misamis Oriental b. Cities i. Cagayan de Oro City ii. Gingoog City iii. Iligan City iv. Malaybalay City v. Oroquieta City vi. Ozamis City vii. Tangub City viii. Valencia City, Bukidnon}}</ref>

==Etymology==
The current name of the region was derived from its position on [[Mindanao]] island. The term was officially coined by the Americans after the establishment of American colonial rule in the Philippines due to the defeat of Filipino revolutionaries. There have been proposals to rename the current Northern Mindanao region, which is dominated by the Cebuano ethnic group, into the ''Amihanan'' region. ''Amihanan'' literally translates to 'northern area' from the Cebuano language, which is the lingua franca of the region.

==Geography==

Northern Mindanao has a total land area of {{Convert|2049602|ha}}.<ref name=nscbprov/> More than 60% of Northern Mindanao's total land area are classified as [[forest]] land. Its seas abound with fish and other marine products. The abundant vegetation, natural springs and high elevation contribute to the region's cool, mild and invigorating climate.

===Administrative divisions===
{{Stack|float=right|[[File:Northern Mindanao.svg|right|250px|thumb|Map of Region X]]}}
The region is composed of five provinces: [[Bukidnon]], the island province of [[Camiguin]], [[Lanao del Norte]], [[Misamis Occidental]] and [[Misamis Oriental]]. It has two ''highly urbanized'' cities, [[Cagayan de Oro]] and [[Iligan]], that are administered independently from any province. The provinces are further subdivided into seven component cities and 84 municipalities, with a total of 2,022 [[barangay]]s. The regional center is [[Cagayan de Oro]].

Northern Mindanao is the 8th most populous region in the [[Philippines]] and 2nd in [[Mindanao]] (after [[Davao Region]]) according to the 2015 Philippine Statistics Authority Census. The major urban area of Region X is [[Metro Cagayan de Oro]] which includes Cagayan de Oro and some municipalities and cities of Misamis Oriental and Bukidnon.

The vast majority of the region's inhabitants descended from migrants from [[Cebu]], [[Bohol]], [[Siquijor]] and [[Negros Oriental]]. Some other inhabitants are of [[Bicolano people|Bicolano]], [[Hiligaynon people|Hiligaynon]], [[Ilocano language|Ilocano]], [[Ivatan people|Ivatan]], [[Kapampangan people|Kapampangan]], [[Pangasinan people|Pangasinan]], [[Tagalog people|Tagalog]], [[Waray people|Waray]], [[Zamboangueño people|Zamboangueño]], [[Maguindanao people|Maguindanaon]], [[Maranao people|Maranao]] and [[Tausug people|Tausug]] descent, while the autochthonous [[Lumad#Bukidnon|Bukidnons]] and [[Manobo]]s populate the inner highlands.

The largest religion is [[Christianity in the Philippines|Christianity]], with [[Roman Catholicism]] forming 72% of the population and Protestants forming a significant minority of 14%. The second largest religion is [[Islam in the Philippines|Islam]], forming 8% of the population.<ref>http://psa.gov.ph/sites/default/files/attachments/hsd/specialrelease/_REGION%20X_Statistical%20Tables.xls {{Dead link|date=October 2023}}</ref>

The main language spoken in the region is [[Cebuano language|Cebuano]] which is spoken with Northern Mindanao variant that is slightly different from standard Cebuano. The region is also home to sizeable speakers of [[Boholano dialect|Bohol dialect]] of Cebuano. [[Higaonon language|Higaonon]], [[Manobo languages|Manobo]] and [[Bukid language|Binukid]] are among the languages native in the region, as are [[Maguindanao language|Maguindanaon]] and [[Maranao language|Maranao]]. Also spoken varyingly in the region are [[Hiligaynon language|Hiligaynon]], [[Ilocano language|Ilocano]], [[Ivatan language|Ivatan]], [[Kapampangan language|Kapampangan]] and [[Waray language|Waray]]. [[Tagalog language|Tagalog]] ([[Filipino language|Filipino]]) and [[English language|English]] are widely understood and often used for administrative functions by the local government, business and in education.

==Economy==
{{Stack|float=left|{{PH poverty incidence}}}}
[[File:CAG Skyline Aug 2017.jpg|thumb|right|An aerial view of Cagayan de Oro as seen in August 2017]]
The economy of Northern Mindanao is the second largest regional economy in the island of Mindanao.<ref name="BWM">{{cite web|url=http://www.businessweekmindanao.com/2010/01/region-10-mindanaos-dominant-regional.html|title=Region 10: Mindanao's dominant regional economy|last=Pelone|first=Ruel V.|date=February 7, 2010|publisher=Business Week Mindanao|access-date=February 4, 2010|location=Cagayan de Oro|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110708105130/http://www.businessweekmindanao.com/2010/01/region-10-mindanaos-dominant-regional.html/|archive-date=July 8, 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref> While still a mainly agricultural region, there is also a booming growth of industries particularly in [[Cagayan de Oro]] and in [[Iligan]]. [[Del Monte Foods|Del Monte Philippines]] is located in the province of [[Bukidnon]] and its processing plant is located in [[Cagayan de Oro]], where it ships its products to the rest of the [[Philippines]] and Asia-Pacific region. The Agus-IV to VII Hydroelectric Plants in [[Iligan]] and [[Balo-i, Lanao del Norte]] supply most of its electrical power in Mindanao.
