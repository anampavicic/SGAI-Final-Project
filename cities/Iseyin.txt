'''Iseyin''' is a town located in [[Oyo State|Oyo]], [[Nigeria]]. It is approximately {{cvt|100|km}} north of [[Ibadan]]. The town was estimated to have a population of 236,000, according to [[United Nations]] 2005 estimate, which increased to 362,990 in 2011,<ref>National Population Commission of Nigeria (web)</ref> and has a total land mass of {{cvt|2,341|km2}}.<ref>{{Cite journal|last1=Adewuyi|first1=Kehinde|last2=Adeyemo|last3=Adejumo|date=December 2018|title=Use of GIS in production of soil series map in Oyo State, southwestern Nigeria|url=http://www.isca.me/EARTH_SCI/Archive/v6/i12/2.ISCA-IRJES-2018-019.pdf|journal=International Research Journal of Earth Sciences|volume=6|issue=12|pages=12–21|issn=2321-2527|via=www.isca.in}}</ref> Its inhabitants are mostly of [[Yoruba people|Yoruba]] origin and its ruler is the [[Aseyin]] of Iseyin, with the current being [[Oba Sefiu Oyebola Adeyeri III, Ajirotutu I]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Adegbite |first=Ademola |date=2023-12-14 |title=Makinde presents staff to Aseyin |url=https://punchng.com/makinde-presents-staff-to-aseyin/ |access-date=2023-12-25 |website=Punch Newspapers |language=en-US}}</ref>

==Education==
The town houses College of Agriculture and Renewarable Natural Sciences of Ladoke Akintola University of Technology, Iseyin campus. The town also has a private Polytechnic named SAF Polytechnic, Iseyin, a Government Technical College and the Oyo State NYSC (National Youth Service Corps) permanent orientation camp.<ref>{{Cite web |last= |first= |date=2022-12-29 |title=LAUTECH governing council lauds Makinde over new Iseyin campus |url=https://guardian.ng/features/lautech-governing-council-lauds-makinde-over-new-iseyin-campus/ |access-date=2023-08-08 |website=The Guardian Nigeria News - Nigeria and World News |language=en-US}}</ref>

The town has a number of secondary schools like SAF Poly International School, Kelani College Iseyin, Saint Thomas College, Greater Love Model College, Iseyin District Grammar School, Ansarudeen Grammar School,  Muslim Grammar School, ETC.<ref name="Map - Iseyin - MAPNALL.COM">{{Cite web |title=Map - Iseyin - MAP[N]ALL.COM |url=https://www.mapnall.org/en/Map-Iseyin_1332629.html |access-date=2023-06-21 |website=www.mapnall.org}}</ref>

Iseyin also has an ultra modern private Digital Library named Raji Oke-Esa Memorial Library, the library was commissioned by the Nigeria former president, General (Rtd.) [[Olusegun Obasanjo]], and Ebedi Writers' Residency situated at the hill-side of Barracks area of the city. This International Residency has brought great writers, journalists and authors all over the world including Africa's first Nobel Laurel, Prof. Wole Soyinka, Jumoke Verissiomo, Funmi Aluko, Richard Ali, Paul Liam and others.<ref name="Map - Iseyin - MAPNALL.COM"/>

==Industry==
Iseyin is the headquarters of Oke-Ogun zone of Oyo state. A Sugar Cane Processing Company was established in the town. The primary industry of the area is [[cotton]]-based [[textiles]].<ref name="Columbia Encyclopaedia"> {{cite web | url = http://www.encyclopedia.com/doc/1E1-Iseyin.html | title = The Columbia Encyclopedia | access-date = 2007-04-06}}</ref> Iseyin is the fourth largest built environment in Oyo state, after Ibadan, Ogbomoso and arguably Oyo. Iseyin is also known as the home of [[Aso Òkè fabric|Aso Òkè]]. Aso Òkè or Òfì is a popular traditional fabric mostly used for ceremonies amongst the Yoruba people of Nigeria.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-09-24 |title=The History of Iseyin, Nigeria's Home of Aso-oke Iseyin is an ancient city |url=https://refinedng.com/the-history-of-iseyin-nigerias-home-of-aso-oke/ |access-date=2023-08-08 |website=RefinedNG |language=en-US}}</ref>

==Agriculture==
[[Tobacco]] is grown in Iseyin, amongst other food and cash crops,<ref name="Columbia Encyclopaedia" /> it is as a result of this that a big tobacco company - British American Tobacco - has a "leave office" in the town. Iseyin is part of the Oke-Ogun towns referred to as the "food basket" of [[Nigeria]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Tobi |first=Majaro |title=Iseyin search |url=https://www.academia.edu/37803172}}</ref>

Because of sustainable annual [[rainfall]] in the area, the major agricultural activities are [[Agriculture|farming]], [[hunting]], [[fishing]], and food processing, among others. Iseyin produces virtually all farm produces such as [[yam (vegetable)|yam]], [[maize]], [[cassava]], [[Cooking banana|plantain]] among many others.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-09-24 |title=The History of Iseyin, Nigeria's Home of Aso-oke Iseyin is an ancient city |url=https://refinedng.com/the-history-of-iseyin-nigerias-home-of-aso-oke/ |access-date=2023-08-07 |website=RefinedNG |language=en-US}}</ref>

==Ikere Gorge dam==
Ikere Gorge dam is located in the town, it was said to be the second largest [[dam]] when it was discovered alongside Kanji dam during President Obasanjo's regime (Military). Ikere gorge dam is a man-made dam, It is a source of water for communities in Iseyin and its suburbs. During the [[rain]]y season, the dam swells to full capacity. Tons of fish are produced from the dam, which can be a source of IGR for the government. The dam when maintained, is capable of generating electricity for the whole South-West states of Nigeria. The dam can be used for irrigation and tourist center.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.premiumtimesng.com/investigationspecial-reports/436794-ikere-gorge-dam-nigerias-abandoned-hydropower-fish-production-facility.html?tztc=1 |access-date=2023-06-21 |website=www.premiumtimesng.com}}</ref>

== Traditional weaving ==
The primary industry of Iseyin is cotton-based textiles, and Iseyin reputed as the [http://sunnewsonline.com/i-s-e-y-i-n-home-of-yorubas-aso-ofi/ home of Aso Ofi or Aso Oke], a popular traditional fabric worn on special occasions by the Yoruba usually for coronation, chieftaincy, wedding engagement, festivals, naming ceremony, and other important events.<ref>{{Cite journal |last1=Diyaolu |first1=Idowu |last2=Omotosho |first2=Halima |date=2020-01-01 |title=Aso Òkè Weaving Techniques and Perception in Iseyin, Oyo State, Nigeria |url=https://digitalcommons.unl.edu/tsaconf/1139 |journal=Textile Society of America Symposium Proceedings}}</ref>

The maiden Aso-Ofi festival was observed on September 27, 2016, during World Tourism Day. The festival was conceptualized to showcase and celebrate the locally made fabric.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://sunnewsonline.com/i-s-e-y-i-n-home-of-yorubas-aso-ofi/|title=I-S-E-Y-I-N : Home of Yoruba's Aso-Ofi|website=Sun Newspaper|date=21 September 2017 }}</ref>

== Climate ==
