'''Samarinda''' is the [[capital city]] of the [[Provinces of Indonesia|Indonesian province]] of [[East Kalimantan]] on the island of [[Borneo]]. The city lies on the banks of the [[Mahakam River]] with a land area of {{cvt|718|km2}}. Samarinda is known for its traditional food ''[[amplang]]'', as well as the cloth ''sarung samarinda''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://gamezxz133.blogspot.com/?m=1|title=Perhatikan 4 Hal Ini Saat Berburu Oleh-oleh Khas Samarinda|work=JalanTikus}}</ref> As of 2021, the city has 3 bridges connecting its river banks: [[Mahakam Bridge]], {{ill|Mahakam Ulu Bridge|id|Jembatan Mahakam Ulu}}, and {{ill|Achmad Amins Bridge|id|Jembatan Achmad Amins}}. The city center is on one side and the other side is named Samarinda Seberang.

==Etymology==
The name Samarinda originates from the description of the way in which the [[Bugis]] houses were constructed. At that time houses were customarily built on a raft and generally had the same height. This provided important social symbolism of equality between residents; no person's house, and thus no person, was seen as higher or lower than another. They named the settlement “Samarenda”, meaning “equally low”. After hundreds of years of use the pronunciation of the name changed slightly and the city became known as Samarinda.<ref>{{Cite book|title=Asal-Usul Nama Samarinda Sejak Zaman sebelum Kemerdekaan, Nama Ini Sudah Terkenal di Seluruh Indonesia|last=Dahlan|first=Oemar|publisher=Ministry of Education and Culture|year=1978|location=Jakarta}}</ref>

== History ==
At the start of the Gowa War, the [[Netherlands|Dutch]] under Admiral Speelman's command attacked [[Makassar]] from the sea. Meanwhile, the Netherlands' [[Bugis people|Bugis]] ally [[Arung Palakka]] led a ground attack. The [[Sultanate of Gowa|Kingdom of Gowa]] was forced to surrender and [[Hasanuddin of Gowa|Sultan Hasanuddin]] was made to sign the [[Treaty of Bongaja]] on 19 November 1667.<ref name=":0">{{Cite book|title=Sejarah Kota Samarinda|last=Ars|first=Moh|publisher=Ministry of Education and Culture|year=1986|location=Jakarta}}</ref>

The treaty did not quell all trouble for the Dutch however, since the [[Bugis]] from Gowa continued their struggle using guerilla tactics.{{cn|date=September 2024}} Some Buginese moved to other islands close by such as Kalimantan. A few thousand people led by Lamohang Daeng Mangkona or Pua Ado I, moved to East Kalimantan, known then as [[Kutai]], where they were welcomed by the local Sultan.{{cn|date=September 2024}}

Samarinda was a small, sleepy town in 1942 with several small oil fields in the vicinity. It was occupied by the Japanese after the [[Dutch East Indies]] had fallen.<ref>{{cite web|first=Klemen |last=L |url= https://warfare.gq/dutcheastindies/borneo.html |title=The conquest of Borneo Island, 1941-1942 |date=1999–2000 |work=Forgotten Campaign: The Dutch East Indies Campaign 1941-1942}}</ref>

In 1955, the Apostolic Vicariate of Samarinda was established in the city.  In 1961, it was promoted as the Diocese of Samarinda.  In 2003, the diocese was promoted as the [[Metropolitan Archdiocese of Samarinda]].<ref name=":0" />

==Climate==
Samarinda has a [[tropical rainforest climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification|Köppen]]: ''Af'') with heavy rainfall and hot, oppressively humid temperatures year-round. Hail is extremely rare, it was recorded on 21 November 2019.<ref>{{cite news |title=Hujan Es di Samarinda Seberang, Biasanya Terjadi di Awal Musim Hujan|url=https://kaltim.tribunnews.com/2019/11/21/hujan-es-di-samarinda-seberang-biasanya-terjadi-di-awal-musim-hujan|access-date=28 September 2021|newspaper=Tribun News|date=21 November 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210928082558/https://kaltim.tribunnews.com/2019/11/21/hujan-es-di-samarinda-seberang-biasanya-terjadi-di-awal-musim-hujan|archive-date=28 September 2021 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Viral, Hujan Es Bikin Heboh Warga Samarinda|url=https://kaltim.idntimes.com/news/kaltim/yuda-almerio-pratama-lebang/viral-hujan-es-bikin-heboh-warga-samarinda/2|access-date=28 September 2021|newspaper=IDN Times|date=21 November 2019|archive-url=https://archive.today/20210928085805/https://kaltim.idntimes.com/news/kaltim/yuda-almerio-pratama-lebang/viral-hujan-es-bikin-heboh-warga-samarinda/3|archive-date=28 September 2021|url-status=live}}</ref> The lowest recorded temperature in Samarinda is {{convert|18.0|°C|°F|sigfig=3}} in October 1982,<ref>{{cite news|title=Samarinda Alami Fenomena Aphelion, Apa Itu?|url=https://sapos.co.id/2021/07/07/samarinda-alami-fenomena-aphelion-apa-itu/|access-date=28 September 2021|newspaper=Samarinda Post|date=7 July 2021|archive-url=https://archive.today/20210928090541/https://sapos.co.id/2021/07/07/samarinda-alami-fenomena-aphelion-apa-itu/|archive-date=28 September 2021|url-status=live}}</ref> while the highest recorded is {{convert|40.2|°C|°F|sigfig=3}} on 8 February 2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://kaltim.tribunnews.com/2019/10/23/bmkg-catat-suhu-terpanas-di-indonesia-wilayah-di-kalimantan-catatkan-rekor-maksimum|title=BMKG Catat Suhu Terpanas di Indonesia, Wilayah di Kalimantan Catatkan Rekor Maksimum|website=kaltim.tribunnews.com|access-date=2024-02-27}}</ref>

==Demographics==

The territory's population in mid 2023 was 861,876, with an annual growth rate of 1.43% in 2022–2023.<ref name="Badan Pusat Statistik 2024">Badan Pusat Statistik, Jakarta, 28 February 2024, ''Kota Samarinda Dalam Angka 2024'' (Katalog-BPS 1102001.6472)</ref> The majority of the people of Samarinda are of Native Indonesian and Chinese descent. There are also Americans, Canadians, Japanese and Koreans working in Samarinda. Life expectancy in Samarinda was 73.6 years as of 2014.

===Religion===
[[File:Islamic Centre Samarinda by bloesafir - panoramio.jpg|thumb|[[Samarinda Islamic Center Mosque]], the largest mosque in Samarinda]]
Samarinda's main religions are Islam and Christianity. The Muslim community forms the majority of the population numbering around 91%. The Christian community numbers at nearly 66,000 individuals or slightly over 7% of the total population; Protestants form a larger number than [[Roman Catholic Church|Roman Catholics]] at a rate of 10:3. There are also small Buddhist, [[Hinduism|Hindu]] and other religious communities.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Badan Pusat Statistik |url=https://samarindakota.bps.go.id/indicator/12/217/1/jumlsah-penduduk-menurut-kecamatan-dan-agama-yang-dianut-di-kota-samarinda.html |access-date=2024-06-06 |website=samarindakota.bps.go.id}}</ref>

==Economy==
[[File:Malampalaran.jpg|thumb|Samarinda is [[East Borneo|East Kalimantan]]'s largest container ports]]

Since 2004, trade has been the engine of Samarinda's economic growth.<ref>{{cite web|title=Samarinda in Figures 2005|work=BPS Samarinda|access-date=28 September 2021|url=https://samarindakota.bps.go.id/publication/2006/07/31/71d59f68c71045b7da96d872/kota-samarinda-dalam-angka-2005.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210928092515/https://samarindakota.bps.go.id/publication/2006/07/31/71d59f68c71045b7da96d872/kota-samarinda-dalam-angka-2005.html|archive-date=28 September 2021|url-status=live}}</ref> It is also driven by the large amount of logging and oil extraction companies based there. Similar to Balikpapan, many national logging companies are based in Samarinda.{{cn|date=September 2024}} There are many abandoned coal mines in Samarinda. Coal mining used to be very popular in Samarinda. However, the Indonesian government revoked many mining licenses due to the use of illegal chemicals and machinery.{{cn|date=September 2024}}

Tourism sector also plays an important role in Samarinda's economy; it attracted over 2,000 international tourists and 1.2 million domestic tourists in 2019,<ref>{{cite web|title=Samarinda in Figures 2020|work=BPS Samarinda|access-date=26 September 2021|url=https://samarindakota.bps.go.id/publication/2020/04/27/4f263d1ab55ba7b650c25f14/kota-samarinda-dalam-angka-2020.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210628191146/https://samarindakota.bps.go.id/publication/2020/04/27/4f263d1ab55ba7b650c25f14/kota-samarinda-dalam-angka-2020.html|archive-date=28 June 2021|url-status=live}}</ref> making Samarinda the 2nd most popular tourists destination in [[East Borneo|East Kalimantan]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Orang Samarinda dan Bontang paling gemar berwisata, Derawan paling favorite|work=Niaga Asia|date=20 November 2021 |access-date=20 November 2021|url=https://www.niaga.asia/orang-samarinda-dan-bontang-paling-gemar-berwisata-derawan-paling-favorit/|archive-url=https://archive.today/20211120144530/https://www.niaga.asia/orang-samarinda-dan-bontang-paling-gemar-berwisata-derawan-paling-favorit/|archive-date=20 November 2021|url-status=live}}</ref> In 2020, agriculture constituted only 2 per cent of [[GDP]], and consists of growing flower varieties (rose, jasmine, orchid) and fruits (pomelo citrus fruit).<ref name=samarinda2021/>

There are about 850,000 vehicles registered in Samarinda, the highest number of vehicles of any city in East Kalimantan.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://kaltim.bps.go.id/publication/2022/02/25/30d7f530df6226df3d80356e/provinsi-kalimantan-timur-dalam-angka-2022.html|title=East Kalimantan in Figures, 2022|website=BPS East Kalimantan|language=en|access-date=31 May 2022}}</ref> Samarinda has 3 bus terminals: Sungai Kunjang Bus Terminus, Lempake Bus Terminus and Seberang Bus Terminus.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://kaltimprov.go.id/halaman/terminal-smd|title=Terminal Bus, Samarinda|website=Government of East Kalimantan|language=id|access-date=31 May 2022}}</ref>

Samarinda is connected by [[Trans-Kalimantan Highway Southern Route]] from [[Balikpapan]] to Samarinda; the highway runs in parallel with the first [[controlled-access highway|controlled-access expressway]] in Borneo, the [[Samarinda-Balikpapan Expressway]], which is now under construction, and expected to be operational by the end of 2018.<ref name="kaltara">[http://preservasi-pupera.info/prv/wp-content/uploads/2016/07/PETA-PROVINSI-KALIMANTAN-UTARA.pdf North Kalimantan Province Road Map] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180429025047/http://preservasi-pupera.info/prv/wp-content/uploads/2016/07/PETA-PROVINSI-KALIMANTAN-UTARA.pdf |date=29 April 2018 }} - by the Road Preservation Directorate, Ministry of Public Works and Public Housing, Indonesia.</ref>

[[Samarinda International Airport|APT Pranoto (Samarinda Sungai Siring, AAP) International Airport]] is the primary airport for the city and has been at Sungai Siring since 2018. With over 1 million passengers annually, it is one of East Kalimantan's busiest airports in terms of passenger and cargo movements. AAP is an important Australian passenger gateway for East Kalimantan's wildlife. AAP was built to replace [[Temindung Airport]] in Bandara, Sungai Pinang.

The prominent coal loading port of [[Tanjung Bara]] (TBCT) lies about 160 kilometres to the north of Samarinda.<ref name="asd">{{cite book|title=Admiralty sailing directions - Indonesia|date=15 July 2015|publisher=UK Hydrographic office|location=Taunton|edition=10th}}</ref>
