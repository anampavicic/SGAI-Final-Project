'''Kasese''' is a town in the [[Western Region, Uganda|Western Region]] of [[Uganda]].  It is the capital of [[Kasese District]]. Kasese is also the largest town in the [[Rwenzururu region]]. In 2020 it had an estimated population 115,400. It lies north of [[Lake George (Uganda)|Lake George]] and east of [[Rwenzori Mountains]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Home |url=https://www.kasese.go.ug/ |access-date=2023-04-16 |website=KASESE DISTRICT |language=en-US}}</ref>

==Population==
The national census of 1969 enumerated the population of Kasese Town at 7,213 people. In 1980, the population census that year put the population of the town at 9,917. In 1991, the national census numerated 18,750 inhabitants in Kasese. That population had increased to 85,697 people, according to the 2002 national census. On 27 August 2014, the census and national housing survey enumerated 101,065 people in Kasese Town Council.<ref name="5R">{{cite web|url=https://www.citypopulation.de/en/uganda/cities/ | title=The population of all Ugandan cities and towns with more than 15,000 inhabitants according to census results and latest official projections |work=Citypopulation.de Quoting [[Uganda Bureau of Statistics]] |date=14 June 2020 | author=Uganda Bureau of Statistics |access-date=18 August 2021}}</ref>

In 2020, the [[Uganda Bureau of Statistics]] (UBOS), estimated the mid-year population of the town at 115,400 inhabitants. UBOS calculated that the population of Kasese Municipality increased at an average rate of 2.3 percent annually, between 2014 and 2020.<ref name="5R"/>

==Population dynamics==
[[File:Old Kasese Train Station.jpg|thumb|Old Kasese Train Station]]{{Historical populations| 1969|7,213|1980|9,917|1991|18,750|2002|85,697|2014|101,065|2020|

During the 1990s Kasese Town was one of the fastest growing municipalities in Uganda. The reasons for this rapid population growth include:<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.newvision.co.ug/new_vision/news/1336451/causes-river-nyamambwa-floods | title=Causes of River Nyamambwa floods | location=Kampala | access-date=13 May 2016 | date=10 January 2014 | newspaper=[[New Vision]] |
author=Alex Kwatampora Binego}}</ref>

* Increased tourism - Kasese is the gateway to Queen Elizabeth National Park, one of the most popular in Uganda, and the Rwenzori National Park.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.ugandawildlife.org/explore-our-parks/parks-by-name-a-z/rwenzori-mountains-national-park | title=Rwenzori Mountains National Park: Tourism: "Kasese, 437 km west of Uganda's capital Kampala, is the gateway to the park" | access-date=13 May 2016 | date=13 May 2016 | location=Entebbe | publisher=Ugandawildlife.org (UWO) | last=UWO | url-status=dead | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160522235502/http://www.ugandawildlife.org/explore-our-parks/parks-by-name-a-z/rwenzori-mountains-national-park | archive-date=22 May 2016 }}</ref>
* Kilembe Mines employs a large number of workers; over 3,000 as of January 2015.<ref name="Cblt"/><ref>{{cite web | newspaper=[[The EastAfrican]] | url=https://www.theeastafrican.co.ke/business/Copper-production-to-resume-at-Kilembe-/-/2560/2585030/-/tgtux1/-/index.html | title=Copper production to resume at Kilembe | access-date=13 May 2016 | date=10 January 2015 | last=Khisa |first=Isaac | location=Nairobi}}</ref>
* [[Hima Cement Limited]] is another big employer located in [[Hima, Uganda|Hima]], approximately {{convert|24|km|0}}, by road, north of Kasese.<ref>{{cite web | access-date=13 May 2016 | date=13 May 2016 | last=GFC | url=http://distancecalculator.globefeed.com/Uganda_Distance_Result.asp?fromplace=Kasese%2C%20Western%20Region%2C%20Uganda&toplace=Hima%2C%20Western%20Region%2C%20Uganda%2C&dt1=ChIJ27nqCi3yYRcRcP7Ml11wWSQ&dt2=ChIJsaDATV4gYhcR9dqQJYs5yDU | title=Distance between Kasese, Western Region, Uganda and Hima, Western Region, Uganda | publisher=Globefeed.com (GFC)}}</ref>
* Increased trade with the eastern districts of the DRC. The border town of [[Mpondwe]] is only {{convert|60|km|mi}} south-west of Kasese.<ref name="3R"/>

==Industry==

Kasese Cobalt Company Limited (KCCL), located on the road from Kasese to [[Rubirizi]] just south of the central business district of Kasese, extracts cobalt from the sludge left after copper is extracted from the raw ore.<ref name="Cblt">{{cite web|publisher=China Africa Reporting|first=Fredrick|
