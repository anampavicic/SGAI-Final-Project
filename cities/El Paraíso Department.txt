'''El Paraíso''' ({{IPA|es|el paɾaˈiso}}) is one of the 18 [[Departments of Honduras|departments]] ''(departamentos)'' into which [[Honduras]] is divided.

The territory of El Paraíso was initially part of the departments of [[Francisco Morazán Department|Tegucigalpa]] (renamed Francisco Morazán in 1943) and [[Olancho Department|Olancho]] after Central America gained its independence in 1825. The department of El Paraíso was created with municipalities taken from the departments of Tegucigalpa and Olancho on 28 May 1869 by congressional decree in the third political division of Honduras, during the presidential term of [[José María Medina]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.xplorhonduras.com/departamento-de-el-paraiso/ |title=Departamento de El Paraiso|website=XplorHonduras.com. The departmental capital is [[Yuscarán]]. El Paraíso is bordered to the north by the department of Olancho, to the south by the department of de Choluteca, to the east by the [[Nicaragua|Republic of Nicaragua]], and to the west by the department of Francisco Morazán.

El Paraíso department covers a total surface area of 7489&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> and, in 2015, had an estimated population of 458,742.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ine.gob.hn/V3/proyecciones-por-departamento-2014-2030/|title=PROYECCIONES POR DEPARTAMENTO 2014-2030 – INE|website=www.ine.gob.hn}}</ref>
