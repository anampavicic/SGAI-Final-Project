'''Delhi Cantonment''' ([[ISO 15919|ISO]]: ''Dillī Chāvanī''; popularly referred to as '''Delhi Cantt''') is a Class I [[Cantonment board|Cantonment Board]] established in 1914. The area of the Cantonment is {{convert|10452|acre|km2}} and the population of the Cantonment as per the [[2011 Census of India|2011 census]] is 110,351.<ref name="About us">{{Cite web|title=About Us – DELHI CANTONMENT BOARD|url=https://delhi.cantt.gov.in/about-us/|access-date=2022-02-13|website=delhi.cantt.gov.in}}</ref>
[[File:Three Municipalities of Delhi as of 2022.png|thumb|left|Municipalities of Delhi]]
The Cantonment is governed by the Cantonments Act, 2006<ref>Document, http://www.cbdelhi.in/Documents/ca2006.pdf {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140531124052/http://www.cbdelhi.in/Documents/ca2006.pdf |date=31 May 2014 }}</ref> while various policy letters and instructions from the [[Ministry of Defence (India)|Ministry of Defence]] pertaining to the area are issued from time to time. Although the board functions as a local municipal body, it remains under the administrative control of the Directorate General Defence Estates, New Delhi and Principal Director, Defence Estates, [[Western Command (India)|Western Command]], [[Chandigarh]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.cbdelhi.in/history.aspx |title=Delhi Cantonment Board, Ministry of Defence |access-date=30 May 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140531105836/http://www.cbdelhi.in/history.aspx |archive-date=31 May 2014 |url-status=dead |df=dmy-all }}</ref>

It is one of three local bodies in the [[National Capital Territory of Delhi]], the others being [[Municipal Corporation of Delhi]] and [[New Delhi Municipal Council]].

==History==
[[Cantonment]]s in Delhi and [[Ahmedabad Cantonment|Ahmedabad]] were originally established by the British. The Delhi Cantonment houses the [[Indian Army]] Headquarters, Delhi Area; the Army Golf Course; the Defence Services Officers Institute; military housing; Army and Air Force Public Schools; and various other defence-related installations. The cantonment also houses the [[Army Hospital Research and Referral]], a tertiary care medical centre of the armed forces of India.

There is a railway station within the cantonment, [[Delhi Cantonment railway station]], from where trains depart for various parts of the country.

==Transport==
Delhi Cantonment is connected by road, railway and air. [[Indira Gandhi International Airport]] is about 5 kilometres from Delhi Cantonment. Its nearest metro stations are [[Delhi Cantonment metro station|North Delhi Cantonment]] on the [[Pink Line (Delhi Metro)|Pink Line]], [[Sadar Bazaar Cantonment metro station|South Delhi Cantonment]] and [[Shankar Vihar metro station]] on the [[Magenta Line (Delhi Metro)|Magenta Line]] and [[Dhaula Kuan metro station]] on the [[Orange Line (Delhi Metro)| Airport Express]]. Delhi Cantonment has a [[Delhi Cantonment railway station|railway station]]. Almost all the trains plying from Delhi towards Rajasthan or Gujarat have either a stop here or originates from this station itself.

It is easily accessible by road and local bus to the residents of nearby residential neighbourhoods such as [[Palam]], [[Dabri, Delhi|Dabri]], [[Dwarka, Delhi|Dwarka]],  [[Aerocity]], [[Dhaula Kuan]], [[Delhi University| DU South Campus]], [[Tilak Nagar (Delhi)|Tilak Nagar]], [[Hari Nagar]], [[Mayapuri]], [[Sagarpur]], [[Vasant Vihar]], [[Vasant Kunj]], [[Naraina]], [[Inder Puri]] and [[Janakpuri]].
