'''Bentong''' ([[Jawi alphabet|Jawi]]: ﺑﻨﺘﻮڠ), the seat of [[Bentong District]], is a town located in western [[Pahang]], Malaysia, at the border with the state of [[Selangor]] in the west and the state of [[Negeri Sembilan]] in the south.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.themalaysianinsight.com/s/12573/|title = Bentong declared as ecotourism destination &#124; the Malaysian Insight}}</ref>

== Government ==
Bentong Municipal Council ({{lang-ms|Majlis Perbandaran Bentong}}) is the local authority for the whole of Bentong District including Bentong town. From 5 May 1955 until 30 June 1981, Bentong town was governed by the Bentong Town Council ({{lang-ms|Majlis Bandaran Bentong}}). On 1 July 1981, the Bentong Town Council was merged with 5 local councils of Karak, Sungai Dua, Telemong, Manchis and Bukit Tinggi and 8 village councils of Kampung Shafie, Kampung Simpang Pelangai, Sungai Gapoi New Village, Kampung Jambu Rias, Kampung Benus, Genting Sempah, Kampung Janda Baik and Sungai Penjuring New Village to form the Bentong District Council ({{lang-ms|Majlis Daerah Bentong}}).

The administrative area of Bentong District Council at the time of establishment was {{convert|106.18|km2|mi2}}, consisting {{convert|80.26|km2|mi2}} operational area and {{convert|25.92|km2|mi2}} control area. It was then increased to {{convert|1105.19|km2|mi2}} on 1 January 2001 consisting new operational area of {{convert|867.69|km2|mi2}} and control area of {{convert|237.5|km2|mi2}}. Bentong District Council was upgraded to the present-day Municipal Council on 16 July 2005.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mpbentong.gov.my/ms/mpb/profil/latar-belakang|title=Latar Belakang}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mpbentong.gov.my/en/mpb/profile/background|title=Background}}</ref>

[[Bentong (federal constituency)|Bentong]] is currently represented in the [[Dewan Rakyat]] by [[Young Syefura Othman]] from [[Democratic Action Party|DAP]]. Bentong has four seats in [[Pahang State Legislative Assembly]]. [[Bilut (state constituency)|Bilut]], [[Ketari (state constituency)|Ketari]], [[Sabai (state constituency)|Sabai]] and [[Pelangai (state constituency)|Pelangai]]. Both [[Bilut (state constituency)|Bilut]] and [[Ketari (state constituency)|Ketari]] are held by [[Pakatan Harapan]] while [[Sabai (state constituency)|Sabai]] and [[Pelangai (state constituency)|Pelangai]] are held by [[Barisan Nasional]].
