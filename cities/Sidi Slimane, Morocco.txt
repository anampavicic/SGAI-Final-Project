'''Sidi Slimane''' ({{langx|ar|سيدي سليمان}}) is a small city in the northwestern centre of [[Morocco]] in the [[Rabat-Salé-Kénitra]] economic region. It is the administrative headquarters for [[Sidi Slimane Province]] and is located between the major cities of [[Kenitra]] and [[Meknes]].

The city recorded a population of 92,989 in the [[2014 Moroccan census]],<ref name=census2014/> up from 78,060 inhabitants in 2004.

The economy is mainly focused on agriculture. The population consists mainly of rural migrants. The society is still plagued with major problems such as [[illiteracy]], unemployment and slums.<ref>{{Cite web|date=8 November 2010|title=Sidi Slimane: Nouvelle approche contre l'habitat insalubre (Sidi Slimane: New Approach against slums)|language=French|publisher=Sidi Slimane City|url=http://www.sidislimanecity.com/index.php/regionales/1413-sidi-slimanenouvelle-approche-contre-lhabitat-insalubre.html|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140528211715/http://www.sidislimanecity.com/index.php/regionales/1413-sidi-slimanenouvelle-approche-contre-lhabitat-insalubre.html|archivedate=28 May 2014|url-status=live}} citing {{Cite news|author=Boumehdi, Ahmed|date=7 November 2010|title=Sidi Slimane: Nouvelle approche contre l'habitat insalubre|newspaper=Le Matin du Sahara et du Maghreb}}</ref>  Sidi Slimane is mostly renowned for its quality citrus products.  It has three high schools, a public library, a downtown called "filaj" (village in French) and a local stadium.

Sidi Slimane is home to a Moroccan Air Force base, [[Sidi Slimane Air Base]].

It lies on the main railway line from [[Tanger]] to [[Oujda]].
