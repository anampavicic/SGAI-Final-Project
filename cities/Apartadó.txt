'''Apartadó''' ({{IPA|es|apaɾtaˈðo}}) is a town and [[Municipalities of Colombia|municipality]] in the [[Antioquia Department]], [[Colombia]]. It is part of the [[Urabá Antioquia|Urabá]] sub-region.

"Apartadó" means "river of plantains" in the local Indian language. The town is located near the Atlantic Ocean in the [[Gulf of Urabá]], the economy is based in bananas, plantain, corn, cassava, cocoa, wood and livestock. The mean maximum temperature is {{convert|30|C|F}} and the relative humidity is above 80% all year round.

Apartadó is divided in 48 neighborhoods, and here is the best high school of the region of [[Urabá Antioquia]]. Today,{{when|date=October 2018}} the government is stimulating industrialization because it is near the [[Caribbean Sea]] and to the center of the country.
The municipality had a population of 127,744 in 2020.<ref name="pop" />

==Climate==
Apartadó has a [[tropical rainforest climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification|Köppen]] ''Af'') with heavy rainfall year-round.
