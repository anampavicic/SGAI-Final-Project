The '''Limassol District''',{{Efn|{{langx|el|Επαρχία Λεμεσού|translit=Eparkhía Lemesoú}} {{IPA-el|/éparxía lemesú/|}}; {{langx|tr|Limasol Kazası}}}} or simply '''Limassol'''{{Efn|{{IPAc-en|ˈ|l|ɪ|m|ə|s|ɒ|l}}}} or '''Lemesos''',{{Efn|{{langx|el|Λεμεσός|translit=Lemesos}} {{IPA-el|/lemesós/|}}; {{langx|tr|Leymosun}} or ''Limasol''}} is one of the six [[districts of Cyprus]]. {{As of|2021}}, it had a population of 262,238, 75,7% of which was urban<ref>{{Cite web |title=CENSUS OF POPULATION AND HOUSING 2021: PRELIMINARY RESULTS BY DISTRICT AND MUNICIPALITY/COMMUNITY |url=https://library.cystat.gov.cy/NEW/Census2021-Preliminary_Results-EN-040823.pdf |access-date=10 March 2024 |archive-date=12 August 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230812233809/https://library.cystat.gov.cy/NEW/Census2021-Preliminary_Results-EN-040823.pdf |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Cyprus population has grown 9.9% in a decade |url=https://www.cbn.com.cy/article/2023/8/8/726603/cyprus-population-has-grown-99-in-a-decade/ |access-date=10 March 2024 |website=Cyprus population has grown 9.9% in a decade |language=en |archive-date=10 March 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240310212147/https://www.cbn.com.cy/article/2023/8/8/726603/cyprus-population-has-grown-99-in-a-decade/ |url-status=live }}</ref> The district's capital city is [[Limassol]]. Part of the [[British Overseas Territory]] of [[Akrotiri and Dhekelia]] forms an enclave on the [[Akrotiri Peninsula (Cyprus)|Akrotiri Peninsula]], under the sovereignty of the [[United Kingdom]].

==History==
The buried ancient city of [[Amathus]] is {{convert|11|km}} from Limassol. Archaeological excavations have unearthed ruins of the Byzantine period and a tomb of the 7th century BC.{{Sfn|Weiß|Sabo|Peitz-Hlebec|Schwarz|2001|p=28}} The ancient [[Kolossi Castle]], which is located {{convert|9|km}} to the west of Limassol, reflects the fall of [[Acre, Israel|Acre]] and history of the [[Templars]] and their confiscated property allotted to the Limassol District for cultivation of wine and sugarcane.{{Sfn|Weiß|Sabo|Peitz-Hlebec|Schwarz|2001|pp=26, 28}}

==Geography==
Limassol District forms much of the southwestern-central part of Cyprus. The [[Kouris River]] rises in the southern slopes of [[Troodos mountains]], which lie in the northern part of the district towards the centre of Cyprus, and flows to the sea near the ancient city of [[Kourion]]. This river has been dammed by the [[Kouris Dam]], which has caused the near drying up of the river in its lower reaches.{{Sfn|Jasink|Bombardieri|2010|p=1}} Limassol, to the northeast of the Akrotiri peninsula lies on [[Akrotiri Bay]], while [[Pissouri]], to the northwest of the peninsula, lies on [[Episkopi Bay]].<ref>{{Google maps |url=https://www.google.com/maps/place/34%C2%B047%2700.0%22N+32%C2%B055%2700.0%22E/@34.6722618,32.9724119,43970m/data=!3m1!1e3!4m2!3m1!1s0x0:0x0?hl=en |access-date=19 April 2015}}</ref> Episkopi Bay is a nesting ground for [[green sea turtle|green]] and [[loggerhead turtle]]s, both of which are on the [[International Union for Conservation of Nature|IUCN]] list of endangered species.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://wcd.coe.int/com.instranet.InstraServlet?command=com.instranet.CmdBlobGet&InstranetImage=1921561&SecMode=1&DocId=1776560&Usage=2 |title=Sea turtle mortality in the British Sovereign Base Areas (SBAs) of Episkopi and Akrotiri (Cyprus) |publisher=31st Standing Committee Meeting of the Contracting Parties to the Convention on the Conservation of European Wildlife and Natural Habitat |access-date=19 April 2015 |archive-date=22 August 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160822032403/https://wcd.coe.int/com.instranet.InstraServlet?command=com.instranet.CmdBlobGet&InstranetImage=1921561&SecMode=1&DocId=1776560&Usage=2 |url-status=live }}</ref> Amathous Beach, Dassoudi Beach and part of [[Governor's Beach, Limassol|Governor's Beach]] are also situated in the district.

==Landmarks==
Limassol, as the regional capital and a major centre for European tourism, contains many of the administrative and cultural buildings, and a large number of hotels along the seafront. Limassol District Court is located on Lord Byron Avenue near the Limassol city centre. It consists of a court complex with multiple buildings.<ref name=supremecourt.gov.cy>{{cite web |title=Limassol District Court |url=http://www.supremecourt.gov.cy/Judicial/SC.nsf/All/EC613F991D288949C22572C0003BCD06?OpenDocument |publisher=Supreme Court of Cyprus |access-date=18 April 2015 |url-status=dead}}</ref> The city is known for its wineries, and revelry and nightlife.{{Sfn|Weiß|Sabo|Peitz-Hlebec|Schwarz|2001|p=25}} The Limassol District Archaeological Museum, located in Limassol, has historical artefacts from the towns of Kourion and [[Amathus]].{{Sfn|Weiß|Sabo|Peitz-Hlebec|Schwarz|2001|p=26}} The collections cover several periods, including Preneolithic (Akroteri culture), Early Neolithic (Shillourokambos culture), Neolithic I, Neolithic II (Sotira culture), Chalcolithic, Erimi Culture, Early Bronze Age, Μiddle Bronze Age, Late Bronze Age, Cypro-Geometric period, Cypro-Archaic period, Cypro-Classical period, Hellenistic period, Roman period, and Late Roman/Early Christian/Early Byzantine period.<ref name=limassolmunicipal.com>{{cite web |title=The
