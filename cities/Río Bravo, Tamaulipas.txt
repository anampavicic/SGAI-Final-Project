'''Río Bravo''', formally '''Ciudad Río Bravo''', is a city on the northern border of the [[Mexican state|state]] of [[Tamaulipas]] in northeastern [[Mexico]].

It is the municipal seat of [[Río Bravo Municipality]].

==Geography==
The city lies in the [[Lower Rio Grande Valley|Rio Grande Valley]] on the south of the [[Río Bravo del Norte]] (American synonym: [[Rio Grande]]), and was named after the river.

It is also around {{convert|5|mi|km}} south of the [[Mexico–United States border]] with [[Texas]]. It is east of [[Reynosa, Tamaulipas|Reynosa]]. North across the border and the [[Donna – Río Bravo International Bridge]] over the Río Bravo/Rio Grande are the Texan towns of [[Scissors, Texas|Scissors]], and [[Donna, Texas|Donna]] on [[Farm to Market Road 493]].

Río Bravo Municipality stretches southward into less densely populated territory, and is slowly becoming a large and popular industrial center conveniently located along the Reynosa−Matamoros highway between the booming "[[Maquiladora]]" centers in Reynosa and [[Matamoros, Tamaulipas|Matamoros]].  [[Mexican Federal Highway 2]] passes through the city, paralleling the border.

==Populations==
At the census of 2010, the city reported a population of 95,647, the eighth largest city in the state, following Reynosa, Matamoros, Nuevo Laredo, Ciudad Victoria, Tampico, Madero, and [[Miramar, Tamaulipas|Miramar]]. The next largest community within the municipality is [[Nuevo Progreso, Río Bravo, Tamaulipas|Nuevo Progreso]], in the northeastern area on the Río Bravo/Río Grande.

The municipality had a population of 118,259, and an areal extent of {{convert|1,562.94|km2|abbr=on}}.
