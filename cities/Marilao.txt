'''Marilao''' ({{IPA|tl|mɐɾiˈlaʊ|}}), officially the '''Municipality of Marilao''' ({{lang-tgl|Bayan ng Marilao}}), is a 1st class [[Greater Manila Area|urban]] [[municipality of the Philippines|municipality]] in the [[Philippine Province|province]] of [[Bulacan]], [[Philippines]]. According to the 2020 census, it has a population of 254,453 people.{{PH census|current}} It is {{convert|22|km|sp=us}} from [[Manila]] and {{convert|23|km|sp=us}} from the provincial capital [[Malolos City]].

With the continuous expansion of [[Metro Manila]], Marilao is part of [[Greater Manila Area|Manila's built-up area]] which reaches [[San Ildefonso, Bulacan|San Ildefonso]] on its northernmost part.

==Etymology==
Marilao is derived from the [[Tagalog language|Tagalog]] word "marilaw", which translates to yellowish, with its root word "rilaw" is linked to "dilaw," the Tagalog term for yellow. This is in reference to a yellow plant that grew abundantly in the area.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://manilastandard.net/?p=294566|title=Halamang Dilaw Festival at SM Marilao draws big crowd|date=May 12, 2019|first=Jun|last=David|accessdate=September 14, 2024|work=[[Manila Standard]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://bulacan.gov.ph/cities-and-municipalities/marilao/|title=Marilao|website=Provincial Government of Bulacan|accessdate=September 14, 2024}}</ref>

==History==
Long before the establishment as an independent town, Marilao traces its origin as little as a ''barrio''. It was initially a ''barrio'' of its neighboring town [[Meycauayan]] and the Franciscan missionaries from Meycauayan built a ''visita'' (chapel) dedicated to [[Saint Michael the Archangel]].

Marilao, just like [[Pangil]], a town in [[Laguna (province)|Laguna]], Philippines, was under the stewardship of the Franciscan order.

On April 21, 1796, the Barrio of Marilao was established as a ''pueblo'' as approved by the Alcalde Mayor of [[Bulacan]] and the Franciscan friars of Meycauayan, with the approval of [[Archbishop of Manila]], the ''visita'' of San Miguel Arcangel became a town church where Padre Vicente de Talavera served as its parish priest. Between 1901 and 1903, it was consolidated with [[Meycauayan]].<ref>{{cite PH act|chamber=Act|number=932|date=October 8, 1903|url=https://issuances-library.senate.gov.ph/legislative%2Bissuances/Act%20No.%20932|title=An Act Reducing the Twenty-five Municipalities of the Province of Bulacan to Thirteen|accessdate=July 3, 2023|website=Senate of the Philippines Legislative Digital Resources|archive-date=October 21, 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231021150820/https://issuances-library.senate.gov.ph/legislative%2Bissuances/Act%20No.%20932|url-status=live}}</ref> In 1913, Marilao completely became an independent town.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://bulacan.gov.ph/cities-and-municipalities/marilao|title=Marilao|website=Provincial Government of Bulacan|accessdate=July 3, 2023|archive-date=September 30, 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230930094548/https://bulacan.gov.ph/cities-and-municipalities/marilao/|url-status=live}}</ref>

===Pollution and flooding===
In 2007, Marilao, along with neighboring Meycauayan, share a slot in the list of the world's 30 most polluted places in the developing world drawn up by a private New York-based institute. In its report, “The World’s Worst Polluted Places” for 2007, the [[Pure Earth|Blacksmith Institute]] said: “Industrial waste is haphazardly dumped into the Meycauayan, Marilao and Obando River system, a source of drinking and agricultural water supplies for the 250,000 people living in and around”<ref name="dirty">{{Cite news |newspaper=Philippine Daily Inquirer|url=http://newsinfo.inquirer.net/breakingnews/regions/view_article.php?article_id=89091 |title=Meycauayan, Marilao in world's 'Dirty 30'-- report |access-date=2007-09-17 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090607035342/http://newsinfo.inquirer.net/breakingnews/regions/view_article.php?article_id=89091 |archive-date=2009-06-07 |url-status=dead }}</ref> the Meycauayan-Marilao area.

Marilao is also notorious for frequent flooding during the monsoon season. A section of MacArthur Highway near SM City Marilao is in particular, prone to floods.<ref>{{cite news|work=GMA News|title=Flood remains gutter-deep along McArthur Highway in Marilao, Bulacan|url=https://www.gmanetwork.com/news/news/regions/699428/flood-remains-gutter-deep-along-mcarthur-highway-in-marilao-bulacan/story/|agency=GMA News|date=July 1, 2019|access-date=July 31, 2019|archive-date=July 30, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190730231806/https://www.gmanetwork.com/news/news/regions/699428/flood-remains-gutter-deep-along-mcarthur-highway-in-marilao-bulacan/story/|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Education==
The Pambayang Dalubhasaan ng Marilao ''Municipal College of Marilao'' is a public college in Marilao. The public primary and secondary schools in Marilao are governed by the [[Department of Education (Philippines)|Department of Education]]-Provincial Schools Division of Bulacan. Several private schools in Marilao also provide education services.

;Public High Schools
* Assemblywoman Felicita G. Bernardino Memorial Trade School (AFGBMTS)
* Prenza National High School (PNHS)
* Marilao Central Integrated School (MCIS (JHS founded in 2022) formerly Marilao Central School founded in 1920)

====[[National Shrine of The Divine Mercy (Philippines)|National Shrine of the Divine Mercy]]====
It was elevated to the status of [[National Shrine]] by Archbishop Orlando Quevedo of the [[Catholic Bishops Conference of the Philippines]]. The first mass was held at the site on February 2, 1992, the [[Feast of the Presentation]]. Located in Barangay Santa Rosa I.

On the occasion of the [[World Apostolic Congress on Mercy]] in 2017, a 38-meter [[Divine Mercy image|Statue of Merciful Jesus]] was built here.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ucanews.com/news/tallest-divine-mercy-statue-unveiled-in-philippines/78158|title=Tallest Divine Mercy statue unveiled in Philippines - UCA News|access-date=May 17, 2019|archive-date=May 15, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190515103330/https://www.ucanews.com/news/tallest-divine-mercy-statue-unveiled-in-philippines/78158|url-status=live}}</ref>
