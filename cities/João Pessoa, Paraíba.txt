'''João Pessoa''' ({{IPA|pt|ʒuˈɐ̃w peˈsoɐ}}) is a port city in northeastern [[Brazil]]. It is the largest city in, and capital of, the state of [[Paraíba]] with an estimated population of 833,932 (as of 2022). It is located on the right bank of the [[Paraíba do Norte river]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Joao Pessoa: Brazil |url=https://www.britannica.com/place/Joao-Pessoa-Brazil |publisher=[[Encyclopaedia Britannica]] |access-date=22 April 2020}}</ref>

The new "Estação Ciência, Cultura e Artes" (Science, Culture and Art Station),<ref name="ESTAÇÃO CIÊNCIA, CULTURA e ARTES">{{cite book|url=http://www.joaopessoa.pb.gov.br/projetos/estacaocienciaculturaartes/|title=ESTAÇÃO CIÊNCIA, CULTURA e ARTES|publisher=E.C.C.A.|location=joão pessoa, Brazil|format=PDF|isbn=85-240-3919-1|access-date=January 10, 2008|year=2006|language=pt|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071224214719/http://www.joaopessoa.pb.gov.br/projetos/estacaocienciaculturaartes/|archive-date=December 24, 2007}}</ref> located at the most eastern point of the Americas (''[[Ponta do Seixas]]''), is an educational and cultural institution as well as a national landmark. The complex, inaugurated in 2008, was created by Brazilian architect [[Oscar Niemeyer]] and is one of his final projects.

The capital of Paraíba received the title of [[City of Crafts and Folk Arts|Creative City]] by [[UNESCO]] in 2017,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.joaopessoa.pb.gov.br/titulo-de-cidade-criativa-da-unesco-coloca-joao-pessoa-na-rota-turistica-mundial-atraves-da-arte-popular/|title=Unesco Creative City title puts João Pessoa on the world tourist route through folk art|access-date=August 29, 2018|date=November 3, 2017|publisher=City Hall of João Pessoa|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190112052115/http://www.joaopessoa.pb.gov.br/titulo-de-cidade-criativa-da-unesco-coloca-joao-pessoa-na-rota-turistica-mundial-atraves-da-arte-popular/|archive-date=January 12, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> appointing João Pessoa as "Brazilian city of [[handicrafts]]".

==History==
{{Quote box |width=10em |align=left   |bgcolor=#B0C4DE
|title=City name throughout history|fontsize=90% |quote={{flagicon|POR|1640}}1585–1588 Royal City of Our Lady of the Snows<br />
{{flagicon|ESP|1588}}1588-1654 Philipeia of Our Lady of the Snows 
<br />[[File:Flag of New Holland.svg|border|22px]]1630–1654 Fredrikstad (Frederick City)<br />{{flagicon|POR|1654}}1654–1930 Parahyba do Norte<br />{{flagicon|BRA}}1930–present João Pessoa}}

The city of João Pessoa was founded on August 5, 1585, during the celebration of the peace pact between the Portuguese represented by the Portuguese João Tavares, and the indigenous people, represented by the Piragibe [[Indigenous peoples in Brazil|Indian]], chief of the [[Tabajara]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://jornaldaparaiba.com.br/comunidade/joao-pessoa-em-bairros-varadouro-narra-origem-e-formacao-da-cidade/|title=oão Pessoa em bairros: Varadouro narra origem e formação da cidade|publisher=Jornal da Paraíba|access-date=March 19, 2023}}</ref> on the banks of the Paraíba River. The [[France|French]] inhabited the locality, and had an alliance with the indigenous tribe of [[Potiguaras]]. With the alliance of the Portuguese with the [[Tabajaras]] (rivals of the [[Potiguaras]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pm.pb.gov.br/arquivos/Historia_da_Paraiba.pdf|title=História da Paraíba|publisher=PMPB|access-date=March 19, 2023}}</ref>), the Portuguese colonists, led by Frutuoso Barbosa, finally managed to expel the [[France|french]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pm.pb.gov.br/arquivos/Historia_da_Paraiba.pdf|title=História da Paraíba|publisher=PMPB|access-date=March 19, 2023}}</ref> and conquer Paraíba. After the conquest, they built the forts of São Tiago and São Felipe. They brought masons, carpenters, engineers and others to build the City of Nossa Senhora das Neves. With the beginning of the works, they went to [[Baía da Traição]] to expel the rest of the [[France|french]] who remained in Paraíba. There was the nomination of João Tavares to be the captain of the Fort.

The Royal City of Nossa Senhora das Neves<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pm.pb.gov.br/arquivos/Historia_da_Paraiba.pdf|title=História da Paraíba|publisher=PMPB|access-date=March 19, 2023}}</ref> was the third city to be founded in Brazil, with the signing of an agreement between João Tavares and the chief Piragibe of the [[Tabajaras]]. Called "'''Royal City of Our Lady of the Snows'''", in honor of the saint of the day it was founded,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.de.ufpb.br/~ronei/JoaoPessoa/histor.htm|title=História de João Pessoa|publisher=UFPB(Federal University of Paraiba)|access-date=June 6, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190312135209/http://www.de.ufpb.br/~ronei/JoaoPessoa/histor.htm|archive-date=March 12, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> its first structures were on the banks of the Sanhauá River, a tributary of the Paraíba River, now known as Porto do Varadouro, in the neighborhood of the same name.

In 1588, the city was renamed "'''Philipeia of Our Lady of the Snows'''", in honor of King [[Philip III of Spain|Philip II]], who at the time accumulated the thrones of Spain and Portugal.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.joaopessoa.pb.gov.br/secretarias/setur/historia/|title=Quatro séculos de história|publisher=PREFEITURA MUNICIPAL DE JOÃO PESSOA|access-date=June 6, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190607003218/http://www.joaopessoa.pb.gov.br/secretarias/setur/historia/|archive-date=June 7, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

In 1634, attracted by the sugar wealth of the then-capital of the Paraíba, [[Netherlands|the Dutch]] invaded and named it '''Fredrikstad (Frederick City)''', in honor of the prince of Orange, [[Frederick Henry, Prince of Orange|Frederick of Orange]],<ref name="A HISTÓRIA DE SEU POVO">{{cite web|url=http://zeoserver.pb.gov.br/portalparaiba/mapadeoportunidades/conheca-a-paraiba/uma-paraiba-vestida-de-sol/a-historia-de-um-povo|title=A HISTÓRIA DE SEU POVO|publisher=zeoserver|access-date=March 19, 2023}}</ref> was one of the two main cities of [[Dutch Brazil|New Holland]], brought the town a period of great prosperity,<ref name="Agência Senado">{{cite web|url=https://www12.senado.leg.br/noticias/materias/2011/08/05/senado-comemora-426-anos-de-fundacao-do-estado-da-paraiba|title=Senado comemora 426 anos de fundação do estado da Paraíba|publisher=Agência Senado|access-date=March 19, 2023}}</ref> the installation of mills on the coast enabled greater efficiency in sugar production, with the main consequence being the guarantee of large profits, but, on the other hand, it required the practice of monoculture that was developed in increasing extensions of land, the latifundia. After the decline of [[Dutch Brazil|New Holland]]<ref name="A HISTÓRIA DE SEU POVO"/> and with the departure of the Dutch, the city was again dominated by the Portuguese and acquired the name "'''Parahyba do Norte'''" in 1654.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://turismo.joaopessoa.pb.gov.br/um-pouco-da-sua-historia/|title=Um pouco da sua história|publisher=Secretary of Tourism of the Municipal Government of João Pessoa|access-date=June 6, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190506210835/https://turismo.joaopessoa.pb.gov.br/um-pouco-da-sua-historia/|archive-date=May 6, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="Agência Senado"/>

The city was renamed João Pessoa in September 1930 in memory of the former governor of Paraíba, [[João Pessoa Cavalcanti de Albuquerque]], who had been a vice-presidential candidate and was murdered on 26 July that year.

João Pessoa is the third oldest city in Brazil.<ref name=lp>{{cite web|url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/brazil/the-northeast/joao-pessoa|title=Introducing João Pessoa|publisher=Lonely Planet|access-date=May 10, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140512221705/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/brazil/the-northeast/joao-pessoa|archive-date=May 12, 2014|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Geography==

===Environment===

João Pessoa has {{convert|20|km|mi}} of beachfront.<ref name=lp/>

João Pessoa has many green areas distributed among its avenues, parks, and residential neighborhoods, supporting its claim as "the second greenest city in the world" with more than {{convert|7|km2|sqmi}} of forested land, second only to Paris. This claim is a matter of conjecture, however, originating from a publicity stunt carried out by the city's mayor during an [[Earth Summit]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://g1.globo.com/pb/paraiba/noticia/cidade-mais-verde-extremo-oriental-saiba-o-que-e-verdade-sobre-joao-pessoa.ghtml|title=Cidade mais verde? Extremo oriental? Saiba o que é verdade sobre João Pessoa|publisher=[[G1 (website)|G1]]|language=pt-BR}}</ref>

===Hydrography===
In João Pessoa, there are about twelve rivers. The Jaguaribe River is born in the Esplanada complex, crosses the Benjamim Maranhão Botanical Garden, in the middle of the Atlantic Forest, and flows into the Atlantic Ocean on the border with the municipality of Cabedelo. The water to supply the houses is taken from the Gramame-Mumbaba system, from the Paraíba Water and Sewerage Company. In this system, these two rivers take turns to supply the city with water. However, the most historically important river is the Sanhauá River, because it was on its banks that the [[city]] was born and where the first houses were built.
<ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.cagepa.pb.gov.br|title=Site da Cagepa}}</ref>

There is also the Solón de Lucena Park Lagoon in the City Center. The lagoon was the main tourist spot in the city during the time when most of the city was far from the beaches. At the end of 2010, during the Christmas celebrations, the lagoon was revitalized and gained devices such as ambient music.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.de.ufpb.br/~ronei/JoaoPessoa/lagoa.htm|title=Parque Solon de Lucena |publisher=Universidade Federal da Paraíba}}</ref>

The capital of Paraíba has a coastline of about 24 kilometers in length, nine beaches in the municipality alone, in addition to the beaches of the Metropolitan Region, such as the city of Cabedelo, the city of Lucena and the District of Jacumã in the municipality of Conde , where the Tambaba Naturist Beach is located. Urban beaches are characterized by white sand beaches and crystal clear waters. Many have preserved the Atlantic Forest, as well as being ideal for bathing thanks to a natural barrier about 6 kilometers from the coast that protects a large part of the Pessoa and Cabedelo coast, allowing children to play in the calm waters. There is the Urban Tartarugas Project, which operates on the beaches of Bessa and Intermares, a spawning area for the hawksbill turtle, a scenario for environmental preservation. Surfing is also practiced in the city.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://viagem.estadao.com.br/noticias/geral,barra-de-gramame,70002740964 |title=Barra de Gramame: uma das praias mais cênicas|publisher=Estadão}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://correiodaparaiba.com.br/cidades/joao-pessoa/400-mil-fieis-participam-da-procissao-em-romaria-da-penha-em-joao-pessoa/|title=400 THOUSAND FEIS PARTICIPAM DA PROCISSÃO EM ROMARIA DA PENHA EM JOÃO PESSOA|publisher=Correio da Paraíba|access-date=2021-02-08}}</ref>

Among the main beaches, we can mention the ''Praia de Tambau'', about 8 kilometers long, composed of fine, beaten sand, with blue-green waters; the ''bessa beach'' is where the caribessa is located, a quiet beach with crystalline waters; also the ''Praia de Manaíra'', a totally urban beach, formed by coral reefs, which makes its waves weak and clear water in summer. It is the point of several kiosks and bars, with sports fields on its edge. The ''Cabo Branco beach'', white sand and warm water; ''Playa Seixas'', which is where the ''most eastern point of America'' is located. In addition to ''Praia da Penha'', where the historic Chapel of Our Lady of Penha, built in [[1763]], where the penha pilgrimage takes place every year, from the historic center to the beach.; The ''Jacarape beach'', where the [[Centro de Convenções Poeta Ronaldo Cunha Lima|Poet Ronaldo Cunha Lima Convention Center]] is located; then ''Praia do Sol'', which is a quiet and airy place; and the ''barra de gramame beach'', which situates the meeting of the river gramme with the sea.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://viagemeturismo.abril.com.br/blog/brasis/10-praias-da-paraiba-que-voce-precisa-conhecer/|title=Praias da Paraíba|publisher=editora abril|access-date=2021-02-08|language=pt-BR}}</ref>

===Climate===
João Pessoa has a [[tropical monsoon climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification|Köppen]] ''Am'') with very warm to hot temperatures all year long and strong rainfall in most of the months; however, October to December have rather low rainfall.

The city has a ''Benjamin Maranhão Botanical Garden'', which preserves the [[Atlantic forest]], the [[animals]], and opens the visitation, which is the João Pessoa Botanical Garden, considered one of the largest remains of the natural Atlantic Forest in an urban area of [[Brazil]].
<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sudema.pb.gov.br/servicos/servicos-ao-publico/jardim-botanico|title=Jardim Botanico|publisher=Sudema|language=pt-BR}}</ref> It is a excellent opportunity for people to be in contact with pure and crystalline nature. Among these, [[environmental education]] is a relevant element, allowing interesting approaches, from knowledge and interest in plants to stimulating curiosity and knowledge of this important space for visitation and research.

There are 18 stations distributed along the Rio and Preguiça trails using as criteria elements mainly related to history, botany and ecology, contributing to knowledge of the environment and the need for its preservation.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.destinoparaiba.pb.gov.br/ondeir/jardim-botanico/|title=Jardim Botanico|publisher=Destination Paraíba|language=pt-BR}}</ref> The area has about 515ha, of which 343ha are home to the Botanical Garden, where the flora is not very soft in the recreational and educational activities promoted on the spot. It includes the largest natural urban Atlantic forest reserve in the country, and an important historical heritage of the capital of [[Paraíba]].

==Demographics==
According to the [[2022 Brazilian census|2022 census]], João Pessoa had a population of 833,932. Its racial makeup was 50.6% [[Pardo]] (two or more races), 39.7% [[White Brazilian|White]], 9.2% [[Afro-Brazilian|Black]], 0.2% [[Indigenous peoples in Brazil|Amerindian]] and 0.1% [[Asian Brazilian|Asian]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Censo 2022 - Panorama |url=https://censo2022.ibge.gov.br/panorama/}}</ref>

== Transportation==

===International airport===
Joao Pessoa is served by [[Presidente Castro Pinto International Airport]].<ref name=fp>{{cite web|url=http://www.footprinttravelguides.com/latin-america/brazil/recife-and-the-northeast-coast/paraaba/joao-pessoa-and-around/|title=João Pessoa in Brazil|publisher=Footprint Travel Guides|access-date=March 29, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150829011712/http://www.footprinttravelguides.com/latin-america/brazil/recife-and-the-northeast-coast/paraaba/joao-pessoa-and-around|archive-date=August 29, 2015|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref>

===Rail system===
The [[Sistema de Trens Urbanos de João Pessoa|João Pessoa Urban Trains System]] is operated by [[Diesel engine|diesel]] components on one railway line with a 30&nbsp;km extension spanning four municipalities, João Pessoa, Cabedelo, Bayeux and Santa Rita, constituted by the Cabedelo stretch, with 10 stations in operation, carrying around 10,100 passengers a day. Composed of three locomotives and 17 passenger cars, the trains form two compositions that conduct 28 trips a day.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cbtu.gov.br/index.php/pt/sistemas-cbtu/joao-pessoa/|title=João Pessa|publisher=CBTU-Brazilian company of urban trains|access-date=August 29, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180830041343/https://www.cbtu.gov.br/index.php/pt/sistemas-cbtu/joao-pessoa/|archive-date=August 30, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Economy==
João Pessoa's Gross Domestic Product (GDP) was [[Brazilian real|R$]] 14,841,805 as of 2010.<ref>{{cite book|url=http://www.ibge.gov.br/home/estatistica/economia/pibmunicipios/2005/tab01.pdf |title=GDP |publisher=[[Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics|IBGE]] |location=João Pessoa, Brazil |isbn=85-240-3919-1 |access-date=July 18, 2007 |year=2005 |language=pt |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081002024524/http://www.ibge.gov.br/home/estatistica/economia/pibmunicipios/2005/tab01.pdf |archive-date=October 2, 2008 }}</ref>

The city's per capita income was [[Brazilian real|R$]] 19,284 as of 2010.<ref>{{cite book|url=http://www.ibge.gov.br/home/estatistica/economia/pibmunicipios/2005/tab01.pdf |title=per capita income |publisher=[[Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics|IBGE]] |location=João Pessoa, Brazil |isbn=85-240-3919-1 |access-date=July 18, 2007 |year=2005 |language=pt |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081002024524/http://www.ibge.gov.br/home/estatistica/economia/pibmunicipios/2005/tab01.pdf |archive-date=October 2, 2008 }}</ref>

==Culture==
The Historic Center of João Pessoa, recognized as [[National Institute of Historic and Artistic Heritage]] in December 2007,<ref name="Prefeitura de Joao Pessoa">{{cite web | author=Prefeitura de Joao Pessoa | title= João Pessoa National Heritage
 |url=http://www.joaopessoa.pb.gov.br/secretarias/copacjp/patrimonio/ | access-date=March 19, 2023|date=}}</ref> covers areas in the neighborhoods of the Center, Róger, Jaguaribe, Tambiá and Varadouro. There are [[List of National Historic Heritage sites of Brazil|listed]] 37 [[hectare]]s in area and estimated around 700 buildings, in addition to streets, squares and historic parks that integrate this set, comprising most of the neighborhoods of Varadouro and the Center of the city. Its buildings make up a scenario of different styles and eras full of townhouses, squares, colonial houses and secular churches, being considered the main architectural collection of Paraíba, reporting the various phases of local history, and one of the largest and most important historical sites in<ref name="Prefeitura de Joao Pessoa"/> Brazil.<ref>{{cite web | author=Prefeitura de Joao Pessoa | title= Tourism in João Pessoa - Historic Center
 |url=http://www.hotelguarani.com.br/turismo-joao-person-center-historico.php | access-date=March 19, 2023|date=}}</ref>

The delimited area has assets that represent various periods in the history of João Pessoa, such as the [[baroque]] of the Igreja da Ordem Terceira de São Francisco; from the [[rococo]] of the [[Church and Convent of the Third Order of Our Lady of Carmo]], [[Cathedral Basilica of Our Lady of the Snows, João Pessoa|Cathedral Basilica of Our Lady of the Snows]]; of the [[Neoclassical architecture]] of the [[Church of Saint Peter Gonzalez]], [[Church and Convent of Our Lady of the Rosary (João Pessoa)|Church and Convent of Our Lady of the Rosary]], of the [[Baroque architecture]] of the [[São Francisco Cultural Center]], [[Monastery of St. Benedict (João Pessoa)|Monastery of St. Benedict]], of the [[Mannerist style]] of the [[Church of Our Lady of Mercy (João Pessoa)|Church of Our Lady of Mercy]], all from the [[17th century]];<ref>{{cite web | author=José Chrispiniano | title= João Pessoa Historic Center is listed as a heritage site
 |url=http://www2.uol.com.br/historiaviva/noticias/centro_historico_de_joao_pessoa_e_tombado.html | access-date=March 19, 2023|date=}}</ref> of the [[colonial architecture of Brazil|colonial architecture]] and [[Eclecticism in architecture|eclectic architecture]] of civil houses, in addition to [[Art Nouveau]] and [[Art Deco]], from the [[1920s|20s]] and [[1930s|30s]] decades, predominant in square Antenor Navarro and in the former Hotel Globo, now transformed into a cultural center.<ref>{{cite web | author=Brasil Sabor | title= The historic center of João Pessoa
 |url=http://brasilsabor.com.br/por/roteiros/article/327 | access-date=March 19, 2023|date=}}</ref>

===Diving===
The [[coastline]] of João Pessoa includes the ''Alvarenga'' shipwreck, an artificial reef for developing marine life.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://turismo.joaopessoa.pb.gov.br/o-que-fazer/esportes/surf/|title=mergulho|access-date=October 2, 2018|publisher=Secretary of Tourism of the Municipality of João Pessoa|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181003014317/https://turismo.joaopessoa.pb.gov.br/o-que-fazer/esportes/surf/|archive-date=October 3, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

* '''Alvarenga Shipwreck''' : The Alvarenga was a vessel used to transport supplies to ships. It wrecked about 9.6&nbsp;km from the tip of '''''Bessa's Beach''''' and is submerged {{convert|20|m|ft}} deep. It remains whole, {{convert|20|m|ft|abbr=on}} in length by {{convert|5|m|ft|abbr=on}} in beam. It is possible for divers to penetrate the small bow and stern [[compartment (ship)|compartment]]s safely. On the prow the winch to hoist the anchor remains in view. It is also common to find large stingrays and shoals of fish, such as the top cock and the hook.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://turismo.joaopessoa.pb.gov.br/o-que-fazer/esportes/surf/|title=Naufrágio Alvarenga|access-date=October 2, 2018|publisher=Secretary of Tourism of the Municipality of João Pessoa|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181003014317/https://turismo.joaopessoa.pb.gov.br/o-que-fazer/esportes/surf/|archive-date=October 3, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>
