'''Iwo''' is a city in [[Osun State]], [[Nigeria]]. The Iwo people, like all other people of [[Yoruba people|the Yoruba]] kingdom, are said to have originated from [[Ile-Ife]], where they migrated sometimes in the 11th century according to Alademomi kenyon and Prince Adelegan Adegbola (2009).<ref>{{Cite web |last=Tomori |first=Lola |date=22 February 2018 |title=THE FOUNDATION OF IWO TOWN AND ITS MIGRATION HISTORY FROM ILE-IFE |url=https://www.macosconsultancy.com/Publication/THE%20FOUNDATION%20OF%20IWO%20TOWN%20AND%20ITS%20MIGRATION.pdf |url-status=bot: unknown |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220818192259/https://www.macosconsultancy.com/Publication/THE%20FOUNDATION%20OF%20IWO%20TOWN%20AND%20ITS%20MIGRATION.pdf |archive-date=18 August 2022 |access-date=23 September 2022 |website=www.macosconsultancy.com }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Hub |first=Iwoland |date=2019-07-22 |title=Short History of Iwo land: The Knowledge of Iwo You Must Know |url=https://iwolandhub.com.ng/history-of-iwo-land/ |access-date=2022-09-22 |website=Iwoland Hub |language=en-US}}</ref> The only predicted land with the symbol of the [[Parrot|parrots]] (which signifies the location of the promised land) is the [[Iwo Kingdom|Iwo kingdom]]. The city was formerly part of [[Old Oyo|old Oyo state]] and was later separated and became one of the major townships in [[Osun State]], [[Nigeria]].

It has over 30 ancient and powerful Kings all under the [[Oluwo of Iwoland]], HIM Oba [[Abdulrasheed Adewale Akanbi]] as the only Consenting Authority and first class [[paramount ruler]]  in an area of 245&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> and a population of 191,348 (central city/Local Government) most populous Local Government in Osun State by the 2006 Nigeria National census figures. The other local governments in Iwo from [[satellite town]]s are [[Aiyedire|Aiyedire Local Government]], 265.783&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> area and 76,309 by population, as well as [[Ola Oluwa|Ola-Oluwa Local Government]], 332.117&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> area and 76,227 by population. The headquarters of the local governments are Iwo (Central), Iwo; Aiyedire, [[Ileogbo|Ile Ogbo]]; and Ola-Oluwa, [[Bode Osi|Bode-Osi]]. Iwo now has additional four local governments: Iwo East, Olomowewe; Iwo West, Agberire; Ọla Oluwa South East, Ilemowu; Ayedire South East, Oluponna.

==Education==
{{unreferenced section|date=June 2016}}
The source of orthodox education in Iwo was, initially, primarily Christian missionary based.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Hub |first=Iwoland |date=2019-07-23 |title=History and Background of Education in Iwo land |url=https://iwolandhub.com.ng/history-of-education-in-iwo/ |access-date=2022-09-22 |website=Iwoland Hub |language=en-US}}</ref> These were mostly [[Baptists|Baptist]], as each Baptist Church branch in the central city established a primary school of its own with corresponding name after the branch: Aipate Baptist Day School, Olukotun Baptist Day School, Feesu Baptist Day School, Oke-Odo Baptist Day School. The Baptists also established a secondary school, Baptist High school, Iwo, the only institution in Iwo awarding Advanced Level of [[West African Senior School Certificate Examination|West African School Certificate]] in addition to the Ordinary Level certificate. There was also a Baptist Modern school (now obsolete in Nigeria educational system) which now metamorphosed to Baptist Grammar school – awarding ordinary level certificates like other secondary schools. These in addition to the old Baptist College, a Teacher Training College, and one of the oldest of such in Africa and main source of teachers to the Nigerian and other [[List of sovereign states and dependent territories in Africa|African countries]] Education system. The college is now the site of [[Bowen University]], the secular Baptist University and is one of the three universities in Iwo and its environment.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://bowenuniversity.edu.ng/new/ |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170121034502/http://bowenuniversity.edu.ng/new |archive-date=2017-01-21 |title=Bowen University Iwo - Home}}</ref> 
The Baptists were followed by the Methodist who had a [[Methodism|Methodist]] Primary School in the central city, as well as a Modern school, now United Methodist High School.

The [[Catholic Church|Catholics]] had a Modern School, now Catholic Grammar School, and a Female Secondary School – St. Mary's High School which is now a co-educational school.

The government later joined the Christian missionaries in establishing many [[Primary school|primary schools]], a Modern School, Local authority Modern School, now Local Authority Commercial Grammar School, a Teacher Training College, LATCO, later changed in 1964 to Iwo Grammar School.

Muslim missionaries also joined the efforts later in establishing Ansar-U-deen Primary School, [[Ansar Ud Deen|Ansar-U-deen]] Modern School which is now Ansar-U-deen Grammar School, and  [[Anwar ul-Islam Girls High School|Anwar-ul-Islam]] Grammar School. The first Shariah court in Nigeria was established in Iwo.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.premiumtimesng.com/news/131497-islam-came-to-south-before-northern-nigeria-isa-yuguda.html|title = Islam came to South, before Northern Nigeria - Isa Yuguda &#124; Premium Times Nigeria|date = 26 April 2013}}</ref>
Iwo people are majorly Muslim as early as 1655 when the first [[mosque]] in [[Yorubaland]] was built which serves as the first place of worship and [[Islamic studies|Islamic education]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://nairaproject.com/projects/2169.html|title=Analytical Study of Rijāl Al-'Ilm of Shaykh Tajudeen Al-Umari Al-Iwowi}}</ref> Among the major secondary schools are the Iwo Grammar School, the [[United Methodist Church|United Methodist]] High School, the Baptist High School, St. Mary's High School, the L.A. Commercial Grammar School, Islahudeen Grammar School and the Anwar-ul-Islam Grammar School. Iwo is well noted for Arabic/Islamic education.  There are several Arabic schools [[Madrasa|(Modrassah)in]] the City and many Iwo indigenes usually have a taste of this along with the secular schools. The popular Modrassah include Islamic youth center (morkaz shabaab) which was established by late Islamic scholar named Sheikh Ahmad Muhally Adedimeji Aroworeki, Islahudeen Arabic School, Obatedo,  founded by late Sheikh Abdulbaaqi Muhammad in the early 1960s. Amin Training Center, Araromi is another prominent school established by late  Sheikh Badrudeen Al-Amin in the 1950s. Islamic cultural center (Markaz Abdul Razaq Abdul Rahman), [[Adam Abdullah Al-Ilory|Sheik Adam]] ilory Villa, Agbowo, Iwo.

There are many primary schools in the [[satellite town]]s established by the former Western Regional Government as well as many secondary schools in Iwo and its satellite towns which were established during the tenure of Chief Bola Ige as the governor of the old Oyo state.

==Community based organisations==

Among the leading community based organisations in Iwo are: Iwo Board of Trustees (IBOT), Iwo Action Council (IWAC), Iwo Progressive Union (IPU), Iwoland Development Coalition (IDC),Guildance Community Development Foundation, Charitable Youth for Nation Building Initiative, Iwo Odidere Leo Club.
Iwo Action Council (IWAC) is the umbrella body of all organisations in Iwo. IWAC has been known for being in the forefront as developmental focus organisation.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://thenationonlineng.net/iwo-indigenes-want-aregbesola-approve-journalism-institute/|title = Iwo indigenes want Aregbesola to approve journalism institute| newspaper=The Nation Newspaper |date = 22 October 2016}}</ref>

Iwoland Development Coalition (IDC) was formed on 29 August 2014 to champion development of Iwo, [[Aiyedire]] and [[Ola Oluwa]] Local Government. ==Iwo Chamber of Commerce and Industry Limited/GTE==

People who are interested in economic development of Iwoland came together in 2018 to form Iwo Chamber of Commerce and Industry Limited/GTE. The organization was registered with Corporate Affairs Commission in 2021 which is completely different from the defunct one established in 1989.
Iwo Chamber of Commerce and Industry was established for the promotion and protection of trade and industry and to represent and express the opinion of the business community on matters affecting trade and industry in Iwoland which consists of the three local government areas in the federal constituency (Iwo, [[Aiyedire|Ayedire]] and [[Ola Oluwa]]) Iwoland Federal constituency and where applicable, the whole State of Osun and the Federation. It is a non–profit–sharing organization and its income and properties are applied solely for the promotion of ICCI's objectives.
Among those people that made it happen then are: Olusegun Dada (Esq), Kudaisi Ismaila (Esq), Olawale Rasheed, Senator [[Adelere Adeyemi Oriolowo|Adelere Oriolowo]], Alhaji Mokanju Musibau FCA, Professor Waheed Hassan, Otunba Jire Ayinla, Akogun Olaposi Adiatu,  Adebayo Lasisi PhD, FCA, Kabiru Adisa FCA, Lanre Omotayo PhD, Princess Funmi Lamuye<ref>{{cite web|url = https://affairstv.com/2022/02/07/dada-kudaisi-rasheed-elected-president-deputy-presidents-of-iwo-chamber-of-commerce-and-industry-icci/|title = Home|date = 7 February 2022|access-date = 11 March 2022|archive-date = 19 August 2022|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20220819175439/https://affairstv.com/2022/02/07/dada-kudaisi-rasheed-elected-president-deputy-presidents-of-iwo-chamber-of-commerce-and-industry-icci/|url-status = dead}}</ref>

== Climate ==

Iwo has a tropical wet and dry or savanna climate and is zero metres above sea level (Aw classification). The area averages a yearly temperature of {{convert|29.66|C}}, which is 0.2% higher than the national average for Nigeria. Iwo generally experiences 248.57 wet days (68.1% of the time) annually with average precipitation of {{convert|133.63|mm}}.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Iwo, Osun, NG Climate Zone, Monthly Averages, Historical Weather Data |url=https://tcktcktck.org/nigeria/osun/iwo |access-date=2023-09-26 |website=tcktcktck.org}}</ref>
