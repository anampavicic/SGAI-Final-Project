'''Bitung''', officially the '''City of Bitung''' ({{Langx|id|'''Kota Bitung'''}}), is a coastal [[List of regencies and cities in Indonesia|city]] at the north-eastern edge of the island of [[Sulawesi]] in [[Indonesia]].  Bitung is located in the province of [[North Sulawesi]] ({{Lang|id|Sulawesi Utara}}), and faces [[Lembeh Island]] (which forms two districts of the city), and the [[Lembeh Strait]], which is known for its colourful [[marine life]], in particular [[Nudibranch|sea slugs]].  Bitung has a population of 187,932 at the 2010 Census, making it the second most populated city in the province after the capital [[Manado]],<ref name="Biro Pusat Statistik 2011">Biro Pusat Statistik, Jakarta, 2011.</ref> rising to 225,134 at the 2020 Census;<ref name="Badan Pusat Statistik 2021">Badan Pusat Statistik, Jakarta, 2021.</ref> the official estimate as at mid 2023 was 232,440 (comprising 119,120 males and 113,330 females).<ref name="Badan Pusat Statistik 2024">Badan Pusat Statistik, Jakarta, 28 February 2024, ''Kota Bitung Dalam Angka 2024'' (Katalog-BPS 1102001.7172)</ref>

A number of development projects have been completed, or are currently undergoing centring around the city; these include the Bitung Special Economic Zone (SEZ), [[Manado–Bitung Toll Road]], Bitung International Ocean Going Ship Port, and the Makassar-Bitung rail track; which will automatically make the city a gateway to the Pacific region.<ref>{{Cite news|editor=Priyambodo RH|date=14 February 2017|url=https://en.antaranews.com/news/109398/training-of-manpower-necessary-for-development-of-bitung-sez|title=Training of manpower necessary for development of Bitung SEZ|website=En.AntaraNews.com|location=[[Manado]], Indonesia|publisher=[[Antara News]]|access-date=27 April 2023}}</ref>

==Nature reserve==
[[Tangkoko Batuangus Nature Reserve]] is two hours' drive away from Manado, and one hour from Bitung.  [[Tarsius tarsier|Tarsius spectrum]] can be found easily in the nature reserve.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://koran-jakarta.com/index.php/detail/view01/120230|title=Taman Nasional Tangkoko|trans-title=Tangkoko National Park|website=Koran-Jakarta.com|language=id|publisher=Koran Jakarta, PT Berita Nusantara|date=24 June 2013|access-date=27 May 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130624082650/http://koran-jakarta.com/index.php/detail/view01/120230|archive-date=24 June 2013}}</ref>

==Economy==
[[File:COLLECTIE TROPENMUSEUM Machinekamer van een kokosoliefabriek in Bitung TMnr 20017948.jpg|thumb|left|Coconut processing in Bitung|alt=]]
===Bitung Special Economic Zone (SEZ)===
Bitung was named as one of the country's Special Economic Zones in 2014.  Priority sectors in the area are logistics, herbal pharmacy, fish processing and coconut processing.  Based on Government Regulation No.32/2014 on Bitung SEZ, it will occupy an area of {{Convert|534|ha|abbr=off|lk=on}}.  The SEZ is divided into three zones, namely industrial zone, logistics zone, and [[Export Processing Zone]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://en.tempo.co/read/753693/chinese-company-promises-better-development-in-bitung/|title=Chinese company promises better development in Bitung|website=En.Tempo.co|publisher=Tempo|date=15 March 2016|access-date=27 April 2023}}</ref>

==Transport==
===Manado-Bitung Toll Road===
On 16 January 2014, local authorities passed responsibility to acquire land for the [[Manado–Bitung Toll Road]] to the Public Works Ministry, although the initiation of works after the formality would take some time.<ref>{{Cite news|author=Ryo Noor|editor=Andrew Pattymahu|date=16 January 2014|url=http://manado.tribunnews.com/2014/01/16/jalan-tol-manado-bitung-di-launching|title=Jalan Tol Manado Bitung di Launching|trans-title=Manado Bitung Toll Road is launching|language=Indonesian|access-date=27 April 2023}}</ref>  The construction of the {{Convert|39.9|km|abbr=off|lk=on}} [[toll road]] was expected to cost [[Indonesian Rupiah|Rp]]&nbsp;6.7&nbsp;trillion ([[United States Dollar|US$]]&nbsp;503&nbsp;million).  It was completed in early 2022, and formally opened on 25 February 2022.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Sugianto|first=Danang|date=2 May 2017|title=Proyek Jalan Tol Manado-Bitung Habiskan Dana Hingga Rp 6,7 T|url=https://finance.detik.com/berita-ekonomi-bisnis/3488934/proyek-jalan-tol-manado-bitung-habiskan-dana-hingga-rp-67-t|trans-title=Manado-Bitung Toll Road project spends up to IDR 6.7 T|website=finance.detik.com|language=Indonesian|publisher=[[Detik]]|access-date=27 April 2023}}</ref>

===Davao-Bitung Roll-On Roll-Off Ferry Service===
[[File:Port of Bitung ⚓.jpg|thumb|[[Port of Bitung]]|alt=]]
On 28 April 2017, [[President of the Philippines|Philippines President]] [[Rodrigo Duterte]] and [[President of Indonesia|Indonesia President]] [[Joko Widodo]] inaugurated the Davao-Bitung Roll-on Roll-Off Ferry Service that will allow journey time of just three days between two cities.  The original shipping route from [[Davao City]] in [[Mindanao]], [[Philippines]], took up to five weeks, because ships first have to sail northwards to [[Manila]], passing through [[South China Sea]] and [[Malaysia]] waters before it reaches various ports in Indonesia.  Under the programme, there are 5 to 10 companies who will use the initial voyage of the [[Cebu]]-based Asia Marine Transport Corp.'s M/V ''Super Shuttle'' RORO 12 with a 500&nbsp;[[Twenty-foot equivalent unit|TEU]] capacity.  Among the goods to be traded include [[animal feed]]s, aqua products, [[charcoal]], [[coffee bean]]s, construction materials, [[copra]], feed ingredients, [[fertiliser]]s, food and beverages, fresh fruits, ice cream products, meats, [[peanut]]s, [[poultry]] ([[halal]]), [[Soybean|soya]], sugar, and synthetics.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://jakartaglobe.id/news/jokowi-duterte-open-new-shipping-line-between-davao-city-and-bitung|title=Jokowi, Duterte open new shipping line between Davao City and Bitung|website=JakartaGlobe.id|location=[[Jakarta]], Indonesia|publisher=[[Jakarta Globe]]|date=1 May 2017|access-date=27 April 2023}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://bisniskeuangan.kompas.com/read/2017/04/28/215206626/sah.kapal.ro-ro.davao-bitung.mulai.beroperasi.30.april|title=Sah, Kapal Ro-Ro Davao-Bitung Mulai Beroperasi 30 April|trans-title=Legitimately, the Davao-Bitung Ro-Ro vessel started operations 30 April|language=id|website=bisniskeuangan.kompas.com|publisher=PT. Kompas Cyber Media (Kompas Gramedia Digital Group)|date=28 April 2017|access-date=27 April 2023}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.rappler.com/business/158396-philippines-indonesia-new-shipping-route|title=Soon: shorter shipping route between PH, Indonesia|website=Rappler.com|publisher=Rappler|date=15 January 2017|access-date=27 April 2023}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|author=Antonio L. Colina IV|date=28 April 2017|url=http://www.mindanews.com/top-stories/2017/04/davao-gensan-bitung-roro-route-to-decongest-ports-in-manila/|title=Davao-GenSan-Bitung RORO route to decongest ports in Manila|website=MindaNews.com|location=[[Davao City]], Philippines|publisher=[[Mindanao Institute of Journalism]]|access-date=27 April 2023}}</ref>  By 2019, however, the route had ceased operations due to a lack of demand caused by similarities in commodities produced by the two cities.<ref>{{Cite news|author=Sri Mas Sari|editor=Hendra Wibawa|date=18 July 2019|title=Pengoperasian Ulang Rute Bitung - Davao Masih Tunggu Insentif|url=https://ekonomi.bisnis.com/read/20190718/98/1126126/pengoperasian-ulang-rute-bitung-davao-masih-tunggu-insentif|trans-title=Re-operation of Bitung - Davao route still waiting for incentives|language=id|website=Bisnis.com|location=[[Jakarta]], Indonesia|publisher=Bisnis Indonesia|access-date=27 April 2023}}</ref>
