'''Cairo Governorate''' ({{langx|ar|محافظة القاهرة}}) is one of the 27 [[governorates of Egypt]]. It is formed of the city of [[Cairo]], both the national capital of [[Egypt]] and the governorate's, in addition to five [[Satellite city|satellite cities]]: the [[New Administrative Capital]] - set to become the seat of national government, [[New Cairo]], Shourok, [[Badr, Egypt|Badr]], Capital Gardens, and [[15th of May (city)|15th of May]]. These [[City|cities]] form almost half of the [[Greater Cairo]] [[metropolitan area]] by population.

Cairo is one of three [[city-state]]s in Egypt, and the governor of Cairo is also the head of the city.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Entities |url=http://www.cairo.gov.eg/en/Pages/entites.aspx |access-date=2022-12-23 |website=Cairo Governorate}}</ref> Nonetheless, the governate of Cairo and the city of Cairo are two semi-distinct levels of [[Governorates of Egypt|local government]], and as with other governorates, the governor is appointed by the president.

==Overview==
Parts of the governorate - the [[Helwan]] district and the satellite cities, were spun off in April 2008 to form the [[Helwan Governorate]], only to be reincorporated into the Cairo Governorate in April 2011.

==Municipal divisions==
The governorate is divided into the city of [[Cairo]], and the unallocated desert hinter land ({{transliteration|ar|al-zahir al-sahrawi}}). 
[[File:Cairo city administrative boundary and districts En.jpg|thumb]]

Cairo city is subdivided into four Areas ({{transliteration|ar|manatiq}}) headed by a deputy governor each, and 38 Districts ({{transliteration|ar|ahya}}, sing. {{transliteration|ar|Hayy}}) with their own governor appointed heads.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Areas |url=http://www.cairo.gov.eg/en/Pages/Regions.aspx?SubmID=141 |access-date=2022-12-22 |website=Cairo Governorate}}</ref> Some districts are represented by one police station ({{transliteration|ar|qism}}, plr. {{transliteration|ar|aqsam}}), while others may be subdivided by two or more.

'''The Northern Area''' divided into 8 Districts:

[[Shubra]], Al-Zawiya al-Hamra, Hadayek al-Kobba, [[Rod El Farag|Rod al-Farg]], al-Sharabiya, al-Sahel, al-Zaytoun, and Al-Amiriya

'''The Eastern Area''' divided into 9 Districts:

Misr al-Gadidah ([[Heliopolis, Cairo|Heliopolis]]), Al-Nozha, [[Nasr City]] East (Sharq Madinet Nasr), [[Nasr City]] West (Gharb Madinet Nasr), al-Salam Awwal, al-Salam Thani, [[El Matareya, Cairo|al-Matariya]], and [[El Marg|al-Marg]].

'''The Western Area''' divided into 9 Districts:

[[Manshiyat Naser|Manshiyat Nasser]], Al-Wayli, Wasat al-Qahira ({{transliteration|ar|Qism}}: [[Al-Darb al-Ahmar]], [[Al-Mu'izz Street|al-Gamaliyya]], al-Hussein), [[Boulaq]], Gharb El-Qahira ([[Zamalek]], [[Garden City, Cairo|Garden City]], [[Downtown Cairo|Down Town]]), [[Abdeen Palace|Abdeen]], [[Al Azbakiya|Al-Azbakiya]], al-Muski, and Bab El-Shaaria.

'''The Southern Area''' divided into 12 Districts:

Masr El-Qadima ([[Old Cairo]]), al-Khalifa, [[Mokattam|al-Moqattam]], al-Basatin, Dar El-Salam, El-Sayeda Zeinab, [[El Tebin|al-Tebin]], [[Helwan]], [[El Masara|al-Ma'sara]], [[Maadi|al-Maadi]], [[Tura, Egypt|Tora]], and [[15th of May (city)|15th may City]].

In addition to Cairo City, there are five satellite cities that are under the jurisdiction of the [[Ministry of Housing, Utilities & Urban Communities (Egypt)|Minister of Housing, Utilities and Urban Communities]] who chairs the [[New Urban Communities Authority]] that directly controls them (planning, land sales, zoning), while the governorate departments ({{transliteration|ar|muduriyat}}) control police and select public services (education, health, subsidised food).<ref>{{Cite web |title=Managing public services in the New Cities - Tadamun |url=http://www.tadamun.co/managing-public-services-in-the-new-cities/?lang=en#.Y6YnDMTMIoA |access-date=2022-12-23}}</ref> The more populated ones are subdivided into {{transliteration|ar|qisms}}, while the ones still under construction are yet to have governorate representation. These cities are:

[[Badr, Egypt|Badr]] ({{transliteration|ar|Qism}})
