'''Cẩm Phả''' ({{audio|Cam Pha.ogg|listen|help=no}}) is a [[Provincial city (Vietnam)|city]] of [[Quảng Ninh Province]] in the north-east region of [[Vietnam]]. It is an important [[coal]] exporting [[port]] of Vietnam (UNCTAD: VNCPH).<ref name="yicang">{{cite web|title=Cẩm Phả Port Information|url=http://www.yicang.com/|website=www.yicang.com|publisher=Yicang Port Database|access-date=15 June 2017|archive-date=2 February 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190202110904/http://www.yicang.com/|url-status=dead}}</ref> As of 2019, the city had a population of 190,232.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Cẩm Phả (District-level City, Quảng Ninh, Vietnam) - Population Statistics, Charts, Map and Location |url=https://www.citypopulation.de/en/vietnam/admin/qu%E1%BA%A3ng_ninh/195__c%E1%BA%A9m_ph%E1%BA%A3/ |access-date=2024-02-07 |website=www.citypopulation.de}}</ref> The city covers an area of 486&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>. The city was promoted from '''Cẩm Phả District''' by February 2012.<ref name=Chinhphu>{{in lang|vi}}[http://www2.chinhphu.vn/portal/page/portal/chinhphu/hethongvanban?class_id=509&_page=1&mode=detail&document_id=155316 Về việc thành lập thành phố Cẩm Phả thuộc tỉnh Quảng Ninh], Government Web Portal</ref> Cẩm Phả was the site of one of the biggest [[coal mine]]s in south of Asia which has been exploited for almost a century.<ref>T. H. Yellowdawn (2008). ''Fermented Foods: Naturally Enzymatic Therapy''.  p. 274: "Cẩm Phả is a town in Vietnam, where was the place of coal mine, the biggest coal industry in south of Asia has been exploited in almost a century, close to seashore of Ha Long Bay, where is the eighth wonderful scenery of thousand ..."</ref> The coal mine featured on a special commemorative Vietnam stamp in March 1959.<ref>[http://www.vietstamp.net/2/Product/1457/ Cẩm Phả coal mine] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131102134455/http://www.vietstamp.net/2/Product/1457/ |date=2013-11-02 }}</ref> Coal still forms the basis of Cẩm Phả's local economy, with other industries including quarrying, [[Lime (material)|lime]] production, port, agriculture, forestry and fisheries.

The official town website claims several scenic and historical sites in Cẩm Phả, including the [[Cửa Ông Temple|Cửa Ông]] Temple, Rều Island (Monkey Island), Thẻ Vàng Island, the Vũng Đục historical site, and Hang Hanh cave.<ref>[http://www.quangninh.gov.vn/en-US/Pages/Overview%20Detail.aspx?tongquanid=31  Cẩm Phả town website] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303220711/http://www.quangninh.gov.vn/en-US/Pages/Overview%20Detail.aspx?tongquanid=31 |date=2016-03-03 }} 2/25/2011</ref>

== History ==
In 1884, Cẩm Phả came under [[French Indochina|French administration]]. On 24 August 1886, Bavienpour established the coal company {{Lang|vi|Công ty Pháp mỏ than Bắc Kỳ}} ({{Langx|fr|Société Francais des Charbonneges du Ton Kin}}; SFCT). SFCT had the right to manage and exploit coal in the [[Hòn Gai]], Cẩm Phả, and [[Mông Dương]] regions.<ref>{{Cite web |last=MEDIATECH |title=Di tích trụ sở Công ty Pháp mỏ than Bắc Kỳ |url=https://baoquangninh.com.vn/di-tich-cong-ty-phap-mo-than-bac-ky-3164053.html |access-date=2022-03-22 |website=baoquangninh.com.vn |language=vi}}</ref>

On 27 September 1945, during the [[August Revolution]], workers drove the French out of Cẩm Phả. The French troops returned in November 1946, but the inhabitants fought back, destroying the coal mines, and hundreds of people were killed. The last French soldier withdrew from Cửa Ông, near the Cẩm Phả area on 22 April 1955.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.quangninh.gov.vn/vi-VN/huyenthi/txcampha/Trang/Tổng%20quan%20chi%20tiết.aspx?tongquanid=12|title=Lịch sử - văn hoá - xã hội |language=vi|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140103113359/http://www.quangninh.gov.vn/vi-VN/huyenthi/txcampha/Trang/T%E1%BB%95ng%20quan%20chi%20ti%E1%BA%BFt.aspx?tongquanid=12 |access-date=2022-03-22 |archive-date=3 January 2014 |website=CỔNG THÔNG TIN ĐIỆN TỬ TỈNH QUẢNG NINH}}</ref> 

During the [[Vietnam War]], American aircraft bombed Cẩm Phả several times due to its strategic importance.<ref>{{Cite book |url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/774384452 |title=The search for peace in Vietnam, 1964-1968 |date=2004 |publisher=Texas A & M University Press |author1=Lloyd C. Gardner |author2=Ted Gittinger |isbn=978-1-60344-650-1 |edition=1st |location=College Station |oclc=774384452}}</ref>

On 30 October 1963, Cẩm Phả, formerly in Hồng Quảng province, came under the jurisdiction of [[Quảng Ninh province]] when Hải Ninh and Hồng Quảng provinces merged.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Nghị Quyết hợp nhất tỉnh Hải Ninh và khu Hồng Quảng và sáp nhập xã Hữu Sản thuộc huyện Đình Lập, tỉnh Hải Ninh, huyện Sơn Động, tỉnh Hà Bắc |url=https://thuvienphapluat.vn/van-ban/Bo-may-hanh-chinh/Nghi-Quyet-hop-nhat-tinh-Hai-Ninh-va-khu-Hong-Quang-va-sap-nhap-xa-Huu-San-thuoc-huyen-Dinh-Lap-tinh-Hai-Ninh-huyen-Son-Dong-tinh-Ha-Bac-42700.aspx |access-date=2022-03-22 |website=thuvienphapluat.vn}}</ref>

On 10 September 1981, Cẩm Phả's boundaries were redistributed. The town of Mông Dương was dissolved to form Mông Dương ward and Cẩm Hải commune.  Cửa Ông town became Cửa Ông ward. Cọc 6 town and Thái Bình commune became Cẩm Phú and Cẩm Thịnh wards.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Quyết định 63-HĐBT phân vạch địa giới xã, phường thị trấn tỉnh Quảng Ninh |url=https://thuvienphapluat.vn/van-ban/Bo-may-hanh-chinh/Quyet-dinh-63-HDBT-phan-vach-dia-gioi-xa-phuong-thi-tran-tinh-Quang-Ninh-43124.aspx |access-date=2022-03-22 |website=thuvienphapluat.vn}}</ref>

On 21 February 2012, Cẩm Phả was upgraded from a town to a city.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Nghị quyết 04/NQ-CP thành lập thành phố Cẩm Phả tỉnh Quảng Ninh |url=https://thuvienphapluat.vn/van-ban/Bo-may-hanh-chinh/Nghi-quyet-04-NQ-CP-thanh-lap-thanh-pho-Cam-Pha-tinh-Quang-Ninh-134997.aspx |access-date=2022-03-22 |website=thuvienphapluat.vn}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Thành phố Cẩm Phả: Lễ kỷ niệm 10 năm ngày thành lập |url=http://campha.gov.vn/thanh-pho-cam-pha-le-ky-niem-10-nam-ngay-thanh-lap-p13n51984.html |access-date=2022-03-22 |website=campha.gov.vn |language=vi}}</ref>

==Administrative divisions==
The city contains 13 ''[[phường]]'': Quang Hanh, Cẩm Thạch, Cẩm Thuỷ, Cẩm Trung, Cẩm Thành, Cẩm Tây, Cẩm Bình, Cẩm Đông, Cẩm Sơn, Cẩm Phú, Cẩm Thịnh, Cửa Ông, [[Mông Dương]], and 3 communes: Dương Huy, Cộng Hoà, Cẩm Hải.

==Port of Cẩm Phả==
Though the port of Cẩm Phả has a general maximum draft of 4.8 metres, it has a separate coal loading terminal with a [[dredged]] depth of 6.1 metres and a channel depth of 7.1 metres.<ref>{{cite web|title=Port information - Cẩm Phả|publisher=Sea rates}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Port of Cẩm Phả - port information|url=http://port.sol.com.cn|website=www.port.sol.com.cn|publisher=China ports directory|access-date=15 June 2017}}</ref> This allows ships of up to 10 metres [[Draft (hull)|draft]] to load due to the [[tidal range]] in the port.<ref name="gpe">{{cite book|title=Guide to port entry - Port of Cẩm Phả - Ship Masters report|date=1 January 2017|publisher=IHS Fairplay|location=London}}</ref> The coal loading terminal is 299 metres long, and can accommodate ships that are 165 metres in length, at an average loading rate of 6,000 tonnes per day.<ref name="yicang"/>

== Environment ==
Coal mining in Cẩm Phả started over 100 years ago. Mining area discharges to the sea about 9 million m<sup>3</sup> waste water and 8 million m<sup>3</sup> domestic waste water. Another issue is deforestation, for example, from 1969 to 1974, about 42% of area of forest land was reduced.<ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Vg8Ukw15XjIC&q=cam+pha&pg=PA266 |page=266|year=1996|title=Mining Science and Technology: Proceedings of the '96 International Symposium on Mining Science and Technology|author =Yuguang Guo, Tad S. Golosinski|publisher=CRC Press |isbn=9789054108252}}</ref>
