'''Moundou''' ({{Langx|ar|موندو}}) is the [[List of cities in Chad|second-largest city]] in [[Chad]] and is the capital of the [[regions of Chad|region]] of [[Logone Occidental Region|Logone Occidental]].

The city lies on the [[Mbéré River]] (a tributary of the Western [[Logone River|Logone]]) some 475 kilometres south of the capital [[N'Djamena]]. It is the main city of the [[Sara people|Ngambai]] people. Moundou has grown as an industrial centre, home to the Gala Brewery, which produces Chad's most popular beer and the [[cotton]] and [[Petroleum|oil]] industries.

== History ==
The city was created on 8 November 1923 by the French sergeant and administrator Joseph-François Reste, Lieutenant-General of Chad from 1923 to 1926 and future Governor General of [[French Equatorial Africa]], 
<ref>Mario J. Azevedo, Samuel Decalo, ''Historical Dictionary of Chad'', Rowman & Littlefield, USA, 2018, p. 355</ref><ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=http://lalbere.net/queconnaitre/legouverneur.htm|title=Le gouverneur Général François Reste de Roca|website=lalbere.net|access-date=2016-12-11|archive-date=27 August 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190827041750/http://lalbere.net/queconnaitre/legouverneur.htm|url-status=dead}}</ref> who, from the whaleboat upon which he navigated the Logone, found the site pretty.<ref name=":4">{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=EGLydjKGM2YC&q=moundou+petit+paris&pg=PA170|title=Tchad|last1=Auzias|first1=Dominique|last2=Labourdette|first2=Jean-Paul|date=2010-10-01|publisher=Petit Futé|isbn=978-2746929432|pages=170|language=fr}}</ref> By 1916, the military conquest of Chad was completed, however movements of resistance to the colonial regime took place. It was especially in the southwest of the country that dissensions continued until about 1930.<ref name=":6" /><ref name=":7" /> He decided to found the post of Moundou in the centre of the rebellious zone.<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|url=http://www.persee.fr/docAsPDF/outre_0300-9513_1993_num_80_300_3118.pdf|title=Résistances et mouvements anticoloniaux au Tchad (1914-1940)|page=429}}{{Dead link|date=May 2023 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> The rebellious situation did not change much with the arrival of Lieutenant Robert Reverdy. Chief of the district of the Middle Logone in 1925, Reverdy, who became a director in 1927, completed an uninterrupted stay for five years and eight months. He moved the chief town of the district to Moundou in 1927. Riding the country, on horseback and by [[Litter (vehicle)|litter]], he subjected the organisation of strong chieftains, first of village then of township.

On 20 April 1930, Governor Georges Prouteaux of [[Ubangi-Shari|Oubangi-Chari]] (the district was attached to Oubangui-Chari in 1926) signed a decision reorganising the "indigenous of the Middle Logone" by creating 40 cantons, divided into five subdivisions. Reverdy had his right-hand man, local chief Hassan Moundou or Hassan Baguirmi, of [[Bagirmi language|Baguirmian]] or rather Baguirmianised [[Baggara|Dekakire Arab]] origin, installed as chief of the township of Moundou.<ref name=":8" /><ref name=":6">{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=bcwwAQAAIAAJ&q=moundou+chef+ouaidou|title=L'Islam au Tchad: actes du colloque organisé au Centre d'étude d'Afrique noire de l'Institut d'études politiques de Bordeaux, 1990; sous la direction de Jean-Pierre Magnant|last=Magnant|first=Jean-Pierre|date=1992-01-01|publisher=CEAN|isbn=9782908065121|pages=115|language=fr}}</ref><ref name=":7">{{Cite web|url=http://www.persee.fr/docAsPDF/outre_0300-9513_1993_num_80_300_3118.pdf|title=Résistances et mouvements anticoloniaux au Tchad (1914-1940)}}{{Dead link|date=May 2023 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> Not all chiefs were of traditional origin. In the animist country, custom only recognised clan chiefs or war or initiation leaders, who were strictly specialised and temporary. However, the system was accepted by the population, and some chiefs remained in office for more than a quarter of a century. Their descendants are still in place today.<ref name=":8">{{Cite web|url=http://www.persee.fr/docAsPDF/outre_0300-9513_1993_num_80_300_3118.pdf|title=Résistances et mouvements anticoloniaux au Tchad (1914-1940)|page=430}}{{Dead link|date=May 2023 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

Reverdy<ref name=":5" /> (called ''Baoguel'', the "Left-Handed", in the [[Ngambay language]])<ref name=":4" /> urbanised the post of Moundou that he established. He planted the flowers (from February to May) that line the roads of Moundou, which became the origin of the nickname of the city during the colonial period, "''Moundou-la-Rouge''".<ref name=":4" />

In 1926 the Cotton Company of the Congo installed a [[ginning]] factory in Moundou. The cotton company later became Cotonfran in 1928 and then [[Cotontchad|CotonTchad]] in 1972.<ref>{{Cite book|title=Étude des mécanismes de croissance urbaine du Tchad, l'exemple de Moundou et Sahr (ex Fort Archambault)|last=Nojibaye|first=Daniel|publisher=Université Lille 2|year=1976|location=Lille}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ePX_BgAAQBAJ&q=moundou&pg=PA158|title=Chad Mining Laws and Regulations Handbook Volume 1 Strategic Information and Basic Law|last=Inc|first=IBP|date=2008-03-03|publisher=Lulu.com|isbn=9781433077159|pages=158|language=en}}</ref>

The first [[Cadastral surveying|cadastral]] map of the town of Moundou was manufactured on 2 February 1926. Since there were not, at the time, the materials for substantial [[Topography|topographical]] surveys, the drainage pipeline of the town was facing the river in the belief that the natural inclination would head toward the river, when in reality the river level is higher than the level of the city. In 2012, the mayor of Moundou, Laoukein Kourayo Médard, said that "every time there was a flood, all the waters of the Logone discharged to [[Lake Wey]] (in the West), which in turn sprays its water into the city, flooding Moundou entirely."<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://tidjanicapi.centerblog.net/195-urbanisme|title=Urbanisme|date=2012-05-15|newspaper=Centerblog|access-date=2016-12-09}}</ref>

== Geography ==

=== Climate ===
Like other parts of southern Chad and the [[East Sudanian savanna]], Moundou has a typical [[tropical savanna climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification|Köppen]] ''Aw''), with a wet season and a dry season and the temperature being hot year-round. The average annual high temperature is {{convert|34.0|C|F|abbr=on}}, while the average annual low temperature is {{convert|20.1|C|F|abbr=on}}. The hottest time of year is from March to May, just before the wet season starts. March has the highest average high at {{convert|38.6|C|F|abbr=on}}, while the highest average low is {{convert|24.2|C|F|abbr=on}} in April. August has the lowest average high at {{convert|29.8|C|F|abbr=on}} (the only average high below {{convert|30.0|C|F|abbr=on}}), while December has the lowest average low at {{convert|14.6|C|F|abbr=on}}.

Moundou receives {{convert|1082.8|mm}} of rain over 85 precipitation days, with a distinct wet and dry season like most tropical savanna climates. December and January receive no precipitation at all, with almost no rain falling from November to March. August, the wettest month, receives {{convert|284.8|mm}} of rainfall on average. August also has 19 precipitation days, which is the most of any month. Humidity is much higher in the wet season than the dry season, with February having a humidity at just 28% and August having a humidity at 81%. Moundou receives 2810.4 hours of sunshine annually on average, with the sunshine being distributed fairly evenly across the year, although it is lower during the wet season. December receives the most sunshine, while August receives the least.

== Economy ==
The city has an industry of ginning and processing of cotton (oil and soap) under [[Cotontchad|CotonTchad]]. The export of cotton was done before the discovery of oil, the current main source of income for Chad. Cotton cultivation has been funded by French corporations (through the CCCE, now the [[French Development Agency|AFD]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.africotton.org/aca/fr/IMG/pdf/a.c.a-2016-presentation_cotontchad-fauba.pdf|title=LA FILIERE COTONNIERE DU TCHAD Historique, Evolution et Perspectives|date=2016|page=7|access-date=10 December 2016|archive-date=20 December 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161220095552/http://www.africotton.org/aca/fr/IMG/pdf/a.c.a-2016-presentation_cotontchad-fauba.pdf|url-status=dead}}</ref> and the public company Dagris, now [[:fr:Geocoton|Geocoton]]) and the [[European Development Fund]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://eeas.europa.eu/archives/delegations/tchad/documents/press_corner/lettre8_fr.pdf|title="10eme fed" - mai 2008 - Europa|date=May 2008}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=k-KnzH85w2oC&q=Fonds+europ%C3%A9en+pour+le+d%C3%A9veloppement+coton+tchad&pg=PA125|title=Le sud du Tchad en mutation: Des champs de coton aux sirènes de l'or noir|last=Magrin|first=Géraud|date=2001-01-01|publisher=Editions Quae|isbn=9782876144620|pages=125|language=fr}}</ref> of the [[European Union]].

The city has also a historical beer industry through the Brasseries du Logone and the Gala brand,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://bdt-td.com/fr/histoire.php|title=Histoire des Brasseries du Tchad|website=bdt-td.com|access-date=2016-12-10|archive-date=15 November 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161115132149/http://www.bdt-td.com/fr/histoire.php|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=k-KnzH85w2oC&q=Brasseries+du+Logone&pg=PA178|title=Le sud du Tchad en mutation: Des champs de coton aux sirènes de l'or noir|last=Magrin|first=Géraud|date=2001-01-01|publisher=Editions Quae|isbn=9782876144620|pages=178|language=fr}}</ref> synonymous with luxury beer in Chad and in Cameroon.

Cigarettes are made in the city by the Manufacture des Cigarettes du Tchad (MCT) company.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.petitfute.com/v45701-moundou/c1173-visites-points-d-interet/c976-archeologie-artisanat-science-et-technique/c980-industrie/158596-manufacture-des-cigarettes-du-tchad-mct.html|title=MANUFACTURE DES CIGARETTES DU TCHAD (MCT) - Petit Futé}}</ref>

== Transports ==
Moundou is situated on one of the main roads in southern Chad. The road runs from [[Léré, Chad|Léré]] on the border with [[Cameroon]], through [[Pala, Chad|Pala]], [[Kélo]], Moundou, [[Doba, Chad|Doba]], [[Koumra]] and [[Sarh]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.google.co.uk/maps/dir/Lere,+Mayo-Kebbi+Ouest,+Chad/Moundou,+Chad/Sarh,+Moyen-Chari,+Chad/@9.0869009,15.1744168,474074m/data=!3m2!1e3!4b1!4m20!4m19!1m5!1m1!1s0x10e2fef02562c759:0xdf26554525a29fce!2m2!1d14.2250605!2d9.6554876!1m5!1m1!1s0x10e73ebffc18d83f:0x8fe7eb07cb7dda17!2m2!1d16.0757749!2d8.5914775!1m5!1m1!1s0x10d93e76f16b3be7:0x90bec5c5a1b8079a!2m2!1d18.3682641!2d9.1412086!3e0?hl=en|title=Google Maps|website=Google Maps|access-date=2016-12-11}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.afdb.org/fileadmin/uploads/afdb/Documents/Environmental-and-Social-Assessments/30776511-EN-TCHAD-RESUME-EIES-KOUMRA-SARH.PDF|title=RÉSUMÉ DE L'ÉTUDE D'IMPACT ENVIRONNEMENTAL ET SOCIAL DU TRONÇON ROUTIER DOBA - SARH|date=December 2008|publisher=Fonds Africain de Développement}}</ref>

The town is served by [[Moundou Airport]] {{airport codes|MQQ|FTTD}}, with a paved runway.

== Education ==
Moundou is home to several schools and, since 2002, a higher education institution, the [[:fr:Université de Moundou|University of Moundou (fr)]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.etudiant-tchadien.com/universit%C3%A9-de-moundou-udm.html|title=Université de Moundou (UDM) - L'Etudiant tchadien|last=Moustapha|website=www.etudiant-tchadien.com|access-date=2016-12-09|archive-date=28 January 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180128130608/http://www.etudiant-tchadien.com/universit%C3%A9-de-moundou-udm.html|url-status=dead}}</ref> which was created by ordinance No. 013 / PR / 2008 of 5 March 2008.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=http://legitchad.cefod-tchad.org/texte/1637|title=Légitchad|website=legitchad.cefod-tchad.org|access-date=2016-12-10|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161220144638/http://legitchad.cefod-tchad.org/texte/1637|archive-date=20 December 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> The current university used to be the Moundou University Institute of Business Techniques or IUTEM (''Institut universitaire des techniques d'entreprise de Moundou'') which was created by Law No. 10 / PR / 02 of 2 September 2002.<ref name=":2" />
