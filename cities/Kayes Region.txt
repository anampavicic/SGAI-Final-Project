'''Kayes Region''' ([[Bambara language|Bambara]]: ߞߊߦߌ ߘߌߣߋߖߊ tr. Kayi Dineja, {{Langx|ar|منطقة كايس|minṭaqa Kāyas}}) is one of ten first level national subdivisions in [[Mali]] called [[Regions of Mali|Regions]]. It is the first administrative area of Mali and covers an area of {{convert|120,760|sqkm|sqmi|-1|disp=or}}. Its capital is the town of [[Kayes]]. The province was historically part of the [[Ghana Empire]] and the [[Mali Empire]].

== Geography ==

The region of Kayes is bordered to the north by [[Mauritania]], to the west by [[Senegal]], to the south by [[Guinea]] and to the east by the region of [[Koulikoro Region|Koulikoro]].

In 2009 the region has a population of 1,996,812 inhabitants. Ethnic groups of the area include [[Soninke people|Soninké]]s, [[Khassonké]]s, [[Malinké]]s, [[Dialonké]]s, and [[Fula people|Fulas]] ({{langx|fr|Peuls}}; {{langx|ff|Fulɓe}}).

Several rivers cross the region: the [[Baloué River|Baoulé]], the [[Bafing River|Bafing]], and the [[Bakoy River|Bakoy]] which join at the town of [[Bafoulabé]] to form the [[Sénégal River]]. The [[Félou Falls|Falls of Félou]] (15&nbsp;km from Kayes), the [[Gouina Falls|Falls of Gouina]] (60&nbsp;km to the south-east of Kayes on the Sénégal River), [[Talari Gorges]], [[Lake Magui]] and [[Lake Doro]] are located in the region.

At the Guinean border, the climate is rather wet, but becomes [[Sudan (region)|Sudanian]] and later [[Sahel]]ian to the north.

The largest cities in the region are [[Kayes]], [[Kita, Mali|Kita]], [[Bafoulabé]], [[Nioro du Sahel]], [[Diéma, Mali|Diéma]], [[Yélimané]], Diangounté, [[Sadiola]], and [[Kéniéba]] 
The [[Bafing National Park]] and the [[Boucle du Baoulé National Park]] are located in the region.

== History ==
The region of Kayes is the cradle of the Kingdom of [[Khasso]] founded at the beginning of the 19th century.

In 1855, [[Louis Faidherbe]], Governor of Senegal, built a fort at [[Siege of Medina Fort|Medina]] which would be besieged by [[Umar Tall|El Hadj Omar Tall]], in an 1857 war against the sovereign of Khasso. In 1892, the town of [[Kayes]] became the capital of [[French Sudan]].

The construction of the [[railway line of Dakar-Niger]], inaugurated in 1904, made of Kayes a city-crossroads. Essential at the time, the railroad had an important place in the lives of the inhabitants, as described in [[Ousmane Sembène]]'s novel ''God's Bits of Wood''.
