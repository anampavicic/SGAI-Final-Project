'''Douliu''' ([[Hokkien]] [[Pe̍h-ōe-jī|POJ]]: ''Táu-la̍k'') is a [[county-administered city]] and the [[county seat]] of [[Yunlin County]], [[Taiwan]]. It is also the political and economic center of the county. Douliu City is served by [[National Highway No. 3 (Taiwan)|National Highway No. 3]].

==Name==
Its former name ({{zh|c=斗六門|poj=Táu-la̍k-mn̂g}}) came from a language of the [[Hoanya people]], a tribe of the [[Taiwanese plains aborigines]].

==History==

===Empire of Japan===
[[File:Txu-pclmaps-oclc-6550512-toroku-2218-iii.jpg|left|thumb|Map of Douliu (labeled as Toroku) and surrounding area (1944)]]
In 1901, during [[Taiwan under Japanese rule|Japanese rule]], {{nihongo||斗六廳|Toroku Chō}} was one of twenty local administrative offices established. In 1909, part of Toroku was merged into {{nihongo4||南投廳|Nanto Chō}}, while the remainder was merged with {{nihongo4||嘉義廳|Kagi Chō}}. In 1920, {{nihongo4|Toroku Town|斗六街}} was established and governed under {{nihongo4|Toroku District|斗六郡}}, [[Tainan Prefecture]]. Toroku Town covered modern-day Douliu and [[Linnei, Yunlin|Linnei Township]].

===Republic of China===
[[File:Txu-oclc-6557994-index-453.jpg|left|thumb|Map including Douliu (labeled as Tou-liu (Torkou) {{lang|zh-tw|斗六}}) (1951)]]
On 25 December 1981, Douliu was upgraded from an [[Township (Taiwan)|urban township]] to [[county-administered city]].

==Places of interest==
*'''Taiping Street:''' An old street featuring [[Taiwan under Japanese rule|Japanese-era]] baroque buildings from three different periods. The street is a common location for special events and performing arts venues.
*'''Renwen Park & Sports Ground:'''  (運動公園及人文公園) Located across from the [[National Yunlin University of Science and Technology]], the park includes a skating rink, trails, etc. It serves as a public transportation, sports, and entertainment venue. It is also a common tourist destination during major festivals.
*'''Douliu Roundabout:''' Also called "Douliu Door", it is a landmark of Yunlin County. Water shows occur every evening with music.
*'''Yukihiro Memorial:''' In 1915 during [[Taiwan under Japanese rule|Japanese rule]], Crown Prince [[Hirohito]] (the future Emperor Showa) came to inspect the construction of public buildings. The area has been developed into a public space and community activity center.
*'''Toroku POW Camp Memorial:''' A memorial stone located at the Gouba Elementary School. It preserves the memory of POWs of the Japanese during World War II.
*'''Renwen Park Night Market:''' Located next to Renwen Park, a temporary night market sets up on Saturdays.
*'''Taiwan Temple Art Museum:''' (台灣寺廟藝術館)
* '''[[House of Citizen-Memorial Hall of Attendance]]'''
* '''[[Hushan Dam]]'''
* '''[[General Baokeng]]'''

===Rail===
Douliu is served through [[Douliu Station]] and [[Shiliu Station]] by [[Taiwan Railway]]. Douliu Railway Station opened in 1904,<ref>{{cite web |title=Douliou Station |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111201221057/https://www.railway.gov.tw/Douliou/CP.aspx?SN=11017 |website=Taiwan Railways Administration |publisher=Taiwan Railways Administration |access-date=21 August 2024}}</ref> and Shiliu in 1905. It is also served via [[Yunlin HSR station]] by [[Taiwan High Speed Rail]], located In Huwei Township. Currently, there is no rail connection from the HSR station to Douliu Railway Station.
