'''Brusque''' is a city in the [[Federative units of Brazil|state]] of [[Santa Catarina (state)|Santa Catarina]], [[Brazil]]. It was founded in 1860, by 55 German immigrants.

The city was originally called Colônia Itajahy, but on 17 January 1890 it was renamed Brusque after former Santa Catarina province president Francisco Carlos de Araújo Brusque.<ref>{{cite web|title=O legado de Francisco Carlos de Araújo Brusque|url=http://www.congressodacidade.brusque.sc.gov.br/web/conteudo.php?noticia=12:O_legado_de_Francisco_Carlos_de_Araujo_Brusque|website=Prefeitura de Brusque|access-date=23 January 2016|language=pt|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160128183643/http://www.congressodacidade.brusque.sc.gov.br/web/conteudo.php?noticia=12%3AO_legado_de_Francisco_Carlos_de_Araujo_Brusque|archive-date=28 January 2016}}</ref>

== Sports ==

The official [[Association football|football]] team is [[Brusque Futebol Clube]], founded on October 12, 1987.<ref name="arquivodeclubes">{{cite web | title=Brusque Futebol Clube | url=http://www.arquivodeclubes.com/sc/brusque.htm | access-date=2008-12-07 | language=pt | publisher=Arquivo de Clubes | url-status=dead | archive-url=https://archive.today/20130103005801/http://www.arquivodeclubes.com/sc/brusque.htm | archive-date=2013-01-03 }}</ref>

Brusque has a strong women's volleyball team in the [[Brazilian Volleyball Super League (Women)|Superliga Brasileira de Voleibol]].

== Curiosities ==
{{Unreferenced section|date=March 2023}}
- The city is a pioneer in the invention of the voting machine, and the first polling station to use it was also in Brusque (1988).

- First Latin American city to have public computers (1995).

- Headquarters of the first spinning industry of Santa Catarina (1892 - Textile Factory Renaux).

- Headquarters of the only English colony of Santa Catarina (1867).

- The only city in Brazil to use [[chlorine dioxide]] to treat water consumed by the population.

Current ethnic composition:
90% Whites; 5% Pardos; 4% Blacks; 0.5% Native American; 0.5% Asian. 
Original Population: 50% German; 30% Italian; 10% Portuguese; 9% African; 1% Asian.

== Religions ==
