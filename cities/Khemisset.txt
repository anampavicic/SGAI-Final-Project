'''Khemisset''' ({{langx|ar|الخميسات}}) is a city in northern [[Morocco]] with a population of 131,542 recorded in the [[2014 Moroccan census]].<ref name=census2014/> It is situated on the A2 motorway between [[Rabat]] (81&nbsp;km) and [[Meknès]] (57&nbsp;km), and is the capital of [[Khémisset Province]].

From 1912 to 1914 the French built a 600&nbsp;mm [[History of rail transport in Morocco#Chemins de Fer de Penetration du Maroc|narrow gauge railway]] from Rabat via Souk el Abra des Sehoul, [[Tiflet]], [[Dar Caid Bou Driss]] to Khemisset. It was abandoned in 1935 and lifted before 1942. Between [[Tiflet]] and Khemisset, the old [[track bed]] of narrow gauge line  was later built to Rabat Khemisset main road.{{Citation needed|date=March 2024}} 

== Geography ==

[[Khémisset Province]] is located between the Atlas and the northwest, and is 37 km away from the ocean. The climate is moderate and conducive to agriculture. The region has two dams, eighteen valleys, and a dayet roumi.{{Citation needed|date=March 2024}}

The Khemisset region represents Morocco's production of lentils, where 10,000 hectares are cultivated annually, and the production is to 100,000 quintals. Sweet grapes for wine are also widely produced.{{Citation needed|date=March 2024}}

=== Potash field ===
In 2019, 580 million tons of [[potash]] were discovered in the province of Khemisset. Production will begin in 2024 by company Emmerson PLC. The initial production will be an estimated 800,000 tons annually.{{Citation needed|date=March 2024}}
