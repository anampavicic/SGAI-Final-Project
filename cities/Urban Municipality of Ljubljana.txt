The '''Urban Municipality of Ljubljana''' ({{IPA|sl|ljuˈbljaːna|pron}}), also the '''City of Ljubljana''' ({{langx|sl|Mestna občina Ljubljana}}, acronym '''MOL''') is one of twelve [[Municipalities of Slovenia|city and metropolitan municipalities in Slovenia]]. Its seat is [[Ljubljana]], the largest and capital city of Slovenia. {{As of|December 2021}}, its mayor is [[Zoran Janković (politician)|Zoran Janković]].

==Economy==
The [[budget]] of MOL was 346,505,748 [[euro]]s for 2011. It was shaped by the sale of [[land lot]] and the construction of the [[Stožice Sports Park]]. With 125 million euros of [[debt]], MOL was the most indebted Slovenian municipality in April 2010.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://beta2.finance-on.net/pics/cache_07/07b9Untitled-15.1323374528.gif |title=Zadolženost občin (v mio EUR) |language=Slovenian |trans-title=Municipal indebtness |publisher=Finance.net}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.rtvslo.si/gospodarstvo/mol-s-125-milijoni-dolgorocnega-dolga-najbolj-zadolzena-mestna-obcina/255221|title = MOL s 125 milijoni dolgoročnega dolga najbolj zadolžena mestna občina}}</ref>

==Holiday==
Since 1945, the holiday of the City of Ljubljana has been celebrated on 9 May. On that day, the liberation of the city from German occupation during [[World War II in the Slovene Lands|World War II]] was announced in 1945. In 1964, the holiday was added to the statute of the municipality as "the day of the liberation of Ljubljana in the victorious national liberation war 1941–1945". Since 1995, it has been known as "the day of freedom and peace". In 1995, despite disagreements, 14 April was added to the statute as the date of the first mention of Ljubljana in written sources, based on a document from 1243.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.delo.si/novice/slovenija/bo-ljubljana-ostala-brez-enega-praznika.html |title=Bo Ljubljana ostala brez enega praznika? |language=Slovenian |trans-title=Will Ljubljana Remain Without One Holiday? |first=Nina |last=Krajčinović |date=3 February 2012 |newspaper=Delo.si |publisher=Delo, d. d.}}</ref>

The holiday on 14 April was much less known than the holiday on 9 May.<ref name="Predlog2011" /> In February 2011, the mayor presented a proposal to only celebrate 9 May to the municipal council, because it was inexpedient for Ljubljana to be the only municipality in Slovenia to celebrate two days as municipal holidays, and to celebrate the municipal holiday on the date of the first mention, which may change with time.<ref name="Predlog2011">{{cite news |url=http://www.ljubljana.si/file/1086552/5.-toka---spremembe-in-dopolnitve-statuta.pdf |title=Predlog Sprememb in dopolnitev Statuta Mestne obĉine Ljubljana s predlogom za hitri postopek |language=Slovenian |trans-title=A Proposal of Changes and Amendments to the Statute of the Urban Municipality of Ljubljana with a Proposal for a Summary Procedure |publisher=Urban Municipality of Ljubljana |date=24 February 2011 }}{{Dead link|date=November 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> The proposal was passed by the municipal council and became valid in February 2012. Since then, the only holiday of the Urban Municipality of Ljubljana has been 9 May, the day of freedom and peace.<ref>{{cite news |language=Slovenian |url=http://www.uradni-list.si/1/objava.jsp?urlurid=2012610 |title=Spremembe in dopolnitve statuta Mestne občine Ljubljana |trans-title=Changes and Amendments to the Statute of the Urban Municipality of Ljubljana |date=29 February 2012 |newspaper=Official Gazette of the Republic of Slovenia}}</ref>
