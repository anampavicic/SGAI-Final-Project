'''Anju-si''' ({{IPA|ko|an.dzu}}) is a city in the [[South Pyongan Province|South P'yŏngan]] province of [[North Korea]]. Its population was 240,117 in 2008.<ref name="NKCSB" /> The [[Ch'ongch'on River]] passes through Anju.  [[image:Sinanju Chongnyon Station.jpg|thumb|200px|[[Sinanju Chongnyon station]] in the western part of Anju]]

==Economy==
Anju lies near large deposits of anthracite coal, and contains one of the largest coal production facilities in the country.<ref name="handbook">{{cite book |title=North Korea Handbook |year=2003 |publisher=M.E. Sharpe |isbn=0765610043 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=JIlh9nNeadMC |access-date=2012-07-18}}</ref>
The deposits contain more than 130 million metric tons of coal.<ref name="minind">{{cite news |title=The mineral industry of North Korea |last=Kuo |first=Chin S. |year=1994 |url=http://minerals.usgs.gov/minerals/pubs/country/1994/9315094.pdf |access-date=2012-07-18}}</ref> Namhŭng-dong is the location of the [[Namhung Youth Chemical Complex|Namhŭng Youth Chemical Complex]], one of North Korea's most important chemical combines.<ref name=38north>{{cite web|url=http://38north.org/2014/04/jbermudez041014/|title=North Korea’s Namhung Youth Chemical Complex: Seven Years of Construction Pays Off|author=Joseph S. Bermudez Jr.|publisher=US-Korea Institute at SAIS|date=10 April 2014}}</ref> Anju also contains at least one hotel open for foreigners, used primarily to accommodate for more travelers during peak holiday times.<ref name=YPT>{{cite web|url=https://www.youngpioneertours.com/anju-county/|title=Anju County, North Korea|author=Gareth Johnson|publisher=Young Pioneer Tours|date=20 December 2019}}</ref>

Anju is the location of the [[Cemetery for Martyrs of the Chinese People's Volunteers]], commemorating [[Korean War]] dead.

==Transportation==
Anju-si is served by several stations on the [[Pyongui Line|P'yŏngŭi]] and [[Kaechon Line|Kaech'ŏn]] lines of the [[Korean State Railway]].

Anju has a [[trolleybus]] system with one line to Sinanju Chongnyon station. It formerly had another line to Namhung Youth Chemical Complex that looped around the entire complex which closed around 2000.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Anju|url=https://transphoto.org/city/1293/|access-date=2021-01-02|website=transphoto.org}}</ref>
