'''Neftekamsk''' ({{langx|ru|Нефтека́мск}}; {{langx|ba|Нефтекама|translit=Neftekama}}) is a [[types of inhabited localities in Russia|city]] in the [[Republic of Bashkortostan]], [[Russia]], located in the northwest of the republic on the [[Kama River]], {{convert|220|km|sp=us}} from the republic's [[capital city|capital]] [[Ufa]]. It is a large industrial and cultural center of the republic. Population: {{ru-census|p2010=121,733|p2002=122,290|p1989=106,801}}

==Etymology==
The city is named after the [[Kama River]] and [[petroleum]] ({{lang|ru|нефть}}, ''neft'' in Russian).

==Geography==
The city's territory borders [[Krasnokamsky District|Krasnokamsky]] and [[Yanaulsky District]]s. A road network connects the town to Ufa, [[Birsk]], [[Yanaul]], [[Agidel]], [[Oktyabrsky, Republic of Bashkortostan|Oktyabrsky]], [[Izhevsk]], [[Perm, Russia|Perm]], and [[Kazan]]. The nearest railway station for long-distance trains is located in [[Amzya]], {{convert|15|km|sp=us}} away. There is a local train connecting Neftekamsk with Yanaul via Amzya. [[Neftekamsk Airport]] serves the city.

==History==
It was founded in 1957 after the discovery of an oil field in [[Krasnokamsky District]].{{citation needed|date=June 2010}} Town status was granted to it in 1963.{{citation needed|date=June 2010}}

==Administrative and municipal status==
Within the [[subdivisions of Russia#Administrative divisions|framework of administrative divisions]], it is, together with seven rural localities, incorporated as the '''[[city of federal subject significance|city of republic significance]] of Neftekamsk'''—an administrative unit with the status equal to that of the [[administrative divisions of the Republic of Bashkortostan|districts]].<ref name="Resolution">Resolution #391</ref> As a [[subdivisions of Russia#Municipal divisions|municipal division]], the city of republic significance of Neftekamsk is incorporated as '''Neftekamsk Urban Okrug'''.<ref name="Law">Law #162-z</ref>

==Economy==
The principal place of business of the city is the [[Neftekamsk Automotive Plant]], which is one of the major producers of passenger buses in Russia.
