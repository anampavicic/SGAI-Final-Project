'''Carúpano''' is a city in the eastern [[Venezuela]]n [[States of Venezuela|state]] of [[Sucre (state)|Sucre]].  It is located on the [[Venezuela]]n [[Caribbean]] coast at the opening of two valleys, some 120&nbsp;km east of the capital of Sucre, [[Cumaná, Venezuela|Cumaná]].<ref name=autogenerated1>[http://edosucre.gov.ve/municipios/bermudez.htm Gobernación Del Estado Sucre<!-- Bot generated title -->] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070920175445/http://edosucre.gov.ve/municipios/bermudez.htm |date=2007-09-20 }}</ref>   This city is the [[County seat|shire town]] of the [[Bermúdez Municipality]] and, according to the 2010 Venezuelan [[census]], the municipality has a population of 173,877 inhabitants.<ref name=autogenerated2>[http://www.ine.gov.ve/censo/fichascenso/tiro.asp?cod_entidad=19 Primeros Resultados XIII Censo General de Población y Vivienda<!-- Bot generated title -->] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070825041032/http://www.ine.gov.ve/censo/fichascenso/tiro.asp?cod_entidad=19 |date=August 25, 2007 }}</ref> Carúpano is considered the gateway to the [[Paria Peninsula]] and its main commercial and financial center.

==History==
It was somewhere on the Peninsula of Paria, near Carúpano, where [[Christopher Columbus]] first set foot on the American continent for the only time, during his third voyage (in all his other trips he only explored the [[Caribbean]] islands).

It was in Carúpano where [[Simón Bolívar]], the liberator of Venezuela, issued a decree ending slavery in 1814.<ref>[http://www.venezuelatuya.com/oriente/carupano.htm Carúpano - Venezuela Tuya<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

In 1815, King Ferdinand VII of Spain, sent a fleet of 18 warships and 42 cargo ships to Carupano and [[Isla Margarita]] with the mission of pacifying the revolts against the Spanish monarchy in the South American colonies.

In May 1962 Carúpano was the scene of a short-lived military rebellion against the government of [[Rómulo Betancourt]], in which rebel military officers took over the city. The incident is known as ''[[El Carupanazo]]''.

In July 1997, [[1997 Cariaco earthquake|a violent earthquake]] struck the city and most of the state.  This earthquake was centered in the town of [[Cariaco]], where most of the deaths and damage occurred.<ref>[http://elobservador.rctv.net/Reportajes/VerReportaje.aspx?ReportajeId=212 El Observador Online<!-- Bot generated title -->] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070820113458/http://elobservador.rctv.net/Reportajes/VerReportaje.aspx?ReportajeId=212 |date=August 20, 2007 }}</ref><ref>[http://cidbimena.desastres.hn/docum/ops/publicaciones/who69s/index.htm Crónicas de Desastres - Terremoto de Cariaco, Venezuela (Pan American Health Organization (PAHO) / Organización Panamericana de la Salud (OPS), 1999, 57 p.)<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

==Economy==
[[Cocoa bean|Cacao]], [[coffee]], [[sugar]], [[cotton]], [[timber]] and [[rum]]<ref>[[Carupano (rum)|Carupano Distillery]]</ref> have been important exports of Carúpano since colonial times.<ref>{{Cite EB1911|wstitle=Carúpano|volume=5|page=436}}</ref> Carupanese rums are highly appreciated nationwide, so the internal consumption usually leaves little surplus rum for export.

Currently the local [[General José Francisco Bermúdez Airport]] does not carry regular commercial flights.<ref>[http://www.avior.com.ve/ Avior Airlines...un Viaje de Diferencias<!-- Bot generated title -->] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070928081048/http://www.avior.com.ve/ |date=September 28, 2007 }}</ref>

==Demographics==
[[File:Venezuela - Sucre - Bermúdez.svg|thumb|Bermúdez Municipality in Sucre State]]
The Bermúdez Municipality, according to the 2001 Venezuelan census, has a population of 175,877 (up from 100,794 in 1990).  This amounts to 15.5% of Sucre's population.<ref name=autogenerated2 />  The municipality's population density is 1,559 people per square mile (601.95/km<sup>2</sup>).<ref name=autogenerated1 /><ref name=autogenerated2 />

==Sites of interest==
{{Unreferenced section|date=March 2023}}
===House of Cable===
The House of Cable was where the first submarine cable between Europe and America arrived, joining the French city of [[Marseille]] with Carúpano, back in the late 19th century. This house is today the headquarters of the Tomas Merle foundation and the Paria Project, two organizations that promote tourism and industry.
