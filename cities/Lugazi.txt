[[File:Nimbus Clouds over the Lugazi Sugarcane Plantation Hills in Lugazi District.jpg|thumb|A car moving towards Lugazi coming from Jinja via the Lugazi sugar plantation]]
'''Lugazi''' is a town in the [[Buikwe District]] of the [[Central Region, Uganda|Central Region]] of [[Uganda]]. The town is also called "Kawolo", and the two names are interchangeably used by the local inhabitants.<ref>[https://ir.kiu.ac.ug/bitstreams/51acb801-87cf-405b-94ae-5399d743364e/download TFIE CREATION OF NEW DISTRICT LOCAL GOVERNMENTS AND SERVICE DELIVERY IN LUGAZI, BUIKWE DISTRICT, UGANDA]</ref>

==Location==
The town is on the [[Kampala-Jinja Highway]], approximately {{convert|46|km|0}} east of [[Kampala]], Uganda's capital and largest city.<ref>{{google maps|

==Points of interest==
The following points of interest lie within or close to the town:
[[File:Uganda 2012 a010.jpg|thumb|Cathedral of Our Lady Queen of Peace]]
* offices of Lugazi Town Council
* headquarters of the [[Roman Catholic Diocese of Lugazi]]
* Lugazi central market
* headquarters of the [[Mehta Group]] in Uganda
* [[Uganda Military Engineering College|University of Military Science and Technology]], which is owned and administered by the [[Uganda People's Defence Force]]
* [[Mount Saint Mary's College Namagunga]], located {{convert|5.5|km|mi}}, by road, west of downtown Lugazi in [[Mukono District]]
* Kawolo Hospital, a 200-bed public hospital administered by the [[Uganda Ministry of Health]]
* Lugazi Golf Course, which is located on the grounds of SCUL
* branch of the [[National Social Security Fund (Uganda)]]
* [[Kampala-Jinja Highway]], passing through the center of town in an east/west configuration

==Population==
The 2002 national census estimated the population of Lugazi at 27,979. In 2010, the Uganda Bureau of Statistics (UBOS) estimated the population at 34,500. In 2011, UBOS estimated the population at 35,500.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ubos.org/onlinefiles/uploads/ubos/pdf%20documents/TP52010.pdf |format=PDF |title=Estimated Population of Lugazi In 2002, 2010 & 2011 |date=2011 |accessdate=21 January 2015 |publisher=[[Uganda Bureau of Statistics]] (UBOS) |last=UBOS |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140707231502/http://www.ubos.org/onlinefiles/uploads/ubos/pdf%20documents/TP52010.pdf |archivedate=7 July 2014 }}</ref> In 2014, the national census put the population at 114,163.<ref name="LGZI">"{{cite web | url=http://citypopulation.de/Uganda-Cities.html | title=The Population of The Regions of the Republic of Uganda And All Cities And Towns of More Than 15,000 Inhabitants | date=27 August 2014 | accessdate=26 February 2015 | last=UBOS | publisher=Citypopulation.de Quoting [[Uganda Bureau of Statistics]] (UBOS)}}</ref>

==Sports==
In 2012, a Little League baseball team from Lugazi qualified for the [[2012 Little League World Series]] in [[Williamsport, Pennsylvania]]. This was the second ever team from Uganda to qualify and the first to make the trip to the USA.<ref>{{cite web |
last=Communications Division | title=Lugazi Little League of Uganda Wins Regional Title, to be First African Team In Little League Baseball World Series | publisher=Little League Online | url=http://www.littleleague.org/Page58970.aspx | accessdate=21 January 2015 | date=16 July 2012}}</ref> The team that qualified in [[2011 Little League World Series|2011]] were denied visas by the State Department, therefore having to concede the [[Little League World Series (Middle East-Africa Region)|MEA]] region title to [[Dhahran|Dhahran, Saudi Arabia]].

Lugazi is also the home of the [[Uganda Revenue Authority SC]], a member of the Uganda Premier Soccer League.
