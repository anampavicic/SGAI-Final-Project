{{Nihongo|'''Hakusan'''|白山市|Hakusan-shi}} is a [[Cities of Japan|city]] located in [[Ishikawa Prefecture]], [[Japan]]. {{As of|2018|01|31}}, the city had an estimated [[population]] of 113,375 in 43246 households, and a [[population density]] of 290 persons per km<sup>2</sup>.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.city.hakusan.lg.jp/kikakusinkoubu/jouhoutoukei/toukei/DATABOOK/toukei.html |title=Official statistics page |access-date=2018-03-04 |archive-date=2019-08-05 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190805101603/http://www.city.hakusan.lg.jp/kikakusinkoubu/jouhoutoukei/toukei/DATABOOK/toukei.html |url-status=dead }}</ref> The total area of the city was {{convert|754.93|sqkm|sqmi}}. It is the second-most populous city in Ishikawa Prefecture after Kanazawa.

==Geography==
Hakusan is located in southwestern Ishikawa Prefecture in the [[Hokuriku region]] of [[Japan]] and is bordered by the [[Sea of Japan]] to the west and [[Fukui Prefecture]] to the south, and [[Gifu Prefecture]] and [[Toyama Prefecture]]s to the east. The southeastern portion of the city is dominated by high mountains. Parts of the city are within the borders of [[Hakusan National Park]].

===Climate===
Hakusan has a [[humid subtropical climate]] (Köppen ''Cfa'') characterized by mild summers and cold winters with heavy snowfall.  The average annual temperature in Hakusan is 14.2&nbsp;°C. The average annual rainfall is 2542&nbsp;mm with September as the wettest month. The temperatures are highest on average in August, at around 26.8&nbsp;°C, and lowest in January, at around 2.9&nbsp;°C.<ref>[https://en.climate-data.org/location/714962/ Hakusan climate data]</ref>

== History ==
The area around Hakusan was part of ancient [[Kaga Province]]. The area became part [[Kaga Domain]] under the [[Edo period]] [[Tokugawa shogunate]].  Following the [[Meiji restoration]], the area was organised into [[Ishikawa District, Ishikawa]]. The town of [[Mattō, Ishikawa|Mattō]] was established with the creation of the modern municipalities system on April 1, 1889. It became a city on October 10, 1970. On February 1, 2005, Mattō merged with the towns of [[Mikawa, Ishikawa|Mikawa]] and [[Tsurugi, Ishikawa|Tsurugi]], and the villages of [[Kawachi, Ishikawa|Kawachi]], [[Oguchi, Ishikawa|Oguchi]], [[Shiramine, Ishikawa|Shiramine]], [[Torigoe, Ishikawa|Torigoe]] and [[Yoshinodani, Ishikawa|Yoshinodani]] (all from Ishikawa District) to form the city of Hakusan.

==Government==
Hakusan has a [[mayor-council]] form of government with a directly elected mayor and a [[unicameral]] city legislature of 21 members.

==Education==
Hakusan has 19 public elementary schools and ten middle schools operated by the city government, and three public high schools operated by the Ishikawa Prefectural Board of Education and one by the city government. There is also one private high school. [[Kinjo College]], a private junior college is also located in Hakusan.
