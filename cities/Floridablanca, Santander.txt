'''Floridablanca''' ({{IPA|es|floˌɾiðaˈβlaŋka}}, locally also simply '''Florida''') is a [[municipality]] in the department of [[Santander Department|Santander]] situated at an altitude of {{convert|925|m|ft}} in the [[Cordillera Oriental (Colombia)|Eastern Ranges]] of the Colombian [[Andes]]. It is part of the metropolitan area of the departmental capital [[Bucaramanga]]. Floridablanca is known for its parks and the Piedra del Sol, a large rock with spirals and circles carved by the [[Guane people|Guane]] over 1,000 years ago.<ref name=VivaFlorida>[http://www.vivatravelguides.com/south-america/colombia/eastern-colombia/bucaramanga/near-bucaramanga/floridablanca/ Floridablanca travel guide], vivatravelguides.com. Accessed 27 November 2022.</ref> This city is home of the tallest statue of Jesus Christ in Colombia, "El Santisimo". The statue is {{convert|40|m|ft}} high and weighs {{convert|40|t|lb}}.<ref name=MinubeStatue>[https://www.minube.com.co/rincon/ecoparque-cerro-del-santisimo-a3671409 Ecoparque Cerro del Santísimo]</ref> The city is situated along the Ruta Nacional 45A, a branch of the [[Pan American Highway]], connecting the capital [[Bogotá]] with [[Santa Marta]] at the Caribbean coast and [[Cúcuta]] as a gateway to [[Venezuela]].<ref name=TiempoAlegria>[http://www.eltiempo.com/archivo/documento/MAM-843801 La ciudad de la alegría] - [[El Tiempo (Colombia)|El Tiempo]]</ref> Together with other neighboring municipalities of Bucaramanga, [[Girón, Santander|Girón]] and [[Piedecuesta]], Floridablanca is one of the growth areas for the capital of Santander with numerous constructions happening in recent years.<ref name=VanguardiaExpansion>[http://www.vanguardia.com/economia/negocios/406450-hacia-donde-va-el-crecimiento-del-area-metropolitana-de-bucaramanga Hacia dónde va el crecimiento de Bucaramanga y el área], ''[[Vanguardia Liberal|Vanguardia]]''. Accessed 27 November 2022.</ref>

== Geography and climate ==
Floridablanca is situated at an altitude of about {{convert|925|m|ft}} in the [[Cordillera Oriental (Colombia)|Eastern Ranges]] of the Colombian [[Andes]], though locally the topography varies greatly. To the east of the city the higher mountains of the Andes rise above the city, thrusted upwards by the north–south-trending [[Bucaramanga-Santa Marta Fault]], one of the major megaregional [[:Template:Tectonism in Colombia|faults of Colombia]]. The area is dominated by the [[Bucaramanga Nest]], the second-most seismically active region in the world.<ref name=SaludNido>[https://www.opinionysalud.com/santander-segundo-nido-sismico-mas-activo-del-mundo/ Santander, el segundo nido sísmico más activo del mundo]</ref> Because of the relatively low altitude, the city enjoys a very favorable climate all year round. Floridablanca's climate is classified as tropical. When compared with winter, the summers have much more rainfall. The climate is classified as [[Tropical savanna climate|Aw]] (tropical savanna climate) by the [[Köppen climate classification|Köppen-Geiger system]]. The average annual temperature is {{convert|23.6|C|F}} in Floridablanca. The rainfall averages {{convert|1055|mm|in}} per year.<ref name=ClimateFlorida>[http://en.climate-data.org/location/34552/ Climate-data.org - Floridablanca]</ref>

== Geology and biology ==
The surrounding areas of urban Floridablanca are composed of the [[Jurassic]] [[Girón Formation|Girón and Jordán formations]], to the east of the Bucaramanga-Santa Marta Fault, running north–south in the east of the municipality, the [[Precambrian]] Bucaramanga Gneiss and Early Jurassic La Corcova Quartzmonzonite crop out and the Ruitoque Massif comprises the [[Tambor Formation]].<ref name=Plancha120>Plancha 120, 2010</ref>

The rural areas surrounding the city are home to the [[endemism|endemic]] [[San Gil climbing salamander]] (''Bolitoglossa nicefori'').<ref name=IUCNBolitoglossaNicefori>[http://maps.iucnredlist.org/map.html?id=59185 ''Bolitoglossa nicefori''] at [[IUCN]] RedList</ref>

== History ==
{{see also|Spanish conquest of the Chibchan Nations#Guane}}
Before the [[Spanish conquest of the Muisca|Spanish conquest]], the area of Floridablanca was populated by the [[Guane people|Guane]]. The Guane were submitted to the rule of the [[Spanish Empire]] by [[list of conquistadors in Colombia|conquistador]] [[Martín Galeano]] when he founded [[Vélez, Santander|Vélez]] in 1539, until then part of the [[Muisca Confederation]]. Modern Floridablanca was founded on 7 November 1817 as "Floridablanca de San Juan Nepomuceno" by Javier Guerra de Mier.<ref name=HistoryFloridablanca>[http://www.floridablanca.gov.co/MiMunicipio/Paginas/Pasado-Presente-y-futuro.aspx History Floridablanca], floridablanca.gov.co. Accessed 27 November 2022.</ref> The church of San Juan Nepomuceno finished construction in 1832.<ref name=ChurchFloridablanca>[https://www.minube.com.co/rincon/iglesia-san-juan--nepomuceno-a3660595 Iglesia San Juan Nepomuceno], minube.com.co. Accessed 27 November 2022.</ref>

== Demography ==
[[File:PoblacionFloridablanca.png|thumb|right|350px|Growth of the city's population from 1964 to 2005]]
According to official [[National Administrative Department of Statistics|DANE]] census, Floridablanca has 262,165 inhabitants, being the second most populated city in Santander. 47,37% are men, while 52,63% are women. 95,6% of the population over 5 years know how to write and read, meaning, illiteracy rate is about 4,4%.

Statistics show that most of the adult people living in the city are from Bucaramanga:
* 7,7% from Floridablanca
* 44% from [[Bucaramanga]]
* 33% from other cities of [[Santander Department|Santander]]
* 16% from other departments

== Economy ==
[[File:Jesus Statue, Floridablanca, Santander, Colombia.jpg|right|250px|thumb|Statue of [[Jesus Christ]] overlooking Floridablanca from the east]]
A study performed in 1998 provided data on the economical activities in Floridablanca, that has grown since those times. Commerce was the most important sector with 39% of the economical make-up, industrial production of clothing was nearly 10% and the food and drinks industry comprised 7% of the economical activities.<ref name=EconomyFloridablanca>[http://www.floridablanca.gov.co/MiMunicipio/Paginas/Economia.aspx Economy of Floridablanca]</ref> The production of sweets, and especially the traditional Colombian dessert of [[oblea]]s, has given Floridablanca the name "the sweet municipality of Colombia".<ref name=DulceFlorida>[https://www.bucaramanga.com/articulo/floridablanca-el-municipio-dulce-de-colombia.html Floridablanca, el 'municipio dulce' de Colombia]</ref>

The central part of the city hosts a concentration of malls; Cañaveral, constructed in 1984,<ref name=CanaveralFlorida>[https://www.minube.com.co/rincon/centro-comercial-canaveral--a3660133 Centro Comercial Cañaveral]</ref> and Parque Caracolí, inaugurated in 2013.<ref name=CaracoliFlorida>[https://www.minube.com.co/rincon/mall-parque-caracoli--boulevar-del-parque-a3660290 Parque Caracolí]</ref>

The city, in recent years grown to connect with Bucaramanga, forms the entry point to the [[Chicamocha Canyon]], located at an hour drive from the city, and hosts touristic activities with a [[paragliding]] school on the ''Mesa de Ruitoque'', the Ruitoque Massif in the southwest of the city.<ref name=ParapenteFlorida>[https://www.minube.com.co/rincon/el-voladero-de-las-aguilas-a55893 El voladero de las águilas]</ref> Local buses to tourist hotspots as [[San Gil]] and the Chicamocha Canyon leave from a transport station called "Papi Quiero Piña" ("Dad, I want pineapple").<ref name=VanguardiaPapi>[http://www.vanguardia.com/area-metropolitana/floridablanca/432472-parador-papi-quiero-pina-fue-encerrado-con-vallas Parador 'Papi Quiero Piña' fue encerrado con vallas] - [[Vanguardia Liberal|Vanguardia]]</ref>

The Jardín Botánico Eloy Valenzuela, a [[botanical garden]] in Floridablanca, was once owned by the El Paragüitas tobacco company.<ref name=VivaFlorida/>

In 2017, the city was designated by the [[Ministry of Commerce, Industry and Tourism (Colombia)|Ministry of Commerce, Industry and Tourism]] as one of five "sustainable touristic destinations" of Colombia.<ref name=VanguardiaTourism>[http://www.vanguardia.com/area-metropolitana/floridablanca/428299-floridablanca-destino-turistico-sostenible-en-santander Floridablanca: destino turístico sostenible en Santander] - [[Vanguardia Liberal|Vanguardia]]</ref>
