'''Vizianagaram''', also known as '''Vijayanagaram''', is a city and the headquarters of the [[Vizianagaram district]] in the [[Indian state]] of [[Andhra Pradesh]].<ref name="map">{{cite web |title=District Census Hand Book : Vizianagaram (Part A) |url=http://www.censusindia.gov.in/2011census/dchb/2812_PART_A_DCHB_VIZIANAGARAM.pdf |website=[[Census of India]] |publisher=Directorate of Census Operations, Andhra Pradesh |access-date=10 June 2019 |pages=11,992–993 |format=PDF |date=2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161114033243/http://www.censusindia.gov.in/2011census/dchb/2812_PART_A_DCHB_VIZIANAGARAM.pdf |archive-date=14 November 2016 |url-status=live }}</ref> It is located in the [[Eastern Ghats]], about {{cvt|24|km}} west of the Bay of Bengal and {{cvt|40|km}} north-northeast of [[Visakhapatnam]]. The city has a population of 228,025 and was established as the capital of the [[Vizianagaram estate]] by Raja Vijayaram Raj from the [[Pusapati]] dynasty. The rulers had a notable role in the history of the region in the 18th century and were patrons of education and arts.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.britannica.com/place/Vizianagaram|title=Vizianagaram {{!}} India|website=Encyclopedia Britannica|language=en|access-date=2019-11-27|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191127195902/https://www.britannica.com/place/Vizianagaram|archive-date=27 November 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

== History ==
[[File:Vijayanagaram.JPG|thumb|[[Coat of arms]] of the Vizianagaram kingdom]]

Excavations at this town revealed copper coins belonging to the remains of 900 [[Before Christ|B.C.]] (Kalinga period).<ref>{{cite web |title=Welcome to Vizianagaram Municipality |url=http://cdma.ap.gov.in/VIZIANAGARAM/ |publisher=Municipal Administration & Urban Development Department, Govt. of Andhra Pradesh |access-date=23 October 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140810142240/http://cdma.ap.gov.in/VIZIANAGARAM/ |archive-date=10 August 2014 |url-status=live}}</ref>

[[Vizianagaram Estate|Vizianagaram Princely State]] was ruled by Pusapati Kings & Gajapati Maharajas. Maharaja Vijayarama Gajapati Raju was a notable King. They are Suryavanshi Kshatriyas. Their ancestors are Udaipur Maharanas. Buddhist and Jain remains were excavated around hills near [[Ramateertham]] village.

== Demographics ==
As of the [[2011 Census of India]], the town had a population of 227,533. The total population constituted 111,596 males and 115,937 females—a sex ratio of 1,039 females per 1,000 males, higher than the national average of 940 per 1,000.<ref name=population>{{cite web|url=http://www.citypopulation.de/php/india-andhrapradesh.php|title=Andhra Pradesh (India): Districts, Cities, Towns and Outgrowth Wards – Population Statistics in Maps and Charts|work=citypopulation.de|access-date=2 October 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160724215047/http://www.citypopulation.de/php/india-andhrapradesh.php|archive-date=24 July 2016|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Sex Ratio|url=http://censusindia.gov.in/2011-prov-results/indiaatglance.html|publisher=The Registrar General & Census Commissioner, India|access-date=2 September 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140922103426/http://censusindia.gov.in/2011-prov-results/indiaatglance.html|archive-date=22 September 2014|url-status=live}}</ref> 20,487 children are in the age group of 0–6 years, of which 10,495 are boys and 9,992 are girls. The average literacy rate stood at 81.85% with 169,461 literates, significantly higher than the national average of 73.00%.<ref name=population /><ref>{{cite web|title=Chapter–3 (Literates and Literacy rate)|url=http://www.censusindia.gov.in/2011census/PCA/PCA_Highlights/pca_highlights_file/India/Chapter-3.pdf|publisher=Registrar General and Census Commissioner of India|access-date=2 September 2014|format=PDF|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131113231419/http://www.censusindia.gov.in/2011census/PCA/PCA_Highlights/pca_highlights_file/India/Chapter-3.pdf|archive-date=13 November 2013|url-status=live}}</ref>

== Governance ==

=== Civic administration ===
The Vizianagaram Municipal Corporation is the civic body of the city, constituted as a [[municipality]] in 1888<ref name="ulb">{{cite web |title=Statistical Information of ULBs and UDAs |url=http://www.dtcp.ap.gov.in/dtcpweb/ulbs/List%20of%20ULBs-27-2-2019.pdf |website=Directorate of Town and Country Planning |publisher=Government of Andhra Pradesh |access-date=10 June 2019 |page=1 |format=PDF |date=27 February 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190717151418/http://dtcp.ap.gov.in/dtcpweb/ulbs/List%20of%20ULBs-27-2-2019.pdf |archive-date=17 July 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> before being upgraded to corporation on 9 December 2015.<ref>{{cite news|title=Masula, Srikakulam, Vizianagaram upgraded into corporations|url=http://www.thehindu.com/news/cities/Vijayawada/masula-srikakulam-vizianagaram-upgraded-into-corporations/article7967915.ece|access-date=10 December 2015|work=The Hindu|date=10 December 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160409032222/http://www.thehindu.com/news/cities/vijayawada/masula-srikakulam-vizianagaram-upgraded-into-corporations/article7967915.ece|archive-date=9 April 2016|location=Vijayawada}}</ref> The jurisdictional area of the corporation is spread over {{convert|29.27|km2|mi2|abbr=on}} with 38 [[Ward (electoral subdivision)|election wards]].<ref name="ulb" /> During 2010–11, the municipality spent {{INRConvert|1887.75|c|abbr=on}} and generated {{INRConvert|2367.51|c|abbr=on}}.<ref name="basic info">{{cite web|title=Basic Information of Municipality|url=http://cdma.ap.gov.in/VIZIANAGARAM/Basic_information_Municipality.html|website=Commissioner & Director of Municipal Administration|publisher=Municipal Administration & Urban Development Department, Govt. of Andhra Pradesh|access-date=3 September 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140810145501/http://cdma.ap.gov.in/VIZIANAGARAM/Basic_information_Municipality.html|archive-date=10 August 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

=== Utility services ===
[[File:SH 26 in Vizianagaram.jpg|thumb|SH-26 towards [[Visakhapatnam]] in Vizianagaram]]
Utility services include a water supply, sewer connections, drainage systems, parks, grounds, community centres, and healthcare. The municipality maintains 458 public taps, 749 bore–wells, {{convert|330|km|mi|abbr=on}} of roads, {{convert|243.25|km|mi|abbr=on}} of drains, 32 parks, 2 playgrounds, 49 elementary and 3 secondary schools and 1 maternity and child health centre.<ref>{{cite web|title=Public services/amenities|url=http://cdma.ap.gov.in/VIZIANAGARAM/Public%20services_amenities.html|website=Commissioner & Director of Municipal Administration|publisher=Municipal Administration & Urban Development Department, Govt. of Andhra Pradesh|access-date=3 September 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141202102522/http://cdma.ap.gov.in/VIZIANAGARAM/Public%20services_amenities.html|archive-date=2 December 2014|url-status=live}}</ref>

== Geography ==
Vizianagaram is located at {{Coord|18.12|N|83.42|E|}}. It has an average elevation of 74&nbsp;metres (242&nbsp;feet). The [[Champavathi River]] flows near the city.

=== Climate ===
Vizianagaram has a [[tropical savanna climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification]] ''Aw'') characterised by high [[humidity]] nearly year-round, with oppressive summers and good seasonal rainfall.<ref>{{Cite web |title=About Vizianagaram {{!}} JNTUK |url=https://jntukucev.ac.in/know-ucev/about-vizianagaram/ |access-date=2023-02-13 |language=en-US}}</ref> The summer season extends from March to May, followed by [[monsoon]] season, which continues to September. October and November constitute the post-monsoon or retreating monsoon season. The normal rainfall of the district for the year is 1,131.0&nbsp;mm, as compared to the actual rainfall of 740.6&nbsp;mm received during 2002–2003. The district gets the benefit of both the southwest and northeast monsoon. The season from December to February generally has fine weather.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Atlas |first=Weather |title=Vizianagaram, India - Climate & Monthly weather forecast |url=https://www.weather-atlas.com/en/india/vizianagaram-climate |access-date=2023-02-13 |website=Weather Atlas |language=en}}</ref>
