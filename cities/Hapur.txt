'''Hapur''' is a city in, as well as the headquarters of, [[Hapur district]], in [[Uttar Pradesh]], India. Located about {{convert|60|km|mi}} east of [[New Delhi]], the city is part of the [[National Capital Region (India)|Delhi National Capital Region (NCR)]]. [[National Highway 9 (India)|National Highway 9]] passes through the city, connecting it to [[Delhi]].

==History==
Hapur is said to have been founded in the tenth century.<ref name=EB11V12Hapur>{{cite EB1911|wstitle=Hapur |volume= 12 |page = 934 }}</ref><ref name=EB14V11Hapur>{{cite encyclopedia |title=Encyclopædia Britannica |edition=14th |volume=11 |page=167 |chapter=Hapur |year=1956 |location=Chicago |publisher=Encyclopædia Britannica, Inc.}}</ref>

Hapur is listed in the ''[[Ain-i-Akbari]]'' as a [[pargana]] under Delhi [[sarkar (administrative division)|sarkar]], producing a revenue of 2,103,589 [[dam (Indian coin)|dam]]s for the imperial treasury and supplying a force of 300 infantry and 4 cavalry.<ref name="Ain-i-Akbari">{{cite book |last=Abu'l-Fazl ibn Mubarak |translator-last=Jarrett |translator-first=Henry Sullivan |title=The Ain-i-Akbari |date=1891 |publisher=Asiatic Society of Bengal |location=Calcutta |page=288 |url=https://archive.org/details/ainiakbarivolum00mubgoog |access-date=21 January 2021}}</ref>

It was granted by [[Daulat Scindia]] to his French general [[Pierre Cuillier-Perron]] at the end of the 18th century. Under the [[British Raj]], Hapur was within [[Meerut District]], was surrounded by several fine groves, and carried on considerable trade in sugar, [[jaggery]] (gur), grain, cotton, timber, bamboo, and brass and steel utensils.<ref name=EB11V12Hapur /> The important [[cavalry]] remount depot and farm of [[Babugarh]] adjoined the town.<ref name=EB14V11Hapur /> Earlier it was within [[Ghaziabad district, Uttar Pradesh|Ghaziabad]] district but in 2012 it became a separate district. It comes within Meerut commissionaire.

==Geography==
Hapur is located at {{Coord|28.72|N|77.78|E|}}.<ref>[http://www.fallingrain.com/world/IN/36/Hapur.html Falling Rain Genomics, Inc – Hapur]</ref> It has an average elevation of 213 metres (699&nbsp;feet) (higher than its neighbours).

==Climate==
{{Main|Climate of Uttar Pradesh}}
Hapur has a [[monsoon]]-influenced [[humid subtropical]] climate characterised by very hot summers and cool winters. Summers last from early April to late June and are extremely hot, with temperatures reaching {{convert|43|C|F}}.<ref name=ch3>{{cite web|title=Chapter 3 – Findings: Metro Cities of India|url=http://www.cpcb.nic.in/EnvironmetalPlanning/ground/Chapter3.pdf|publisher=Central Pollution Control Board|access-date=1 April 2011|pages=63|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150923210838/http://www.cpcb.nic.in/EnvironmetalPlanning/ground/Chapter3.pdf|archive-date=23 September 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> The monsoon arrives in late June and continues until the middle of September. Temperatures drop slightly, with plenty of cloud cover, but with higher humidity. Temperatures rise again in October; and the city then has a mild, dry winter season from late October to the middle of March.<ref name=ch3/>

Rainfall is about 90&nbsp;cm to 100&nbsp;cm per annum, which is suitable for growing crops. Most of the rainfall is received during the monsoon. Humidity varies from 30 to 100%.<ref name=ch3/>

According to the 2011 census, Hapur had a population of 262,983, consisting of 139,525 males and 123,458 females. The literacy rate was 63.40%: 80.66% for males and 66.59% for females. 14.35% of the population was from 0–6 years. Scheduled Castes make up 29.62% of the population.<ref name="districtcensus">{{cite web |year=2011 |title=District Census Handbook: Ghaziabad |url=https://censusindia.gov.in/nada/index.php/catalog/1214/download/3870/DH_2011_0909_PART_A_DCHB_GHAZIABAD.pdf |website=censusindia.gov.in |publisher=[[Registrar General and Census Commissioner of India]]}}</ref>

[[Hinduism]] is the majority religion in Hapur city, with 174,278 (66.27%) followers. Islam is the second-most popular religion in Hapur, with 84,477 (32.12%). Followers of [[Sikhism]] number 2,163 (0.82%), [[Jainism]] 981 (0.37%), Christianity 765 (0.29%), and [[Buddhism]] 162 (0.06%). Approximately 156 (0.06%) stated "No Particular Religion" and 1 (0.00%) stated "Other Religion".<ref name="religion">{{Cite web|date=2011|title=Table C-01 Population by Religion: Uttar Pradesh|url=https://censusindia.gov.in/nada/index.php/catalog/11394/download/14507/DDW09C-01%20MDDS.XLS|website=censusindia.gov.in|publisher=[[Registrar General and Census Commissioner of India]]}}</ref>

==Hapur-Pilkhuwa Development Authority==
Many high-standard educational and research institutes are being established in the Hapur-Pilkhuwa Development Region, e.g. a dental college and research institute, engineering colleges, commerce colleges a [[Delhi Public School Society|Delhi Public School]] branch, and other educational and research centres. Similarly, in housing development, many famous developers like Ansal Housing Group, Eros Group, etc., are investing large amounts of money in constructing housing for the town.<ref name=HPDAProfile>{{cite web |title=A Brief Profile – The Genesis of Hapur Pilkhuwa Development Authority |url=http://hpdaonline.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=7&Itemid=7 |website=Hapur-Pilkhuwa Development Authority |access-date=13 May 2014}}</ref>
