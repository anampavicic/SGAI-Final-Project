'''Youssoufia''' ({{Langx|ar|اليوسفية}}) is the capital of [[Youssoufia Province]] in [[Marrakesh-Safi]], [[Morocco]]. In the [[2014 Moroccan census]] it recorded a population of 67,628,<ref name=census2014/> up from 64,518 ten years ago.<ref>[https://archive.today/20121205090801/http://www.world-gazetteer.com/wg.php?x=1248940887&men=gcis&lng=en&des=wg&geo=-6441&srt=npan&col=adhoq&msz=1500 World Gazetteer]</ref>

It is known for its [[phosphate]] production. The city was founded in 1931 by the French when they began to exploit the phosphate deposits of the Gantour basin.{{Citation needed|date=March 2024}}

==Notable people==
* [[Ismahane Elouafi]],  geneticist,  was born here in 1971.
* [[Samir Sarsare]], a football player.
