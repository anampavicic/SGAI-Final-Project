'''Mallawi''' ({{langx|ar|ملوي}}&nbsp; {{IPA|arz|mæl.ˈlæ.wi|}}; <small>[[Saidi Arabic|Saidi]] pronunciation:</small> {{IPA|arz|ˈmal.la.wi|}}, {{Langx|cop|ⲙⲁⲛⲗⲁⲩ}}) is a [[List of cities and towns in Egypt|city]] in [[Egypt]], located in the governorate of [[Al Minya Governorate|Minya]].

== Name ==
The name of the city is derived from [[Coptic language|Coptic]] and literally means "the place of textile" (ⲙⲁⲛ – "place of", ⲗⲁⲩ – "textile").<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://ccdl.libraries.claremont.edu/cdm/ref/collection/cce/id/1867|title=Toponymy, Coptic :: Claremont Coptic Encyclopedia|website=ccdl.libraries.claremont.edu|access-date=2017-11-22}}</ref>

== Geography ==
The total area of the city is about {{convert|3|acre|m2}}. The southern limit is Allah Mansion (possibly a religious structure?), the northern limit is a television transmitter, the eastern border is the [[Nile]], and the western boundary is Dirotiah Lake.
The city contains many ancient [[Egypt]]ian artifacts.

=== Climate ===
[[Köppen-Geiger climate classification system]] classifies its climate as [[hot desert climate|hot desert]] (BWh), as the rest of [[climate of Egypt|Egypt]].
