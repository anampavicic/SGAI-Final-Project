'''Ribeirão Pires''' (''Pires Stream'') is a city in the Metropolitan Region of the city of [[São Paulo]], in the state of [[São Paulo (state)|São Paulo]], [[Brazil]]. It is part of the "[[ABC Region]]” of [[Greater São Paulo]]. The population is 124,159 (2020 est.) in an area of 99.1&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>.<ref name=ibge>[http://cod.ibge.gov.br/239KA Instituto Brasileiro de Geografia e Estatística]</ref> The elevation is 763 m.

Its neighboring cities are [[Ferraz de Vasconcelos]] to the north, [[Suzano]] to the northeast and east, [[Rio Grande da Serra]] to the southeast and south, [[Santo André, São Paulo|Santo André]] southwest, and [[Mauá]] northwest. The city is served by trains from [[Line 10 (CPTM)|Line 10]] of [[Companhia Paulista de Trens Metropolitanos|CPTM]]. It became its own municipality in 1953, following its emancipation from Santo André.

== Media ==
In telecommunications, the city was served by [[:pt:Companhia_Telefônica_da_Borda_do_Campo|Companhia Telefônica da Borda do Campo]]. In July 1998, this company was acquired by Telefónica, which adopted the [[Vivo (telecommunications company)|Vivo]] brand in 2012. The company is currently an operator of cell phones, fixed lines, internet (fiber optics/4G) and television (satellite and cable).<ref name=":vivo">{{Cite web|url=https://ri.telefonica.com.br/en/the-company/our-history/|title=Our History - Telefônica RI|access-date=2024-06-14|website=Telefônica}}</ref>
