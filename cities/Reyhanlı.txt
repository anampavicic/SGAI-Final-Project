'''Reyhanlı''' ({{IPA|tr|ɾejˈhanɫɯ|pron}}; {{langx|ar|الريحانية}}, ''ar-Rayḥānīyah'') is a municipality and [[Districts of Turkey|district]] of [[Hatay Province]], [[Turkey]].<ref>[https://www.e-icisleri.gov.tr/Anasayfa/MulkiIdariBolumleri.aspx Büyükşehir İlçe Belediyesi], Turkey Civil Administration Departments Inventory. Retrieved 19 September 2023.</ref> Its area is 367&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.harita.gov.tr/uploads/files-folder/il_ilce_alanlari.xlsx|title=İl ve İlçe Yüz ölçümleri|publisher=General Directorate of Mapping|access-date=19 September 2023}}</ref> and its population is 108,092 (2022).<ref name=tuik>{{Cite web |title=Address-based population registration system (ADNKS) results dated 31 December 2022, Favorite Reports|url=https://biruni.tuik.gov.tr/medas/?kn=95&locale=en |access-date=19 September 2023|publisher=[[TÜİK]]|language=en|format=XLS}}</ref> It is near the country's [[Syria–Turkey border|border with Syria]].

== History ==
Formerly known as İrtah ([[Artah]]) and Ar-Rayhaniya, Its southernmost environ, Yenişehir, is thought to be near the ancient village of Imma (or Immae), involved in the [[Battle of Immae]] in 272 and probably also with the so-called [[Battle of Antioch (218)|Battle of Antioch]] of 218.

The [[2013 Reyhanlı car bombings]] were a terrorist attack that involved the explosion of two [[car bomb]]s in Reyhanlı on 11 May 2013. 51 people were killed, and 140 more were injured in the attack.<ref name="Hurriyet-Death toll">{{cite news|url=http://www.hurriyetdailynews.com/explosions-hit-turkish-town-on-border-with-syria-killing-four-and-injuring-18.aspx?pageID=238&nID=46682&NewsCatID=341|title=Death toll rises to 42 as explosions hit Turkish town on border with Syria|work=Hurriyet Daily News|date=11 May 2013|access-date=11 May 2013}}</ref> The car bombs were left outside Reyhanlı's town hall and post office. The first exploded at around 13:45 local time, (10:45 UTC)<ref name="Hurriyet-Death toll"/> and the second exploded about 15 minutes later. People attempting to help those injured in the first explosion were caught in the second blast.<ref name="BBC-Blasts">{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-middle-east-22494128|title=Blasts kill dozens in Turkish town Reyhanli on Syria border|publisher=BBC News|date=11 May 2013|access-date=11 May 2013}}</ref> At that time, the attack was the deadliest single act of terrorism in the [[History of the Republic of Turkey|history of modern Turkey]],<ref name="Istanbulian">{{cite news|url=http://istanbulian.blogspot.com/2013/05/deadliest-terror-attack-in-turkeys.html|title=Deadliest Terror Attack in Turkey's History Might Be Another Attempt to Derail Peace Talks? But Which One? Syria or PKK?|publisher=The Istanbulian|date=11 May 2013|access-date=11 May 2013}}</ref><ref name="Bloomberg news">{{cite news|url=http://www.businessweek.com/news/2013-05-11/explosions-kill-18-in-turkey-near-syria-border-minister-says|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130608095311/http://www.businessweek.com/news/2013-05-11/explosions-kill-18-in-turkey-near-syria-border-minister-says|url-status=dead|archive-date=June 8, 2013|title=Turkey Blames Syria's Assad for Its Deadliest Terror Attack|publisher=Bloomberg News|date=11 May 2013|access-date=11 May 2013}}</ref> only to be surpassed by the 103 victims of the [[2015 Ankara bombings]].<ref>{{Cite news |title=Does Turkey have to learn to live with terror? |newspaper=[[Hürriyet Daily News]] |url=http://www.hurriyetdailynews.com/does-turkey-have-to-learn-to-live-with-terror.aspx?pageID=238&nID=96501&NewsCatID=429 |date=16 March 2016 |access-date=17 March 2016}}</ref>

On 5 July 2019, [[2019 Reyhanlı car bombing|another car bombing]] in the town killed 3 people.<ref name="reuters">{{cite news|author=|title=Erdogan says car explosion in Turkey kills three, may be terrorism-related|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-turkey-security/car-explosion-in-southern-turkey-kills-two-demiroren-news-agency-idUSKCN1U013Z?il=0|access-date=5 July 2019|website=Reuters|date=5 July 2019 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|date=5 July 2019|title=Car bombing left 3 people dead|url=https://www.dailysabah.com/turkey/2019/07/05/car-explodes-in-reyhanli-town-center-in-southern-turkeys-hatay-cause-unknown|access-date=5 July 2019|publisher=[[Daily Sabah]]}}</ref>

== Geography ==
The climate is typical of the Mediterranean region, and Reyhanlı is an agricultural district watered from Reyhanlı reservoir, growing cotton, wheat and other grains and raising cattle, sheep and goats.

The town lies on the main road between [[İskenderun]] and [[Aleppo]] in Syria. There is a [[Bab al-Hawa Border Crossing|border crossing point to Bab al-Hawa]] in Syria at '''Cilvegözü''', {{convert|5|km|0|abbr=off}} south east of Reyhanlı town, which is the busiest land border post between Turkey and Syria.<ref>[http://www.lonelyplanet.com/turkey/transport/getting-there-away#228236 Lonely Planet ''Turkey'']</ref>
