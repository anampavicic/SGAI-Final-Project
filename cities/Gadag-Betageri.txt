'''Gadag-Betageri'''  is a city municipal council in [[Gadag district]] in the state of [[Karnataka]], India. It is the administrative headquarters of [[Gadag District]]. The original city of Gadag and its sister city Betageri (or ''Betgeri'') have a combined city administration. The municipality of Gadag-Betageri has a population of  172,813 and an area of {{cvt|54.0956|km2}}. [[Kanaginahal]] of Gadag is the birthplace of the first co-operative society in Asia.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Nayak |first=N. Dinesh |date=2016-03-18 |title=Reviving the Kanaginahal cooperative society |language=en-IN |work=The Hindu |url=https://www.thehindu.com/news/national/karnataka/reviving-the-kanaginahal-cooperative-society/article8368164.ece |access-date=2022-03-05 |issn=0971-751X}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |author=Vincent D'Souza|date=May 13, 2009 |title=Asia's first co-op bank's future bleak {{!}} Hubballi News - Times of India |url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/hubballi/asias-first-co-op-banks-future-bleak/articleshow/4526313.cms |access-date=2022-03-05 |website=The Times of India |language=en}}</ref> The temples of [[Veeranarayana Temple, Gadag|Veera Narayana]] and [[Trikuteshwara]]<ref>[http://www.nkpost.kar.nic.in/tourismgadag.htm "Gadag"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304040213/http://www.nkpost.kar.nic.in/tourismgadag.htm |date=4 March 2016 }} www.nkpost.kar.nic.in. Retrieved September 9, 2012</ref> are places of religious and historic importance.

==Gadag style of architecture==

The ''Gadag style of Architecture'',<ref>{{cite web
|url= http://www.tifr.res.in/~vahia/SN-search.pdf
|title=In search of Indian records of Supernovae
|access-date=2009-03-12
}}</ref> marked by ''Ornate pillars with intricate sculpture'',<ref>{{cite web
|url= http://www.templenet.com/Karnataka/kalyani_chalukya.html
|title=Kalyani Chalukyan & chanakya temples
|access-date=2009-03-12}}</ref> originated during the period of the [[Western Chalukya]] (or Kalyani Chalukyas) king ''Someswara I'', and it flourished for a period of 150 years (During {{CE|1050}} to {{CE|1200}}) during which period some 50 temples were built; some examples being:
The Trikuteshwara temple complex at Gadag,
The [[Kasivisvesvara temple, Lakkundi]],
The [[Doddabasappa Temple]] at [[Dambal]],
The Amriteshwara temple at [[Annigeri]],
etc.

==Demographics==
{{As of|2011}} India [[census]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.censusindia.net/results/town.php?stad=A&state5=999|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20040616075334/http://www.censusindia.net/results/town.php?stad=A&state5=999|archive-date=2004-06-16|title= Census of India 2001: Data from the 2001 Census, including cities, villages and towns (Provisional)|access-date=2008-11-01|publisher= Census Commission of India}}</ref> Gadag-Betageri had a population of 172,813. Males constitute 86,165 of the population and females 86,648. Gadag-Betageri has an average literacy rate of 85.56%. The Sex ratio is about 1006 females per 1000 males to females. 18,419 of the population is under 6 years of age.

[[Kannada]] is the main and widely spoken language, while English and [[Hindi]] are spoken and understood by few people.

== Notable people ==
{{anchor|Great Personalities of Gadag-Betgeri}}

* [[Kumara Vyasa]] - Gadag immediately brings to mind the name of Naranappa, popularly known as [[Kumara Vyasa]], the [[author]] of [[Karnata Bharata Kathamanjari]]. It is the classic [[Mahabharata]] in [[Kannada]]. Naranappa was born in the nearby village of [[Koliwada, Gadag|Koliwada]]. He composed his work sitting before Lord Veera Narayana, his chosen deity. 
* [[Ganayogi Panchakshara Gawai]], [[visually challenged]] singer from Gadag. His [[music school]] Veereshwara Punyashrama <ref>Parvathi Menon [http://www.frontlineonnet.com/fl2212/stories/20050617000106500.htm "A movement for music"] ''[[Frontline (magazine)|Frontline]]'', frontlineonnet.com. Volume 22, Issue 12, 4–17 June 2005. Retrieved 9 September 2012</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Shri Ganayogi Panchakshara Gawai |url=http://sangeetyogashram.org/panchgawai.shtml |access-date=6 December 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110727234317/http://sangeetyogashram.org/panchgawai.shtml |archive-date=27 July 2011 }}</ref> is a renowned institution in north [[Karnataka]].
* [[Puttaraj Gawai]], recipient of the "[[Padma Bhushan]]" Award from Govt Of India, visually challenged Indian musician  (classical Hindustani tradition), scholar, music teacher and social servant. A student of  Ganayogi Panchakshara Gawai (above). He headed the Veereshwara Punyashrama until he died in late 2010.
* [[Shri Aluru Venkataraya]]
Shri Aluru Venkataraya from Holealur is revered as Karnataka Kulapurohita (High priest of the Kannada family) in the Karnataka region for his contribution towards the cause of a separate Karnataka state. He became famous for undertaking a Karnataka Ekikarana movement in support of the formation of a state for the Kannada-speaking population of Mysore, Bombay Presidency and the Nizam's Hyderabad.
* [[Bhimsen Joshi]], [[Hindustan]]i singer, recipient of the [[Bharat Ratna]] Award - Highest Civilian Honour of India
* [[Sunil Joshi]], cricketer, born in Gadag.
* [[Huilgol Narayana Rao]], writer of "Udayavagali namma cheluva kannada nadu" the [[anthem]] which heralded the birth of [[Karnataka]] state.<ref>{{cite book | title= The Encyclopaedia Of Indian Literature (Volume Two) (Devraj To Jyoti) | url=https://books.google.com/books?id=zB4n3MVozbUC&q=Huilgol+Narayana+Rao |edition=Volume 2 | author=Amaresh Datta| year=2006 | page=1585 | publisher=[[Sahitya Akademi]], [[New Delhi]]| isbn=81-260-1194-7|access-date=9 September 2012}} Note: Google Books link</ref>
* [[Vijay Sankeshwar]], businessman, founder of [[VRL Group]], ''[[Vijaya Karnataka]]'' newspaper and Vijayavani.
* Vilas Nilgund, runner, who represented India in 100 metre sprint, and 4x100 metre.<ref>[http://archive.deccanherald.com/deccanherald/mar212004/sp13.asp "Islamabad-bound athletes named"] [[Deccan Herald]](on-line), 21 March 2004. Retrieved 9 September 2012</ref>
* [[R. S. Mugali]] famous writer and author of "Kannada Sahitya Charitre"
* Giraddi Govindaraj famous critic and former president of "Karnataka Sahitya Akademi"<ref>{{Cite news  |date=11 May 2018 |title=Giraddi Govindraj passes away |language=en-IN |work=The Hindu |url=https://www.thehindu.com/news/national/karnataka/giraddi-govindraj-passes-away/article23858297.ece |access-date=17 July 2022 |issn=0971-751X}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |author=Staff Correspondent |date=2016-01-22 |title=Ambikatanayadatta Award for Giraddi Govindaraj, Venkatesh Murthy |language=en-IN |work=The Hindu |url=https://www.thehindu.com/news/national/karnataka/Ambikatanayadatta-Award-for-Giraddi-Govindaraj-Venkatesh-Murthy/article14013101.ece |access-date=2022-03-05 |issn=0971-751X}}</ref>
* [[G. B. Joshi]] famous playwright and founder of "Manohara Granthamala"
