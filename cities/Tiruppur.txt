'''Tiruppur''' or '''Tirupur''' ({{IPA-ta|t̪ɪɾɨppuːr|}} {{audio|Tirupur.ogg}}), is a city in the [[States and union territories of India|Indian state]] of [[Tamil Nadu]]. It is located on the banks of the [[Noyyal river]] and is the fifth largest [[List of urban agglomerations in Tamil Nadu|urban agglomeration in Tamil Nadu]]. It is the administrative capital of [[Tiruppur district]] and is administered by the [[Tiruppur Municipal Corporation]] which was established in 2008.

The [[Kongu Nadu|region]] was ruled by the [[Chera dynasty|Cheras]] during the [[Sangam period]] between the 1st and the 4th centuries CE. It was under the rule of [[early Pandyas]] [[medieval Cholas]], [[Later Cholas]] and the [[Vijayanagara Empire]] till the 15th century followed by the [[Madurai Nayaks|Nayaks]] who introduced the [[Polygar|Palayakkarar system]]. In the later part of the 18th century, the it came under the [[Kingdom of Mysore]] and later the [[British East India Company|British Raj]] as a part of [[Madras Presidency]]. The region played a prominent role in the [[Poligar War]]s.

Post [[Independence of India|Indian Independence]] in 1947, Tiruppur was part of [[Coimbatore district]]. It was established as the capital of the newly formed Tiruppur district in 2009. Tiruppur tis a part of [[Tiruppur (Lok Sabha constituency)|Tiruppur Lok Sabha constituency]] that elects its [[Member of Parliament, Lok Sabha|member of parliament]]. The town was predominantly an [[agriculture|agricultural]], but with the advent of textile boom in the 20th century and rapid industrialization has seen Tiruppur become a major [[textile]] and [[knitwear]] hub. As of 2021–22, Tiruppur exported garments worth 480 billion USD, contributing to nearly 54% of the all the textile exports from India.

==Etymology==
The name "Tiruppur" might have its origin from the Indian epic [[Mahabharata]] wherein cattle stolen from [[Pandavas]] were returned by [[Arjuna]]. It is a portmanteau of two [[Tamil language|Tamil]] words, "Thiruppu" meaning "to turn" and "oor" meaning "a place" meaning "place where they were returned".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tiruppur.dcourts.gov.in/|title=History of Tiruppur|publisher=[[Government of India]]|access-date=2 June 2023}}</ref>

The region came under the rule of the [[Vijayanagara Empire]] by the 15th century and later [[Polygar|Palayakkarars]], the chieftains of [[Madurai Nayaks]] ruled the region.<ref>{{cite news|title=The land called Kongunadu|url=http://www.hindu.com/mp/2005/11/19/stories/2005111902090200.htm|access-date=9 June 2010|newspaper=[[The Hindu]]|date=19 November 2005|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110528171005/http://www.hindu.com/mp/2005/11/19/stories/2005111902090200.htm|archive-date=28 May 2011|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> In the later part of the 18th century, the region came under the [[Kingdom of Mysore]], following a series of wars with the [[Madurai Nayak Dynasty]]. After the [[Siege of Coimbatore|defeat]] of [[Tipu Sultan]] in the [[Anglo-Mysore Wars]], the [[British East India Company]] annexed the region into the [[Madras Presidency]] in 1799.

Tiruppur was an [[agriculture|agricultural]] town with irrigated farms and the farmers became small owners of various textile related units during the 1970s. The boom in the [[textile]] industry led to an inter woven network of the small scale units leading to growth of the city into a major textile hub.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tiruppur.com/history.htm|title=History of Tiruppur|publisher=tiruppur.com|access-date=6 September 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150903031554/http://www.tiruppur.com/history.htm|archive-date=3 September 2015|url-status=live|df=dmy-all}}</ref> Tiruppur became a [[Tiruppur Municipal Corporation|municipal corporation]] in 2008 and became the headquarters of a separate [[Tiruppur district]] was carved out from parts of [[Coimbatore district]] and [[Erode district]] in 2009.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tiruppur.nic.in/distformation.html|title=Tiruppur district formation|publisher=Government of Tamil Nadu|access-date=6 September 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304040548/http://tiruppur.nic.in/distformation.html|archive-date=4 March 2016|url-status=live|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

==Geography==
Tiruppur is located at {{Coord|11.1075|N|77.3398|E|}} on the banks of the [[Noyyal River]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.fallingrain.com/world/IN/25/Tiruppur.html |title=Falling Rain Genomics, Inc - Tiruppur |publisher=Fallingrain.com |access-date=24 September 2009 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100415153801/http://www.fallingrain.com/world/IN/25/Tiruppur.html |archive-date=15 April 2010 |url-status=live |df=dmy-all }}</ref> It has an average elevation of 295&nbsp;metres (967&nbsp;feet) and covers an area of {{convert|159.6|km2|sqmi|abbr=on}}.<ref name="TC">{{Cite web|url=https://www.tnurbantree.tn.gov.in/tiruppur/|title=Tiruppur Corporation|publisher=[[Government of Tamil Nadu]]|access-date=1 November 2023}}</ref>

==Climate==
The climate in Tiruppur is [[semi-arid climate#Hot semi-arid climates|hot semi-arid]] ([[Köppen climate classification|Köppen]] ''BSh'') with the mean maximum and minimum temperatures varying between {{convert|35|and|22|°C|°F|1}}. The summer occurs during the months March, April and May when the weather is hot and dry. The maximum temperature during the summer months will be around {{convert|35|°C|°F}} and the minimum temperature will be around {{convert|29|°C|°F|1}}. The monsoon months are the months of June, July and August. These months are mainly characterised by mild showers and a reduced temperature. The post monsoon or winter months are September, October, November, December and January. These months generally have a cooler climate with temperatures rarely rise beyond a maximum of around {{convert|29|°C|°F|1}}. The minimum temperature during this season will be around {{convert|24|°C|°F|1}}.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mustseeindia.com/Tirupur-weather|title=Overview|access-date=11 December 2010|publisher=Must see India|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100713195940/http://www.mustseeindia.com/Tirupur-weather|archive-date=13 July 2010|url-status=live|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

Due to the presence of the [[Palghat gap]], the city receives some rainfall from the [[south-west monsoon]] from June to August. After a humid September, the [[north-east monsoon]] brings rains from October which lasts till early December. The average annual rainfall is around {{convert|700|mm|abbr=on}} with the north-east and the south-west monsoons contributing 47% and 28%, respectively, to the total. The soil is predominantly black, which is suitable for cotton cultivation, but Tiruppur district also has some red loamy soil. Tiruppur falls under the Class III/IV [[Earthquake hazard zoning of India|seismic zone]], having experienced a 6.0 [[Richter magnitude scale|Richter scale]] earthquake in 1900.

Tiruppur is administered by [[Tiruppur Municipal Corporation]] headed by a [[Mayor of Tiruppur|mayor]]. The municipality was established in 1917 and was upgraded to a municipal corporation in 2008. The city is divided into 60 wards.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tiruppuronline.in/city-guide/local-bodies-in-tirupur|title=Local bodies in Tirupur|access-date=6 September 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150905210252/http://www.tiruppuronline.in/city-guide/local-bodies-in-tirupur|archive-date=5 September 2015|url-status=live|df=dmy-all}}</ref> Each ward is represented by a councillor who is elected by direct election and the [[mayor of Tiruppur]] is elected by councillors. The executive wing of the corporation is headed by a corporation commissioner and maintains basic services like water supply, sewage and roads. Law and order is maintained by Tiruppur police headed by a police commissioner who is equivalent to the rank of [[Inspector-general of police|IGP]] (Inspector General Of Police). The city police has seven police stations, three traffic police stations and over 1,000 personnel.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/coimbatore/Tirupur-city-to-have-its-own-police-commissioner-from-September-15/articleshow/22355263.cms|title=Tiruppur to have its own police commissioner|newspaper=[[Times of India]]|date=6 September 2013|access-date=6 September 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161003095331/http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/coimbatore/Tirupur-city-to-have-its-own-police-commissioner-from-September-15/articleshow/22355263.cms|archive-date=3 October 2016|url-status=live|df=dmy-all}}</ref> The Tiruppur District Court is the ultimate judicial authority in the district.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ecourts.gov.in/tn/tiruppur|title=Tiruppur courts|publisher=ecourts.gov.in|access-date=6 September 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150909234828/http://ecourts.gov.in/tn/tiruppur|archive-date=9 September 2015|url-status=live|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

Tiruppur has two assembly constituencies [[Tiruppur North (State Assembly Constituency)|Tiruppur North]] and [[Tiruppur South (State Assembly Constituency)|Tiruppur South]]. Tiruppur is part of [[Tirupur (Lok Sabha constituency)|Tiruppur Lok Sabha constituency]] which was created during the delimination in 2008 consisting parts of erstwhile [[Coimbatore (Lok Sabha constituency)|Coimbatore]], [[Gobichettipalayam (Lok Sabha constituency)|Gobichettipalayam]] and [[Palani (Lok Sabha constituency)|Palani]] constituencies.<ref>{{cite web| url = http://archive.eci.gov.in/se2001/background/S22/TN_ACPC.pdf| title = List of Parliamentary and Assembly Constituencies| access-date = 11 October 2008| location = Tamil Nadu| publisher = Election Commission of India| archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20090304010951/http://archive.eci.gov.in/se2001/background/S22/TN_ACPC.pdf| archive-date = 4 March 2009| url-status = dead| df = dmy-all}}</ref>

Tiruppur has three major bus stations: Central (Old bus stand), North (New bus stand) and South (Kovilvazhi bus stand). Tiruppur is connected by moffusil bus services run by [[Tamil Nadu State Transport Corporation|TNSTC]] to all major towns across Tamil Nadu and prominent cities in [[Kerala]], [[Karnataka]] and [[Andhra Pradesh]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tiruppurcorporation.tn.gov.in/sal_bus.htm|title=Bus Stand|publisher=Tiruppur corporation|access-date=12 October 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120425134557/http://tiruppurcorporation.tn.gov.in/sal_bus.htm|archive-date=25 April 2012|url-status=live|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

===Rail===
[[Tiruppur railway station]] is the major rail head serving the city. It falls on the electrified [[Erode Junction railway station|Erode]] - [[Coimbatore Junction railway station|Coimbatore]] [[broad gauge]] ({{Track gauge|5ft6in|lk=on}}) line and is well connected by trains to destinations across India.<ref>{{Cite news| url=http://www.thehindu.com/todays-paper/tp-national/tp-tamilnadu/article534236.ece| website=The Hindu| title=Inadequate infrastructure irks rail passengers in Tirupur| date= 26 July 2010| first=R. Vimal| last=Kumar}}</ref> Also most of the trains stops for only 2 mins which makes the cargo handling not at all possible.<ref>{{Cite news| url=http://www.thehindu.com/news/national/tamil-nadu/participatory-approach-needed-to-improve-facilities-at-tirupur-railway-station-drm/article9199947.ece|title=Participatory approach needed to improve facilities at Tirupur Railway Station: DRM|newspaper=[[The Hindu]]|date=8 October 2016|access-date=27 November 2016}}</ref>

===Air===
The nearest airport is [[Coimbatore International Airport]] (45&nbsp;km) with regular flights to domestic destinations and select international destinations including [[Sharjah (city)|Sharjah]] and [[Singapore]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.thehindu.com/news/cities/Coimbatore/coimbatore-airport-to-get-more-facilities/article4097670.ece|title=Coimbatore airport to get more facilities|date=15 November 2012|newspaper=[[The Hindu]]|access-date=1 July 2013}}</ref>

==Public utility services==
While Tiruppur itself has only a few engineering colleges, but is located in proximity to the major educational hub of Coimbatore. There are 7 government hospitals at the taluk level with a total number of 896 beds and a total of 43 primary health centers<ref>{{Cite web|title=Department of Public Health & Preventive Medicine|url=http://www.tnhealth.org/dph/dphpm.php|website=www.tnhealth.org|publisher=Tamil Nadu Government|access-date=3 May 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150702012435/http://www.tnhealth.org/dph/dphpm.php|archive-date=2 July 2015|url-status=live|df=dmy-all}}</ref> in the rural areas<ref>{{cite web|title=List of Government Hospitals|url=http://tiruppur.tn.nic.in/hospitals.html|website=tiruppur.tn.nic.in|publisher=Tamil Nadu Government|access-date=3 May 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150305004559/http://www.tiruppur.tn.nic.in/hospitals.html|archive-date=5 March 2015|url-status=live|df=dmy-all}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=List of Private Hospitals|url=http://tiruppur.tn.nic.in/HospitalPhoneNo.html|website=tiruppur.tn.nic.in|publisher=Tamil Nadu Government|access-date=3 May 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150617214956/http://tiruppur.tn.nic.in/Hospitalphoneno.html|archive-date=17 June 2015|url-status=live|df=dmy-all}}</ref> Electricity supply is regulated and distributed by [[Tamil Nadu Electricity Board]] (TNEB) as the city along with its suburbs forms Tiruppur Electricity Distribution Circle. A Chief Distribution engineer is stationed at the regional headquarters.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.windpro.org/pdf/MISC/TNEB%20Address.pdf|title=Important Address|year=2011|publisher=Indian Wind Power Association|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121016045943/http://www.windpro.org/pdf/MISC/TNEB%20Address.pdf|archive-date=16 October 2012|url-status=dead|access-date=29 December 2012}}</ref> Water supply and other public utility services are provided by the corporation.

Tiruppur, like any other industrial town, faces its share of environmental pollution complaints.<ref>{{cite web|title=The Judgement Information System|url=http://judis.nic.in/judis_chennai/qrydispfree.aspx?filename=39737|publisher=High Court of Madras|access-date=3 May Tiruppur and its hinterland have faced issues due to the discharge of industrial effluents into Noyyal and other water bodies.<ref name="The Hindu">{{Cite news|title=Effluent discharge into water bodies continues in Tirupur|url=http://www.thehindu.com/news/national/tamil-nadu/effluent-discharge-into-water-bodies-continues-in-tirupur/article7163882.ece|access-date=3 May 2015|work=The Hindu}}</ref> Untreated effluents, mostly containing dyes and chemicals in high concentration are let off clandestinely once again in large quantities through storm water drains into water bodies or into open areas mostly during the early morning hours or during rain.<ref>{{Cite news|title=TNPCB issues notice to CETP after effluent discharge noticed|url=http://www.thehindu.com/todays-paper/tnpcb-issues-notice-to-cetp-after-effluent-discharge-noticed/article7120613.ece|access-date=3 May 2015|work=The Hindu|date=20 April 2015}}</ref> In 2015, the [[Madras High Court]] directed that zero liquid discharge (ZLD) should be strictly followed by the knitwear industries during the effluent treatment process.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Tirupur knitwear cluster asked to abide by court directives on 2015|newspaper=[[The Hindu]]}}</ref> Failure to implement this order, led to a court ordered closure of dyeing units and bleaching units.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Along the banks of Noyyal, farmers in As per a report by CRISIL in February 2011, ensuring Zero Liquid Discharge (ZLD), will affect the operational costs of dyeing and bleaching units, increasing it by 7% to 10%.<ref>{{Cite ==Places of interest==
Old temples in Tiruppur were built during the reign of [[Chola Dynasty|Cholas]] and [[Pandyas]]. Temples in the area include [[Sukreesvarar Temple, Kurakkuthali|Sukreeswarar Temple]], [[Tiruppukkozhiyur|Avinashilingeshwarar temple]], Sivanmalai and Konganagiri.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.hindu.com/2009/10/01/stories/2009100155010500.htm|title=Sukreeswarar Temple|access-date=2 October 2009|location=Chennai, India|date=1 October 2009|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091003011115/http://www.hindu.com/2009/10/01/stories/2009100155010500.htm|archive-date=3 October 2009|url-status=dead|newspaper=[[The Hindu]]|df=dmy-all}}</ref> Other places of interest include [[Thirumoorthy hills|Thirumoorthy Hills]], [[Amaravathi Dam]], [[Nanjarayan Tank Bird Sanctuary]] and [[Tiruppur Kumaran]] memorial.
