{{nihongo|'''Komaki'''|小牧市|Komaki-shi}} is a [[Cities of Japan|city]] located in [[Aichi Prefecture]], Japan. {{As of|2019|10|01}}, the city had an estimated [[population]] of 148,872 in 68,174 households,<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.city.komaki.aichi.jp/admin/profile/jinkou/30/26927.html | title=Komaki City official statistics | language=ja | access-date=2019-10-23 | archive-date=2019-10-23 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191023144548/http://www.city.komaki.aichi.jp/admin/profile/jinkou/30/26927.html | url-status=dead }}</ref> and a [[population density]] of {{convert|2370|PD/km2}}. The total area of the city was {{convert|62.81|km2|lk=on}}. Komaki is commonly associated with the former [[Nagoya Airfield|Komaki Airport]], which is located on the border between Komaki and neighboring [[Kasugai, Aichi|Kasugai]].

==Geography==
[[File:Komaki City Central Library exterior 2021-03-27 ac (1).jpg|thumb|200px|[[Downtown]] of Komaki City]]
Komaki is located in the middle of the [[Nōbi Plain]], west-central Aichi Prefecture, north of the Nagoya metropolis. The city skyline is dominated by [[Mount Komaki]], which is topped with [[Komaki Castle]].

===Climate===
The city has a climate characterized by hot and humid summers, and relatively mild winters ([[Köppen climate classification]] ''Cfa'').  The average annual temperature in Komaki is {{convert|15.7|C}}. The average annual rainfall is {{convert|1769|mm|abbr=on}} with September as the wettest month. The temperatures are highest on average in August, at around {{convert|28.1|C}}, and lowest in January, at around {{convert|4.2|C}}.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://en.climate-data.org/asia/japan/aichi/komaki-4931/ | title = Komaki climate data | language=en}}</ref>

==History==
[[File:Komakishirekishikan.jpg|thumb|200px|left|[[Komakiyama Castle]]]]
[[File:Odanobunaga.jpg|thumb|200px|[[Oda Nobunaga]]]]

===Ancient history===
Archaeological remains from the [[Japanese Paleolithic]] through [[Yayoi period|Yayoi]] period have been found in what is now Komaki, and [[kofun|burial tumuli]] from the [[Kofun period]] are also common.

===Feudal period===
During the [[Sengoku period]], [[Oda Nobunaga]] used Komaki Castle as his headquarters from which he launched his invasion of [[Mino Province]] and later the area surrounding Mount Komaki was the site of the [[Battle of Komaki and Nagakute]] in 1584.

===Early modern period===
It was part of the holdings of [[Owari Domain]] during the [[Edo period]], and prospered as a [[shukuba|post town]] on the route connecting Nagoya with the [[Nakasendō]] highway.

===Late modern period===
During the [[Meiji period]] establishment of the modern municipalities system, the area was organized into villages under [[Higashikasugai District, Aichi]].
Komaki was proclaimed a [[towns of Japan|town]] on July 16, 1906, through the merger of four villages.

===Contemporary history===
Komaki was raised to city status on January 1, 1955, after merging with the village of Kitasato in [[Nishikasugai District, Aichi]].

==Government==
[[File:KomakiCityHall02.jpg|thumb|200px|Komaki City hall]]
Komaki has a [[mayor-council]] form of government with a directly elected mayor and a [[unicameral]] city legislature of 25 members. The city contributes two members to the Aichi Prefectural Assembly.  In terms of national politics, the city is part of [[Aichi 16th district]] of the [[House of Representatives of Japan|lower house]] of the [[Diet of Japan]].

==Economy==
[[File:Downtown Komaki seen from Komakiyama Castle.jpg|thumb|200px|Komaki [[Central business district|CBD]]]]
[[File:Tokadai Chuo Park09.jpg|thumb|200px|Toukadai New Town]]
Komaki has a mixed economy, with agriculture (rice and horticulture), commence, and light manufacturing industries playing important roles.

Komaki's GDP per capita (Nominal) was {{JPY|5859100}} in 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.city.komaki.aichi.jp/material/files/group/7/29nenkan12.pdf |  archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180822081125/http://www.city.komaki.aichi.jp/material/files/group/7/29nenkan12.pdf | archive-date=2018-08-22 | url-status=dead | script-title=ja:市民所得 | trans-title = Citizen Income | df=dmy-all}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.city.komaki.aichi.jp/admin/profile/jinkou/before29/14/12943.html |title=Komaki Population (December 2014) | date=2014-12-01 | df=dmy-all}}</ref>
