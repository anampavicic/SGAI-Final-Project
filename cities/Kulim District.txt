The '''Kulim District''' is a [[Districts of Malaysia|district]] and town in the state of [[Kedah]], [[Malaysia]]. It is located on the southeast of Kedah, bordering [[Penang]]. The town of Kulim, a mere {{Convert|27|km|abbr=on}} east of [[Penang]]'s capital city, [[George Town, Penang|George Town]], also forms part of [[Greater Penang Conurbation|Greater Penang]], Malaysia's second largest conurbation.<ref>{{Cite journal |last=Samat |first=Narimah |last2=Mahamud |first2=Mohd Amirul |last3=Abdul Rashid |first3=Siti Masayu Rosliah |last4=Elhadary |first4=Yasin |last5=Mohd Noor |first5=Norzailawati |year=2019 |title=Urbanisation Beyond Its Core Boundary and Its Impact on the Communities in George Town Conurbation, Malaysia |url=https://doi.org/10.21837/pm.v17i10.627 |journal=Planning Malaysia Journal |publication-date=2019-09-04 |volume=17 |issue=2 |pages=38-49 |doi=10.21837/pm.v17i10.627 |via=MyJurnal |doi-access=free}}</ref>

== Etymology ==
The name of this district is taken after the name of the ''kulim'' tree or its scientific name [[Scorodocarpus|''Scorodocarpus borneensis'' Becc.]] which grew in many places in this district in the past.<ref>{{Cite book |last=Thow |first=Eng Kee |title=Kedah selepas Perang Kulim: Faktor British dan Siam dalam kajian perkembangan sosio-politik Kedah, 1888-1909 |publisher=Nealdy Publisher & Distributor |year=1995 |isbn=9789839996104 |location=Alor Setar |language=ms |trans-title=Kedah after the Kulim War: British and Siamese factors in the study of Kedah's socio-political development, 1888-1909}}</ref>

== History ==
Kulim is believed to have been opened and settled in since the 18th century by 100 [[Pattani Malay]] descent. A Chinese artisan known as Chin Ah Cheoh might have been the founder of Kulim.<ref>Mohd Bakri Jaffar (2007). .''Untukmu Malaysia sempena 50 tahun merdeka : menjejaki warisan kita : himpunan gezet monumen dan bangunan bersejarah.''  Shah Alam, Selangor, Malaysia: Dr. Azmi Morsidi. Page 118. {{ISBN|978-983-43540-0-8}}</ref> Around the mid-19th century, [[tin ore]] was discovered in Kulim. This led to Chinese tin miners starting operations in Kulim. Kulim had around 400 Chinese tin miners in 1890. The largest tin mines in Kulim were in Taman Tunku Putra, Kampung Bukit Besar, Karangan, Terap and Kelang Lama.<ref>[http://www.mpkk.gov.my/background Kulim’s Background] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100408015611/http://www.mpkk.gov.my/background |date=2010-04-08 }}, Laman Web Rasmi Majlis Perbandaran Kulim (Official Website of Kulim Government). January 16, 2010.</ref><ref>[http://www.mpkk.gov.my/web/guest/background Kulim’s City Centre] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101031174004/http://www.mpkk.gov.my/web/guest/background |date=2010-10-31 }}, Kulim’s Municipal council website-Kulim’s Background October 31, 2010.</ref>

==Economy==
Kulim District is currently most notable for its flagship high technology industrial park [[Kulim Hi-Tech Park]].

In March 2019, [[Ministry of Economic Affairs (Malaysia)|Economic Affairs Minister]] [[Mohamed Azmin Ali|Azmin Ali]] announced the [[Government of Malaysia|Federal Government]]'s approval to invest [[Malaysian ringgit|MYR]] 1.6 billion (~USD 380 million) to build the proposed Kulim International Airport.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2019/03/19/putrajaya-announces-rm1.6b-for-kulim-airport-as-part-of-11mp-review/1734200|title=Putrajaya announces RM1.6b for Kulim airport as part of 11MP review {{!}} Malay Mail|last=Tee|first=Kenneth|website=www.malaymail.com|date=19 March 2019 |language=en|access-date=2019-08-28}}</ref> In August 2019, it was announced that the proposed 17&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> airport will have two runways, which will be able to cater to 60 flight movements per hour, or one flight landing/take-off per minute.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2019-08-28 |title=Kulim airport to have two runways |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/08/28/kulim-airport-to-have-two-runways |access-date=2019-08-28 |website=The Star Online |language=en}}</ref> Around July 2023, the Federal Government halted construction for this airport due to lack of need.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Reporters |first=F. M. T. |date=2023-07-01 |title=Govt says no-go for Kulim airport for now |url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2023/07/01/govt-says-no-go-for-kulim-airport-for-now/ |access-date=2024-02-08 |website=Free Malaysia Today (FMT) |language=en-US}}</ref>
