'''Campana''' is a city in the [[Buenos Aires Province|province of Buenos Aires]], Argentina. It is the [[Capital city|seat]] of the [[Campana Partido]]. It is located about {{convert|75|km|mi|0|abbr=on}} from the [[Buenos Aires|Autonomous City of Buenos Aires]], on the right-hand margin of the [[Paraná River]]. Its population is 94,333 inhabitants as per the {{census-ar|2010}}.

Campana and [[Zárate, Buenos Aires|Zárate]] make up an important industrial region. The city is linked to Zárate and the [[Zárate-Brazo Largo Bridge]] (and from there to [[Mesopotamia, Argentina|Mesopotamia]]) by Provincial Route 6. The [[Pan-American Highway]] (Route 9) links Campana to Buenos Aires, [[Rosario, Santa Fe|Rosario]], [[Córdoba, Argentina|Córdoba]] and the north of Argentina.

The village of Campana was officially created in 1875. It was on July 6, 1885 that Campana Partido was created as an offshoot of the Exaltación de la Cruz Partido.

Campana has been since late 19th century and to a lesser degree today an important shipyard and port for passengers traveling to the remote [[Ibicuy Islands]] of the [[Paraná Delta]].

Japanese automobile manufacturer [[Honda]] opened a factory in 2011, where it has built the [[Honda City]] and [[Honda HR-V]].

==Demographics==
Campana has a total of 94,333 inhabitants (INDEC, 2010).<ref>Argentine Census of 2010</ref>

===Population origins===
[[Image:Otamendi02.JPG|thumb|left|Welcome Centre at the Otamendi Natural Reserve, outside Campana.]]
Most of the city's population consists almost entirely by descendants of [[Italian Argentines|Italian]] and [[Spanish Argentine|Spanish]] (mostly [[Galicia (Spain)|Galicians]]) [[Immigration to Argentina|immigrants]], although there are other minorities such as Germans, British, French and [[Romani people|Roms]]. In recent years there has been a slight increase in population from East Asia (China and South Korea) and Latin American countries like [[Bolivia]], [[Paraguay]] and [[Peru]].
