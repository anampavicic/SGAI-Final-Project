'''Piracicaba''' ({{IPA|pt|piɾɐsiˈkabɐ}} or {{IPA|pt|piˌɾasiˈkabɐ|}}) is a city located in the [[Brazil]]ian [[Brazilian state|state]] of [[São Paulo (state)|São Paulo]]. The population is 423,323 (2022) in an area of 1,378.07&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>.<ref name=ibge>{{cite web|url=http://cod.ibge.gov.br/234XG|title=IBGE - Instituto Brasileiro de Geografia e Estat韘tica|work=ibge.gov.br|access-date=4 March 2016}}</ref> It is at an elevation of 547 m above sea level.

==Name==
The place name comes from a word in the [[Tupi language]] that means "place where the fish stops", and it is formed by the junction of the terms ''pirá'' ("fish"), ''syk'' ("stop") and ''aba'' ("place").<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fflch.usp.br/dlcv/tupi/vocabulario.htm |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120531040347/http://www.fflch.usp.br/dlcv/tupi/vocabulario.htm |archive-date=31 May 2012 |url-status=dead |title=Vocabulário tupi-português das lições |author=[[Universidade de São Paulo]] (USP) |access-date=7 June 2012}}</ref>
The name refers to the waterfalls of the [[Piracicaba River]], which bisects the city, which is a point where the "piracema"—fish swimming upstream to reproduce— are stopped.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.urbal.piracicaba.sp.gov.br/caracteriza%E7%E3o/caracteriza%E7%E3o,%20historia%20e%20histsan/piracicabadireito/historiaecultpiracicaba.htm |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110223170415/http://www.urbal.piracicaba.sp.gov.br/caracteriza%E7%E3o/caracteriza%E7%E3o,%20historia%20e%20histsan/piracicabadireito/historiaecultpiracicaba.htm |archive-date=23 February 2011 | url-status=live |title=Piracicaba/BR |author=URB-AL |date=29 August 2006 |access-date=17 February 2011}}</ref>

==History==
{{expand section | sourced details regarding the history between 1877 and 1944, and from 1944 to present | small = no|date=March 2016}}
In 1766, Antonio Correa Barbosa, charged with the task of establishing a settlement on the estuary of Piracicaba river, opted for a location about {{convert|90|km|mi}} from it.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.urbal.piracicaba.sp.gov.br/caracteriza%25E7%25E3o/caracteriza%25E7%25E3o,%2520historia%2520e%2520histsan/piracicabadireito/historiaecultpiracicaba.htm|title=Prefeitura do Município de Piracicaba|last=Tecnologia|first=Imagenet|website=www.urbal.piracicaba.sp.gov.br|language=BR|access-date=2016-03-04}}</ref>  The settlement  was officially founded on August 1, 1767, as a ''povoação'' subordinated to the ''vila of'' [[Itu, São Paulo|Itu]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://cidades.ibge.gov.br/painel/historico.php?lang=&codmun=353870&search=sao-paulo%7Cpiracicaba%7Cinfograficos:-historico|title=IBGE {{!}} Cidades {{!}} São Paulo {{!}} Piracicaba {{!}} Histórico|website=cidades.ibge.gov.br|access-date=2016-03-04}}</ref> In 1784,  Piracicaba got emancipated from Itu, becoming a ''[[freguesia]].''<ref name=":0" />

In 1821, the freguesia was promoted to ''vila'', known as Vila Nova da Constituição. In  1856, Vila Nova da Constituição was promoted to city status and in 1877 it was officially named "Piracicaba", following a law by the then councilman and future Brazilian president [[Prudente de Morais]].''<ref name=":0" /> ''

In 1944, the city was made the seat of the [[Roman Catholic Diocese of Piracicaba]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.diocesedepiracicaba.org.br/capa.asp?p=325|title=Criação da Diocese {{!}} Diocese de Piracicaba|last=Piracicaba|first=Diocese de|website=www.diocesedepiracicaba.org.br|access-date=2016-04-04}}</ref>

==Notable institutions, events, resources==
{{More citations needed|date=April 2019}}
Piracicaba is home to the oldest agricultural university in Brazil, the [[Luiz de Queiroz College of Agriculture, University of São Paulo|Luiz de Queiroz School of Agriculture]] of the [[University of São Paulo]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.en.esalq.usp.br/who-we-are|title=Who we are {{!}} University of São Paulo|website=www.en.esalq.usp.br|access-date=2016-03-04}}</ref> Founded in 1901, the school is located on a farm with a large collection of trees and plants.{{citation needed|date=March 2016}} It is also recognized as a leader in the field of [[precision agriculture]].{{citation needed|date=March 2016}} Other universities based in Piracicaba are the [[Methodist University of Piracicaba]] (UNIMEP)<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://unimep.edu.br/|title=UNIMEP}}</ref> and the  [[University of Campinas]]' School of Dentistry.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.fop.unicamp.br/index.php/pt-br/|title=Portal FOP-Unicamp|website=www.fop.unicamp.br|access-date=2016-10-05}}</ref>

Piracicaba is known by the nickname "Noiva da Colina",<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.aprovincia.com.br/memorial-piracicaba/almanaque/a-noiva-da-colina-e-o-veu/|title=A "Noiva da Colina" e o véu — A Província - Paixão por Piracicaba|website=A Província - Paixão por Piracicaba|date=16 April 2013|language=pt-BR|access-date=2016-03-04}}</ref> meaning "Bride of the Hill", and is the annual host to the "Festa das Nações" (Nations' Party, where people may find foods from all over the world),<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.festadasnacoespiracicaba.com.br/festa+das+nacoes+de+piracicaba+33+anos+de+historia.aspx|title=Festa das Naçoes de Piracicaba:33 anos de história}}</ref> and to the "Salão Internacional do Humor" (International Humor Exhibition), where cartoonists from around the world display comic drawings.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://memoria.ebc.com.br/agenciabrasil/noticia/2013-08-25/criado-durante-ditadura-militar-salao-de-humor-de-piracicaba-completa-40-anos|title=EBC|website=memoria.ebc.com.br|access-date=2016-03-04}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://salaointernacionaldehumor.com.br/historia/|title=Salão Internacional de Humor de Piracicaba {{!}} História|website=salaointernacionaldehumor.com.br|date=8 July 2010|access-date=2016-03-04}}</ref>

Piracicaba is famous for its [[Sugarcane#Cultivation|sugarcane plantation]]s,<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Audi|first=Raul|title=A study on sugarcane cultures in the Piracicaba region by means of photointerpretation: Part II|journal=Bragantia|year=1968|volume=27|issue=2|pages=383–394|doi=10.1590/S0006-87051968000200013|issn=0006-8705|doi-access=free}}</ref> traditional [[cachaça]] production,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www1.folha.uol.com.br/folha/cotidiano/ult95u67509.shtml|title=Piracicaba volta a investir em cachaça}}</ref> and traditional music.<ref>http://www.seer.ufsj.edu.br/index.php/temposgerais/article/download/1439/1080 {{Bare URL inline|date=August 2024}}</ref>

==Economy==
[[File:Casas de alto padrão em Piracicaba SP.jpg|thumb|right|180px|Upscale houses in the city]]
[[File:Avenidas em Piracicaba SP.jpg|thumb|right|180px|View of an avenue in Piracicaba.]]

The city is an important and traditional producer of sugarcane.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.apla.org.br/a-forca-da-cana|publisher=APLA|access-date=August 16, 2023|title=A força da cana}}</ref> The harvest of sugarcane allows for the production of many derivates, including sugar, oil, and [[ethanol]].<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=bxE8AwAAQBAJ|title=Production of Ethanol from Sugarcane in Brazil: From State Intervention to a Free Market|last1=Moraes|first1=Márcia Azanha Ferraz Dias de|last2=Zilberman|first2=David|date=2014-03-31|publisher=Springer Science & Business Media|isbn=9783319031408|page=87|language=en}}</ref> [[Copersucar]], a large company that has a center of research on genetic improvement of sugarcane, participates in this growing industry.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://g1.globo.com/economia/agronegocios/noticia/2011/01/ctc-vira-sa-e-volta-a-ter-copersucar-no-comando.html|title=Centro de Tecnologia Canavieira vira S.A. e Copersucar volta ao comando|website=G1|date=12 January 2011|access-date=2023-08-17}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ruralcentro.com.br/noticias/17010/cerradao-amplia-para-36-o-n%C2%BA-de-socias-da-copersucar/|title=Cerradão amplia para 36 o nº de sócias da Copersucar|website=Rural Centro|date=18 December 2009|access-date=2023-08-17}}</ref>

Another characteristic economic feature is the presence of large industries, the main ones being [[Caterpillar Inc.|Caterpillar]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://memorialacipi.wordpress.com/2015/08/07/caterpillar-no-brasil/|title=A Caterpillar no Brasil|website=MEMORIAL DO EMPREENDEDORISMO|date=7 August 2015|access-date=2016-03-04}}</ref> [[ArcelorMittal]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.camarapiracicaba.sp.gov.br/camara-homenageia-belgo-grupo-arcelor-mittal-3139|title=Câmara homenageia Belgo - Grupo Arcelor Mittal|website=www.camarapiracicaba.sp.gov.br|language=pt-BR|access-date=2016-10-05}}</ref> [[Raízen|Raizen]] and [[Hyundai Motor Company|Hyundai]].<ref>{{cite web| author = AW Staff | date = 2011-02-28 | url=http://www.automotiveworld.com/news/emerging-markets/86061-brazil-hyundai-breaks-ground-on-piracicaba-plant|title=Brazil: Hyundai breaks ground on Piracicaba plant|work=automotiveworld.com|access-date=4 March 2016 |url-access=subscription | quote = [Quoting:] Hyundai has launched construction on its Piracicaba plant in Brazil's Sao Paulo state. ==Geography==
Piracicaba's altitude is 554 meters.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ipplap.com.br/docs/Localizacao%20Relevo%20Extensao%20Territorial.pdf|title=LOCALIZAÇÃO, RELEVO E EXTENSÃO TERRITORIAL DE PIRACICABA|publisher=IPPLAP|access-date=April 4, 2016}}</ref>  Temperatures through the year vary from average highs of 30&nbsp;°C to average lows of 12.0&nbsp;°C,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pt.weatherspark.com/y/30183/Clima-caracter%C3%ADstico-em-Piracicaba-Brasil-durante-o-ano|title=Clima e condições meteorológicas médias em Piracicaba no ano todo|publisher=Weather Spark|access-date=August 8, 2023}}</ref> and Piracicaba experiences an average annual rainfall of 123 centimeters.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.lce.esalq.usp.br/exceldados/MEDIAS.XLS|title=DADOS REFERENTES A: MEDIAS MENSAIS E TOTAL DE CHUVA - PIRACICABA - PERIODO 1917 A 2008|publisher=ESALQ|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090306164756/http://www.lce.esalq.usp.br/exceldados/MEDIAS.XLS |access-date=August 8, 2023|archive-date=2009-03-06 }}</ref> The area of the city is 1,378 square kilometers, making it the 13th largest city in the State of São Paulo.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ibge.gov.br/geociencias/organizacao-do-territorio/estrutura-territorial/15761-areas-dos-municipios.html?=&t=o-que-e|title=Áreas Territoriais|publisher=IBGE|access-date=August 8, 2023}}</ref> The urban area of Piracicaba is 233.36 square kilometers.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ipplap.com.br/site/a-cidade/informacoes-gerais/|title=Informações Gerais|publisher=IPPLAP|access-date=August 8, 2023}}</ref>

Like the cultural variety in Piracicaba, there are several religious manifestations in the city. It is possible to find in the city dozens of different Protestant denominations, as well as the practice of Buddhism, Islamism, Spiritism, and others. In the last decades, Buddhism and Eastern religions have grown in the city. There also are the Jewish, Mormon and Afro-Brazilian religions. According to data from the 2019 Census conducted by the IBGE, the population of Piracicaba was composed that year by Catholics (59.78%), Evangelicals (27.42%), no religious affiliation (7.47%), Spiritist (2.9%) and Jehovah's Witnesses (0.95%). The remaining 2.09% of the resident population declared themselves of other denominations or of undetermined religion.

Source: IBGE - Census 2019

=== Catholic Church ===
The Catholic church in the municipality is part of the [[Roman Catholic Diocese of Piracicaba]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.catholic-hierarchy.org/diocese/dsapa.html|title=São Paulo (Archdiocese) [Catholic-Hierarchy]|access-date=2024-06-22|website=www.catholic-hierarchy.org}}</ref>

=== Protestant Church ===
The most diverse evangelical beliefs are present in the city, mainly [[Pentecostalism|Pentecostal]], including the [[Assembleias de Deus|Assemblies of God in Brazil]] (the largest evangelical church in the country),<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://confradesp.com.br/campos-eclesiasticos/|title=Campos Eclesiásticos|date=2018-12-10|access-date=2024-06-22|website=CONFRADESP|language=Portuguese}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ad.org.br/locations/|title=Arquivos: Locais|access-date=2024-06-22|website=Assembleia de Deus Belém – Sede|language=Portuguese}}</ref> [[Christian Congregation in Brazil]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://congregacaocristanobrasil.org.br/localidade|title=Localidade - Congregação Cristã no Brasil|access-date=2024-06-22|website=congregacaocristanobrasil.org.br|language=Portuguese}}</ref> among others. These denominations are growing more and more throughout Brazil.

==Utilities==
As of 2010, water and power are supplied at a level approaching 100% of the households.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ipplap.com.br/docs/Nivel%20de%20Atendimento%20de%20Abastecimento%20de%20Agua%20(SEADE)%20-%201991,%202000%20e%202010.pdf|title=NÍVEL DE ATENDIMENTO DE ABASTECIMENTO DE ÁGUA NO MUNICÍPIO DE PIRACICABA - 1991, 2000 E 2010|publisher=IPPLAP- Instituto de Pesquisas e Planejamento de Piracicaba|access-date=October 4, 2016}}</ref> As of 2019 there were 2.15 inhabitants per car in the city,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://g1.globo.com/sp/piracicaba-regiao/noticia/2019/09/03/piracicaba-limeira-e-santa-barbara-doeste-tem-indice-elevado-de-carros-por-habitantes.ghtml|title=Piracicaba, Limeira e Santa Bárbara d'Oeste têm índice elevado de carros por habitantes|date=3 September 2019 |publisher=G1|access-date=August 14, 2023}}</ref> and, in 2022, roughly 0.72 inhabitants per mobile phone line, with 99% of the city's population calculated as having access to a mobile phone.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://sampi.net.br/piracicaba/noticias/2776341/cidade/2023/07/piracicaba-tem-mais-numeros-de-celular-do-que-habitantes|title=Piracicaba tem mais números de celular do que habitantes|publisher=JP|access-date=August 14, 2023}}</ref>
