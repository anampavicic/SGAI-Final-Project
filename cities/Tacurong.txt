'''Tacurong''', officially the '''City of Tacurong''' ({{langx|hil|Dakbanwa sang Tacurong}}; [[Maguindanao language|Maguindanaon]]: ''Kuta nu Takurung'', [[Jawi Alphabet|Jawi]]: كوت نو تكروڠ; {{langx|fil|Lungsod ng Tacurong}}), is a 4th class [[Cities of the Philippines#Legal classification|component city]] in the [[Provinces of the Philippines|province]] of [[Sultan Kudarat]], [[Philippines]]. According to the 2020 census, it has a population of 109,319 people.{{PH census|current}}

Tacurong has a total land area of 15,340 hectares, the smallest in land area among the province's 12 municipalities but the most populous. Tacurong became a component city of Sultan Kudarat in 2000 by virtue of Republic Act No. 8805 in September 2000.<ref>[http://www.nscb.gov.ph/factsheet/pdf01/fs1_01.asp NSCB - 2001 Factsheet - 12 New Cities Created], July–December 2000.</ref>

==History==
Tacurong was originally a barangay of the Municipality of [[Buluan, Maguindanao|Buluan]]. On August 3, 1951, it was created into a municipality by Executive Order Number 462 signed by the President [[Elpidio Quirino]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Executive Order No. 462, s. 1951 - Creating the Municipalities of Tacurong and M'lang in the Province of Cotabato |url=http://www.officialgazette.gov.ph/1951/08/03/executive-order-no-462-s-1951/ |website=Official Gazette of the Republic of the Philippines|access-date=May 31, 2018}}</ref> At that time Tacurong had an estimated area of 40,000 hectares and 14 barangays. In 1961, the southern portion of Tacurong was separated to form the municipality of [[Tantangan, South Cotabato|Tantangan]], and in 1973, Tacurong lost some of its eastern portions when the municipality of [[President Quirino, Sultan Kudarat|President Quirino]] was created.<ref name="profile">{{cite web |title=Tacurong City Profile |url=http://sultankudaratprovince.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/Tacurong-City-Profile1.pdf |website=sultankudaratprovince.gov.ph |publisher=Province of Sultan Kudarat |access-date=May 31, 2018}}</ref>

===Cityhood===
{{main|Cities of the Philippines}}
Following the efforts of Angelo O. Montilla, the Congressman of the [[Legislative districts of Sultan Kudarat#Lone District (defunct)|Legislative District of Sultan Kudarat]], Tacurong was made the first component city in the Province of Sultan Kudarat in 2000 by virtue of House Bill No. 6497 and Republic Act 8805. This was confirmed by the “Takurs” in a plebiscite held on September 18, 2000.<ref name="profile"/><ref>{{cite web |title=Republic Act No. 8805 - An Act Converting the Municipality of Tacurong into a Component City of the Province of Sultan Kudarat, To Be Known as the City of Tacurong |url=http://www.chanrobles.com/republicactno8805.html#.Ww_7eoqIrb1 |website=www.chanrobles.com |publisher=Chan Robles Virtual Law Library > Philippine Laws, Statutes & Codes |access-date=May 31, 2018 |language=en |date=August 16, 2000}}</ref>

===Security incidents===

Tacurong City has the most number of bombing incidents in Southern Philippines since 2000,{{citation needed|date=May 2018}} as the city reportedly has the presence of different extortion and terrorist groups like [[Abu Sayyaf]], BIFF and [[New People's Army|NPA]].

;New Year's Eve bombing: December 31, 2017, 11:05 PM, 2 casualties and 17 hurt during the Improvised Explosive Device blast in Villa Garde KTV Bar National Highway, Barangay Buenaflor.<ref>url=https://newsinfo.inquirer.net/956620/news-mindanao-bomb-blast-tacurong-city-sultan-kudarat-new-year</ref>

;April 3, 2017 bombing: Eight persons, mostly workers of an electric cooperative, were injured after an improvised bomb went off along the national highway in Tacurong City on Monday morning (Apr. 3), Bangsamoro Islamic Freedom Fighters (BIFF) is the suspected group who is responsible on the terrorist attack.<ref>{{cite news|last=Fernandez |first=Edwin O. |url=https://newsinfo.inquirer.net/885976/8-hurt-in-tacurong-city-blast/amp |title=8 hurt in Tacurong City blast |newspaper=Philippine Daily Inquirer|access-date=October 24, 2019}}</ref>

;Twin bombing: Seven people were hurt after two explosions rocked Tacurong City on April 17, 2017, the first explosion took place at 6:40&nbsp;p.m. on the rooftop of Dragon Gas Station along National Highway in Barangay Isabela. Minutes later, or at 7:10&nbsp;p.m., another explosion occurred, this time at the gas station's compound.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.gmanetwork.com/news/news/regions/607304/7-hurt-as-twin-blasts-rock-tacurong-city/story/ |title=7 hurt as twin blasts rock Tacurong City &#124; News|work=GMA News|access-date=October 24, 2019}}</ref>

;February 2, 2016 bombing: Improvise explosive device (IED) rocked Tacurong City two hours after the convoy of vice presidential candidate [[Leni Robredo]] left the city on Tuesday afternoon. The bomb exploded around 2 p.m. near the national highway along Jose Abad Santos Street in Tacurong City. Three people were reportedly wounded.<ref>{{cite news|author=Karlo Paolo R. Pates |url=http://www.sunstar.com.ph/davao/local-news/2016/02/03/bomb-explodes-tacurong-city-455202 |title=Bomb explodes in Tacurong City |work=SunStar|date=February 2, 2016 |access-date=October 24, 2019}}</ref>

;Yellow bus bombing attempt: The powerful bomb fashioned from two 60&nbsp;mm mortars as main charge and mobile phone as trigger mechanism was found at 10:50&nbsp;a.m. April 2015 inside Yellow Bus Line unit with body number 9208 at a police checkpoint in Barangay Dos along the Tacurong-Isulan highway. The bus was heading toward Isulan from Tacurong City when subjected to inspection by police after Army and policemen received a report about the suspected bomb inside the bus.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mindanaodailynews.com/army-pnp-foil-bus-bombing-in-tacurong-city/ |title=Mindanao Daily News – Army, PNP foil bus bombing in Tacurong City |website=www.mindanaodailynews.com |access-date=January 17, 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180102012746/http://www.mindanaodailynews.com/army-pnp-foil-bus-bombing-in-tacurong-city/ |archive-date=January 2, 2018 |url-status=dead}}</ref>

;November 6, 2022 bombing: A homemade bomb went off in a bus as it was approaching a transport terminal in Tacurong City. The explosion killed a passenger and wounded 10 others.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://apnews.com/article/bombings-philippines-d1a07bde948a9401b1b8a1a9614ab8ff |title=Bus bomb kills 1, wounds 10 others in southern Philippines |publisher=AP|date=November 6, 2022 |access-date=November 8, 2022}}</ref>

==Geography==
Tacurong is in South Central Mindanao and the only city in the province of Sultan Kudarat. It is {{convert|92|km}} from General Santos, {{convert|96|km}} from Cotabato City and {{convert|178|km}} from Davao City. It is situated at the crossroads of Davao-GenSan-Cotabato highways.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tacurong.gov.ph/history/ |title=Profile &#124; Tacurong City |publisher=Tacurong.gov.ph |access-date=October 24, 2019}}</ref>

==Economy==
{{PH poverty incidence}}
Tacurong City is the commercial hub of the province of Sultan Kudarat, catering the needs of nearby municipalities and the province of Maguindanao.

===Health care facilities===

Tacurong serves as the premier Health Care Center in the province of Sultan Kudarat and nearby provinces.

St. Louis Hospital is a modern tertiary hospital in the city, equipped with modern facilities such as 16-Slices CT Scan and the first and only Gamma Camera in Region 12.

Other notable facilities include:
*Tomboc - Salayog Hospital
*Quijano Clinic and Hospital
*Sandig Medical Clinic and Hospital
*Sultan Kudarat Doctors Hospital
*Medical Mission Group Hospital and Health Services Cooperative
*Lu Clinic and Hospital

==Education==
Tacurong City is also a center of education in Sultan Kudarat. Learners and Student from neighboring towns and municipalities, even from outside the Sultan Kudarat like [[Tantangan]] in [[South Cotabato]] and the municipalities in [[Maguindanao]] will go in the city to attend school.
