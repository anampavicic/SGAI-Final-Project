'''Maran''' is a town in [[Maran District]], [[Pahang]], [[Malaysia]]. [[Bandar Tun Razak, Jengka|Bandar Tun Abdul Razak, Jengka]] is located in outskirt of the town. Surrounded by isolated forest and oil palm plantations mainly in the area of [[FELDA]] Jengka, Maran town is roughly halfway between [[Temerloh]] and [[Kuantan]].

==Economy==
[[File:Maran Market (Pasar Besar Maran).jpg|thumb|The main market of Maran Town.]]
Main economy activities in Maran is plantation mainly by the largest settlement of [[FELDA]] in Malaysia, the [[Jengka]] Settlement. Other economy activities are focuses on small enterprises in agriculture, farming and fishing.

Maran is also famous among Hindus for the [[Sri Marathandavar Bala Dhandayuthapani Alayam]] temple, where devotees from all over the country and even Singapore come here to perform their rituals and prayers.

==Transportation==
[[File:Maran District Council Building.jpg|thumb|The building of Maran District Council.]]
===Public transport===
Maran, as with Kuantan and most of eastern Pahang, is not served by any rail line. [[Rapid Kuantan|rapidKuantan]] buses also do not cover Maran currently. Instead, there are ''Cityliner'' buses that connect Maran to Kuantan and [[Jerantut]].

===Car===
In contrast, going to Maran is much easier by car. The [[East Coast Expressway]] has two exits serving Maran constituency: Exit 825 to Maran and Exit 827 to [[Sri Jaya]].

The [[Malaysia Federal Route 2|old Kuala Lumpur-Kuantan federal road Federal Route 2]] runs through downtown Maran. [[Malaysia Federal Route 64|Federal Route 64]] connects Maran to [[Jerantut]]. Connection to [[Pekan District|Pekan]], the royal capital of Pahang is also possible through a series of [[Malaysian State Roads system|state-level roads]] and then [[Malaysia Federal Route 82|Federal Route 82]].

==Politics==
[[File:Maran Resort at Taman Tasik Maran.jpg|thumb|Maran Resort at Taman Tasik Maran.]]
Maran in turn is divided into three state seats in the [[Pahang State Legislative Assembly]]:
* Chenor
* Kuala Sentul
* Luit
in which all three are also currently held by UMNO.
Another state constituency, [[Jengka]], is under the administration of the Maran District Council but is represented under [[Kuala Krau (federal constituency)|Kuala Krau]] parliamentary constituency.
