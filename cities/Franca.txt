'''Franca''' is a municipality in the [[São Paulo (state)|state of São Paulo]], Brazil. The city is located in the northeastern portion of the state, distant 401 km (249 mi) from the state capital ([[São Paulo]]), and 676 km (420 mi) from [[Brasília|Brasilia]]. It covers a total area of 605,679 km<sup>2</sup> of which 86,92 km<sup>2</sup> comprises the urban area.<ref>{{Cite web |title=ibge-panorama |url=https://cidades.ibge.gov.br/brasil/sp/franca/panorama}}</ref> As of the 2021 Census, the city's population was 358,539. 

In Brazil, the city is known as <nowiki>''The National Capital of Footwear'', and ''The National Capital of Basketball''</nowiki>.

The city is the industrial and economic center of an urban agglomerate comprising 19 municipalities with a total of 672.053 inhabitants, being consecutively among the top Brazilian cities with the best urban sanitation.<ref>{{Cite web |title=IBGE atualiza a listagem dos municípios que integram os recortes territoriais brasileiros {{!}} IBGE |url=https://censos.ibge.gov.br/2013-agencia-de-noticias/releases/24445-ibge-atualiza-a-listagem-dos-municipios-que-integram-os-recortes-territoriais-brasileiros-6.html |access-date=2022-09-19 |website=censos.ibge.gov.br |language=pt-BR |archive-date=2022-09-20 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220920173107/https://censos.ibge.gov.br/2013-agencia-de-noticias/releases/24445-ibge-atualiza-a-listagem-dos-municipios-que-integram-os-recortes-territoriais-brasileiros-6.html |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Franca drops to 5th position of the best urban sanitation in Brazil, The city of Santos is leading. |url=https://gcn.net.br/noticias/430436/franca/1969/12/franca-cai-para-5-melhor-saneamento-basico-do-pais-santos-lidera }}{{Dead link|date=April 2024 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> 

The parish of Franca was founded in 1805, being part of [[Mogi Mirim]] until 1824. The city status was received in 1856.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2015-12-12 |title=Memorial: Veja lembranças de Mogi nas décadas de 50 e 60 |url=https://opopularmm.com.br/memorial-veja-lembrancas-de-mogi-mirim-nas-decadas-de-50-e-60-16664 |access-date=2022-09-18 |website=opopularmm.com.br |language=pt-BR |archive-date=2022-09-20 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220920171822/https://opopularmm.com.br/memorial-veja-lembrancas-de-mogi-mirim-nas-decadas-de-50-e-60-16664 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==History==

The history of Franca begins with the [[Bandeirantes|bandeiras]] of [[Bartolomeu Bueno da Silva]] (The Anhanguera) in the 18th century. 

During the opening of the route known as "Caminho de Goiás" (route to Goiás), new settlements were established along the route to serve as resting / stopping points for the entourages travelling along the way from São Paulo to [[Goiás]], in search of gold.<ref>{{Cite web |title=aspectgeo |url=http://educar.sc.usp.br/biologia/prociencias/cp/Franca/aspectgeo.htm |access-date=2022-09-18 |archive-date=2012-01-18 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120118114332/http://educar.sc.usp.br/biologia/prociencias/cp/Franca/aspectgeo.htm |url-status=dead }}</ref> 

The settlement where the city is now located was formerly known as ''Pouso dos Bagres'' (stop of the catfishes).

In 1819, Franca was visited by French naturalist [[Augustin Saint-Hilaire|''Auguste de Saint-Hilaire'']] who dedicated the following message:

''"The village of Franca, where I landed, is pleasantly in the midst of vast pastures, in an uncovered region, sown by bushes and cut by deep valleys. This charming village occupies the center of a path of land, wide and rounded, on each side. bathed by a small stream. At the time of my voyage, there were only about fifty houses there, but the sites for the construction of a large number of them were already marked, and it was easy to see that Franca would not take long to acquire greatness.<ref>{{Cite web |title=ceatec_ppgurb_dr_Dirceu_PJ |url=https://repositorio.sis.puc-campinas.edu.br/bitstream/handle/123456789/16106/ceatec_ppgurb_dr_Dirceu_PJ.pdf?sequence=1&isAllowed=y}}</ref>''

The parish of Franca was founded on December 03, 1805, being part of the territory of ''Vila de Mogi Mirim'' until 1824, when it was  emancipated by [[John VI of Portugal|João VI]] and named ''Vila Franca do Imperador'' in honor of the [[Pedro I of Brazil|''Emperor Pedro I of Brazil'']]. It received the status of city on April 24, 1856.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Lei n° 21, de 24/04/1856 |url=https://www.al.sp.gov.br/norma/139894 |access-date=2022-09-18 |website=www.al.sp.gov.br |language=pt-br}}</ref>

Around 1890, Franca was served by the ''Estrada de Ferro Mogiana'', however, the railroad branch was deactivated after the construction of a new branch connecting [[Ribeirão Preto]] to [[Uberaba]].

Due to the expansion of coffee production between the XIX and XX centuries, many italian immigrants settled in Franca, and the first shoe factory emerged in the late 1920s.<ref>{{Cite web |title=coffee, immigrants and enterprises in the northeast of são paulo |url=https://www.hehe.org.br/index.php/rabphe/article/view/239/311}}</ref>

Franca took part in the [[Constitutionalist Revolution|''Constitutionalist Revolution of 1932'']], losing six residents who fought to death for São Paulo.<ref>{{Cite web |title=700 francanos são homenageados nesta 4ª |url=https://gcn.net.br/noticias/256672/franca/2014/07/700-francanos-sao-homenageados-nesta-4 |access-date=2022-09-18 |website=GCN |language=pt-br}}</ref>

== Economy ==
Franca is the largest footwear producer in Brazil and Latin America, housing thousands of medium and large industries, including the production of footwear components. The city houses reputed design centers intended for training new professionals to work in Brazil and abroad.{{cn|date=March 2023}}

Franca is also traditionally connected to agriculture. It is located in the area of ''Alta Mogiana'', one of the best coffee producing territories in the world, due to the abundance of purple soil, and favorable climate for cultivation.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Alta Mogiana:Coffee |url=http://www.amsc.com.br/noticias/003.htm |access-date=2022-09-18 |archive-date=2009-02-05 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090205050252/http://www.amsc.com.br/noticias/003.htm |url-status=dead }}</ref>

The city houses a diversified industrial park, the production of metal, furniture, food, and beverage is very active. The industry of jewelry and diamond has been active for many years in Franca, placing the city as one of the largest diamond cutting centers in Brazil.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Diamantes e Franca |url=https://gcn.net.br/noticias/46744/franca/2009/08/diamantes-e-franca |access-date=2022-09-18 |archive-date=2022-09-21 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220921210508/https://gcn.net.br/noticias/46744/franca/2009/08/diamantes-e-franca |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Geography==
[[File:Região montanhosa próximo a Franca , SP, Brazil 12012019.jpg|thumb|Franca is located on a plateau where ''[[Serra da Canastra National Park]]'' can be sighted.]]
Franca is located in the northeastern region of the state of São Paulo, the fifth highest municipality in the state with an altitude of 1,040&nbsp;m above the sea level. The territory of Franca is covered by sandy soils dominated by the sandstones of Bauru, and Botucatu.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Geografiaagricola/50 |url=http://observatoriogeograficoamericalatina.org.mx/egal15/Geografiasocioeconomica/Geografiaagricola/50.pdf}}</ref> Vegetation is dominated by grasses, and the forests are restricted to mountainous slopes. 

The basin of ''Rio Canoas'' (Canoas River) provide water for the city and the surrounding area.

The municipality of Franca borders [[Batatais]], [[Cristais Paulista]], [[Patrocínio Paulista]], [[Ibiraci]] and [[Claraval]].

===Climate===

Franca has a [[tropical savanna climate]] (Aw), milder due to the elevation, with dry winters, rainy summers and moderate temperatures throughout the year. It was [[Humid subtropical climate|humid subtropical]] (''Cwa'') before the current climatic table (1961-1990 period). Occurrence of rain is high and it is one of the rainiest cities in the State of São Paulo.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Volume de Chuvas – Franca |url=https://g1.globo.com/sp/ribeirao-preto-franca/noticia/2020/01/29/franca-sp-registra-maior-volume-de-chuvas-em-janeiro-no-estado-de-sp-aponta-instituto.ghtml}}</ref>

The lowest recorded temperature was 0 °C on July 5, 1953, and the highest temperature was 37.8 °C on October 15, 2014.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Normais Climatológicos do Brasil |url=https://accounts.google.com/v3/signin/identifier?dsh=S-1140687269%3A1663519955854797&continue=https%3A%2F%2Fdrive.google.com%2Ffile%2Fd%2F0BwM-LY650N0NRjNkR2V6YXF5bU0%2Fview&followup=https%3A%2F%2Fdrive.google.com%2Ffile%2Fd%2F0BwM-LY650N0NRjNkR2V6YXF5bU0%2Fview&osid=1&passive=1209600&service=wise&flowName=WebLiteSignIn&flowEntry=ServiceLogin&ifkv=AQDHYWqVBB7LqPI0Twg9U1LzpdShQfg29Ui6aiTlhf8Ur1YEx2e9E8c_t8OiN3PNexf94B6jpAxNhg |access-date=2022-09-18 |website=accounts.google.com}}</ref>

The highest recorded rainfall reached 146 millimeters on March 28, 1931, and recently, 133.7 millimeters was recorded on November 21, 2018.
