'''Abha''' ({{langx|ar|أَبْهَا}}, ''{{transl|ar|DIN|ʾAbhā}}'') is the capital of [['Asir Region]] in Saudi Arabia. It is situated {{convert|2,270|m|ft|abbr=off}} above sea level in the fertile [[Asir Mountains]] of south-western Saudi Arabia, near [[Asir National Park]]. Abha's mild climate makes it a popular [[tourist destination]] for Saudis. Saudis also call the city the Bride of Mountain due to its position above the sea.

==History==
[[File:Map of Arabia 600 AD.svg|thumb|left|Map of [[Arabia]] in 600 AD]]

Abha was the capital city for the Prince of Asir Ibn Ayde under the authority of the [[Ottoman Empire]] until [[World War I]].<ref name=EB/> In 1918, the Prince of Asir, Yahya bin Hasun Al Ayde, grandson of Ibn Ayed, returned to his family throne conquered in Abha with complete independence. In 1920, Asir was conquered by the [[Ikhwan]] tribesmen of Nejd loyal to [[Ibn Saud]] during the [[Unification of Saudi Arabia]]. Abha has many historic places such as forts and other locations, thanks to the region's cultural heritage. Bani Shehr, Bani Amr, Bal-Ahmar, Bal-Asmar, Bal-Qarn, Shumran and some others all belong to "Al-Azd" and some extended families [[Qahtan]], Shahran which is belong to Hood. [[Azd]]i tribes had migrated after "Marib Dam" collapsed for the third time in the third century AD. Al-Namas, Billasmar Region, Hawra Billasmar Center, Khaled, Eyaa Valley, Athneen Billasmar (which is the capital), Subuh Billahmar, Al-Nimas, Tanomah, Al-Majaredah place, Bal-Qarn Center, and Sabt Alalyaa place (Bishah, Wadi Bin Hashbaal) and some other known places that belong to the Asir Region.{{citation needed|date=December 2019}}

In 2015, a group of terrorists attacked a big mosque in Abha. Some of the people who were killed were police officers.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.abc.net.au/news/2015-08-06/17-killed-in-bomb-attack-on-saudi-police-at-mosque-state-tv-says/6679144|title=15 killed in bomb attack on Saudi police at mosque: Interior ministry|publisher=ABC News|date=6 August 2015}}</ref>

==Transportation==
A coastal road connecting [[Jeddah]] and Abha was completed in 1979.<ref name=EB/> A [[Saudi Public Transport Company|SAPTCO]] bus station connects Abha to other destinations within Saudi Arabia.

[[Abha International Airport]] ({{langx|ar|مَطَار أَبْهَا|Maṭār Abhā}}, IATA: AHB[3], ICAO: OEAB) is Abha's main airport. International connections are available to Yemen (Sana'a), Egypt (Cairo), Qatar (Doha) and the UAE (Dubai, Sharjah), as well as to other destinations within Saudi Arabia. Construction of Dutch-designed Abha Airport began in mid-1975, and flights began in 1977. Before construction of the airport, domestic flights were serviced by the military airport near [[Khamis Mushait]] by Ali Misfer Ibn Misfer, who was the founder of aviation in Abha in 1945.{{citation needed|date=December 2014}}

==Landmarks==
[[File:Art Street of Abha, Saudi Arabia 2024.jpg|thumb|Art Street of Abha, which was popular for its iconic [[jacaranda]] trees.]]
The city of Abha is composed of four quarters, the largest of which contains a fortress. Hilltop fortresses are a characteristic feature of the city.<ref name=EB/> [[Shadda Palace]], built in 1927, is now a museum displaying local handicrafts and household items. Other notable buildings in Abha include the Abha Great Mosque, the Al-Tahy restaurant,<ref name="WalkerButler2010">{{cite book|last1=Walker|first1=Jenny|last2=Butler|first2=Stuart|title=Oman, UAE & Arabian Peninsula|url=https://archive.org/details/isbn_9781741791457|url-access=registration|access-date=27 August 2012|date=1 October 2010|publisher=Lonely Planet|isbn=978-1-74179-145-7|page=[https://archive.org/details/isbn_9781741791457/page/336 336]}}</ref> the Abha Palace Hotel, and the funpark next to Lake Sadd. The New Abha five-star hotel is a recent construction on the lake, {{convert|1.5|km|mile|abbr=on}} southwest of the town.<ref name="HamShams2004">{{cite book|last1=Ham|first1=Anthony|last2=Shams|first2=Martha Brekhus|last3=Madden|first3=Andrew|title=Saudi Arabia|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=PddTr1X7hEgC&pg=PA129|access-date=27 August 2012|date=15 September 2004|publisher=Lonely Planet|isbn=978-1-74059-667-1|page=129}}</ref> The head office and the main printing presses of ''[[Al Watan (Saudi Arabia)|Al Watan]]'', a major Saudi daily, are in Abha.

==Culture==
The Saudi government has promoted Abha as a tourist destination. The city hosts events to attract visitors to the city and its surroundings, including the summer Abha Festival, sporting events, shows, exhibitions, and musical performances poets and singers. The artist [[Talal Maddah]] died on stage during one such performance in Abha.<ref>{{cite book|last=Buchele|first=Nicholas|title=Saudi Arabia Culture Smart|year=2009|publisher=Kuperard}}</ref>

==Sport==
[[Abha Club]] is the city's professional [[association football|football]] club, currently playing in the [[Saudi Professional League]], the highest tier of Saudi football.<ref>{{cite web |title=نبذه عن النادي |url=http://abha-club.net/?page_id=3248}}</ref> Their home stadium is the 25 000 capacity [[Prince Sultan bin Abdul Aziz Stadium]].

==Education==
[[King Khalid University]] is a public university in Abha. The University was established in 1998 by merging the Imam Muhammed bin Saud University of Islamic Studies and the King Saud University of the South.{{citation needed|date=December 2018}} The total number of enrolled students is approximately 85,000.

==Geography and climate==
[[File:Abha, Saudi Arabia.jpg|thumb|300px|Abha sky]]

Abha is located in the southern region of [[Asir]] at an elevation of about {{convert|2270|m|ft|abbr=off}} above sea level.{{citation needed|date=December 2014}} Abha lies on the western edge of [[Hijaz mountains|Mount Al-Hijaz]],<ref name=EB>{{cite encyclopedia|editor-first=Dale H.|editor-last=Hoiberg|encyclopedia=Encyclopædia Britannica|title=Abhā|edition=15th|year=2010|publisher=Encyclopædia Britannica Inc.|volume=I: A-ak Bayes|location=Chicago, Illinois|isbn=978-1-59339-837-8|page=[https://archive.org/details/newencyclopaedia2009ency/page/30 30]|url-access=registration|url=https://archive.org/details/newencyclopaedia2009ency/page/30}}</ref> near [[Jabal Sawda]], the highest peak in Saudi Arabia. Treating the Asir Mountains as part of the Sarawat, the landscape is otherwise dominated by the [[Sarawat Mountains]].<ref name="Burrows2010">{{cite book |author=Robert D. Burrowes |title=Historical Dictionary of Yemen |pages=5–340 |publisher=[[Rowman & Littlefield]] |year=2010 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=tjXRfqBv_0UC |isbn=978-0-8108-5528-1}}</ref>

Abha has a [[desert climate|hot desert climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification|Köppen]]: [[semi-arid climate|BWh]]) that is influenced by city's high elevation. The city's weather is generally mild throughout the year, becoming noticeably cooler during the "low-sun" season. Abha seldom sees temperatures rise above {{convert|35|C|1}} during the course of the year. The city averages {{convert|278|mm|in|0}} of rainfall annually, with the bulk of the precipitation occurring between February and April, with a secondary minor wet season in July and August associated with the northward movement of the [[Intertropical Convergence Zone]]. Abha gets about 11 percent of its irrigation water from [[rainwater harvesting]].<ref name="Mays2009">{{cite book|last=Mays|first=Larry|title=Integrated Urban Water Management: Arid and Semi-Arid Regions: UNESCO-IHP|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=BuSO55RvXuQC&pg=PA46|access-date=27 August 2012|date=10 April 2009|publisher=CRC Press|isbn=978-0-415-45348-6|page=46}}</ref> 

The highest recorded temperature was {{convert|40|C}} on August 25, 1983, while the lowest recorded temperature was {{convert|-2|C}} on December 29, 1983.<ref name="Voodoo Skies">{{cite web|title=Abha, Saudi Arabia|url=http://voodooskies.com/weather/saudi-arabia/abha|publisher=Voodoo Skies|access-date=24 August 2013}}</ref><ref name="Saudi Business">{{cite web|title=Abha guide|url=https://saudibusiness.directory/location/%D8%A7%D8%A8%D9%87%D8%A7/|publisher=Saudi Business|access-date=24 August 2013}}</ref>
