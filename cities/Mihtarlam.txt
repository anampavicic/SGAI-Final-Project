'''Mihtarlam''' ({{langx|fa|مهترلام}}, {{langx|ps|مهترلام}}), also spelled '''Mehtar Lam''', is the [[List of cities in Afghanistan|sixteenth-largest city]] of [[Afghanistan]]. It is the capital of [[Laghman Province]] and center of [[Mihtarlam District]]. It is the only large urban settlement in the province. The town is situated in the valley formed by the [[Alishang]] and [[Alingar River|Alingar]] rivers, 47 km northwest of the city of [[Jalalabad]]. There is a paved road between the cities that takes approximately one hour to travel by car.

On 13 August 2021, Mihtarlam was seized by [[Taliban]] fighters, becoming the twenty-third provincial capital to be captured by the Taliban as part of the wider 2021 Taliban offensive.

== Etymology ==
According to local legend, the surrounding [[Laghman Province]] (also known as Lamghan) is said to have been named after [[Lamech (father of Noah)|Lamech]], father of Noah. 

In the [[Persian language]], ''mihtar'' means "headman", "lord" or "chief", and "Lam" is an abbreviation for Lamech. <ref>{{Cite web |title=Afghanistan: Metar Lamech Shrine |url=https://www.culturalprofiles.org.uk/Afghanistan/Units/293.html |access-date=15 December 2020 |website=www.culturalprofiles.org.uk}}</ref>

==History==
Amir [[Habibullah Khan]] built [[Qala-e-Seraj]] {{Circa|1912}}–13 in Mihtarlam.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Qalat us-Seraj Palace, Mehtarlam, Laghman. {{!}} ACKU Images System|url=https://ackuimages.photoshelter.com/image/I0000v0Om7i3953g|access-date=|website=ackuimages.photoshelter.com}}</ref>

On 6 February 2006, two people were killed by police in riots in Mihtarlam in events of the [[Jyllands-Posten Muhammad cartoons controversy]].<ref>{{cite news |title=First deaths in Muhammad cartoon protests |work=Times |date=6 February 2006 |url=http://www.timesonline.co.uk/article/0,,3-2027502,00.html |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080113002232/http://www.timesonline.co.uk/article/0%2C%2C3-2027502%2C00.html |archivedate=13 January 2008 |location=London |first1=Simon |last1=Freeman |accessdate=13 June 2015 |url-status=dead}}</ref>

On 26 February 2017, two students were killed and seven others wounded when a rocket landed in Shaheed Mawlawi Abdul Rahman School in [[Basram]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Two Students Killed As Rocket Hits School In Laghman |url=https://tolonews.com/afghanistan/two-students-killed-rocket-hits-school-laghman |access-date=1 March 2021 |website=TOLOnews |language=en}}</ref> On 14 April 2019, at least seven children were killed when [[unexploded ordnance]] detonated in Basram on the outskirts of Mihtarlam.<ref>{{Cite web |date=15 April 2019 |title=Explosion in Afghanistan's Laghman province leaves 7 children dead |url=https://timesofislamabad.com/15-Apr-2019/explosion-in-afghanistans-laghman-province-leaves-7-children-dead|access-date=1 March 2021 |website=Times of Islamabad |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Explosion In Eastern Afghanistan Leaves Seven Children Dead |url=https://tolonews.com/afghanistan/explosion-eastern-afghanistan-leaves-seven-children-dead |access-date=1 March 2021 |website=TOLOnews |language=en}}</ref>

Local officials spent 22 million [[Afghan Afghani|Afs]] to rebuild Qala-e-Seraj in 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=TOLOnews status|url=https://mobile.twitter.com/tolonews/status/1244831085227323392|access-date=|website=Twitter|language=en|quote=Archaeologists and residents in the eastern province of #Laghman said on Monday that the restoration of the historically-significant Seraj Castle (Qala -e- Seraj), which started two years ago, has been completed. #Afghanistan}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Seraj Castle Restoration Completed|url=https://tolonews.com/arts-culture/seraj-castle-restoration-completed|access-date=|website=TOLOnews|language=en}}</ref>

On [[May 2020 Afghanistan attacks|2 May 2020]], a motorbike bomb exploded outside the provincial prison in Mihtarlam, killing three civilians and injuring four members of the [[Afghan security forces]]. Noor Mohammad, director of Laghman's provincial prison directorate, was among the injured.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://iranpress.com/en/asia-i146713-three_killed_four_injured_in_blast_outside_afghanistan%E2%80%99s_laghman_prison |title=Three killed, four injured in blast outside Afghanistan's Laghman prison |date=2 May 2020 |via=https://iranpress.com/}}</ref> On 5 October 2020, Provincial Governor of Laghman, [[Rahmatullah Yarmal]], was slightly wounded after his convoy was targeted by a suicide car bomber.<ref>{{Cite web |last= |first= |date= |title=Afghan governor left injured after being targeted by suicide bomber |url=https://www.trtworld.com/asia/afghan-governor-left-injured-after-being-targeted-by-suicide-bomber-40289 |archive-url= |archive-date= |access-date= |website=TRT World}}</ref>

On 24 May 2021, Afghan government forces clashed with Taliban militants in Mihtarlam.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Afghan Army, Taliban Clash Close To Kabul |url=https://gandhara.rferl.org/a/afghan-army-taliban-clash-close-to-kabul/31271496.html |access-date=3 July 2021 |website=RFE/RL |language=en}}</ref>
