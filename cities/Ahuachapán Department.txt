'''Ahuachapán''' ({{IPA|es|awatʃaˈpan}}) is a [[Departments of El Salvador|department]] of [[El Salvador]] in the west of the country.<ref name=":0">{{Cite book |last=Perez |first=Orlando J. |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=dTSHDAAAQBAJ&dq=%22Ahuachap%C3%A1n+Department%22+-wikipedia&pg=PA22 |title=Historical Dictionary of El Salvador |date=2016-07-26 |publisher=[[Rowman & Littlefield]] |isbn=978-0-8108-8020-7 |pages=22 |language=en}}</ref> The capital is [[Ahuachapán]].<ref>{{Cite book |last=Cohen |first=Saul Bernard |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=C6PLxz8EMk0C&dq=%22Ahuachap%C3%A1n+Department%22+-wikipedia&pg=PA1789 |title=The Columbia Gazetteer of the World: A to G |date=2008 |publisher=[[Columbia University Press]] |isbn=978-0-231-14554-1 |pages=39 |language=en}}</ref> In the South it has the Apaneca-Ilamatepec Range and the Cerro Grande de [[Apaneca]] (Apaneca Grand Hill).<ref name=":0" /> Its has a population of over 360,000 people.<ref name=":0" />  The Ahuachapán Department was created by the Legislative Decree of February 9, 1869 under the administration of [[Francisco Dueñas]].
