'''Nizamabad''' is a city in the Indian state of [[Telangana]]. It is governed by municipal corporation and is the headquarters of the [[Nizamabad district]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Nizamabad district|url=http://www.telangana.gov.in/About/Districts/Nizamabad |website=Telangana State Portal|access-date=26 August 2015}}</ref> Previously part of [[Hyderabad State]] and then [[Andhra Pradesh]] state, Nizamabad became a part of the newly formed state of Telangana under the [[Andhra Pradesh Reorganisation Act, 2014]]. It is located about {{convert|186|km|mi|0}} north of the state capital, [[Hyderabad]].

== Etymology ==
Nizamabad, also known as Indhooru and Indhrapuri,{{Citation needed|date=September 2024}} during the 8th century, the town was under the reign of Indra Vallabha Panthya Vasrha Indra Som who belonged to the [[Rashtrakuta Dynasty]]. The place was named Indhrapuri after the king.

The name Nizamabad is derived from ''Nizam'' meaning "[[Nizam of Hyderabad]]" and ''abad'' meaning "City" in Urdu. Sometimes the place is also referred as "The City of Nizams".<ref name="nativeplanet.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.nativeplanet.com/nizamabad/|title=Nizamabad Tourism, Travel Guide & Tourist Places in Nizamabad – Native Planet|work=nativeplanet.com}}</ref>

== History == 

Originally Nizamabad was called '''Induru''' known to have originated in the name of '''King Indradatta''' who had ruled this region during the 5th century CE. During the prime minister-ship of Sir Salar Jung-I in the 1876, the districts in the Nizam’s dominion were reorganized whereupon Indur became a district.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://nizamabad.telangana.gov.in/about-district/ | title=Nizamabad district profile &#124; Nizamabad District &#124; India }}</ref>

Nizamabad was a part of princely [[Hyderabad State|State of Hyderabad]] in 1724 and continued to be part of the [[Nizam of Hyderabad|Nizam's]] dominions until 1948.<ref name="(India)Dept1971">{{cite book|title=Maharashtra State Gazetteers: Nanded|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=2Y5hAAAAIAAJ|year=1971|publisher=Director of Government Printing, Stationery and Publications, Maharashtra State|pages=4, 576}}</ref> After India [[Independence Day (India)|gained independence]] in 1947, the [[Indian Armed Forces]] integrated Hyderabad and ended the rule of the Nizam in [[Operation Polo]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hyderabad.co.uk/policeaction.htm |title=Police Action |first=Mohan |last=Guruswamy |publisher=Hyderabad on the Net |access-date=31 July 2012}}</ref> It was during 1876 Nizamabad became a separate district when the districts in Hyderabad State were reorganized by Salar Jung-I, the then prime minister. In 1905 the railway line between [[Secunderabad]] and [[Manmad]] was laid.<ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Dig-AwAAQBAJ&q=Secunderabad+to+Manmad+1905&pg=PA125|title=Tourism in India|isbn=9788192681979|last1=Babu|first1=Prof K. Vijaya|last2=Gade|first2=Jayaprakashnarayana|date=2014-03-12|publisher=Zenon Academic }}</ref> The city was named after [[Nizam Ali Khan]], Asaf Jah II|Nizam Ali Khan the second [[Nizam of Hyderabad]] state. And during the reign of last Nizam, [[Nizam Sagar]] dam was constructed in 1923 across the [[Manjira River]] at the village of [[Achampet, Telangana|Achampet]] which irrigates {{convert|250000|acre|km2 sqmi|lk=in|abbr=on}} of land in Nizamabad District.<ref name="telanganafocus.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.telanganafocus.com/nizamabad.html|title=Welcome to Telangana Focus.com|access-date=20 October 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304053003/http://www.telanganafocus.com/nizamabad.html|archive-date=4 March 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

== Geography ==
Nizamabad is located at {{Coord|18|41|N|78|6|E|type:city_region:IN}}.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fallingrain.com/world/IN/02/Nizamabad.html |title=Falling Rain Genomics, Inc – Nizamabād |publisher=Fallingrain.com |access-date=20 November 2012}}</ref> The city is bounded on the North by [[Nirmal]], on the East by [[Jagtial]] and [[Karimnagar]], on the South lies [[Kamareddy]], and on the West it shares its boundaries with [[Nanded]] of [[Maharashtra]] state.

The city is administrated into 3 zones, ''[[Nizamabad North Mandal|Nizamabad North]]'', ''[[Nizamabad South Mandal|Nizamabad South]]'' of [[Nizamabad Urban Assembly constituency|Nizamabad Urban constituency]] and ''Nizamabad Rural'' of [[Nizamabad Rural Assembly constituency|Nizamabad Rural constituency]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://newdistrictsformation.telangana.gov.in/template1-assets/maps/NIZAMABAD.jpg |title=Archived copy |access-date=14 October 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161018230806/http://newdistrictsformation.telangana.gov.in/template1-assets/maps/NIZAMABAD.jpg |archive-date=18 October 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://nizamabad.telangana.gov.in/mandals-villages/ |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170411134906/http://nizamabad.telangana.gov.in/mandals-villages/ |archive-date=11 April 2017 |title=Mandals & Villages – THE OFFICIAL WEBSITE OF NIZAMABAD DISTRICT}}</ref> The [[Nizamabad Municipal Corporation]] governs an area of {{convert|42.9|km2}} under Nizamabad Urban which mainly comprises the suburbs of Nizamabad North and South mandals.

== Climate ==
As the city is situated at a considerable distance from the coast, the climate is [[tropical savanna climate|tropical savanna]] with most rainfall from June to October. In winter there is less rainfall than in summer. In the month of May 2015, Nizamabad recorded {{convert|46.1|°C|°F}} which was highest recorded temperature in Telangana during this summer.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thehindu.com/news/cities/Hyderabad/nizamabad-sizzles-at-461-degree-celsius/article7225566.ece|title=Nizamabad sizzles at 46.1 degree Celsius|author=Special Correspondent|newspaper=The Hindu|date=2015-05-19}}</ref>
The temperature goes as low as {{convert|5|C}} while the average is {{convert|18|°C|°F}} during winter, while in summer the temperature rises as high as {{convert|47|C}} and the average is {{convert|46|°C|°F}}. Average annual temperature is {{convert|27|°C|°F}}.The average annual rainfall is 1108&nbsp;mm.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.climate-data.org/location/714766/|title=Climate: Nizamabad|work=climate-data.org}}</ref>

== Administration ==
=== Nizamabad Urban Development Authority ===
The [[Government of Telangana]] released a GO in 2017 setting up [[Nizamabad Urban Development Authority|Nizamabad Urban Development Authority (NUDA)]] to develop [[Nizamabad Municipal Corporation]] and surrounding 60 villages situated in six mandals.<ref name="auto2" /> NUDA with a geographical area of {{convert|169.37|km2}}<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.deccanchronicle.com/nation/politics/240118/merger-of-13-nizamabad-villages-on-the-cards.html | title=Merger of 13 Nizamabad villages on the cards| date=2018-01-24}}</ref><ref name="auto2">{{cite web | url=https://telanganatoday.com/go-released-set-nizamabad-urban-development-authority | title=GO released to set up Nizamabad Urban Development Authority}}</ref> spreading across North, South and Rural mandals of the city sees over the infrastructure development and planning for the 633,933<ref>{{cite web | url=https://telanganatoday.com/nizamabad-urban-assembly-constituency-profile | title=Nizamabad Urban Assembly constituency profile}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://telanganatoday.com/nizamabad-rural-assembly-constituency-profile | title=Nizamabad Rural Assembly constituency profile}}</ref> residents under its jurisdiction.

=== Municipal corporation ===
The [[Nizamabad Municipal Corporation]] is the municipal governing body of the city which consists of democratically elected members, headed by a mayor which administers the city's infrastructure, civic administration and water supply. Nizamabad Municipality was constituted in the year 1931 and upgraded as special grade Municipality in the year 1987 and as a Corporation on 5 March 2005 vide GO. No:109. The jurisdiction of the civic body is spread over an urbanized but dense area of {{convert|42.9|km2|mi2|abbr=on}} and has 60 municipal wards.<ref name="mcn">{{cite web|title=Basic Information of Corporation {{!}} Nizamabad Corporation|url=http://mcnizamabad.in/basic-information-of-municipality-2/|website=mcnizamabad.in|access-date=8 August 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160327175224/http://mcnizamabad.in/basic-information-of-municipality-2/|archive-date=27 March 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

=== E-Governance services ===
For providing facilities to the people the [[Government of Telangana]] started [[Mee Seva (Telangana)|MeeSeva]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.telangana.gov.in/services/meeseva|title=Telangana State Portal MeeSeva Services|work=telangana.gov.in}}</ref> (At your service) centres all around the city and the state. The main purpose of this facility was to bring public services closer to home. MeeSeva provides online signed digital certificates like Income Certificates, Residence Certificates, Land Records and other Registration Records. These records are further stored online for database.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thehindubusinessline.com/news/national/ap-to-offer-331-mee-seva-services-by-oct/article4598834.ece|title=AP to offer 331 Mee Seva services by Oct|author=Our Bureau|work=The Hindu Business Line|date=9 April 2013 }}</ref> Nizamabad along with Hyderabad are the only districts<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thehindu.com/todays-paper/tp-national/tp-andhrapradesh/vizag-passport-office-to-cater-to-guntur-krishna-yanam/article7233224.ece|title=Vizag passport office to cater to Guntur, Krishna, Yanam|author=Special Correspondent|newspaper=The Hindu|date=2015-05-21}}</ref> in the Telangana state which have their own [[List of Passport Offices in India|Passport Seva Kendras]], these offices carter the need of other districts of Telangana state.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/visakhapatnam/Districts-realigned-under-Visakhapatnam-Hyderabad-passport-offices/articleshow/47127883.cms|title=Districts realigned under Visakhapatnam, Hyderabad passport offices|work=The Times of India|date=2 May 2015 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.telugupeople.com/news/article_00097237_Regional_Passport_Office_to_re-align_districts.asp|title=Regional Passport Office to re-align districts}}</ref>

=== Law and order ===
{{Main|Nizamabad Police}}
[[File:Nizamabad Police Vehicle.jpg|thumb|right|Nizamabad City Police SUV]]

The [[Nizamabad Police]] Commissionerate is the local law enforcement agency for the city and is headed by the [[Commissioner of Police]]. The city police traces its origins to 1847 under [[Hyderabad State]]. The city is under strict vigilance by the commissionerate as there are 72 CCTV cameras installed at various traffic junctions<ref>{{cite news|title=CCTVs lie unused in Nizamabad|url=http://m.thehindu.com/news/national/telangana/cctvs-lie-unused-in-nizamabad/article6662198.ece/|access-date=18 April 2015|work=The Hindu|date=4 December 2014|archive-url=https://archive.today/20150507185335/http://m.thehindu.com/news/national/telangana/cctvs-lie-unused-in-nizamabad/article6662198.ece/|archive-date=7 May 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> and the infrastructure includes Interceptor Vehicles, Online Presence and a large fleet of [[Mahindra Bolero]] SUVs. Apart from Crime branch, the police also has Traffic branch, SHE teams, Blue Colts and Mobile patrolling fleet.

=== Healthcare ===

Being the third largest city in the state, Nizamabad is known for its medical care in northern Telangana region. [[Government General Hospital, Nizamabad|Government General Hospital]] is one of the largest government hospital in the state, also serving patients from the cities and neighbouring districts of [[Nanded district|Nanded]] and [[Adilabad district|Adilabad]].<ref>{{cite news|title=State Government Seeks Rs 750 Cr from Centre for Pushkarams|url=http://www.newindianexpress.com/states/telangana/State-Government-Seeks-Rs-750-Cr-from-Centre-for-Pushkarams/2015/03/19/article2720350.ece|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150321001303/http://www.newindianexpress.com/states/telangana/State-Government-Seeks-Rs-750-Cr-from-Centre-for-Pushkarams/2015/03/19/article2720350.ece|url-status=dead|archive-date=21 March 2015|access-date=18 April 2015|work=The New Indian Express|date=19 March 2015}}</ref> ''Khaleelwadi'', also known as ''Doctor's Lane'', is a densely hospital clustered area comprising more than 200 major private hospitals<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rainrays.com/topic/hospitals_and_clinics_in_nizamabad_list_one-2678-141.htm|title=Hospitals and Clinics in Nizamabad List One|work=rainrays.com}}</ref> and many laboratories. Considering the better infrastructure and resources available, [[Government Medical College, Nizamabad|Government Medical College]] was established in 2012. There are speciality hospitals for maternity care, chest hospitals, Meghna Hospital for Dental care, Indur Cancer Hospital. Apart from these multi-speciality hospitals in the city, Pragati Hospital is one of the only hospitals among the neighbouring districts which has recently established a heart transplantation wing.

=== Railways ===

{{stnlnk|Nizamabad Junction}} is administered under [[Hyderabad railway division]] of [[South Central Railway zone]] and is the convergence station for [[Nizamabad–Peddapalli section]] and [[Jankampet]]–[[Bodhan]] line with [[Kacheguda–Manmad section]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Hyderabad Division System Map|url=http://scr.indianrailways.gov.in/cris/view_section.jsp?lang=0&id=0,1,291,355|website=South Central Railway|access-date=28 October 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Nizamabad rail station to get junction status|url=https://www.deccanchronicle.com/nation/current-affairs/290316/nizamabad-rail-station-to-get-junction-status.html|access-date=29 October 2016|work=Deccan Chronicle|date=29 March 2016}}</ref>

This Nizamabad–Peddapalli section connects Nizamabad with the [[Grand Trunk Express|Grand Trunk Route]] of [[New Delhi–Chennai main line]] and the distance from Nizamabad to [[Karimnagar]] and [[Warangal]] reduced to a large extent.<ref>{{cite web|title=Press Release|url=http://www.scr.indianrailways.gov.in/view_detail.jsp?lang=0&dcd=5834&id=0,5,268|website=South Central Railway Zone|publisher=Indian Railways|access-date=29 October 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Rail budget boost to 2 last-mile projects|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/hyderabad/Rail-budget-boost-to-2-last-mile-projects/articleshow/51174096.cms|access-date=29 October 2016|work=The Times of India|date=28 February 2016}}</ref>

In 2023, the station was selected as part of the [[Amrit Bharat Station Scheme]]

== Economy ==
[[File:Reliance Mall Nizamabad.jpg|thumb|left|[[Reliance Retail]] Mall]]
[[File:Shopping Mall Nizamabad.jpg|thumb|right|Shopping Malls on RP Road]]
[[File:Walmart Nizamabad.jpg|thumb|right|A [[Walmart]] store in Nizamabad]]
The economy of the city is largely based on industries and private businesses<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://dcmsme.gov.in/dips/amended%20%20nizamabad.pdf |title=Archived copy |access-date=27 November 2020 |archive-date=18 November 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171118095024/http://dcmsme.gov.in/dips/amended%20%20nizamabad.pdf |url-status=dead }}</ref> which are both under government and public sector. For most families the main source of income is either government jobs or family citizens working in [[Gulf countries]]. After the bifurcation of [[Andhra Pradesh]] and Telangana, with an initiative to develop the backward districts the state government has set up industrial parks in rural areas such as Sarangpur and Nehru Nagar.

Some of major industries include [[Nizam Sugar Factory]] which was the biggest sugar factory in Asia during the reign of Nizams,<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://apind.gov.in/Library/District/nizamabad.pdf |title=Archived copy |access-date=27 November 2020 |archive-date=13 May 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140513195611/http://www.apind.gov.in/Library/District/nizamabad.pdf |url-status=dead }}</ref> and major Spice Park.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thehindubusinessline.com/news/national/telangana-to-set-up-spices-park-in-nizamabad/article8541681.ece|title=Telangana to set up Spices Park in Nizamabad| date=30 April 2016 }}</ref> As Nizamabad is the largest producer of rice in the state there are many of Rice mills situated across the district and mostly in Khanapur region.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thehansindia.com/posts/index/Telangana/2016-05-30/Source-wise-Irrigation-in-Bangaru-Telangana/231451|title=Source-wise Irrigation in Bangaru Telangana|website=The Hans India| date=30 May 2016 }}</ref>

In 2018, with the aim of promoting establishment of IT industries in Tier-II cities, the state government of Telangana started the construction work of the proposed IT Tower and Incubation hub in the north part of the city.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://telanganatoday.com/telangana-rs-25-cr-it-tower-nizamabad |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190121172751/https://telanganatoday.com/telangana-rs-25-cr-it-tower-nizamabad |archive-date=21 January 2019 |title=Telangana: Rs 25 cr IT tower for Nizamabad}}</ref> The IT Hub was inaugurated in August 2023.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.newindianexpress.com/amp/story/states/telangana/2023/aug/10/focus-on-honing-skills-to-boost-employability-ktr-2603834.html | title=Focus on honing skills to boost employability: KTR - the New Indian Express }}</ref>

== Culture ==
[[File:Chicken Hyderabadi Biryani.JPG|thumb|[[Biryani]] is the most served dish across the restaurants]]
Nizamabad's culture enriched under the patronage of Nizams. The city has a diverse culture with major proportion of city population being Hindus and Muslims and this mixing of religions has also resulted in many festivals being celebrated in Nizamabad such as [[Ganesh Chaturthi]] and [[Diwali]] of Hindu tradition and [[Eid ul-Fitr]], [[Mawlid]] and [[Eid al-Adha]] by Muslims. Telugu and Urdu are major languages spoken by the city people, however English is also widely used for communication purpose. Even though the city has different religious communities, the people of Nizamabad are known for their harmony, cultural amalgamation and religious unity .<ref name="nativeplanet.com" />

=== Cuisine ===
Nizamabadi cuisine mainly comprises rice, wheat and [[Lamb and mutton|meat]] dishes. [[South Indian cuisine]] which include [[Dosa (food)|Dosa]], [[Vada (food)|Vada]], [[Puri (food)|Puri]] and [[Idly]] are common breakfast items. [[Hyderabadi biriyani]] with its blend of [[Mughlai cuisine|Mughlai]] and [[Arab cuisine|Arab]] influences and ''[[Tahri (dish)|Tahari]]'' are other dishes. The traditional food items like [[Harees]], [[Haleem]] and [[Nihari]] are common foods during [[Ramadan]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://twocircles.net/2015jun19/1434701835.html#.VYvddUY5HIU|title=Hyderabad's old city comes alive as Ramadan begins|work=twocircles.net| date=19 June 2015 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thehindu.com/news/national/telangana/shopping-mania-grips-nizamabad/article6221607.ece|title=Shopping mania grips Nizamabad|author=P. Ram Mohan|work=The Hindu| date=17 July 2014 }}</ref>

=== Tourism ===
[[File:Gurudwar Nizamabad.jpg|thumb|left|A [[Gurudwara]] in Nizamabad]]
[[File:Sri Ram Sagar Project (Pochampahad).jpg|thumb|right|[[Sriram Sagar Project]] at Pochampahad]]
[[Nizamabad Fort]] is situated on the southwest of the city which was built by Rashtrakuta Kings in around the 10th century, the fort was captured by Asaf Jahis who were the [[Nizam of Hyderabad|Nizams of Hyderabad]], who then rebuilt the fort.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.india9.com/i9show/-Andhra-Pradesh/Quilla-Ramalayam-24147.htm|title=Quilla Ramalayam|work=india9.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://gonizamabad.com/nizamabad-quilla-ramalayam-raghunath-temple-photos/|title=Nizamabad Quilla Ramalayam – Raghunath Temple Photos|work=Go Nizamabad|access-date=3 August 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150711021940/http://gonizamabad.com/nizamabad-quilla-ramalayam-raghunath-temple-photos/|archive-date=11 July 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref>
Archaeological and Heritage Museum was opened in October 2001 and is situated in the ''Tilak Garden'' premises which is opened seasonally. The Museum houses various artifacts and antiquities representing the evolution of human civilization from [[Paleolithic]] to [[Vijayanagara Empire]](i.e. 16th. A.D). The Museum is divided into three sections, namely Archaeological Section, Sculptural gallery and Bronze and Decorative gallery. Bidri articles and arms and weapons of extensive variety are also exhibited.<ref name="telanganafocus.com"/>

[[Alisagar lift irrigation scheme|Alisagar]] is a park and tourist attraction which is 13&nbsp;km from Nizamabad and 2&nbsp;km (1.2&nbsp;mi) off the Nizamabad-Bodhan road.<ref>{{cite web|title=Indur Tourism|url=http://nizamabad.nic.in/code/tourism.htm|website=Official website of Nizamabad district|access-date=6 December 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151020110351/http://nizamabad.nic.in/code/tourism.htm|archive-date=20 October 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> The garden is spread across 33 acres, with a deer park, trekking and water sports facility. It also has a hilltop guest house built by Nizam and is under renovation as of 2015.{{update inline|date=October 2019}} Ashok Sagar is a lake, park and tourist attraction situated in [[Jankampet]] area, 07&nbsp;km from centre of the city. It has a rock garden with illuminated rocks and also has boating facilities.<ref name="auto">{{cite news|url=http://www.thehindu.com/news/national/telangana/ecotourism-to-be-developed-in-nizamabad/article7145243.ece|title=Ecotourism to be developed in Nizamabad|author=Special Correspondent|work=The Hindu| date=26 April 2015 }}</ref> Neela Kanteshwar Temple is a temple dedicated to the Powerful [[Shiva|Shiva (Mahadev)]] .The temple attracts a large number of devotees coming from different corners the state and the district. This famous temple was constructed for the Jains by the Satavahana king, Satakarni-II. The structure has a lot of resemblance with the North Indian Architectural style. The festival of Ratha sapthami is an annual event celebrated on a grand scale with a lot of pomp in this temple.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Divine Destinations in Telangana :: Telangana Tourism |url=https://telanganatourism.gov.in/partials/destinations/divine-destinations/nizamabad/partials/destinations/divine-destinations.html |access-date=2022-04-28 |website=telanganatourism.gov.in |language=en}}</ref>

[[Badapahad]] dargah is one of the oldest Muslim pilgrim centres, built in the memory of Hazrat Syed Hussaini, on top of a hillock located at a distance of 38&nbsp;km from the city.

== Education ==
{{Main|List of educational institutions in Nizamabad (India)}}
[[File:VREC,A Block.jpg|thumb|left|VREC, A Block, Nizamabad]]

Nizamabad is a major education centre in Telangana. The city is the home to 10 Engineering colleges, [[Vijay Rural Engineering College]] (VREC), affiliated to [[Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University, Hyderabad]] (JNTUH) is the reputed institution located in city. [[Kakatiya Institutions]] of Nizamabad is a group of well known schools & colleges in state of Telangana and the erstwhile [[Andhra Pradesh]], as they have been constantly fetching state top ranks. Some other major educational institutions for higher education are [[Arkay College of Engineering and Technology]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.arkaycollege.com/|title=Arkay College of Engineering & Technology<!-- Bot generated title -->|work=arkaycollege.com|access-date=23 February 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160307215549/http://arkaycollege.com/|archive-date=7 March 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> Kshatriya College of Engineering,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kcea.ac.in/|title=Welcome to Kshatriya College of Engineering|work=kcea.ac.in}}</ref> Kakatiya Engineering College for Women, Ganga Pharmacy college, [[Government Medical College, Nizamabad|Government Medical College]],Girraj Government Degree College<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gmcnzbd.org/|title=Nizamabad Medical College|work=gmcnzbd.org}}</ref> Nishitha Degree College, etc. Many students from neighbouring districts of Nanded, Adilabad and Karimnagar take up admissions here for their higher education.
Also 15 Model schools were established in the year 2013 by central government in EBBs, to uplift the education system & provide free education in English medium.

[[Telangana University]] is a public university under the Government of Telangana, located at [[Dichpally]], 15&nbsp;km from the city. Telangana University alone has 149 college affiliations of which 86 colleges are in Nizamabad and 63 colleges in Adilabad district.<ref>{{cite web|title=About Telangana University|url=http://www.telanganauniversity.ac.in/html/aboutUniversity.html|website=Telangana University|access-date=25 June 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150625022249/http://www.telanganauniversity.ac.in/html/aboutUniversity.html|archive-date=25 June 2015}}</ref> The university has also signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with [[Chicago State University]] of United States for exchange of students and teachers. The MoU was signed by the officials of the two universities in the presence of Chief Minister [[Kalvakuntla Chandrashekar Rao|K.Chandrasekhar Rao]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thehindu.com/news/cities/Hyderabad/exchange-of-students/article7623905.ece|title=Exchange of students|work=The Hindu| date=6 September 2015 }}</ref>
