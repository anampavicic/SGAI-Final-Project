'''Guarapari''' is a coastal town of [[Espírito Santo]], [[Brazil]], a popular tourist destination. Its beach is famous for the high natural radioactivity level of its sand.

==Geography==
===Location===
Guarapari is a part of Greater Vitoria, 47&nbsp;km south of the state capital [[Vitória, Espírito Santo|Vitória]]. Its population is 126,701 (2020) and its area is 592&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>.
It is a well-known tourist destination, known for its curving white sand beaches backed by commercial development, which extend southward to Nova Guarapari and [[Meaípe]]. With its heavily built-up coastline like [[Vila Velha]] and Vitória, it caters heavily to seasonal tourists, and consequently has quite a dramatic seasonal population fluctuation.

The municipality contains the {{convert|953|ha}} [[Concha d'Ostra Sustainable Development Reserve]], established in 2003 to protect the mangroves of the Bay of Guarapari.<ref>{{cite web |title=RDS de Concha D'Ostra |language=pt |publisher=IEMA: Instituto Estadual de Meio Ambiente e Recursos Hídricos |url=http://www.meioambiente.es.gov.br/default.asp?pagina=16701 |access-date=2016-10-27 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161028083720/http://www.meioambiente.es.gov.br/default.asp?pagina=16701 |archive-date=2016-10-28}}</ref> It also contains the {{convert|1500|ha}} [[Paulo César Vinha State Park]], which protects an area of dunes, lagoons and marshes along the Atlantic shore.<ref>{{cite web |title=Parque Estadual Paulo César Vinha |language=pt |publisher=IEMA: Instituto Estadual de Meio Ambiente e Recursos Hídricos |url=http://www.meioambiente.es.gov.br/default.asp?pagina=16712 |access-date=2016-10-26 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161026232344/http://www.meioambiente.es.gov.br/default.asp?pagina=16712 |archive-date=2016-10-26}}</ref> Formerly called the Setiba nature reserve, it is a pristine example of a coastal ecosystem and important for local turtle and bird populations.

==History==
The municipality has had European presence since 1585 when [[Jesuit]] missionaries built a small chapel and dug a well.

==Transportation==
The city is served by [[Guarapari Airport]].

==Radioactivity==

Along a roughly 500-mile (800&nbsp;km) portion of Brazil's Atlantic coast that runs from north of [[Rio de Janeiro]] up to the region south of [[Bahia]], the sands of old beaches are naturally radioactive. Sea waves pound coastal mountains rich in [[monazite]], a phosphate of rare earth metals containing uranium and thorium.<ref>{{cite web |publisher=Industrias Nucleares do Brasil |url=http://www.inb.gov.br/eng/WebForms/Imprimir.aspx?campo=252&secao_id=106 |title=Heavy Minerals |access-date=2012-08-06 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://archive.today/20121127045933/http://www.inb.gov.br/eng/WebForms/Imprimir.aspx?campo=252&secao_id=106 |archive-date=2012-11-27 }}</ref><ref name="Japan">{{cite web |publisher=Health Research Foundation |location=Japan |url=http://www.taishitsu.or.jp/radiation/guarapari-e.html |title=High natural background radiation areas – Guarapari, Brazil}}</ref> The [[background radiation]] level on some spots on the Guarapari beach read 175&nbsp;[[mSv]] per year (20μSv/h);<ref>{{Cite journal| issn = 0001-3765| volume = 53| issue = 4| pages = 683–691| last1 = Pfeiffer| first1 = W. C.| last2 = Penna-Franca| first2 = E.| last3 = Ribeiro| first3 = C. C.| last4 = Nogueira| first4 = A. R.| last5 = Londres| first5 = H.| last6 = Oliveira| first6 = A. E.| title = Measurements of environmental radiation exposure dose rates at selected sites in Brazil| journal = Anais da Academia Brasileira de Ciências| date = December 1981| pmid = 7345962}}</ref> Some other spots can reach dosages of up to 55&nbsp;μSv/h.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RvgAx1yIKjg&t=48s  |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211221/RvgAx1yIKjg |archive-date=2021-12-21 |url-status=live|type=Video |title=Brazil 2012: sunbathing on radioactive beaches |at=at 50s |via=YouTube}}{{cbignore}}</ref> The average exposure level across the United States is 0.34&nbsp;μSv/h<ref>{{cite web |title=Relative Radioactivity Levels |url=https://radwatch.berkeley.edu/dosenet/levels |website=Berkeley RadWatch |publisher=[[UC Berkeley]] |access-date=18 October 2018}}</ref> while a [[chest x-ray]] is a one time exposure of 0.1&nbsp;mSv, and an abdominal and pelvic CT scan with and without contrast is 20-30&nbsp;mSv.<ref>{{cite web |title=Radiation Sources and Doses |url=https://www.epa.gov/radiation/radiation-sources-and-doses |website=Radiation Protection |publisher=EPA |language=en}}</ref>

In the Guarapari city, radiation levels are far lower: a study among 320 inhabitants showed an average received dose of 0.6&nbsp;μSv/h, corresponding to 5.2&nbsp;mSv per year.<ref>{{cite web |author1=N.
