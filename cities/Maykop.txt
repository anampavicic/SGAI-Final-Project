'''Maykop'''{{efn|{{langx|ru|Майкоп}}, {{IPA|ru|mɐj'kop|IPA}}; {{langx|ady|Мыекъуапэ|Myiequape}}, {{IPA|ady|məjɜqʷaːpɜ|IPA}}}} is the capital city of [[Adygea]], [[Russia]], located on the right bank of the [[Belaya (Kuban)|Belaya River]]. It borders [[Maykopsky District]], from which it is administratively and municipally independent, to the east and south; [[Giaginsky District]] to the north, and [[Belorechensky District]] of [[Krasnodar Krai]] to the west.

Population: {{ru-census|p2021=143,385|p2010=144,249|p2002=156,931|p1989=148,608}}{{Historical populations|1897|34300|1926|52525|1939|55871|1959|82135|1970|110212|1979|127828|1989|148608|2002|156931|2010|144249|2021|143385|type=|footnote=Source: Census data}}

==History==
The city gave its name to the early [[Bronze Age]] [[Maykop culture]] after the discovery of a royal burial site there in 1897.<ref>{{cite journal|last= Ivanova|first= Mariya|title= The Chronology of the "Maikop Culture" in the North Caucasus: Changing Perspectives|journal= Armenian Journal of Near Eastern Studies|year= 2007|volume= II|pages= 7–39|url= https://www.academia.edu/2543641}}</ref>

Following the establishment of a military camp in 1825,<ref>

</ref> the [[Imperial Russian Army]] built a military fort at Maykop in 1857.<ref>{{cite book|author1= James B. Minahan|title= Encyclopedia of the Stateless Nations: Ethnic and National Groups Around the World A-Z|date= 30 May 2002|publisher= ABC-CLIO|isbn=9780313076961|page= 38}}</ref>

In 1910 [[petroleum|oil]] deposits were discovered in the vicinity of Maykop.<ref>

</ref> The city was the administrative center of the [[Maykopsky Otdel]] of the [[Kuban Oblast]].

In 1936, Maykop and the surrounding region merged with [[Adyghe Autonomous Oblast]] and became the [[administrative centre]] of the autonomy. The [[Wehrmacht]] occupied Maykop on 10 August 1942 without a fight as a result of a [[Brandenburgers|Brandenburger]] [[commando]] operation. German attempts to re-start oil production in the scorched-earth area proved only minimally successful. On 29 January 1943, the [[Transcaucasian Front]] of the [[Red Army]] re-took the town.<ref>[https://www.soldat.ru/spravka/freedom/1-ssr-4.html Освобождение городов]</ref>

Since 1991 Maykop has served as the capital of the Republic of Adygea in the [[Russian Federation]].

==Economy==
[[File:Солодовенный цех пивоваренного завода "Майкопа"1.jpg|thumb|Brewery (cultural heritage)]]
The discovery of extensive underground oil reserves has made Maykop a major centre for oil extraction for the [[Soviet Union]] and, subsequently, [[Russia]]. Other economically important sectors are food processing and the timber industry.

==Administrative and municipal status==
Within the [[subdivisions of Russia#Administrative divisions|framework of administrative divisions]], it is, together with eight rural localities, incorporated as '''Maykop [[City of federal subject significance|Republican Urban Okrug]]'''—an administrative unit with the status equal to that of the [[list of administrative and municipal divisions of the Republic of Adygea|districts]].<ref name="Ref1">Law #171</ref> It has the following rural localities under its jurisdiction:<ref name="Mun">Law #223</ref>
*''[[khutor]]'' of [[Gaverdovsky]]
*''[[stanitsa]]'' of [[Khanskaya]]
*''khutor'' of [[Kosinov (rural locality)|Kosinov]]
*settlement of [[Podgorny, Maykop, Republic of Adygea|Podgorny]]
*settlement of [[Rodnikovy]]
*settlement of [[Severny, Republic of Adygea|Severny]]
*''khutor'' of [[Vesyoly, Maykop, Republic of Adygea|Vesyoly]]
*settlement of [[Zapadny, Republic of Adygea|Zapadny]]

As a [[subdivisions of Russia#Municipal divisions|municipal division]], Maykop Republican Urban Okrug is incorporated as '''Maykop Urban Okrug'''.<ref name="Ref1m">Law #171 stipulates that the borders of the republican urban okrugs are identical to those of the municipal urban okrugs.</ref>

==Education==

[[File:Средняя школа №5 (женская гимназия) Майкопа 1.jpg|thumb|left|Maykop female grammar school]]

Maykop is home to the [[Adyghe State University]] and [[Maykop State Technological University]]. There are also several facilities of professional education in Maykop.
There are many schools. For example, Adyghe Republican Gymnasium which is located near the mosque, the theatre and the park of friendship. It has 11 grades. There are many rules: students must wear a uniform, girls must have pony tails or braids. The school has five buildings. There is a sports hall where students play basketball and volleyball. There is also a football pitch where boys play football. A canteen is a separate building.

==Military==
On the south side of the city, alongside the Belaya River is the [[Military base|military complex]] housing the [[131st Motor Rifle Brigade]] of the [[Southern Military District]] of the [[Russian Armed Forces]] which took part in the [[First Chechen War]].
