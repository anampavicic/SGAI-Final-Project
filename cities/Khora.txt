'''Khora''' is a locality in [[Ghaziabad|Ghaziabad city]] in the [[Ghaziabad district, India|Ghaziabad district]] of [[Uttar Pradesh]], [[India]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.census2011.co.in/census/city/106-khora.html|title=Khora City Population Census 2011 {{!}} Uttar Pradesh|website=www.census2011.co.in|access-date=2018-09-15}}</ref> Its official census population is approximately 190,000 residents (2011),<ref name=":0" /> but reports<ref>{{Cite book|title=Accumulation in post-colonial capitalism|editor1=Mitra, Iman Kumar |editor2=Samāddāra, Raṇabīra |editor3=Sen, Samita|date = 29 July 2016|isbn=978-9811010378|location=Singapore|pages=109–123|oclc=955138429}}</ref> suggest a population closer to 1,500,000 residents inside the city's area of 10 square kilometers. It is located on the [[NH-24]] highway, near [[Gaziabad]] and [[Delhi]], and is within the [[National Capital Region (India)|National Capital Region of India]]. It is bound on the west and southwest by the village of [[Ghazipur (Delhi)|Ghazipur]], on the north and northwest by [[Hindon River|Hindon Canal]], on the northeast by [[Ghaziabad, Uttar Pradesh#Demographics|Indirapuram (Ghaizabad)]], and on the east and southeast by [[Noida]]. The Chairman of Khora is Mohini Sharma<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.amarujala.com/delhi-ncr/ghaziabad/mohini-sharma-destroyed-bjps-fort-in-khoda-shahibabad-news-c-30-1-106285-2023-05-14 | title=खोड़ा में मोहिनी शर्मा ने ढहाया भाजपा का किला | date=14 May 2023 | publisher=www.amarujala.com | access-date=14 May 2023 }}</ref>

==Accessibility==
It is connected by road to [[NH 24]], and by rail to [[Anand Vihar Railway Station]], While Anand Vihar is closer, the [[Ghaziabad Junction railway station]] (9&nbsp;km) and [[Hazrat Nizamuddin Railway Station]] (10&nbsp;km) are also proximate.
It is also linked to [[Vaishali (Ghaziabad)|Vaishali Station]] and [[Kaushambi metro station|Kaushambi Station]] by metro.

==Census (2011)==
As per provisional reports of Census India, population of Khora in 2011 is 190,005; of which male and female are 102,574 and 87,431 respectively. Hinduism is majority religion in Khora city with 84.18 % followers. Islam is second most popular religion in city of Khora with approximately 14.91 % following it. In Khora city, Christianity is followed by 0.22 %, Jainism by 0.06 %, Sikhism by 0.16 % and Buddhism by 0.16 %. Around 0.01 % stated 'Other Religion', approximately 0.39 % stated 'No Particular Religion'. Literate Population of Khora is 135,994; of which 79,636 are Male and 56,358 are Female. Child Population(0-6) is 27,026; of which male and female are 14,587 and 12,439 respectively.
