'''Shibin El Kom''' ({{langx|ar|شبين الكوم}} {{IPA|arz|ʃɪˈbiːn elˈkoːm|pron}}, colloquially shortened to ''Shibin'') is a city in [[Egypt]]'s [[Nile Delta]], and the capital of the [[Monufia Governorate]].<ref name="Monofeya Egypt Governorate">{{cite web |title=Sheben El Koum |url=http://www.monofeya.gov.eg/HaykalTanzemy/cities/ShebenElkoum/ShebenElkoumCity/default.aspx |website=Monofeya Egypt Governorate |access-date=2018-11-20 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180223143806/http://www.monofeya.gov.eg/HaykalTanzemy/cities/ShebenElkoum/ShebenElkoumCity/default.aspx# |archive-date=2018-02-23 |url-status=live }}</ref>
{{Coord|30|33|31|N|31|0|36|E|display=title}}

== Etymology ==
The city was previously known as '''Shaybin as-Ssarya''' ({{Langx|ar|شيبين السَّرِى}}) the first part of which Ramzi connects to {{Langx|ar|أشيب}} ''ʾašyab'' "grey-coloured, old".<ref name="مماتي156">ابن مماتي ص156</ref><ref>{{Cite book |last=Peust |first=Carsten |title=Die Toponyme vorarabischen Ursprungs im modernen Ägypten |pages=85}}</ref> It appears to be a translation of {{Langx|la|veteranorum|lit=of veterans/old}} akin to Shaybin al-Qasr ({{Langx|ar|شيبين القصر|lit=castle of the old}}, {{Langx|la|Scenae Veteranorum|lit=tents of veterans/old}}), modern [[Shibin El Qanater|Shibin el-Qanatir]], and possibly points out that Shibin el Kom used to be one of the Roman military camps in [[Lower Egypt]].

==Facilities==
While the city is not a new one, its infrastructure is being modernized. The most important central and local government offices are located in the city, as well as the main branches of [[Menoufia University]]. The city has several public and private schools, hospitals, a large stadium, a regional office of [[Telecom Egypt]], organized trade unions, athletic teams, political parties and social organizations and a chamber of commerce.

== Climate ==
{{See also|Nile Delta#Climate}}

Shibin's climate is classified by [[Köppen-Geiger climate classification system]] as [[hot desert climate|hot desert]] (BWh), as the rest of [[climate of Egypt|Egypt]].

The highest record temperature was {{convert|48|°C}} recorded on June 7, 1961, while the lowest record temperature was {{convert|-3|°C}} recorded on January 23, 1996.<ref name="Voodoo Skies">{{cite web | url =http://voodooskies.com/weather/egypt/shibin-el-kom | title =Shibin el-Kom, Egypt | access-date =4 July 2013 | publisher =Voodoo Skies | archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20150610210830/http://voodooskies.com/weather/egypt/shibin-el-kom# | archive-date =2015-06-10 | url-status =live }}</ref>
