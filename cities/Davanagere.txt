'''Davanagere''' is a city in the centre of the [[South India|southern Indian]] state of [[Karnataka]]. It is the seventh largest city in the state, and the administrative headquarters of eponymous [[Davanagere district|Davangere district]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.davanagerecity.gov.in/statistics |title=Davangere town Statistics |access-date=8 October 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121221145823/http://www.davanagerecity.gov.in/statistics |archive-date=21 December 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Hitherto being a cotton hub and hence popularly known before as ''the Manchester of Karnataka'', the commercial ventures of the city is now dominated by education and agro-processing industries.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Tejaswi |first=Marx |date=21 April 2019 |title=Caste factor holds key in Karnataka’s Manchester |url=https://www.newindianexpress.com/states/karnataka/2019/Apr/21/caste-factor-holds-key-in-karnatakas-manchester-1967022.html |access-date=13 September 2024 |website=The New Indian Express |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Shastri |first=Vittal |date=25 February 2018 |title=Battleground karnataka: Davangere Where the Lingayats are keeping everyone guessing |url=https://www.deccanchronicle.com/nation/politics/250218/battleground-karnataka-davangere-where-the-lingayats-are-keeping.html |access-date=13 September 2024 |website=Deccan Chronicle |language=en}}</ref> Davanagere became a separate district in 1997,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://davanagere.nic.in/en/history/|title=History {{!}} Davanagere District, Government of Karnataka|language=en-US|access-date=2019-03-27}}</ref> when it was separated from the erstwhile undivided district of [[Chitradurga]] for administration conveniences. Davanagere is known for rich culinary traditions which encompass the [[Karnataka cuisine|diversity of entire Karnataka's]] dishes due to its geographical position in the state as its centre. Notable among them is its aromatic [[benne dose]].<ref name="District Gazetteers Karnataka state">[http://gazetteer.kar.nic.in/gazetteer/distGazetteer.html# District Gazetteers Karnataka state]</ref><ref>{{Cite news |last=Mujumdar |first=Neha |date=2012-12-22 |title=In search of DAVANGERE BENNE DOSE |language=en-IN |work=The Hindu |url=https://www.thehindu.com/features/magazine/in-search-of-davangere-benne-dose/article4221252.ece |access-date=2022-07-26 |issn=0971-751X}}</ref>

Davanagere was selected as one of the hundred Indian cities to be developed as a [[smart city]] under the [[Government of India|Central government]]'s [[Smart Cities Mission]]. It was among the first 20 towns to be developed under the mission by [[Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs|Ministry of Urban Development]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.newindianexpress.com/states/karnataka/Davanagere-Belagavi-Among-1st-Smart-Cities/2016/01/29/article3250365.ece | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160130085923/http://www.newindianexpress.com/states/karnataka/Davanagere-Belagavi-Among-1st-Smart-Cities/2016/01/29/article3250365.ece | url-status=dead | archive-date=30 January 2016 | title=Davanagere, Belagavi Among 1st Smart Cities | publisher=The New Indian Express-28-Jan-2016 | access-date=10 February 2016}}</ref> According to 2020 human living index, it was selected as the top 9th most livable city with a population under 10 million in India.

==Civic administration==
{{See also|Davanagere City Corporation}}
Davangere has been at the forefront in municipal administration with the city attaining the status of a municipality as early as 1870.<ref name="Uchicago.edu">{{Cite web|url=https://dsal.uchicago.edu/reference/gazetteer/pager.html?volume=11&objectid=DS405.1.I34_V11_210.gif|title=Imperial Gazetteer2 of India, Volume 11, page 204 -- Imperial Gazetteer of India -- Digital South Asia Library|website=dsal.uchicago.edu}}</ref> The ''Imperial Gazetteer of India'' (1911) says that the receipts and expenditure of the municipality, during the ten years ending 1901, averaged Rs 14,200 and Rs 12,600 respectively.<ref name="Uchicago.edu"/> The civic administration of the city was managed by the ''Nagarasabhe'', before it was established as a municipality on 7 August 1951. It now has the status of a [[Davanagere City Corporation|City Corporation]], which was upgraded on 6 January 2007. It is headed by a Mayor, assisted by Commissioners and council members. The city is divided into 45 wards, and the council members (corporators) are elected by the people of the city.

==Geography==
Davanagere is the "Heart of Karnataka". Davanagere is surrounded by the  [[Chitradurga]], [[Vijayanagara]], [[Shimoga]], [[Chikmagalur]] and [[Haveri]] districts. Davanagere is at the centre of Karnataka, 14°28' N latitude, 75°59' longitude and {{convert|602.5|m|ft|0}} above sea level. Davanagere District receives an average annual rainfall of {{convert|abbr=on|644|mm|in|1}}.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://davanagere.nic.in/Backup/profile.htm|title=NIC.in|accessdate=28 April 2024}}</ref>

Davanagere lies in the semi arid region. The district is bounded by Shimoga (Malenadu) Area of Hills, Haveri, Chitradurga, Chikmagalur and Vijayanagara districts. The southern and western parts of the district are irrigated by the waters of the [[Bhadra River|Bhadra reservoir]]. It has Asia's 2nd largest irrigation tank called Sulekere (shanthi sagara) which is a major water source for farmers in the district.

As of the 2011 census, Davanagere city had a population of 435,125. Males constitute 52% of the population, and females 48%. Davanagere has an average literacy rate of 85%, higher than the national average of 74.04%: male literacy is 89% and, female literacy is 81%.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.census2011.co.in/census/city/442-davanagere.html | title=Davanagere City Census 2011 data | publisher=Census 2011 | access-date=14 September 2018 }}</ref> In Davanagere, 12% of the population is under 6 years of age.

== Transportation ==
Davangere is well-connected by road to [[Bangalore]],  [[Chitradurga]], [[Dharwad]], [[Pune]], [[Goa]], [[Mangalore]], [[Mysore]] and [[Chennai]] through [[National Highway 48 (India)|NH 48]] (previously National Highway 4). [[Karnataka State Road Transport Corporation]], has a divisional office situated in Davanagere City. The city is well-connected to Bengaluru and other cities in the state by KSRTC buses. Many privately operated buses offer overnight services to other places within the state.

===Rail===
The city's [[Davangere railway station|railway station]] comes under the [[South Western Railway zone]] and was constructed during the era of the British Raj and has recently been renovated. The station code is DVG. The railway station is quite conveniently located in the centre of the city. Regular train services are available towards Bengaluru, Hubli and Mysuru.

===Air===
The nearest domestic airport to Davanagere is [[Shivamogga Airport]] (IATA:RQY), which is {{convert|100|km|mi|0}} from the city and the nearest International airports are [[Kempegowda International Airport|Bangalore International Airport]] and [[Mangalore International Airport]] both of which are about 275&nbsp;km away. The ubiquitous Autorickshaws are the backbone for travelling within the city. The city bus services are operated by both private as well as state owned entreprises.
