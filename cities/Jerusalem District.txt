The '''Jerusalem District''' ({{langx|he|מחוז ירושלים}}; {{langx|ar|منطقة القدس}}) is one of the [[Districts of Israel|six administrative districts]] of Israel. The district capital is [[Jerusalem]] and its total land area is 652&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>. The population of 1,159,900 is 66.3% [[Jews|Jewish]] and 32.1% [[Arab]].<ref name="cbs-populations" /> A fifth (21%) of the Arabs in Israel live in the Jerusalem Municipality, which includes both [[East Jerusalem|East]] and [[West Jerusalem]].<ref>[http://pewforum.org/future-of-the-global-muslim-population-israel.aspx The Future of the Global Muslim Population] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130725185300/http://www.pewforum.org/future-of-the-global-muslim-population-israel.aspx |date=2013-07-25 }}, PEW Forum</ref> [[Jerusalem Law|Israel's annexation]] of East Jerusalem has not been recognized by the international community.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-middle-east-31760946|title=Jerusalem: Israeli police hit in Palestinian car attack|work=[[BBC]]|date=6 March 2015}}</ref>

The majority of Arabs in the Jerusalem District are [[Palestinians]], eligible to apply for citizenship under Israeli law, but either decline to apply or are unsuccessful in doing so.{{Citation needed|date=June 2021}} [[Arab citizens of Israel]] constitute a significant minority in the district, living in [[Abu Ghosh]], [[Beit Safafa]] and [[East Jerusalem]], where Arab professionals have settled since the late 1970s, mainly for the provision of legal and other services to the local population.{{citation needed|date=June 2021}} The non-Jewish population is 95.2% [[Muslims|Muslim]], 3.5% [[Christians|Christian]] with the others unclassified by religion.<ref name="cbs-population-groups" />
