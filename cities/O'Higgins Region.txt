It is subdivided into three [[provinces of Chile|provinces]]. It is named in honour of [[Bernardo O'Higgins|Bernardo O'Higgins Riquelme]], one of Chile's [[founding father]]s.

The Libertador General Bernardo O'Higgins Region is bordered to the west by the Pacific Ocean, to the east by the Republic of [[Argentina]], to the north by the [[Valparaíso Region|Valparaíso]] and [[Santiago Metropolitan Region|Santiago Metropolitan]] Regions, and to the south by the [[Maule Region]]. It extends approximately between the parallels of 33°&thinsp;51' and 35°&thinsp;01' south latitude, and between the meridian of 70°&thinsp;02' west longitude and the Pacific Ocean.

The capital and largest city of the region is [[Rancagua]]. The second major town is [[San Fernando, Chile|San Fernando]].

==Geography==
[[File:O'Higgins Köppen.png|thumb|left|Köppen climate types in the O'Higgins Region.]]

In pre-[[Quaternary]] times extensive [[Nothofagus]] forests covered much of Libertador General Bernardo O'Higgins Region.<ref>Thomas T. Veblen, Robert S. Hill and Jennifer Read (1996) ''The Ecology and Biogeography of Nothofagus Forests'', Yale University Press, 403 pages  {{ISBN|0-300-06423-3}}</ref>

The Libertador General Bernardo O'Higgins Region is part of the very restricted range of the [[endangered]] [[Chilean Wine Palm]], ''[[Jubaea chilensis]]''; in prehistoric times this Chilean [[endemism|endemic]] tree had a significantly larger range.<ref>C. ==History==
From 9000 [[BCE]] to 300 BCE (the [[Archaic period in the Americas|Archaic Period]]), the humans who inhabited the region moved between the coast and the valley as well as the Andes. At sites such as [[Pichilemu]], [[Cáhuil]] and [[Bucalemu]], they left trash deposits or [[Midden#Shells|shell middens]] bearing testimony to their raids {{Citation needed|date=October 2007}}. During the [[Agroalfarero Period]] (300 BCE – 1470 [[BCE|CE]]), the inhabitants experienced changes in their way of life, the most important being the cultivation of vegetables and the manufacture of clay objects. From 600 CE onwards, they started cultivating beans, maize, [[Squash (plant)|squashes]], pumpkins and [[quinoa]]{{Citation needed|date=October 2007}}. All of these except [[quinoa]] and some types of maize required irrigation, which prompted them to move to the banks of creeks and rivers. During this period, groups of people lived in [[Quincha]] houses with straw roofs, in the vicinity of irrigation channels and horticulture crops, a style of life attributable to the [[Promaucaes]] or [[Picunches]] and to the [[Chiquillanes]]. During the [[Captaincy General of Chile|Colonial Period]] (1541 CE to 1811 CE), the region became dominated, like the rest of the country, by the Spanish, and a system of ranching became predominant {{Citation needed|date=October 2007}}.

==Demography==

The Libertador General Bernardo O'Higgins region contains a large part of the rural population (surpassed only by the [[Maule Region]]). Amongst the highly populated cities, [[Rancagua]] (206,971 inhabitants) stands out for having been transformed, in recent times, into an outskirt of Santiago. It is close being located 87&nbsp;km south of Santiago. It is the capital of the [[Cachapoal Province]] as well the Libertador General Bernardo O'Higgins Region. According to the census of 2002,<ref name="census2002">"[http://www.ine.cl/canales/chile_estadistico/demografia_y_vitales/proyecciones/Informes/Microsoft%20Word%20-%20InforP_T.pdf Chile: Proyecciones y Estimaciones de Población. Total País 1950–2050]" Instituto National de Estadísticas (INE)</ref> other densely populated cities are: [[San Fernando, Chile|San Fernando]] (49,519 inhabitants); [[Rengo, Chile|Rengo]] (30,891); [[Machalí]] (23,920); [[Graneros]] (21,616); [[San Vicente de Tagua Tagua]] (18,914); [[Santa Cruz, Chile|Santa Cruz]] (18,603); [[Chimbarongo]] (13,795); [[Pichilemu]] (12,392), a well-known beach resort city founded by [[Agustin Ross Edwards]] in the late 19th century; and [[San Francisco de Mostazal]] (12,037).

==Economy==
The main industrial and export activity takes place at CODELCO's [[El Teniente]] mine, which contributes 7.7% of Chile's copper production. The ore is processed at the [[Sewell, Chile|Sewell]] and [[Colón, Chile|Colón]] concentrator plants, smelted and refined at [[Caletones]], and shipped from the port of San Antonio, in the Valparaíso Region. Byproducts include [[molybdenum]] and silver.

Agriculture contributes 30.1% of the region's GDP. One out of every four hectares of fruit orchards in Chile is in the Libertador General Bernardo O'Higgins Region. The main crops are apples and pears, followed by table grapes, plums, kiwis, and nectarines.

Manufacturing activity in the region is mostly related to [[copper mining]], [[agribusiness]], and food and beverages. One particular growth area is the production of fruit juices and [[juice vesicles|pulp]], and dehydrated fruit.

Over the past few years, there has been significant development in the forestry sector, especially plantations of eucalyptus and radiata pine.

==Law and government==
{{Unsourced|section|date=July 2023}}
The region is co-governed by a presidential regional delegate, who is appointed by the [[List of presidents of Chile|president]] —currently independent [[Fabio López]]— and popularly-elected governor of O'Higgins, [[Pablo Silva Amaya]], who was elected in 2021 and will serve until 2025. <ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.goreohiggins.cl/gobernador-regional | title=Gobernador Regional }}</ref>

The administration of the region rests with the [[Regions of Chile|regional government]] which is headed by the regional governor and the regional council (''Consejo Regional de Chile''). The latter comprises twenty regional council members, who are popularly elected since 2013 (thirteen from [[Cachapoal Province|Cachapoal]], five from [[Colchagua Province|Colchagua]], and two from [[Cardenal Caro Province|Cardenal Caro]]).

===Administrative divisions===
For purposes of interior administration, the Libertador General Bernardo O'Higgins Region is divided into three provinces:
* '''[[Cachapoal Province]]''', capital: [[Rancagua]],
* '''[[Colchagua Province]]''', capital: [[San Fernando, Chile|San Fernando]],
* '''[[Cardenal Caro Province]]''', capital: [[Pichilemu]].

The provinces are subdivided into 33 [[List of communes in Chile|communes]].

==Culture==
This area is known as the "huaso province" after the name of the Chilean cowboy, the [[huaso]]. Sashes and mantas – traditional items of the huaso costume – are woven in [[Doñihue]] on heavy vertical looms. Designs imitate vine leaves, bunches of [[grapes]], [[pine]]s and [[copihues]]. Other designs of colored stripes are woven on horizontal looms.

The population is a mixture of both European (including [[Argentine]] immigrants) and [[Indigenous peoples in Chile|indigenous]] races and cultures, thus the region has a homogeneous culture known as ''Chileanidad'' is present and a [[mestizo]] imprint is evident.

The Libertador General Bernardo O'Higgins Region was settled by Spaniards (notably [[Andalusian people|Andalusians]], [[Basque people|Basques]], Aragonese and [[Navarre]]se) and other Europeans. [[French Chilean|French]] and [[Italian Chilean|Italian]] families established agriculture including the important [[Chilean wine|wine]] industry: [[the Wine Route]] is one of the main tourist attractions of the [[Colchagua valley]]. Breweries can be found as well, the legacy of [[German Chilean|German]] and [[Swiss Chilean|Swiss]] immigration. Livestock herding was especially influenced by [[British Chilean|British]], [[Greek Chilean|Greek]] and [[Yugoslavia]]n settlers.
