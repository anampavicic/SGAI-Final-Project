'''Tangier-Tetouan-Al Hoceima''' ({{langx|ar|طنجة - تطوان - الحسيمة|ṭanja - tiṭwān - al-ḥusayma}}) is the northernmost of the twelve [[regions of Morocco|regions of]] [[Morocco]]. It covers an area of 15,090&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> and recorded a population of 3,556,729 in the [[2014 Moroccan census]].<ref name=statoids/><ref name=census2014/> The capital of the region is [[Tangier]].<ref name=Bulletin>{{cite web |url=http://www.pncl.gov.ma/fr/EspaceJuridique/DocLib/d%C3%A9cret%20fixant%20le%20nombre%20des%20r%C3%A9gions.pdf |title=Décret fixant le nom des régions |date=20 February 2015 |access-date=11 December 2015 |work=Portail National des Collectivités Territoriales |language=fr |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150518083233/http://www.pncl.gov.ma/fr/EspaceJuridique/DocLib/d%C3%A9cret%20fixant%20le%20nombre%20des%20r%C3%A9gions.pdf |archive-date=18 May 2015 }}</ref>

==Geography==
[[Tanger]]-[[Tétouan]]-[[Al Hoceima]] is the northernmost of Morocco's twelve regions. In the north it faces the [[Strait of Gibraltar]] and the [[Mediterranean Sea]] and borders the Spanish [[exclave]] of [[Ceuta]]. It also borders the Moroccan regions of [[Rabat-Salé-Kénitra]] to the southwest, [[Fès-Meknès]] to the southeast and [[Oriental (Morocco)|Oriental]] to the east. The [[Rif]] rises in the eastern part of the region and is the location of [[Al Hoceima National Park]] and [[Talassemtane National Park]]. The land near the [[Atlantic Ocean|Atlantic]] coast in the west is less rugged, and the fertile southwestern corner of the region is drained by the [[Loukkos River]].

==History==
Tanger-Tetouan-Al Hoceima was formed in September 2015 by adding [[Al Hoceima Province]], formerly part of [[Taza-Al Hoceima-Taounate]] region, to the former [[Tangier-Tetouan]] region.<ref name=Bulletin />

==Economy==
[[Agriculture in Morocco|Agriculture]] is a major economic activity in the Tanger-Tetouan-Al Hoceima region. Major crops include cereals, pulses, olives and sugarcane. Animal husbandry and fishing are also important contributors to the [[Economy of Morocco|economy]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.apdn.ma/ancien%20portail/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=302&Itemid=286&lang=en&tmpl=component |title=General information on the agricultural sector |access-date=12 December 2015}}</ref> Various [[free economic zone]]s have been set up in the area surrounding the [[Tanger-Med]] [[container port]], one of Africa's [[List of world's busiest container ports|busiest]], to promote industrial growth and foreign investment.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.tangerfreezone.com/ |title=Tanger Med Zones |access-date=12 December 2015}}</ref> The region is also heavily reliant on tourism.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.moroccobusinessnews.com/Morocco/Tangier.asp |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080512135516/http://www.moroccobusinessnews.com/Morocco/Tangier.asp |url-status=usurped |archive-date=May 12, 2008 |title=Regions of Morocco: Tangier-Tetouan |publisher=Centre marocain de conjoncture |access-date=12 December 2015}}</ref>

==Infrastructure==
The [[Rabat–Tangier expressway|A1 expressway]] connects Tangier with the national capital [[Rabat]]. There are also two shorter expressways in the region: the A4 bypasses Tangier and links the A1 expressway to the Tanger-Med port, and the [[Tetouan–Fnideq expressway|A6]] connects [[Tetouan]] with [[M'Diq]] and [[Fnideq]]. [[National Route 2 (Morocco)|National Route 2]] connects Tangier and Tetouan with Al Hoceima via an inland route, while [[National Route 16 (Morocco)|National Route 16]] provides a coastal route between the same cities. [[National Route 13 (Morocco)|National Route 13]] branches off the N2 near [[Chefchaouen]] and runs south via [[Ouezzane]] to [[Meknes]] and beyond.

Tangier's [[Tangier Ibn Battouta Airport|Ibn Battouta Airport]] is the [[List of the busiest airports in Morocco|busiest]] airport in the region. Al Hoceima's [[Cherif Al Idrissi Airport]] and Tetouan's [[Sania Ramel Airport]] also receive commercial flights.<ref>{{cite news |date=26 November 2015 |title=Région Tanger-Tétouan-Al Hoceima: Plus de 743.000 passagers ont transité via les aéroports à fin octobre 2015 |url=http://lavieeco.com/news/actualite-maroc/region-tanger-tetouan-al-hoceima-plus-de-743-000-passagers-ont-transite-via-les-aeroports-a-fin-octobre-2015.html |language=fr |newspaper=[[La Vie Éco]] |access-date=12 December 2015}}</ref>

The first phase of the [[Casablanca–Tangier high-speed rail line]] was opened between Tangier and [[Kenitra]] in November 2018.<ref>{{cite news |last=Alaoui |first=Mohamed Chakir |url=http://fr.le360.ma/economie/lgv-voici-la-date-de-mise-en-service-du-tgv-marocain-al-boraq-178827 |title=LGV: voici la date de mise en service du TGV marocain, Al Boraq |language=fr |publisher=Le360 |date=19 November 2018 |access-date=13 December 2018}}</ref>
