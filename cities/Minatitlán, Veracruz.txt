'''Minatitlán''' is a city in the [[List of states of Mexico|Mexican state]] of [[Veracruz]]. It is located in the southeastern part of the state, in the [[Olmeca Region|Olmeca region]], to the north of the [[Isthmus of Tehuantepec]], along the [[Coatzacoalcos River]].

In 2010 the greater metropolitan area had a population of 356,020.<ref name="censo">{{Cite web|url=http://www.censo2010.org.mx/doc/cpv10p_00.pdf|title=Censo 2010, ZM de Minatitlán|format=pdf|year=2010|access-date=25 September 2011|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110912021151/http://www.censo2010.org.mx/doc/cpv10p_00.pdf|archive-date=12 September 2011}}</ref>

Minatitlán is home to the ''Refinería Gral. Lázaro Cárdenas del Río'' (now named for President [[Lázaro Cárdenas]]) a 1906 [[oil refinery]] that was the first such facility built in [[Latin America]].<ref name="pemex">{{Cite web|url=http://www.ref.pemex.com/octanaje/23laza.htm|title=La primera refinería de Latinoamérica Refinería Gral. Lázaro Cárdenas|publisher=[[Pemex]]|language=es|access-date=25 September 2011|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120525084449/http://www.ref.pemex.com/octanaje/23laza.htm|archive-date=25 May 2012}}</ref>  The refinery underwent an expansion that started in 2003 to bring the capacity of the plant up to 240,000 barrels per day, up from its previous capacity of 185,000 barrels per day.<ref name="rosenberg">{{Cite web|last=Rosenberg|first=Mica|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/mexico-oil-idUSN1227600820110113|title=UPDATE 1-Mexico's Pemex restarts Minatitlan refinery|publisher=[[Reuters]]|date=12 January 2011|access-date=25 September 2011}}</ref><ref name="barrera">{{Cite web|last=Barrera|first=Adriana|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/mexico-oil-idUSN1E75J1VD20110620|title=UPDATE 1-Mexico's Pemex says Minatitlan expansion ready end-Aug|publisher=[[Reuters]]|date=20 June 2011|access-date=25 September 2011}}</ref>

It is a sister city of [[Minatitlán, Colima|Minatitlán]], in the state of [[Colima]], on the other side of the country.
The local economy is largely dependent on the oil industry and trade.

The Feria del Café y Minería (Coffee and Mining Fair) is held each year in January.<ref name="rincones23">{{cite book |editor1-first=Victor Manuel |editor1-last=Jiménez González |title=Veracruz Guia para descubrir los encantos del estado |trans-title=Veracruz Guide to discover the charms of the state |edition=first |year=2010 |publisher=Editorial Oceano de Mexico SA de CV |location=Mexico |language=es |isbn=978-607-400-323-9 |page=23 }}</ref>

== Sports ==
The [[Petroleros de Minatitlán]] (Minatitlán Oilers) play baseball in the [[Mexican League]].  The Gavilanes de Minatitlán (Minatitlán Hawks) play in the [[Veracruz Winter League]]. Both teams' homefield is the [[Parque 18 de marzo de 1938]].

==Geography==
The city is located in the [[Olmec]] region of the state and the north of the [[Isthmus of Tehuantepec]].

The municipality covers an area of {{convert|4123.91|km2|sqmi|sp=us}} and includes many small outlying [[communities]].

Much of the city sits on reclaimed wetlands, and many new homes built on this reclaimed land have a tendency to sink up to several meters before settling. Much of the surrounding undeveloped land is marshy, especially towards the northeast direction of [[Coatzacoalcos]].
