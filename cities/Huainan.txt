'''Huainan'''<!--Chinese in infobox; see WP:MOS-ZH--> ({{Zh|c=淮南|p=Huáinán}}) is a [[prefecture-level city]] with 3,033,528 inhabitants as of the 2020 census in north-central [[Anhui]] province, China. It is named for the [[Han dynasty|Han]]-era [[Principality of Huainan]]. It borders the provincial capital of [[Hefei]] to the south, [[Lu'an]] to the southwest, [[Fuyang]] to the west, [[Bozhou]] to the northwest, [[Bengbu]] to the northeast and [[Chuzhou]] to the east. Huainan is one of the core cities of Hefei Metropolitan Circle<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://xxgk.ah.gov.cn/UserData/DocHtml/731/2016/12/15/572807095431.html |title=安徽省人民政府网——安徽省人民政府办公厅关于印发长江三角洲 城市群发展规划安徽实施方案的通知 |access-date=2018-04-10 |archive-date=2018-07-02 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180702011113/http://xxgk.ah.gov.cn/UserData/DocHtml/731/2016/12/15/572807095431.html |url-status=dead }}</ref> and is known for its coal industry and [[thermal power plants]]. Its built-up area made of 4 urban districts (all but Panji not yet conurbated) and Fengtai County largely being urbanized, was home to 1,868,944 inhabitants as of 2020. Its city flower is the Chinese rose (''[[Rosa chinensis]]'') and its city tree is the Old-World Plane Tree (''[[Platanus orientalis]]''). It is also considered to be the hometown and birthplace of [[tofu]].<ref name="ShurtleffAoyagi2013">{{cite book|author1=William Shurtleff|author2=Akiko Aoyagi|title=History of Tofu and Tofu Products (965 CE to 2013)|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=gGrUNvZt0_YC&pg=PA55|date=May 2013|publisher=Soyinfo Center|isbn=978-1-928914-55-6|page=55}}</ref>

The [[prefecture-level city]] of Huainan administers seven [[Administrative divisions of the People's Republic of China#County level|county-level divisions]], including five [[District of China|districts]] and two [[County (People's Republic of China)|counties]].

*[[Tianjia'an District]] ({{lang|zh-hans|田家庵区}})
*[[Panji District]] ({{lang|zh-hans|潘集区}})
*[[Xiejiaji District]] ({{lang|zh-hans|谢家集区}})
*[[Datong District, Huainan|Datong District]] ({{lang|zh-hans|大通区}})
*[[Bagongshan District]] ({{lang|zh-hans|八公山区}})
*[[Fengtai County]] ({{lang|zh-hans|凤台县}})
*[[Shou County]] ({{lang|zh-hans|寿县}})

These are further divided into 66 [[Administrative divisions of the People's Republic of China#Township level|township-level divisions]], including 24 [[town of China|towns]], 23 [[Township (People's Republic of China)|townships]] and 19 [[Subdistricts of China|subdistrict]]s.

==Geography==
The urban centre is located on a plain on the south bank of the [[Huai River]], bordering [[Gaotang Lake]] on the east and a forested hill area on the south. To the west are [[Bagong Mountain Scenic Spot|Bagongshan District]] and [[Shou County]].

==Economy==
[[File:安徽省淮南市优山美地小区公寓楼 - panoramio - luchangjiang~鲁昌江 (1).jpg|thumb|High rise apartments, 2010]]
Huainan is a major production center for [[coal]], with an output of 43.28 million tons in 2006.

The city hosted the 17th China Tofu Cultural Festival on September 15–17, 2010, including the National Bean Products Exhibition.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://english.anhuinews.com/system/2010/09/16/003356173.shtml |title=The 17th Huainan Tofu Festival Draws Curtain-Tofu,Festival,-ÖÐ°²ÔÚÏß-english |access-date=2010-10-08 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101205152717/http://english.anhuinews.com/system/2010/09/16/003356173.shtml |archive-date=2010-12-05 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Transportation==
East of the city a bridge crosses the Huai River, shared by the [[Fuyang-Huainan Railway]] and highway S225. Near the city center, a ferry provides connection to the (rural) north bank of the Huai River.
