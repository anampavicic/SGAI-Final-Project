== Government ==
In the local elections of March 2019 [[Nilüfer Elik Yılmaz]] was elected as Mayor.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.yenisafak.com/yerel-secim-2019/mardin-kiziltepe-ilcesi-secim-sonuclari|title=Mardin Kızıltepe Seçim Sonuçları – Kızıltepe Yerel Seçim Sonuçları|last=Şafak|first=Yeni|date=2019-11-17|website=Yeni Şafak|language=tr-TR|access-date=2019-11-16}}</ref> But on 15 November 2019 she was dismissed and a trustee was appointed.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://ipa.news/2019/11/05/turkey-removes-15th-pro-kurdish-mayor-since-march-over-alleged-terror-links/|title=Turkey removes 15th pro-Kurdish mayor since March over alleged terror links|date=2019-11-05|website=IPA NEWS|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-16}}</ref> The current District Governor is Huseyn Cam, who was also appointed as the state appointed trustee.<ref name=":0" />

== History ==
The town has a historic 13th century [[Ulucami|great Friday mosque]] built by the [[Artuqids]].

On 1 July 1915, during the [[1915 genocide in Diyarbekir]], there was a massacre in the village where its Christian population, the Assyrians were murdered by militia and Kurds. About seventy women were raped in the church, then put to death. Men, women, and children were killed indiscriminately and many victims were decapitated. After the massacre, Kurdish women stabbed any survivors to death. [[Rafael de Nogales]] visited weeks later and found "corpses barely covered with heaps of stone from which emerged here and there a bloody tress or an arm or leg gnawed on by hyenas".<ref>{{Cite book|last1=Üngör |first1=Uğur Ümit|chapter=How Armenian was the 1915 Genocide? |title=Let Them Not Return: Sayfo - The Genocide Against the Assyrian, Syriac, and Chaldean Christians in the Ottoman Empire|year=2017|publisher=Berghahn Books|page=39|isbn=978-1-78533-499-3}}</ref>

In the late 1980s there existed a refugee camp for Kurds who fled persecution by [[Saddam Hussein]].<ref>{{Cite book|title=United States Policy Toward Iraq: Human Rights, Weapons Proliferation, and International Law : Hearing Before the Committee on Foreign Relations, United States Senate, One Hundred First Congress, Second Session, June 15, 1990|last=Relations|first=United States Congress Senate Committee on Foreign|date=1990|publisher=U.S. Government Printing Office|location=Washington D.C.|pages=67|language=en}}</ref>

It was also the scene of clashes between protesting [[Kurdish people|Kurds]] and Turkish riot police in 2006.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/4867934.stm|title=Turkey warns children off clashes|date=April 2006}}</ref> In Kiziltepe have been imposed curfews in the past.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://m.bianet.org/bianet/world/158990-curfew-in-6-southeastern-cities|title=Curfew in 6 Southeastern Cities|website=Bianet|access-date=16 November 2019}}</ref>

Kızıltepe, with +48.8&nbsp;°C (119.84&nbsp;°F) on August 14, 1993, holds the record for the highest temperature ever recorded in Turkey.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mgm.gov.tr/genel/sss.aspx?s=sicaklikenleri2 |title = Sıkça Sorulan Sorular - Meteoroloji Genel Müdürlüğü}}</ref>
