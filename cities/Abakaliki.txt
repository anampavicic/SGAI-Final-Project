'''Abakaliki''' is the capital city of [[Ebonyi State]] in southeastern [[Nigeria]], located {{convert|64|km|mi}} southeast of [[Enugu]]. <ref name="cohen">{{harvnb|Cohen|1998|p=2}}</ref> The inhabitants are primarily members of the [[Igbo (people)|Igbo]] nation.<ref name=eb>{{harvnb|Hoiberg|2010|p=7}}</ref> It was the headquarters of the [[Ogoja]] province before the creation of the Southeastern State in 1967.
[[File:Abakaliki metropolis with Azugwu Hill in background 03 (cropped).jpg|thumb|Abakaliki metropolis with Azugwu Hill in background 03 (cropped)]]

==Etymology==
The name Abakaliki originally means 'Aba Nkaleke' and is the name of a community in Izzi land (Nkaleke).

==History==
Abakaliki was an important center for the slave trade in the 17th century.<ref>{{harvnb|Oriji|2011|pp=107–108}}</ref> The slave trade continued in the area with [[Aro People|Aro]] raids into Abakaliki and surrounding areas through the 18th century.<ref>{{harvnb|Oriji|2011|p=126}}</ref>

The [[Odozi Obodo (cult)|Odozi Obodo Society]] was a secret cult that operated between 1954 and 1958 in Abakaliki.<ref name=evboise>{{cite magazine |last=Evboise |date=June 1969 |title=Odozi Obodo|magazine=Spear |location=Lagos |publisher=Daily Times }}</ref>

[[File:Old Abakaliki.png|thumb|center|900px|Old Abakaliki, ca.1910]]

==Economy==
Abakaliki, as in the past, is a center of agricultural trade including such products as [[yam (vegetable)|yam]]s, [[cassava]], [[rice]], and both [[palm oil]] and [[palm kernel]]s,.<ref name=eb/> It is also known for its local [[lead]], [[zinc]], [[salt]], and [[limestone]] mining or quarrying.<ref name=eb/><ref name=cohen/> They host a golf course and many hotels. There are also isolated [[poultry]] and [[egg]] production farms across the state.<ref name=eb/>

==Population==
The last known population of Abakaliki was 223,000 (year 2022). The population of the metro area was estimated to be 662,000 (year 2023).<ref>{{Cite web |title=Abakaliki, Nigeria Metro Area Population 1950-2024 |url=https://www.macrotrends.net/cities/206382/abakaliki/population |access-date=2023-07-02}}</ref>

===Demographics===
Abakaliki is generally populated by the Igbo people. Abakaliki is predominantly populated by the Northeastern Igbo of the Afikpo-Abakaliki axis.<ref>{{harvnb|Oriji|2011|p=10}}</ref> Abakaliki is also use to refer to people of old Abakaliki political block comprising Ohaukwu-Ishielu-Izzi-Ezza-Ikwo.

== Climate ==
 
The dry season in Abakaliki is hot, muggy, and partially cloudy while the wet season is warm, oppressive, and overcast. The temperature rarely falls below 58°F or rises over 92°F throughout the year, often fluctuating between 65°F and 89°F.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Abakaliki Climate, Weather By Month, Average Temperature (Nigeria) - Weather Spark |url=https://weatherspark.com/y/58525/Average-Weather-in-Abakaliki-Nigeria-Year-Round |access-date=2023-07-15 |website=weatherspark.com |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Abakaliki, Ebonyi, NG Climate Zone, Monthly Averages, Historical Weather Data |url=https://tcktcktck.org/nigeria/ebonyi/abakaliki#:~:text=Abakaliki%20Climate%20Summary&text=The%20district%27s%20yearly%20temperature%20is,%25%20of%20the%20time)%20annually. |access-date=2023-07-15 |website=tcktcktck.org}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Abakaliki Annual Weather Averages |url=https://www.worldweatheronline.com/abakaliki-weather/ebonyi/ng.aspx |access-date=2023-07-15 |website=WorldWeatherOnline.com |language=en}}</ref>

== Infrastructure ==
Abakaliki lies at the intersection of the [[Enugu]], [[Afikpo]] and [[Ogoja]] Roads.<ref name=eb/> Abakaliki also hosts a Federal [[hospital]],<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-06-25|title=Ebonyi rates highest on drug abuse, says official|url=https://www.vanguardngr.com/2021/06/ebonyi-rates-highest-on-drug-abuse-says-official/|access-date=2021-06-27|website=Vanguard News|language=en-US}}</ref> which has largely contributed to the affordability of public healthcare delivery in the city and the state. There has been massive infrastructural developments ongoing in the urban center; these include road construction, shopping malls and market places, trans-Sahara fly-over bridges at presco and spera-in-deo junctions amongst others.

===Education===
[[Ebonyi State University]] main campus is located on the outskirts of the city.

==Religion==

Abakaliki people like other southeastern Nigerians are predominantly Christians. Other religious faith like Traditionalist, Islam, etc. are practiced by handful of the natives as well as non-natives from other parts of the country. Roman Catholic, Presbyterian, Anglican and other Pentecostal missions are the dominant Christian faiths. On March 1, 1973, the city was made the seat of the [[Roman Catholic Diocese of Abakaliki]].<ref>{{Cite book|last=Steensel|first=Nico van|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=slYuAQAAIAAJ&q=1973+Roman+Catholic+Diocese+of+Abakaliki+-wikipedia|title=The Izi: Their History and Customs|date=1996|publisher=Abakaliki Literacy and Translation Committee|language=en}}</ref>
