'''Datu Odin Sinsuat''', officially the '''Municipality of Datu Odin Sinsuat''' ([[Maguindanao language|Maguindanaon]]: ''Inged nu Datu Odin Sinsuat''; [[Jawi script|Jawi]]: داتو أودين سينسوات; {{lang-tl|Bayan ng Datu Odin Sinsuat}}), is a {{#ifeq:{{#property:P1879}}|no value|{{void}}|2nd class}} [[municipality of the Philippines|municipality]] and capital of the [[Philippine Province|province]] of [[Maguindanao del Norte]], [[Philippines]]. According to the 2020 census, it has a population of 116,768 people.{{PH census|current}}

The town's name was formerly known as '''Dinaig'''. It was changed to Datu Odin Sinsuat in 1994, by virtue of ''Muslim Mindanao Autonomy Act No. 29''.<ref name=RLA-ARMMGovPH>{{cite web|title=Regional Act No. 3; An Act Changing the Name of the Municipality of Dinaig in the Province of Maguindanao to the Municipality of Datu Odin Sinsuat|url=http://rla-armmgov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/MMAA-29.pdf|website=Regional Legislative Assembly, Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao|access-date=27 January 2016}}</ref>

The municipality is home to the [[Cotabato Airport|Awang Domestic Airport]] that serves the province and [[Cotabato City]].

==History==
Datu Odin Sinsuat (Dinaig) was organized as a municipality through Executive Order No. 82 signed by President [[Manuel Roxas]] on August 18, 1947.<ref>{{cite web |title=Executive Order No. 82, s. 1947 |url=https://www.officialgazette.gov.ph/1947/08/18/executive-order-no-82-s-1847/ |website=Official Gazette of the Republic of the Philippines |access-date=31 January 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170822180507/https://www.officialgazette.gov.ph/1947/08/18/executive-order-no-82-s-1847/ |archive-date=August 22, 2017 |date=August 18, 1947}}</ref> In 1955, the barrio of Upi was separated from Datu Odin Sinsuat to become the town of [[Upi, Maguindanao|Upi]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://lawph.com/statutes/ra1248-creating-the-municipality-of-upi,-cotabato.html|title=An Act to Create the Municipality of Upi in the Province of Cotabato|publisher=LawPH.com|access-date=2011-04-11}}</ref>

The town was part of the province of [[Shariff Kabunsuan]] and served as its capital from October 2006 until its nullification by the Supreme Court in July 2008.

In 2023, there were proposals to create two new municipalities out of Datu Odin Sinsuat, which was met with opposition from town officials, who suggested the town be converted into a component city instead.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Rommel |first1=Rebollido |title=Maguindanao del Norte’s largest town faces split or city upgrade as legislators clash |url=https://www.rappler.com/nation/mindanao/datu-odin-sinsuat-maguindanao-del-norte-faces-split-city-upgrade-legislators-clash/ |access-date=11 April 2024 |work=Rappler |date=13 July 2023}}</ref> On {{date|2023|12|20}}, the Bangsamoro parliament approved Parliament Bill Nos. 190 and 191, both seeking to carve the proposed municipalities of Datu Balabaran Sinsuat and Sheik Abas Hamza from Datu Odin Sinsuat; the measure gained mixed reactions, with United Bangsamoro Justice Party (UBJP) and their supporters favoring and town officials and their supporters opposing.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://newsinfo.inquirer.net/1878182/barmm-parliament-okays-split-of-2-maguindanao-del-norte-towns |title=BARMM parliament OKs split of 2 Maguindanao del Norte towns |last1=Fernandez |first1=Edwin |last2=Dela Cruz |first2=Sheila Mae |website=Inquirer.net |date=22 December 2023 |access-date=11 April 2024}}</ref> However, the vote was shelved after the [[Supreme Court of the Philippines|Supreme Court]] ruled on August 20, 2024 that provisions of the enabling act passed by the [[Bangsamoro]] government for the plebiscite were unconstitutional, particularly allowing only voters from barangays of the would-be municipalities to participate in the exercise.<ref>{{cite news |title=SC rules vs BARMM laws, stops plebiscites for creation of 3 new towns |url=https://www.rappler.com/philippines/mindanao/sc-rules-barmm-laws-stops-plebiscites-creation-of-new-towns-maguindanao-del-norte-august-20-2024/ |access-date=21 August 2024 |publisher=[[Rappler]] |date=August 21, 2024}}</ref>

== Tourism ==
[[File:Amaya Beach Resort Maguindanao.jpg|thumbnail|right|Amaya Beach Resort at Barangay Kusiong]]
* '''Mount Minandar''' is known for its green color due to being dominantly covered with grass. It is a two-hour [[Hiking|hike]] from the foot to the summit of the mountain. It is located at the Barangay kusiong.
* '''Blue Lagoon''' is also known as Enchanted Lagoon because, according to the old people living nearby, there are [[Magician (paranormal)|enchantress]]es living in it. This deep lagoon situated at the Barangay Margues is the top tourist attraction of the municipality.
* '''Kusiong Beach''' is a grayish sand beach with a lot of resorts like Amaya Beach Resort, Sahara Beach Resort, and the newest Precious Cabana Resort. This is the nearest beach in [[Cotabato City]].
* '''Masjid Datu Untong Balabaran''' of Taviran is a native pagoda-style [[mosque]], one of the last pagoda-style mosques in the Philippines.
