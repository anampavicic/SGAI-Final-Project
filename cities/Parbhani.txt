'''Parbhani''' ({{IAST|parbhani}}, {{IPA-mr|pəɾbʰəɳiː|-|LL-Q1571 (mar)-SangeetaRH-परभणी.wav}}) is a city in [[Maharashtra]] state of India. It is the administrative headquarters of [[Parbhani District]]. Parbhani is one of the largest cities in [[Marathwada]] region. Parbhani is around {{convert|200|km}} away from regional headquarters of [[Aurangabad, Maharashtra|Aurangabad]] while it is {{cvt|491|km}} away from the state capital [[Mumbai]].
 
Along with the entire Marathwada region, Parbhani was a part of the erstwhile [[Nizam State]]; later a part of [[Hyderabad State]]; after [[States Reorganisation Act, 1956|reorganization of states in 1956]] it became a part of the then-[[Bombay state]]; since 1960, it has been part of the present Maharashtra state.<ref name=pbnnic>{{cite web|url=http://parbhani.nic.in/htmldocs/Overview.html|title=OVERVIEW OF PARBHANI DISTRICT|website=Parbhani.nic.in|access-date=19 April 2017|archive-date=1 May 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170501174845/http://www.parbhani.nic.in/htmldocs/Overview.html|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Parbhani is home to [[Vasantrao Naik Marathwada Agricultural University]], which is one of [[List of agricultural universities in India#Maharashtra|only four agriculture universities in Maharashtra]]. Moreover, Parbhani also has an annual festival at [[Turabul Haq Dargah]], which attracts lakhs of tourists each year.<ref name=Britannica>{{cite web |title= Parbhani, India |url=http://www.britannica.com/place/Parbhani|website= Encyclopædia Britannica |access-date=19 July 2015 }}</ref><ref name=esakal>{{cite news |title= सय्यद शाह तुराबुल हक साहेब (फोटो फिचर) |url= http://www.esakal.com/esakal/20110204/4667279271518338312.htm |access-date= 19 July 2015 |newspaper= [[Sakal]] |language= mr |url-status= dead |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20150924002158/http://www.esakal.com/esakal/20110204/4667279271518338312.htm |archive-date= 24 September 2015}}</ref> Parbhani is named after Goddess Prabhavati.

==Etymology, and history==
In ancient times, Parbhani was known as "''Prabhavati nagari''"<ref>{{cite web|title=Parbhani at a glance|url=http://parbhani.nic.in/htmldocs/Overview.html|website=parbhani.nic.in|access-date=1 April 2017|archive-date=29 March 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170329033822/http://parbhani.nic.in/htmldocs/Overview.html|url-status=dead}}</ref> ({{lang-mr|प्रभावतीनगरी}}) on account of the existence of a massive temple of Goddess Prabhavati. The name "''Prabhavati''" means goddess [[Lakshmi]] and [[Parvati]].<ref>{{cite web |title= Meaning of Prabhavati |url=http://www.bachpan.com/Meaning-of-Prabhavathi.aspx |website= bachpan.com |access-date= 23 July 2015}}</ref> Present name Parbhani is a corrupt form of Prabhavati.<ref>{{cite web|title=Parbhani District Gazetteers chapter 1|url=https://cultural.maharashtra.gov.in/english/gazetteer/parbhani/chapters/general/general.htm|website=Cultural.maharashtra.gov.in|access-date=23 July 2015}}</ref>

Parbhani was for over 650 years under Muslim rule, under the [[Deccan sultanates]], the [[Mughals]] and later the [[Nizam of Hyderabad]]. The town remained a part of the [[Hyderabad State|Princely State of Hyderabad]] under the rulership of the Nizam until [[Operation Polo]] of the Indian Army in 1948.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://indianarmy.nic.in/Site/FormTemplete/frmTempSimple.aspx?MnId=nmnz/S66ueKkrJc8PBO1kw==&ParentID=z2xdy5FtH8G+oZz4hw/CKg== |title=Official Website of Indian Army |website=Indianarmy.nic.in |access-date=2017-04-18}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hoparoundindia.com/maharashtra/history-of-parbhani.aspx |title=History of Parbhani City Maharashtra Origin-Background-Significance |website=Hoparoundindia.com |access-date=2017-04-18}}</ref> Thereafter it became part of the independent Republic of India.<ref name="MetcalfMetcalf2006">{{cite book|author1=Barbara D. Metcalf|author2=Thomas R. Metcalf|title=A Concise History of India|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=jGCBNTDv7acC|year=2006|edition=2nd|publisher=Cambridge University Press|isbn=978-0521682251}}</ref> Until 1956 the town remained a part of Hyderabad State within India. Under the administrative reforms that year and the break-up of the [[State of Hyderabad]], Parbhani and the adjacent towns were transferred to the multilingual [[Bombay]] State.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.britannica.com/place/Aurangabad |title=Aurangabad &#124; India |website=Encyclopædia Britannica |date=2014-09-18 |access-date=2017-04-18}}</ref> Since 1960 it has been a part of [[State of Maharashtra]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Parbhani, Nizam, and post 1947|url=http://parbhani.nic.in/htmldocs/Overview.html|website=parbhani.nic.in|access-date=1 April 2017|archive-date=29 March 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170329033822/http://parbhani.nic.in/htmldocs/Overview.html|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Demographics==
According to the 2011 census, the population of Parbhani city is 307,170. Male and female populations are 157,628 and 149,563 respectively, a ratio of 949 females per 1000 males. The average literacy rate of Parbhani city is 84.34 per cent (225,298 people), with male literacy at 90.71 per cent and female at 77.70 per cent. According to the census report, population of children (aged 0–6 years) in Parbhani city is a total of 40,075 of which 21,187 are males and 18,888 are females, a ratio of 981 females per 1000 males.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.census2011.co.in/census/city/358-parbhani.html |title=City Population Census 2011 &#124; |publisher=Census2011.co.in |access-date=2015-05-29}}</ref>

[[Hinduism]] forms a plurality in Parbhani city with 138,562 followers. [[Islam]] is the second major religion in the city of Parbhani with approximately 126,702 following it and [[Buddhism]] by 36,203, Christianity is followed by 697, Jainism by 2,870.<ref name="districtcensus">{{cite web |title=District Census Hand Book – Parbhani |url=https://censusindia.gov.in/nada/index.php/catalog/818/download/2921/DH_2011_2717_PART_A_DCHB_PARBHANI.pdf |website=[[Census of India]] |publisher=[[Registrar General and Census Commissioner of India]]}}</ref>

==Cleanliness==
According to the "Swacch Surveskshan 2018" (a cleanliness survey) under [[Swachh Bharat Abhiyan]], Parbhani city was ranked under "greatest improvement over last year" category. Parbhani was one of the three cities of Maharashtra state to get the rank, along with [[Bhusawal]], and [[Bhiwandi]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://indianexpress.com/article/cities/mumbai/swachh-survekshan-2018-rankings-tags-mumbai-as-cleanest-state-capital-5179690/|title=Swachh Survekshan 2018 rankings: Mumbai cleanest state capital}}</ref> In the same survey Parbhani was declared India's best city in giving citizen feedback.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thehindu.com/news/cities/mumbai/surprise-city-ranked-1-in-cleanliness-survey/article23906836.ece|title=Surprise: city ranked #1 in cleanliness survey|date=17 May 2018|work=The Hindu}}</ref>

==Geography, and climate==
[[File:Parbhani city, Maharashtra - around dusk.jpg|thumb|left|Parbhani after sunset]]
Parbhani is located at {{Coord|19.27|N|76.78|E}}.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fallingrain.com/world/IN/16/Parbhani.html |title=Maps, Weather, and Airports for Parbhani, India |website=Fallingrain.com |access-date=2017-04-19}}</ref> It has an average elevation of 347 metres.

[[Parbhani district]] is uniformly laid by the Deccan lava flows in the form of horizontally bedded sheets, similar to Deccan trap formation of basaltic composition.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mahaforest.gov.in/fckimagefile/Parbhani%20Note.pdf |title=Result of Sample Tree Enumeration of Parbhani Forest Division |website=Mahaforest.gov.in |access-date=2017-04-19}}</ref> Being in Godavari Valley, city's soil is very fertile, deep and rich in nutrients.<ref name="auto2">{{cite web|url=http://mahaagri.gov.in/level3PdfDisp.aspx?Id=5&subid=15&sub2id=21&FileName=chapter+2.pdf |title=EXECUTIVE SUMMARY OF PARBHANI DISTRICT |website=Mahaagri.gov.in |access-date=2017-04-19}}</ref> <!-- page number 18 -->

==Education==
{{See also|List of educational institutions in Parbhani district}}
In the fields of academics, the city is known mainly for the Agriculture University, and because of the university it is also known for the preparation of competitive exams for civil services like [[Maharashtra Public Service Commission]], and [[Union Public Service Commission]].

===Agricultural University===
{{Main|Vasantrao Naik Marathwada Agricultural University}}
[[File:University gate Parbhani 1 (cropped).jpg|thumb|right|Vasantrao Naik Marathwada Agricultural University]]
The history of agriculture research in Parbhani dates back to [[British Raj|pre-independence era]]. It began with the commencement of "Main Experimental Farm" of [[Hyderabad State|erstwhile Nizam State]] in 1918. However, agricultural education was available only at Hyderabad but crop research centres for sorghum, cotton, fruits among few others existed in Parbhani.<ref name="vnmkv1">{{cite web |url=http://www.vnmkv.ac.in/doe/maufrgen.html|title=General Information |website=Vnmkv.ac.in |access-date=2017-04-19 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170506141754/http://vnmkv.ac.in/doe/maufrgen.html |archive-date=6 May 2017 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

The first college of Agriculture was established in this region at Parbhani in 1956 by Hyderabad State Government just before the Maharashtra State reorganization. The current day [[Vasantrao Naik Marathwada Agricultural University]] (VNMAU) was established on 18 May 1972 as "Marathwada Agriculture University" to fulfill the regional aspirations of agrarian growth, with further responsibilities to provide education in agriculture and allied fields, and to undertake research and facilitate technology transfer in Marathwada region.<ref name="vnmkv1"/> It is one of the only four Agricultural Universities in the State of Maharashtra.

Since the 1970s, Parbhani has proved itself as the hub of educational, research, and extension activities.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.icar.org.in/en/node/960|title=Convocation address by Dr Mangala Rai, Secretary, DARE & Director General, ICAR at Marathwada Agricultural University, Parbhani – Indian Council of Agricultural Research|website=Icar.org.in|access-date=19 April 2017|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170405073528/http://www.icar.org.in/en/node/960|archive-date=5 April 2017}}</ref> The famous 'Gaorani' cotton, a breed of Indian cotton, is the result of research facilities at Parbhani.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://agropedia.iitk.ac.in/content/marathwada-agricultural-university |title=Marathwada Agricultural University &#124; agropedia |website=Agropedia.iitk.ac.in |date=2009-08-20 |access-date=2017-04-18}}</ref> The university was renamed after the name of former Chief Minister of Maharashta, [[Vasantrao Naik]], in 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mumbaimirror.indiatimes.com/mumbai/other//articleshow/20672650.cms |title=Marathwada agriculture university to be renamed after Vasantrao Naik|website=The Times of India |date=2013-06-20 |access-date=2017-04-18}}</ref>

"College of Veterinary and Animal Sciences" is located on the campus of VNMAU which is one of the constituent Veterinary Colleges coming under the authority of [[Maharashtra Animal and Fishery Sciences University]], Nagpur from 2000. Earlier this college was established in 1972 as a part of Marathwada Agriculture University.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mkv2.mah.nic.in/doe/maufrgen.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120210052825/http://mkv2.mah.nic.in/doe/maufrgen.html|title=General Information|url-status=dead|archive-date=10 February 2012|date=10 February 2012}}</ref>

==Religion, and culture==
{{Main|Tourism in Marathwada}}
[[File:Parbhani dargah festival at night.jpg|right|thumb|Annual [[Turabul Haq Dargah|Parbhani Dargah Festival]]]]
Temples in Parbhani include Shree Motha Maruti a temple of [[Lord Hanuman]], Shree Supari Hanuman Mandir, Jabreshwar, Ashtbhuja Mandir, Pardeshwar Temple and Beleshwar Temple of Lord Mahadev, Shri Siddhivinayak Ganpati Temple of [[lord Ganesha]], Nagraj Temple, and a temple of [[Hinglaj Mata|Maata Hingulambika.]] There are some more temples in the interior of Parbhani those are, Mudgaleshwar Temple at Mudgal- a village in Manwat, Pingaleshwar Temple in Pingli, Dharasur has some ancient temples. Gangakhed is known for its river and is the birthplace of Sant Janabai.

[[Turabul Haq Dargah]] (Turatpir) is located in Parbhani Town.<ref name="esakal" /> The Dargah consists of the tomb of Turabul Haq Shah, a Muslim saint who lived here during the last days of his life. The Dargah is famous for annual [[Urs]], the death anniversary. Urs is an important festival where people of all religions come together and celebrate for a week (usually start from 2 to 15 February). Dargah of Qurban Ali shah, dargah of Akhund Shah and dargah of many other saints are also situated in the city.

Churches in Parbhani Include Christ Gospel Church, Church of the Nazarene, Good News Church, New Apostolic Church.

==Healthcare==
===Private Hospitals===
Essential basic needs of healthcare are present, as well as high-end facilities. A number of intensive care units and four Multi Super Specialty Hospitals are operational as of January 2017.

===Government Hospitals===
City has District Civil Hospital with 412 beds.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://parbhani.nic.in/htmldocs/ch_hospital_suvidha_18_07_2016.pdf |title=sFks vkjksX; fo"k;d ;kstuk dk;ZfUor vkgsr- |website=Parbhani.nic.in |access-date=2017-04-19|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170202072402/http://parbhani.nic.in/htmldocs/ch_hospital_suvidha_18_07_2016.pdf|archive-date=2017-02-02|url-status=dead}}</ref> City has Government Eye Hospital as well. It also has a separate Government Hospital for Women.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://parbhani.nic.in/htmldocs/Women_Hospital_Parbhani.pdf |title=Microsoft Word – WH Parbhani |website=Parbhani.nic.in |access-date=2017-04-19}}</ref>
Parbhani Civil Hospital proficiently carries out government operations as 
[[Beti Bachao, Beti Padhao Yojana]], "National Rural Health Mission" for school going kids,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://parbhani.nic.in/htmldocs/ch_rbsk_cell_18_07_2016.pdf |title=National Rural Health Mission |website=Parbhani.nic.in |access-date=2017-04-19}}</ref> "National Program for Prevention and Control of Diabetes, Cardio-Vascular Diseases, Cancer and Stroke".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://parbhani.nic.in/htmldocs/ch_ncd_18_07_2016.pdf |title=National Program for Prevention and Control of Diabetes, Cardio-Vascular Diseases, Cancer and Stroke |website=Parbhani.nic.in |access-date=2017-04-19}}</ref> It also has a department to prevent and take action against swine flu. Through its workshops of "Adolescent Reproductive & Sexual Health" the Civil Hospital tries to spread awareness against underage marriages, and teen pregnancy.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://parbhani.nic.in/htmldocs/ch_arsh_programme_18_07_2016.pdf |title=Adolescent Reproductive and Sexual Health |website=Parbhani.nic.in |access-date=2017-04-19}}</ref> Along with these, the Civil Hospital carries out its traditional duties of spreading awareness of basic hygiene, healthcare, and vaccinations. ==Transport==
===Rail===
[[File:Parbhani railway junction panorama.jpg|thumb|right|Panoramic photo of Parbhani railway station, taken at dawn.]]
The transport system of the city is largely dependent on rails. [[Parbhani railway station]] is a railway junction situated on [[Secunderabad–Manmad section]] of [[South Central Railway zone]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cr.indianrailways.gov.in/redevelopment_view_details_r.jsp?ID1=PARBHANI%20JN|title=Station : Parbhani Jn |website=Cr.indianrailways.gov.in |access-date=2017-04-19}}</ref> The town has connectivity to major cities of Maharashtra, such as [[Mumbai]], [[Pune]], [[Nagpur]], [[Nanded]], [[Aurangabad, Maharashtra|Aurangabad]], [[Nashik]] and [[Kolhapur]]. It is also connected to other Indian cities like [[New Delhi]], [[Bangalore]], [[Hyderabad]], [[Chennai]], [[Ajmer]], [[Bhopal]], [[Amritsar]], [[Allahabad]], [[Rameshwaram]], [[Tirupati]] and [[Visakhapatnam]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://indiarailinfo.com/departures/parbhani-junction-pbn/1674|title=PBN/Parbhani Junction Station – 80 Train Departures SCR/South Central Zone – Railway Enquiry|publisher=India Rail Info|access-date=19 April 2017}}</ref> Parbhani Junction has many facilities, such as baggage check, metal detectors, VIP Lounge, parking, ATMs, ATVM (Automatic Ticket Vending Machines), canteen (Rail Aahar), fruit stalls, book stalls, Indian Post (Bhartiya Daak), foot overbridges, water for passengers, milk dairy, television, wifi network (54&nbsp;Mbit/s), etc.

===Bus===
Central Bus Station of Parbhani connects Parbhani to other states of India, such as [[Andhra Pradesh]], Telangana, [[Karnataka]], [[Gujarat]] and Madhya Pradesh.
There are multiple daily buses of private operators and [[MSRTC]] Parbhani Division between Parbhani to other metropolitan cities within Maharashtra, and although at less frequency, there are buses to metropolitan cities from other states mentioned above.

Parbhani Bus Port Is Under Construction in CBS Parbhani.

===Air===
On 31 December 1998 State Government approved to construct an airport in Parbhani. Which was later stalled on 1 November 2001. This project is currently shelved. The location for airport was never decided.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.processregister.com/Parbhani_Airport/Project/pid9710.htm|title=Parbhani Airport Project by Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation|website=Processregister.com|access-date=19 April 2017}}</ref>

"Auto Rikshaw" (three wheelers) is only public transport available in the city. During the yearly [[Urs|Urus]] of [[Turabul Haq Dargah]], first fortnight of February month, the municipal corporation starts daily city buses across all major areas/neighborhoods of Parbhani city.

==Economy==
Economy of Parbhani is largely depended on agriculture, and [[agribusiness]].<ref name="auto2"/><!-- page number 33 --> It has [[Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation]] area for development of industries in the region, but does not have any major industry.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://dcmsme.gov.in/dips/DIPS%20Parbhani.pdf |title=Brief Industrial Profile of Parbhani District |website=Dcmsme.gov.in |access-date=2017-04-19}}</ref> It is one of the most backward districts of India and thus have very low per capita income. The city is considered as forsaken as there have been lack of any initiatives by the state and central governments. It also holds the record for having highest fuel prices in the country, which add further backwardness.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://indianexpress.com/article/cities/mumbai/backward-forsaken-parbhani-now-pays-among-highest-for-fuel-in-india-7869524/ |title = Backward, forsaken Parbhani now pays among highest for fuel in India | access-date=2023-02-15}}</ref> 
Parbhani is very famous for its cotton produce. Parbhani exports it's agriculture products like vegetable and fruits on large scale.

==Media==
Parbhani's [[All India Radio]] (Ākāshvāṇī) radio station was commissioned and became operational in 1968. With four studios, it produces programmes in [[Marathi language|Marathi]], [[Hindi]], English, [[Urdu]] and [[Sanskrit]] languages. It also provides dubbing services. The transmission covers Parbhani, [[Nanded]], [[Hingoli]], [[Latur]], [[Jalna district|Jalna]], [[Beed]] and [[Osmanabad]] districts, resulting an audience of approximately 10 lakhs. The frequency of transmission is 1305&nbsp;kHz (MW).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://allindiaradio.gov.in/Station/PARBHANI/Pages/default.aspx |title=Morning |website=Allindiaradio.gov.in |access-date=2017-04-19}}</ref>
Parbhani also has [[Doordarshan]] Relay Centre.

All the major newspapers, and electronic news media channels have their offices, or representatives in the city.

==Governance==
{{Further|Parbhani Municipal Corporation|Parbhani (Lok Sabha constituency)|Parbhani (Vidhan Sabha constituency)}}

In 2011 [[Maharashtra State]] cabinet decided to elevate Parbhani along with [[Latur]] and [[Chandrapur]] to municipal corporation as city crossed mark of 3,00,000 population in 2011 census. First election for the Municipal Corporation was conducted in 2012, after the predecessor City Council's term ended.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/nagpur/Chandrapur-is-now-municipal-corporation/articleshow/10435175.cms|title=Chandrapur is now municipal corporation|website=The Times of India|access-date=19 April 2017}}</ref>

"Parbhani Lok Sabha constituency" is one of the 48 [[Lok Sabha]] (parliamentary) constituencies in [[Maharashtra]] state in western India. This constituency covers the entire [[Parbhani district]] and a part of [[Jalna district]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ceo.maharashtra.gov.in/acs.php|title=District wise List of Assembly and Parliamentary Constituencies|publisher=Chief Electoral Officer, Maharashtra website|access-date=2017-04-19|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100318055304/http://ceo.maharashtra.gov.in/acs.php|archive-date=18 March 2010}}</ref>

Since 1989, Parbhani is stronghold of [[Shiv Sena]] both in [[Maharashtra Legislative Assembly]] elections and in [[Lok Sabha]] elections for [[Indian Parliament]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.elections.in/maharashtra/assembly-constituencies/parbhani.html |title=Parbhani (Maharashtra) Election Results 2014, Current and Previous MLA |publisher=Elections.in |access-date=2015-05-29}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.elections.in/maharashtra/parliamentary-constituencies/parbhani.html |title=Parbhani(Maharashtra) Lok Sabha Election Results 2014 with Sitting MP and Party Name |publisher=Elections.in |access-date=2015-05-29}}</ref>
