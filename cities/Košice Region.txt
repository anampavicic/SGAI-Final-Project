The '''Košice Region''' ({{langx|sk|Košický [[kraj]]}}, {{IPA-sk|ˈkɔʂitskiː ˈkraj|pron}}; {{langx|hu|Kassai kerület}}; {{langx|uk|Кошицький край}}) is one of the eight [[Regions of Slovakia|Slovak administrative regions]]. The region was first established in 1923 and its present borders were established in 1996. It consists of 11 districts ([[okres]]y) and 440 municipalities, 17 of which have a town status. About one third of the region's population lives in the agglomeration of [[Košice]], which is its main economic and cultural centre.

==Geography==
{{historical population|1970|622257|1980|698825|1991|741195|2001|766012|2011|791723|2021|782216|align=right|cols=1|source=Censuses<ref>{{cite web |title=Statistical lexikon of municipalities 1970-2011|url=https://slovak.statistics.sk/wps/wcm/connect/cd33d897-7314-41d0-a12b-a95e537d7a39/Statisticky_lexikon_obci_Slovenskej_republiky_2011.pdf?MOD=AJPERES&CVID=kRHTpsy&CVID=kRHTpsy&CVID=kRHTpsy&CVID=kRHTpsy&CVID=kRHTpsy&CVID=kRHTpsy&CVID=kRHTpsy&CVID=kRHTpsy&CVID=kRHTpsy&CVID=kRHTpsy&CVID=kRHTpsy&CVID=kRHTpsy&CVID=kRHTpsy&CVID=kRHTpsy&CVID=kRHTpsy&CVID=kRHTpsy|language=sk}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Census 2021 - Population - Basic results|url=https://www.scitanie.sk/obyvatelia/zakladne-vysledky/pocet-obyvatelov/|publisher=Statistical Office of the Slovak Republic|date=2021-01-01}}</ref>}}It is located in the southern part of eastern [[Slovakia]] and covers an area of 6,752&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>. The western part of the region is composed of the eastern part of the [[Slovak Ore Mountains]], including its subdivisions: [[Slovak Karst]], [[Slovak Paradise]], [[Volovec Mountains|Volovské vrchy]], [[Čierna hora]]. The [[Hornád Basin]] is located in the northwest. The area between Slovak Ore Mountains and [[Slanské vrchy]] is covered by the [[Košice Basin]], named after the city. The area east of [[Slanské vrchy]] is covered by the [[Eastern Slovak Lowland]] and there is a volcanic range, [[Vihorlat Mountains]], in the northeast, close to the [[Ukraine|Ukrainian]] border. Bigger rivers include [[Slaná river|Slaná]] in the southwest, [[Hornád]] in the west and centre, [[Uzh|Uh]] and [[Bodrog]] in the east, along with a small part of the [[Tisza]] river in the extreme southeast. As for administrative divisions, the region borders [[Prešov Region]] in the north, [[Zakarpattia Oblast]] of Ukraine in the east, [[Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg]] and [[Borsod-Abaúj-Zemplén]] counties of [[Hungary]] in the south and the [[Banská Bystrica Region]] in the west.

==Demographics==
The average population density in the region is 117.9 inhabitants per km<sup>2</sup>, which is very similar to the country's average (110 per km<sup>2</sup>). The largest towns are [[Košice]], [[Michalovce]], [[Spišská Nová Ves]], [[Trebišov]] and [[Rožňava]]. According to the 2001 census, there were 766,012 inhabitants in the region, with a majority of [[Slovaks]] (81.8%), but there is a numerous [[Hungarian people|Hungarian]] minority (11.2%) in the south, and there are minorities of [[Romani people|Roma]] (3.9%) and [[Czechs]] (<1%).<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.statistics.sk/webdata/english/census2001/tab/tab3a.htm|title=POPULATION AND HOUSING CENSUS 2001 - Tab. 3a|date=29 November 2006|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20061129153653/http://www.statistics.sk/webdata/english/census2001/tab/tab3a.htm |accessdate=27 June 2023|archive-date=29 November 2006 }}</ref>

==Economy==
The economy of the Košice region accounted for 11.47% of [[Slovakia|Slovakia's]] GDP in 2013, which made it the region with the second highest GDP in Slovakia after the [[Bratislava Region|Bratislava region]]. However, it lags behind the majority of Slovakia's regions in GDP per capita.<ref>[http://slovak.statistics.sk/wps/wcm/connect/6241078f-62cf-4c5a-ac78-35e4b73dc550/Regionalny_hruby_domaci_produkt_1995_2013_revidovane.pdf?MOD=AJPERES Domaci produkt 1995–2013] slovak.statistics.sk</ref>

The salaries are on average 40% higher in the Bratislava region than in the Košice region, but the living costs are considerably higher there as well.<ref name=nezamvkos/>

The unemployment in the region was at 15.6% in 2014.<ref name="nezamvkos">[https://www.postovabanka.sk/novinky/analyzy-trhu/zaujalo-nas/bratislavsky-vs-kosicky-kraj-v-okoli-hlavneho-mesta-su-vyssie-platy-a-nizsia-nezamestnanost,-ale-domacnosti-su-aj-viac-zatazene-pozickami/ Bratislavský vs. Košický kraj]. Retrieved 29 April 2015</ref>

==Administrative division==
The Košice Region consists of 11 districts. In Košice city itself there are four districts; seven districts are outside the city. There are 440 municipalities, of which 17 are towns.
