'''Itaguaí''' ({{IPA|pt|itɐgwaˈi}}) is a [[List of municipalities in Rio de Janeiro|municipality]] located in the [[States of Brazil|Brazilian state]] of [[Rio de Janeiro (state)|Rio de Janeiro]] and contains several important iron ore loading ports of the world including Ilha Guaiba.<ref name="book">{{cite book |title=Port Information book - Ilha Guaiba |publisher=Vale |url=http://www.vale.com/global/business/shiptracking/PT/PortInformation/InformacoesPortuarias_TIG.pdf |access-date=22 April 2019 |archive-date=23 June 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170623042957/http://www.vale.com/global/business/shiptracking/PT/PortInformation/InformacoesPortuarias_TIG.pdf |url-status=dead }}</ref> Its population was 134,819 in 2020 and its area is 273&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>.<ref name="IBGE2">{{cite web |title=Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics|url=http://ftp.ibge.gov.br/Estimativas_de_Populacao/Estimativas_2018/estimativa_dou_2018_20181019.pdf |year=2018 |quote=2018 Estimates of Population |access-date=6 March 2019}}</ref> The city was founded in 1688 and lies midway between [[Rio de Janeiro]] and [[Angra dos Reis]].<ref>{{cite book |title=Sailing Directions - East coast of Brazil |publisher=UKHO |location=Taunton |edition=13th}}</ref>

Itaguaí is located approximately 75&nbsp;km west of the city of [[Rio de Janeiro]] on the road to [[Santos (São Paulo)|Santos]] (SP). It is located between the shore of Sepetiba Bay and the Atlantic Rainforest.<ref>{{cite web |title=Sepetiba tecon - a profile |url=https://www.bnamericas.com/company-profile/en/sepetiba-tecon-sa-stsa |website=bnamericas.com |publisher=BN Americas |access-date=5 September 2018}}</ref>

Itaguai and the region around it contain some of the largest [[ore]] exporting [[ports]] in Brazil.<ref>{{cite web |title=Port of Itaguai and Port of Guaiba island |url=http://www.vale.com/EN/business/logistics/ports-terminals/Pages/default.aspx |website=vale.com |publisher=Vale |access-date=5 September 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite book |title=Guide to port entry |publisher=World ports guides |location=London}}</ref> It also serves also as dormitory town for workers of the industrial western zone (Zona Oeste) of Rio de Janeiro.  It is the seat of the [[Roman Catholic Diocese of Itaguaí]].

==Port of Itaguai (Sepetiba / Guaiba island)==

'''The Port of Itaguai''' was opened as a deepwater port in 1982, primarily to export [[alumina]] and other minerals found in the [[Minas Gerais]] region. It includes ports of Itaguai, Sepetiba and Guaiba island.<ref>{{cite web |title=Is iron ore the next copper? |url=https://www.cmegroup.com/trading/metals/files/CME_Platts_Webinar_Is_Iron_Ore_the_Next_Copper.pdf |website=cmegroup.com |publisher=Platts |access-date=5 September 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Official website - Port of Itaguai|language=pt |url=http://www.portosrio.gov.br/itaguai/index.htm |website=portosrio.gov.br |publisher=Ports rio |access-date=5 September 2018}}</ref> The port of [[Sepetiba]] is located in the port of Itaguaí.<ref>{{cite web |title=Itaguai port |url=https://www.marinetraffic.com/en/ais/details/ports/1034/Brazil_port:ITAGUAI |website=marinetraffic.com |publisher=Marine traffic |access-date=5 September 2018}}</ref> As of August 2018, the port accommodates large bulk carriers (170,000 [[Deadweight tonnage|deadweight]]).<ref>{{cite news |title=Still room for Sepetiba in latest box pecking order |url=http://www.portstrategy.com/news101/world/americas/even_more_brazil_photos |access-date=5 September 2018 |publisher=Port Strategy |date=25 March 2009}}</ref> The Port of Sepetiba is further divided into two administrative regions - Sepetiba Bay and Sepetiba terminal.<ref>{{cite web |title=Sepetiba bay |url=https://www.port-directory.com/ports/sepetiba/ |website=port-directory.com |publisher=Port Directory |access-date=5 September 2018}}</ref>

'''The Port of Guaíba island''' (also known as '''Ilha Guaiba''' terminal),<ref>{{cite web |title=The Ports of Guaiba and Itagui |url=http://www.vale.com/EN/business/logistics/ports-terminals/Pages/default.aspx |website=Vale official website |publisher=Vale |access-date=22 April 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite book |title=Investigation report - the sinking of Stellar Daisy |date=18 April 2019 |publisher=registry of The Marshall Islands |location=Virginia USA |url=https://www.register-iri.com/wp-content/uploads/Republic-of-the-Marshall-Islands-Office-of-the-Maritime-Administrator-STELLAR-DAISY-Casualty-Investigation-Report.pdf |access-date=22 April 2019}}</ref> located close by has separate terminals for exporting mineral ores, but is privately owned by the mining company [[Vale (mining company)|Vale]] and comes under the authority of the Port of Itaguai (Rio de Janeiro).<ref>{{cite news |title=CSN Receives 10 Offers for Brazil Box Terminal |url=https://www.marinelink.com/news/maritime/sepetiba-tecon-container-terminal |access-date=5 September 2018 |publisher=Marine Link |date=8 January 2016}}</ref> Guaiba consists of deep water berths for loading the largest bulk carriers in the world, but has no residents and no road connections.<ref name="valeironwebsite">{{cite web |title=Our mines and How Iron ore is made - Interactive Infographic |url=http://www.vale.com/brasil/EN/business/mining/iron-ore-pellets/Pages/default.aspx |website=www.vale.com |publisher=Vale |access-date=22 April 2019}}</ref> Iron ore reaches the terminal through a railway bridge that connects the island to the mainland, and further, to the Vale mines in [[Minas Gerais]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Itaguai port pins hopes on extra trans Atlantic calls |url=https://www.joc.com/maritime-news/trade-lanes/trans-atlantic/itaguai-port-pins-hopes-extra-trans-atlantic-calls_20170831.html |access-date=5 September 2018 |publisher=JOC}}</ref> In March 2019, the port, which exports 40 million tonnes of iron ore each year, was closed by the local municipality due to excessive pollution and after the [[Brumadinho]] disaster in another mine in Brazil.<ref>{{cite news |title=Brazilina town fines Vale, closes port terminal in Rio de janeiro state |publisher=Reuters |date=12 March 2019}}</ref> The closure was short though, as a court injunction was granted to Vale a day later and the port resumed operations.<ref>{{cite news |title=Brazil's Vale to resume operations are port after court injunction |publisher=Reuters |date=19 March 2019}}</ref>
