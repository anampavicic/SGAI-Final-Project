'''Narasaraopeta''' is a city and district headquarters of [[Palnadu district|Palnadu District]] of the Indian state of [[Andhra Pradesh]]. The town is a [[municipality]] and mandal headquarters of [[Narasaraopet mandal]] and Headquarters of [[Narasaraopet revenue division]]. The town also is the seat of the court of Additional District Judge.<ref>{{cite web|title=Guntur District Mandals|url=http://censusindia.gov.in/2011census/maps/atlas/28part32.pdf |publisher=Census of India|access-date=19 January 2015|pages=76,109|format=PDF}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=District Census Handbook – Guntur |url=http://www.censusindia.gov.in/2011census/dchb/2817_PART_B_DCHB_GUNTUR.pdf|website=Census of India|access-date=18 January 2015|page=14,46|format=PDF}}</ref> Narasaraopet acts as a melting point for the Palnadu and Delta regions of the district. The town is also referred as the 'Gateway of [[Palnadu]]'. The main offices of [[Nagarjuna Sagar Dam|Nagarjuna Sagar]] Jawahar Canal (N.S. Right Canal) Operation & Maintenance Lingamguntla Circle<ref>{{Cite web|title=NAGARJUNASAGAR DAM|url=https://irrigationap.cgg.gov.in/wrd/static/approjects/Nagarjuna.html|access-date=2020-10-28|website=irrigationap.cgg.gov.in}}</ref> are located in this town. Also it is considered as holy place.

== Etymology ==
The original name of the village was Atluru. The name Narsaraopet comes from Rajah Malraju Narasa Rao, the local Zamindar, who owned much of the surrounding areas.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news |date=2015-12-03 |title=Narasaraopet gears up for centenary |language=en-IN |work=The Hindu |url=https://www.thehindu.com/news/national/andhra-pradesh/narasaraopet-gears-up-for-centenary/article7946821.ece |access-date=2023-05-05 |issn=0971-751X |quote=The town has a hoary past dating back to 1797 when it got its name. The first municipality was formed on June 18, 1915. The town got its name after a local zamindar of Atluru, Raja Malraju Venkata Gundarayaningaru constructed a fort in the name of Narsarayuni.}}</ref><ref>A Manual of the Kistna District in the presidency of Madras, Gordon MacKenzie</ref>

== Geography and climate ==

Narasaraopeta is the major commercial trading centre in Palanadu district. Narasaraopeta is located on flat land ringed by hills that are an extension of the [[Eastern Ghats]] with red rock soil. The summer months can get exceedingly hot up to {{cvt|45|°C}}, while winters are mild around {{cvt|21|°C}}. Rainfall is due to the [[monsoon]] and is concentrated in the months of July–September.
The average annual rainfall is {{convert|761|mm}}. The southwest monsoon season contributes {{cvt|400|mm}}, while the northeast monsoon contributes {{cvt|299|mm}}. Pre-monsoon thundershowers contribute {{cvt|75|mm}}.

== Demographics ==
[[File:Sivvuni bomma.jpg|thumb|శివుడి బొమ్మ]]

{{As of|2011}} [[Census of India|census]], Narasaraopet had a population of 116,329 with 28,186 households. The total population constitute, 58,898 males and 57,431 females —a sex ratio of 975 females per 1000 males. 10,445 children are in the age group of 0–6 years, of which 5,390 are boys and 5,055 are girls —a ratio of 938 per 1000. The average literacy rate stands at 79.45% with 84,123 literates, significantly higher than the state average of 67.41%.<ref name=population /><ref>{{cite web|title=Literacy of AP (Census 2011)|url=http://www.ap.gov.in/AP%20State%20Statistical%20Abstract%20May%202014/2%20AP%20Demography.pdf|publisher=Official Portal of Andhra Pradesh Government|access-date=16 November 2014|page=43|format=PDF|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140714213923/http://www.ap.gov.in/AP%20State%20Statistical%20Abstract%20May%202014/2%20AP%20Demography.pdf|archive-date=14 July 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

The Urban Agglomeration had a population of 118,568, of which males constitute 60,514, females constitute 58,054 —a sex ratio of 975 females per 1000 males and 10,519 children are in the age group of 0–6 years. There are a total of 84,889 literates with an average literacy rate of 79.30%.<ref name=UA>{{cite web|title=District Census Handbook - Guntur |url=http://www.censusindia.gov.in/2011census/dchb/2817_PART_B_DCHB_GUNTUR.pdf|website=Census of India|access-date=18 January 2015|page=14,46|format=PDF}}</ref>

== Economy ==
It serves as a major commercial centre to the surrounding villages and nearby villages of the [[Prakasam district]] and [[Bapatla district]]. The City is the regional centre for higher education and health services. As a result of proximity to several towns/cities like [[Chilakaluripet]] ({{cvt|21|km|disp=sqbr}}), [[Guntur]] ({{cvt|47.9|km|disp=sqbr}}), [[Piduguralla]] ({{cvt|34.4|km|disp=sqbr}}), [[Sattenapalle]] ({{cvt|22.3|km|disp=sqbr}}), [[Vinukonda]] ({{cvt|43.7|km|disp=sqbr}}), on an average around 50,000 floating population is observed on a daily basis.

== Transport ==

The [[Guntur]] to [[Kurnool]] / Ananthapuram / Ballari NH 544D bypasses the city and the NH167A road (Piduguralla to Chirala) passes through the city. The town has a total road length of {{Convert|157.08|km|mi|abbr=on}}.<ref>{{cite web|title=Details of Roads in Each ULB of Andhra Pradesh|url=http://centralapp.cdma.ap.gov.in:8080/CDMAAPTaxesInfo/RoadDetails.jsp|website=Commissioner and Directorate of Municipal Administration|publisher=Municipal Administration and Urban Development Department - Government of Andhra Pradesh|access-date=28 March 2017|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160801101300/http://centralapp.cdma.ap.gov.in:8080/CDMAAPTaxesInfo/RoadDetails.jsp|archive-date=1 August 2016}}</ref> [[APSRTC]] operates buses from Narasaraopet bus station.<ref>{{cite web|title=Bus Stations in Districts |url=http://www.apsrtc.gov.in/Bus%20Stations.aspx |website=Andhra Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation |access-date=8 March 2016 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160322233330/http://apsrtc.gov.in/Bus%20Stations.aspx |archive-date=22 March 2016 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Srinivas|first1=Rajulapudi|title=25 passengers rescued from stranded APSRTC bus in Narsaraopet|url=http://www.thehindu.com/news/national/andhra-pradesh/25-passengers-rescued-from-stranded-apsrtc-bus-in-narsaraopet/article5271407.ece|access-date=8 March 2016|work=The Hindu|date=25 October 2013|location=Guntur}}</ref>

[[Narasaraopet railway station]] is located on the [[Nallapadu–Nandyal section]] and administered under [[Guntur railway division]] of [[South Central Railway zone]].

== Tourism ==
[[Kotappakonda]] hill is a major religious destination dedicated to Lord [[Shiva]]. On the auspicious day of Mahasivarathri, a grand fair is conducted nearby the hill. During which people around the surrounding regions visit the shiva temple on the Kottappakonda hill.

Bheema lingeswara Swamy Temple and Pathuru Shivalayam (renovated) located in the city also receive limited tourism. Sri Veera brahmamendra Swamy Temple, located at Lingamguntla Agraharam is also quite known spiritual place is {{convert|2|km|spell=in}} away from main city.
