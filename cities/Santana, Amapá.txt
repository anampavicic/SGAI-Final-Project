'''Santana''' ({{IPA|pt|sɐ̃'tɐ̃nɐ}}) is a [[Municipalities of Brazil|municipality]] located in the southeast of the state of [[Amapá]] in [[Brazil]]. Its population is 123,096 and its area is {{convert|1,541|km2|mi2|lk=out|abbr=off}}, which makes it the smallest municipality of Amapá.  Santana is a suburb of [[Macapá]], the state capital, and the two cities make up the Metropolitan Area of Macapá. Its location is nearly on the equator.  The planet's second longest river, the [[Amazon River|Amazon]] is to the south.<ref name="IBGE_Pop_2012"/><ref name="his"/>

Santana was historically a part of Macapá. In 1981 it was elevated to the category of a District of Macapá. It became a separate municipality in the state of Amapá on 1 January 1982.<ref name="his"/>

==History==
[[File:Lateral externa do campus.jpg|thumb|left|Campus of the university]]
The history of Santana began in 1753 when [[Ilha de Santana]], an island in the [[Amazon River]] in front of the city of Santana, became an official settlement.<ref name="ibge">{{cite web|url=https://cidades.ibge.gov.br/brasil/ap/santana/historico|title=Santana|website=[[IBGE]]|access-date=2 April 2021|language=pt}}</ref> The settlement on the coast started to develop in the 1950s when [[manganese]] was discovered in [[Serra do Navio]]. Santana has developed into the main harbour and industrial centre for the state, and has become the second most populous city.<ref name="amapa">{{cite web|url=https://www.portal.ap.gov.br/conheca/santana |title=Santana|website=Government of Amapá|access-date=6 April 2021|language=pt}}</ref> In 1990, a campus of the [[Federal University of Amapá]] was opened in Santana.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www2.unifap.br/santana/?page_id=179 |title=Apresentação e Infraestrutura do Campus Universitário de Santana -CSTN|website=Unifap|access-date=6 April 2021|language=pt}}</ref>

===Port of Santana===
{{anchor|Port Santana}}
The Port of Santana was built in 1980. It was originally intended for river transport, but due to its favourable position, it quickly developed into a main harbour for international transport to and from the [[Caribbean]], [[United States]] and [[European Union]]. The harbour was inaugurated on 6 May 1982, and became a [[public company]] in 2002.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.docasdesantana.com.br/index.php/o-porto |title=O Porto |website=Docas De Santana|access-date=1 April 2021|language=pt}}</ref>

The Port of Santana also provides ferry services. In 2020, the ferry between Santana and [[Belém]] was the largest ferry of Brazil measuring {{convert|115|m}}.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://selesnafes.com/2020/01/maior-balsa-do-brasil-comeca-a-operar-no-amapa/ |title=Maior balsa do Brasil começa a operar no Amapá|website=Seles Nafes|access-date=5 April 2020|language=pt}}</ref>
