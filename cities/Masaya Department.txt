'''Masaya''' ({{IPA|es|maˈsaʝa}}) is a [[Departments of Nicaragua|department]] in [[Nicaragua]]. It is the country's smallest department by area (611&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>) and has a population of 397,632 (2021 estimate). The capital is the city of [[Masaya]]. It is famous among Nicaraguan people for its nickname, "'''La Cuna Del Folklore'''" which translates to (The Cradle of Folklore). It is also the site of the [[Masaya Volcano]], an active 635m volcano which last erupted in 2016. The [[Indigenous peoples of the Americas|Indigenous]] inhabitants of Masaya are the [[Nicarao people|Nahuas]] and the [[:es: Chorotegas|Chorotegas]], and was the location of the [[pre-Columbian]] Nahua chiefdom of Masatepet.<ref>Vida de González Dávila, Gil.
