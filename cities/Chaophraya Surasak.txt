'''Chaophraya Surasak''' ({{langx|th|เทศบาลนครเจ้าพระยาสุรศักดิ์}}) is a city in [[Chonburi Province]], [[Thailand]].  It was established as a sanitary district in 1967. In 1999, all sanitary districts were upgraded to subdistrict municipalities (''[[Thesaban#Subdistrict-municipality|thesaban tambon]]''). In 2013 it was upgraded to a city municipality (''[[Thesaban#City-municipality|thesaban nakhon]]''). As of 2022, it has a population of 153,687.<ref name=population/> The city covers parts of five subdistricts (''[[tambon]]'') of [[Si Racha District]], the [[List of municipalities in Thailand|ninth-largest]] urban city area in Thailand. The city is named after [[Chaophraya Surasakmontri]].

==History==
Originally named Ao Udom became a sanitation (''Sukhaphiban'') on 12 December 1967, which covered parts of subdistricts Thung Sukhla and Surasak, Si Racha district, Chonburi province. Later on 6 October 1970 it was enlarged with subdistrict Nong Kham, Bueng district and area of subdistrict Thung Sukhla. On 24 December 1991 the area has been enlarged again and contains five subdistricts, Surasak, Nong Kham, Bueng, Bo Win and Kha Khan Song. The sanitation changed to subdistrict municipality (''[[Thesaban#Subdistrict-municipality|thesaban tambon]]'') on 24 February 1999, effectively on 25 May 1999. The name changed to ''Chaophraya Surasak subdistrict municipality'' on 23 July 2002. The status was upgraded to ''Chaophraya Surasak city municipality'' (''[[Thesaban#City-municipality|thesaban nakhon]]'') on 9 April 2013, effectively on 1 May 2013, while the total area was reduced from {{convert|306.44|to|276.98|sqkm}}.<ref name="history">{{cite web |title=History of Chao Phraya Surasak City Municipality |url=https://chaoprayasurasak.go.th/public/list/data/index/menu/1142 |access-date=20 July 2023 |department=Chao Phraya Surasak City Municipality}}</ref>

==Geography==
===Location===
Chaophraya Surasak city municipality (''[[Thesaban#City-municipality|thesaban nakhon]]'') with a total area of 276.98 km<sup>2</sup> is located on the east coast of [[Gulf of Thailand]]. It is about 40 kilometers from [[Chonburi]] and 120 kilometers from [[Bangkok]]. Chaophraya Surasak borders to the '''north''': subdistricts (''tambon'') Bang Phra and Nong Kham, district (''amphoe'') Si Racha, province Chonburi, '''east''': subdistrict Khlon Kio, district Ban Bueng and subdistricts Khao Khan Song and Bo Win, district Si Racha, province Chonburi, '''south''': subdistrict Takhian Tia, [[Bang Lamung District|district Bang Lamung]], province Chonburi, '''west''': [[Laem Chabang]] city municipality and [[Si Racha]] town municipality (''[[Thesaban#Town municipality|thesaban mueang]]''), district Si Racha, province Chonburi and the [[Bay of Bangkok]].<ref name=location>{{cite web |url=https://chaoprayasurasak.go.th/public/list/data/index/menu/1144 |title=characteristics and location |department=Chao Phraya Surasak City Municipality |access-date=20 July 2023}}</ref>

===Topography===
In the east of the Chaophraya Surasak municipality is the [[Khao Khiao Massif|Khao Khiao]] mountain range. The foothills alternate with plains to the sea.<ref name=topography>{{cite web |url=https://chaoprayasurasak.go.th/public/list/data/index/menu/1144 |title=topography |department=Chao Phraya Surasak City Municipality |access-date=20 July 2023}}</ref>

===Wildlife Sanctuary===
There is one wildlife sanctuary in Khao Khan Song subdistrict: [[Khao Khiao - Khao Chomphu Wildlife Sanctuary]], 145 km<sup>2</sup> (56 sq.mi.)

===Climate===
Chaophraya Surasak city has a tropical savanna climate (Köppen climate classification category Aw). Winters are dry and warm. Temperatures rise until May. Mansoon season runs from May through October, with heavy rain and somewhat cooler temperatures during the day, although nights remain warm. Weather station Laem Chabang, climate data from 1981 to 2010: The maximum temperature is 39.0&nbsp;°C (102.2&nbsp;°F) in January and the minimum temperature is 14.0&nbsp;°C (57.2&nbsp;°F) in December. The maximum average temperature is 33.3&nbsp;°C (91.9&nbsp;°F) in April and the minimum average temperature is 22.0&nbsp;°C (71.6&nbsp;°F) in December. Annual rainfall is 1,151 millimeter (45.3 inch) and in January was for 24 hour the rainfall 176.5 millimeter (7 inch).<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.climate.tmd.go.th/content/file/75 |title=Climatological Data for the Period 1981-2010 |publisher=Thai Meteorological Department |page=20 |access-date=22 December 2019}}</ref>

===Water resources===
There are three water reservoirs:<ref name=waterresources>{{cite web |url=https://chaoprayasurasak.go.th/public/list/data/menu/1144 |title=characteristics of water resources |department=Chao Phraya Surasak City Municipality |access-date=20 July 2023}}</ref>
* Huai Nong Kho, capacity 21,400,000 cub.m.
* Huai Saphan, capacity 7,650,000 cub.m.
* Huai Nong Klang Dong, capacity 3,840,00 cub.m.
Further there is natural stream Huai Lam, consisting of 20 natural places.

As of 2019 95% of the population in Chaophraya Surasak city municipality self-identified as ''[[Buddhism|Buddhists]]'' of the [[Theravada#Thailand and Cambodia|Theravada Buddhism]]. There are 32 temples (''[[wat]]'') and seven "samnak song", temples who are not official registered. ''[[Muslims]]'' comprising 2% of the population with three [[mosque]]s. ''[[Christians]]'' represent also 2% of the population with three [[Church (building)|churches]]. The remaining 1% of the population are followers of other religions.<ref name="religious">{{cite web |url=https://www.chaoprayasurasak.go.th/public/list/data/index/menu/1144 |title=Religious |department=Chao Phraya Surasak City Municipality |access-date=20 July 2023}}</ref><ref name=temples>{{cite web |url=https://www.dhammastudy.org/temple/index?sort=-nikai_id&page=2 |title=list of temples |access-date=23 July  2023 |postscript= , > no.122 >126 >132 >136}}</ref>

==Logo==
The city logo shows a freight train, which symbolizes industry, buildings that characterize urbanization, pineapples are a reference to agriculture and the mountains in the background pointing to the Khao Khiao mountain range.<ref name=history/>

==Infrastructure==
===Education===
There are eight early childhood (Kindergarten) schools and 20 schools for basic education: 10 schools with  primary 1 to secondary 3 level and 10 schools with primary 1 to secondary 6 level. The city has no vocational or tertiary education.<ref name=education>{{cite web |url=https://chaoprayasurasak.go.th/public/list/data/index/menu/1144 |title=education |department=Chao Phraya Surasak City Municipality |access-date=20 July 2023}}</ref>

===Health===
There are eight health promoting hospitals; Surasak 1, Bueng 2, Nong Kham 2, Bo Win 1, Khao Khan Song 2.<br>
Further Khao Khan Song has one health center (Chaloem Phra Kiat).<ref name=health>{{cite web |url=https://chaoprayasurasak.go.th/public/list/data/index/menu/1144 |title=public health facility |department=Chao Phraya Surasak City Municipality |access-date=20 July 2023}}</ref>

===Waterworks===
"Si Racha District Waterworks Office" produced 47,000 cub.m./day tapwater, which is used by 30,100 households or 30% of the total area in the municipality.<br>
"Laem Chabang Waterworks Office" produced 59,140 cub.m/day tapwater, which is used by 36,400 households or 13% of the total area in the municipality.<br>
The conclusion is: 38% of the households (41,039) or 57% of the total area in the municipality do not used tapwater.<ref name=waterworks>{{cite web |url=https://chaoprayasurasak.go.th/public/list/data/index/menu/1144 |title=Waterworks Offices |department=Chao Phraya Surasak City Municipality |access-date=20 July 2023}}</ref>

===Economy===
The total area of agricultural land is 76,610 rai ~ {{convert|122.6|sqkm}}, which accounts for about 44% of the total area in the municipality. The farming mostly delivers tapioca, pineapple, dragon fruit. Animal husbandry consists of seven pig farms and 37 chicken farms. Sriracha Tiger Zoo with an area of 40 ha (100 acres) is in Nong Kham subdistrict. Tourism revenue is 110 million Baht/year. The total number of factories is 214, many of which are concentrated in four "Industrial Estates":<ref name=economy>{{cite web |url=https://chaoprayasurasak.go.th/public/list/data/index/menu/1144 |title=economic nature |department=Chao Phraya Surasak City Municipality |access-date=20 July 2023}}</ref>
* Pinthong Industrial Estate 1 - 160 ha (400 acres)
* Pinthong Industrial Estate 2 - 120 ha (300 acres)
* Pinthong Industrial Estate 3 - 160 ha (400 acres)
* Bo Win Industrial Estate - 540 ha (1330 acres)
