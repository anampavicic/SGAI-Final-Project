'''Kindia''' ([[N'Ko script|N’ko]]: ߞߌ߲ߘߌߦߊ߫) is the fourth largest city in [[Guinea]], located about {{convert|85|mi}} northeast of the nation's capital, [[Conakry]]. Its estimated population in 2008 was 181,126.<ref>[http://world-gazetteer.com/wg.php?x=&men=gcis&lng=en&des=wg&srt=npan&col=abcdefghinoq&msz=1500&geo=-89 World Gazetteer] {{dead link|date=July 2021|bot=medic}}{{cbignore|bot=medic}}, Retrieved on June 16, 2008</ref> Kindia serves as the capital and largest city of [[Kindia Prefecture]] and [[Kindia Region]]. It also functions as a [[Sub-prefectures of Guinea|sub-prefecture]] of Guinea.

== Geography ==
[[File:Le "Voile" de la mariée à Kindia.png|300px|thumb|right|Mariée Falls.]]
The city is near [[Mount Gangan]] and the [[Mariée Falls]].

== History ==
[[File:46-Kindia-La Rue principale.jpg|300px|thumb|right|Main street in 1905.]]
[[File:44-Kindia-Gare.jpg|300px|thumb|right|Station in 1905.]]
The city was founded in 1904 on the route of Conakry Railway in Kankan.<ref>''Britannica'',
[https://www.britannica.com/place/Kindia "Kindia"], britannica.com, US, accessed on June 23, 2019</ref>

== Economy ==
Kindia grew around [[banana]] [[plantation]]s following the construction of a now-closed [[railway]] to the capital.

==Demography==
The city of Kindia has an ethnically diverse population, although the [[Susu people|Susu]] make up the majority of the population, followed by the [[Mandinka people|Mandinka]]. The city is home to virtually all of the country's ethnic groups. After the capital Conakry, Kindia is home to the second largest [[Sierra Leonean]] community in Guinea. An estimated 9,000 Sierra Leoneans reside in the city, many of them have obtain Guinean citizenship. Like every city in Western Guinea, including the capital Conakry, the [[Susu language]] is the most widely spoken language in Kindia and is understood by virtually the entire population.

==Climate==
Kindia has a [[tropical savanna climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification]] ''Aw'').
