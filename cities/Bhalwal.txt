'''Bhalwal'''  ([[Punjabi language|Punjabi]] and {{langx|ur|{{Nastaliq|بھلوال}}}}), is a city and capital of [[Bhalwal Tehsil]] of [[Sargodha District]] in the centre of the [[Punjab (Pakistan)|Punjab]] province of [[Pakistan]].<ref>[http://www.statpak.gov.pk/depts/pco/statistics/admin_unit/admin_list_tehsil.html List of Tehsils and districts of Pakistan&nbsp;– Statpak.gov.pk] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101230183032/http://www.statpak.gov.pk/depts/pco/statistics/admin_unit/admin_list_tehsil.html |date=30 December 2010 }}</ref> It is the [[Largest cities of Pakistan|99th largest city of Pakistan]], according to 2017 census. The city of Bhalwal is the headquarters of Bhalwal Tehsil and lies in an agricultural area, close to the [[National Highways of Pakistan|M-2 motorway]].<ref>[http://www.fallingrain.com/world/PK/04/Bhalwal.html Location of Bhalwal&nbsp;– Falling Rain Genomics]</ref>
Bhalwal is famous for Orange crops of [[Pakistan]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://herald.dawn.com/news/1153950|title=Why citrus production is not living up to its potential|date=27 December 2017}}</ref>

In 2011, Government of Punjab decided to urbanize 154 small cities and towns in Punjab, as the  result of colossal migration of people from small cities to larger cities like Lahore, Faisalabad, Rawalpindi, Gujranwala, and Islamabad. The master urbanization plan included Bhalwal, due to sharp increase of migration to larger cities.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Hasnain |first=Khalid |date=2021-05-29 |title=Govt decides to ‘urbanise’ 154 small towns, cities across Punjab |url=https://www.dawn.com/news/1626245 |access-date=2023-03-15 |website=DAWN.COM |language=en}}</ref>
