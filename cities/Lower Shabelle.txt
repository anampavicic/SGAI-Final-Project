'''Lower Shabelle''' ({{langx|so|Shabeellaha Hoose}},<ref>[http://geonames.nga.mil/namesgaz/ GeoNames Search]</ref> {{langx|ymm|Shibelithy Hoosy}}, {{langx|ar|شبيلي السفلى}}, {{langx|it|Basso Scebeli}}) is an administrative region (''[[Administrative divisions of Somalia|gobol]]'') in southern [[Somalia]].<ref name="Cwfsom">{{cite web|title=Somalia|work=The World Factbook|publisher=Central Intelligence Agency|location=Langley, Virginia|url=https://www.cia.gov/the-world-factbook/countries/somalia/|access-date=6 December 2013}}</ref>

==Geography==
Lower Shabelle is bordered by the regions of [[Banaadir]], [[Middle Shabelle]] (Shabeellaha Dhexe), [[Hiran, Somalia|Hiran]], [[Bay, Somalia|Bay]], [[Middle Jubba]] (Jubbada Dhexe) and by the Somali Sea. It is named after the [[Shebelle River]], which passes through it.

Until 1984, when the regions were reassigned, it was part of the larger [[Benadir]] region and its capital was [[Mogadishu]]. [[Merca]] is now the Lower Shabelle capital.

==Violence in Golweyn==
On 30 July 2017, an [[AMISOM]] convoy was [[2017 Golweyn ambush|ambushed]] by [[Al-Shabaab (militant group)|al-Shabaab]] insurgents, killing and wounding several [[Uganda People's Defence Force|Ugandan soldiers]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Somalia: Military convoy targeted in roadside bomb attack in Lower Shabelle |url=https://www.garoweonline.com/en/news/somalia/somalia-military-convoy-targeted-in-roadside-bomb-attack-in-lower-shabelle |access-date=2022-05-18 |website=Garowe Online |language=en}}</ref>

Eight children were killed and dozens were wounded when a bomb exploded in Golweyn on 31 January 2021.<ref>{{cite news |title=Somalia's al-Shabab rebels attack hotel in the capital city |url=https://abcnews.go.com/International/wireStory/somalias-al-shabab-rebels-attack-hotel-capital-city-75596874|access-date=31 January 2021 |work=ABC News |date=31 January 2021 |language=en}}</ref>
