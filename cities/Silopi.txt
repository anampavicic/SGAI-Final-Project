'''Silopi''' ({{langx|ku|Silopî}})<ref>{{Cite book |last=Avcıkıran |first=Adem |url= |title=Kürtçe Anamnez, Anamneza bi Kurmancî |website= |editor-last= |editor-first= |page=57 |access-date=}}</ref> is a city and seat of [[Silopi District]] in the [[Şırnak Province]] of [[Turkey]].<ref name=":12">{{Cite web |title=Türkiye Mülki İdare Bölümleri Envanteri |url=https://www.e-icisleri.gov.tr/Anasayfa/MulkiIdariBolumleri.aspx |access-date=19 December 2022 |website=T.C. İçişleri Bakanlığı |language=tr}}</ref> The city is mainly populated by [[Kurds]] of Sipêrtî and Zewkan [[Kurdish tribes|tribes]] and had a population of 108,880 in 2023.<ref name=":1">{{Cite book |last=Baz |first=Ibrahim |title=Şırnak aşiretleri ve kültürü |year=2016 |isbn=9786058849631 |pages=106 and 129 |language=tr}}</ref><ref name=tuik/>

Adalet Fidan of the [[Peoples' Democratic Party (Turkey)|HDP]] was elected mayor in 2019<ref>{{Cite web |title=Şırnak Silopi Seçim Sonuçları - 31 Mart 2019 Yerel Seçimleri |url=https://www.sabah.com.tr/secim/31-mart-2019-yerel-secim-sonuclari/sirnak/silopi/ilcesi-yerel-secim-sonuclari |access-date=2019-09-10 |website=www.sabah.com.tr}}</ref> but deposed the same year and replaced by the sub-governor ([[kaymakam]]), Sezer Işiktaş, as acting mayor.

Large sections of the city were damaged in 2016 during [[Şırnak clashes (2015–2016)|clashes]] between [[Turkish Land Forces|Turkish forces]] and the [[PKK]].<ref>{{Cite news |date=15 May 2016 |title=Over 6,600 homes damaged by fighting in Kurdish Silopi, Ankara says |work=[[Rûdaw]] |url=https://www.rudaw.net/english/middleeast/turkey/15032016 |access-date=26 December 2022}}</ref>

== Neighborhoods ==
The city is divided into the eleven neighborhoods: Barbaros, Başak, Cudi, Cumhuriyet ({{Lang|ku|Kizir}}), Dicle, Karşıyaka, Nuh, Ofis, Şehit Harun Boy, Yenişehir and Yeşiltepe.<ref name=":12" />

== History ==

=== Recent history ===
Since July 2015, the [[Kurdistan Workers' Party insurgency|Kurdish-Turkish conflict]] in the region has escalated, and 34 people have been killed in Silopi. On 7 August 2015 clashes between Turkish security forces and the Kurdish militant [[Kurdistan Workers' Party|PKK]] group may have killed three people. Government reports claimed "terrorists" were killed,<ref>{{cite news |date=7 August 2015 |title=Three killed in clashes in southeastern town of Silopi |url=http://www.todayszaman.com/national_three-killed-in-clashes-in-southeastern-town-of-silopi_395864.html |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150807132718/http://www.todayszaman.com/national_three-killed-in-clashes-in-southeastern-town-of-silopi_395864.html |archivedate=7 August 2015 |accessdate=7 August 2015 |publisher=Today's Zaman |ref=Zaman}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |date=7 August 2015 |title=PKK terrorists clash with armed forces in Turkey's southeastern Silopi, three terrorists killed |url=http://www.dailysabah.com/nation/2015/08/07/pkk-terrorists-clash-with-armed-forces-in-turkeys-southeastern-silopi-three-terrorists-killed |accessdate=7 August 2015 |publisher=Daily Sabah}}</ref> while pro-Kurdish [[Peoples' Democratic Party (Turkey)|Peoples' Democratic Party]] lawmaker [[Faysal Sarıyıldız]] said that the casualties were civilians and that he had seen no sign of armed militants.<ref name="reuters2015">{{cite news |date=7 August 2015 |title=Five killed in clashes between Turkish forces, Kurdish rebels |url=http://uk.reuters.com/article/uk-mideast-crisis-turkey-kurds-idUKKCN0QC0XS20150807 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151225073021/http://uk.reuters.com/article/uk-mideast-crisis-turkey-kurds-idUKKCN0QC0XS20150807 |archive-date=25 December 2015 |accessdate=7 August 2015 |website=Reuters |agency=Reuters}}</ref> Turkish authorities placed the city under curfew from 14 December 2015 to 4 January 2016. According to the Peoples' Democratic Party, 20 people were killed.<ref>{{cite web |title=HDP Information Desk: Current Situation in Cizre, Silopi and Sur Districts (Updated on 04.01.2016) {{!}} |url=https://hdpenglish.wordpress.com/2016/01/06/hdp-information-desk-current-situation-in-cizre-silopi-and-sur-districts-updated-on-04-01-2016/ |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304210635/https://hdpenglish.wordpress.com/2016/01/06/hdp-information-desk-current-situation-in-cizre-silopi-and-sur-districts-updated-on-04-01-2016/ |archive-date=4 March 2016}}</ref>

==Employment==

[[Şırnak Silopi power station]] is powered by [[asphaltite]] coal and is claimed both to emit air pollution and to be an important source of employment.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://sirnakhaber.com/index2.php/silopiye-7-kere-5/|title=SİLOPİ'YE 7 KERE 5…!?|date=2020-02-09|website=ŞIRNAK HABER|language=tr|access-date=2020-02-11}}</ref> In 2020 the [[European Bank for Reconstruction and Development|EBRD]] proposed a [[just transition]] to support workers who may lose jobs due to the decline of [[coal in Turkey]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=The EBRD just transition initiative|url=https://www.ebrd.com/just-transition|access-date=|website=}}</ref>

Peanuts are grown.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Yer fıstığı Silopi'den dünyaya açılacak|url=https://www.haberler.com/yer-fistigi-silopi-den-dunyaya-acilacak-13392446-haberi/|access-date=2020-08-04|website=Haberler.com|language=tr}}</ref>
