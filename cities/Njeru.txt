'''Njeru''' is a town in [[Buikwe District]], in the [[Central Region, Uganda|Central Region]] of [[Uganda]]. It is the largest town in the district. It is mainly a residential town. However, it hosts  industries such as East African Packaging Solutions Limited, a manufacturer of paper packaging supplies, [[Nile Breweries Limited]], a subsidiary of [[AB InBev]] and [[Nyanza Textile Industries Limited]] (Nytil), a textile manufacturer.<ref name="2R">{{cite web| newspaper=[[Daily Monitor]] | url=https://www.monitor.co.ug/Business/Nytil-moving-textile-ladder-turbulent-past/688322-3163324-28ibsm/index.html | title=Nytil Is Moving Up The Textile Ladder After A Turbulent Past |date=17 April 2016 |author=Ismail Musa Ladu |access-date=5 January 2020 | location=Kampala}}</ref>

==Economic activity==
There are several industries and businesses located in Njeru that provide employment to a significant number of people and contribute significantly to the economy of [[Uganda]]. Some of these include:<ref name="5R"/>

1. Nile Breweries Limited, a subsidiary of [[SAB Miller]], based in [[South Africa]] and ultimately of [[AB InBev]] of [[Belgium]]

2. [[Nalubaale Power Station]] - Installed Capacity 180 MW, owned by the Ugandan government. Operated under concession by Eskom (Uganda), a subsidiary of [[Eskom]]

3. East African Packaging Solutions - A 50/50 joint venture between the [[Madhvani Group]] and Graphic Systems Limited.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[Kakira Sugar Works]] |date=5 January 2021 
|url=http://www.kakirasugar.com/content/packaging |access-date=5 January 2021 |title=Madhvani Group: Packaging - East African Packaging Solutions Limited |author=Kakira Sugar Works}}</ref>

4. Nile Vocational Institute - A technical college with over 100 students founded by German charity [[Kindernothilfe]]

5. Vitafoam Industries - A Factory Manufacturing Mattresses.

6. Njeru Stock Farm - Formerly a government livestock farm, now used as an Army Detach.

7. [[Nyanza Textile Industries Limited|Nytil Picfare]] - The biggest garments factory in Uganda, making garments out of locally grown organic cotton.<ref name="2R"/>

==Transportation==
Both the old [[Kampala-Jinja Highway]] and the new [[Kampala–Jinja Expressway]], pass through Njeru and are part of the northern corridor of the Trans-Africa Highway. The Kampala–Jinja Expressway connects to the [[Source of the Nile Bridge]] in Njeru.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.newvision.co.ug/news/1486630/jinja-bridge-ready |title=Jinja's New Bridge Ready |newspaper=[[New Vision]] | date=28 September 2018 |author=Donald Kiirya |access-date=5 January 2021 |location=Kampala}}</ref>

==Population==
In 2014, the national population census put the population of Njeru at 159,549. In 2020, UBOS estimated the population of Njeru at 178,800 people. The population agency calculated the average annual growth rate of the town's population at 1.97 percent every year, between 2014 and 2020.<ref name="1R">{{cite web| url=https://www.citypopulation.de/en/uganda/cities/ | title=Population of Njeru Town, Buikwe District, Central Region, Uganda |publisher=Citypopulation.de |date=14 June 2020 |
