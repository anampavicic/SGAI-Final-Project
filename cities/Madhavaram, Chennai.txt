'''Madhavaram''' is a neighbourhood of [[Chennai]], [[Tamil Nadu]], [[India]]. Located in the northern part of Chennai, it is also a ''taluk'' in [[Chennai District]] and a ''zone'' in [[Greater Chennai Corporation]]. It is located in between [[Perambur]] and [[Kodungaiyur]]. As of 2011, the neighbourhood had a population of 119,105. The Chennai district was expanded on 16 August 2018 by transferring Madhavaram taluk from [[Tiruvallur district]] to [[Chennai district]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Kabirdoss|first1=Yogesh|title=Chennai district boundaries likely to be redrawn on April 1|url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/chennai/chennai-district-boundaries-likely-to-be-redrawn-on-april-1/articleshow/62560754.cms|work=The Times of India |date=19 January 2018 |access-date=7 March 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Kabirdoss |first1=Yogesh |title=Withsuburbs, Chennai district officially doubles in size |url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/chennai/with-suburbs-chennai-district-officially-doubles-in-size/articleshow/65668183.cms |work=The Times of India| date=4 September 2018 |access-date=7 March 2019}}</ref>

==History==
The Madhavaram ''taluk'' was formed on 1 July 2009 when the large Ambattur ''taluk'' was split into two. The latter was considered the largest land area in Chennai, which consisted of five ''fircas'' (towns). The new Madhavaram ''taluk'' consists of two ''fircas'' (Madhavaram and Red Hills) and 36 villages.<ref name="taluk">{{cite news | url= http://www.thehindu.com/news/cities/Chennai/article98891.ece| title= Bifurcation of the large Ambattur taluk | author= The Hindu (2 February 2010)  | access-date=15 July 2010 | location=Chennai, India | date=2 February 2010}}</ref> The old name of Madhavaram is Mathavapuram, as seen in a stone in front of the Vinayagar temple in Rajaji Street.{{Citation needed|reason=Reliable source needed for the whole sentence|date=December 2016}}

[[Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes]] accounted for 12.4% and 0.28% of the population respectively. The average literacy of the town was 80.61%, compared to the national average of 72.99%.<ref name="dashboard"/> The town had a total of 29,792 households. There were a total of 43,385 workers, comprising 148 cultivators, 233 main agricultural labourers, 765 in house hold industries, 36,871 other workers, 5,368 marginal workers, 89 marginal cultivators, 65 marginal agricultural labourers, 283 marginal workers in household industries and 4,931 other marginal workers.<ref name=2011census>{{cite web|title=Census Info 2011 Final population totals - Madhavaram|url=http://www.censusindia.gov.in/pca/SearchDetails.aspx?Id=674609|publisher=Office of The Registrar General and Census Commissioner, Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India|year=2013|access-date=26 January 2014}}</ref>

===Religion===
As per the religious census of 2011, Madhavaram (M) had 82.73% [[Hindus]], 4.08% [[Muslims]], 12.48% [[Christians]], 0.05% [[Sikhs]], 0.04% [[Buddhists]], 0.16% [[Jainism|Jains]], 0.46% following other religions and 0.01% following no religion or did not indicate any religious preference.<ref name=religion2011>{{cite web|title=Population By Religious Community - Tamil Nadu|year=2011|url=http://www.censusindia.gov.in/2011census/C-01.html|format=XLS|publisher=Office of The Registrar General and Census Commissioner, Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India|access-date=13 September 2015}}</ref>

There is a Shivan temple<ref>[http://www.greenmesg.org/temples_chennai/m/madhavaram_kailasanathar_temple.php Kailasanathar Temple]</ref> in Madhavaram which is believed to be 1,300 years old. There is also a Vishnu Temple<ref>[http://www.greenmesg.org/temples_chennai/m/madhavaram_kari_varadaraja_perumal_temple.php Kari Varadharaja Perumal Koil]</ref> and some smaller temples in the locality.
St. Sebastian's Church, the local parish, is in Madhavaram. Other churches are on MTH Road, KKR Garden, Gangaiamman Koil street. There are few mosques in KKR Garden, Milk Colony and Vinayagapuram.

==Social organizations==

West Cancer Trust advocates cancer awareness by counselling and screening the locals, both the rural people and the students. "LIGHT" [[NGO]] is situated in Manjampakkam neighbourhood, working for children, women, health and education. It is also home to a large number of the Anglo Indian community.

==Administration and politics==

Madhavaram is a newly formed state legislative constituency with more than 200,000 (2 [[lakh]]) voters. It consists of areas like Madhavaram, Mathur MMDA, Manali, [[Madhavaram Milk Colony|Milk Colony]], Puzhal, [[Red Hills, Chennai|Red Hills]], Sholavaram and Vadakarai. Madhavaram belongs to Chennai's corporation zone lll. Presently, Mr.S.Sudarsanam is the Member of Legislative Assembly. {{clarify |date=December 2016 |reason= Election of what? Mayor, perhaps?}}

===Truck Terminal===
In the interest of the economy and trade and to decongest the central business district, [[Chennai Metropolitan Development Authority|CMDA]] has developed the truck terminal at Madhavaram over an extent about {{convert|100|acre|km2}} at the cost of about [[Indian rupee|â‚¹]] 60 million (6 [[crore]]s). It is located near the junction of 100 Feet Road and the GNT Road, with easy access to [[Chennai city]], port and railways. The terminal has been functioning since 1992. The objective of the project is to provide modern and functionally efficient truck terminals for the benefit of the city.

A [[Madhavaram botanical garden|botanical garden]] development broke ground on a 28-acre site in September 2010. The project was expected to complete in February 2013, at a cost of â‚¹57.3 million. The facility has almost 400 species of plants and also a herbal garden along with a nursery. A glasshouse, fountains, a birds' sanctuary, an auditorium and a children's play area are other facilities of the garden.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://tnhorticulture.tn.gov.in/horti/node/576 |title= Botanical Garden at Madhavaram |date= 11 August 2012 |publisher= Agriculture Department, Government of Tamil Nadu |access-date=16 December 2016 }}</ref>
