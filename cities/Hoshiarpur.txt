'''Hoshiarpur''' ({{IPA-pa|ɦʊʃɪˈaːɾpʊɾ|lang|Hoshiarpur.ogg}}) is a city and a [[Municipal corporations in India|municipal corporation]] in [[Hoshiarpur district]] in the [[Doaba]] region of the Indian state of [[Punjab, India|Punjab]]. It was founded, according to tradition, during the early part of the fourteenth century. In 1809, it was occupied by the forces of Maharaja Karanvir Singh and was united into the greater state of Punjab in 1849.<ref>{{cite EB1911 |wstitle=Hoshiarpur |volume=13 |pages=787–788}}</ref>

Hoshiarpur has an average elevation of {{convert|296|m}}. Hoshiarpur district is located in the north-east part of the Indian state of Punjab. It falls in the Jalandhar Revenue Division and is situated in the Bist Doab portion of the Doaba region. Hoshiarpur shares a boundary with [[Kangra district]], and [[Una district]] of [[Himachal Pradesh]] in the northeast. In the southwest, it borders [[Shahid Bhagat Singh Nagar district]], [[Jalandhar district]], and [[Kapurthala district]], and in the northwest it borders [[Gurdaspur district]].

The [[Literacy|literacy rate]] was 89.11 per cent.<ref>{{cite web| url = http://www.censusindia.gov.in/2011-prov-results/paper2/data_files/India2/Table_3_PR_UA_Citiees_1Lakh_and_Above.pdf | title = Urban Agglomerations/Cities having population 1 lakh and above |work= Provisional Population Totals, Census of India 2011 |access-date = 7 October 2015 }}</ref>

==History==
The archaeological explorations during the recent years have revealed the antiquity of the Hoshiarpur District to the [[Indus Valley civilisation|Harappan Period]]. On the basis of surface exploration, the following new sites have been brought on the Archaeological map of India and the traces of the selfsame people as at [[Harappa]] and [[Mohenjo-daro|Mohenjadaro]] have also been detected in the Hoshiarpur District.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hoshiarpur.nic.in/html/history.htm#ancient|title=History & Culture}}</ref>

==Transport==
===Road===
[[File:A statue of Maharishi Valmiki at Hoshiarpur.jpg|thumb|A statue of Maharishi Valmiki at Hoshiarpur.]]
[[File:Hoshiarpur bus stand.JPG|right|thumb|Bhagwan [[Valmiki]] ISBT]]
Hoshiarpur's Bus Stand is Bhagwan [[Valmiki]] Interstate Bus Terminal, which has a large network of bus services of [[Punjab Roadways]], Himachal Roadways, [[Delhi]], [[Haryana Roadways]], [[PEPSU Road Transport Corporation|P.R.T.C]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pepsurtc.gov.in/AboutUS.htm|title=PEPSU Road Transport Corporation, Patiala|access-date=15 June 2014|archive-date=12 January 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170112090333/http://www.pepsurtc.gov.in/AboutUS.htm|url-status=dead}}</ref> [[Chandigarh Transport Undertaking]], Jammu & Kashmir Roadways, Rajasthan State Roadways, apart from private operators.

===Rail===
[[Hoshiarpur railway station]] is a main railway station serving Hoshiarpur. Its code is HSX. It serves Hoshiarpur city. The station consists of one platform. The platform is not well sheltered. The station was constructed in 1905.The station has direct railway connectivity to [[Delhi]], [[Amritsar]], [[Jalandhar]] and [[Ferozpur]].

===Air===
The closest airport to Hoshiarpur is [[Adampur Airport]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hoshiarpur.nic.in/how-to-reach/|title=How to reach}}</ref> {{cvt|25|km}} south-west of the city. [[Adampur Airport]], is a [[regional airport]] which serves one daily flight by [[Spicejet]] to [[Delhi Airport]] and another spicejet flight to Mumbai (Frequency varies). The nearest full fledge International Airport is [[Amritsar Airport|Sri Guru Ram Dass Jee International Airport]] in [[Amritsar]], which is situated around 125&nbsp;km North-West of Hoshiarpur.
