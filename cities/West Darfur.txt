'''West Darfur State''' ({{Langx|ar|ولاية غرب دارفور}} Wilāyat Ḡarb Dārfūr), historically '''Dar Masalit''' ({{Langx|ar|دار مساليت}}),<ref>{{Cite book |last=Hastrup |first=Anders |title=The War in Darfur: Reclaiming Sudanese History |publisher=Taylor & Francis |year=2013 |isbn=9781135120009 |pages=79 |language=en}}</ref> is one of the [[states of Sudan]], and one of five comprising the [[Darfur]] region. Prior to the creation of two new states in January 2012, it had an area of 79,460&nbsp;km² and an estimated population of approximately 1,775,945 in 2018. It borders [[North Darfur|North]] and [[Central Darfur]] to the east. The [[Chad]]ian [[Prefectures of Chad|prefectures]] of [[Biltine Prefecture|Biltine]] and [[Ouaddaï Prefecture|Ouaddaï]] lie to the west, while to the north is the prefecture of [[Borkou-Ennedi-Tibesti Prefecture|Bourkou-Ennedi-Tibesti]]. [[Geneina|Al-Junaynah]] is the capital of the state. West Darfur has been the site of much of the [[Darfur conflict]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/08/sudan-signs-peace-deal-rebel-groups-darfur-200831091517458.html |title=Sudan signs peace deal with rebel groups from Darfur |website=Al Jazeera |date=31 August 2020 }}</ref>

During the [[2023 Sudan conflict]], ethnic violence has been rampant across the region.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Haq |first=Ingrid Formanek,Kareem El Damanhoury,Sana Noor |date=2023-07-26 |title=10,000 reported killed in one West Darfur city, as ethnic violence ravages Sudanese region |url=https://www.cnn.com/2023/07/26/africa/sudan-west-darfur-thousands-killed-intl/index.html |access-date=2023-07-31 |website=CNN |language=en}}</ref> This has included several [[Masalit massacres (2023–present)|Masalit massacres]].
