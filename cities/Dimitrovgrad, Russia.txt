'''Dimitrovgrad''' ({{langx|ru|Димитровград}}; {{IPA|ru|dʲɪˌmʲitrɐvˈɡrat}}), formerly '''Melekkes''', '''Melekes''', and '''Melekess''' ({{langx|cv|Мелеккес}}; {{langx|ru|Мелекес/Мелекесс}};<ref>''GRE''</ref> {{langx|tt|Mələkəs}}) until 1972, is a [[types of inhabited localities in Russia|city]] in [[Ulyanovsk Oblast]], [[Russia]]. It is the [[administrative center]] of [[Melekessky District]], although it is not within the district and is an [[City of oblast significance (Russia)|independent city]]. The city is located in the [[Volga Region]], at the [[confluence]] of the Melekesska River and the [[Bolshoy Cheremshan River]], a [[tributary]] of the [[Volga River]]. Dimitrovgrad has a population of {{ru-census2010|122,580|,}} the second-largest city in Ulyanovsk Oblast, after [[Ulyanovsk]].<ref name="google">{{cite book|title=Russia Investment and Business Guide|author=Publications, U.I.B.|date=2007|publisher=International Business Publications USA|isbn=9781433041686|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=BFC0zTM4TkAC&pg=PA250|page=250|access-date=January 8, 2017}}{{Dead link|date=February 2024 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

==History==
The city was founded in 1714 as a village for workers of the local [[distillery]].<ref name="britannica">{{cite web|url=https://www.britannica.com/place/Dimitrovgrad-Russia|publisher=britannica.com|title=Dimitrovgrad &#124; Russia &#124; Britannica.com|access-date=January 8, 2017}}</ref> By 1897 its population had grown to&nbsp;8,500, and in 1919 it was granted town status. Until 1972, the city's name was Melekess ({{lang|ru|Мелекесс}}), after the local Melekesska River which runs through the town.{{citation needed|date=May 2010}} On July&nbsp;15, 1972, Melekess was renamed to Dimitrovgrad, celebrating the posthumous 90th birthday of [[Georgi Dimitrov]], the first leader of the communist [[People's Republic of Bulgaria]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.dimitrovgrad.ru/town/history.html |language=ru |title=Наш Димитровград. История |accessdate=2012-11-05 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130329231314/http://www.dimitrovgrad.ru/town/history.html |archivedate=2013-03-29 |url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Administrative and municipal status==
Within the [[subdivisions of Russia#Administrative divisions|framework of administrative divisions]], Dimitrovgrad serves as the [[administrative center]] of [[Melekessky District]], even though it is not a part of it.<ref name="Ref150">Law #126-ZO</ref> As an administrative division, it is incorporated separately as the '''[[city of federal subject significance|city of oblast significance]] of Dimitrovgrad'''—an administrative unit with the status equal to that of the [[administrative divisions of Ulyanovsk Oblast|districts]].<ref name="Ref150" /> As a [[subdivisions of Russia#Municipal divisions|municipal division]], the city of oblast significance of Dimitrovgrad is incorporated as '''Dimitrovgrad Urban Okrug'''.<ref name="Ref310">Law #043-ZO</ref>

==Economy==
The city's leading enterprise, located {{convert|6|km|0|abbr=on}} to the south-west, is the [[Research Institute of Atomic Reactors]]. 
Dimitrovgrad's industry also includes an auto parts manufacturer (Dimitrovgrad Automobile Parts Plant,<ref name="google2">{{cite book|title=Russia Automobile Industry Directory - Strategic Information and Contacts|author=IBP, I.|date=2009|publisher=International Business Publications USA|isbn=9781438740249|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=vG-uBgAAQBAJ&pg=PA85|page=85|access-date=January 8, 2017}}</ref> [[carburetor]]s, [[Fuel pump (engine)|fuel pump]]s), a carpet manufacturing plant (Kovrotex), and a chemical processing equipment manufacturer (Dimitrovgradkhimmash and Zenith Khimmash).

==Education and culture==
[[File:Dimitrovgrad(Russia)Church of George-Victorious.JPG|thumb|Church of St.&nbsp;George the Victorious in Dimitrovgrad]]
The city has a variety of educational institutions. It has twenty-two secondary schools, three lyceums, one [[gymnasium of Dimitrovgrad|gymnasium]], three branches of state universities, and two branches of private higher educational institutions. The city also has the State Scientific Center of Russian Federation, [[Research Institute of Atomic Reactors]].<ref name="google3">{{cite book|title=Underground Injection Science and Technology|author1=Tsang, C.F.|author2=Apps, J.A.|date=2005|publisher=Elsevier Science|isbn=9780080457901|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=8RhO-B0TEj8C&pg=PR20|access-date=January 8, 2017}}</ref><ref name="google4">{{cite book|title=Handbook of Small Modular Nuclear Reactors|author1=Carelli, M.D.|author2=Ingersoll, D.T.|date=2014|publisher=Elsevier Science|isbn=9780857098535|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=rJlzAwAAQBAJ&pg=PT389|access-date=January 8, 2017}}</ref>

There also is a [[drama]] theater in Dimitrovgrad.
