'''São Vicente''' (after [[Vincent of Saragossa|Saint Vincent of Saragossa]], the patron Saint of [[Lisbon]], [[Portugal]]) is a coastal [[Municipalities of Brazil|municipality]] in southern [[São Paulo (state)|São Paulo]], [[Brazil]]. It is part of the Metropolitan Region of [[Baixada Santista]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Lei Complementar nº 815, de 30 de julho de 1996 |url=https://www.al.sp.gov.br/leis/legislacao-do-estado/ |access-date=2023-08-22 |website=www.al.sp.gov.br |language=pt-br}}</ref> The population is 329,911 (2022 est.) in an area of {{convert|148,151|km²|2|abbr=off}}.<ref name=":0" />

Founded in 1532, by the Portuguese [[List of explorers|explorer]] and colonial administrator [[Martim Afonso de Sousa]], it is the first permanent village (colonial settlement) established in the [[Americas]], and the first election on the American continent also took place there. Because of this, it is considered the "cradle of American democracy".<ref>{{Cite web |title=Considerado o berço da democracia nas Américas, São Vicente completa 489 anos |url=https://www.al.sp.gov.br/noticia/?id=417165 |access-date=2024-02-07 |website=[[Legislative Assembly of São Paulo|Assembleia Legislativa de São Paulo]] |language=pt-br}}</ref>

São Vicente is one of the 15 municipalities in São Paulo considered [[seaside resort]]s by the state of São Paulo, as they meet certain prerequisites defined by State Law. This status guaranteed the municipality a larger budget from the State to promote regional tourism. Furthermore, the municipality acquires the right to add, next to its name, the title of "Estância [[Balneario|Balneária]]" (Balneary Resort), a term by which it is designated both by official municipal records and by state references. [[Heritage tourism|Historical tourism]] and [[Beachgoing|beach tourism]] are among the most relevant sectors of its economy.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Patrimônio: Lazer & Turismo - Revista Eletrônica - UNISANTOS |url=https://www.unisantos.br/pos/revistapatrimonio/artigosfe96.html/ |access-date=2024-02-07 |website=[[Catholic University of Santos|UniSantos]]}}</ref>

==History==
[[File:Benedito Calixto de Jesus - Fundação de São Vicente, Acervo do Museu Paulista da USP.jpg|left|thumb|237x237px|Foundation of São Vicente ([[Benedito Calixto]])]]
It was the first permanent [[Portugal|Portuguese]] settlement in the [[Americas]]<ref name="Lawrence183">[[#Lawrence69|Rachel Lawrence]]: 2010, Page  183</ref> and the first capital of the [[Captaincy of São Vicente]], roughly the present state of São Paulo. Established as a proper village in 1532 by [[Martim Afonso de Sousa]] on what was then the Porto dos Escravos ("Port of the Slaves"), operated by three Portuguese colonists who trafficked on slaves captured by allied tribes, São Vicente is titled ''Cellula Mater'' (Mother Cell) of Brazil for being the first organized town in the country. The first City Council of all the Americas was democratically elected and established in São Vicente on August 22, 1532.<ref name="b"/>

A [[Battle of São Vicente|battle took place here]] on 3 February 1583 when three English warships attempting to trade with the Portuguese, was set upon by three Spanish galleons. The Spanish ships were repelled and retreated leaving one galleon sunk.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Wilgus|first1=Alva Curtis|title=The Development of Hispanic America|date=1941|publisher=Farrar & Rinehart, Incorporated|page=[https://archive.org/details/developmentofhis00wilg/page/181 181]|url=https://archive.org/details/developmentofhis00wilg|url-access=registration}}</ref>

==Geography==

São Vicente is located on the western half of coastal [[São Vicente Island (São Paulo, Brazil)|São Vicente Island]], being mostly a [[bedroom community]] for the larger and wealthier neighbouring port and commercial city of [[Santos, São Paulo|Santos]] on the island's eastern half.<ref name="b"/> A small north–south hill range separates for the most part the two cities' urban areas, but they are contiguous in the north and on a narrow beach strip in the south. A narrow strip of land of the basin of the small [[Cubatão River (São Paulo)|Cubatão River]] extends west out of the central area of São Vicente, north and parallel to the municipality of Praia Grande.

[[São Vicente Suspension Bridge]], linking the island to [[Praia Grande]] on the mainland, was constructed in 1914; a second link, the Mar Pequeno Bridge, was opened in 1981. São Vicente is linked to the capital city of [[São Paulo]] by the [[Rodovia dos Imigrantes|Imigrantes Highway]] and to the southern coast by the Manuel da Nóbrega Highway. São Vicente has a small fishing industry in its portion on the mainland, where there are a few small settlements.

==Noted places==

The Monument of 500 years of Brazil (''Monumento dos 500 anos do Brasil'') is a viewing platform on [[Porchat Island]], an islet which extends from the south of São Vicente Island. The platform was designed by the architect [[Oscar Niemeyer]] (1907–2012). It faces roughly north in an imaginary line to the [[National Congress of Brazil|National Congress]] in [[Brasília]], completed by Niemeyer and others in 1960.<ref name="m"/>
The municipality contains part of the {{convert|901|ha}} [[Xixová-Japuí State Park]], created in 1993.<ref>{{citation|ref={{harvid|PES Xixová-Japuí – ISA}}|language=pt
