'''Kilifi County'''  was formed in 2010 as a result of a merger of [[Kilifi District]] and [[Malindi District]], [[Kenya]]. Its capital is [[Kilifi]] and its largest town is [[Malindi]]. Kilifi county is one of the five counties<ref>{{Cite web |date=2021-01-23 |title=Counties of Kenya {{!}} Mappr |url=https://www.mappr.co/counties/counties-of-kenya/ |access-date=2022-10-17 |website=mappr.co |language=en-US}}</ref> that make up the Kenyan Coast.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Campbell |first=Paul |title=Kenyan Coast {{!}} Indian Ocean {{!}} Beach Holidays {{!}} Diani {{!}} Lamu {{!}} Mombasa |url=https://www.travelbutlers.com/kenya/the-kenyan-coast/ |access-date=2022-10-17 |website=travelbutlers.com |language=en-GB}}</ref> The county has a population of 1,453,787 people following the 2019 [[census]] which covers an area of {{convert|12245.90|km2|abbr=on}}.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Position and Size.|url=https://www.kilifi.go.ke/content.php?com=2&item=34|last=Government|first=Kilifi County|website=Kilifi County Government.|access-date=2020-05-29}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=2019 Kenya Population and House Census: Distribution of by Administrative Units. Volume 2|url=https://africacheck.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/03/2019-KPHC-Volume-II_.pdf|date=2019|website=Africa Check|access-date=29 May 2020}}</ref>

The county is located north and northeast of [[Mombasa]]. Kilifi has fewer tourists than [[Mombasa County]], however there is some tourism in [[Kikambala]], [[Watamu]], [[Malindi]] and [[Kilifi]]. The county is known for the [[Ruins of Gedi]], which includes mosques<ref>{{Cite web |title=The Mosque in Islam {{!}} All About Turkey |url=https://www.allaboutturkey.com/mosque.html |access-date=2022-10-17 |website=allaboutturkey.com}}</ref> and tombs<ref>{{Cite web |last=Mark |first=Joshua J. |title=Tomb |url=https://www.worldhistory.org/tomb/ |access-date=2022-10-17 |website=World History Encyclopedia |language=en}}</ref> dating from the 11th to the 17th century.

==Tourism==
Tourism and fishing in Kilifi County are the major economic activities due to its proximity to the [[Indian Ocean]]. The major tourist attractions in Kilifi County are the Ruins of Gedi,<ref>{{Cite web |title=Ruins of Gedi |url=https://www.crystalbaywatamu.com/eng/ruins-of-gedi/ |access-date=2022-10-15 |language=en}}</ref> [[Watamu Marine National Park]],<ref>{{Cite web |title=About W.M.A. – Watamu Marine Association |url=https://www.watamumarine.co.ke/about-watamu-marine-association/ |access-date=2022-10-15 |language=en-US}}</ref> the [[Malindi Museum]],<ref>{{Cite web |title=Malindi Museum: Malindi Museum Kenya 2020 guide |url=https://www.bwindiugandagorillatrekking.com/malindi-museum/ |access-date=2022-10-15 |website=Bwindi Forest National Park |language=en-US}}</ref> the [[Rabai Museum]],<ref>{{Cite web |title=Rabai Museum |url=https://www.upkenya.com/place/rabai-museum/ |access-date=2022-10-15 |website=UpKenya |language=en-US}}</ref> Matungu Beach, and the [[Kaya (Mijikenda)|Kaya forest]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Kaya Forests – Kenya |url=https://sacredland.org/kaya-forests-kenya/ |access-date=2022-10-15 |website=Sacred Land |language=en-US}}</ref> It also contains the [[Mnarani ruins]] that date back to between the 14th and 17th century.<ref>[http://www.investmentkenya.com/kenya-by-county?id=96&tmpl=component Kilifi County] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130120065208/http://investmentkenya.com/kenya-by-county?id=96&tmpl=component |date=20 January 2013 }}</ref>

== Economy ==
The county has a strong industrial sector with the Mabati Rolling Mill and the Athi River Cement Factory contributing heavily to the region's economy in employment provision and income generation.<ref>{{Cite web|title=MABATI ROLLING MILLS (MRM) DONATES FOOD STUFFS TO FAMILIES IN KILIFI COUNTY|url=https://mabati.com/mabati-rolling-mills-mrm-donates-food-stuffs-to-families-in-kilifi-county/|last=MRM|date=2020-05-04|website=MABATI ROLLING MILLS LIMITED|language=en|access-date=2020-05-29}}</ref>

== Agriculture ==
Opportunities exist in agriculture, particularly dairy and crop farming thanks to fertile soil and good weather. The county had a successful cashew nut milling industry.<ref>{{Cite web|title=AGRICULTURE, LIVESTOCK & FISHERIES.|url=https://www.kilifi.go.ke/department.php?com=14&com2=20&com3=|last=Government|first=Kilifi County|website=Kilifi County Government.|access-date=2020-05-29}}</ref> Maize and cassava are the main subsistence crops and the main cash crops grown in the county include coconuts, cashew nuts, sisal, citrus fruits, mangoes and pineapples .

==Demographics==
Kilifi county has a total population of 1,453,787, of which 704,089 are males, 749,673 are females and 25 are intersex persons. There are 298,472 households, with 4.4 persons per household on average, and a population density of 116 people per square kilometre.<ref>{{Cite web|title=2019 Kenya Population and Housing Census Results|url=https://www.knbs.or.ke/?p=5621|last=Munene|first=Evans|date=2019-11-04|website=Kenya National Bureau of Statistics|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-29}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book |url=https://www.knbs.or.ke/?p=5621 |title=2019 Kenya Population and House Census: Volume 1 – Population by County and Sub-county. |publisher=Kenya National Bureau of Statistics |year=2019 |isbn=9789966102096 |pages=17, 19, 20, 22, 31, 40}}</ref> The major communities living in Kilifi County include [[Mijikenda peoples|Mijikenda]],<ref>{{Cite web |title=Details of the Mijikenda Tribe and People and their Culture in Kenya |url=https://www.govisitkenya.com/mijikenda-people.html |access-date=2022-10-17 |website=Go Visit Kenya}}</ref> [[Swahili people|Swahili]],<ref>{{Cite web |title=Swahili (Language) – an overview {{!}} ScienceDirect Topics |url=https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/medicine-and-dentistry/swahili-language |access-date=2022-10-17 |website=sciencedirect.com}}</ref> [[Bajuni people|Bajuni]],<ref>{{Cite web |date=2013-12-30 |title=The Bajuni People |url=https://www.journeykenya.com/bajuni-people/ |access-date=2022-10-17 |website=Journey Kenya |language=en-US}}</ref> [[Indians in Kenya|Indians]], Arabs and [[White people in Kenya|Europeans]]. {{Historical populations

== Politics ==
[[Gideon Mung'aro]] is the current governor of Kilifi County, having served since August 2022.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Governors & Deputy Governors|url=https://cog.go.ke/39-main-menu/13-governors|website=cog.go.ke|access-date=2020-05-29|archive-date=27 July 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200727070121/https://cog.go.ke/39-main-menu/13-governors|url-status=dead}}</ref>[[Stewart Madzayo]] has represented Kilifi County in the [[Senate of Kenya|Senate]] since 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Members of the Senate {{!}} The Kenyan Parliament Website|url=http://www.parliament.go.ke/the-senate/senators|website=parliament.go.ke|access-date=2020-05-29}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Stewart Mwachiru Shadrack Madzayo|url=https://info.mzalendo.com/person/stewart-mwachiru-shadrack-madzayo/experience/|website=Mzalendo|language=en|access-date=2020-05-29}}</ref> [[Gertrude Mbeyu Mwanyanje]] is the woman representative elected in 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Gertrude Mbeyu Mwanyanje|url=https://info.mzalendo.com/person/gertrude-mbeyu-mwanyanje/|website=Mzalendo|language=en|access-date=2020-05-29}}</ref>

== Transport and Communication ==
The county is covered by a network of 3,678.6&nbsp;km of roads. Of this, 2,230.9&nbsp;km is surfaced by earth, 1,091.5&nbsp;km is [[Gravel road|surfaced by murram]] and 356.2&nbsp;km is covered by [[bitumen]].<ref name=":0" />

The county's main highways are [[Mombasa–Garissa Road|Mombasa Malindi highway]] and Kaloleni Mavueni.

There are 7 post offices, with 6,800 installed letter boxes, 6,120 rented letter boxes and 680 vacant letter boxes.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://www.knbs.or.ke/?page_id=3142|title=County Statistical Abstract Kilifi County 2015|publisher=Kenya National Bureau of Statistics|year=2015|pages=38}}</ref>
