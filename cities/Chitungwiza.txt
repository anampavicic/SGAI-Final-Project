'''Chitungwiza''' is the third populous urban centre in Zimbabwe and town of [[Harare Province]] in [[Zimbabwe]]. It is located on the outskirts of [[Harare]].

== History ==

As of the 2022 census, Chitungwiza had a population of 371,244.<ref name="pop"/>

There are two main highways which connect the city to [[Harare]]<ref>''Financial Gazette'' editorial of 17 May 2006 "Zimbabwe: It's Chombo's Fault"</ref> namely Seke road and Chitungwiza road.

The Chitungwiza Aquatic Complex, built in 1995 for the [[All Africa Games]], is no longer functional, and serves as a music and church venue.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.newsday.co.zw/2013/10/11/chitungwiza-aquatic-complex-begging-restoration/|access-date=22 November 2013|work=Newsday|title=Chitungwiza Aquatic Complex-begging for restoration|date=11 October 2013}}</ref>

== Informal settlements ==

Following the [[Rhodesian Bush War|civil war]], the area experienced rapid [[Rural-urban migration]]. Chitungwiza grew rapidly and the [[Squatting in Zimbabwe|squatted]] area of Chirambahuyo alone had a population of 30,000 in 1979.<ref name="JSSP">{{cite journal |last1=MSINDO |first1=Prince Daniel |last2=GUTSA |first2=Ignatius |last3=CHOGUYA |first3=Naume Zorodzai |title=Squatter Settlements an Urban Menace in Zimbabwe? Examining Factors behind the Continued Resurfacing of Squatter Settlements in Epworth Suburb, Harare |journal=Journal of Settlements and Spatial Planning |date=2013 |volume=4 |issue=2 |url=https://geografie.ubbcluj.ro/ccau/jssp/arhiva_2_2013/04JSSP022013.pdf}}</ref> Chirambahuyo was demolished by the authorities in 1982 and the inhabitants squatted elsewhere in the city in areas such as Mayambara.<ref name="WBUP">{{cite book |last1=Ramsamy |first1=Edward |title=World Bank and Urban Development: From Projects to Policy |date=27 September 2006 |publisher=Routledge |isbn=978-1-134-28696-6 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ssh_AgAAQBAJ |language=en}}</ref>

Slum areas in Chitungwiza were destroyed by [[Operation Murambatsvina]] in 2005.<ref name="JSAS">{{cite journal |last1=Potts |first1=Deborah |title='Restoring Order'? Operation Murambatsvina and the Urban Crisis in Zimbabwe |journal=Journal of Southern African Studies |date=2006 |volume=32 |issue=2 |pages=273–291 |doi=10.1080/03057070600656200 |jstor=25065092 |bibcode=2006JSAfS..32..273P |s2cid=154537881 |url=https://www.jstor.org/stable/25065092 |issn=0305-7070}}</ref> By the mid-2010s, the number of people squatting in [[informal settlements]] was growing.<ref name="Squatted">{{cite news |last1=Matabvu |first1=Debra |last2=Agere |first2=Harmony |title=Squatters: Housing shortages or lawlessness? |url=https://www.sundaymail.co.zw/squatters-housing-shortages-or-lawlessness |access-date=7 March 2021 |work=The Sunday Mail |date=11 January 2015}}</ref> In 2020, the local authorities abandoned their plans to demolish squatter homes in Nyatsime, Seke, St Mary’s and Zengeza, after a court order was requested.<ref name="NZ">{{cite news |title=Chitungwiza Municipality Halts House Demolition Exercise |url=https://www.newzimbabwe.com/chitungwiza-municipality-halts-house-demolition-exercise/ |access-date=20 April 2021 |work=New Zimbabwe |date=12 October 2020}}</ref>

== Notable people ==

Prominent lawyer, Liveours Mundieta comes from Chitungwiza. <ref name="Mundieta">{{cite news| title=Topic : Liveours Mundieta |url=https://www.newsday.co.zw/theindependent/topic/liveours-mundieta |access-date=31 July 2024 |work=Newsday |date=18 Dec 2022 }}</ref>

The musician [[Maskiri]] comes from Chitungwiza.<ref name="Maskiri">{{cite news |title=Maskiri returns with a bang |url=https://www.thestandard.co.zw/2018/03/19/maskiri-returns-bang/ |access-date=20 April 2021 |work=The Standard |date=19 March 2018}}</ref>

The town clerk in 2016 was Dr George Makunde.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thestandard.co.zw/2016/02/14/makunde-is-blind-to-the-poverty-around-him/|title=The Oracle: Makunde is blind to the poverty around him|work=The Standard|date=14 February 2016}}</ref> In 2019 and 2020, Dr. [[Tonderai Kasu]] served as the acting town clerk.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.herald.co.zw/town-clerk-housing-director-suspended/|title=Town clerk, housing director suspended|website=The Herald}}</ref> Emmanuel Makandiwa and [[Alick Macheso]] are from Chitungwiza.
