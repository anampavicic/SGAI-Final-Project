The '''Naguanagua''' ({{IPA|es|naɣwaˈnaɣwa}}) municipality is one of the 14 [[Municipalities of Venezuela|municipalities]] ([[municipio]]s) that makes up the [[Venezuela]]n state of [[Carabobo]] and, according to the 2011 census by the National Institute of Statistics of Venezuela, the municipality has a population of 157,437.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.geohive.com/cntry/venezuela_ext.aspx |title=GeoHive - Venezuela extended population statistics |access-date=2016-06-15 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160315201648/http://www.geohive.com/cntry/venezuela_ext.aspx |archive-date=2016-03-15 }}</ref>  The town of Naguanagua is the [[County seat|shire town]] of the Naguanagua Municipality.<ref name="autogenerated1">[http://www.ine.gob.ve/secciones/division/Carabobo.zip] {{dead link|date=October 2017}}</ref> It forms part of the greater [[Valencia, Carabobo|Valencia]] Metropolitan Area in [[Venezuela]].  It is in the valley of the [[Cabriales River]] at the base of Cerro El Café and the El Trigal Mountain.  Valencia and Naguanagua form a continuous urban area. The highway that runs from the centre of Valencia towards [[Puerto Cabello]] passes through this community; Bolivar Avenue in Valencia becomes University Avenue in Naguanagua on the northern side of a traffic roundabout, or ''redoma''.

==Demographics==
The Naguanagua County, according to a 2007 population estimate by the National Institute of Statistics of Venezuela, has a population of 143,315 (up from 134,728 in 2000).  This amounts to 6.4% of the state's population.<ref>[http://www.ine.gob.ve/sintesisestadistica/estados/carabobo/cuadros/Poblacion5.xls] {{dead link|date=October 2017}}</ref>  The municipality's population density is {{convert|762.31|PD/sqkm}}.<ref>[http://www.ine.gob.ve/sintesisestadistica/estados/carabobo/cuadros/Poblacion4.xls] {{dead link|date=October 2017}}</ref>

==Government==
The mayor of the Naguanagua County is Alejandro Feo la Cruz, elected on November 23, 2008, with 45% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.cne.gob.ve/divulgacion_regionales_2008/index.php?e=07&m=00&p=00&c=00&t=00&ca=00&v=02 |title=Divulgación Elecciones Regionales - 23 de Noviembre de 2008 |access-date=2008-12-07 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101202233846/http://www.cne.gob.ve/divulgacion_regionales_2008/index.php?e=07&m=00&p=00&c=00&t=00&ca=00&v=02 |archive-date=2010-12-02 }}</ref>  He replaced Julio Castillo shortly after the elections. The municipality is divided into one [[parish]] (Naguanagua).<ref name=autogenerated1 />

==History==
{{Unreferenced section|date=January 2023}}
Prior to the arrival of the Spanish in 1547, the area was inhabited by the [[Arawak]] people. The name ''Naguanagua'' is a derivation of the name of the chief who ruled the settlement and the surrounding region, Nagoanagoa. In 1553, the encomienda grant for the area was awarded to Pedro Álvarez, the Mayor of [[Borburata]]. Naguanagua was granted its own parish on 14 May 1782 by the [[Bishop of Caracas]] Don Mariano Martí.<ref>{{Cite web |title=RESEÑA HISTÓRICA DEL MUNICIPIO NAGUANAGUA |trans-title=HISTORICAL REVIEW OF THE  NAGUANAGUA MUNCIPALITY |url=https://alcaldiadenaguanagua.gob.ve/index.php/resena-historica-3/ |archive-url= |archive-date= |access-date=22 July 2024 |website=Alcaldia de Naguanagua}}</ref>

==Tourism and places of interest==
{{Unreferenced section|date=January 2023}}
[[File:01_foto_panoramica_2011-12-08_naguanagua_edo_carabobo_Venezuela.jpg|thumb|left|750px|Panoramic photo of an afternoon in Naguanagua]]
Downtown Naguanagua features the Parochial Church of Naguanagua, the Parochial House and Bolívar Square. Near the Carabobo Hospital is the Spaniards Road (''Camino de Los Españoles''), Colonial Way at the San Esteban National Park (Parque Nacional San Esteban), where one can to go down to San Esteban town near to [[Puerto Cabello]]. Also, you can visit La Entrada, a village near Naguanagua where there is the [[Atanasio Girardot|Atanasio Girardot Monument]]. 
Between the Sport <!--Athlete?-->Square are the "Don Bosco Field" (Campo Don Bosco) in the downtown, the "Simón Bolívar Bicentennial Sport Complex" in La Granja, the "Patinodrome of Capremco" and the "University City Sport Complex" in the Campus Bárbula. Naguanagua has the largest shopping centres Carabobo State including Sambil Centre in Ciudad Jardín Mañongo, La Granja Shopping Centre, Cristal Naguanagua Centre in Las Quintas III Stage and Vía Veneto Shopping Center in Ciudad Jardín Mañongo. 
Other places include the offices <!--original was 'See'--> of ''[[El Carabobeño]]'' newspaper on University Avenue, Los Guayabitos Park, the [[Naguanagua Botanical Garden|Botanical Garden of Naguanagua]] (Salvador Feo La Cruz Avenue), Paseo La Granja Park (Venezuela Avenue), Liberty Park or the Peace Park (Liberty Corner, 10th Street Avenue of Las Quintas, La Granja), Park of Attractions <!--Amusement Centre? Recreation Centre?--> "Dunas" near the first World Trade Centre of Venezuela, "Hesperia Río Convention Center" (Mañongo Road, Mañongo), and the Park of Attractions "Ditto Park" (Tazajal).

==Universities==
{{Unreferenced section|date=January 2023}}
[[Image:UCView.jpg|thumb|left|200px|A View Near FACES at the [[Universidad de Carabobo]]]]
Naguanagua is the home of the main student campus of the University of Carabobo (''Spanish:Universidad de Carabobo'' (UC)). The administrative offices are in Valencia proper. The campus is quite large and operates as a separate community <!-- legally separate or just de facto self-contained?--> with its own security, transportation, and legislative body. Also, there are other universities such as University College of Administration and Trade (Colegio Universitario de Administración y Mercadeo CUAM) near Bárbula Bridge, the National "Open" University <!-- National Distance Universiy?--> (Universidad Nacional Abierta UNA) in El Retobo, the National Polytechnic Research University of the Armed Forces (Universidad Nacional Experimental Politécnica de las Fuerzas Armadas [[UNEFA]]) in Las Quintas I stage, and the Bicentennial University of Aragua (Universidad Bicentenaria de Aragua UBA) in La Campiña.

==Transport==
===Roads===
Road transportation inside the city is provided by the Centre Regional Highway (Autopista Regional del Centro (ARC) and major arterials such as University Avenue, Salvador Feo La Cruz (North - South) Avenue(old Cemetery Avenue), Valencia Avenue, Naguanagua's Bolívar Avenue, Salvador Feo La Cruz (Easth-West) Avenue, 190 Avenue, Salvador Allende Avenue (University City Campus Bárbula Universidad de Carabobo).
[[Image:Quintas naguanagua.jpg|right|thumb|200px|View El Trigal Mountain, Neighborhoods: Las Quintas I, El Rincón, Tazajal, Palma Real and Ciudad Jardín Mañongo, from the Southeast of the city]]

===Rail===
The [[Puerto Cabello and Valencia Railway]] closed in the 1950s. A new railway is being constructed from Puerto Cabello to [[La Encrucijada (Venezuela)|La Encrucijada]]. One of the stations will be in Naguanagua municipality, and it is planned that there will be an [[Transport interchange|interchange]] connecting the national rail system with the [[Valencia Metro (Venezuela)|Valencia Metro]].

In 2016 work on the Puerto Cabello railway was described as "irregular and marked by slow payments by the client as a result of the [[crisis in Venezuela|country’s poor economic conditions]], mainly related to the drop in the price of oil."<ref name="Sal">{{Cite web |url=https://www.khl.com/features/salini-impregilo-beats-targets/122842.article |title=Salini Impregilo beats targets - KHL Group |last=Guthrie |date=2016 |website=www.khl.com |access-date=2019-04-27}}</ref>
