{{Nihongo| '''Sano'''|佐野市|Sano-shi }} is a [[Cities of Japan|city]] located in [[Tochigi Prefecture]], [[Japan]]. {{As of|2020|08|01}},  the city had an estimated [[population]] of 117,669, in 52,066 households <ref name="Sano-hp">{{cite web |url=https://www.city.sano.lg.jp/kurashi_gyosei/shiseijoho_nyusatsu/tokei_johokokai/3/8596.html |title= Sano City official Statistics|location= Japan|language= ja}}</ref> and a [[population density]] of 330 persons per km<sup>2</sup>. The total area of the city is {{convert|356.04|sqkm|sqmi}}. The city is known for its [[:ja:佐野プレミアム・アウトレット|Outlet Mall]], Sano Yakuyoke Daishi Temple, and its local variety of ''[[ramen]]'', [[:ja:佐野ラーメン|Sano Ramen]]. More recently, Sano has gained international recognition for being the home of [[cricket]] in Japan.

==Geography==
Sano is located in the northern [[Kantō plain]], in southwestern [[Tochigi Prefecture]], bordered by [[Gunma Prefecture]] to the west. The location river boundary connected by [[Tochigi (city)|Tochigi]].

===Climate===
Sano has a [[humid subtropical climate]] (Köppen ''Cfa'') characterized by warm summers and cold winters with heavy snowfall. The average annual temperature in Sano is {{cvt|14.5|C}}. The average annual rainfall is {{cvt|1258.0|mm}} with July as the wettest month. The temperatures are highest on average in August, at around {{cvt|26.3|C}}, and lowest in January, at around {{cvt|3.2|C}}.<ref name=normals/>

==History==
During the [[Edo period]], Sano was a [[jōkamachi|castle town]] and the seat of the ''[[daimyō]]'' of [[Sano Domain]]. Following the [[Meiji Restoration]], the town of Sano was created within [[Aso District, Tochigi]] with the establishment of the modern municipalities system on April 1, 1889.Sano was elevated to city status on April 1, 1943, when it merged with the neighboring towns of Inubushi and Horigome and the villages of Sakai and Hatagawa. The city annexed the village of Inazuma (from [[Ashikaga District, Tochigi|Ashikaga District]]) on January 1, 1955, followed by  the town of Akami(from Aso District) on April 1, 1955.

On February 28, 2005, Sano absorbed the towns of [[Kuzu, Tochigi|Kuzu]] and [[Tanuma, Tochigi|Tanuma]] (both from Aso District).

==Government==
Sano has a [[mayor-council]] form of government with a directly elected mayor and a [[unicameral]] city legislature of 26 members. Sano contributes three members to the Tochigi Prefectural Assembly. In terms of national politics, the city is part of [[Tochigi 5th district]] of the [[House of Representatives of Japan|lower house]] of the [[Diet of Japan]].

==Economy==
Agriculture and light manufacturing are mainstays of the local economy, with production of [[Kanpyō (food)|''Kanpyō'']], [[turmeric]] and [[spinach]] being prominent local crops. The city is increasingly a [[commuter town]] for neighboring Utsunomiya.

==Education==
*[[Sano College]]
* Sano also has 26 public [[primary school]]s and 11 public [[middle school]]s operated by the city government. The city has three public [[high school]]s operated by the Tochigi Prefectural Board of Education. The prefecture also operates one middle school. There is also three private high schools.

== Sports ==
Sano has become the headquarter of the [[Japan Cricket Association]] and has hosted a number of [[cricket]] matches between international teams. On 22 March 2016, it was announced that Sano would be home to the [[Sano International Cricket Ground]], which will become Japan's first dedicated cricketing venue built for purpose which no longer has to compete with other sports for usage.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://cricket.or.jp/en/archives/1744|title=International Cricket Ground to be built in Japan|publisher=[[Japan Cricket Association]]|date=23 March 2016|accessdate=1 September 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.japantimes.co.jp/sports/2016/03/23/more-sports/cricket-2/jca-looking-to-build-japans-first-international-standard-cricket-ground/|title=JCA looking to build Japan's first international standard cricket ground|publisher=[[The Japan Times]]|date=23 March 2016|accessdate=1 September 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cricketworld.com/japans-international-standard-stadium-gets-green-light/44367.htm|title=Japan's international standard stadium gets green light|website=www.cricketworld.com|date=23 March 2016|accessdate=1 September 2018}}</ref>
