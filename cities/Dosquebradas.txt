'''Dosquebradas''' ({{IPA|es|doskeˈβɾaðas}}) is the second largest city and a municipality in the [[Risaralda Department]], [[Colombia]]. {{As of|2023}}, with a population of approximately 225,540 inhabitants.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Telefónicas |first=Telencuestas-Encuestas |title=Cuántos habitantes tiene Dosquebradas, Risaralda en 2023 |url=https://telencuestas.com/censos-de-poblacion/colombia/2023/risaralda/dosquebradas |access-date=2023-09-27 |website=Telencuestas |language=es}}</ref> The city is connected by the [[César Gaviria Trujillo Viaduct]] to [[Pereira, Colombia|Pereira]], the capital of the department.

== History ==
The name of the city literally means "two creeks" (''dos quebradas''), Santa Teresita and Las Garzas. Dosquebradas is sometimes called the Pink Zone (''Zona Rosa'') of Pereira because it has many nightclubs and bars, which are filled with young people on the weekends.

It grew up as an industrial center serving Pereira in the 1970s and 1980s. With the construction of El Poblado neighborhood (low-income housing) it experienced a population boom, providing residence to many families that commuted to Pereira for work.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2020-05-29 |title=DOSQUEBRADAS |url=https://www.revistacredencial.com/historia/temas/dosquebradas |access-date=2023-09-27 |website=Revista Credencial |language=es}}</ref>

== Climate ==
Dosquebradas has a [[subtropical highland climate]] with an average annual temperature of 21°C.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Risaralda |first=Gobernación de |date=2016-09-28 |title=Dosquebradas |url=https://www.risaralda.gov.co/publicaciones/100234/dosquebradas/ |access-date=2023-09-27 |website=Gobernación de Risaralda |language=spanish}}</ref>
