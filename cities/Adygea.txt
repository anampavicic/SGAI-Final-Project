The '''Republic of Adygea''', ({{IPAc-en|ˌ|ɑː|d|ɪ|ˈ|ɡ|eɪ|ə}})<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/Adygeya|title=Adygeya at merriam-webster.com|work=m-w.com|access-date=2009-09-23}}</ref>{{efn|{{langx|ady|Адыгэ Республик|Adyge Riespublik}}, [aːdəɣɐ]; {{langx|ru|Республика Адыгея|Respublika Adygeya|p=ɐdɨˈɡʲejə}} [[Help:IPA/Russian|[ɐdɨˈɡʲejə]]]}} also known as the '''Adygean Republic''', is a [[republics of Russia|republic]] of [[Russia]]. It is situated in the [[North Caucasus]] of [[Eastern Europe]]. The republic is a part of the [[Southern Federal District]], and covers an area of {{convert|7600|km2|sp=us}}, with a population of roughly 496,934 residents.<ref name="2021Censusa"/> It is an [[enclave]] within [[Krasnodar Krai]] and is the fifth-smallest Russian federal subject by area. [[Maykop]] is the [[capital city|capital]] and the largest city of Adygea, home to one-third of the republic's population.

Adygea is one of Russia's [[Ethnic groups in Russia|ethnic]] republics, primarily representing the indigenous [[Circassian people]] that form 25% of the Republic's population, while [[Russians]] form a majority at 60%, and with minority populations of [[Armenians]] and [[Ukrainians]]. The official languages of Adygea are [[Adyghe language|Adyghe]] and [[Russian language|Russian]].

==History==
[[File:Adygea02.png|thumb|left|Map of Adygea]]

The [[Adyghe Autonomous Oblast|Cherkess (Adyghe) Autonomous Oblast]] was established within the [[Russian SFSR]] on July&nbsp;27, 1922, on the territories of the [[Kuban-Black Sea Oblast]], primarily settled by the [[Adyghe people]].{{sfnp|Азаренкова|Бондарь|Вертышева|1986|p=154}} At that time, [[Krasnodar]] was the [[administrative center]]. It was renamed Adyghe (Cherkess) Autonomous Oblast on August&nbsp;24, 1922, soon after its creation. In the first two years of its existence the autonomous oblast was a part of the [[Russian SFSR]], but on October&nbsp;17, 1924, it was transferred to the jurisdiction of the newly created [[North Caucasus Krai]] within the RSFSR.<ref name="Carnegie">{{cite book |title=Политический альманах России 1997. Том 2. Социально-политические портреты регионов |trans-title=Political Almanac of Russia 1997. Vol. 2. Social and Political Portraits of the Regions |edition=online |url= http://www.carnegie.ru/ru/pubs/books/volume/218103adygeya.pdf |access-date=2007-04-17 |author1=М. Макфола |author2=Н. Петрова |year=1998 |publisher=Московский Центр Карнеги |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070630091806/http://www.carnegie.ru/ru/pubs/books/volume/218103adygeya.pdf |archive-date=June 30, 2007 |language=ru}}</ref>

It was renamed Adyghe Autonomous Oblast (AO) in July 1928. On January&nbsp;10, 1934, the autonomous oblast became part of the new [[Azov-Black Sea Krai]], which was removed from North Caucasus Krai. Maykop was made the administrative center of the autonomous oblast in 1936. Adyghe AO became part of [[Krasnodar Krai]] when it was established on September&nbsp;13, 1937.

On July&nbsp;3, 1991, the oblast was elevated to the status of a republic under the jurisdiction of the Russian Federation.<ref name="Established">Official website of the Republic of Adygea. [http://www.adygheya.ru/info/index.shtml Основные сведения] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130723024244/http://adygheya.ru/info/index.shtml |date=July 23, 2013 }} {{in lang|ru}}</ref> The first President of the republic was [[Aslan Dzharimov]], elected on 5 January 1992.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Richmond|first1=Walter|title=The Northwest Caucasus: Past, Present, Future|date=2008|publisher=Routledge|isbn=978-1134002498|page=146|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=E6Z5AgAAQBAJ&q=Aslan+Dzharimov+5+january+1992}}</ref>

Relations between the Adyghe and ethnic Russians in Adygea are currently good.{{source?|date=October 2024}} Russians make up two-thirds of the population within Adygea.<ref name="autogenerated1">{{cite web |url=http://www.gks.ru/free_doc/new_site/perepis2010/croc/perepis_itogi1612.htm |script-title=ru:ВПН-2010 |publisher=Gks.ru |access-date=2014-02-22 |language=ru |archive-date=March 15, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130315114013/http://www.gks.ru/free_doc/new_site/perepis2010/croc/perepis_itogi1612.htm}}</ref> The current Head of Adygea is [[Murat Kumpilov]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://english.almanar.com.lb/1687479 | title=Russian Elections: United Russia Winning Most Votes – Al-Manar TV Lebanon }}</ref>

==Geography==
Adygea lies in Russia's [[Southern Federal District]] of Eastern Europe, in the foothills of the [[Western Caucasus|Northwestern Caucasus]] in the [[Caucasus Mountains]] System, with [[plain]]s in the northern areas and mountains in the southern area. Forests (mainly of European beech, oak, and maple) cover almost 40% of its territory.
*''Area'' — {{convert|7792|km2|abbr=on}}.
*''Borders'' — the Republic of Adygea is entirely surrounded by [[Krasnodar Krai]].

==Politics==
[[File:TozujVisit.jpg|thumb|upright|right|Former Head of the Republic of Adygea, Aslan Tkhakushinov, in 2010]]
The chief executive of the government of the Republic of Adygea is the [[Head of the Republic of Adygea|Head]] (called "President" until May 2011), who is appointed for a five-year term. Proficiency in the [[Adyghe language]] is a prerequisite for the candidate.<ref>{{cite book |script-title=ru:"Тихие" конфликты на Северном Кавказе |last=Казенин |first=Константин |year=2009 |publisher=[[Regnum news agency|Regnum]] |language=ru |location=Moscow |isbn=978-5-91150-030-6 |page=17}}</ref>

The current Head, [[Murat Kumpilov]] (since January&nbsp;27, 2017), succeeded [[Aslan Tkhakushinov]], initially as acting Head of the region. There is also a directly elected [[State Council of the Republic of Adygea|State Council]] (''Khase'' or ''Xase''—not to be confused with the Adyghe Khase, a union of Adyghe who supported Sovmen for a second term), which comprises the Council of Representatives and the Council of the Republic. Both councils are elected every five years and have 27 deputies each.

The Republic sends three representatives to the parliament of the Russian Federation; one to the [[State Duma]] and the other two to the [[Federation Council of Russia|Federation Council]].

The [[Constitution of the Republic of Adygea]] was adopted on May&nbsp;14, 1995.

==Divisions==
[[File:Adygea districts.png|thumb|Administrative divisions of the Republic of Adygea]]{{Main|List of administrative and municipal divisions of the Republic of Adygea}}

The Republic of Adygea is administratively divided into seven districts (''[[raion]]s''), two [[types of inhabited localities in Russia|cities/towns]], and (at a lower administrative level) five [[urban-type settlement]]s. [[Subdivisions of Russia#Municipal divisions|Municipally]], the republic is divided into two urban okrugs, five urban settlements, and 46&nbsp;rural settlements.

According to a 2012 survey which interviewed 56,900 people<ref name="2012ArenaAtlas"/> 35.4% of the population of Adygea adheres to the [[Russian Orthodox Church]], 23.6% to [[Islam]], 3% are [[nondenominational Christianity|unaffiliated]] [[Christians]] and 1% are Orthodox Christian believers who do not belong to church or are members of other [[Eastern Orthodoxy|Orthodox churches]]. In addition, 19.8% of the population declares to be "spiritual but not religious" 8% is [[atheism|atheist]], and 8.6% follows other religions or did not answer to the question.<ref name="2012ArenaAtlas"/>

===Education===
[[Adyghe State University]] and [[Maykop State Technological University]], both in the capital Maykop, are the two major higher education facilities in Adygea.

==Economy==
[[File:Солодовенный цех пивоваренного завода Майкопа.jpg|thumb|Maykop Beer Brewery]]
Even though it is one of the poorest parts of Russia, the republic has abundant forests and rich soil. The region is famous for producing [[cereal|grain]], [[sunflower]]s, [[tea]], [[tobacco]], and other produce. Hog and sheep breeding are also developed.

Food, timber, woodworking, pulp and paper, heavy engineering, and metal-working are the most developed industries.

===Transportation===
There is a [[Maykop Airport|small airport]] in [[Maykop]] ([[ICAO airport code]] URKM). Several rail lines pass through the republic.

==Culture==
[[File:WIKITONGUES- Yinal speaking Adyghe and Kabardian.webm|thumb|A man speaking Adyghe.]]
The [[Adyghe language]] (Адыгабзэ) is a member of the [[Northwest Caucasian language|Northwest Caucasian language family]]. Along with [[Russian languages|Russian]], Adyghe is the official language of the republic.

There are 8&nbsp;state and 23&nbsp;public museums in the republic. The largest museum is the National Museum of the Republic of Adygea in Maykop.
