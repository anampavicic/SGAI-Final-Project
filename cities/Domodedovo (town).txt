'''Domodedovo''' ({{langx|ru|Домодедово}}, {{IPA|ru|dəmɐˈdʲedəvə|IPA}}) is a [[types of inhabited localities in Russia|city]] in [[Moscow Oblast]], [[Russia]], located {{convert|37|km|sp=us}} south of the capital [[Moscow]]. The population estimated in different years are {{ru-census|p2021=152,404|p2010=96,145|p2002=54,080|p1989=55,294}}. The increase of population is due to the merger of three neighboring inhabited localities into the town in 2004.{{which|date=January 2011}}{{Historical populations|3=1926|4=300|5=1939|6=8684|7=1959|8=27686|9=1970|10=36323|11=1979|12=46968|13=1989|14=55294|15=2002|16=54080|17=2010|18=96145|19=2021|20=152404|type=|footnote=Source: Census data}}

==Geography==
The town is located on the [[:ru:Москворецко-Окская равнина|Moskva-Oka plain]], in the center of the [[East European Plain]], south-east of the capital, 37&nbsp;km from the center of Moscow, on the Moscow – Kashira highway and the [[Paveletsky suburban railway line, Moscow|Paveletsky suburban railway line]], near the [[Russian federal highways|federal highway]], [[M4 highway (Russia)|M-4 "Don"]]. Most of the town is located to the west of the railway line. The town stretches from north to south along the railroad tracks on 26 kilometers. The climate is [[Humid continental climate|moderate continental]], with cold, snowy winters, and warm, humid summers. The frequent passage of cyclones from the Atlantic and the Mediterranean sometimes causes an increase in cloud cover. Average mid-January temperature is about −10,5&nbsp;°C, and mid-July is +17,5&nbsp;°C. The average duration of the frost-free period is 130 days. Soil is alluvial, gray forest. Water resources are virtually non-existent. The main rivers that flows in the area are [[Rozhayka]], [[Severka]] and [[Pakhra]] Rivers.

==History==
The name of the modern town, Domodedovo which was incorporated in 1900, was given after nearby village with the same name, was first mentioned around 1401 in the spiritual literacy of [[Vladimir the Bold]], Prince of [[Serpukhov]] and Borovski.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://is-tok.ru/duhovnaya-gramota-knyazya-serpuhovskogo-i-borovskogo-vladimira-andreevicha-hrabrogo-okolo-1401-1402-gg/|title=Духовная грамота князя Серпуховского и Боровского Владимира Андреевича Храброго (около 1401-1402 гг.). - ИстокЪ|first=Владимир|last=Шлёнсков|date=12 August 2009}}</ref> The modern city is located on the old road, [[Kashira]] way, that for centuries [[Transport in Russia|connected]] Moscow with Kashira and many other southern cities.

In the 17th century within the town of Domodedovo two villages existed: Nazarevo ({{langx|ru|link=no|Назарьево}}) and Sknilovo ({{langx|ru|link=no|Скнилово}}). The exact date of their disappearance is unknown.

In 1781, during the [[Administrative reform of Catherine the Great|administrative reform]] of [[Catherine II]] a city called [[Nikitsk]] was founded on site Kolychёva village and was a district seat in the [[Moscow Governorate]]. The Nikitsky District included Domodedovo village as well as Nikitskoye, Bityagovo and Zaborye which were part of the territory of today's Domodedovo District, and directly, on the territory of the town itself.

Almost until the middle of the 19th century the town area was overgrown with small [[bush (plant)|bushes]] and [[deciduous tree]]s. During [[Emancipation reform of 1861|1861 the peasant reform]], on part of the land that belonged to the town of [[Podolsk]] in Domodedovo District, the Domodedovo palace forest dacha was formed.

Domodedovo was founded in 1900 as a settlement next to a train station of the same name.{{citation needed|date=March 2012}} It was called after a nearby village of Domodedovo, which is known to have existed since 1410. It was granted town status in 1947.{{citation needed|date=March 2012}} The [[Domodedovo International Airport]] is located a few kilometers away from Domodedovo proper.

In 2007, a referendum on the building of a toll motorway within the city limits took place, where 95% of the inhabitants voted against.<ref>[http://www.moscow_reg.vybory.izbirkom.ru/region/region/moscow_reg?action=show&root=1&tvd=450400785765&vrn=450400785764&region=50&global=&sub_region=50&prver=0&pronetvd=null&vibid=450400785765&type=232 Results of the referendum] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140714122022/http://www.moscow_reg.vybory.izbirkom.ru/region/region/moscow_reg?action=show&root=1&tvd=450400785765&vrn=450400785764&region=50&global=&sub_region=50&prver=0&pronetvd=null&vibid=450400785765&type=232 |date=14 July 2014 }} {{in lang|ru}}</ref> A group of residents of Domodedovo have announced the establishment of the "Russian Democratic Republic"<ref name="Note">The name is alternatively transliterated as "Ruski" by various sources.</ref> and asked the [[European Union]] for official recognition.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://en.ria.ru/russia/20121001/176339100.html|title = Moscow Region Town Declares Independence|date = October 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://prod-euronews.euronews.net/2012/10/08/russian-village-in-independence-bid/ |title=Russian village in independence bid {{!}} euronews, world news |website=prod-euronews.euronews.net |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121015170603/http://prod-euronews.euronews.net/2012/10/08/russian-village-in-independence-bid/ |archive-date=2012-10-15}} </ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://eng.rusadvocat.com/node/132|title = 23 OF OCTOBER 2012 RUSKI DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC: ARE THERE RUSKII HERE? &#124; Association of Russian Lawyers for Human Rights}}</ref>
