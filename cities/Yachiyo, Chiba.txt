{{Nihongo|'''Yachiyo'''|八千代市|Yachiyo-shi}} is a [[Cities of Japan|city]] located in [[Chiba Prefecture]], [[Japan]]. {{As of|2023|12|31}}, the city had an estimated [[population]] of  205,748 in 96,903 households and a [[population density]] of 4000 persons per km<sup>2</sup>.<ref name="Yachiyo-hp">{{cite web |url=http://www.city.yachiyo.chiba.jp/23700/page100050.html |title= Yachiyo city official statistics|location= Japan|language= ja}}</ref> The total area of the city is {{convert|51.39|sqkm|sqmi}}.

==Geography==
Yachiyo is located inland in northwestern Chiba Prefecture on the [[Shimōsa Plateau]],<ref name="nipponika">{{cite encyclopedia | encyclopedia = Nihon Daihyakka Zensho (Nipponika) (日本大百科全書(ニッポニカ) | title = 八千代市 (Yachiyo-shi) | url = http://rekishi.jkn21.com/| access-date = 2012-04-25 | year = 2012 | publisher = Shogakukan | location = Tokyo|language=ja}}</ref>  about 13 kilometers from the prefectural capital at [[Chiba (city)|Chiba]] and 33 kilometers from central Tokyo. The [[Shin River (Chiba)|Shin River]], {{convert|10|km|mi|sp=us}} in length, flows through Yachiyo and forms the upper part of the [[Inba Discharge Channel]].<ref>{{cite encyclopedia | encyclopedia = Dijitaru daijisen | title = 八千代市 (Yachiyo-shi) | url = http://rekishi.jkn21.com/| access-date = 2012-04-25 | year = 2012 | publisher = Shogakukan | location = Tokyo|language=ja}}</ref> [[Pollution]] was once problematic along the river caused by [[phosphorus]], [[potassium]], and [[nitrogen]] draining from vegetable farms along the length of the river.<ref>[http://members.jcom.home.ne.jp/sinkawa.w/rekisi.htm 新川の歴史]{{in lang|ja}}</ref> A {{convert|19|km|mi|sp=us}} walking path was built by the city of Yachiyo, and features a pedestrian [[suspension bridge]] with an observation platform.<ref>[http://www.city.yachiyo.chiba.jp/siyakusyo/kouen/shinkawa.html 新川遊歩道]{{in lang|ja}}</ref>

===Climate===
Yachiyo has a [[humid subtropical climate]] (Köppen ''Cfa'') characterized by warm summers and cool winters with light to no snowfall.  The average annual temperature in Yachiyo is 14.9&nbsp;°C. The average annual rainfall is 1394&nbsp;mm with September as the wettest month. The temperatures are highest on average in August, at around 26.5&nbsp;°C, and lowest in January, at around 4.5&nbsp;°C.<ref>[https://en.climate-data.org/asia/japan/chiba/yachiyo-764711/ Yachiyo climate data]</ref>

==History==
===Early history===
Yachiyo has been inhabited since the [[Japanese Paleolithic]] period, and archaeologists have found [[stone tool]]s dating to over 30,000 years ago. The [[Chiba clan]] controlled the area of present-day Yachiyo from the late [[Heian period]] to the early [[Muromachi period]].<ref name="nipponika"/> In the [[Sengoku period]], the area came under the control of the Murakami clan.

===Edo period===
During the [[Edo period]] the area was divided between ''[[tenryō]]'' territory under the direct control of the [[Tokugawa shogunate]] and areas controlled under the [[Sakura Domain]].<ref name="nipponika"/> It developed as a [[shukuba|post town]] on the pilgrimage route to the [[Narita-san|temples at Narita]].

===Modern history===
After the [[Meiji Restoration]], the villages of Owada in [[Chiba District, Chiba|Chiba District]] and Aso in [[Inba District, Chiba|Inba District]], Chiba Prefecture were founded on April 1, 1889 with the creation if then modern municipalities system. However, much of the area of these villages was already under the control of the [[Imperial Japanese Army]], who had established a training school in 1876 for infantry maneuvers. The school remained an active military installation until 1945. Yachiyo Town was created by the merger of Owada Town with Aso and Mutsu villages in 1954. Yachiyo was elevated to city status on January 1, 1967.

==Government==
Yachiyo has a [[mayor-council]] form of government with a directly elected mayor and a [[unicameral]] city council of 28 members. Yachiyo contributes three members to the Chiba Prefectural Assembly. In terms of national politics, the city is part of [[Chiba 2nd district]] of the [[House of Representatives of Japan|lower house]] of the [[Diet of Japan]].

==Economy==
Yachiyo is a regional commercial center and a [[bedroom community]] for nearby Chiba and Tokyo.<ref>{{cite encyclopedia | encyclopedia = Encyclopedia of Japan | title = Yachiyo | url = http://rekishi.jkn21.com/ | access-date = 2012-04-25 | year = 2012 | publisher = Shogakukan | location = Tokyo}}</ref> The commuting rate to Tokyo is 26.6% (2010 census). There is some residual agriculture, with rice and [[Pyrus pyrifolia|nashi pears]] grown. [[Pearl Drums|Pearl Musical Instrument Company]] is located within Yachiyo.
