'''Ibb''' ({{langx|ar|إِبّ|ʾIbb}}) is a city in [[Yemen]], the capital of [[Ibb Governorate]], located about {{convert|117|km|0|abbr=on}} northeast of [[Mocha, Yemen|Mocha]] and {{convert|194|km|0|abbr=on}} south of [[Sana'a]].<ref name="CarterDunston2007">{{cite book|last1=Carter|first1=Terry|last2=Dunston|first2=Lara|title=Oman, UAE & Arabian Peninsula|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=nDsjyU2Re6AC&pg=PA484|year=2007|publisher=Lonely Planet|isbn=978-1-74104-546-8|page=484}}</ref> A market town and administrative centre developed during the [[History of Yemen#Ottoman influence|Ottoman Empire]], it is one of the most important medium-sized cities in the country.<ref name="Wald1996">{{cite book|last=Wald|first=Peter|title=Yemen|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=IxEuAQAAIAAJ|year=1996|publisher=Pallas Athene|isbn=978-1-873429-11-2|page=179}}</ref> It is situated on a mountain ridge, surrounded by fertile land. As of 2023, it has an estimated population of 771,500 residents. Other names for Ibb, is "The Land of Green"{{citation needed|date=August 2023}}

== History ==
[[File:Ibb, Yemen (4325126410).jpg|thumb|left|Local men]]

Ibb has been occupied since ancient times, and due to its strategic importance, the Ottomans used it as an administrative center.<ref name="CarterDunston2007"/> It thrived as a market town and the Ottomans established the Wednesday market in Ibb.<ref name="Blumi2010">{{cite book|last=Blumi|first=Isa|title=Chaos in Yemen: Societal Collapse and the New Authoritarianism|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=C2a6eVpf_wIC&pg=PA87|date=30 July 2010|publisher=Taylor & Francis|isbn=978-0-203-84742-8|page=87}}</ref> Ibb was governed by a semi-autonomous emir until 1944, when the emirate was abolished. At times in its history many people have migrated to Ibb from areas of Yemen experiencing drought, to find work in farming.<ref name="Burrowes2010">{{cite book|last=Burrowes|first=Robert D.|title=Historical Dictionary of Yemen|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=tjXRfqBv_0UC&pg=PA169|year=2010|publisher=Rowman & Littlefield|isbn=978-0-8108-5528-1|page=169}}</ref> In the mid-1950s there was reportedly an outbreak of [[smallpox]] in the city.<ref name="El-Khalidi2011">{{cite book|last=El-Khalidi|first=Hatem|title=Sojourn in a Dreadful Land (Yemen Chronicles)|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=YGjMQp2OQAgC&pg=PA13|date=September 2011|publisher=Dorrance Publishing|isbn=978-1-4349-1197-1|page=13}}</ref>

German explorer [[Hermann Burchardt]] photographed the city in 1901; these photos are now held at the [[Ethnological Museum of Berlin]].<ref>[http://www.smb-digital.de/eMuseumPlus?service=ExternalInterface&module=collection&objectId=626078&bt=europeanaapi Ibb, by Hermann Burchardt] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924103222/http://www.smb-digital.de/eMuseumPlus?service=ExternalInterface&module=collection&objectId=626078&bt=europeanaapi |date=2015-09-24 }} (Click on photo to enlarge); [http://www.smb-digital.de/eMuseumPlus?service=ExternalInterface&module=collection&objectId=626079&bt=europeanaapi Whitewashed houses] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924103225/http://www.smb-digital.de/eMuseumPlus?service=ExternalInterface&module=collection&objectId=626079&bt=europeanaapi |date=2015-09-24 }}.</ref>

== Climate ==
Ibb is located at an altitude of {{convert|2050|m|ft}} on a spur of Mount Shamāḥī.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.britannica.com/EBchecked/topic/280558/Ibb|title=Ibb|publisher=Encyclopædia Britannica|access-date=22 November 2013}}</ref> Due to its high altitude, Ibb has a dry-winter [[humid subtropical climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification]]: ''Cwa''), and is one of the wettest areas of Yemen,<ref name="CarterDunston2007"/> typically receiving around 500&nbsp;mm of rain per annum.

== Notable landmarks ==
[[File:Ibb flickr02.jpg|thumb|right|Local architecture]]
The city is noted for its towering stone houses, with geometrical friezes and circular stained glass windows known as ''[[qamiriya]]''.<ref name="CarterDunston2007"/> The main mosque was built during the Ottoman period, and there are other mosques and also a fortress in the vicinity, closed to visitors. An ancient overhead [[aqueduct (water supply)|aqueduct]] still remains.<ref name="Ingrams1964">{{cite book|last=Ingrams|first=William Harold|title=The Yemen: Imams, rulers & revolutions|url=https://archive.org/details/yemenimamsrulers00ingr|url-access=registration|year=1964|publisher=Praeger|page=[https://archive.org/details/yemenimamsrulers00ingr/page/31 31]}}</ref> [[Ibb University]] was established in 1996.<ref name="CarterDunston2007"/> The main [[Association football|football]] team is [[Al Sha'ab Ibb]].
