'''Koutiala''' ([[Bambara language|Bambara]]: ߞߎߕߌߊߟߊ tr. Kutiala) is a city in [[Mali]] in the administrative region of [[Sikasso Region|Sikasso]], and is located 140&nbsp;km north of the city of [[Sikasso]].  Koutiala serves as the capital of its administrative [[Koutiala Cercle|Cercle]], home to 575,253 people in 2009.  As of the 2009 Census, Koutiala has 137,919 residents.

==History==
Situated in [[Minianka]] country, Koutiala was founded in the 16th century by members of the Coulibaly family from the [[Bambara people|Bambara]] kingdom of [[Segou]]. It now contains an important hospital for women and children.<ref>[http://www.koutialahospital.org/ Koutiala Hospital]</ref>
Koutiala's sister city is [[Alençon]], [[France]].

==Economy==
Koutiala is the heartland of [[cotton]] production in Mali and is sometimes called "the white gold capital" for its cotton.<ref name="López-Ridaura2005">{{cite book|last=López-Ridaura|first=Santiago|title=Multi-scale sustainability evaluation: a framework for the derivation and quantification of indicators for natural resource management systems|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=_kJEAAAAYAAJ|access-date=5 January 2011|year=2005|publisher=Wageningen University and Research Centre|isbn=978-90-6754-954-7}}</ref> However, the industry has been affected by stagnation since the 1980s.<ref name="BenjaminsenLund2001">{{cite book|last1=Benjaminsen|first1=Tor Arve|last2=Lund|first2=Christian|title=Politics, property and production in the West African Sahel: understanding natural resources management|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=1Wwfaba21mgC&pg=PA263|access-date=5 January 2011|year=2001|publisher=Nordic Africa Institute|isbn=978-91-7106-476-9|page=263}}</ref> Aside from cotton it is also noted for grain production, primarily [[pearl millet]], [[sorghum]] and [[maize]].<ref name="MorrissonLecaillon">{{cite book|last1=Morrisson|first1=Christian|last2=Lecaillon|first2=Jacques|title=Economic Policies and Agricultural Performance|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=If6MEF2bIGkC&pg=PA161|access-date=5 January 2011|publisher=OECD|page=161}}</ref> Koutiala is the second most industrial city in Mali, hosting, among others, the ''[[Compagnie malienne pour le développement du textile]]'' (CMDT) and the ''Huilerie cotonnière du Mali'' (HUICOMA).
