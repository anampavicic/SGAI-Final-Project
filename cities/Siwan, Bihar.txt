'''Siwan''' is a city and [[nagar parishad]] in the Indian state of [[Bihar]].<ref>{{cite web |url= https://siwan.nic.in/public-utility-category/municipality/ |title=Municipalities of Siwan district |website=siwan.nic.in |access-date=22 April 2019}}</ref> It is the administrative headquarters of [[Siwan district]] and one of the urban settlements out of three in the district. It is located close to [[Uttar Pradesh]].<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.siwanonline.com/category/siwan-news-in-hindi/ |title=Siwan News |publisher=www.siwanonline.com |access-date=14 June 2018}}</ref>

==History==
[[File:The bathing Ghat, Siwan, India, ca. 1906 (IMP-CSCNWW33-OS14-3).jpg|thumb|left|A bathing ghat in siwan, ca. 1906]]
The Siwan Municipal Council (SMC) was established in 1924.<ref name="smc">{{cite web |url= http://urban.bih.nic.in/Docs/CDP/CDP-Siwan.pdf |title=City Development Plan for Siwan |website=www.urban.bih.nic.in |access-date=24 June 2018}}</ref> It was the subdivisional headquarters of Siwan subdivision under the old [[Saran district]]. It became district headquarters of the Siwan district when it was formed in 1972.

==Geography and climate==
Siwan city is located at coordinates {{Coord|26.22|N|84.36|E|}} Latitude and longitude. It is counted in one of the tarai region of [[Himalaya]] Mountain range. It has an average elevation of {{convert|72|m|ft}}.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.latlong.net/place/siwan-bihar-india-6984.html |title=Siwan, Bihar, India |website= www.latlong.net |access-date=19 June 2018}}</ref> Daha River, which is a small river, which floods in rainy season pass through the west of the town. The river starts drying up during the summer. The green area in Siwan is about (21%) of its area.

The weather of Siwan is generally mild cool throughout the year and temperature falls down up to 4&nbsp;°C in December and January but it is warm in May, June and July. This climate is considered to be Cwa according to the Köppen-Geiger climate classification.<ref name= "climate"/> and is best suited for agriculture.

==Demographics==
As of [[2011 Indian Census]], Siwan had a total population of 135,066, of which 70,756 were males and 64,310 were females. Population within the age group of 0 to 6 years was 18,282. The total number of literates in Siwan was 92,967, which constituted 68.8% of the population with male literacy of 73.6% and female literacy of 63.6%. The effective literacy rate of 7+ population of Siwan was 79.6%, of which male literacy rate was 85.2% and female literacy rate was 73.5%. The [[Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes]] population was 8,244 and 1,514 respectively. Siwan had 21223 households in 2011.<ref name="Census2011Gov">{{cite web |title=Census of India: Siwan |url=http://www.censusindia.gov.in/pca/SearchDetails.aspx?Id=262233 |website=www.censusindia.gov.in |access-date=4 December 2019}}</ref>

{{As of|2001}} India [[census]], Siwan had a population of 108,172 of which 57,223 were male and 50,949 female.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.censusindia.net/results/town.php?stad=A&state5=999|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20040616075334/http://www.censusindia.net/results/town.php?stad=A&state5=999|archive-date=16 June 2004|title= Census of India 2001: Data from the 2001 Census, including cities, villages and towns (Provisional)|access-date=1 November 2008|publisher= Census Commission of India}}</ref>

===Religion===
Majority of the people follow [[Hinduism]] (65%), followed by [[Islam]] (34%). Small populations of followers of [[Christian]]ity, [[Sikhism]], [[Buddhism]] and [[Jainism]] are also present in the city.<ref name="Religion">{{cite web |title=C-1 Population By Religious Community - Siwan |url=https://www.censusindia.gov.in/2011census/C-01/DDW10C-01%20MDDS.XLS |website=census.gov.in |access-date=17 August 2020}}</ref>

==Transport==
===Roadways===
[[National Highway 531 (India)|NH-531]] connects Siwan to [[Gopalganj, Bihar|Gopalganj]] and [[Chhapra]]. NH-531 meets to [[National Highway 27 (India)|NH-27]] at [[Gopalganj district, India|Gopalganj]]. Which is second longest road in India. [[List of state highways in Bihar|Bihar State Highway-47]] connects [[Barharia]], [[Mairwa]] and [[Guthani]] to Siwan. State Highway-73 and 89 also connects Siwan to nearest town and villages.

===Railways===
There is a [[Siwan Junction railway station]] in Siwan which well connects to major cities of India.

===Airways===
There is no airport in Siwan. [[Jay Prakash Narayan Airport]] which is situated 140&nbsp;km away in [[Patna]] is the nearest airport. [[Gorakhpur Airport]] (100&nbsp;km) and [[Kushinagar Airport]] (95&nbsp;km) are other nearby airports.
