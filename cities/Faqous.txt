'''Faqus''' ({{langx|ar|فاقوس}} ''{{transl|ar|Fāqūs}}'') is a city in the [[Egypt]]ian governorate of [[Ash Sharqiyah Governorate]]. Local attractions include [[ancient Egypt]]ian monuments in [[Tell el-Dab'a]], [[Qantir]], and [[Omm Egrim]].

== History ==
Faqus is identical with the ancient city of ''[[Phacusa]]''<ref name="Hyksos">{{cite book |last1=Van Seters |first1=John |title=The Hyksos: A New Investigation |date=2010 |publisher=Wipf and Stock |pages=147–48 |isbn=9781608995332 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=PJ9MAwAAQBAJ |access-date=17 June 2020}}</ref> ({{langx|grc|Φάκουσα}} ''{{transl|grc|Phákousa}}''),<ref name="MIFAO">{{cite book |last1=Maspero |first1=Jean |last2=Wiet |first2=Gaston |title=Matériaux pour servir à la géographie de l'Égypte |date=1919 |publisher=Institut français d'archéologie orientale |location=Cairo |pages=134–35 |url=https://archive.org/details/MIFAO36/page/n69/mode/2up}}</ref> which was the capital of the [[Nome (Egypt)|nome]] of [[Arabia (nome)|Arabia]].<ref name="Hyksos"/>  It was subsequently mentioned by the medieval geographers [[Qudama ibn Ja'far]], [[Ibn Khordadbeh]], [[Maqrizi]], [[Istakhri]], [[Ibn Hawqal]], and [[al-Muqaddasi]].<ref name="MIFAO"/>

The [[1885 Census of Egypt]] recorded Faqus as a [[nahiyah]] under the district of [[Sawaleh]] in [[Gharbia Governorate]]; at that time, the population of the town was 1,610 (836 men and 774 women).<ref name="1885 Census">{{cite book |last1=Egypt min. of finance, census dept |title=Recensement général de l'Égypte |date=1885 |page=110 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=OpQNAAAAQAAJ |access-date=22 June 2020}}</ref>
