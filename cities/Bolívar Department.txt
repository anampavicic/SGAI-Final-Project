'''Bolívar''' ({{IPA|es|boˈliβaɾ}}) is a [[Departments of Colombia|department]] of [[Colombia]]. It was named after one of the original nine states of the [[United States of Colombia]]. It is located to the north of the country, extending roughly north–south from the Caribbean coast at [[Cartagena, Colombia|Cartagena]] near the mouth of the [[Magdalena River]], then south along the river to a border with [[Antioquia Department]]. The departments of [[Sucre Department|Sucre]] and [[Córdoba Department|Córdoba]] are located to the west, and [[Atlántico Department]] to the north and east (most of the border formed by the [[Canal del Dique]]). Across the Magdalena River to the east is [[Magdalena Department]]. The flag of the department bears a resemblance to the [[flag of Lithuania]].

Its capital is [[Cartagena, Colombia|Cartagena de Indias]]. Other important cities include [[Magangué]], [[El Carmen de Bolívar]] and [[Turbaco]].

==History==
