'''Yenagoa'''<ref>{{Cite web |title=Yenagoa Archives |url=https://guardian.ng/tag/yenagoa/ |access-date=2022-03-05 |website=The Guardian Nigeria News - Nigeria and World News |language=en-US}}</ref> is a [[Local government areas of Nigeria|Local Government Area]]<ref>{{Cite web |title=Nigeria: Administrative Division (States and Local Government Areas) - Population Statistics, Charts and Map |url=https://www.citypopulation.de/php/nigeria-admin.php |access-date=2022-03-09 |website=www.citypopulation.de}}</ref> and capital city of [[Bayelsa State]],<ref>{{Cite web |title=Bayelsa State |url=https://www.bbc.com/pidgin/topics/c5qvpq9d50nt |access-date=2022-03-06 |website=BBC News Pidgin}}</ref> Southern [[Nigeria]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=About Yenagoa|url=https://yenagoa.investbayelsa.by.gov.ng/about-yenagoa/|access-date=2021-09-15|website=City Of Yenagoa|language=en-US}}</ref> It is located at the Niger-Delta region of the country at [[coordinates]] {{Coord|4|55|29|N|6|15|51|E|region:NG_type:city_source:GNS-enwiki}}.

The [[Local government area|LGA]] has an area of 706&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> and a population<ref name="britannica.com">{{Cite web |title=population {{!}} Definition, Trends, & Facts {{!}} Britannica |url=https://www.britannica.com/science/population-biology-and-anthropology |access-date=2022-03-06 |website=www.britannica.com |language=en}}</ref> of 352,285 at the 2006 census.<ref>{{Cite web |title=census {{!}} Facts, Definition, Methods, & History {{!}} Britannica |url=https://www.britannica.com/science/census |access-date=2022-03-06 |website=www.britannica.com |language=en}}</ref>

The [[postal code]] of the area is 561.<ref>{{cite web | title = Post Offices- with map of LGA | publisher = NIPOST | url = http://www.nipost.gov.ng/PostCode.aspx | access-date = 2009-10-20 }}</ref>

The [[Ijaw people|Ijaws]] form the majority of the Local Government Area.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Overview of Bayelsa – Niger Delta Budget Monitoring Group |url=https://www.nigerdeltabudget.org/overview-of-bayelsa/ |access-date=2022-04-20 |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal |last=Ariye |first=E. C |version=Online |title=The Ijo (Ijaw) People of Delta State: Their Early History and Aspects of Social and Cultural Practices |url=https://core.ac.uk/download/pdf/234668573.pdf |journal=Historical Research Letter |volume=8 |pages=1–2 |via=IISTE}}</ref> English is the [[official language]],<ref>{{Cite web |last=Nations |first=United |title=Official Languages |url=https://www.un.org/en/our-work/official-languages |access-date=2022-03-09 |website=United Nations |language=en}}</ref> but [[Epie-Atissa language]] is one of the local languages<ref name="britannica.com"/><ref>{{Cite web |title=Spoken languages of African countries - Nations Online Project |url=https://www.nationsonline.org/oneworld/african_languages.htm |access-date=2022-03-06 |website=www.nationsonline.org}}</ref> spoken in Yenagoa, others such as  Ekpetiama, Gbarian, Buseni and Zarama are [[Ijaw languages|Ijaw dialect]] in Yenagoa LGA.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-11-03|title=Bayelsa moves to save Izon language from extinction|url=https://www.vanguardngr.com/2015/11/bayelsa-moves-to-save-izon-language-from-extinction/|access-date=2021-09-16|website=Vanguard News|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Kolesnik|first=Kay|date=2018-10-30|title=What do you know about Bayelsa State history?|url=https://www.legit.ng/1200389-history-bayelsa-state-interesting-facts-know.html|access-date=2021-09-16|website=Legit.ng - Nigeria news.|language=en}}</ref>

Bayelsa Airport ([[Bayelsa International Airport|Bayelsa Cargo Airport]]) is an airport located in the Bayelsa State<ref>{{Cite web |title=Bayelsa State Government – The Glory of all Lands |url=https://bayelsastate.gov.ng/ |access-date=2022-03-09 |language=en-US}}</ref> capital of Yenagoa, southern [[Nigeria]]. Yenagoa Bayelsa Airport received its first aircraft arrival on February 14, 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Yenagoa Bayelsa Airport Profile {{!}} CAPA|url=https://centreforaviation.com/data/profiles/airports/yenagoa-bayelsa-airport|access-date=2021-09-14|website=centreforaviation.com}}</ref>

Yenagoa is the home of [[Bayelsa United F.C.|Bayelsa United]], a men's [[association football]] club that plays in the second-tier [[Nigeria National League]], and [[Bayelsa Queens F.C.|Bayelsa Queens]], a women's football club in the [[NWFL Premiership]]. The two clubs won the 2021 [[Aiteo Cup]] for men and women respectively. The men's match took place at the new [[Samuel Ogbemudia Stadium]] in [[Benin City]], Benin.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2021-04-10 |title=Samuel Ogbemudia Stadium best in Nigeria —Kwara dep gov |url=https://punchng.com/samuel-ogbemudia-stadium-best-in-nigeria-kwara-dep-gov/ |access-date=2022-03-12 |website=Punch Newspapers |language=en-US}}</ref> Bayelsa United defeated [[Nasarawa United F.C.|Nasarawa United]] 4–3 on penalties after the regular period and added time had ended 2–2.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Bayelsa United upset Nasarawa United as Yenagoa clubs rule Aiteo Cup|url=https://guardian.ng/sport/bayelsa-united-upset-nasarawa-united-as-yenagoa-clubs-rule-aiteo-cup/|access-date=2021-09-18|website=guardian.ng|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Climate ==
Yenagoa experiences [[unpleasant]] [[weather]] all year round, with the wet season being warm and cloudy and the dry season being hot and generally cloudy. The average annual temperature fluctuates between 71&nbsp;°F and 87&nbsp;°F, rarely falling below 63&nbsp;°F or rising over 90&nbsp;°F.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |title=Yenagoa Climate, Weather By Month, Average Temperature (Nigeria) - Weather Spark |url=https://weatherspark.com/y/52895/Average-Weather-in-Yenagoa-Nigeria-Year-Round |access-date=2023-07-25 |website=weatherspark.com |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Yenagoa Annual Weather Averages |url=https://www.worldweatheronline.com/yenagoa-weather/bayelsa/ng.aspx |access-date=2023-07-25 |website=WorldWeatherOnline.com |language=en}}</ref>

It is situated in an area of [[swamp]]s and [[Mangrove forest|mangrove]] [[Tropical rainforest climate|tropical rainforest]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Yenagoa {{!}} Location, Facts, & Population {{!}} Britannica |url=https://www.britannica.com/place/Yenagoa |access-date=2023-08-24 |website=www.britannica.com |language=en}}</ref>

=== Average temperature ===
Yenagoa's seasonal [[temperature]] variations are so negligible that talking about hot and cold seasons doesn't really imply much.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web |title=Simulated historical climate & weather data for Yenagoa |url=https://www.meteoblue.com/en/weather/historyclimate/climatemodelled/yenagoa_nigeria_2318123 |access-date=2023-07-25 |website=meteoblue |language=en}}</ref>

=== Clouds ===
The average proportion of the sky that is covered by [[cloud]]s in Yenagoa varies significantly seasonally throughout the year.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web |last=Atlas |first=Weather |title=Yenagoa, Nigeria - Yearly & Monthly weather forecast |url=https://www.weather-atlas.com/en/nigeria/yenagoa-climate |access-date=2023-07-25 |website=Weather Atlas |language=en}}</ref>

Yenagoa's clearer [[season]] starts about November 17 and lasts for 2.8 months, coming to a finish around February 11.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1">{{Cite web |title=Table 19 {{!}} Public Perception of Climate Change in Yenagoa, Bayelsa State, Nigeria |url=https://www.hindawi.com/journals/geography/2015/208154/tab19/ |access-date=2023-07-25 |website=www.hindawi.com |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Climate & Weather Averages in Yenagoa, Bayelsa, Nigeria |url=https://www.timeanddate.com/weather/nigeria/yenagoa/climate |access-date=2023-07-25 |website=www.timeanddate.com |language=en}}</ref>

In Yenagoa, December is the clearest month of the year, with the [[sky]] remaining clear, mostly clear, or partly cloudy 41% of the time on average.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web |title=Yenagoa weather and climate ☀️ Best time to visit 🌡️ Temperature |url=https://www.besttravelmonths.com/nigeria/yenagoa-2912646/ |access-date=2023-07-25 |website=www.besttravelmonths.com |language=en}}</ref><ref name=":1" />

Beginning at February 11 and lasting for 9.2 months, the cloudier period of the year ends around November 17.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web |title=Weather in Yenagoa, Bayelsa State, Nigeria {{!}} Tomorrow.io |url=https://www.tomorrow.io/weather/NG/BY/Yenagoa/077145/ |access-date=2023-07-25 |website=Tomorrow.io Weather |language=en}}</ref>

In Yenagoa, April is the month with the most clouds, with the sky being cloudy or mostly cloudy 87% of the time on average.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web |title=Weather for Yenagoa, Bayelsa, Nigeria |url=https://www.timeanddate.com/weather/nigeria/yenagoa |access-date=2023-07-25 |website=www.timeanddate.com |language=en}}</ref>

=== Precipitation ===
A day that has at least 0.04 [[inch]]es of [[liquid]] or liquid-equivalent [[precipitation]] is considered to be wet. In Yenagoa, the likelihood of rainy days fluctuates wildly throughout the year.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web |title=Climate in Yenagoa |url=https://www.numbeo.com/climate/in/Yenagoa-Nigeria |access-date=2024-02-14 |website=www.numbeo.com |language=en-US}}</ref>

In the 7.7-month wetter season, which runs from March 20 to November 11, there is a more than 46% chance that any given day would be rainy. In Yenagoa, September has an average of 24.5 days with at least 0.04 inches of precipitation, making it the month with the most wet days.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353695988_Climate_Change_Vulnerability_on_the_Inhabitants_of_Yenagoa_Bayelsa_State|title=(PDF) Climate Change Vulnerability on the Inhabitants of Yenagoa, Bayelsa State}}</ref>

Between November 11 and March 20, or 4.3 months, is the dry season. January has an average of 2.8 days with at least 0.04 inches of precipitation, making it the month with the fewest wet days in Yenagoa.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web |title=Monthly climate in Yenagoa, Nigeria |url=https://nomadseason.com/climate/nigeria/bayelsa/yenagoa.html |access-date=2023-07-25 |website=nomadseason.com}}</ref>

Wet days are categorized into those that include snow, rain, or a combination of the three. In Yenagoa, September has an average of 24.5 days of rain, which is the most of any month. According to this classification, rain alone is the most frequent type of precipitation throughout the year, with a high likelihood of 83% on September 12.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.accuweather.com/en/ng/yenagoa/252198/current-weather/252198 | title=Current Weather Yenagoa, Bayelsa | website=www.accuweather.com}}</ref>
