'''Ambur''' is a town and municipality in newly announced [[Tirupathur district]], [[Vellore]] region of [[Tamil Nadu]], India. It is located on the banks of the [[Palar River]] between [[Chennai]] and [[Bangalore]]. Ambur has a sizeable leather industry, and is known for its spicy [[biryani]] and for the sweet, makkhenpeda. Ambur was the site of two major military actions in the 18th century. The first was the 1749 [[Battle of Ambur]] that opened the [[Second Carnatic War]] between the [[Arcot State]] and the [[Mughal Empire]]. In 1767, the [[siege of Ambur]] took place during the [[First Anglo-Mysore War]], with local troops and a British force successfully resisting an attack by the [[Kingdom of Mysore]] and by the [[Hyderabad State]].

==Administration==
Ambur is a selection grade municipality and headquarters of [[Ambur taluk]] which is an administrative division comprising 79 Revenue villages in the district of [[Tirupattur district|Tirupattur]]. The municipal council has 36 elected members. It elects a member for representing the [[Ambur (State Assembly Constituency)|Ambur assembly constituency]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://ibnlive.in.com/news/hindus-muslims-to-rule-ambur-municipality/192279-60-118.html |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130126020713/http://ibnlive.in.com/news/hindus-muslims-to-rule-ambur-municipality/192279-60-118.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=26 January 2013 |title=Hindus, Muslims to rule Ambur Municipality |date=12 October 2011 |publisher=IBNLive|last=V.|first=Narayana Murthy|access-date=8 August 2013}}</ref>

According to [[2011 census of India|2011 census]], Ambur had a population of 114,608 with a sex-ratio of 1,033 females for every 1,000 males, much above the national average of 929.<ref name="dashboard">{{cite web|title=Census Info 2011 Final population totals|url=http://www.censusindia.gov.in/2011census/censusinfodashboard/index.html|publisher=Office of The Registrar General and Census Commissioner, Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India|year=2013|access-date=26 January 2014}}</ref> A total of 13,235 were under the age of six, constituting 6,716 males and 6,519 females. [[Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes]] accounted for 16.83% and 0.57% of the population respectively. The average literacy of the city was 76.08%, compared to the national average of 72.99%.<ref name="dashboard"/> The city had a total of 25,009 households. There were a total of 40,654 workers, comprising 163 cultivators, 519 main agricultural labourers, 982 in house hold industries, 35,411 other workers, 3,579 marginal workers, 27 marginal cultivators, 174 marginal agricultural labourers, 306 marginal workers in household industries and 3,072 other marginal workers.<ref name=2011census>{{cite web|title=Census Info 2011 Final population totals – Ambur|url=http://www.censusindia.gov.in/pca/SearchDetails.aspx?Id=682333|publisher=Office of The Registrar General and Census Commissioner, Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India|year=2013|access-date=26 January 2014}}</ref>

As per the religious census of 2011, [[Islam in India|Muslims]] were majority in Ambur with 50.1%. 45.8% were [[Hinduism in Tamil Nadu|Hindus]], 3.8% [[Christianity in Tamil Nadu|Christians]] and 0.3% followed other religions.<ref name="religion2011" />

[[Deccani language|Deccani Urdu]] is the most spoken language by 48.27% of the population. [[Tamil language|Tamil]] and [[Telugu language|Telugu]] are the other main languages spoken by 44.36% and 6.17% respectively.

==Geography==
Ambur is geographically located at {{Coord|12.780200|N|78.717700|E|}} with an average elevation of {{convert|346|m}}. It lies roughly halfway between [[Chennai]] (190&nbsp;km away) and [[Bangalore|Bengaluru]] (161&nbsp;km away). Ambur has a tropical wet-and-dry climate, reaching high temperatures during summer and experiences wet winters. The maximum rainfall occurs during October and November, with the northeast monsoon. The area also experiences light rainfall during the southwest monsoon. The temperature falls up to 12&nbsp;°C low in winter. It experience a hot summer where the temperature rises up to 39&nbsp;°C.

==Economy==
The economy is dependent on the leather industry. The town houses leather tanning and manufacturing facilities and is a leading cluster for export of finished leather and leather-related products.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.newindianexpress.com/states/tamil-nadu/2017/may/30/centres-notification-to-slaughter-jobs-at-vellore-tanneries-kill-indias-leather-export-hub-1610653.html|title=Centre's notification to slaughter jobs at Vellore tanneries, kill India's leather export hub|newspaper=New Indian Express|date=30 May 2017|access-date=30 May 2017}}</ref> In the beginning, the development of tanning in Ambur was due to military demand for tanned leather primarily for boot production during and before [[World War I]].<ref>{{Citation| last1 =  Damodaran| first1 = Sumangala | author-link = | first2 = Pallavi | last2 = Mansingh| author-link2 = | editor-last = | editor-first = | editor-last2 = | editor-first2 = | title = CEC Working Paper: Leather Industry in India| contribution = | contribution-url = | series = | date = 2008 | pages = 1–75 | place =  New Delhi, India | publisher = Centre for Education and Communication (CEC)| url = https://www.cec-india.org/libpdf/1437550410LeatherIndustryinIndia.pdf| doi = | id = }}</ref> Nearly 80 tanneries are located in Ambur.<ref>{{cite news| last = M. | first = Serena Josephine| title = Grappling with a slowdown and unmet expectations| newspaper = The Hindu| location = Vellore| pages = | language = English| publisher = THG Publishing Pvt Ltd. | date = 2019| url = https://www.thehindu.com/news/national/tamil-nadu/grappling-with-a-slowdown-and-unmet-expectations-ambur-assembly-constituency-assembly-bypoll-2019/article26750480.ece| access-date = 25 June 2022}}</ref>

==Cuisine==
Rice is the staple food of the people in this region. Ambur is known for its spicy [[biryani]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.thehindu.com/features/metroplus/society/ambur-and-the-art-of-biryani/article7561508.ece|title=Ambur and the art of biryani|last=Kannadasan|first=Akila|work=The Hindu|access-date=2017-02-26|language=en}}</ref>

Ambur [[Biryani]] introduced by the Arcot [[Nawab|Nawabs]], is made with Seeraga Samba rice and is known for the unique flavours and texture. The legendary biryani came into existence by Hussain Baig, which became a family business and passed down to generations by his son Khurshid Baig through a small diner, Rahmaniya Briyani by Khurshid's son-in-law Nazeer Ahmed, and Star Biryani by his son Muneer Ahmed.<ref>{{Cite web |title=About us |url=https://www.amburstarbriyani.com/about |website=Ambur Star Briyani}}</ref>

==Transport==
Ambur has a regular [[Ambur railway station|railway station]] (code name: AB) with double electric-line track. 24 trains stop at this station.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.totaltraininfo.com/station/ab/ | title=AB / Ambur Railway Station &#124; Train Arrival / Departure Timings at Ambur }}</ref> Ambur bus stand was constructed in 1988.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.tnurbantree.tn.gov.in/ambur/bus-stand/ |title=Bus Stand, Ambur Municipality |website=tnurbantree.tn.gov.in}}</ref> Ambur is located in between [[Bangalore]] ({{cvt|160|km|disp=sqbr}}), and [[Chennai]] ({{cvt|185|km|disp=sqbr}}), and connected well with frequent bus and train services.
