'''Huánuco''' ({{IPA|es|ˈwanuko}}; {{langx|qu|Wanuku}}) is a city in central [[Peru]]. It had a population of 196,627 as of 2017<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://desa.inei.gob.pe/censos2007/tabulados/ |title=74.774 according to 2007 census: see |access-date=3 April 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100605082210/http://desa.inei.gob.pe/censos2007/tabulados/ |archive-date=5 June 2010 |url-status=dead }}</ref> and in 2015 it had a population of 175,068. It is the capital of the [[Huánuco Region]] and the [[Huánuco District]]. It is the seat of the [[diocese of Huánuco]]. The metropolitan city of Huanuco is 170,000 hab (2011, urban pop, INEI). It has three districts, Huanuco (head), Amarilis, and Pillco Marca. In this city, the Higueras river meets the Huallaga river, one of the largest rivers in the country.

== History ==
{{Unreferenced section|date=January 2023}}
[[File:Huánuco in 1615 by Guamán Poma.jpg|thumb|Huánuco in 1615 by the [[Inca]] painter [[Felipe Guaman Poma de Ayala|Guamán Poma]].]]
[[File:Plaza de Huánuco con la Iglesia de San Sebastian al fondo.jpg|thumb|260px|right|Plaza of Huánuco with San Sebastián church.]]
The city of Huánuco was founded by [[Spain|Spanish]] [[conquistador]] [[Gómez de Alvarado]] in 1539,<ref name="EB1911">{{Cite EB1911|wstitle=Huánuco|volume=13|page=845}}</ref> in the [[Inca]] town of the [[Felipe Guaman Poma de Ayala#Biography|Yarowilca clan]], [[Huánuco Pampa|Wanako]]. In 1541, the city was moved to its current location in the Pillco Valley.

The indigenous chronicler [[Juan de Santa Cruz Pachacuti Yamqui Salcamaygua]] notes that during the [[Inca Empire]], Pillco was a significant source of [[Aclla]] nuns for the capital city of [[Cusco]], stating, "...there were maidens from all nations, especially from three, namely: Cusco and its territory, the Chachapoyas, and Pillco, which they now call Guánuco."<ref>{{Cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=sbJS7zAK8BoC |title=Tres relaciones de antigüedades peruanas: Publícalas el Ministerio de fomento. [Inh.:] Santillan, Fernando de: Relacion del orígen, descendencia, política y gobierno de los incas. (S. 1 - 133.) Relacion de las costumbres antiguas de los naturales del Pirú. Anónima. (S. 135 - 227.) Pachacuti, Joan de Santacruz: Relacion de antigüedades deste reyno del Pirú. (S. 229 - 328.) |date=1879 |publisher=Tello |page=180 |language=es |quote=... había doncellas de todas las naciones y principalmente de tres, á saber: del Cuzco y su territorio, de las Chachapoyas, y de las de Pillco, que agora llaman Guánuco.}}</ref>

Huánuco was the scene of one of the Chilean massacres during their occupation of Peruvian territory from 1881 to 1883.<ref name="EB1911"/>

===Climate===
Huánuco has a mild [[semi-arid climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification|Köppen]] ''BSh''). The temperatures are pleasant throughout the year with very warm days and comfortable nights due to its elevation of {{convert|1913|m|ft|0|disp=or}}.

== Transportation ==
It is served by the [[Alférez FAP David Figueroa Fernandini Airport]]. One of the main highways of the country passes by Huanuco, connecting Lima-Callao with Tingo Maria and Pucallpa in the Peruvian Amazonia.{{cn|date=January 2023}}
