'''Krasnogorsk''' ({{langx|ru|Красногорск}}, {{IPA|ru|krəsnɐˈgorsk|IPA}}) is a [[types of inhabited localities in Russia|city]] and the [[administrative center]] of [[Krasnogorsky District, Moscow Oblast|Krasnogorsky District]] in [[Moscow Oblast]], [[Russia]], located on the [[Moskva River]], adjacent to the northwestern boundary of [[Moscow]]. It has a population of {{ru-census|p2021=187634}}

==History==
An [[urban-type settlement]] was established here in 1932, to which town status was granted in 1940.{{citation needed|date=April 2014}}

In the 1940s, the [[Antifascist Central School]], in which many foreign Communists studied and lectured, was located at Krasnogorsk.{{Citation needed|date=February 2021}} After the [[Battle of Stalingrad]] in 1943, Krasnogrsk became the birthplace of The [[National Committee for a Free Germany]] (NKFD), an organization of German exiles living in the [[Soviet Union]]. Many members of this organization would play a role in the establishment of the [[East Germany|German Democratic Republic]] and its [[National People's Army|military]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=deutschlandfunk.de |title=Vorgeschichte der DDR - Die Ankunft der Gruppe Ulbricht im Nachkriegs-Berlin |url=https://www.deutschlandfunk.de/vorgeschichte-der-ddr-die-ankunft-der-gruppe-ulbricht-im-100.html |access-date=2024-03-28 |website=Deutschlandfunk |language=de}}</ref><ref>''The Free Germany movement: a case of patriotism or treason?'', by Kai P. Schoenhals, Greenwood Press, 1989</ref>

After the [[Eastern Front (World War II)|war]], the German V2 rocket scientists whom the Soviet Army had captured were taken here with their families for [[sharashka]] work.{{Citation needed|date=February 2021}}

==Administrative and municipal status==
Within the [[subdivisions of Russia#Administrative divisions|framework of administrative divisions]], Krasnogorsk serves as the [[administrative center]] of [[Krasnogorsky District, Moscow Oblast|Krasnogorsky District]].<ref name="MoscowO_admlist">Resolution #123-PG</ref> As an administrative division, it is, together with two [[types of inhabited localities in Russia|rural localities]], incorporated within Krasnogorsky District as the '''[[Town of district significance|Town]] of Krasnogorsk'''.<ref name="MoscowO_admlist" /> As a [[subdivisions of Russia#Municipal divisions|municipal division]], the Town of Krasnogorsk is incorporated within Krasnogorsky Municipal District as '''Krasnogorsk Urban Settlement'''.<ref name="MoscowOKrasnogorskyD_mun">Law #70/2005-OZ</ref>{{Historical populations|5=1939|6=18385|7=1959|8=35183|9=1970|10=62690|11=1979|12=77370|13=1989|14=90477|15=2002|16=92545|17=2010|18=116896|19=2021|20=187634|type=|footnote=Source: Census data}}

==Economy==
The city is known for the [[Krasnogorsky Zavod]] company, which produced the [[Zorki]], [[Zenit (camera)|Zenit]], and [[Krasnogorsk (camera)|Krasnogorsk]] cameras there until the{{Citation needed|date=February 2021}} early 1990s. The coat of arms acknowledges this by featuring a prism and light rays.

Krasnogorsk is one of the few oblast cities that is connected to Moscow via [[Moscow Metro]]. It is served by Metro station [[Myakinino (Moscow Metro)|Myakinino]] (named after a nearby village).

[[Crocus Expo]], Russia's largest exhibition center, opened in 2002 provided entertainment and congregation until [[Crocus City Hall attack|the March 22, 2024 terrorist attack]] at [[Crocus City Hall]] killed 145, and injured 551 concertgoers, heavily damaged by terrorists using incendiary explosives.{{Citation needed|date=February 2021}}

==Education and culture==
Education is represented by 18 government and four private schools.

In Krasnogorsk there is The Moscow Region Palace of Culture, the Scarlet Sails Concert Hall with an organ function (the Krasnogorsk Philharmonic Society operates on the basis of the hall). Krasnogorsk has the only museum of German anti-fascists in the country.{{fact|date=March 2024}}

==Sports==
The city is home to [[Zorky Krasnogorsk]] [[bandy]] team, who are former [[List of Russian bandy champions|national champions for men]] (three titles, one each for [[Soviet Union]], [[Commonwealth of Independent States]] (the only season that title was played for) and Russia). After getting in financial problems, the team did not play in the [[2016–17 Russian Bandy Super League]], but will return to the highest division for the 2017–18 season.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://translate.google.co.uk/translate?hl=en&sl=ru&u=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.rusbandy.ru%2Fnews%2F10767 |title=Google Translate}}</ref> The club has also become national champions for women. Their home arena, [[Zorky Stadium]], has a capacity of 8,000.<ref>Rusbandy.ru.
