'''Tabora Region'''  (''Mkoa wa Tabora'' in [[Swahili language|Swahili]]) is one of [[Tanzania]]'s 31 administrative [[Regions of Tanzania|regions]] located in mid-western part of Tanzania. The regional capital is the municipality of [[Tabora]]. Tabora  is bordered by [[Shinyanga Region|Shinyanga]] to the north, [[Singida Region|Singida]] to the east, [[Mbeya Region|Mbeya]] and [[Songwe Region|Songwe]] to the south. lastly, [[Katavi Region|Katavi]], [[Kigoma Region|Kigoma]] and [[Geita Region|Geita]], border Tabora to the west. Tabora is by far the largest region in Tanzania by area. Most of the population in the region is concentrated in the north in [[Nzega District|Nzega]] district. According to the 2022 national census, Tabora Region had a population of 3,391,679.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Tanzania: Regions and Cities - Population Statistics, Maps, Charts, Weather and Web Information |url=https://www.citypopulation.de/en/tanzania/cities/ |access-date=2024-02-09 |website=www.citypopulation.de}}</ref><ref name="2012 Census">{{Cite web|url=http://www.nbs.go.tz/sensa/PDF/Census%20General%20Report%20-%2029%20March%202013_Combined_Final%20for%20Printing.pdf|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130502230453/http://www.nbs.go.tz/sensa/PDF/Census%20General%20Report%20-%2029%20March%202013_Combined_Final%20for%20Printing.pdf|url-status=dead|title=Population Distribution by Administrative Units, United Republic of Tanzania, 2013|archivedate=May 2, 2013}}</ref>

== Etymology ==
The name "Tabora" ([[Nyamwezi language]]: ''Matoborwa)'' meaning s''weet potatoes'', a common food ingredient among the [[Nyamwezi people]]. Foreigners corrupted the world " ''matoborwa''"  into ''Tobora'' and later turned it to Tabora, the name of the region today.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Tabora Regional Profile|url=http://www.tzonline.org/pdf/Tabora.pdf |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220121164943/http://www.tzonline.org/pdf/Tabora.pdf |archive-date=21 January 2022|access-date=13 October 2021}}</ref>

== Geography ==
=== Geology and terrain ===
Tabora Region is located on the central plateau of at the latitude between 4 and 7 degrees south of the equator,with majority of its land area lying between 1000m to 1500 m above sea-level. Tabora Region has an area of {{convert|76151|km2}},<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.nbs.go.tz/takwimu/references/Abstract2011.zip|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131105105231/http://www.nbs.go.tz/takwimu/references/Abstract2011.zip|url-status=dead|title=Statistical Abstract 2011, Tanzania National Bureau of Statistics|archivedate=November 5, 2013}}</ref> the region is slightly larger than the Central American country of [[Panama]] ({{convert|75417|km2}}).  

Tabora Region is in the central-western part of the country. The highest point in Tabora Region is Wumbo peak at 1395m located in eastern [[Sikonge District]]. The most prominent mountain is Mount [[Kizuge]] located in northern Tabora in [[Nzega District]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Tabora Peaks|url=https://peakvisor.com/adm/tabora.html|access-date=13 October 2021|archive-date=27 October 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211027181844/https://peakvisor.com/adm/tabora.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

The longest river in Tabora Region is the [[Ugalla River]] which feeds in to the [[lake Tanganyika]] drainage basin. Other major rivers in Tabora territory are the [[Malagarasi River]] which forms the western border with Kigoma Region, in the north is the [[Wembere River|Wembere]] River and in the north is the [[Gombe River]].<ref name=":0" /> Another prominent river is the [[Manonga River]] which drains east into [[lake Eyasi]] in [[Arusha Region|Arusha]] Region. However, most of rivers in Tabora dry up during the [[dry season]]. The Malagarasi swamp is the largest Swamp in Tabora Region and one of the largest in Tanzania. Tabora borders a small eastern part of [[Lake Sagara]].

=== Flora and fauna ===
[[forest reserve|Forest reserves]] cover {{convert|34698|km2}} (46% of the region), and [[game reserve]]s cover {{convert|17122|km2}} (22% of the region).<ref>[http://www.investintabora.com/Tabora%20Overview.html "Tabora Overview", Invest in Tabora - Tanzania, Tabora Municipal Council] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140429080251/http://www.investintabora.com/Tabora%20Overview.html |date=2014-04-29 }}</ref> As of 2019, Two national Parks are now located in parts of Tabora Region.<ref>{{Cite web|title=National Parks In Tabora|url=https://dailynews.co.tz/news/2019-09-105d77a87c7468b.aspx|access-date=13 October 2021}}</ref> [[Ugalla River National Park]] is located in South western Tabora shared with northeast Katavi Region. [[Kigosi Game Reserve]], which is officially a national park is located in north western Tabora, shared with southern Geita Region. The vegetation in Tabora is mostly [[Miombo]] woodland. however, there is a thick impenetrable shrub-land area in the northeast called the Itigi thicket. Tabora is home to birds like the [[White-browed coucal]], which is also the regional bird. 

=== Climate ===
Tabora Region has a tropical savanna climate with the [[Köppen climate classification]] of Aw. The total average annual precipitation in Western Tabora is 1010mm.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Tabora Climate|url=https://www.tabora.climatemps.com/|access-date=13 October 2021|archive-date=29 October 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211029174657/https://www.tabora.climatemps.com/|url-status=live}}</ref> While its 700mm or less in east Tabora towards the Singida border. The daily mean temperature is 23 degree Celsius. 

== Demographics ==
=== Population ===
According to the 2012 national census, the region had a population of 2,291,623, which was lower than the pre-census projection of 2,539,715 .<ref name="2012 Census" />{{rp|page 2}} For 2002–2012, the region's 2.9 percent average annual population growth rate was the ninth-highest in the country.<ref name="2012 Census" />{{rp|page 4}} It was also the 24th-most-densely-populated region with 30 people per square kilometre.<ref name="2012 Census" />{{rp|page 6}} The region's average household size of 6.0 persons was the third-highest in the country.<ref name="2012 Census" />{{rp|page 8}}

== Economy ==
Despite being the largest region in the country by area, Tabora Region was the [[List of regions of Tanzania by GDP|11th]] largest economy in Tanzania with a regional [[Gross domestic product|GDP]] of [[Tanzanian shilling|TSh&nbsp;]]4.7&nbsp;trillion in 2018,contributing 3.7% to the national economy.<ref>{{Cite web|title=National Accounts Statistics 2018|url=https://www.nbs.go.tz/nbs/takwimu/na/National_Accounts_Statistics_of_Tanzania_Mainland_2018.pdf|access-date=14 October 2021|archive-date=29 October 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211029205210/https://www.nbs.go.tz/nbs/takwimu/na/National_Accounts_Statistics_of_Tanzania_Mainland_2018.pdf|url-status=live}}</ref>  Tabora has a GDP per capita of US$2,185 ([[Purchasing power parity|PPP]]), ranking 19/23 on the mainland. 

=== Agriculture ===
Tabora Region's largest employer is agriculture at 64% of the economic activities in the region, mainly in commercial and food crops, together with livestock.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Tabora Regional Profile|url=https://www.nbs.go.tz/index.php/en/regional-profiles?highlight=WyJ0YW5nYSIsInJlZ2lvbmFsIiwicHJvZmlsZSIsInRhbmdhIHJlZ2lvbmFsIiwidGFuZ2EgcmVnaW9uYWwgcHJvZmlsZSIsInJlZ2lvbmFsIHByb2ZpbGUiXQ==|access-date=14 October 2021|archive-date=7 July 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220707054930/https://www.nbs.go.tz/index.php/en/regional-profiles?highlight=WyJ0YW5nYSIsInJlZ2lvbmFsIiwicHJvZmlsZSIsInRhbmdhIHJlZ2lvbmFsIiwidGFuZ2EgcmVnaW9uYWwgcHJvZmlsZSIsInJlZ2lvbmFsIHByb2ZpbGUiXQ%3D%3D|url-status=live}}</ref>  Major crops grown in Tabora are [[maize]], mainly for domestic consumption. Major cash crops are [[Peanut|groundnuts]], [[cassava]], [[Bean|beans]], [[tobacco]], [[rice]], [[sunflower oil]] and cotton. [[Cattle]] herding is not a major part of Tabora's economy, but a part of the resident's traditional lifestyle. Most of the cattle are the local [[Zebu]] breed, that is drought resistant and pest resistance compared to the imported European breeds in other regions. Lack of permanent rivers and lakes have further hindered the development of efficient agriculture in the region. However, Mwamapuli provides water for [[Igunga District|Igunga district]] and Igombe Dam provides water for Tabora City. 

=== Forestry and honey production ===
Tabora is home to some of the largest miombo forests in Africa and the largest forest reserve in Tanzania. The region is one of the largest supplier of natural timber and the largest [[honey]] producer in Tanzania, producing 13,5000 tons in 2008. Tabora has a very productive bee keeping industry in the region, thus earning the nick name the honey region.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=The Tabora Report|url=http://mci.ei.columbia.edu/files/2012/12/kpmg-tabora-report.pdf|access-date=13 October 2021|archive-date=21 January 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220121164901/http://mci.ei.columbia.edu/files/2012/12/kpmg-tabora-report.pdf|url-status=live}}</ref> Tabora produces half of all honey products in Tanzania. Tanzania is the second largest honey producer in Africa producing 27,000 tons in 2008, after Ethiopia which produces 44,000 Tons. Tabora produces unprocessed honey which is exported to [[Arusha|Arusha City]] and [[Dar es Salaam|Dar es Salaam City]] to be processed for export and domestic use.<ref name=":1" />  The Companies in Tabora honey production are Jempem Enterptises Ltd and Tabora Pure Honey.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Jempem Enterprises Ltd|url=https://www.supplierss.com/jempem_enterprises_ltd_e1066277.html|access-date=14 October 2021|archive-date=29 October 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211029174638/https://www.supplierss.com/jempem_enterprises_ltd_e1066277.html|url-status=live}}</ref>
