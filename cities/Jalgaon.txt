'''Jalgaon''' ({{audio|Jalgaon.ogg|pronunciation}}) is a city in [[Maharashtra]], India. The city is located in [[North Maharashtra]] in the subregion of [[Khandesh]], and serves as the administrative headquarters of its namesake district, the [[Jalgaon district]]. Situated on Asian Highway 53 and 76.3 Km from '[[Ajanta Caves]]'.

The [[Girna River|Girna river]] flows from the western part of the city. Jalgaon is colloquially known as the "''Banana city of India''" as the region's (Jalgaon district) farmers grow approximately two-thirds of Maharashtra's banana production.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.indianexpress.com/oldStory/49165/ |title=Banana republic Jalgaon bears fruit |work=The Indian Express|date=18 June 2004 |access-date=3 January 2011}}</ref>

=== Railway ===
The city is served by the [[Jalgaon Junction railway station]]. The railway connects the city to major cities like [[New Delhi]], [[Mumbai]], [[Hyderabad]], [[Kolkata]], [[Panvel]], [[Chennai]], [[Agra]], and [[Lucknow]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=How to Reach {{!}} District Jalgaon, Government of Maharashtra {{!}} India|url=https://jalgaon.gov.in/how-to-reach/|access-date=2022-01-24|language=en-US}}</ref>

==Education==

==Climate==
Jalgaon has a [[hot semi-arid climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification|Köppen]] ''BSh'') owing to the [[rain shadow]] of the [[Western Ghats]]. There are three seasons in Jalgaon: the sweltering and arid "hot" season from March to mid-June, the warm and "wet" under [[monsoon of South Asia|the monsoon]] from mid-June to September, and the  dry "cool" season from October to February. In Jalgaon District, the average annual rainfall is around 690&nbsp;mm and the temperature can range from 10 to 48 degrees Celsius, making it a diverse climate with scorching summers of up to 45 degrees Celsius.<ref>{{Cite web |title=About District {{!}} District Jalgaon, Government of Maharashtra {{!}} India |url=https://jalgaon.gov.in/about-district/ |access-date=2023-02-01 |language=en-US}}</ref>

==Tourist attractions==
Gandhi Teerth is one of the biggest tourist attractions in Jalgaoncity. It is a research institution and museum based on the life of Mahatma Gandhi. It was established on 25 March 2012 by the Gandhi Research Foundation.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Gandhi Teerth, Jalgaon: Gandhi Tourist Places |url=https://www.mkgandhi.org/gandhiyatra/gandhi-teerth-jalgaon.html|access-date=2021-09-26|website=mkgandhi.org|language=en}}</ref>

[[Guru Purnima]] festival at [[Vyasa|Maharishi Krishna Dvaipayana Vedvysa]] Temple in [[Yawal]] is also a popular pilgrimage destination. It is visited by pilgrims from within Jalgaon and neighboring states like Madhya Pradesh. The temple is located at the confluence of the river Hadkhai-Khadkai also known previously as River Harita and Sarita. Vyasa was invited by the sage [[Lomasha]] to perform a sacrifice for the [[Pandava]]s after their incognito exile. It is one of the main three temples of Vyasa, the others being Vyas Chatti, [[Badrinath]] and Vyas [[Varanasi|Kashi]] Temple.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Programs in Yaval Vyasa Temple|work=Lokmat|url=https://www.lokmat.com/jalgaon/various-programs-guruparnimela-maharishi-vyas-temple-yaval/}}</ref>

Other ancient pilgrim places and tourist destination include,

* [[Changdeva Temple]] 
* [[Patnadevi]], an old temple built by the Mandlik kings of [[Seuna (Yadava) dynasty|Yadav]] completed in year 1128 AD. 
* [[Mudhai Devi Temple]], of [[Hemadpanti architecture]] built around 1150–1200 AD 
* Saint [[Muktabai]] Temple, home to Muktabai Dindi, which has been an integral part of the [[Pandharpur Wari]] for over 310 years.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sant Muktabai Temple|url=https://templesofindia.org/temple-view/sant-muktabai-temple-jalgaon-maharashtra-169wjb|access-date=2022-01-26|website=templesofindia.org|language=en}}</ref>
* [[Satpuda Manudevi Temple, Adgaon]]
* Unapdev Hot Spring.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Unapdev Hot Water Fountain|url=https://jalgaon.gov.in/tourist-place/unapdev-hot-water-fountain/}}</ref>
* [[Padmalaya|Padmalaya – Ganpati Temple (Prabhakshetra)]], is one of the Saade-teen (three and a half) 'Shree [[Ganesha|Ganapati]] Peeth' in India. It has two swayambhu idols consist of corals and one has its trunk curving to the right and other one to the left.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Shree Kshetra Padmalaya|url=https://jalgaon.gov.in/tourist-place/shree-padmalaya-jalgaon/}}</ref>

==Notable people==
{{Further|Category: People from Jalgaon|label1=list of people from Jalgaon}}
* [[Bhāskara II]] (c. 1114–1185), aka Bhāskarāchārya, was an [[Indian people|Indian]] [[Indian mathematicians|mathematician]] and [[astronomer]] {{Additional citation needed|date=September 2023}}
* [[Bahinabai Chaudhari]] (1880–1951), a farmer whose poetry, published posthumously, helped popularize the Ahirani dialect.
* [[Pandurang Sadashiv Sane]], aka Sane Guruji, social activist and freedom fighter worked as the teacher in Pratap High School in [[Amalner]] town.<ref>{{Cite news|last=tojsiab|title=पांडुरंग सदाशिव साने इतिहास देखें अर्थ और सामग्री – hmoob.in|language=en|url=https://www.hmoob.in/wiki/Sane_Guruji|access-date=2022-01-26}}</ref>
* [[Balkavi]] (1890–1918), a Marathi poet.
* Keki Moos (1912-1989), a famous artist and painter<ref>{{Cite news|title=A forgotten genius: State ignores restoration of Keki Moos|work=Mumbai Mirror|url=https://mumbaimirror.indiatimes.com/mumbai/other/a-forgotten-genius-state-ignores-restoration-of-keki-moos-extraordinary-artwork-decrepit-bungalow/articleshow/50520293.cms}}</ref>
* [[Pratibha Patil]] (1934–present), a former [[president of India]] (2007–12) and governor of [[Rajasthan]] (2004–07).
* [[Suresh Jain]], 8 times MLA from city and Ex-minister of Govt of Maharashtra.
* [[Ishwarlal Jain]] (1946 – present), three times MLA and 6 years Rajya Sabha MP. NCP Party Treasurer.
* [[Bhavarlal Jain]] (1937–2016), an entrepreneur who founded [[Jain Irrigation Systems]].
* [[Ujjwal Nikam]], a public prosecutor who has worked on high-profile murder and terrorism cases.
* [[Haribhau Jawale]] (1953 – present), 3 term MLA and Member of Parliament from Raver.
* [[Eknath Khadse]] (1952–present), a politician of the [[Nationalist Congress Party]].
* [[Namdeo Dhondo Mahanor]] (1942–2023), a Marathi poet and recipient of the [[Padma Shri]] award.
* [[Gulab Raghunath Patil]] (1966–present) Senior Leader of Shiv Sena and Minister in Maharashtra.
* [[Suresh Damu Bhole]] (1965–present), MLA & President of [[Bharatiya Janata Party]] Jalgaon District.
