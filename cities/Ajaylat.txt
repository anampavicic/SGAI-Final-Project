'''Ajaylat''' ({{langx|ar|العجيلات}} ''al ʿajaylat'') is the name of a [[region]] and small city located in the [[Nuqat al Khams]] district of [[Libya]]. It is located roughly 80 kilometers west of [[Tripoli, Libya|Tripoli]]. From 1983 to 1988, the region was a [[Districts of Libya|district of Libya]] with the city as its capital.<ref name="statoids">{{cite web|url=http://statoids.com/uly.html|title=Districts of Libya|publisher=Statoids.com|access-date=7 November 2010| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20101129045155/http://statoids.com/uly.html| archive-date= 29 November 2010 <!--DASHBot-->|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Tribal groups==
The regional tribal groups (''qabā’il'')<ref>القبائل tribe - Hans Wehr Dictionary of Modern Written Arabic p.868</ref> are currently subdivided into two primary tribal federations or sections. The first of these is known as Gehat al-Wadi (جهة الوادي) ''Aulād'' Hamid, which consists of; ''Al-e’rīshāt'' ( العريشات); ''Al-ma’īzāt'' (المعيزات); ''Ul-qauādī'' (القواضى); and ''Al-hersheh'' (الهرشة) tribes.<ref>''Aulād'' means Descendants – ''Ibid'' p.1285</ref>

The second tribal federation is known as the ''Aulād Rāshid (''اولاد راشد''), ''which consists of; Aulād Al-Sheikh; and Aulād Rāshid. Aulad Al-Shikh (اولاد الشيخ) which consists of Aulad Mousa (اولاد موسى)  Aulad Bozed (اولاد ابوزيد) Al-Mashara (المشارة), and Al-Draba (الدرباء) tribes.

The total population in the ''minţaqa'' (area) Al-A’jēlāt is estimated at 100,000 individuals scattered among those tribes.

==Economy==
The Ajaylat region is most famous for the cultivation of [[palm trees]], [[olives]] and various types of [[vegetables]]. There is also a medical center.

==Libyan civil war==
On 14 August 2011, [[anti-Gaddafi forces]] fighting in the [[2011 Libyan Civil War|Libyan Civil War]] claimed they had taken Ajaylat.
