'''Abohar''' is a city and [[municipal corporation]] in the [[Fazilka district]] of the [[India]]n [[States and territories of India|state]] of [[Punjab (India)|Punjab]],<ref>{{cite web |title=Municipal Council - Fazilka |url=https://fazilka.nic.in/public-utility/muncipal-council-abohar/ |website=fazilka.nic.in |access-date=21 August 2020}}</ref> southeast of [[Fazilka|Fazilka city]] and northeast of [[Sri Ganganagar]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.puda.nic.in/img/approved_masterplan_files/Abohar_rpt_2011.pdf |title=Archived copy |access-date=19 November 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161109102300/http://www.puda.nic.in/img/approved_masterplan_files/Abohar_rpt_2011.pdf |archive-date=9 November 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref> It is near the India-Pakistan border. Abohar's population is 145,302 as of 2011.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.censusindia.gov.in/pca/SearchDetails.aspx?Id=39067 |title=Census India 2011|publisher=Office of the Registrar General and Census Commissioner; India |access-date=1 January 2017}}</ref> The city is known for [[kinnow]] production and accounts for 60% of the country's produce.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Kamal |first1=Neel |title=Punjab: Good kinnow crop this year, but salinity poses a challenge |url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/amritsar/good-kinnow-crop-this-year-but-salinity-poses-a-challenge/articleshow/68226131.cms |access-date=14 February 2020 |work=The Times of India |date=2 March 2019 |language=en}}</ref>

==Wildlife sanctuary==
Abohar is home to the [[Abohar Wildlife Sanctuary]], which provides a free-range sanctuary for [[black buck]]s.  The open sanctuary is spread over an area of 18,650 hectares across farmlands and villages.<ref>{{cite web |title=Black Buck Sanctuary Abohar {{!}} District Fazilka, Government of Punjab {{!}} India |url=https://fazilka.nic.in/tourist-place/black-buck-sanctuary-abohar/ |website=fazilka.nic.in |access-date=22 February 2021}}</ref> The Abohar Wildlife Sanctuary is located in the Fazilka District of Punjab. The formation of sanctuary relates to a unique success story of wildlife conservation in which people of Bishnoi community united themselves for protecting the [[Black Buck]] which is regarded as a sacred animal by them. It is also the State Animal of Punjab. It is home to several varieties of flora and fauna viz. [[Albizia lebbeck]], [[Vachellia nilotica|Acacia nilotica]], [[Azadirachta indica]], [[Vachellia_tortilis|A. tortilis]], [[Nilgai]], [[porcupine]], hare, jackal, etc.

'''Location and distance'''

[[Abohar]], located approximately 32 kilometers (19.8 miles) south of [[Fazilka]], is the nearest border town to [[Fazilka]]. It serves as a connection point between the two Indian states of [[Punjab]] and [[Rajasthan]]. Within [[India]], [[Abohar]] is situated 75.6 kilometers (47 miles) west of [[Bathinda]], 258 kilometers southwest of the holy city of [[Amritsar]], 217 kilometers southwest of the industrial city of [[Ludhiana]], 300 kilometers southwest of the state capital [[Chandigarh]], and 373 kilometers northwest of the national capital, [[New Delhi]].
