'''Ismailia Governorate''' ({{langx|ar|محافظة الإسماعيلية}}) is one of the Canal Zone [[governorates of Egypt]]. Located in the northeastern part of the country, its capital is the city of [[Ismailia]]. It was named after [[Isma'il Pasha|Ismail Pasha]], who as Ottoman Viceroy of Egypt, oversaw the country during the building of the [[Suez Canal]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Ismail-Pasha|url=https://www.britannica.com/biography/Ismail-Pasha|website=Britannica|access-date=16 October 2016|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161019030103/https://www.britannica.com/biography/Ismail-Pasha|archive-date=19 October 2016}}</ref> It is located between the other two Canal governorates; [[Port Said Governorate]], in the Northern part of Egypt and [[Suez Governorate]].<ref name="Al-Ismailiyyah" />

==Municipal divisions==
The governorate is divided into [[Subdivisions of Egypt#Municipal divisions|municipal divisions]], with a total estimated population as of January 2023 of 1,479,511.<ref>{{Cite web |title=الجهاز المركزي للتعبئة العامة والإحصاء |url=https://www.capmas.gov.eg/Pages/populationClock.aspx |access-date=2024-08-14 |website=www.capmas.gov.eg}}</ref> In the case of Ismailia governorate, there are 4 kism, 5 markaz and 1 new city.<ref name="CAPMAS2023" />

The divisions are generally seven: Ismailia which is the capital, Tell El Kebir, Abu Suwir,  Qassasin, Fayid, Qantara West and Qantara East.<ref name="Egypt Governorate Site">{{cite web |title=Ismailia site |url=http://www.ismailia.gov.eg/pages/default.as |website=ismailia.gov.eg |language=ar |publisher=Ismailia Government }}{{Dead link|date=January 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref><ref name="Egypt Gov Map of Ismailia">{{cite web |title=Map of Ismailia |url=http://www.ismailia.gov.eg/ImageLibrary/%D8%AE%D8%B1%D9%8A%D8%B7%D8%A9%20%D9%85%D8%AD%D8%A7%D9%81%D8%B8%D8%A9%20%D8%A7%D9%84%D8%A7%D8%B3%D9%85%D8%A7%D8%B9%D9%8A%D9%84%D9%8A%D8%A9.jpg |website=ismailia.gov.eg |publisher=Ismailia Government |access-date=2018-11-26 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180224074652/http://ismailia.gov.eg/ImageLibrary/%D8%AE%D8%B1%D9%8A%D8%B7%D8%A9%20%D9%85%D8%AD%D8%A7%D9%81%D8%B8%D8%A9%20%D8%A7%D9%84%D8%A7%D8%B3%D9%85%D8%A7%D8%B9%D9%8A%D9%84%D9%8A%D8%A9.jpg |archive-date=2018-02-24 |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Population==
According to population estimates, in 2015 the majority of residents in the governorate lived in rural areas, with an urbanization rate of only 45.4%. Out of an estimated 1,479,511 people residing in the governorate, 807,813 people lived in rural areas as opposed to only 671,678 in urban areas.<ref name="pop">{{cite web|title=Population Estimates By Sex & Governorate 1/1/2015|url=http://www.msrintranet.capmas.gov.eg/pdf/EgyptinFigures2015/EgyptinFigures/Tables/PDF/1-%20%D8%A7%D9%84%D8%B3%D9%83%D8%A7%D9%86/pop.pdf|website=CAPMAS|access-date=23 October 2016|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151019093240/http://www.msrintranet.capmas.gov.eg/pdf/EgyptinFigures2015/EgyptinFigures/Tables/PDF/1-%20%D8%A7%D9%84%D8%B3%D9%83%D8%A7%D9%86/pop.pdf|archive-date=19 October 2015}}</ref>

==Annual film festival==
Hashim El Nahas founded the [[Ismailia International Film Festival for Documentaries and Shorts]] and originally considered establishing it in Luxor. Instead this festival has been celebrating its annual events in Ismailia Governorate. It is sponsored by the Ministry of Culture, Ismailia Governorate, The National Cinema Center and other sponsors.<ref name="FilmFestival">{{cite web|title=Ismailia International Film Festival For Documentaries and Shorts|url=http://ismailiafilmfest.com/ismailia2015/IsmailiaFestival_En.aspx|website=Ismailia FilmFestival|access-date=23 October 2016|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161023143838/http://ismailiafilmfest.com/ismailia2015/IsmailiaFestival_En.aspx|archive-date=23 October 2016}}</ref>

==Ismailia Canal==
The Ismailia Governorate is on the banks of the Suez Canal and its [[Ismailia Canal]] extends from the Nile River near Cairo, to the Suez Canal at the city of Ismailia, on [[Lake Timsah]].<ref>{{cite book|title=Merriam Webster's Geographical Dictionary|date=1997|isbn=0-87779-546-0|page=[https://archive.org/details/merriamwebstersg1998merr/page/535 535]|publisher=Merriam-Webster |edition=3rd|url-access=registration|url=https://archive.org/details/merriamwebstersg1998merr/page/535}}</ref> The Ismailia Canal was built to provide fresh water to workers during the building of the Suez Canal.<ref name="Al-Ismailiyyah">{{cite web|title=Al-Ismailiyyah|url=https://www.britannica.com/place/Al-Ismailiyyah|website=Britannica|access-date=17 October 2016|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161019041612/https://www.britannica.com/place/Al-Ismailiyyah|archive-date=19 October 2016}}</ref> The [[Suez Canal Authority]] headquarters is located in Ismailia.<ref>{{cite web|title=Suez Canal|url=https://www.suezcanal.gov.eg/sc.aspx?show=67|website=Suez Canal Government of Egypt|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161018215850/https://www.suezcanal.gov.eg/sc.aspx?show=67|archive-date=2016-10-18|access-date=2016-10-17}}</ref>

==Conflicts and wars==
From 1967 to 1970, Ismailia became a war zone and was damaged in the [[War of Attrition|conflict with Israel]]. Many battles were fought, in and around Ismailia, including at the end of the conflict in the [[Battle of Ismailia]]<ref name="Yom Kippur">{{cite book|last1=Dunstan|first1=Simon|title=The Yom Kippur War 1973 (2): The Sinai|date=20 November 2012|publisher=Bloomsbury Publishing|page=9|isbn=9781846036484|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=3Em1CwAAQBAJ&q=Yom+Kippur+War+Ismailia&pg=PA93}}</ref> and the area was able to begin rebuilding after a cease-fire was negotiated between Egypt and Israel in 1973.<ref name="Conflicts">{{cite book|last1=Tucker|first1=Spencer C.|title=Wars That Changed History: 50 of the World's Greatest Conflicts|date=2015|publisher=ABC-CLIO|isbn=9781610697866|page=549|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=xhGFCgAAQBAJ&q=Yom+Kippur+War+Ismailia&pg=PA549}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|last1=Potholm|first1=Christian P.|title=Understanding War: An Annotated Bibliography|date=2016|publisher=UPA|isbn=9780761867746|page=584|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=VGy2DAAAQBAJ&q=Yom+Kippur+War+Ismailia&pg=PA584}}</ref>

==UN sustainable programme==
After a successful SIP (Sustainable Ismailia Programme) from 1992 to 1997, the United Nations expanded the project to include the entire Governorate of Ismailia calling it SGIP (Sustainable Ismailia Governorate Programme). Its goal is to work with local partners to improve the communities in Ismailia.<ref>{{cite book|title=Sustainable Cities Programme 1990-2000: A Decade of United Nations Support for Broad-based Participatory Management of Urban Development|date=2002|publisher=UN|page=30|isbn=9789211316322|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=wJekgiYoA5YC&q=Ismailia&pg=PA30   }}</ref>
