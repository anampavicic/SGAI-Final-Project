}}'''Hama Governorate''' ({{langx|ar|مُحافظة حماة}} / [[ALA-LC]]: ''Muḥāfaẓat Ḥamā'') is one of the 14 [[Governorates of Syria|governorates]] of [[Syria]]. It is situated in western-central Syria, bordering [[Idlib Governorate|Idlib]] and [[Aleppo Governorate]]s to the north, [[Raqqa Governorate]] to the east, [[Homs Governorate]] to the south, and [[Tartus Governorate|Tartus]] and [[Latakia Governorate]] to the west. It is the only Governorate (excluding [[Damascus Governorate|Damascus]]) that does not border a foreign country. Measures of its area vary from 8,844&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup><ref name="Statoids">{{cite web|url=http://www.statoids.com/usy.html|title=Syria Provinces|website=www.statoids.com}}</ref> to 8,883&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>,<ref>{{cite web| url = http://www.citypopulation.de/Syria.html| title = Syria: Governorates, Major Cities & Localities - Population Statistics, Maps, Charts, Weather and Web Information}}</ref> with its capital being the city of [[Hama]].

===Modern Syria===
Hama has historically been a centre of opposition to the Assad regime, and it was the centre of an [[Islamist uprising in Syria|uprising]] in the late 1970s to the early 1980s that resulted in the [[1982 Hama massacre]].<ref name=time1>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,921108,00.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101015051513/http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,921108,00.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=October 15, 2010|title=Syria: Bloody Challenge to Assad|date=8 March 1982|magazine=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]}}</ref><ref name="New York Times, 12 February 1982">{{cite news|author=JOHN KIFNER|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1982/02/12/world/syrian-troops-are-said-to-battle-rebels-encircled-in-central-city.html |title=Syrian Troops Are Said To Battle Rebels Encircled in Central City |location=Hama (Syria); Syria |work=The New York Times |date=12 February 1982 |access-date=20 January 2012}}</ref>

===Syrian Civil War===
The city was one of several that saw anti-Assad protests in 2011, the violent suppression of which ultimately led to the outbreak of civil war.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://english.aljazeera.net/news/middleeast/2011/07/2011780473138345.html |title='Half a million' protest on streets of Hama - Middle East |publisher=Al Jazeera English |date=8 July 2011 |access-date=3 August 2011}}</ref> Despite this, for the most part the governorate has stayed under the Syrian Government's control, with exceptions of parts of the north-west in the early years of the conflict.<ref>{{cite news |title=Rebels seize towns in central Syria |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-syria-crisis-idUSBRE8AJ1FK20121219 |access-date=20 December 2012 |work=Reuters |date=19 December 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121219142936/http://www.reuters.com/article/2012/12/19/us-syria-crisis-idUSBRE8AJ1FK20121219 |archive-date=19 December 2012 |url-status=live }}</ref> The eastern desert regions of the governorate fell under the control of [[Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant]] by 2016,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.almasdarnews.com/article/breaking-saa-begins-military-operation-recapture-isis-held-raqqa/|title=Syrian Army begins new offensive to liberate Raqqa|work=Al-Masdar news|date=2 June 2016|access-date=23 March 2020|archive-date=8 May 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190508193412/https://www.almasdarnews.com/article/breaking-saa-begins-military-operation-recapture-isis-held-raqqa/|url-status=dead}}</ref> but by September 2017 the Syrian Army had managed to oust them.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.almasdarnews.com/article/breaking-isis-agrees-surrender-territory-rural-hama-syrian-army/|title=Breaking: ISIS agrees to surrender all its territory in rural Hama to the Syrian Army|date=2017-09-21|work=AMN - Al-Masdar News {{!}} المصدر نيوز|access-date=2017-09-21|language=en-US}}</ref>

==Geography==
The western regions of the governorate are part of the [[Syrian Coastal Mountain Range]] (Nusayriyah Mountains), running north to south.<ref name="Syria-profile">Federal Research Division, Library of Congress (2005) [http://memory.loc.gov/frd/cs/profiles/Syria.pdf "Country Profile: Syria"] page 5</ref> Roughly parallel with the mountains to the east is the [[Al-Ghab Plain]], with the [[Orontes river]] flowing through it, Hama city lies on this river.<ref name=aquastat>{{cite web|title=Asi-Orontes Basin|url=http://www.fao.org/nr/water/aquastat/basins/asi-orontes/index.stm|publisher=Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations|access-date=18 March 2018|date=2016}}</ref> The central and eastern regions of the governorate consist of flatter desert terrain.

==Economy==
Agriculture dominates the labor force of Hama Governorate, with 48% of employment occurring within this sector. The remaining employment is spread between industry (22%) and trade and other services (30%). The main agricultural crops are wheat, barley, cotton, beetroot, onion, tobacco, and various vegetables. Fruit trees such as olive, pistachio, apple, pear, plum and peach are also commonly grown here. Animal breeding of sheep, goats, poultry and bees are prevalent in Hama.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |date=July 2015 |title=Economics of Hama countryside |url=https://www.syrianef.org/assets/reports/english/economics_of_hama.pdf |access-date=29 October 2022 |website=syrianef.org |publisher=Syrian Economic Forum}}</ref>

Between 1926 and 1949, Hama witnessed developments in its public sector with industry expanding on sugar plants, onion drying, oils manufacturing, cotton grinning factories, and cement production and processing. Other manufacturing industry that was expanded include soda, ice cream, candy, textile, wool, cotton, rugs, carpets, gowns, and [[horse saddles]]. The region is also Syria's center of its chemical and manufacturing industry.<ref name=":0" />

== Demographics ==
As per the 2004 Syrian census the population was 1,385,000.<ref name="Statoids"/> A 2011 [[UNOCHA]] estimate put the population at 1,628,000, though this has likely changed since the start of the war.<ref>{{citation |url=https://reliefweb.int/sites/reliefweb.int/files/resources/Syria%20governorate%20profiles%206%20August%202014.pdf|publisher= UNOCHA |title= Syrian Arab Republic - Governorates profile|date=June 2014|access-date= 20 March 2020}}</ref>
