'''Suryapet'''{{IPA audio link|Suryapet pronunciation.ogg}} is a city in the Indian state of [[Telangana]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Cities having population 1 lakh and above, Census 2011|url=http://censusindia.gov.in/2011-prov-results/paper2/data_files/India2/Table_2_PR_Cities_1Lakh_and_Above.pdf}}</ref><ref name="district">{{cite web|title=Suryapet district|url=http://newdistrictsformation.telangana.gov.in/uploads/gos-circulars/1476130644554246.Suryapet.pdf|website=New Districts Formation Portal|access-date=11 October 2016|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161011225153/http://newdistrictsformation.telangana.gov.in/uploads/gos-circulars/1476130644554246.Suryapet.pdf|archive-date=11 October 2016|df=dmy-all}}</ref> It is a [[municipality]] and the headquarters of its [[Suryapet district|eponymous district]]. The [[Government of India]] announced Suryapet to be developed under the Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation ([[Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation|AMRUT]]) scheme.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.eenaduindia.com/State/AndhraPradhesh/2015/06/24090313/AP-identifies-31-Telangana-15-cities-under-AMRUT-scheme.vpf|title=AP identifies 31, Telangana 15 cities under AMRUT scheme|work=Eenadu English Portal|access-date=29 November 2017|archive-date=27 November 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151127104028/http://www.eenaduindia.com/State/AndhraPradhesh/2015/06/24090313/AP-identifies-31-Telangana-15-cities-under-AMRUT-scheme.vpf|url-status=dead}}</ref> Suryapet has been awarded the "Cleanest city" in South [[India]] by the Ministry of Urban development in [[Swachh Survekshan 2017]].<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.newindianexpress.com/states/telangana/2017/may/01/suryapet-among-top-10-cleanest-cities-in-india-1599727.html | title=Suryapet among top 10 cleanest cities in India | date=1 May 2017 | newspaper=The New Indian Express | first=S Bachan Jeet | last=Singh | access-date=15 August 2019 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://swachh-survekshan.in/SS_2017_Report.pdf|title=Swachh Survekshan -2017 – ranks of 434 cities|access-date=3 November 2017|archive-date=12 July 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170712194228/http://swachh-survekshan.in/SS_2017_Report.pdf|url-status=dead}}</ref> It is also known as "Gateway of Telangana".<ref>http://suryapetmunicipality.com/</ref> It is located about {{convert|134|km|mi|0}} east of the state capital, [[Hyderabad]] and also it is located about {{convert|138|km|mi|0}} West of [[Vijayawada]].

== Etymology ==
Previously Suryapet is known as Bhanupuri, which is modernized into the name Suryapet. People also call the town as Suryapeta.

== Geography ==
Suryapet or Suriapet is located at {{Coord|17.1500|N|79.6167|E|}}.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fallingrain.com/world/IN/02/Suriapet.html|title=Maps, Weather, and Airports for Suryapet, India|work=fallingrain.com}}</ref> It has an area of {{convert|54|km2}}.

== Climate ==
Under the Köppen climate classification, Suryapet experiences [[tropical savanna climate|Tropical Wet and Dry Climate]]. The annual mean temperature is 36&nbsp;°C. Monthly mean temperatures are {{convert|19|–|40|C|F}}. Summers (March–June) are hot and humid, with temperatures in the low 30s Celsius; during dry spells, maximum temperatures often exceed 45&nbsp;°C in May and June. Winter lasts for only about {{frac|2|1|2}} months, with seasonal lows dipping to {{convert|9|–|11|C|F}} in December and January. May is the hottest month, with daily temperatures ranging from {{convert|35|–|43|C|F}}; January, the coldest month, has temperatures varying from {{convert|15|–|23|C|F}}. The highest recorded temperature is {{convert|51.02|°C|°F|abbr=on}}, and the lowest is {{convert|9|°C|°F|abbr=on}}.

Rains brought by the south-west summer monsoon lash Suryapet between June and September, supplying it with most of its annual rainfall of 821.0&nbsp;mm. The highest total monthly rainfall, 180.0&nbsp;mm, occurs in July. There is also significant rainfall in October and November from northeast monsoons. Winter season starts in December and lasts through February. Temperatures range from a minimum of 18&nbsp;°C and can reach 33&nbsp;°C.

Apart from the city population, it has a floating population of approximately 10,000 from the neighbouring villages daily. Males constitute 52% of the population and females 48%. Suryapet has an average literacy rate of 84.88%, higher than the national average of 74.04.%: male literacy is 91.18%, and female literacy is 78.74%. In Suryapet, 11% of the population is under 6 years of age.

== Governance ==

=== Civic administration ===
[[File:Suryapet Municipality Tractor.jpg|right|thumbnail|Suryapet Municipality tractor collecting garbage]]

Suryapet municipality was constituted in 1952. It was upgraded to ''Grade–II'' in 1984, ''Grade–I'' on 7 November 1998.<ref name="info" /> The area of the municipality is spread over an area of {{convert|54.00|km2|abbr=on}} with 48 wards.<ref name=civicbody /> The present municipal commissioner is ''V. Surender'' and the chairman is ''Perumalla Annapurna''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Commissioner Contact|url=https://suryapetmunicipality.telangana.gov.in/pages/chairman-contact|website=Official website of Kodad municipality|access-date=21 February 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Commissioner Contact|url=http://suryapetmunicipality.com/elected-representatives-2/|website=Official website of Suryapet municipality|access-date=21 February 2016}}</ref>

The city also serves as the headquarters of [[Suryapet mandal]] in the [[Suryapet revenue division]].

== Awards ==
Suryapet Municipality became the first ''waste-compliant'' city of India in 2003.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thehindu.com/arts/magazine/trash-driving/article4126243.ece|title=Trash driving|author=Keya Acharya|work=The Hindu|date=24 November 2012 }}</ref> It had won ''Green Leaf Award'' for ''Best Garbage Disposal'' in 2007.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hindu.com/2007/02/26/stories/2007022602720200.htm|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130920193425/http://www.hindu.com/2007/02/26/stories/2007022602720200.htm|url-status=dead|archive-date=2013-09-20|work=[[The Hindu]]|date=2007-02-26|title=Green Leaf Awards presented}}</ref>
Suryapet is the first municipality in the country to receive the (ISO 14001-2004 certificate.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.thehindu.com/todays-paper/tp-national/tp-andhrapradesh/suryapet-municipality-given-iso-certificate/article3070979.ece|title = Suryapet Municipality given ISO certificate|newspaper = The Hindu|date = 5 September 2006}}</ref>
Suryapet Municipality has won the HUDCO Award under environmental 
management category.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://suryapetneeds.blogspot.in/2014/08/hudco-award-to-suryapet-municipality.html?m=1|title = HUDCO Award to SURYAPET Municipality}}</ref>

The primary and secondary school education is imparted by government, aided and private schools, under the School Education Department of the state.

The medium of instruction followed by schools are English, Telugu and Urdu.
