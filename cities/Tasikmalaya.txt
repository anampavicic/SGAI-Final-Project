'''Tasikmalaya''' is a landlocked city in [[West Java]], [[Indonesia]]. The city is sometimes dubbed ''[[kota santri]]'' (city of religious learners) or "the City of a Thousand [[Pesantren]]s" for its abundance of Islamic boarding schools.<ref>"City of contrasts". 2004. ''The Straits Times'' February 27.</ref> Located around {{Convert|120|km|mi}} southeast from the provincial capital of [[Bandung]], Tasikmalaya is passed by [[Indonesian National Route 3]].

The city is located in the mountainous [[Priangan]] region of Java at an elevation of 351 metres (1,151 feet).

The population of the city (excluding the Tasikmalaya Regency, which surrounds the city to the west, south and east) was 634,948 according to the 2010 census,<ref name="Biro Pusat Statistik 2011">Biro Pusat Statistik, Jakarta, 2011.</ref> and increased to 716,155 at the 2020 census;<ref name="Badan Pusat Statistik 2021">Badan Pusat Statistik, Jakarta, 2021.</ref> the official estimate as at mid 2023 was 741,760 (comprising 375,737 males and 366,023 females).<ref name="Badan Pusat Statistik 2024">Badan Pusat Statistik, Jakarta, 28 February 2024, ''Kota Tasikmalaya Dalam Angka 2024'' (Katalog-BPS 1102001.3278)</ref> Its built-up (''or metro'') area (made of Tasikmalaya City and 14 neighbouring districts spread over Tasikmalaya and Ciamis Regencies) was home to 1,214,000 inhabitants as at mid 2023.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.macrotrends.net/global-metrics/cities/206493/tasikmalaya/population | title=Tasikmalaya, Indonesia Metro Area Population 1950-2024 }}</ref>

==1996 riot==
The city of Tasikmalaya was the site of a widely reported riot in late December 1996. Four people were killed and several churches and dozens of mostly Chinese-owned businesses were destroyed in the violence, which was triggered by allegations of [[police brutality]]. It was also fueled and angered by frustration with allegedly corrupt local government officials.<ref>{{Cite journal |last=Collins |first=Elizabeth Fuller |date=2002 |title=Indonesia: A Violent Culture? |url=https://www.jstor.org/stable/10.1525/as.2002.42.4.582 |journal=Asian Survey |volume=42 |issue=4 |pages=582–604 |doi=10.1525/as.2002.42.4.582 |jstor=10.1525/as.2002.42.4.582 |issn=0004-4687}}</ref> The event was among the earliest of many riots with [[religion]]- and [[social class|class]]-based undertones that occurred in Java during the late 1990s.<ref>{{Cite journal |last=Bird |first=Judith |date=1998 |title=Indonesia in 1997: The Tinderbox Year |url=https://www.jstor.org/stable/2645675 |journal=Asian Survey |volume=38 |issue=2 |pages=168–176 |doi=10.2307/2645675 |jstor=2645675 |issn=0004-4687}}</ref>

==Natural disasters==
On 5 April 1982, the [[volcano]] of [[Gunung Galunggung]] erupted about 24&nbsp;km from the city, causing major damage through [[lahar]] and [[Volcanic ash|ash]] projection, and forcing a temporary evacuation of the area.

On 2 September 2009 [[2009 Java earthquake|a magnitude 7 earthquake struck]], destroying a number of buildings in the city and killing several people.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/in_depth/8233344.stm|title=Deadly earthquake hits Indonesia|date=September 2, 2009|via=news.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>

On 26 June 2010, a magnitude 6.3 earthquake struck Tasikmalaya at 4:45pm Waktu Indonesia Barat (WIB). No injuries or destroyed buildings were reported.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thejakartapost.com/news/2010/06/26/quake-rattles-tasikmalaya-felt-jakarta.html |title=Quake rattles Tasikmalaya, felt in Jakarta &#124; the Jakarta Post |access-date=2012-05-21 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120524023017/http://www.thejakartapost.com/news/2010/06/26/quake-rattles-tasikmalaya-felt-jakarta.html |archive-date=2012-05-24 }}</ref>

On 20 May 2012, a magnitude 5 earthquake struck. It happened at 07:37pm Waktu Indonesia Barat (WIB). No injuries or destroyed buildings were reported.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://news.detik.com/berita/d-1920328/gempa-5-sr-guncang-tasikmalaya|title=Gempa 5 SR Guncang Tasikmalaya|website=detiknews}}</ref>

==Toll Road==
A toll road was planned to be built in 2018 connecting Tasikmalaya and Bandung's Ring Road 2.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.pikiran-rakyat.com/node/145722|title=Connected with Bandung Ring Road 2}}</ref>

==Wiriadinata Airport==
[[Tasikmalaya Airport|Wiriadinata Airport]] in Cibeureum (also called Cibeureum Airport) was solely a military airport, but on 9 June 2017 it became a military airport with a civil airport enclave. With its 1,200 metres runway, it can accommodate [[ATR 72|ATR 72-600]] aircraft.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.pikiran-rakyat.com/jawa-barat/2017/06/10/ini-fasilitas-di-bandara-wiriadinata-tasikmalaya-402881 |title=Ini Fasilitas di Bandara Wiriadinata Tasikmalaya |author=Satrio Widianto |date=June 10, 2017}}</ref>

==Archeology==
Several Idols of the Gods, including [[Ganesha]] were found at the banks of the Parit Galunggung river in 2020.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://regional.kompas.com/read/2020/02/12/08070091/penemuan-arca-ganesha-dan-manusia-kerdil-di-tasikmalaya-dari-zaman-berbeda?page=all |title=Penemuan Arca Ganesha dan Manusia Kerdil di Tasikmalaya, dari Zaman Berbeda Diduga Bukan Benda Lama}}</ref>
