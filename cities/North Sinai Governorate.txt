'''North Sinai Governorate''' ({{langx|ar|محافظة شمال سيناء}} ''{{transl|arz|Muḥāfẓet Shamāl Sīnāʾ}}'') is one of the [[governorates of Egypt]]. It is located in the north-eastern part of the country, and encompasses the northern half of the [[Sinai Peninsula]]. It is bordered in the north by the [[Mediterranean Sea]], in the south by [[South Sinai Governorate]], in the west by [[Port Said Governorate|Port Said]], [[Ismailia Governorate|Ismailia]], and [[Suez Governorate]]s,<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.sis.gov.eg/En/Templates/Articles/tmpArticles.aspx?CatID=2657#.VNWjtXa6WhA |title=State Information Service |access-date=2015-02-07 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150207093509/http://www.sis.gov.eg/En/Templates/Articles/tmpArticles.aspx?CatID=2657#.VNWjtXa6WhA#.VNWjtXa6WhA |archive-date=2015-02-07 |url-status=live }}</ref> and in the east by the [[Gaza Strip]] in [[State of Palestine|Palestine]] ([[Rafah Governorate]]) and [[Israel]] ([[Southern District (Israel)|Southern District]]). Its capital is the city of [[El Arish]]. A governorate is administered by a governor, who is appointed by the [[President of Egypt]] and serves at the president's discretion.

North Sinai has a rich history dating back to ancient times and is home to several ancient settlements that hold significant historical and [[Bible|Biblical]] importance – [[Ostrakine]] and [[Ras Kouroun|Kasion]], both of which served as a border city between Egypt and [[Syria (region)|Syria]] and an important bishopric in the [[Byzantine Empire|Byzantine]] era; [[Kadesh (biblical)|Kadesh Barnea]], an important site, with its history tied to the events described in the Bible.

The population of the North Sinai Governorate as at 2015 was 434,781 people, comprising predominantly [[Bedouin]] tribesmen. The Governorate covers an area of 27,574 square kilometers. The population density is 15 inhabitants per square kilometer.

North Sinai has since 2011 been especially affected by the [[Sinai insurgency]] and measures by government forces to combat it,<ref>IRIN: [http://www.irinnews.org/report/99316/economic-life-slows-to-a-crawl-amid-crackdown-in-north-sinai Economic life slows to a crawl amid crackdown in North Sinai] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150207093606/http://www.irinnews.org/report/99316/economic-life-slows-to-a-crawl-amid-crackdown-in-north-sinai# |date=2015-02-07 }}</ref> which has resulted in many casualties. On October 14, 2016, twelve Egyptian troops were killed at a checkpoint near [[El Arish]].<ref name="AP Militants">{{cite news|last1=SWEILAM|first1=Ashraf|title=Militant attack kills 12 Egyptian soldiers in Sinai|url=http://hosted2.ap.org/APDEFAULT/cae69a7523db45408eeb2b3a98c0c9c5/Article_2016-10-14-ML-Egypt/id-befdc78daeb941f9b8bd9bf13f2a8210|access-date=17 October 2016|agency=AP|date=October 14, 2016}}</ref> On November 27, 2017, a mosque in the village of [[Al-Rawda, North Sinai|Al-Rawda]] was [[2017 Sinai mosque attack|attacked]] by roughly 40 gunmen, killing at least 305 and injuring up to 128 others, making it the deadliest terror attack in Egyptian history.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.egypttoday.com/Article/1/34941/Egypt%E2%80%99s-Mufti-performs-Friday-prayers-at-Rawdah-mosque|title=Egypt's Mufti performs Friday prayers at Rawdah mosque - Egypt Today|website=www.egypttoday.com|access-date=2017-12-02}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Walsh|first1=Declan|last2=Youssef|first2=Nour|date=24 November 2017|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/11/24/world/middleeast/mosque-attack-egypt.html |title=Militants Kill 305 at Sufi Mosque in Egypt's Deadliest Terrorist Attack|newspaper=The New York Times |agency=New York Times|access-date=29 June 2020 }}</ref>

According to population estimates, in 2024 the majority of residents in the governorate lived in urban areas, with an urbanization rate of 60.2%. Out of an estimated total of [https://www.capmas.gov.eg/Pages/populationClock.aspx 450,531] people, 261,219 people lived in urban areas and 179,312 lived in rural areas.<ref name="pop 2015" />
