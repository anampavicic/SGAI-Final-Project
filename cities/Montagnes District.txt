'''Montagnes District''' ({{langx|fr|District des Montagnes}} {{IPA|fr|mɔ̃.taɲ|pron}}, "Mountains") is one of fourteen [[Districts of Ivory Coast|administrative districts]] of [[Ivory Coast]]. The district is located in the western part of the country, bordering [[Liberia]] and [[Guinea]] to the west, [[Woroba District]] to the north, [[Sassandra-Marahoué District]] to the east, and [[Bas-Sassandra District]] to the south.<ref name=statoids>{{cite web|title=Districts of Côte d'Ivoire (Ivory Coast)|url=http://www.statoids.com/uci.html|website=Statoids.com|access-date=24 June 2015}}</ref> The capital of the district is [[Man, Ivory Coast|Man]].

==Creation==
Montagnes District was created in a 2011 administrative reorganisation of the [[subdivisions of Ivory Coast]].<ref>Décret n° 2011-263 du 28 septembre 2011 portant organisation du territoire national en Districts et en Régions.</ref> The territory of the district was composed by merging the former regions of [[Dix-Huit Montagnes]] and [[Moyen-Cavally]].<ref name=statoids>{{cite web|title=Districts of Côte d'Ivoire (Ivory Coast)|url=http://www.statoids.com/uci.html|website=Statoids.com|access-date=24 June 2015}}</ref> The district was suppressed in 2014 and restored as an autonomous district in 2021.

==Population==
According to the 2021 census, Montagnes District has a population of 3,027,023, making it the second most populous district in Ivory Coast, behind only [[Abidjan Autonomous District]].<ref name="pop"/>
