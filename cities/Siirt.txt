'''Siirt''' ({{langx|ar|سِعِرْد|Siʿird}}; {{langx|hy|Սղերդ|S'gherd}};{{cn|date=December 2022}} {{langx|syr|ܣܥܪܬ|Siirt}};<ref>Thomas A. Carlson et al., “Siirt — ܣܥܪܬ ” in The Syriac Gazetteer last modified December 9, 2016, http://syriaca.org/place/188.</ref> {{langx|ku|script=Latn|Sêrt}}<ref>{{cite book|author1=Adem Avcıkıran |title=Kürtçe Anamnez Anamneza bi Kurmancî|date=2009|page=57 |url=http://tirsik.net/danegeh/pirtuk/ismail%20bulbul/anamneza%20bi%20kurmanc%C3%AE.pdf |access-date=17 December 2019 |language=tr, ku}}</ref>) is a city in the [[Siirt District]] of [[Siirt Province]] in [[Turkey]].<ref name=":2">{{Cite web |title=Türkiye Mülki İdare Bölümleri Envanteri |url=https://www.e-icisleri.gov.tr/Anasayfa/MulkiIdariBolumleri.aspx |access-date=19 December 2022 |website=T.C. İçişleri Bakanlığı |language=tr}}</ref> It had a population of 160,340 in 2021.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |title=31 ARALIK 2021 TARİHLİ ADRESE DAYALI NÜFUS KAYIT SİSTEMİ (ADNKS) SONUÇLARI |url=https://www.tuik.gov.tr/indir/duyuru/favori_raporlar.xlsx |access-date=16 December 2022 |website=TÜİK |language=tr |format=XLS}}</ref>

== Neighborhoods ==
The city is divided into the neighborhoods of Afetevlerı, Alan, Algul, Bahçelievler, Barış, Batı, Conkbayır, Çal, Doğan, Dumlupınar, Halenze, İnönü, Karakol, Kooperatif, Sakarya, Tınaztepe, Ulus, Ülkü, Veysel Karani and Yeni.<ref name=":0" />

== History ==
Previously known as ''Saird'', in pre-Islamic times Siirt was a diocese of the [[Eastern Orthodox Church]] (''Sirte'', Σίρτη in [[Medieval Greek|Byzantine Greek]]). In the medieval times, [[Arzen]]  was the main city and it competed with Hasankeyf over the control the region, Siirt was only to become a center of the region in the 14th century. But it was still dependent from [[Hasankeyf]] until the 17th century.<ref>{{Cite book|last=Sinclair|first=T. A.|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Na1EBAAAQBAJ&q=siirt+bridge+architecture&pg=PA296|title=Eastern Turkey: An Architectural & Archaeological Survey, Volume III|date=1989-12-31|publisher=Pindar Press|isbn=978-0-907132-34-9|pages=295|language=en}}</ref> An illuminated manuscript known as the [[Syriac Bible of Paris]] might have originated from the Bishop of Siirt's library, Siirt's Christians would have worshipped in [[Syriac language|Syriac]], a liturgical language descended from [[Aramaic language|Aramaic]] still in use by the [[West Syriac Rite|Syriac Rite]],[[Chaldean Rite]], other [[Saint Thomas Christians|Eastern Christians in India]], and the [[Nestorian Christians|Nestorians]] along the Silk Road as far as China. The [[Chronicle of Seert]] was preserved in the city; it describes the ecclesiastical history of the Persian realm through to the middle of the seventh century. From 1858 to 1915 the city was the seat of [[Seert (Chaldean Diocese)|a bishop]] of the [[Chaldean Catholic Church]]. Most of the city's [[Assyrian people|Assyrians]], including [[Addai Scher]] their archbishop were murdered during the [[Assyrian genocide]] along with the loss of artefacts such as the Syriac manuscript of [[Theodore of Mopsuestia]]'s De Incarnatione.<ref>{{citation|last1=Gaunt|first1=David|last2= Beṯ-Şawoce|first2=Jan|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=4mug9LrpLKcC|title= Massacres, resistance, protectors: Muslim-Christian relations in Eastern Anatolia during World War I|pages=251–253|year=2006|publisher=Gorgias Press LLC|isbn=978-1593333010}}</ref> Also during [[World War I]], the Armenian population of Siirt became a victim of the [[Armenian genocide]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Kevorkian|first=Raymond|author-link=Raymond Kévorkian|date=2016-01-25|title=The Extermination of Ottoman Armenians by the Young Turk Regime (1915-1916) {{!}} Sciences Po Mass Violence and Resistance - Research Network|url=http://extermination-ottoman-armenians-young-turk-regime-1915-1916.html/|url-status=|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-01-31|website=extermination-ottoman-armenians-young-turk-regime-1915-1916.html|language=en}}</ref>
== Demographics ==

=== Historical ===
[[Mark Sykes]] recorded Siirt as a city inhabited by Kurds, Arabs, Assyrians, and Armenians.<ref>{{cite book |last1=Sykes |first1=Mark |title=Dar-ul-Islam: A Record of a Journey Through Ten of the Asiatic Provinces of Turkey |date=1904 |publisher=Bickers & Son |page=264}}</ref> During the second half of the 19th century, many Armenians left Siirt due to persecutions and poor economic conditions.<ref name=vgm>{{Cite web |title=Kaza Siirt / ܣܥܪܬ Siʿret / Sa'irt / Սղերդ - Sġerd / Sgherd |url=https://virtual-genocide-memorial.de/region/the-six-provinces/bitlis-vilayet/sancak-slirt/kaza-siirt-%DC%A3%DC%A5%DC%AA%DC%AC-si%CA%BFret-%D5%BD%D5%B2%D5%A5%D6%80%D5%A4-sgerd/ |access-date=2023-09-17 |website=Virtual Genocide Memorial |language=en-US}}</ref> During the 1895 [[Hamidian massacres]], many Armenians were forcibly converted to Islam and the clergy was massacred.<ref name=vgm/> Before World War I, the [[sanjak]] of Siirt formed a Christian enclave with 60,000 Christians: 25,000 [[Armenian Apostolic Church|Armenians]], 20,000 [[Syriac Orthodox Church|Syriac Orthodox]], and 15,000 [[Chaldean Catholics]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Sancak Siirt / ܣܥܪܬ Siʿret / Sa'irt / Սղերդ - Sġerd / Sgherd |url=https://virtual-genocide-memorial.de/region/the-six-provinces/bitlis-vilayet/sancak-slirt/ |access-date=2023-09-17 |website=Virtual Genocide Memorial |language=en-US}}</ref> According to the [[Armenian Patriarchate of Constantinople]], in 1914 there were 4,437 Armenians in the [[kaza]], with three churches, one monastery and two schools.<ref name=vgm/> [[Agha Petros]] mentions 40 [[Assyrian people|Nestorian Assyrian]] families in the city of Siirt.<ref name=vgm/> Mardin Chaldean priest Joseph Tfinkdji lists 5,430 Chaldeans in the diocese: 824 in the town and the rest in surrounding villages.<ref name=vgm/> The community was led by [[Addai Sher]].<ref name=vgm/> [[David Gaunt]] mentions some Yezidi presence.<ref name=vgm/>

According to the 1927 census, the population in the whole district was almost exclusively Muslim, with the exception of two Catholics, one Protestant, four Armenians, 17 other Christians, and 38 "other religion".<ref>{{Cite journal |last=BabaoğLu |first=Resul |date=2018-01-01 |title=CUMHURİYET İDARESİNE GEÇİŞ SÜRECİNDE SİİRT'TE DEMOGRAFİK GÖSTERGELER |url=http://www.johschool.com/Makaleler/259130195_19.%20R.%20Babao%C4%9Flu%20-%20Cumhuriyet%20%C4%B0daresine%20G%C3%B6re.529-563..pdf |journal=Journal of History School |language=en |volume=11 |issue=XXXIV |pages=529–563 |doi=10.14225/Joh1285 |issn=1308-5298}}</ref>

[[İsmet İnönü]] referred to the city as an Arab city eager to get [[Turkification|Turkified]], while Kurds lived in the outskirts.<ref>{{cite book |last1=Mumcu |first1=Uğur |author1-link=Uğur Mumcu |title=Kürt Dosyası |date=August 1993 |publisher=Uğur Mumcu Araştırmacı Gazetecilik Vakfı |location=Ankara |page=72 |isbn=9786054274512 |edition=42 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=uHOCDwAAQBAJ&pg=PA72 |access-date=17 September 2022}}</ref>

=== Modern ===
[[Kurds]] constitute a majority in the city with a significant Arab community.<ref>{{Cite book |last=Taş |first=Latif |title=Legal Pluralism in Action: Dispute Resolution and the Kurdish Peace Committee |publisher=[[Routledge]] |year=2016 |isbn=9781317106159 |pages=33}}</ref><ref>{{cite book |last1=Akay |first1=Sedat |title=Geçmişten Günümüze Siirt Arapçası |date=September 2, 2021 |publisher=DBY Yayınları |pages=33 |isbn=9786257471053 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=bPhKEAAAQBAJ |access-date=17 September 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Siirt halkı: Kürtçe yok sayılırken 'biz kardeşiz' denilmesi bir anlam ifade etmiyor |url=https://ilkha.com/guncel/siirt-halki-kurtce-yok-sayilirken-biz-kardesiz-denilmesi-bir-anlam-ifade-etmiyor-113820 |website=İLKHA |access-date=29 October 2022}}</ref> The Kurdish tribes living in the city are the Botikan, Dudêran, Elîkan, Keşkoliyan, Silokan and Sturkiyan.<ref>{{Cite book |last=Mendeş |first=Hurşit Baran |title=Ertoşîler |publisher=Doz |year=2022 |pages=33 |language=tr}}</ref>

== Government ==
In the municipal [[2019 Turkish local elections|elections of March 2019]] Berivan Helen Işık of the [[Peoples' Democratic Party (Turkey)|Peoples' Democratic Party]] (HDP) was elected mayor.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Siirt Seçim Sonuçları - 31 Mart 2019 Yerel Seçimleri|url=https://www.sabah.com.tr/secim/31-mart-2019-yerel-secim-sonuclari/siirt/ili-yerel-secim-sonuclari|website=www.sabah.com.tr|access-date=2020-05-15}}</ref> She was dismissed from her post on 15 May 2020 and detained over terror charges. Ali Fuat Atik, the Governor of the Siirt province was appointed trustee by the Ministry of the Interior.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Four mayors detained over alleged terror links - Turkey News|url=http://www.hurriyetdailynews.com/four-mayors-detained-over-alleged-terror-links-154785|website=Hürriyet Daily News|date=15 May 2020 |language=en|access-date=2020-05-15}}</ref>

== Landmarks ==
The city's landmark is the [[Congregational mosque|Great Mosque]] (''Ulu Cami'') built in 1129 by the [[Great Seljuk]] [[Mahmud II of Great Seljuk|Sultan Mahmud II]] who belonged to the main branch of the dynasty that ruled from [[Baghdad]] after the [[Seljuks]] had split into several branches. The mosque was further developed by the [[Ottoman Empire]]. The mosque was restored in 1965.

== Trivia ==
Siirt was [[Prime Minister of Turkey|Turkish Prime Minister]] [[Recep Tayyip Erdoğan]]'s constituency from 2003 to 2007. His wife, Mrs. [[Emine Erdoğan]], is from Siirt and the PM had been elected to the [[Turkish Grand National Assembly]] in a by-election held in Siirt in 2003.

Although Siirt remains one of the poorer cities in Turkey, some neighbourhoods have fine and modern housing including new shops, banks and hotels.<ref>[http://www.pbase.com/dosseman/siirt Siirt in South East Turkey Photo Gallery by Dick Osseman at]. Pbase.com. Retrieved on 2011-04-16.</ref>

== Climate ==
Siirt has a [[hot-summer Mediterranean climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification|Köppen]]: ''Csa,'' [[Trewartha climate classification|Trewartha]]'': Cs'') with very hot, dry summers and chilly, wet winters. During winter months there is frequent frost and occasional snowfall.
