'''Casanare''' ({{IPA|es|kasaˈnaɾe}}, {{langx|es|Departamento de Casanare}}) is a [[departments of Colombia|department]] located in the central eastern of [[Colombia]]. It is famous for its oil and natural gas production as well as its livestock and extensive plains.<ref>{{Cite web |last=S.A.S |first=Editorial La República |title=Campos petroleros de Casanare, los que más aportaron al aumento de la producción |url=https://www.larepublica.co/economia/campos-petroleros-de-casanare-los-que-mas-aportaron-al-aumento-de-la-produccion-3418252 |access-date=2023-09-12 |website=Diario La República |language=es}}</ref> It is also the tenth largest department with an area of 44,490&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>, similar to that of [[Denmark]], but also the seventh least densely populated. 
 
Its capital is [[Yopal]], which is also the episcopal seat of the [[Roman Catholic Diocese of Yopal]].
 
It contains [[oil field]]s and an 800&nbsp;km [[Pipeline transport|pipeline]] leading to the coastal port of [[Coveñas]] owned by [[BP]].
