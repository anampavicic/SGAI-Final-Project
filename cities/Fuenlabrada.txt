'''Fuenlabrada''' ({{IPA|es|fwenlaˈβɾaða|lang|Pronunciation of Fuenlabrada in Spanish.ogg}}) is a city and [[Municipalities of Spain|municipality]] of [[Spain]] located in the [[Community of Madrid]]. {{As of|2018}}, it has a population of 193,586, making it the region's fourth most populated municipality.

== Placename ==
The etymology of ''Fuenlabrada'' comes from the contraction of ''Fuente Labrada'',{{Sfn|García Sánchez|2008}} Spanish for "carved fountain".

== Geography ==
[[File:(Fuenlabrada) Madrid ESA354454 (cropped).jpg|thumb|left|Fuenlabrada as seen by the [[European Space Agency]]'s [[Sentinel-2]].]]
The highest point in the municipality (721 metres above sea level) is located in a hill to the northwest of the city proper.{{Sfn|Cabrero Garrido|García Lledó|García Pérez|2004|p=388}}
The city proper's altitude gently decreases from the Northwest to the Southeast; the lowest areas in the municipality (slightly over 600 metres above sea level) are located in the East, past the [[Autovía A-42|A-42]] near the border with [[Getafe]].{{Sfn|Cabrero Garrido|García Lledó|García Pérez|2004|pp=388–389}}

The southeast of the municipality is covered by the {{ill|Polígono Cobo Calleja|es|Polígono industrial Cobo Calleja}}, a huge economic area overwhelmingly dedicated to the wholesale distribution of Chinese imports (considered the biggest one in Europe in the latter regard).<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.telemadrid.es/noticias/madrid/Poligono-Cobo-Calleja-Fuenlabrada-celebra-0-2156184366--20190906094722.html|title=El polígono Cobo Calleja de Fuenlabrada celebra su medio siglo de vida|date=6 September 2019|publisher=[[Telemadrid]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lainformacion.com/economia-negocios-y-finanzas/Cobo-Calleja-paraiso-chino-IVA-evasion_0_1040297549/|website=La Información|title=Cobo Calleja, el paraíso chino del 'todo a cien' al por mayor, al por menor y sin IVA|date=30 June 2017}}</ref>

The residential area of {{ill|Loranca|es|Loranca (Fuenlabrada)}} (203.6 ha in size){{Sfn|Santiago Rodríguez|2007|p=28}} lies in the west,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://elpais.com/diario/1997/07/03/madrid/867929064_850215.html|website=[[El País]]|date=3 July 1997|title=Un nuevo enlace de 2,5 y kilómetros hace más fácil el acceso a Ciudad Loranca|first=Fernando|last=Neira}}</ref> near [[Móstoles]], semi-detached from the main urban nucleus by the {{ill|M-407|es}} highway.

The main hydrographic feature is the {{ill|Arroyo Culebro|es}}, a tributary of the [[Manzanares (river)|Manzanares]].{{Sfn|Cabrero Garrido|García Lledó|García Pérez|2004|p=389}} Other parts of the municipality (including the Arroyo de Fregacedos) are however part of the [[Guadarrama (river)|Guadarrama]] catchment area.{{Sfn|Cabrero Garrido|García Lledó|García Pérez|2004|pp=389–390}}

== History ==
There are archeological sites tracing back the human presence in the area to the [[Paleolithic]] (''La Cantueña'') and the [[Bronze Age]] (''Casas del Cerro'').{{Sfn|Cabrero Garrido|García Lledó|García Pérez|2004|p=392}} Other settlements in the current municipal area, including Loranca, Albas and Fregacedos, existed during the Upper Middle Ages.{{Sfn|Cabrero Garrido|García Lledó|García Pérez|2004|p=393}}

The founding of the village of Fuenlabrada has been tentatively traced to the late 14th century, populated by the dwellers of the nearby settlements of Loranca, Albas and Fregacedos, which had been simultaneously depopulated.{{Sfn|Cabrero Garrido|García Lledó|García Pérez|2004|p=394}} The first mention of Fuenlabrada, which by the early 16th century was part of the Land of [[Madrid]], was recorded later in the century, during the reign of [[Philip II of Spain|Philip II]], in the so-called ''[[Topographic Relations of Philip II]]''.{{Sfn|Cabrero Garrido|García Lledó|García Pérez|2004|p=394}} The village suffered the loss of a quarter of the population in the second half of the 18th century.{{Sfn|Cabrero Garrido|García Lledó|García Pérez|2004|p=397}}

While the agricultural sector of the economy remained dominant, the making of {{ill|rosquilla|es|rosquilla|lt=''rosquillas''}} and [[bun]]s in the municipality became renowned among the surrounding towns by the end of the 19th century.{{Sfn|Cabrero Garrido|García Lledó|García Pérez|2004|p=399}} Following the end of the 1936–1939 [[Spanish Civil War|Civil War]], in which some parts of Fuenlabrada endured serious damage, the Dirección General de Regiones Devastadas undertook some building works in the municipality during the 1940s and 1950s.{{Sfn|Cabrero Garrido|García Lledó|García Pérez|2004|pp=405–407}}

The population experienced a demographic explosion in the 1970s, going from 7,369 inhabitants in 1970 to 65,181 in 1980, the biggest relative increase in Spain in that period.{{Sfn|Cabrero Garrido|García Lledó|García Pérez|2004|p=408}} The demographic growth continued in the ensuing decades.

Since the [[1979 Spanish local elections|1979 municipal elections]], the municipality has been a stronghold of the [[Spanish Socialist Workers' Party]] (PSOE)<ref>{{Cite journal|url=https://www.lavanguardia.com/politica/20181106/452781379155/el-comite-federal-del-psoe-se-reunira-este-sabado-en-fuenlabrada.html|title=El Comité Federal del PSOE se reunirá este sábado en Fuenlabrada|date=6 November 2018|journal=[[La Vanguardia]]}}</ref> and has been part of the so-called "[[Red belt (Community of Madrid)|red belt]]" of the Community of Madrid.

== Politics and government ==

Thorough its modern democratic history, the municipality of Fuenlabrada has been always ruled by the [[Spanish Socialist Workers' Party of the Community of Madrid]], belonging to the so-called "[[Red belt (Community of Madrid)|red belt]]" of the region.

== Transport ==
[[File:Fuenlabrada Central.jpg|thumb|Fuenlabrada [[Cercanías Madrid|Central Railway]] (and [[Madrid Metro|Metro]]) Station.]]

=== Roads ===
The city is linked with the [[Autopista de Circunvalación M-50|M-50]], [[Autovía A-42|A-42]], M-506, M-407 and M-409 highways. It is connected as well to the neighbouring towns of [[Humanes de Madrid]] and [[Moraleja de Enmedio]] through the M-413 and M-405 local roads, respectively.

=== Metro ===
Fuenlabrada has  good transport connections with [[Madrid]], [[Móstoles]], [[Getafe]], [[Alcorcón]] and [[Leganés]] through the [[Madrid Metro]] line [[Line 12 (Madrid Metro)|12]] (also known as ''Metrosur''), including five stations: [[Loranca (Madrid Metro)|Loranca]], [[Hospital de Fuenlabrada (Madrid Metro)|Hospital de Fuenlabrada]], [[Parque Europa (Madrid Metro)|Parque Europa]], [[Fuenlabrada Central (Madrid Metro)|Fuenlabrada Central]] (transfer with Fuenlabrada Cercanías station) and [[Parque de los Estados (Madrid Metro)|Parque de los Estados]].

=== Commuter train ===
Fuenlabrada is connected to Madrid via the [[Cercanías Madrid]] line C-5, a [[commuter train]] service, having two stations: Fuenlabrada (Transfer with Fuenlabrada Central metro station) and La Serna.

== Demography ==
Fuenlabrada witnessed a dramatic population explosion in the 1980s, due to large scale immigration of young workers coming from the capital city and other agrarian regions (chiefly [[Extremadura]], [[Castilla-La Mancha]], [[Andalusia]] and [[Galicia (Spain)|Galicia]]) seeking affordable prices in housing. By 1975, the municipality had already reached a population of 18,442. Foreign workers have also settled in the city, most of them coming from Latin America, Africa, the Maghreb, China and Romania.

==Education==
[[File:Facultad de Ciencias Jurídicas y Sociales (URJC).JPG|thumb|right|URJC Faculty of Law and Social Sciences]]

=== Primary and Secondary education ===
Fuenlabrada has 33 [[kindergarten]] and daycare centers, 50 [[primary schools]] and 15 [[secondary schools]].

=== Higher education ===
The [[Universidad Rey Juan Carlos]] (URJC) has a secondary campus in the city, housing the faculties of [[communication sciences]], [[telecommunications]] engineering, [[tourism]], [[business administration]], among other courses.

The [[National University of Distance Education]] (UNED) also has a branch here.

==Sports==
[[File:Polideportivo Fernando Martín, Fuenlabrada.jpg|thumb|[[Polideportivo Fernando Martín]]]]
The city is home to basketball team [[Baloncesto Fuenlabrada]], currently playing in the first tier division ([[Liga ACB]]), having competed in the top flight division of Spanish basketball for several years. Their home games are played at [[Polideportivo Fernando Martín|Fernando Martín]] sports centre, which has a capacity of 5,700.

[[CF Fuenlabrada]] is the local football team, playing in Primera Federación (third tier in Spanish league). They play as home team at the [[Estadio Fernando Torres]], inaugurated by the footballer's parents on 1 September 2011. The stadium has a capacity of 7,500.

Due to [[Yugoslav Wars]], Partizan had to play abroad as the host in the 1991/1992 season. The management of the club decided to "host" in the Madrid suburb of Fuenlabrada, and that season they became champions of the old continent.
