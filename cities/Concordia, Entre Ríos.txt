'''Concordia''', officially '''San Antonio de Padua de la Concordia''', is the head town of the [[Concordia Department|Concordia]] [[Departments of Argentina|department]], [[Entre Ríos Province]], located in the [[Argentina|Argentine]] [[Mesopotamia, Argentina|Mesopotamia.]] It has 179,203 inhabitants according to the {{census-ar|2022}},<ref>https://www.entrerios.gov.ar/dgec/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Primero-datos-provisionales-Censo-2022-Entre-Rios-Febrero-2023-1.pdf {{Bare URL PDF|date=August 2024}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Oficial: esta es la población de cada ciudad de Entre Ríos |url=https://www.unoentrerios.com.ar/la-provincia/oficial-esta-es-la-poblacion-cada-ciudad-rios-n10143130.html |access-date=2024-07-29 |website=Uno Entre Rios}}</ref> making it the second largest in its province, and the [[List of cities in Argentina by population|24th largest in Argentina.]]

==History==
The area was inhabitated by the [[Charruas|Charrúas]] and the [[Yaro people]] when the [[Spain|Spanish]] had arrived.

==Geography==
Concordia lies on the right-hand (western) shore of the [[Uruguay River]], opposite the city of [[Salto, Uruguay|Salto]] in [[Uruguay]]. The two cities are joined by [[Salto Grande Bridge|a road/railway link]] that is part of the [[Salto Grande Dam]] complex (starting on the Argentine side 18&nbsp;km north from the center of Concordia). It is located approximately 430 km north of [[Buenos Aires]].

===Climate===
According to the [[Köppen climate classification]], Concordia has a [[humid subtropical climate]] (''Cfa'').<ref name=INTAclima>{{cite web |last1=Ramos |first1=Sergio |last2=De Ruyver |first2=Roberto |last3=Gattinoni |first3=Natalia |last4=Garín |first4=Rubén |last5=Garran |first5=Sergio |url=https://inta.gob.ar/sites/default/files/inta_concordia_50_anos_estacion_agrometeorologica_eea_concordia_inta.pdf |title=Estación agrometeorológica del INTA Concordia 50 años de servicio a la comunidad |publisher=Instituto Nacional de Tecnología Agropecuaria |language=Spanish |accessdate=24 July 2018}}</ref>{{rp|8}} Mean monthly temperatures range from {{convert|12.5|C|F|1}} in July, the coldest month, to {{convert|25.3|C|F|0}} in January, the warmest month.<ref name=INTAclima/>{{rp|8}} Concordia receives a mean annual precipitation of {{convert|1372.9|mm|in|0|abbr=on}}.<ref name=INTAclima/>{{rp|11}} Fall (March to May) is the wettest season while winter (June to August) is the driest season.<ref name=INTAclima/>{{rp|11}} However, there is great year to year variability in annual precipitation.<ref name=INTAclima/>{{rp|12}} The frost free period is 10 months long and in an average year, there are 8.6 days with frost.<ref name=INTAclima/>{{rp|9}}

===Nature===
The city is known as the national capital of [[citrus]] production. [[El Palmar National Park]], an important reserve for Yatay [[palm tree]]s, lies 60&nbsp;km south of the city.

==Transport==
The area is served by an [[Comodoro Pierrestegui Airport|airport]] {{airport codes|COC|SAAC}} located at {{coord|31|18|S|58|0|W|}}, but which is ostensibly not operational. 

A binational airport shared with the city of [[Salto, Uruguay]] (which lies opposite Concordia), and it is expected to be finalized before the end of 2024.<ref>{{Cite web |title=El Aeropuerto Binacional Concordia-Salto estaría listo para mediados del año que viene |url=https://www.ambito.com/uruguay/el-aeropuerto-binacional-concordia-salto-estaria-listo-mediados-del-ano-que-viene-n5885568 |access-date=2024-06-29 |website=www.ambito.com |language=es-UY}}</ref>
