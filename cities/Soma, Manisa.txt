'''Soma''' is a municipality and [[Districts of Turkey|district]] of [[Manisa Province]], [[Turkey]].<ref>[https://www.e-icisleri.gov.tr/Anasayfa/MulkiIdariBolumleri.aspx Büyükşehir İlçe Belediyesi], Turkey Civil Administration Departments Inventory. Retrieved 12 July 2023.</ref> Its area is 820&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.harita.gov.tr/uploads/files-folder/il_ilce_alanlari.xlsx|title=İl ve İlçe Yüz ölçümleri|publisher=General Directorate of Mapping|access-date=12 July 2023}}</ref> and its population is 111,789 (2022).<ref name=tuik>{{Cite web |title=Address-based population registration system (ADNKS) results dated 31 December 2022, Favorite Reports|url=https://biruni.tuik.gov.tr/medas/?kn=95&locale=en |access-date=12 July 2023|publisher=[[TÜİK]]|language=en|format=XLS}}</ref> The town lies at an elevation of {{convert|161|m|ft|0|abbr=on}}.

==History==
From 1867 until 1922, Soma was part of the [[Aidin Vilayet]] of the [[Ottoman Empire]].

== Economy ==
[[Lignite]] mining and a lignite-fired thermal power plant are the main economic activities in Soma.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.turkishweekly.net/columnist/2553/soma-manisa-turkey-thermal-power-plant-brief-report-for-future-investors.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101218044756/http://www.turkishweekly.net/columnist/2553/soma-manisa-turkey-thermal-power-plant-brief-report-for-future-investors.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=2010-12-18|title=Soma Manisa Turkey-Thermal Power Plant|access-date=2009-04-11|author=The Journal of Turkish Weekly}}</ref> During [[World War I]] the mines were opened up for production. The lignite extracted during that time was described as being of "very bad" quality. That lignite was burned with German [[coal]] for trains in the region. A small portion was exported to [[İzmir]].<ref name=Prothero101>{{cite book|last=Prothero|first=G.W.|title=Anatolia|year=1920|publisher=H.M. Stationery Office|location=London|page=101|url=http://www.wdl.org/en/item/11766/view/1/101/}}</ref> A [[just transition]] from coal could be supported by the [[European Bank for Reconstruction and Development]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=The EBRD's just transition initiative|url=https://www.ebrd.com/what-we-do/just-transition-initiative|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=[[European Bank for Reconstruction and Development]]}}</ref>

The [[Soma Wind Farm]], with 119 [[wind turbine]]s and an installed total capacity of 140.4&nbsp;[[Watt|MW]], is one of [[List of wind farms in Turkey|Turkey's largest wind farms]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://haber.gazetevatan.com/ruzgarda-en-buyuk-olacak/338182/2/Haber |newspaper=[[Vatan (2002 newspaper)|Vatan]] |title='Rüzgar'da en büyük olacak |date=2010-11-02 |language=Turkish |accessdate=2012-06-30 }}</ref>

Olive, walnut and almond cultivation is among the important agricultural activities of Soma.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Gazete|first=Banka|date=21 November 2021|title=Manisalı üreticilere fidan desteği devam ediyor|page=https://gazetebanka.com/|work=Gazete Banka|url=https://gazetebanka.com/manisali-ureticilere-fidan-destegi-devam-ediyor/11362/|access-date=21 November 2021}}</ref>

== 2014 Soma coal mine disaster==
{{main|Soma mine disaster}}
On 13 May 2014, as a result of an explosion caused by a mine fire at the [[Soma coal mine]], 301 workers were killed.<ref name="ntv298">{{cite web|url=http://www.ntvmsnbc.com/id/25516321/ | publisher = ntvmsnbc |date=17 May 2014 |accessdate=17 May 2014 |title=Faciada son bilanço: 298 ölü }}</ref>
