{{Nihongo|'''Musashino'''|武蔵野市|Musashino-shi}} is a [[Cities of Japan|city]] located in the [[Western Tokyo|western portion]] of the [[Tokyo Metropolis]], [[Japan]]. {{As of|2024|02|01}}, the city had an estimated [[population]] of 147,754 in 78,614 households, and a [[population density]] of 13,000 persons per km<sup>2</sup>. The total area of the city is {{convert|10.98|sqkm|sqmi}}.<ref name="Musashino-hp">{{cite web |url=http://www.city.musashino.lg.jp/shisei_joho/musashino_profile/jinkotokei/1031544.html |title= Musashino city official statistics|location= Japan|language= ja}}</ref> Based on the 2019 survey by SUUMO, the Kichijoji neighborhood of Musashino was the third most desirable place to live in central Japan.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2019-03-07 |title=Yokohama ranks as most desirable city for living in Kanto for 2nd year in a row |url=https://japantoday.com/category/features/lifestyle/yokohama-ranks-as-most-desirable-city-for-living-in-kanto-for-the-second-year-in-a-row |access-date=2023-07-17 |website=Japan Today |language=en}}</ref>

Popular attractions in Musashino include [[Kichijōji]]; a residential and shopping neighborhood with malls such as Atre Kichijoji, recreational areas such as [[Inokashira Park]], Musashino Chuo Park, [[Musashino Municipal Athletic Stadium]] and Musashino Sports Complex.

==Geography==
[[File:Sun Road.jpg|thumb|Sun Road, Kichijōji|right|250px]]
Musashino is located in the [[Musashino Terrace]] of central Tokyo Metropolis. It is bordered by the 23 [[Special Wards of Tokyo]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=64 Things to Do in Musashino: Points of Interest + Activities|url=https://www.inspirock.com/japan/things-to-do-in-musashino|access-date=2021-01-08|website=Inspirock|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Adriana|date=2019-05-22|title=Kichijoji – Tokyo's hottest suburban area|url=https://www.rethinktokyo.com/kichijoji|access-date=2021-01-08|website=REthink Tokyo - Real Estate Information for Buyers and Investors|language=en}}</ref>

Musashino is composed of the following neighborhoods: Kichijoji Kitamachi, Kichijoji Higashi Cho, Kichijoji Honcho, Kichijoji Minamicho, Kyonan Cho, Gotenyama, Sakai, Sakurazuki, Sekimae, Nakacho, Nishikubo, Midoricho, and Yahata Cho.<ref>[http://www.mapion.co.jp/address/13203/ Mapion]. 住所一覧から地図を検索 > 関東 > 東京都 > 武蔵野市 地図. ===Climate===
Musashino has a [[humid subtropical climate]] (Köppen ''Cfa'') characterized by warm summers and cool winters with light to no snowfall. The average annual temperature in Musashino is 14.5&nbsp;°C. The average annual rainfall is 1647&nbsp;mm with September as the wettest month. The temperatures are highest on average in August, at around 26.0&nbsp;°C, and lowest in January, at around 3.1&nbsp;°C.<ref>[https://en.climate-data.org/asia/japan/tokyo/musashino-4747/ Musashino climate data]</ref>

==History==
The area of present-day Musashino was part of ancient [[Musashi Province]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=22 May 2020|title=Kokubunji, Musashino, the belly button of Tokyo|url=https://www.mactionplanet.com/blog/kokubunji-tourism-video/|access-date=2021-01-08|website=www.mactionplanet.com}}</ref> In the post-[[Meiji Restoration]] cadastral reform of July 22, 1878, the area became part of [[Kitatama District, Tokyo|Kitatama District]] in [[Kanagawa Prefecture]]. The village of Musashino was created on April 1, 1889 with the establishment of modern municipalities law. Kitatama District was transferred to the administrative control of Tokyo Metropolis on April 1, 1893.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Writers|first=YABAI|title=The Tourist's Complete Guide to Exploring the City of Kokubunji {{!}} YABAI - The Modern, Vibrant Face of Japan|url=http://yabai.com/p/3836|access-date=2021-01-08|website=YABAI}}</ref> Musashino was elevated to town status in 1928. [[Nakajima Aircraft Company]] had an aircraft engine plant in Musashino, which became a target for American bombers in [[World War II]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=3 December 2020|title=THIRD B-29 RAID ON JAPANESE CAPITAL TOKYO|url=https://ww2days.com/third-b-29-raid-on-japanese-capital.html|access-date=2021-01-08|website=ww2days.com}}</ref> Musashino was elevated to city status on November 3, 1947.<ref>{{Cite web|title=2017 Musashino City Overview|url=http://www.city.musashino.lg.jp/area/material/youran/2017english/pageindices/index54.html|access-date=2021-01-08|website=www.city.musashino.lg.jp}}</ref>

==Government==
Musashino has a [[mayor-council]] form of government with a directly elected mayor and a [[unicameral]] city council of 26 members. Musashino contributes one member to the Tokyo Metropolitan Assembly. In terms of national politics, the city is part of [[Tokyo 18th district]] of the [[House of Representatives of Japan|lower house]] of the [[Diet of Japan]].

==Musashino in popular culture==
* [[Satoshi Kon]]'s [[anime series]] ''[[Paranoia Agent]]'' and Oyuki Konno's [[shōjo]] novel series ''[[Maria-sama ga Miteru]]'' take place in Musashino.
* [[Shōhei Ōoka]]'s 1951 novel ''A Wife in Musashino (Musashino Fujin)'' is a drama in which a moral and stoic woman, trapped in a loveless marriage with a selfish and morally decadent man, becomes implicated against her will in what looks like an affair with her younger cousin. The story ends tragically as she is let down by him as well as by her husband. [[Kenji Mizoguchi]] made the story into a film in 1951, starring [[Kinuyo Tanaka]] and [[Masayuki Mori (actor)|Masayuki Mori]].
* Innocent Grey's 2007 [[visual novel]] ''[[Kara no Shoujo]]'' features many scenes set in Musashino in 1956. Inokashira Park in particular serves as a pivotal setting for much of the story.
* In the ''[[Yokohama Kaidashi Kikō]]'' manga by [[Hitoshi Ashinano]], Musashino is the "former capital of the East" in a post-Apocalyptic Japan in which the ocean level continually rises, implying Tokyo itself had been submerged many years before it.
* In the anime ''[[Shirobako]]'' the animation studio is named Musashino Animation.
*In the manga series ''[[Great Teacher Onizuka]]'', Onizuka lives in Musashino and his first job is at Musashino Public High School. He later gets a job at a private school in [[Kichijōji]].
