'''Jaraguá do Sul''' ({{Coord|26|29|10|S|49|04|00|W|type:city(124661)_scale:30000_region:BR}}) is a city in the north of the Brazilian state of [[Santa Catarina (state)|Santa Catarina]].

==History==
The city was founded on July 25, 1876, by engineer and [[Brazilian Army]] colonel Emílio Carlos Jourdan and his family. The city was originally named just Jaraguá, but it was later renamed to Jaraguá do Sul due to a city already named [[Jaraguá, Goiás|Jaraguá]] in Goiás state.<ref name="sc">{{in lang|pt}} [http://www.sc.gov.br/clipping_governo/noticia_int.asp?str_data=13/07/2006&cd_noticia=134218&str_retorno=clipping.asp  Os prós e os contras de Jaraguá – Government of Santa Catarina] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110524050605/http://www.sc.gov.br/clipping_governo/noticia_int.asp?str_data=13%2F07%2F2006&cd_noticia=134218&str_retorno=clipping.asp |date=May 24, 2011 }}</ref>

==Etymology==
''Jaraguá'' means ''Lord of the Valley'' in a [[Tupi–Guarani languages|Tupi–Guarani language]]. It was the name given by the indigenous population of the city to the Boa Vista Hill (Morro da Boa Vista).<ref>{{in lang|pt}} [https://ocp.news/entretenimento/por-que-jaragua-do-sul-tem-esse-nome – Por que Jaraguá do Sul tem esse nome? 4 curiosidades sobre a história da cidade]</ref>

==Geography==
It borders [[Campo Alegre, Santa Catarina|Campo Alegre]] and [[São Bento do Sul]] to the north; [[Blumenau]], [[Massaranduba, Santa Catarina|Massaranduba]], [[Pomerode]] and [[Rio dos Cedros]] to the south; [[Guaramirim]], [[Joinville]] and [[Schroeder, Santa Catarina|Schroeder]] to the east and [[Corupá]] to the west.

The [[Rio Itapocu|Itapocu River]] is Jaraguá do Sul's important river, crossing the city, being [[Jaraguá River]] and [[Itapocuzinho River]] its main affluents.

The [[climate]] is considered [[temperate]], with average annual temperature around 21&nbsp;°C. In summer temperatures often exceed 35&nbsp;°C, although they may reach 40&nbsp;°C in a few days. The winter is relatively cold for Brazilian standards, with an average minimum temperature around 12&nbsp;°C in the months of June and July. [[Frost]]s occur almost every winter. Zero temperatures are rare, and that mark was reached most recently on July 14, 2000. The lowest temperature occurred on July 19, 1975, when thermometers recorded −2&nbsp;°C. The highest temperature ever recorded was 42.5&nbsp;°C on January 3, 1973.

==Sports==
[[Malwee/Jaraguá]], formerly known as Associação Desportiva Jaraguá, is a successful [[futsal]] club, which have won the [[Liga Futsal|national league]] twice. In 2007, the city's arena, named [[Arena Jaraguá]], hosted the final matches of the [[Grand Prix de Futsal]].<ref name="grandprixdefutsal">{{in lang|pt}} [http://www.futsalbrasil.com.br/noticias/noticia.php?cd_noticia=3926 3º Grand Prix de Futsal: Definidos os quatro grupos da competição – Futsal Brasil] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090707230518/http://www.futsalbrasil.com.br/noticias/noticia.php?cd_noticia=3926 |date=July 7, 2009 }}</ref> The arena also hosted two [[Ultimate Fighting Championship]] events, [[UFC on FX: Belfort vs. Rockhold|UFC on FX 8]] in 2013 and [[UFC Fight Night: Machida vs. Mousasi|UFC Fight Night 36]] in 2014.

The city's [[association football|football (soccer)]] club is [[Grêmio Esportivo Juventus]]. The retired goalkeeper [[Eduardo Roberto Stinghen]], nicknamed Ado, was born in the city in 1946. Also, [[Filipe Luís]], who previously played for [[Flamengo]], was born in the city.

==Industry==
The local economy is mainly based in metallurgy and clothing industries. The city was the origin for several worldwide companies, like [[WEG Industries|WEG]] (electric motors and industrial electronics), Marisol (clothing), Duas Rodas (food seasoning), Malwee (clothing), Menegotti (construction equipment) and many others. The city is the 7th largest economy of the state.<ref>{{in lang|pt}} [https://www.jdv.com.br/sc-tem-cinco-entre-os-maiores-pibs-do-brasil-jaragua-tem-a-setima-economia – SC tem cinco entre os maiores PIBs do Brasil; Jaraguá tem a sétima economia]</ref>

==FEMUSC==
Jaraguá do Sul is home to the Festival de Música de Santa Catarina (FEMUSC) which was created by [[Alex Klein]].  The festival is held in late January and attracts students and faculty from all over the world. The program involves faculty and students recitals, orchestral and band performances, lessons and masterclasses.
<ref>{{in lang|pt}} [https://www.alexoboeklein.com/femusc – FEMUSC]</ref>

==Schützenfest==
The [[Schützenfest]] (also known in Brazil as ''Festa dos Atiradores'') is a traditional festival held annually since 1988, celebrated in October in Jaraguá do Sul. It is part of the popular festivals celebrated in that month in Santa Catarina state due to the German colonization in the region.<ref name="schutzenfest">{{in lang|pt}} [http://turismo.jaraguadosul.com.br/modules/xt_conteudo/index.php?id=240 Schützenfest  – Turismo de Jaraguá do Sul] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080316090837/http://turismo.jaraguadosul.com.br/modules/xt_conteudo/index.php?id=240 |date=March 16, 2008 }}</ref>

==Demographics==

===Ethnic groups===
The first inhabitants of the city were the [[Indigenous peoples in Brazil|indigenous people]] of the [[Xokleng people|Xokleng]] and the [[Kaingang people|Kaingang]].<ref name="guia">{{in lang|pt}} [http://www.guiasantacatarina.com.br/jaraguadosul/cidade.php3 Jaraguá do Sul – Guia Santa Catarina]</ref> Later the following ethnic groups immigrated to the city: Germans, Hungarians, Italians, Poles, and [[Afro-Brazilian|African]]s, who were brought to the city as slaves.<ref name="prefeitura">{{in lang|pt}} [http://cultura.jaraguadosul.com.br/modules/xt_conteudo/index.php?id=352 Etnias – Prefeitura Municipal de Jaraguá do Sul] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110728172628/http://cultura.jaraguadosul.com.br/modules/xt_conteudo/index.php?id=352 |date=July 28, 2011 }}</ref>
By 2002 City Hall research concluded that 45% of the people from Jaraguá do Sul are descended from Germans. The other main groups were Italians (25%), Poles (6%), and Hungarians (3%); 21% had other ancestry.[33]
