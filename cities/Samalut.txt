'''Samalut''' ({{langx|ar|سمالوط}}, from {{Langx|cop|ⲧⲥⲉⲙⲟⲩⲗⲟⲧ}} ''t-Semulot)'' is a [[List of cities and towns in Egypt|city]] and a municipal division in the [[Minya Governorate]] in [[Egypt]]. It is located on the west bank of the [[Nile]], a few hours by train south of [[Cairo]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.worldatlas.com/af/eg/mn/where-is-samalut.html|title=Where is Samalut, Egypt?|date=2015-10-02|website=WorldAtlas|language=en|access-date=2019-04-12}}</ref>

The earliest known reference to the city of Samalut can be found in a Coptic funerary inscription that possibly dates back to the 5th century AD.<ref>SB Kopt II 1089.1-2</ref>

==Religion==
[[File:Church of Virgin Mary in Gabal el Teir, Samalut Minya.jpg|thumb|Church of the Blessed Virgin Mary of Gabal el Teir]]
Near Samalut is the [[Coptic Orthodox]] [[Monastery]] of the [[Virgin Mary]] at [[Gebel el-Teir]], an important [[Christians|Christian]] pilgrimage site. Samalut's church was built by [[Helena of Constantinople|Empress Helena]], mother of [[Constantine the Great]], in AD 328, on one of the sites where the [[Holy Family]] is believed to have stayed during their [[flight into Egypt]].
