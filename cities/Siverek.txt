'''Siverek''' (from {{langx|hy|Սեւավերակ|lit=black ruins|translit=Sevaverag}},<ref>{{Cite web |title=KAZA SIVEREK / ՍԵՎԵՐԵԿ – SEVEREK / SIVERAK |url=https://virtual-genocide-memorial.de/region/the-six-provinces/diyarbakir-vilayet/sancak-diyarbekir-diyarbakir/kaza-siverek-սեվերեկ-severek/ |access-date=15 June 2022}}</ref> {{langx|ku|Sêwreg}})<ref>{{cite book |author1=Adem Avcıkıran |title=Kürtçe Anamnez Anamneza bi Kurmancî |date=2009 |page=57 |url=http://tirsik.net/danegeh/pirtuk/ismail%20bulbul/anamneza%20bi%20kurmanc%C3%AE.pdf |accessdate=17 December 2019 |language=tr, ku}}</ref> is a municipality and [[Districts of Turkey|district]] of [[Şanlıurfa Province]], [[Turkey]].<ref>[https://www.e-icisleri.gov.tr/Anasayfa/MulkiIdariBolumleri.aspx Büyükşehir İlçe Belediyesi], Turkey Civil Administration Departments Inventory. Retrieved 19 September 2023.</ref> Its area is 3,936&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.harita.gov.tr/uploads/files-folder/il_ilce_alanlari.xlsx|title=İl ve İlçe Yüz ölçümleri|publisher=General Directorate of Mapping|access-date=19 September 2023}}</ref> and its population is 267,942 (2022).<ref name=tuik>{{Cite web |title=Address-based population registration system (ADNKS) results dated 31 December 2022, Favorite Reports|url=https://biruni.tuik.gov.tr/medas/?kn=95&locale=en |access-date=19 September 2023|publisher=[[TÜİK]]|language=en|format=XLS}}</ref> Siverek is in the Şanlıurfa province but is geographically closer to the large city of [[Diyarbakır]] (approx. 83&nbsp;km).

==History==
Siverek was historically known in medieval Arabic as '''Hisn ar-Ran''' ({{langx|ar|حصن الران}}), which was corrupted into Greek as '''Chasanara''' ({{langx|el|Χασαναρᾶ}}), as found in the [[Escorial Taktikon]].<ref name="Dumbarton Oaks 1991">{{Catalogue of Byzantine Seals at Dumbarton Oaks and in the Fogg Museum of Art|volume=4 |page=174}}</ref> The town came under [[Byzantine Empire|Byzantine]] control sometime after 956 and had become the seat of a ''[[strategos#Byzantine use|strategos]]'' by the early 970s.<ref name="Dumbarton Oaks 1991"/> Together with [[Edessa]], [[Gerger|Gargar]], [[Samsat|Samosata]] and [[Adıyaman|Hisn Mansur]] formed part of the Byzantine defence system up to the 1060s when 200 Frankish horsemen were stationed there.{{sfn|Beihammer|2017|p=115}} 

In the [[Ottoman Empire]] period, Siverek was within the [[Diyarbekir vilayet]], and it had several Christian settlements.<ref name=":022">{{Cite book|title=Social Relations in Ottoman Diyarbekir, 1870-1915|publisher=Brill|year=2012|isbn=9789004225183|editor-last=Jorngerden|editor-first=Joost|pages=304–310|editor-last2=Verheij|editor-first2=Jelle}}</ref>

== Demographics ==
9,275 Armenians lived in the [[kaza]] on the eve of the First World War according to the [[Armenian Patriarchate of Constantinople]]. They had eight churches and three schools. 5,450 Armenians and 1,200 Syriacs lived in the town of Siverek. 6,550 Syriacs lived in 32 surrounding villages.<ref name=vgm>{{Cite web |title=Kaza Siverek / Սեվերեկ - Severek / Siverak / Siwarak ܣܝܒܪܟ |url=https://virtual-genocide-memorial.de/region/the-six-provinces/diyarbakir-vilayet/sancak-diyarbekir-diyarbakir/kaza-siverek-%D5%BD%D5%A5%D5%BE%D5%A5%D6%80%D5%A5%D5%AF-severek/ |access-date=2023-09-18 |website=Virtual Genocide Memorial |language=en-US}}</ref> Armenian men were massacred in May 1915, followed by the deportation of the women and children, as part of the [[Armenian genocide]].<ref name=vgm/> [[Faiz El-Ghusein]] reported that the road between Urfa and Severek was littered with corpses.<ref name=vgm/>

==Politics==
In common with other districts of Şanlıurfa, business and politics in Siverek are strongly influenced, even controlled, by a powerful clan. Siverek is the home town of [[Sedat Bucak]], the former [[DYP]] member of parliament who survived the car crash in the [[Susurluk scandal]]. He is the leader of the Bucak tribe, one of whom has represented the area in the Turkish Parliament since its foundation. Sedat Bucak remains a friend of former DYP leader [[Mehmet Ağar]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.sabah.com.tr/2006/09/04/siy118.html |title=Ağar'ın Bucak sevgisi |work=Sabah |date=2006-09-04 |language=tr |access-date=2008-10-10 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://archive.today/20070503130204/http://www.sabah.com.tr/2006/09/04/siy118.html |archive-date=2007-05-03 }}</ref> In the [[2019 Turkish local elections|local elections in March 2019]], Şehmus Aydın was elected mayor.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sabah.com.tr/secim/31-mart-2019-yerel-secim-sonuclari/sanliurfa/siverek/ilcesi-yerel-secim-sonuclari|title=Şanlıurfa Siverek Seçim Sonuçları - 31 Mart 2019 Yerel Seçimleri|website=www.sabah.com.tr|access-date=2020-03-26}}</ref> He resigned due to health problems in 2020, and was succeeded by Ayşe Çakmak (AKP).<ref>[https://www.aa.com.tr/tr/turkiye/siverek-belediye-baskanligina-ayse-cakmak-secildi/2064273 Siverek Belediye Başkanlığına Ayşe Çakmak seçildi], [[Anadolu Agency]], 3 December 2020.</ref> The current [[kaymakam]] is Musa Aydemir.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.siverek.gov.tr/kaymakam-aziz-golbasi|title=Siverek Kaymakamlığı|website=www.siverek.gov.tr|access-date=11 October 2023}}</ref>
