'''Baraut''' is a  city and municipal board in [[Bagpat district|Baghpat]] district, Uttar Pradesh, India.

==Name==
According to Paul Whalley, the name ''Baṛaut'' is derived from ''baṛ'', one of the Hindi-Urdu names for the [[banyan]] tree, plus the Sanskrit possessive suffix ''[https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/-वत्#Sanskrit -vat]'' (which has often been shortened to ''-aut'' in modern place names), so the name originally meant "having banyan trees".<ref name="Whalley 1927">{{cite journal |last1=Whalley |first1=Paul |title=Place-Names in the United Provinces of Agra and Oudh, Chapter III, Section 2: Suffixes |journal=The Journal of the United Provinces Historical Society |date=1927 |volume=3 |issue=3 |pages=52–98 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=PwdDAAAAYAAJ |access-date=22 July 2023}}</ref>{{rp|89–90}}

==Geography==
Baraut is located at [https://tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Baraut&params=29.6_N_77.16_E_ 29.6°N 77.16°E], covering an area of 10.36 square kilometres (4.00 sq mi) and lying between the [[Ganges|Ganga]] and [[Yamuna]] river plains. Its average elevation is 231 m (758 feet). Baraut is served by [[National Highway 709B (India)|NH-709B]] and state highways SH-57 and SH-82.

Baraut is located 55&nbsp;kilometers (34&nbsp;mi) from [[Delhi]] (the national capital of India) and 55&nbsp;kilometers from Meerut, and is within the [[National Capital Region (India)|National Capital Region (NCR)]].

==Demographics==
===Population===
As of [[2011 Indian Census]], Baraut had a total population of 103,764, of which 55,013 were males and 48,751 were females. Population within the age group of 0 to 6 years was 14,149. The total number of literates in Baraut was 68,690, which constituted 66.2% of the population with male literacy of 71.8% and female literacy of 59.9%. The effective literacy rate of 7+ population of Baraut was 76.7%, of which male literacy rate was 83.5% and female literacy rate was 69.0%. The [[Scheduled Castes]] population was 6,357. Baraut had 17924 in 2011.<ref name="Census2011Gov">{{cite web |title=Census of India: Baraut |url=https://censusindia.gov.in/pca/SearchDetails.aspx?Id=136886 |website=www.censusindia.gov.in |access-date=23 November 2020}}</ref>

Most people in the city are followers of [[Hinduism]], with significant population of [[Muslim]]s and [[Jainism|Jains]].<ref name="Religion"/>

Baraut is a multi-religious place with 32 [[Hindu temple|temples]], 21 [[mosque]]s, 1 [[gurdwara]], and 2 [[Church (building)|church]]es in Baraut city.<ref name="BagpatBarautAboutOld">{{cite web |title=About Baraut |url=http://bagpat.nic.in/about_baraut.htm |date=2002 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070410121708/http://bagpat.nic.in/about_baraut.htm |archive-date=10 April 2007 |access-date=15 July 2018}}</ref>

===Languages===
The official languages of the city is [[Hindi]], and [[Urdu]] is the additional official language.<ref name="langoff"/> [[Khariboli]] (similar to [[Haryanvi]]) is the regional Hindi dialect spoken in Baraut and the adjoining area.

== Administration and politics ==
[[Baraut Assembly constituency|Baraut]] is one of the 403 constituencies of the [[Uttar Pradesh Legislative Assembly]], India. The first election of this assembly constituency was held in 1952.<ref>{{cite web |title=DPACO (1952) |url=https://old.eci.gov.in/files/file/3952-dpaco-1951/ |website=Election Commission of India |access-date=16 December 2018}}</ref> In 1952, Umrao Dutta Ved won the elections and became the first [[Member of Legislative Assembly]] (MLA) of Baraut.<ref name=" 1952 Election Results ">{{cite news|title= 1952 Election Results|publisher=[[Election Commission of India]] website|access-date=1 October 2015|url=http://eci.nic.in/eci_main/StatisticalReports/SE_1952/StatRep_UP_1952.pdf}}</ref> After the 1969 elections, the constituency was dissolved. In 2008, when the "Delimitation of Parliamentary and Assembly Constituencies Order, 2008" was passed, the constituency was re-constituted and elections were held in 2012. The constituency is assigned identification number 51. Krishan Pal Malik of BJP is the MLA after winning second consecutive term in 2017 and 2022.<ref>{{cite news |title=krishan-pal-malik in Uttar Pradesh Assembly Elections 2022 |url=https://www.news18.com/assembly-elections-2022/uttar-pradesh/krishan-pal-malik-baraut-candidate-s24a051c01/ |access-date=29 May 2022 |work=News18 |language=en}}</ref> Babita Tomar is the chairman of the town.<ref name="Baraut_NPP"/>

Baraut is part of the [[National Capital Region (India)|National Capital Region]] (NCR). It is part of the [[Baghpat district]], which was established as a separate district in 1997. Prior to that, Baghpat was a [[tehsil]] within [[Meerut district]].
