'''Murmansk Oblast'''{{efn|{{bulleted list|{{langx|ru|Му́рманская о́бласть|Murmanskaya oblast'}}, {{IPA|ru|ˈmurmənskəjə ˈobləsʲtʲ}}|{{langx|sjd|Мурман е̄ммьне|Murman jemm'ne}}|{{langx|no|Murmansk fylke<ref>{{cite web | url=https://snl.no/Murmansk_-_fylke | title=Murmansk – fylke | date=September 21, 2023 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.nrk.no/urix/russisk-jagerfly-har-styrtet-i-murmansk-fylke-1.16388882 | title=Russisk jagerfly har styrtet i Murmansk fylke | date=April 26, 2023 }}</ref>}}}}}} is a [[federal subjects of Russia|federal subject]] (an [[oblast]]) of [[Russia]], located in the northwestern part of the country, with a total land area of {{convert|144,900|km2}}. Its only internal border is the [[Republic of Karelia]] to the south, and it is bordered internationally by [[Finland]] to the west and [[Norway]] to the northwest and the [[Barents Sea]] lies to the north and [[White Sea]] lies to the south and east. Its [[administrative center]] is the [[types of inhabited localities in Russia|city]] of [[Murmansk]]. As of the [[Russian Census (2010)|2010 Census]], its population was 795,409,<ref name="2010Census">{{ru-pop-ref|2010Census}}</ref> but at the 2021 Census this had declined to 667,744.<ref>Federal State Statistics Service Russia (web).</ref>

== Geography ==
[[File:Вайдагубский маяк.jpg|thumb|Vaidagubsky [[lighthouse]] in Murmansk Oblast]]
Geographically, Murmansk Oblast is located mainly on the [[Kola Peninsula]] almost completely north of the [[Arctic Circle]]<ref name="Atlas2007">''2007 Atlas of Murmansk Oblast'', p.&nbsp;2</ref> and is a part of the larger [[Lapland (region)|Sápmi (Lapland) region]] that spans over four countries.<ref name="Lapland">Ratcliffe, p.&nbsp;1</ref> The oblast borders with the [[Republic of Karelia]] in Russia in the south, [[Lapland, Finland|Lapland Region]] in [[Finland]] in the west, [[Finnmark|Finnmark County]] in [[Norway]] in the northwest, and is bounded by the [[Barents Sea]] in the north and the [[White Sea]] in the south and east.<ref name="Atlas2007" /> [[Arkhangelsk Oblast]] of Russia lies across the White Sea.<ref name="Atlas2007" />

Much of the oblast's relief is hilly, with the [[Khibiny Mountains|Khibiny]] and [[Lovozero Massif|Lovozero]] ranges rising as high as {{convert|1200|m|sp=us}} above [[sea level]] and stretching from west to east.<ref name="Atlas2007" /> The highest point of Murmansk Oblast is [[Yudychvumchorr]], a flat-topped peak of the Khibiny.<ref name="PGSR">[https://web.archive.org/web/20140128005117/http://geo.1september.ru/2006/04/16.htm Физико-географическая статистика России (Physical and geographical statistics of Russia) – Yudychvumchorr]</ref> The north of the oblast is mostly covered by [[tundra]]; forest tundra prevails further south, while the southern regions are in the [[taiga]] zone.<ref name="Atlas2007" /> There are over 100,000 lakes and 18,000 rivers in the oblast.<ref name="Atlas2007" /> The coast contains the [[Rybachy Peninsula]] and the [[Cape Svyatoy Nos, Murmansk Oblast|Cape Svyatoy Nos]] peninsulas.

The climate is harsh and unstable, due to the proximity of the [[Gulf Stream]] on one side and Arctic cold fronts on the other.<ref name="Atlas2007" /> Sharp temperature changes, high winds, and abundant [[precipitation]] are common throughout the year, with the heating season lasting for ten straight months.<ref name="Atlas2007" /> However, the waters of the [[Murman Coast]] in the south remain warm enough to remain ice-free even in winter.<ref name="Field">Field</ref>

There is also a large number of islands belonging to the oblast, the main ones being (west to east) the [[Aynovy Islands]], [[Bolshoy Oleny Island]], [[Kildin Island]] [[Malyy Oleniy Island]], [[Kharlov Island]], [[Vesknyak Island]], [[Litskiye Island]], [[Nokuyev Island]], [[Vitte Island]], [[Lumbovskiy Island]], [[Goryainov Island]] and [[Sosnovets Island]].

=== Climate ===

== History ==
{{Main|Kola Peninsula#History|l1=Kola Peninsula History}}

The [[Sami people|Saami]], now a very small minority, are the indigenous people of the region.  [[Russians]] started exploring the shores of the White Sea as early as in the 12th century, and in 1916 during [[World War I]] founded the Russian city of [[Murmansk]] as an ice-free supply port.<ref>

As of 2010, Murmansk had {{ru-census2010|307,257|,}} people – nearly 40% of the oblast's population. Many [[Murmansk Finns|Finns]] also immigrated to Murmansk during the [[Finnish famine of 1866–68|Finnish famine]], around the year 1860.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://helda.helsinki.fi/handle/10138/323421|title=Muurmannin rannalle ja Venäjän Lappiin : Suomalaisten siirtolaisuus Kuolan niemimaalle ja kuolansuomalaiset 1858–1917|year=2020|last1=Mikko|first1=Kuitula}}</ref>

The oblast was established on May&nbsp;28, 1938 from [[Murmansk Okrug]] of [[Leningrad Oblast]] (comprising the city of Murmansk, [[Kirovsky District, Murmansk Oblast|Kirovsky]], [[Kolsky District|Kolsky]], [[Lovozersky District|Lovozersky]], [[Polyarny District|Polyarny]], [[Saamsky District|Saamsky]], [[Teribersky District|Teribersky]], and [[Tersky District, Murmansk Oblast|Tersky Districts]]) and [[Kandalakshsky District]] of the [[Karelian Autonomous Soviet Socialist Republic|Karelian ASSR]].<ref name="Established">Decree of May&nbsp;28, 1938</ref>

The area of [[Pechengsky District]] (''Petsamo'' in [[Finnish language|Finnish]]), which was ceded to Finland by the [[Treaty of Tartu (Russian–Finnish)|1920 Treaty of Tartu]] and gave Finland access to the [[Barents Sea]], became once again part of the [[RSFSR]] in 1940. After the [[Paris Peace Treaties, 1947|Paris Peace Treaties of 1947]], the local Saami population was given the choice either of staying in Soviet Russia or resettling in Finland.{{citation needed|date=May 2012}} Most of them chose the second option.{{citation needed|date=May 2012}} On 30 October 1997, Murmansk, alongside [[Astrakhan Oblast|Astrakhan]], [[Kirov Oblast|Kirov]], [[Ulyanovsk Oblast|Ulyanovsk]], and [[Yaroslavl Oblast|Yaroslavl]] signed a [[Politics_of_Russia#Power_sharing | power-sharing agreement]] with the government of Russia, gaining autonomy.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://jamestown.org/program/yeltsin-signs-power-sharing-agreements-with-five-more-russian-regions/ |title= Yeltsin Signs Power-Sharing Agreements With Five More Russian Regions|date= 1997-11-03|website= Jamestown|language= en-US|access-date=2019-05-02}}</ref> The autonomous status operated until 31 May 2003.<ref>{{Cite journal|last= Chuman |first= Mizuki|title=The Rise and Fall of Power-Sharing Treaties Between Center and Regions in Post-Soviet Russia|url= http://demokratizatsiya.pub/archives/19_2_L7H017206G216817.pdf|journal=Demokratizatsiya|pages=146}}
</ref>

== Politics ==
[[File:Здание городской и областной администрации в Мурманске..JPG|thumb|Oblast Administration (right) and City Administration (left) buildings on Lenina Avenue, Murmansk]]

During the [[Soviet Union|Soviet]] period, the high authority in the oblast was shared between three persons: The first secretary of the Murmansk CPSU Committee (who in reality had the biggest authority), the chairman of the oblast Soviet (legislative power), and the Chairman of the oblast Executive Committee (executive power). Since 1991, CPSU lost all the power, and the head of the Oblast administration, and eventually the governor was appointed/elected alongside elected [[Regional parliaments of Russia|regional parliament]].

The Charter of Murmansk Oblast is the fundamental law of the region. The Legislative Assembly of Murmansk Oblast is the province's standing legislative (representative) body. The Legislative Assembly exercises its authority by passing laws, resolutions, and other legal acts and by supervising the implementation and observance of the laws and other legal acts passed by it. The highest executive body is the Oblast Government, which includes territorial executive bodies such as district administrations, committees, and commissions that facilitate development and run the day to day matters of the province. The Oblast administration supports the activities of the Governor who is the highest official and acts as guarantor of the observance of the oblast Charter by the [[Constitution of Russia]].

According to a 2012 survey<ref name="2012ArenaAtlas" /> 41.7% of the population of Murmansk Oblast adheres to the [[Russian Orthodox Church]], 3% are [[nondenominational Christianity|unaffiliated]] generic [[Christians]], 1% are Orthodox Christian believers who do not belong to any church or are members of other (non-Russian) [[Eastern Orthodox Church|Orthodox church]]es, 1% are adherents of [[Islam]], 0.4% are adherents of [[Rodnovery]] (Slavic native faith) and other indigenous folk religions, and 1% are members of the [[Catholic Church]]. In addition, 28% of the population declares to be "spiritual but not religious", 12% is [[atheism|atheist]], and 12.5% follows other religions or did not give an answer to the question.<ref name="2012ArenaAtlas" />

== Economy ==
The Murmansk Oblast is very rich in natural resources and has deposits of over 700 minerals.<ref name="fas">{{cite web|url=http://www.fas.org/nuke/guide/russia/industry/docs/mark0241.htm|title=Overview of Murmansk Region|publisher=Federation of American Scientists|access-date=2009-06-05}}</ref> The main industries of the region are in the sphere of raw material extraction and basic processing.<ref name="globalsecurity">[http://www.globalsecurity.org/military/world/russia/murmansk-ob.htm Murmansk Oblast] Globalsecurity.org</ref> The largest industries are metallurgy (36,6%), electric power-production (22,9%) and food-industry, including fishing (13,7%).<ref name="amk">{{cite web|url=https://www.amk.fi/bin/get/id/5aBm9Nrfx|title=Murmansk region|publisher=Häme Polytechnic|access-date=2009-06-05|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110720175523/https://www.amk.fi/bin/get/id/5aBm9Nrfx|archive-date=July 20, 2011|df=mdy-all}}</ref><ref name="rustradeuk">{{cite web|url=http://www.rustradeuk.org/cooperation/rusregion/about_regions/files/Murmansk_region.ppt|title=Murmansk Region|access-date=2009-06-05}}</ref> The icefree port of Murmansk plays an important role in marine transportation in Russia, and the oblast has a 41% share of the total Russian marine transport market.<ref name="barentsinfo" /><ref name="economyreport">[http://img.custompublish.com/getfile.php/618220.623.vbftubsawf/Barents+Review.Economic+Development+in+the+Murmansk+Region.pdf Economic Development in the Murmansk Region in 2007]</ref> The fishing industry is among the most profitable in the region, supplying 16% of Russia's total fish production. Murmansk is a key base for three fishing fleets, including Russia's largest, the [[Murmansk Trawl Fleet]].<ref name="globalsecurity" />
[[File:Monchegorsk factories.jpg|thumb|A [[Norilsk Nickel]] plant (formerly, [[Severonikel]]) in [[Monchegorsk]]]]

The economy of the region is export-oriented. Main export items are nickel products, [[apatite]] concentrate, copper and copper products, aluminium and ferrous metals.<ref name="barentsinfo">{{cite web|url=http://www.barentsinfo.org/?newsid=8370&deptid=13875&languageid=4&news=1|title=Non-working link message – Barentsinfo|work=barentsinfo.org|access-date=January 26, 2017}}{{Dead link|date=April 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> The Murmansk Region produces almost 100 percent of Russia's apatite concentrate (3.7 million tons in 1998), 43 percent of nickel, 15 percent of copper, 12 percent of iron ore and iron ore concentrate (17.7 million and 6.4 million tons in 1998), and 40 percent of cobalt.<ref name="fas" /><ref name="amk" />
{{Further|Western Zhdanovskoye mine}}

The largest companies of the region – constituting 90% of the oblast's production – are [[Pechenganickel]], [[Olcon]], the [[Kola Nuclear Power Plant]], [[Sevrybkholodflot]], [[Murmanrybprom]], [[Murmansk Trawl Fleet]] and [[Murmansk Shipping Company]].<ref name="fas" />

Large oil and gas resources have been discovered on the shelf of the Barents sea, including the massive [[Shtokman field]] – one of the world's largest gas fields with estimated reserves of 3.8 trillion cubic meters.<ref name="fas" /><ref name="economyreport" /><ref name="reuters_gas">{{cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/gazprom-shtokman-idUSL1589543420071115|title=UPDATE 1-Russia's Gazprom ups Shtokman reserves to 3.8 tcm|date=November 15, 2016|access-date=January 26, 2017|newspaper=Reuters}}</ref> Prospective oil fields could potentially yield up to 40 million tons in the next 10–15 years.<ref name="fas" /> However, the development of the oil and gas resources will require considerable investment.<ref name="fas" />

In 2006, the Murmansk Oblast's [[gross regional product]] was 141.9 billion rubles, which amounts to about 0.4% of the Russian GDP.<ref name="barentsinfo" /> Unemployment in 2006 was 3,4%.<ref name="barentsinfo" /> GRP pro capita in 2007 was 225 044 rubles.<ref name="grpc2007">[http://www.gks.ru/bgd/free/b01_19/IssWWW.exe/Stg/d000/dusha98-07.htm Валовой региональный продукт на душу населения] Федеральная служба государственной статистики</ref> Regional automobile code is 51.

=== Transport ===
* Airports in [[Murmansk Airport|Murmansk]] (international), [[Kirovsk–Apatity Airport|Kirovsk]], Kandalaksha, [[Severomorsk-3 (air base)|Severomorsk]] (military), [[Lovozero (air base)|Lovozero]] (military), Ponoy, and Krasnoshchelye (small planes and choppers).
* Strategic [[Oktyabrskaya Railway]] which connects Murmansk with [[Saint-Petersburg]] and central [[Russia]]. Main stations are Murmansk, Olenegorsk, Kandalaksha. There is also important shoulder to [[Nikel]], the [[Murmansk-Nikel Railway]].
* Local one-way railways
* Sea routes to small military towns and naval bases on [[Murman Coast]] (Ostrovnoy, Svyatoy Nos).
* Automobile roads

== Military ==
The Murmansk Oblast plays an important role for the [[Russian Navy]], the [[Northern Fleet]] having its headquarters in [[Severomorsk]], 25&nbsp;km north of Murmansk. The Navy has several other bases and shipyards in the Murmansk Oblast.

The [[200th Separate Motor Rifle Brigade]] is stationed at [[Pechenga (urban-type settlement), Murmansk Oblast|Pechenga]].
