'''Gharbia Governorate''' ({{langx|ar|محافظة الغربية}} ''{{transl|ar|Muḥāfaẓat al-Gharbiyya}}'', {{IPA|arz|elɣɑɾˈbejjɑ, -jæ|IPA}}, "the western governorate") is one of the [[governorates of Egypt]]. It is located in the north of the country, south of [[Kafr El Sheikh Governorate]], and north of [[Monufia Governorate]]. Its capital is [[Tanta]], which is 90&nbsp;km north of [[Cairo]], and 120&nbsp;km south east of [[Alexandria]]. The largest city in Gharbia is [[El Mahalla El Kubra]]. The total area of Gharbia governorate is 1,942&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>.

==Municipal divisions==
The governorate is divided into [[Subdivisions_of_Egypt#Municipal_divisions|municipal divisions]], with a total estimated population, as of January 2023, of [https://www.capmas.gov.eg/Pages/populationClock.aspx 5,500,507]. In some instances there is a markaz and a kism with the same name.<ref name="CAPMAS2023" />

==History==
''al-Gharbiyya'' is an [[Arabic language|Arabic]] term meaning "Western one" or "Western side".<ref name="EI2">{{EI2|title=al-G̲h̲arbiyya|last=Herzog|first=R.|volume=2|page=1010}}</ref> In the 13th century it comprised 165 villages.  The 15th-century geographer [[al-Qalqashandi]] describes the region as fertile and prosperous.<ref name="EI2"/>

[[El Mahalla El Kubra|El Mahalla El Kobra]] was the provincial capital until 1836,  later succeeded by [[Tanta]].<ref name="EI2" />

The region is notable for the cultivation of [[cotton]], and its textile industry.<ref name="EI2"/>

==Population==
In 1960, the governorate numbered 1,815,000 inhabitants.<ref name="EI2"/> According to population estimates, in 2015 the majority of residents in the governorate lived in rural areas, with an [[urbanization]] rate of only 30.0%. Out of an estimated 4,751.865 people residing in the governorate, 3,324,630 people lived in rural areas as opposed to only 1,427,235 in urban areas.<ref name="pop">{{cite web|title=Population Estimates By Sex & Governorate 1/1/2015|url=http://www.msrintranet.capmas.gov.eg/pdf/EgyptinFigures2015/EgyptinFigures/Tables/PDF/1-%20%D8%A7%D9%84%D8%B3%D9%83%D8%A7%D9%86/pop.pdf|website=CAPMAS|access-date=23 October 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151019093240/http://www.msrintranet.capmas.gov.eg/pdf/EgyptinFigures2015/EgyptinFigures/Tables/PDF/1-%20%D8%A7%D9%84%D8%B3%D9%83%D8%A7%D9%86/pop.pdf|archive-date=2015-10-19|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Projects and programs==
In 2016, Switzerland committed to funding a solid waste management program in Gharbia, a project with the [[Ministry of Environment (Egypt)|Egyptian Ministry of Environment]] that will conclude in 2021. The ''National Solid Waste Management Programme'' (NSWMP) involves the construction of infrastructure for new, as well as the expansion and improvement of existing waste treatment, landfill, and recycling facilities.<ref name="Swiss waste mgmt 2016">{{cite news |title=Switzerland funds programme to improve solid waste management in 4 Egyptian governorates |url=https://dailynewsegypt.com/2016/10/05/switzerland-funds-programme-improve-solid-waste-management-4-egyptian-governorates/ |work=Daily News Egypt |date=5 October 2016}}</ref>
