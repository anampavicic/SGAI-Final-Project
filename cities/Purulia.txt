'''Purulia''', officially '''Purulia Sadar''', is a city and a municipality in the [[India]]n [[States and territories of India|state]] of [[West Bengal]]. It is the headquarters of the [[Purulia district]]. It is located on the north of the [[Kangsabati River]].

===Location===
Purulia is located at {{coord|23.33|N|86.37|E|}}.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fallingrain.com/world/IN/28/Puruliya.html|title=Maps, Weather, and Airports for Puruliya, India}}</ref> It has an average elevation of 228&nbsp;metres (748&nbsp;feet).

===Area overview===
Purulia district forms the lowest step of the [[Chota Nagpur Plateau]]. The general scenario is undulating land with scattered hills. [[Purulia Sadar subdivision]] covers the central portion of the district. 83.80% of the population of the subdivision lives in rural areas. The map alongside shows some urbanization around Purulia city. 18.58% of the population, the highest among the subdivisions of the district, lives in urban areas. There are 4 census towns in the subdivision. The [[Kangsabati River|Kangsabati]] (locally called Kansai) flows through the subdivision. The subdivision has old temples, some of them belonging to the 11th century or earlier. The focus is on education - the university, the sainik school, the Ramakrishna Mission Vidyapith at [[Bongabari]], the upcoming medical college at [[Hatuara]], et al.<ref>Houlton, Sir John, ''Bihar, the Heart of India'', 1949, p. 170, Orient Longmans Ltd.</ref><ref>{{cite web  |url = http://wbpspm.gov.in/publications/District%20Statistical%20Handbook  |title = District Statistical Handbook 2014 Purulia  |work = Tables 2.1, 2.2  |publisher = Department of Planning and Statistics, Government of West Bengal  |access-date = 12 January 2020  |archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20190121045803/http://www.wbpspm.gov.in/publications/District%20Statistical%20Handbook  |archive-date = 21 January 2019  |url-status = dead  }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.censusindia.gov.in/2011census/DCHB/DCHB_A/19/1914_PART_A_DCHB_PURULIYA.pdf | title = District Census Handbook, Puruliya, Series 20, Part XII A| work= Deulghat - ===Climate===
Summers are hot and dry, with temperatures ranging from lows of 23&nbsp;°C to highs above 38&nbsp;°C. Winters are dry and cool, with daily temperatures ranging from 12&nbsp;°C to 26&nbsp;°C. Most of the rainfall occurs during the wet monsoons.

==Purulia arms drop==
{{See also|Purulia arms drop scandal}}
On 18 December 1995, a mysterious weapon consignment was dropped from the sky over Joypur Jhalda area under Purulia district of West Bengal. The consignment was discovered the next morning. The reasons are still unknown.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/india/Purulia-Expose-Indias-best-kept-secret/articleshow/8106795.cms |title=Purulia Expose: India's best kept secret | date=28 April 2011 | newspaper=The Times of India | access-date=10 December 2018}}</ref>
