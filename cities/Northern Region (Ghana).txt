The '''Northern Region''' is one of the sixteen [[regions of Ghana]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=No new Covid-19 cases in Northern Region in 3 weeks - MyJoyOnline.com |url=https://www.myjoyonline.com/no-new-covid-19-cases-in-northern-region-in-3-weeks/ |access-date=2021-05-18 |website=MyJoyOnline. |language=en-US}}</ref> It is situated in the northern part of the country and ranks as the second largest of the sixteen regions. Before its division, it covered an area of 25,000 square kilometres, representing 10 percent of [[Ghana]]'s area. In [[2018 Ghanaian new regions referendum|December 2018]], the [[Savannah Region]] and [[North East Region, Ghana|North East Region]] were created from it.<!--, precisely located in the [[Kingdom of Dagbon (Northern Territories)|Kingdom of Dagbon]].--> The Northern Region is divided into 16 [[district]]s. The region's [[Capital city|capital]] is [[Tamale, Ghana|Tamale]], [[List of cities in Ghana | Ghana's third largest city]].<ref name="Ghanadistricts.com - Northern Region"/>

==Geography and climate==

===Location and size===
The Northern Region, spanning approximately 25,000 square kilometers, stands as Ghana's Second largest region by land area. It shares borders with the [[North East Region (Ghana)|North East Region]] and [[Savannah Region]] to the north, and [[Oti Region|Oti Regions]] to the south, and neighboring countries, including the [[Togo|Republic of Togo]] to the east and La Cote d'voire to the west.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Mutuku |first=Ryan |date=2021-01-25 |title=The Northern Region districts and their capitals in Ghana |url=https://yen.com.gh/180838-the-northern-region-districts-capitals-ghana.html |access-date=2024-02-26 |website=Yen.com.gh - Ghana news. |language=en}}</ref> 

===Climate and vegetation===
[[File:Baobob tree.jpg|thumb|left|130px|A [[Adansonia digitata|baobab tree]]]]
The Northern Region has a [[Guinean forest–savanna mosaic|Guinean forest-savanna mosaic]] ecosystem. The Guinea Savanna is the wettest of the three savanna ecological zones. The vegetation consists predominantly of woodlands and grasslands. The [[wet season]] is between April and October, while the dry season occurs from January to March. There is an average annual rainfall of 750 to 1050&nbsp;mm (30 to 40&nbsp;inches). The highest temperatures are reached in March, at the end of the dry season. From late November to March, the north east [[trade winds]] blows causing [[Harmattan]].<ref>{{Cite journal |last=Lyngsie |first=G. |last2=Awadzi |first2=T. |last3=Breuning-Madsen |first3=H. |date=2011-11-01 |title=Origin of Harmattan dust settled in Northern Ghana — Long transported or local dust? |url=https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S001670611100228X |journal=Geoderma |volume=167-168 |pages=351–359 |doi=10.1016/j.geoderma.2011.07.026 |issn=0016-7061}}</ref> During this time, temperatures can vary between 14&nbsp;°C (59&nbsp;°F) at night and 40&nbsp;°C (104&nbsp;°F) during the day.{{clear left}}

Like all parts of Ghana, more than half of the economically active population are [[Agriculture|agricultural]]. The region is one of the most agriculturally important regions in Ghana. It also has the largest reserve of iron ore.{{clear left}}

==Demographics==
The Northern Region contains much of the territories of the [[Kingdom of Dagbon]] and [[Dagbani language|Dagbani]] is the most widely spoke language,<ref>{{Cite web |title=Learn Dagbanli (Dagbanli Bɔhimbu) → What Is The Dagbanli Language? - Learn Dagbanli {{!}} Learn Dagbani |url=https://learndagbani.org/course/learn-dagbani/ |access-date=2023-02-04 |language=en-US}}</ref> along with [[English language|English]]. Dagbani belongs to the [[Oti–Volta languages|Oti–Volta]] subfamily in the [[Niger–Congo languages|Niger–Congo language family]]. Other languages spoken within the region include Likpakpaln and Nawuri.

== Business ==
[[File:Night View of Tamale Street in Northern Ghana 01.png|thumb|Night view of the city of Tamale, Ghana.]]

The region is a hotspot for investment in Ghana, with Tamale previously been ranked as the fastest growing city in West Africa; experiencing tremendous growth compared to other cities in Ghana.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Tamale is the fastest growing city in West Africa - Tamale in Ghana |url=https://independent-travellers.com/ghana/tamale/84.php |access-date=2023-02-04 |website=independent-travellers.com}}</ref> The region's location, and greater proximity to both Europe and North Africa, compared to Accra, makes it an increasingly attractive destination for investors. Ghana's largest iron ore reserves, estimated to be more than three billion tons,<ref>{{Cite web |date=2019-05-15 |title=N/R: Over 1.7bn tonnes of iron ore discovered in Yendi Municipality- Majority Leader |url=https://www.primenewsghana.com/general-news/n-r-over-1-7bn-tonnes-of-iron-ore-discovered-in-yendi-municipality-majority-leader.html |access-date=2023-02-04 |website=Prime News Ghana |language=en-us}}</ref> makes the region an ideal destination for investments in steel and iron.
