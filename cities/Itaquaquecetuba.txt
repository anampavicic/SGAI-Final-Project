'''Itaquaquecetuba''', also simply called '''Itaquá''',<ref>{{Cite web|last=Notícias|first=Itaquá|date=2015-11-27|url=https://www.itaquanoticias.com/|access-date=2021-05-17|website=Itaquá Notícias|language=pt-BR}}</ref> is a municipality in the state of [[São Paulo (state)|São Paulo]], [[Brazil]]. It is part of the [[Metropolitan Region of São Paulo]].<ref>[http://governo-sp.jusbrasil.com.br/legislacao/1028148/lei-complementar-1139-11 Lei Complementar nº 1.139, de 16 de junho de 2011]</ref> The population is 375,011 (2020 est.) in an area of {{convert|82.62|km2|2|abbr=out}}.<ref name=ibge>[http://cod.ibge.gov.br/234DT Instituto Brasileiro de Geografia e Estatística]</ref> It sits at an elevation of {{convert|790|m|0|abbr=out}}.

The municipality was founded between 1560 and 1563 by [[Society of Jesus|Jesuits]] led by [[Priest|Father]] [[Joseph of Anchieta]], among native villages near the [[Tietê River]], beginning with the [[Roman Catholic Church|Catholic chapel]] of Our Lady of Acute, which was established by Father Joseph.<ref name="Guia da Cidade de Itaquaquecetuba">[http://www.ilocal.com.br/guia-de-cidades/itaquaquecetuba Guia da Cidade de Itaquaquecetuba] {{Webarchive|url=https://archive.today/20120904231059/http://www.ilocal.com.br/guia-de-cidades/itaquaquecetuba |date=2012-09-04 }} {{in lang|pt}} Retrieved on June 2, 2012</ref> The settlement saw little development, and was almost deserted by the early twentieth century. Until 1958 the city was a district of [[Mogi das Cruzes]]. Following the emancipation of the municipality that year, the first [[Mayor]], Victorio Eugenio Deliberato, began a campaign of industrial development that had great geographic and economic impact on Itaquaquecetuba in the decades to come.<ref name="Guia da Cidade de Itaquaquecetuba"/><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://content.bitsontherun.com/players/Fcsgj0tR-ExeYn3JJ.html |title=Archived copy |access-date=2012-10-30 |archive-date=2016-03-04 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304104530/http://content.bitsontherun.com/players/Fcsgj0tR-ExeYn3JJ.html |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Today, Itaquaquecetuba is one of the 10 best Brazilian cities who had significant advances in challenges against inequality in the last 20 years, and has the second-best GDP in the region.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://americaeconomiabrasil.com.br/rankings/maiores-e-melhores-cidades-brasil-2014/introducao/|title=Maiores e melhores cidades Brasil 2014 - AméricaEconomia}}</ref>

It ranked 199th on the list of cities with the highest [[homicide rates]] per 100,000 inhabitants. Itaquaquecetuba has a strong industrial vocation due to its geographic location: [[Highway|highways]] such as [[SP-66 (São Paulo highway)|SP-66]], [[SP-56 (São Paulo highway)|SP-56]], [[SP-88 (São Paulo highway)|SP-88]] and the [[SP-70 (São Paulo highway)|Ayrton Senna Highway]] pass through the city, connecting the city to cities such as [[Mogi das Cruzes]], [[Poá]], [[Suzano]], [[Ferraz de Vasconcelos]] and [[Arujá]]. The city also [[Border|borders]] other large urban centers, such as the [[São Paulo|state capital]] and [[Guarulhos]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=itaquaquecetuba.net.br |title=itaquaquecetuba.net.br {{!}} Guia de empresas e serviços em Itaquaquecetuba |url=https://itaquaquecetuba.net.br/ |access-date=2024-10-10 |website=itaquaquecetuba.net.br |language=pt}}</ref>

== History ==
The origin of the city dates back to Itaquaquecetuba of the twelve villages founded by the Jesuit priest [[José de Anchieta]], in his long stay in Brazil. Its creation is due to the then president of the province, Bernardo José Pinto Gavião Peixoto, named village of Nossa Senhora da Ajuda, on September 7, 1560, being established in the [[Tietê River|River Tietê]], to catechize the guaianases.<ref name="History_R7">{{cite web |url=http://cidadebrasileira.brasilescola.com/sao-paulo/historia-itaquaquecetuba.htm |title=História de Itaquaquecetuba |work=R7 |publisher=Brasil Escola |language=pt |access-date=25 December 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150223025149/http://cidadebrasileira.brasilescola.com/sao-paulo/historia-itaquaquecetuba.htm |archive-date=23 February 2015 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

== Law and Government ==
Itaquaquecetuba's City Hall is located at 267 Vereador José Fernandes da Silva Avenue.<ref>{{cite web|title=Câmara Municipal de Itaquaquecetuba|url=http://www.camaraitaquaquecetuba.sp.gov.br/|access-date=11 April 2014}}</ref>

==Etymology==
The name is derived from its first form in Tupi ''takwakisé-tube'', whose full meaning is "place of abundant bamboo sharp as knives".

==Transportation==
[[File:CAF Q1.2010 015.JPG|thumb|left|Train at Itaquaquecetuba Train Station.]]

===Public Transportation===
The city is served by [[Line 12 (CPTM)|Line 12]] of [[CPTM]] (Companhia Paulista de Trens Metropolitanos). The [[commuter rail]] system has 3 train stations: Engenheiro Manoel, Aracaré and Itaquaquecetuba. Local bus service is provided by Expresso Planalto and Intercity buses are also available connecting the city to [[Poá]], [[Mogi das Cruzes]], [[Guarulhos]], [[Arujá]], [[Santa Isabel, São Paulo|Santa Isabel]], [[Ferraz de Vasconcelos]], [[Suzano]] and São Paulo.<ref name="e">{{cite web|url=http://www.emtu.sp.gov.br/emtu/itinerarios-e-tarifas/consulte-origem-e-destino/por-regiao-metropolitana.fss|title=Por Região Metropolitana|publisher=emtu.sp.gov.br}}</ref> Many bus companies operate such routes under permission of [[EMTU]] - [[Empresa Metropolitana de Transportes Urbanos de São Paulo]], a state-owned company.

===Roads===
Itaquaquecetuba is crossed by four [[highway]]s:
* [[SP-56]] Alberto Hinoto Highway{{Efn|name=fn1|The stretch of [[SP-56]] that passes through Itaquaquecetuba is named after '''Alberto''' "'''Bento'''" '''Hinoto''' (August 7, 1970 - March 2, 1996), [[guitarist]] and member of the iconic satirical rock band [[Mamonas Assassinas]], who was born and raised in '''Itaquaquecetuba'''.}}
* [[SP-66]] João Afonso de Souza Castellano Highway
* [[SP-70]] Ayrton Senna Highway
* [[SP-21 (Brazil)|SP-21]] Mário Covas Beltway

==Geography==
=== Climate ===
The climate of the city, and across the [[Greater São Paulo|Metropolitan Region of São Paulo]], is [[Subtropics|Subtropical]]. Summer is largely slightly hot and rainy.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://koeppen-geiger.vu-wien.ac.at/pics/kottek_et_al_2006.gif |title=World Map of the Köppen-Geiger climate classification |publisher=Institute for Veterinary Public Health |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110410035734/http://koeppen-geiger.vu-wien.ac.at/pics/kottek_et_al_2006.gif |archive-date=April 10, 2011 |url-status=live |access-date=5 October 2012}}</ref>

== Media ==
In telecommunications, the city was served by [[:pt:Companhia Telefônica da Borda do Campo|Companhia Telefônica da Borda do Campo]]. In July 1998, this company was acquired by Telefónica, which adopted the [[Vivo (telecommunications company)|Vivo]] brand in 2012. The company is currently an operator of cell phones, fixed lines, internet (fiber optics/4G) and television (satellite and cable).<ref name=":vivo">{{Cite web|url=https://ri.telefonica.com.br/en/the-company/our-history/|title=Our History - Telefônica RI|access-date=2024-06-14|website=Telefônica}}</ref>
