'''Pushkino''' ({{langx|ru|Пу́шкино}}, {{IPA|ru|ˈpuʂkʲɪnə}}) is a [[types of inhabited localities in Russia|city]] and the [[administrative center]] of [[Pushkinsky District, Moscow Oblast|Pushkinsky District]] in [[Moscow Oblast]], [[Russia]], located at the [[confluence]] of the [[Ucha River|Ucha]] and [[Serebryanka River, Moscow Oblast|Serebryanka Rivers]], {{convert|30|km|sp=us}} northeast of [[Moscow]]. Population: {{ru-census2010|102,874|;}} {{ru-census2002|72,425|;}} {{su-census1989|75,847|;}} 57,000 (1974); 30,000 (1959); 21,000 (1939).

==History==
According to one of the historic versions, the village of Pushkino was first documented in 1499 when it belonged to Grigory Morkhinin, also known as "Pushka"—a [[boyar]] whose male-line descendants include [[Alexander Pushkin|Aleksandr Pushkin]].{{citation needed|date=April 2014}} A statue of "Pushka" graces one of the town's main squares. During the following centuries, the neighborhood evolved into a favored summer retreat of [[Russian nobility]]. Pushkino was granted town status in August 1925.{{citation needed|date=April 2014}}

==Administrative and municipal status==
Within the [[subdivisions of Russia#Administrative divisions|framework of administrative divisions]], Pushkino serves as the [[administrative center]] of [[Pushkinsky District, Moscow Oblast|Pushkinsky District]].<ref name="MoscowO_admlist">Resolution #123-PG</ref> As an administrative division, it is incorporated within Pushkinsky District as the '''[[Town of district significance|City]] of Pushkino'''.<ref name="MoscowO_admlist" /> As a [[subdivisions of Russia#Municipal divisions|municipal division]], the City of Pushkino is incorporated within Pushkinsky Municipal District as '''Pushkino Urban Settlement'''.<ref name="MoscowOPushkinskyD_mun">Law #37/2005-OZ</ref>

==Science==
The city hosts the Institute of Forest Science, one of the few in Russia.

==Architecture and culture==
In 1678, a five-domed church of St.&nbsp;Sergius was built at the manor of Komyagino ([http://sobory.ru/pic/03500/03505_20060219_164846.jpg picture]). Another notable estate is [[Muranovo]], where the Russian poets [[Yevgeny Baratynsky]] and [[Fyodor Tyutchev]] used to spend their summers. A [[dacha]] of [[Vladimir Mayakovsky]], who lived in Pushkino during summer seasons of 1920-1928  is also a [[museum]].
