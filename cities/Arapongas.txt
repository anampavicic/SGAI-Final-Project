'''Arapongas '''is a [[Municipalities of Brazil|municipality]] within the state of [[Paraná (state)|Paraná]], [[South Region, Brazil]]. It is located in the Metropolitan area of [[Londrina]], Microregion [[Apucarana]] and Mesoregion North Central Paranaense, being located at a distance of 386&nbsp;km from the state capital, [[Curitiba]]. It occupies an area of approximately 382&nbsp;km{{sup|2}}.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://cidades.ibge.gov.br/painel/painel.php?codmun=410150|title=IBGE {{!}} Cidades {{!}} Infográficos {{!}} Paraná {{!}} Arapongas {{!}} Dados Gerais|website=cidades.ibge.gov.br|access-date=2016-03-03}}</ref> In 2020, its population was estimated by the [[Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics]] in 124,810 inhabitants, the sixteenth-most populous city of Paraná, and second-most populous metropolitan area of Londrina.<ref name="Caderno Estatístico">{{cite web|title=Caderno Estatístico Municipio de Arapongas IPARDES |url=http://www.ipardes.gov.br/cadernos/MontaCadPdf1.php?Municipio=86700 |access-date=2016-10-21 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151208072247/http://www.ipardes.gov.br/cadernos/MontaCadPdf1.php?Municipio=86700 |archive-date=2015-12-08 }}</ref>

== Geography ==

=== Climate ===
The climate is subtropical humid mesothermal, hot summers with rain falling mostly trend{{clarify|date=November 2024}} (average temperature above 22&nbsp;°C), winters with little frequent frosts (average temperature below 18&nbsp;°C) without a defined dry season.

=== Relief ===
* Relief predominantly flat with slight elevations
* Rivers: Ribeirão Pirapó, Lageado Stream, Stream Tres Bocas, Stream of Tight, Basin dos Bandeirantes

== Demography ==
Between 2000 and 2010, the population of Arapongas grew at an annual average rate of 2.00%, while Brazil's population growth was 1.17% in the same period. In this decade, the city's [[urbanization]] rate increased from 95.74% to 97.79%. In 2010, 104,150 people lived in the city.

Between 1991 and 2000, the city's population grew at an annual average rate of 3.16%. At UF, this rate was 1.39%, while Brazil's population growth  was 1.63% in the same period. In the decade, the city's urbanization rate increased from 92.98% to 95.74%.

Between 2000 and 2010, the dependency ratio in the city rose from 47.22% to 41.23% and the rate of aging, from 6.16% to 8.09%.

* Economically active population of Arapongas: 57,754 (55.45%) (IBGE – Demographic Census 2010).<ref>{{Cite web|url = http://atlasbrasil.org.br/2013/pt/perfil_m/arapongas_pr/|title = Caracteristicas Arapongas|access-date = 2015-12-05|archive-date = 2015-12-08|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20151208152131/http://atlasbrasil.org.br/2013/pt/perfil_m/arapongas_pr/|url-status = dead}}</ref>

=== Tropic of Capricorn ===
The [[Tropic of Capricorn]] is a parallel situated south of the equator and her imaginary line crosses the territory of Arapongas, in BR-369, exit to Apucarana. On site there is a composite structure of shoulders and a landmark, where travelers from different parts of Brazil and South America usually stop to register his year here,{{clarify|date=November 2024}} through photos and videos, or out of curiosity.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Notícia – Prefeitura Municipal de Arapongas |url= http://www.arapongas.pr.gov.br/214_noticia_secretaria-de-obras-recupera-porticos-de-entrada-da-cidade/ |website=www.arapongas.pr.gov.br |date= 17 October 2011 |access-date=2015-12-05}}</ref>

=== First school ===
The School Group Caravelas Marquis, former School of Arapongas Group, was founded in 1943, even before the city of Arapongas was created in the government of Mayor Miguel Blasi, of Londrina and the governor of the state Moysés Lupion. It was built thanks to the efforts of the pioneers: Antonio Garcez Novaes, Deodato Antero France and others who organized raising materials, as well as work done by villagers.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url = http://www.apscaravelas.seed.pr.gov.br/modules/conteudo/conteudo.php?conteudo=11|title = Historico Marquês de Caravelas|access-date = 2015-12-05|archive-date = 2015-12-08|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20151208050327/http://www.apscaravelas.seed.pr.gov.br/modules/conteudo/conteudo.php?conteudo=11|url-status = dead}}</ref>

=== Airway ===
Alberto Bertelli Airport, known as [[Arapongas Airport]] ([[International Air Transport Association|IATA]]: APX – [[International Civil Aviation Organization|ICAO]]: SSOG) is a public airport administered by the city of Arapongas, with up to 200 takeoffs and landings per month.

The airport is located 5&nbsp;km northwest from downtown Arapongas. There are currently no scheduled flights operating out of this airport.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Arapongas – SSOG – Infraestrutura e Logística|url = http://www.infraestrutura.pr.gov.br/modules/conteudo/conteudo.php?conteudo=7|website = www.infraestrutura.pr.gov.br|access-date = 2015-12-05|archive-date = 2020-06-14|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20200614022904/http://www.infraestrutura.pr.gov.br/modules/conteudo/conteudo.php?conteudo=7|url-status = dead}}</ref>

=== Museums ===
The Museum of Art and History of Arapongas (MAHRA), located in a historic building, which housed from 1955 to 2010, the municipal administration, the MAHRA tells the story of Arapongas through photos and documents that are part of a rich historical collection that is now accessible to the population. == Tourism ==
=== Parque dos Pássaros ===
The Parque dos Pássaros was created in 2000 by the city of Arapongas. When created, several schools helped with their students to plant trees.

The name was given by a child in a municipal contest schools, integrated to decide the park's name, in which various names were mentioned and Bird Park was chosen.

It offers a variety of entertainment such as: walking inside the park, football field inside the park, skate park, biking, and fishing (when released for public employees).

In 2014, was made major reforms in the park, from paintings, exchange picket fences, lighting, species management, fruit tree planting, maintenance of flora and fauna, cleaning malls, among others.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Notícia – Prefeitura Municipal de Arapongas|url = http://www.arapongas.pr.gov.br/1546_noticia_reformas-em-curso-do-parque-dos-passaros-ja-atrai-o-araponguense|website = www.arapongas.pr.gov.br| date=25 August 2014 |access-date = 2015-12-05}}</ref>

=== Praça Mauá ===
Dr. Julio Square Junqueira was the first to be built in Arapongas. It was named after Dr. Julio Junqueira, the first mayor of Arapongas.

Located in the city center, the square is popularly called Praça Mauá. It also contains a children's playground.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Praça Mauá de Arapongas Paraná.|url = http://www.guiadearapongas.com.br/site/index.php/noticias/item/48-praca-maua-de-arapongas-parana|website = www.guiadearapongas.com.br|access-date = 2015-12-05|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20151208064751/http://www.guiadearapongas.com.br/site/index.php/noticias/item/48-praca-maua-de-arapongas-parana|archive-date = 2015-12-08|url-status = dead}}</ref>

=== Expoara ===
The Arapongas Exhibition Pavilion – Expoara – is the largest complex in southern Brazil in area for large trade shows and events. Headquartered in the city of Arapongas (northern Parana) – the second-largest furniture hub in Brazil – and the BR 369 which is the main highway to the south, the Expoara is in the [[Mercosur]] route, covering a radius of 100&nbsp;km – between the cities of [[Londrina]] and [[Maringá|Maringa]] – a population of 2 million.

Built in 1997, the pavilion was built to accomplish great fair for the furniture sector as Movelpar – Fair of the State of Paraná Furniture – event that is among the three largest trade fairs in the country, and the FIQ International Fair of Quality in Machines, Raw Materials and Accessories for the Furniture Industry, biannual events that attract exhibitors and visitors from the furniture sector nationwide.

Designed on a flat area, Expoara can be divided into various sizes, meeting the need for different formats of events. The pavilion has 44 000 m2, designed on a plot of 150 000 m2, as well as complete infrastructure for events – electricity, hydro, sewer and telephone in all stands – offers a food court with restaurants for 600 people, sound system, equipped auditorium for 300 seats and 5,000 parking spaces.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Expoara – Arapongas – Paraná|url = http://www.expoara.com/site/Internas.php?tela=Historico|website = www.expoara.com|access-date = 2015-12-05|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20151208125229/http://www.expoara.com/site/Internas.php?tela=Historico|archive-date = 2015-12-08|url-status = dead}}</ref>
