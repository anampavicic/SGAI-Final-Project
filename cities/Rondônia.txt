'''Rondônia''' ({{IPA|pt-BR|ʁõˈdoni.ɐ, ʁõˈdonjɐ|pron|Pt-br Rondônia.ogg}}) is one of the 26 [[states of Brazil]], located in the northern subdivision of the country (central-western part). It is bordered by [[Acre (state)|Acre]] in the west, 
[[Amazonas, Brazil|Amazonas]] in the north, [[Mato Grosso]] in the east, and [[Bolivia]] in the south. Rondônia has a population of 1,815,000 as of 2021. It is the fifth least populated state. Its capital and largest city is [[Porto Velho]], bathed by the [[Madeira River]]. The state was named after [[Cândido Rondon]],<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=gvwC4Ojo9fEC|title=The Burning Season: The Murder of Chico Mendes and the Fight for the Amazon Rain Forest|last=Revkin|first=Andrew|date=2004-09-30|publisher=Island Press|isbn=9781610913485|pages=118|language=en}}</ref> who explored the north of the country during the 1910s. The state, which is home to c. 0.7% of the Brazilian population, is responsible for c. 0.3% of the Brazilian [[GDP]].

The state has 52 municipalities and occupies an area of 237,590.547 [[Square kilometre|km<sup>2</sup>]], equivalent to the territory of [[Romania]] and almost five times larger than [[Croatia]]. In addition to this, there are other important cities such as [[Ariquemes]], [[Cacoal]], [[Guajará-Mirim]], [[Ji-Paraná]], [[Rolim de Moura]] and [[Vilhena]].

==Geography==
[[File:Rondônia Köppen.svg|left|thumb|Köppen map for Rondônia]]

Rondonia used to be home to over 200,000&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> of [[tropical rainforest|rainforest]], but has become one of the most [[deforestation|deforested]] places in the Amazon. By 2003 around 70,000&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> of rainforest had been cleared.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://earthobservatory.nasa.gov/Features/WorldOfChange/deforestation.php|title=Amazon Deforestation|work=Earth Observatory|publisher=NASA|access-date=7 October 2012|date=2009-05-27}}</ref>

The area around the [[Guaporé River]] is part of the [[Beni savanna]] ecoregion.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://worldwildlife.org/ecoregions/nt0702|title=Central South America: Northern Bolivia|last=Robin Sears and Robert Langstroth|work=Tropical and Subtropical Grasslands, Savannas and Shrublands|publisher=WWF|access-date=7 October 2012}}</ref>

The [[Samuel Hydroelectric Dam|Samuel Dam]] is located in the state, on the [[Jamari River]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://eros.usgs.gov/samuel-dam-rondônia-brazil|title=Samuel Dam, Rondônia, Brazil|publisher=U.S. Geological Survey|date=13 October 2011|access-date=26 January 2015}}</ref>

=== Colonial Period ===
The Spaniard [[Ñuflo de Chávez]] was the first European explorer to reach the valley of the [[Guaporé River]] between 1541 and 1542, although he only passed through. [[Bandeirantes]] arrived in the region around 1650, with the goal of exploiting the gold and other minerals of the territory. In the same period, [[Society of Jesus|Jesuit]] priests came to the region and founded the first village.
[[File:Forte_do_Príncipe_da_Beira_(RO).jpg|thumb|363x363px|Forte do Príncipe da Beira, 1930.]]
As a consequence of the discovery of gold on the right bank of the Guaporé River, the Portuguese Crown founded the [[Captaincy of Mato Grosso]] in 1748 with {{Interlanguage link|Antonio Rolim de Moura Tavares|pt}} as governor.<ref>{{cite book |author=Antonio Torres Montenegro |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ThgDg9bmlpEC&pg=PA174 |title=História, cultura e sentimento: outras histórias do Brasil |publisher=Editora Universitária UFPE |year=2008 |isbn=978-85-7315-528-0 |pages=174–}}</ref> On March 19, 1752, the governor designated [[Vila Bela da Santíssima Trindade]] as the capital,<ref>David Price: ''Pareci, Cabixi, Nambiquara. A case study in the western classification of native peoples''. In: ''Journal de la Société des Américanistes'' vol.69, 1983, pp.129-148, doi : 10.3406/jsa.1983.2228 ([http://www.persee.fr/web/revues/home/prescript/article/jsa_0037-9174_1983_num_69_1_2228 online])</ref> from where he commanded the border demarcation following the [[Treaty of Madrid (13 January 1750)|Treaty of Madrid (1750)]]. In 1753, he installed a surveillance post in the village of Santa Rosa Velha, built by the Spanish on the right bank of the Guaporé, and thus in Brazilian lands.<ref>{{cite book |author1=Marcelo Vianna |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ChUCCwAAQBAJ&pg=PA1989 |title=O historiador e as novas tecnologias - reunião de artigos do II Encontro de Pesquisas Históricas - PUCRS: Evento acadêmico - História - Encontro - Pós-graduação - Graduação – PUCRS |author2=Cristiano Enrique de Brum |author3=Débora Soares Karpowicz |publisher=Memorial do Ministério Público do Rio Grande do Sul |isbn=978-85-88802-22-3 |pages=1989–}}</ref> In 1759, the Spanish governor of [[Santa Cruz de la Sierra]] requested that the post be evacuated. Instead, Rolim de Moura built a fort to replace it, which became known as the {{Interlanguage link|Forte de Bragança|lt=Presídio de Nossa Senhora da Conceição|pt}}. Due to the climate and the incursions of the Spanish, the Presidio was soon in ruins. It was rebuilt in 1769 by Governor [[Luís Pinto de Sousa Coutinho, 1st Viscount of Balsemão|Luís Pinto de Sousa Coutinho]], and renamed as Forte de Bragança. Ruined again, in 1776 the [[Forte Príncipe da Beira]] was built in its place. In 1772, {{Interlanguage link|Francisco de Melo Palheta|pt}} led an expedition from [[Belém]] which reached the [[Madeira River]], the [[Mamoré River]] and the Guaporé River, reaching Santa Cruz de la Sierra.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Pontes Pinto |first1=Emanuel |title=VIAGEM DE DESCOBRIMENTO AO RIO MADEIRA E SUAS VERTENTES POR FRANCISCO DE MELO PALHETA |url=http://www.periodicos.unir.br/index.php/veredasamazonicas/article/view/521 |accessdate=13 April 2019 |website=periódicos.unir.br |publisher=Universidade Federal de Rondônia}}</ref> The decline of mining and the proclamation of the [[First Brazilian Republic]] caused the region to lose its economic importance until the end of the nineteenth century, when the exploitation of rubber entered its peak.

=== Postcolonial history ===
In April 1878, following to the [[Treaty of Ayacucho]], the border between Bolivia and Brazil was mapped by cartographic teams and agreed in 1879. The [[Treaty of Petrópolis]] in 1903 led to the construction of the [[Madeira-Mamoré Railroad]], leading to increased settlement.<ref>{{cite book |last1=Lome |first1=Herbert M. |url=https://archive.org/stream/worldswork20gard#page/n3/mode/2up |title=An American Sanitary Triumph in Brazil |date=1910 |publisher=Doubleday, Page & Co. |location=New York |accessdate=13 April 2019}}</ref>

Decree-Law No. 5812 (13 of September 1943) established the {{Interlanguage link|Federal Territory of Guaporé|pt|Território Federal do Guaporé}} was created from parts of the states of [[Amazonas (Brazilian state)|Amazonas]] and [[Mato Grosso]].<ref>{{cite web |title=DECRETO-LEI Nº 5.812, DE 13 DE SETEMBRO DE 1943. |url=http://www.planalto.gov.br/ccivil_03/decreto-lei/1937-1946/Del5812.htm |accessdate=13 April 2019 |website=planalto.gov.br |publisher=Government of Brazil}}</ref> By the law of February 17 1956, the region became known as the Federal Territory of Rondônia, in honor of Marshal [[Cândido Rondon]].<ref>{{cite book |author1=Oliver Marshall |url=https://archive.org/details/isbn_9781848361898 |title=The Rough Guide to Brazil |author2=David Cleary |author3=Dilwyn Jenkins |date=2009 |publisher=Rough Guides Limited |isbn=978-1-84836-189-8 |pages=[https://archive.org/details/isbn_9781848361898/page/437 437]– |url-access=registration}}</ref> The exploitation of [[brazil nuts]] and rubber was the main economic activity until the discovery of [[cassiterite]] deposits, which accelerated the development and settlement of the region. This development led to the territory achieving the status of a state in 1982, with 13 constituent municipalities, including the capital, [[Porto Velho]]. These are: [[Guajará-Mirim]], [[Ji-Paraná]], [[Vilhena]], [[Ariquemes]], [[Jaru, Rondônia|Jaru]], [[Pimenta Bueno]], [[Colorado do Oeste]], [[Cacoal]], [[Ouro Preto do Oeste]], [[Presidente Médici, Rondônia|Presidente Médici]], [[Espigão d'Oeste]] and [[Costa Marques]].

== Demographics ==
{{Historical populations|21=1950|22=36935|23=1960|24=70783|25=1970|26=116620|27=1980|28=503125|29=1991|30=1130874|31=2000|32=1380952|33=2010|34=1562409|35=2022|36=1581196|footnote=Source:<ref name="census2022">{{Cite web|url=https://censo2022.ibge.gov.br/panorama/|title=2022 Census Overview|lang=pt}}</ref>}}It is the third most populous state in the North Region with 1,815,278 inhabitants, according to an estimate by [[Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics|IBGE]] for 2021, being surpassed only by [[Pará]] and [[Amazonas (Brazilian state)|Amazonas]]. The population density was 6.6 inhabitants/km<sup>2</sup>.
[[Urbanization]]: 66.8% (2004); [[Population growth]]: 2.2% (1991-2000); [[House]]s: 430,747 (2005).<ref>Source: PNAD.</ref> Four of its municipalities have a population above 100,000 inhabitants, these being Porto Velho, Ji-Paraná, Ariquemes and Vilhena.

The [[2022 Brazilian census|2022 census]] revealed the following numbers: 936,708 [[Brown people#Pardos in Brazil|Brown]] ([[Multiracial#Brazil|Multiracial]]) people (59.2%), 486,123 [[White Brazilian|White]] people (30.7%), 136,793 [[Afro-Brazilian|Black]] people (8.7%), 17,278 [[Indigenous peoples in Brazil|Amerindian]] people (1.1%), 4,257 [[Asian Brazilian|Asian]] people (0.3%).<ref>{{cite web |title=Censo 2022 - Panorama |url=https://censo2022.ibge.gov.br/panorama/}}</ref>

The population of Rondônia is one of the most diverse in [[Brazil]], composed of [[Human migration|migrants]] from all regions of the country, among whom stand out the [[Paraná (state)|Paraná]], [[São Paulo]], [[Minas Gerais]], and [[Rio Grande do Sul]], who settled in the capital, still preserving the strong Amazonian traits of the native population in cities bathed by large rivers, especially in Porto Velho and Guajará-Mirim, the two oldest cities in the state.

===Religion===
47.6% Roman Catholic in the [[Archdiocese of Porto Velho]] (1925 as a Territorial Prelature) with 30 parishes under Archbishop Roque Paloschi (2015), and the two suffragan dioceses of [[Roman Catholic Diocese of Guajará-Mirim|Guajará-Mirim]] (1929 as a Territorial Prelature) with 13 parishes under Bishop Benedito Araújo (2011), and [[Roman Catholic Diocese of Ji-Paraná|Ji-Paraná]] (1978 as the Territorial Prelature of Vila Rondônia) with 24 parishes under Bishop Norbert Hans Christoph Foerster (2020); 33.8% Protestant, 0.6% Spiritism, 3.7% other religion, 14.3% non-religious.<ref>{{cite web|access-date=12 July 2012|author=Meridional FM|title=População evangélica em Rondônia cresceu 6,6% em 10 anos|url=http://redemeridional.liveradio.com.br/noticias/populacao-evangelica-em-rondonia-cresceu-66-em-10-anos/}}{{dead link|{{subst:DATA}}|date=January 2024}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://catholic-hierarchy.org/diocese/dpove.html | title=Porto Velho (Archdiocese) &#91;Catholic-Hierarchy&#93; }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://catholic-hierarchy.org/diocese/dgumi.html | title=Guajará-Mirim (Diocese) &#91;Catholic-Hierarchy&#93; }}</ref> <ref>{{cite web | url=https://catholic-hierarchy.org/diocese/djipa.html | title=Ji-Paraná (Diocese) &#91;Catholic-Hierarchy&#93; }}</ref>

===Indigenous peoples===
[[Image:Indigenous Territories in Rondônia.svg|thumb|upright=1.5|right|[[Indigenous territory (Brazil)|Indigenous Territories]] in Rondônia.]]
{{As of|2011}} there were 21 [[Indigenous territory (Brazil)|Indigenous Territories]] in Rondônia, with two more in process of being demarcated.<ref>{{cite web|title=Terras Indígenas: Pesquisa por Estado: Rondônia|url=http://pib.socioambiental.org/caracterizacao.php?uf=11|work=Povos Indígenas no Brasil|publisher=Instituto Socioambiental (ISA)|access-date=24 March 2011|language=pt}}</ref> The largest of these, the [[Terra Indigena Uru-Eu-Uaw-Uaw|Uru-Eu-Wau-Wau Indigenous Territory]], covers over 1.8 million hectares.<ref>{{cite web|title=Caracterização Terra Indígena Uru-Eu-Wau-Wau|url=http://pib.socioambiental.org/caracterizacao.php?id_arp=3891|work=Povos Indígenas no Brasil|publisher=Instituo Socioambiental (ISA)|access-date=24 March 2011|language=pt}}</ref> Another, the [[Rio Omerê Indigenous Territory]], is home to the [[Kanoê]] and [[Akuntsu]] people. Both tribes were the victims of massacres by cattle ranchers in the 1970s and 1980s and currently number just four and five individuals respectively.<ref>{{cite web|title=Introduction > Akuntsu|url=http://pib.socioambiental.org/en/povo/akuntsu|work=Povos Indígenas no Brasil|access-date=8 March 2011|author=Instituto Socioambiental (ISA)}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Introduction > Kanoê|url=http://pib.socioambiental.org/en/povo/kanoe|work=Povos Indígenas no Brasil|access-date=8 March 2011|author=Instituto Socioambiental (ISA)}}</ref>

==Economy==
[[File:Sojadaregiao.jpg|thumb|Soybeans in [[Ji-Paraná]]]]
[[File:Plantacao de arroz.jpg|thumb|Rice in [[Presidente Médici, Rondônia|Presidente Médici]]]]
The economy of the state of Rondônia has, as main activities, agriculture, livestock, food industry and vegetal and mineral extraction. In 2016, the state's GDP reached R$ 39.451 billion. Its export basket is mainly composed of frozen beef (43.43%), soy (32.77%), raw tin (7.08%), sawn wood (2.36%) and edible giblets (2.02%).<ref>[https://agenciadenoticias.ibge.gov.br/agencia-sala-de-imprensa/2013-agencia-de-noticias/releases/23038-contas-regionais-2016-entre-as-27-unidades-da-federacao-somente-roraima-teve-crescimento-do-pib Roraima PIB]</ref><ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20140116101837/http://dataviva.info/apps/builder/tree_map/secex/ro/all/all/hs/?depth=hs_6&value_var=val_usd&color_var=color&controls=true&year=2012 Rondonia Exports]</ref>

Beginning in the 1970s, the state attracted farmers from the south-central part of the country, stimulated by the federal government's colonization projects and the availability of cheap and fertile land. The development of agricultural activities has transformed the area into one of the main agricultural frontiers in the country and one of the most prosperous and productive regions in northern Brazil. The state stands out in the production of [[coffee]] (largest producer in the North and 5th largest in Brazil), [[Cocoa bean|cocoa]] (2nd largest producer in the North and 3rd largest in Brazil), [[beans]] (2nd largest producer in the North), [[maize]] (2nd largest producer in the North region), [[soybean]] (3rd largest producer in the North region), [[rice]] (3rd largest producer in the North region) and [[cassava]] (4th largest producer in the North region). Despite the large volume of production and the small territory by the region's standards (7 times smaller than Amazonas and 6 times smaller than Pará), Rondônia still has more than 60% of its territory fully preserved.

In [[coffee]] production, Rondônia was, in 2019, the 5th largest producer in the country, being the 2nd largest producer of [[Coffea canephora]], getting a total of 2.3 million bags of 60 kg of coffee (near 138 thousand tons) this year.<ref>[http://www.rondonia.ro.gov.br/producao-de-cafe-conilon-avanca-e-pode-ter-safra-recorde-em-2019-aquecendo-a-economia-com-a-geracao-de-emprego-e-renda/ Produção de café conilon avança e pode ter safra recorde em 2019 gerando emprego e renda]</ref>

In [[soy]], in the 2019 Brazilian harvest, Rondônia harvested 1.2 million tons, 3rd in the North Region.<ref>[http://www.rondonia.ro.gov.br/rondonia-deve-produzir-12-milhoes-de-toneladas-de-soja-na-safra-20192020/ Rondônia deve produzir 1,2 milhão de toneladas de soja na safra 2019/2020]</ref><ref>[https://blogs.canalrural.com.br/embrapasoja/2019/07/10/soja-e-ouro-do-estado-do-tocantins/ Soja é ouro no estado do Tocantins]</ref><ref>[https://www.embrapa.br/busca-de-noticias/-/noticia/46487476/especialistas-e-produtores-debatem-sobre-a-expansao-da-soja-no-para Especialistas e produtores debatem sobre a expansão da soja no Pará]</ref>

In 2019, the state produced 805 thousand tons of [[maize]], second largest production in the northern region, losing only to Tocantins.<ref>[https://g1.globo.com/ro/rondonia/noticia/2019/07/11/colheita-do-milho-safrinha-cresce-20percent-e-conab-estima-producao-de-805-mil-toneladas-em-rondonia.ghtml Colheita do milho safrinha cresce 20% e Conab estima produção de 805 mil toneladas em Rondônia]</ref>

In [[cassava]] production, Brazil produced a total of 17.6 million tons in 2018. Rondônia was the 11th largest producer in the country, with 583 thousand tons.<ref>[http://www.cnpmf.embrapa.br/Base_de_Dados/index_pdf/dados/brasil/mandioca/b1_mandioca.pdf Produção brasileira de mandioca em 2018]</ref>

In 2018, Rondônia produced 124 thousand tons of [[rice]].<ref>[https://www.rondoniagora.com/agronegocio/safra-de-arroz-tem-aumento-de-produtividade-em-rondonia Safra de arroz tem aumento de produtividade em Rondônia]</ref>

In the production of [[Cocoa bean|cocoa]], Pará has been competing with [[Bahia]] for the leadership of Brazilian production. In 2019, Pará harvested 135 thousand tons of cocoa, and Bahians harvested 130 thousand tons. Rondônia is the 3rd largest cocoa producer in the country, with 18 thousand tons harvested in 2017.<ref>[https://g1.globo.com/economia/agronegocios/globo-rural/noticia/2019/11/03/lideranca-na-producao-brasileira-de-cacau-volta-para-casa-no-para-com-a-uniao-de-agricultores.ghtml Pará retoma liderança na produção brasileira de cacau, com a união de agricultores]</ref><ref>[https://www.rondoniagora.com/agronegocio/rondonia-e-o-terceiro-maior-produtor-de-cacau-do-brasil Rondônia é o terceiro maior produtor de cacau do Brasil]</ref>

In 2017, the state had a [[cattle]] herd of 14,098,031 head of cattle (73,37% for beef and the rest for dairy), second largest herd in the North, second only to Pará, being the 6th largest in the country, 5th in meat exports and 8th in [[milk]] production.<ref>[http://www.rondonia.ro.gov.br/rebanho-bovino-ultrapassa-14-milhoes-de-cabecas-em-rondonia/ Rebanho bovino ultrapassa 14 milhões de cabeças em Rondônia]</ref> The state's milk production in 2018 was around 800 million liters, the largest producer in the North.<ref>[http://www.rondonia.ro.gov.br/producao-de-leite-em-rondonia-resulta-em-cerca-de-800-milhoes-de-litros-por-ano-e-e-um-dos-destaques-da-feira/ Produção de leite em Rondônia resulta em cerca de 800 milhões de litros por ano e é um dos destaques da feira]</ref>

In 2017, Rondônia had 0.62% of the national mineral participation (8th place in the country). Rondônia had production of [[tin]] (10,9 thousand tons at a value of R$ 333 million), [[gold]] (1 ton at a value of R$ 125 million), [[niobium]] (in the form of columbita-tantalita) (3.5 thousand tons at R$ 24 million), and [[zinc]] in gross form (26 thousand tons at R$ 27 million)<ref>[http://www.anm.gov.br/dnpm/publicacoes/serie-estatisticas-e-economia-mineral/anuario-mineral/anuario-mineral-brasileiro/amb_2018_ano_base_2017 Anuário Mineral Brasileiro 2018]</ref> In addition, in gemstones, the state has some production of [[garnet]].<ref>[http://www.cprm.gov.br/publique/Redes-Institucionais/Rede-de-Bibliotecas---Rede-Ametista/Algumas-Gemas-Classicas-1104.html Algumas Gemas Clássicas]</ref>

In [[Industrial sector|industry]], Rondônia had an industrial GDP of R$ 8.2 billion in 2017, equivalent to 0.7% of the national industry. It employs 49,944 workers in the industry. The main industrial sectors are: Industrial Services of Public Utility, such as Electricity and Water (54.4%), Construction (19.2%), Food (17.6%), Wood (1.8%) and Non-metallic minerals (1.2%). These 5 sectors concentrate 94.2% of the state's industry.<ref> [http://perfildaindustria.portaldaindustria.com.br/estado/ro Industry Profile of Rondonia] </ref>

[[Governador Jorge Teixeira de Oliveira International Airport]] is located in the state capital of Porto Velho.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theairdb.com/airport/PVH.html|title=Porto Velho, Belmonte (PVH) information|publisher=theAirDB|access-date=1 January 2013}}</ref>

Domestic airports at [[Ji-Paraná Airport|Ji-Paraná]], [[Ariquemes Airport|Ariquemes]], [[Pimenta Bueno Airport|Pimenta Bueno]], [[Guajará-Mirim]], [[Vilhena Airport|Vilhena]] and Principe da Beira.

Waterways: 

Pôrto Velho is an Amazon River port.

The state of Rondônia has 24,000 kilometers of highways, of which only 7% are paved. The [[BR-364 (Brazil highway)|BR-364]], fully paved in the Rondônia section, crosses the state from the border with Mato Grosso to the border with Acre. It is the main route for the outflow of grain production (especially [[Soybean|soy]]) from the south of Rondônia and the west of Mato Grosso to the city of Porto Velho, where the grain port is located. A bridge is being built over the Madeira River (the first over this river), which aims to consolidate road transport between Brazil and Peru.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Ficha del Proyecto |url=http://www.iirsa.org/proyectos/detalle_proyecto.aspx?h=1346&x=9&idioma=ES |access-date=2023-05-17 |website=www.iirsa.org}}</ref>

== Education ==
The quality of Education in Rondônia is considered the fourteenth best in the country, compared to other Brazilian states. In the list of Brazilian states by [[Human Development Index|HDI]], with data from 2010, the “Education” factor reached an index of 0.557, an increase of more than 67% compared to 2000, when the state reached only 0.345.

According to the Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics ([[Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics|IBGE]]), in 2021 there were 244,815 enrollments in primary education in Rondônia.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Rondônia {{!}} Cities and States {{!}} IBGE |url=https://www.ibge.gov.br/en/cities-and-states/ro.html |access-date=2023-05-17 |website=www.ibge.gov.br}}</ref>
