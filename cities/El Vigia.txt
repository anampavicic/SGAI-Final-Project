The '''Alberto Adriani Municipality''' ({{IPA|es|alˈβeɾto aˈðɾjani}}) (better known as '''El Vigia''') is one of the 23 [[Municipalities of Venezuela|municipalities]] ({{lang|es|[[municipio]]s}}) that makes up the [[Venezuela]]n state of [[Mérida (state)|Mérida]] and, according to a 2007 population estimate by the National Institute of Statistics of Venezuela, the municipality has a population of 128,222.{{Update inline|date=May 2021}}<ref>http://www.ine.gob.ve/sintesisestadistica/estados/merida/cuadros/Poblacion6.xls {{dead link|date=June 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>  The town of El Vigía is the [[County seat|shire town]] of the Alberto Adriani Municipality.<ref name=autogenerated1>http://www.ine.gob.ve/secciones/division/Merida.zip {{dead link|date=June 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

==Climate==
El Vigia experiences a [[tropical rainforest climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification|Köppen]]: ''Af'')<ref>{{Cite journal |date=23 October 2023 |title=Table 1 Overview of the Köppen-Geiger climate classes including the defining criteria. |url=https://www.nature.com/articles/s41597-023-02549-6/tables/1 |journal=Nature: Scientific Data |language=en}}</ref> characterized by consistently hot temperatures and abundant rainfall throughout the year. The total annual precipitation is 2089.5 millimeters (82.27 inches). El Vigia experiences precipitation peaks from April to May and October to November.

==Demographics==
The Alberto Adriani Municipality, according to a 2007 population estimate{{Update inline|date=May 2021}} by the National Institute of Statistics of Venezuela, has a population of 128,222 (up from 109,215 in 2000).  This amounts to 15.2% of the state's population.<ref>http://www.ine.gob.ve/sintesisestadistica/estados/merida/cuadros/Poblacion5.xls {{dead link|date=June 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>  The municipality's population density is {{convert|187.7|PD/sqkm}}.<ref>http://www.ine.gob.ve/sintesisestadistica/estados/merida/cuadros/Poblacion4.xls {{dead link|date=June 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

==Government==
The mayor of the Alberto Adriani Municipality is Luis Guillermo Rojas Mendoza, re-elected on October 31, 2004, with 59% of the vote.{{Update inline|date=May 2021}}<ref>http://www.cne.gob.ve/regionales2004/12/alcaldedis=0&edo=12&mun=1&par=0&cen=0&mesa=0&cua=0.html {{Bare URL inline|date=August 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cne.gob.ve/estadisticas/e016.pdf |title=Archived copy |access-date=2008-03-15 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20061002063906/http://www.cne.gob.ve/estadisticas/e016.pdf |archive-date=2006-10-02 }}</ref>  The municipality is divided into seven [[parish]]es; Presidente Betancourt, Presidente Páez, Presidente Rómulo Gallegos, Gabriel Picón González, Héctor Amable Mora, José Nucete Sardi, and Pulido Méndez.<ref name=autogenerated1 />

==Transportation==
[[Juan Pablo Pérez Alfonzo Airport]] serves the town, with six commercial airlines flying to it.
