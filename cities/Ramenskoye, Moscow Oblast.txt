'''Ramenskoye''' ({{langx|ru|Ра́менское}}, {{IPA|ru|ˈramʲɪnskəjə}}) is a [[types of inhabited localities in Russia|town]] and the [[administrative center]] of [[Ramensky District]] in [[Moscow Oblast]], [[Russia]], located {{convert|46|km|sp=us}} southeast of [[Moscow]].

==Etymology==
The town's name derives from an [[Old East Slavic|Old Slavonic]] word "{{lang|ru|раменье}}" (''ramenye''), meaning "on the edge of forest".
==Population==
Population: {{ru-census2021|114,537|;}} {{ru-census2010|96,317|;}} {{ru-census2002|82,074|;}} {{su-census1989|87,666|;}} 69,000 (1974); 28,000 (1939).

==History==
[[File:Вид на Раменскую фабрику 2020-04 - 50111569847.jpg|thumb|Main building of Ramenskoye textile factory]]
The area where the town now stands was first mentioned in 1328. A stone church was built here by Count Platon Musin-Pushkin in 1725–1730. In the 1770s, the ''[[village#Russia|selo]]'' of '''Novo-Troitskoye''' ({{langx|ru|Ново-Троицкое}}) was established here; its name later changed to Ramenskoye.{{citation needed|date=April 2014}} In 1831, a textile factory was founded in Ramenskoye and by the second half of the 19th century, it had grown to be one of the largest enterprises in the [[Russian Empire]]. On March&nbsp;15, 1926, Ramenskoye was granted town status.{{citation needed|date=April 2014}}

=== Russo-Ukrainian War ===
On the night of 9 September 2024, during a large-scale [[Attacks in Russia during the Russian invasion of Ukraine|Ukrainian drone attack on western Russia]]. a [[Ukraine|Ukrainian]] drone crashed into an apartment building in Ramenskoye, killing 1 and injuring 3 according to Moscow Oblast governor [[Andrey Vorobyov]].<ref>{{Cite news |last=Harding |first=Luke |date=2024-09-10 |title=Ukrainian drone attacks kill one and force airport closures in Moscow |url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/article/2024/sep/10/ukrainian-drone-attacks-residential-building-airports-moscow |access-date=2024-09-10 |work=The Guardian |language=en-GB |issn=0261-3077}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://understandingwar.org/backgrounder/russian-offensive-campaign-assessment-september-10-2024 |title=Russian Offensive Campaign Assessment, September 10, 2024 |publisher=[[Institute for the Study of War|ISW]] |date=2024-09-10 |access-date=2024-09-11 |website=understandingwar.org |language=en-us |author=Nicole Wolkov |author2=Grace Mappes |author3=Angelica Evans |author4=Haley Zehrung |author5=Karolina Hird |quote=Footage published on September 10 reportedly shows the aftermath of a drone strike in Ramenskoye, Moscow Oblast, and Ramenskoye Urban Raion Acting Head Eduard Malyshev claimed that Ukrainian drones damaged two buildings in the raion. }}</ref>
==Administrative and municipal status==
Within the [[subdivisions of Russia#Administrative divisions|framework of administrative divisions]], Ramenskoye serves as the [[administrative center]] of [[Ramensky District]].<ref name="MoscowO_admlist">Resolution #123-PG</ref> As an administrative division, it is, together with the [[village#Russia|village]] of Dergayevo, incorporated within Ramensky District as the '''[[Town of district significance|Town]] of Ramenskoye'''.<ref name="MoscowO_admlist" /> As a [[subdivisions of Russia#Municipal divisions|municipal division]], the Town of Ramenskoye is incorporated within Ramensky Municipal District as '''Ramenskoye Urban Settlement'''.<ref name="MoscowORamenskyD_mun">Law #55/2005-OZ</ref>
