'''Mubi''' is a town in Northern Senatorial District of [[Adamawa State]], northeast [[Nigeria]]. It is subdivided into two Mubi north and Mubi south.

==Demographics==
The major tribes of the town are Fali, Gude, Mafa, Kamwe, Margi and [[Mundang people|Mundang]] (Godo-godo).

==Education==
Mubi has three higher institutions of learning: [[Federal Polytechnic, Mubi]],<ref>[http://federalpolytechnicmubi.edu.ng/ The Federal Polytechnic Mubi]</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://federalpolytechnicmubi.edu.ng/|title=The Federal Polytehcnic Mubi, Adamawa State}}</ref> College of Health Technology (Mubi Campus), and [[Adamawa State University]], Mubi.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20160303185907/http://www.adamawastateuni.net/Hist.htm History of Adamawa State University]</ref>

==Boko Haram takeover==
Some media outlets said that the city was renamed to ''Madinatul [[Islam]]'', meaning ''city of Islam'' by [[jihadist]] group [[Boko Haram]] in October 2014 when they captured the town under their control.{{citation needed|date=March 2020}}

In November 2014, the [[Nigerian Army]] took back control of Mubi from Boko Haram. Adamawa State governor [[Bala James Ngilari]] said [[government of Nigeria|government]] forces had recaptured the town from Boko Haram. Some reports said Boko Haram had withdrawn from the town after [[looting]] peoples goods and money to their hideout from the town, rather than as the result of direct fighting. However, Bala Ngilari's chief of staff, [[Chibudo Babbi]], told the [[BBC Hausa]] Service that remnants of the group were flushed out of Mubi by the military.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-africa-30043867|title=Nigeria army 'retakes Mubi from Boko Haram'|publisher=BBC News|accessdate=13 November 2014}}</ref>

==Massacres==
A [[terrorist]] [[Federal Polytechnic, Mubi attack|attack]] on 2 October 2012 when suspected members of Boko Haram entered the town at around 10pm and killed many people.<ref name=ALF>{{Citation

| title= Nigeria: Gunmen Kill 46 Students in Mubi
| year = 2012

| accessdate = 4 October 2012
}}</ref> The large majority of the victims were students from the three educational institutions ([[Federal Polytechnic, Mubi|Federal Polytechnic Mubi]], School Of Health Technology and [[Adamawa State University]]) based in the town. The death toll was put at 25 initially, however, the number is said to be closer to 45.<ref name=ALF />

A [[2014 Mubi bombing|bombing]] occurred in Mubi on 1 June 2014.

On 21 November 2017, a [[suicide attack|suicide]] [[2017 Mubi bombing|bomb]]ing at a [[mosque]] during [[Morning Prayer (Islam)|morning prayers]] killed an estimate 25-50 people. Boko Haram is the suspected perpetrator.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2017/nov/21/nigeria-mosque-attack-teenage-suicide-bomber-kills-at-least-50|title=Nigeria mosque attack: suicide bomber kills dozens|first=Ruth|last=Maclean|publisher=[[The Guardian]]|date=21 November 2017|accessdate=21 November 2017}}</ref>

Suicide bombings [[2018 Mubi suicide bombings|occurred]] at a mosque and market in Mubi in 2018.

==Cattle market ==
Mubi is the home of the state famous international cattle market popularly known as kasuwan shanu or Kasuwan tike situated at the center of the town, large numbers of cattle and sheep are bought by customers mainly from north eastern Nigeria and being distributed to different parts of southern Nigeria by trailers and trucks.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.osundefender.org/?tag=kashuwar-sanu-cattle-market-in-the-town-of-mubi|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304105059/http://www.osundefender.org/?tag=kashuwar-sanu-cattle-market-in-the-town-of-mubi|url-status=dead|archive-date=2016-03-04|title=Kashuwar Sanu (cattle market) in the town of Mubi – OSUN DEFENDER}}</ref>
