'''Novoshakhtinsk''' ({{langx|ru|Новоша́хтинск}}) is a [[mining]] [[types of inhabited localities in Russia|city]] in [[Rostov Oblast]], [[Russia]], within 20 kilometres of the [[Dovzhansky (border checkpoint)|Dovzhansky border crossing]] to [[Ukraine]].

==History==
It was established in 1939.{{citation needed|date=May 2010}}

The population was: {{ru-census|p2010=111,075|p2002=101,131|p1989=107,772}}

=== Russo-Ukrainian War ===
During the [[2022 Russian Invasion of Ukraine]], an oil refinery in the city was damaged and set ablaze in a targeted strike by a Ukrainian [[Mugin-5]] Pro drone. Russian state media outlet [[TASS]] reported no casualties, and the resulting fire was extinguished.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/fire-russias-novoshakhtinsk-oil-plant-erupts-after-ukrainian-drone-flight-tass-2022-06-22/ | title=Fire at Russia's Novoshakhtinsk oil plant erupts after Ukrainian drone flight - TASS | newspaper=Reuters | date=June 22, 2022 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://tass.com/emergencies/1469935/amp | title=Drones that attacked Russian oil refinery could be produced in Ukraine — local authorities }}</ref> An unverified video of the drone strike recorded by Russian oil refinery workers began circulating on social media, which pro-Ukrainian activists used to mock the Russian air defense systems that failed to stop the drones. The video was also posted to the [[YouTube]] channel run by Ukraine's land forces, who referred to the blaze as a “holy fire.”<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2022-06-22/russia-blames-oil-refinery-blaze-on-ukrainian-drone-strike | title=Russia Blames Oil Refinery Blaze on Ukrainian Drone Strike | newspaper=Bloomberg.com | date=June 22, 2022 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.newsweek.com/russian-oil-refinery-ablaze-reported-hit-ukrainian-drone-rostov-1717956?amp=1 | title=Russian oil refinery ablaze after reported hit from Ukrainian drone | website=[[Newsweek]] | date=June 22, 2022 }}</ref>

==Administrative and municipal status==
Within the [[subdivisions of Russia#Administrative divisions|framework of administrative divisions]], it is incorporated as '''Novoshakhtinsk Urban Okrug'''—an administrative unit with the status equal to that of the [[administrative divisions of Rostov Oblast|districts]].<ref name="Ref133">Law #340-ZS</ref> As a [[subdivisions of Russia#Municipal divisions|municipal division]], this administrative unit also has [[City of federal subject significance|urban okrug]] status.<ref name="Ref870">Law #216-ZS</ref>
