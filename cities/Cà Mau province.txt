'''Cà Mau''' is a [[Provinces of Vietnam|province]] of [[Vietnam]], named after [[Cà Mau|its capital city]]. It is located in the [[Mekong Delta]] of southern Vietnam, and is the southernmost of Vietnam's 63 provinces. It is bordered to the north by [[Kiên Giang province|Kiên Giang]] and [[Bạc Liêu province]]s, to the west by the [[Gulf of Thailand]], and to the south and east by the [[South China Sea]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.camau.gov.vn/index.php?o=modules&n=info&f=intro&root=1&idmenu=11&id=89 |title=Introduction: Geographical Position |access-date=2008-05-31 |last=CA MAU PEOPLE'S COMMITTEE |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090120164850/http://www.camau.gov.vn/index.php?o=modules&n=info&f=intro&root=1&idmenu=11&id=89 |archive-date=2009-01-20 }}</ref>

==Economy==
Being surrounded by water on three sides, fishing is an important industry in Cà Mau province. An extensive network of canals also supports a strong agricultural sector, as well as providing a popular means of transport. The U Minh biosphere reserve and Mũi Cà Mau, the southernmost point of Vietnam, serve also as important tourist destinations. The [[Mũi Cà Mau National Park]] is located at Mũi Cà Mau.

==Typhoon Linda (1997)==
In November 1997, the [[Cà Mau Peninsula]] was struck by [[Tropical Storm Linda (1997)|Typhoon Linda (Openg)]]. Thousands of people were killed, and an estimated 200,000 homes were destroyed, along with much of the Cà Mau fishing fleet.<ref>{{cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=7WvOSgCHrOoC&dq=%22Ca+Mau%22&pg=RA1-PA164 |title=The Rough Guide to Vietnam |access-date=2008-06-02 |last=Dodd |first=Jan |year=2003 |publisher=Rough Guides|isbn=9781843530954 |display-authors=etal}}</ref>
