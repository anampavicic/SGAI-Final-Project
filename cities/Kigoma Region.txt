'''Kigoma Region''' (''Mkoa wa Kigoma'' in [[Swahili language|Swahili]]) is one of [[Tanzania]]'s 31 administrative [[Regions of Tanzania|regions]], with the city of  [[Kigoma]] as the reigonal capital. Kigoma Region borders [[Kagera Region]], [[Geita Region]], [[Katavi Region]], [[Tabora Region]], [[DRC]] and [[Burundi]] According to the 2012 national census, the region had a population of 2,127,930, which was higher than the pre-census projection of 1,971,332.<ref name="2012 Census">[http://www.nbs.go.tz/sensa/PDF/Census%20General%20Report%20-%2029%20March%202013_Combined_Final%20for%20Printing.pdf Population Distribution by Administrative Units, United Republic of Tanzania, 2013] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130612082906/http://www.nbs.go.tz/sensa/PDF/Census%20General%20Report%20-%2029%20March%202013_Combined_Final%20for%20Printing.pdf |date=2013-06-12 }}</ref>{{rp|page 2}} For 2002–2012, the region's 2.4 percent average annual population growth rate was tied for the fourteenth highest in the country.<ref name="2012 Census"/>{{rp|page 4}} It was also the sixteenth most densely populated region with 57 people per square kilometer.<ref name="2012 Census"/>{{rp|page 6}} With a size of {{convert|45066|km2|mi2}},<ref>[http://www.nbs.go.tz/takwimu/references/Abstract2011.zip Statistical Abstract 2011, Tanzania National Bureau of Statistics] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131105105231/http://www.nbs.go.tz/takwimu/references/Abstract2011.zip |date=2013-11-05 }}</ref> the region is slightly smaller than [[Estonia]] ({{convert|45227|km2|mi2}}).

==Geography==
[[File:Malagarasi River.jpg|thumb|left|150px|Most of Kigoma Region showing the [[Malagarasi River]].]]

Kigoma Region resides in the northwestern corner of Tanzania, on the eastern shore of [[Lake Tanganyika]]. The region lies at about 5° south and 30° east of Greenwich and is bordered to the north by both [[Burundi]] and the [[Kagera Region]]. To the east, it is bordered by the [[Geita Region]] and [[Tabora Region|Tabora]] Regions, to the south by the [[Katavi Region]], and to the west by [[Lake Tanganyika]], which forms a border with the [[Democratic Republic of the Congo]].<ref name="Economic-profile">{{Cite web |ref={{harvid|Planning|1998}} | date=December 1998 | publisher=Planning Commission, Dar es Salaam, and the Regional Commissioner's Office, Kigoma | title=Kigoma region: Socio-Economic Profile | page=1 | url=http://www.tzonline.org/pdf/kigoma.pdf| archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20040530221802/http://www.tzonline.org/pdf/kigoma.pdf | archivedate=30 May 2004 | url-status=live}}</ref>

The region's total area is {{convert|45066|km2}},<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://harvestchoice.org/sites/default/files/downloads/publications/Tanzania_2007-8_Vol_5p.pdf |title=''National Sample Census of Agriculture 2002/03: Volume Vq: Regional Report: Kagera Region'', Tanzania National Bureau of Statistics, December 2007, page 1 |access-date=2013-05-15 |archive-date=2016-03-04 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304074838/http://harvestchoice.org/sites/default/files/downloads/publications/Tanzania_2007-8_Vol_5p.pdf |url-status=live }}</ref> of which {{Convert|37037|sqkm|sqmi|0}} is land and {{Convert|8029|sqkm|sqmi|0}} is water.<ref name="Economic-profile"/> The region's total area is just {{convert|161|km2}} less than that of Estonia. {{As of|1998}}, approximately {{convert|20,000|km2}} was in forests and {{convert|12,000|km2}} was suitable for grazing or farming.<ref name="Economic-profile"/>

Kigoma Region is on a plateau that slopes from the northeast at about {{convert|1,750|m}} down to {{convert|800|m}} at the shore of lake Tanganyika.<ref name="SEP-2">{{harvnb|Planning|1998|page=2}}</ref> The topography in the north and east is gently rolling hills that gradually become steeper as they get closer to the [[Albertine Rift]] margin.<ref name="SEP-2" /> The most important river is the [[Malagarasi River|Malagarasi]], with the [[Luiche River|Luiche]] and the [[Ruchugi River|Ruchugi]] being the two other major rivers draining the region.<ref name="SEP-2" />

==History==
[[File:Rencontre_de_Livingstone_-_How_I_found_Livingstone_(fr).png|thumb|Stanley meeting Livingstone]]

In precolonial Africa the region was a source of ivory and slaves.<ref name="History">{{Citation|author=Head of Regional Office, Kigoma |year=2011 |chapter=2.0 Historia Fupi ya Mkoa (Short History of the Region) |title=Taarifa ya Miaka 50 ya Uhuru wa Tanzania Mkoa wa Kigoma (Information concerning 50 Years of the Independence of Tanzania: Kigoma) |language=Swahili |publisher=Office of Prime Minister |pages=4&ndash;7 |chapter-url=http://www.tanzania50uhuru.go.tz/mikoa/kigoma.pdf |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120827212610/http://www.tanzania50uhuru.go.tz/mikoa/kigoma.pdf |archivedate=2012-08-27 }}</ref> [[Tabora]] and [[Ujiji]] were Arab staging areas for shipments to the coast.<ref>{{Cite book | title=Into Africa: The Epic Adventures of Stanley and Livingstone |last=Dugard |first=Martin | year=2004 | publisher=Broadway Books | location=New York | page=[https://archive.org/details/intoafrica00mart/page/68 68] | isbn=978-0-7679-1074-3 | url-access=registration | url=https://archive.org/details/intoafrica00mart/page/68 }} [not the first edition]</ref> It was visited by the early European explorers [[Richard Francis Burton|Richard Burton]], [[John Speke]], [[David Livingstone]], and [[Henry Morton Stanley]]. In fact, Stanley met Livingstone in Ujiji on the shores of Lake Tanganyika on 27 October 1871.<ref>{{Harvnb|Dugard|2004|page=266}}</ref> In colonial times, the Kigoma area was known as the Western Region and the capital was Tabora.<ref name="History" />

==Demographics==
Kigoma Region has a total population of 2,127,930.<ref name=Census>{{cite web|page=103|title=2012 population and housing census: population distribution by administrative areas|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130612082906/http://www.nbs.go.tz/sensa/PDF/Census%20General%20Report%20-%2029%20March%202013_Combined_Final%20for%20Printing.pdf|archive-date=June 12, 2013|publisher=National Bureau of Statistics|url=http://www.nbs.go.tz/sensa/PDF/Census%20General%20Report%20-%2029%20March%202013_Combined_Final%20for%20Printing.pdf}}</ref>

[[Ha people]] are the largest ethnic group living here, alongside other significant populations are of the Wabembe, [[Wamanyema]], [[Tongwe|Watongwe]] and [[Vinza|Wavinza]], [[Sukuma people|Sukuma]] and [[Haya people|Haya]]. Approximately 150,000 refugees from [[Burundi]] and almost another 80,000 refugees from the [[Democratic Republic of Congo]] reside in three [[refugee]] camps in the Kigoma Region, named [[Nyarugusu]], [[Mtendeli]] and [[Nduta]].
