'''Manzanillo''' is a municipality and city in the [[Granma Province]] of [[Cuba]]. By population, it is the [[List of cities in Cuba|14th-largest Cuban city]] and the most populated one not being a provincial seat.

==Geography==
It is a [[port]] city in the [[Granma Province]] in eastern [[Cuba]] on the [[Gulf of Guacanayabo]], near the [[River delta|delta]] of the [[Cauto River]]. Access by sea is limited by the [[coral reef]]s of [[Cay]]o Perla.{{citation needed|date=August 2007}}

The municipality is divided into the [[barrio]]s of Primero, Segundo, Tercero, Cuarto, Quinto y Sexto (part of the city of Manzanillo), as well as the rural communities of Blanquizal, Calicito, Canabacoa, Caño, Ceiba Caridad, Congo, Jibacoa, Palmas Altas, Purial, Remate, Tranquilidad and Zarzal.<ref name=guije>{{cite web |url=http://www.guije.com/pueblo/municipios/omanzanillo/index.htm|title=Manzanillo |author=Guije.com |language=es |access-date=2007-10-06}}</ref>

==History==
Manzanillo was founded in 1784.<ref name="GreatestCitiesEncarta">{{cite web |url=http://www.greatestcities.com/Caribbean/Cuba/Manzanillo_city.html |title=Manzanillo Info. |author=Encarta |author-link=Encarta |work=GreatestCities.com |year=2001}}</ref><ref name=EB1911>{{cite EB1911 |wstitle=Manzanillo (Cuba) |volume=17 |page=626}}</ref> The settlement was sacked by the [[French First Republic|French]] in 1792, and in the following year a fort was built for its protection.<ref name=EB1911/> In 1833 it received an ''ayuntamiento'' (council) and in 1837, for its "loyalty" in not following the lead of Santiago in proclaiming the Spanish Constitution, received from the crown the title of ''Fiel''.<ref name=EB1911/> In 1827 the port was opened to commerce, national and foreign.<ref name=EB1911/> It was the site of [[Battle of Manzanillo (disambiguation)|three battles]] during the [[Spanish–American War]].

==Economy==
Local agriculture is based on raising [[coffee]], [[sugarcane]],<ref name=EB1911/> [[rice]], [[fruit]] and [[tobacco]]<ref name=EB1911/> crops, as well as [[honey]] and [[cattle]].

Local industry is composed of [[sawmill]]s, [[fish]]-[[canning]], [[molasses]] plants, as well as some [[cigar]] and [[leather goods]] factories.<ref name="GreatestCitiesEncarta"/>

[[Zinc]] and [[copper]] deposits are present in the municipality.

==Demographics==
In 2022, the municipality of Manzanillo had a population of 123,542.<ref name="citypopulation">{{Cite web |title=Cuba: Administrative Division (Provinces and Municipalities) - Population Statistics, Charts and Map |url=https://www.citypopulation.de/en/cuba/admin/ |access-date=2024-02-03 |website=www.citypopulation.de}}</ref> With a total area of {{convert|498|km2|sqmi|abbr=on}},<ref name=statoids>{{cite web |url=http://www.statoids.com/ycu.html |title=Municipios of Cuba |author=Statoids |date=July 2003 |access-date=2007-10-06}}</ref> it has a population density of {{convert|250|/km2|/sqmi|abbr=on}}.

==Culture==
Manzanillo is also home of the medical science faculty of the [[University of Granma]].

==Transport==
The city is crossed by the state highway [[Circuito Sur de Oriente]] (CSO) and by the highway 17 to [[Las Tunas (city)|Las Tunas]]. The railway station is the western terminus of a line from [[Bayamo]], and is served by regional and long-distance trains, the latter linking the city with [[Santiago de Cuba]], [[Guantánamo]], [[Camagüey]], [[Ciego de Ávila]], [[Santa Clara, Cuba|Santa Clara]] and [[Havana]].
