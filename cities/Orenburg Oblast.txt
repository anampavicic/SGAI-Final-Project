'''Orenburg Oblast'''{{efn|{{lang-rus|Оренбургская область|r=Orenburgskaja oblastj|p=ɐrʲɪnˈburkskəjə ˈobɫəsʲtʲ}}}} is a [[federal subjects of Russia|federal subject]] of [[Russia]] (an [[oblast]]), mainly located in [[Eastern Europe]]. Its [[administrative center]] is the [[types of inhabited localities in Russia|city]] of [[Orenburg]]. From 1938 to 1957, it bore the name '''Chkalov Oblast'''{{efn|{{lang-rus|Чкаловская область|r=Čkalovskaja oblastj|p=ˈtɕkaɫəfskəjə ˈobɫəsʲtʲ}}}} in honor of [[Valery Chkalov]]. As of the [[2021 Russian census|2021 Census]], the city has population of 1,862,767.<ref name="2021Census">{{ru-pop-ref|2021Census}}</ref>
[[File:YrymburWhiteHouse.jpg|309x309px|thumb|right|The [[House of the Soviets (Orenburg)|House of the Soviets]] (oblast administrative centre)]]

==Geography==
Orenburg Oblast's internal borders are with the republics of [[Bashkortostan]] and [[Tatarstan]] to the north, [[Chelyabinsk Oblast]] to the north-east, and with [[Samara Oblast|Samara]] and [[Saratov Oblast|Saratov]] oblasts to the west. Orenburg Oblast also shares an international border with [[Kazakhstan]] to the east and south. The oblast is situated on the [[boundary between Europe and Asia]]. The majority of its territory lies west of the continental divide in European Russia and smaller sections in the east situated on the Asian side of the divide. The most important river of the oblast is the [[Ural River|Ural]] and the largest lake [[Shalkar-Yega-Kara]]. Orenburg is traversed by the northeasterly line of equal latitude and longitude. The highest point of the oblast is the {{convert|668|m|abbr=on}}-high [[Nakas (mountain)|Nakas]].<ref name="GЕ">[[Google Earth]]</ref>

==History==
In the first half of the 18th century, the Russian Empire constructed the {{Interlanguage link|Irtysh line|ru|Иртышская линия}}, a series of 46 forts, including Orenburg,<ref>"''Russian Colonization and the Genesis of Kazak National Consciousness''". S. Sabol (2003). Springer. p.27 {{ISBN|0230599427}}</ref> to prevent [[Kazakh Khanate|Kazakh]] and [[Dzungar Khanate|Dzungar]] nomads from raiding Russian territory.<ref>"''Central Asia, 130 Years of Russian Dominance: A Historical Overview''". Edward A. Allworth, Edward Allworth (1994). Duke University Press. p. 10. {{ISBN|0822315211}}</ref> 

Orenburg played a major role in [[Pugachev's Rebellion]] (1773–1774), the largest peasant revolt in Russian history. 

During the [[Russian Civil War]], the region was heavily affected by the [[Russian famine of 1921–1922]].

During [[World War II]] in 1941, the command and staff of the newly formed Polish [[Anders' Army]] was based in [[Buzuluk, Orenburg Oblast|Buzuluk]],<ref>{{cite book|author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |title=Ocaleni z "nieludzkiej ziemi"|year=2012|language=pl|publisher=[[Institute of National Remembrance|Instytut Pamięci Narodowej]]|location=Łódź|page=43|isbn=978-83-63695-00-2}}</ref> and in 1942, the [[First Czechoslovak Independent Field Battalion]] was based there. Both formations afterwards fought against [[Nazi Germany]].

On 26 May 2024, a [[Ukraine|Ukrainian]] drone attacked the [[Voronezh radar|Voronezh M]] long-range radar station near [[Orsk]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Ukraine Strikes Russian Early-Warning System in Orenburg – Report |url=https://www.themoscowtimes.com/2024/05/27/ukraine-strikes-russian-early-warning-system-in-orenburg-report-a85228 |work=The Moscow Times |date=27 May 2024}}</ref>

==Economy==
Orenburg Oblast is one of the major agricultural areas of Russia. Its climate is favorable to farming with a humid spring, dry summer and many sunny days, which make perfect conditions for cultivating hard [[wheat]] and [[rye]], [[sunflower]]s, [[potato]]es, [[pea]]s, [[beans]], [[maize|corn]], and [[gourd]]s.

The range of the oblast's export commodities includes oil and oil products, gas and gas produced products, rolled ferrous and non-ferrous metals, [[nickel]], [[asbestos]], [[chromium]] compounds, rough copper, electric engines, and radiators, which are used to make products from the machine-building industry.
