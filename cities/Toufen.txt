'''Toufen''' ([[Hakka Chinese|Hakka]] [[Pha̍k-fa-sṳ|PFS]]: ''Thèu-fun''; [[Hokkien]] [[Pe̍h-ōe-jī|POJ]]: ''Thâu-hūn'') is a [[county-administered city]] in northern [[Miaoli County]], [[Taiwan]]. Its city centre forms a continuous urban area with [[Zhunan]].

==History==
In 2007, there was a revitalization project for the community houses in the city which was funded by [[Ministry of Culture (Taiwan)|Council of Cultural Affairs]] and private sectors which turned the buildings into a museum of chronicling life in the 1950s and 1960s.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.taipeitimes.com/News/taiwan/archives/2015/10/17/2003630276|date=17 October 2015|title=Renovated cluster of Miaoli houses bring tourists, jobs|publisher=Taipei Times|accessdate=11 May 2017}}</ref> On 5 October 2015, Toufen was upgraded from an [[Township (Taiwan)|urban township]] to a county-administered city.<ref>{{citation|url=http://news.ltn.com.tw/news/politics/breakingnews/1381240|publisher=[[Liberty Times]]|author=鄭鴻達|date=2015-07-16|script-title=zh:苗栗頭份改制縣轄市 鎮代表會落槌通過|language=Chinese}}</ref>

==Transportation==
Toufen is accessible by [[Zhunan railway station|Zhunan Station]] of the [[Taiwan Railways Administration]] located in the neighboring [[Zhunan, Miaoli|Zhunan Township]]. The [[Taiwan High Speed Rail]] passes through the central part of the city, but no station is currently planned. 

Toufen is also served by [[National Freeway 1|Freeway 1]], Provincial Highways [[Provincial Highway 1 (Taiwan)|1]], 1B, [[Provincial Highway 3 (Taiwan)|3]], [[Provincial Highway 13 (Taiwan)|13]], 13A, and County Routes 124, 124A, and 124C.
