'''Uberlândia''' ({{IPA|pt|ubɛʁˈlɐ̃dʒiɐ}}) is a [[Municipalities of Brazil|municipality]] in the [[States of Brazil|state]] of [[Minas Gerais]], southeastern [[Brazil]]. It is the second largest municipality in the state of Minas Gerais after the state capital [[Belo Horizonte]]. Its population in 2020 was 699,097, making it the sixth largest city of the interior region of Brazil. The city is located on the [[Brazilian Highlands]] {{convert|2,802|ft|m}} above sea level. It is an important logistic hub between [[São Paulo]] and [[Brasília]]. The city sits within the Brazilian [[cerrado]] and has eight protected zones of tropical savanna vegetation.<ref name="b"/>

Uberlândia is served by [[Uberlândia Airport|Uberlândia–Ten. Cel. Av. César Bombonato Airport]].

[[Image:Estação Mogiana de Uberlândia MG.jpg|thumb|right|Mogiana Uberlândia Railroad Station in 1895]]

==Geography==
[[Image:Selecao brasileira volei udi.jpg|thumb|right|Uberlândia Volleyball team]]

Uberlândia is located in the western part of the state of [[Minas Gerais]], in the region called [[Triângulo Mineiro]], one of the richest agricultural regions of Brazil, between the Paranaíba and Grande rivers.<ref name="b"/> It is connected to major cities by the following federal highways: BR-050, BR-365, BR-455, BR-452, and BR-497. Uberlândia is about 580&nbsp;km away from [[São Paulo]], connected by a modern and safe double trace highway ([[BR-050]]). The capital of the state of Minas Gerais, [[Belo Horizonte]], is {{convert|560|km|mi}} away and [[Brasília]], the capital of the country, lies just {{convert|440|km|mi}} to the north. There are railways connecting Uberlândia via the north-south Centro-Atlântica line.

Uberlândia is a statistical micro-region including the following municipalities: [[Araguari]], [[Araporã]], [[Canápolis]], [[Cascalho Rico]], [[Centralina, Minas Gerais|Centralina]], [[Indianópolis]], [[Monte Alegre de Minas]], [[Prata, Minas Gerais|Prata]], [[Tupaciguara]], and Uberlândia. In 2007 the population of these cities' agglomeration was 818,395 inhabitants in a total area of {{convert|18,864.20|km2|mi2}}. The population density (2000) was 43.38 inhabitants/km<sup>2</sup>.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.citybrazil.com.br/mg/regioes/uberlandia/|title=Citybrazil|website=citybrazil.com.br|access-date=5 April 2018|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080425223747/http://www.citybrazil.com.br/mg/regioes/uberlandia/|archive-date=25 April 2008|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

Due to its location and transport links, Uberlândia has been chosen as a point for the [[Free Economic Zone of Manaus]], the first ever such distribution point.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.farolcomunitario.com.br/uberlandia_100_0058.htm|title=Zona Franca de Manaus terá entreposto em Uberlândia - FarolCom|first=Caldo Quântico Ltda. -|last=www.caldoquantico.com.br|website=www.farolcomunitario.com.br|access-date=5 April 2018}}</ref>

According to the modern (2017) geographic classification by Brazil's National Institute of Geography and Statistics ([[Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics|IBGE]]), the city is the main municipality in the [[Intermediate Geographic Region of Uberlândia]].

==Religion==
According to the 2010 census its citizens are members of the [[Roman Catholic Church]] (54.73%), while [[evangelicalism|Evangelical Christians]] comprise the second largest religious affiliation in the city (25.23%). There are also minorities of [[Kardecist spiritism|Spiritists]] (7.80%) and not religious (7.80%). There are smaller minorities adhering the [[Brazilian Catholic Apostolic Church]], the [[Eastern Orthodox Church]], [[Jehovah's Witnesses]], [[The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints]], [[Islam]], [[Buddhism]], indigenous beliefs, [[Umbanda]] and [[Candomblé]], (all below 1% of Uberlândia's population).<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mai.org.br/tabelas/municipios/censo2010_listareligioes_list.php?a=integrated&ctlSearchFor=Uberl%E2ndia&simpleSrchFieldsComboOpt=&simpleSrchTypeComboNot=&simpleSrchTypeComboOpt=&criteria=and |title=Censo 2010: Listas municipios e religiões|language=pt |year=2010 |publisher=Ministério de Apoio com Informação |access-date=20 October 2012}}</ref>

==Tourism==
The city has architectural, culture, and natural values. Here are the main attractions in the city:
*[[Municipal Market of Uberlândia]]: It is located at the centre of the city. It was established in 1923, but only had its construction started in 1944, under the tenure of Mayor Vasconcelos Costa, with modern era architecture. It was a wholesaler centre until 1977, when the commerce was transferred to the Ceasa. It is situated in Olegario Máciel Road, and offers diverse typical products from the state of [[Minas Gerais]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uberlandia.mg.gov.br/?pagina=Conteudo&id=152|title=Mercado Municipal|language=pt|year=2016|publisher=Prefeitura de Uberlandia|access-date=2 November 2016}}</ref>
*[[Municipal Museum of Uberlândia]]: It is located at the centre of the city, on the Clarimundo Carneiro Square. The same building had been used as City Hall. It has been reformed and today is a place where many cultural projects take place.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uberlandia.mg.gov.br/2014/secretaria-pagina/23/411/secretaria.html|title=Museo Municipal|language=pt|year=2016|publisher=Prefeitura de Uberlandia|access-date=2 November 2016}}</ref>
*[[Sabiá Park]] It is a park/zoo administrated by [[FUTEL]] (the Uberlândian Foundation for Tourism, Sport and Leisure), located in the borough of Tibery, on the East Zone of the city. It is a green complex made up of a Zoo with dozens of animals in captivity; seven lakes that come together to form one big lagoon; an aquarium that has 36 tanks and 36 different fish species; a running track of 5.100 meters; and much more.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uberlandia.mg.gov.br/2014/secretaria-pagina/51/144/secretaria.html|title=Complexo Parque do Sabia|language=pt|year=2016|publisher=Prefeitura de Uberlandia|access-date=2 November 2016}}</ref>
*[[Municipal Park Victoria Siquierolli]]: It is located on the North Zone of Uberlândia, being where legitimate examples of vegetation of the [[Brazilian cerrado]] can be found, with trees with leathery leaves, twisted trunks, colourful flowers and many fruits, as well as a playground for children and a biodiversity museum. Its total area complies of a total of 232 300 square meters and is located between the boroughs of Jardim América II, Residencial Gramado, Nossa Senhora das Graças e Cruzeiro do Sul.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uberlandia.mg.gov.br/2014/secretaria-pagina/51/138/secretaria.html|title=Parque Municipal Vitoria Siquerolli|language=pt|year=2016|publisher=Prefeitura de Uberlandia|access-date=2 November 2016}}</ref>
*[[Rondon Pacheco Avenue]]: It is considered to be the “gastronomic corridor” of Uberlândia. Around it is where the nightlife in Uberlândia can be found, having many bars, restaurants, cachaçarias, pizzerias, cafes, ice cream parlours and sweet shops. There are more than 60 establishments across the avenue. Furthermore, the avenue also has many hotels, convenience stores, gas stations, supermarkets and hypermarkets. The avenue cuts the city from east to west. The Governor Rondon Pacheco Avenue starts with its merge with the Silvio Regain Avenue, on the Tubalina borough, on the southeast region of the city, and ends up on the [[BR-050]] highway, on the furthest side of the Custodio Pereira borough, on the west region of the city.
