== Etymology ==
The name "Elbistan" was pronounced similarly in Byzantine and Islamic sources. Elbistan was known as Plasta and Plastentia ({{langx|el|Πλαστεντία}}) in antiquity. Elbistan was known as ''Ablasta'' ({{langx|hy|Աբլաստա}}) according to Armenian historians in the early 11th century. According to [[Baldric of Dol]] the city was known as ''"Ablistan"'' till 15th century. [[Egyptians|Egyptian]]-[[Mamluk Sultanate (Cairo)|Mamluk]] historian [[Muhammad ibn Iyas]] wrote the city's name as ''"Albistan"''. Alaüddevle Bozkurt Bey from [[Beylik of Dulkadir|Dulkadirids]] used the name ''"Elbistan"'' in the official documents. After [[Beylik of Dulkadir|Dulkadirids]] were conquered by the [[Ottoman Empire]] in the 16th century, the current name became prevalent. Among the rural people of Elbistan it is pronounced as ''"Albıstan"''. Albistan means "the orchard" in Arabic.<ref>[http://www.elbistanafsin.com/elbistanafsinili/ir2-Elbistan-Isminin-Tarihcesi] Elbistan İsminin Tarihçesi</ref>

== History ==
{{section-stub|date=February 2022}}
The settlement of the Elbistan plain around the town of Elbistan goes back to prehistoric times. In 1947, an important [[Anatolian hieroglyphic]] inscription stele was discovered near the village of [[Karahüyük (Elbistan)]], which is located 9 km northwest from Elbistan town. This stele is believed to be from the 12th century BC.<ref>[https://www.hittitemonuments.com/karahoyuk/ Karahöyük (Elbistan) Stele] hittitemonuments.com</ref>

In the mid-10th century, modern settlement of the area begins. The town seems to have been settled first by [[Armenians|Armenian]] immigrants.<ref name="Sinclair">{{cite book |last1=Sinclair |first1=T A |title=Eastern Turkey: An Architectural and Archaeological Survey, Volume II |date=1989 |publisher=ISD LLC |isbn=9781904597735}}</ref> By the end of the 11th century, the town had become the most important one in the Elbistan plain, was fortified against Turkish raiders and was seat of an [[Armenian Apostolic Church|Armenian bishop]].<ref name="Sinclair" /> When the army of the [[First Crusade]] passed through Anatolia recovering land for the [[Byzantine Empire]] in 1097, [[Peter Aliphas]] was installed as governor of Plastentia.<ref name="Frankopan">{{cite book |last1=Frankopan |first1=Peter |title=The First Crusade: The Call from the East |date=2012 |publisher=Harvard University Press |isbn=9780674064997 |page=149}}</ref>

In 1277 the [[Mamluk Sultanate|Mamluks]] led by [[Baybars]] defeated a [[Mongol Empire|Mongol]] army in the [[Battle of Elbistan]]. Thereafter, Elbistan and the region around it became part of the Mamluk northern frontier.<ref name="Muslu">{{cite book |last1=Muslu |first1=Cihan Yüksel |title=The Ottomans and the Mamluks: Imperial Diplomacy and Warfare in the Islamic World |date=25 July 2014 |publisher=Bloomsbury Publishing |isbn=978-0-85773-580-5 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=b7iKDwAAQBAJ |access-date=18 February 2024 |language=en}}</ref> In 1337 [[Zeyneddin Karaca Bey]] captured the town from the Mamluks and established the [[Beylik of Dulkadir]] with the region around Elbistan and [[Marash]] as its center.<ref name="Har-El">{{cite book |last1=Har-El |first1=Shai |title=Struggle for Domination in the Middle East: The Ottoman-Mamluk War, 1485-91 |date=1995 |publisher=Brill |isbn=9789004101807 |page=40 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=KUjl5hWOYVMC |access-date=20 January 2023}}</ref> Nevertheless, Dulkadirids continued to pay homage to the Mamluks and fought with the Karamandids to defend Mamluk interests though they sought for more autonomy.<ref name="Muslu" /> The Dulkadirids controlled the region for 178 years until the Ottomans finally conquered it in 1515.<ref name="Har-El" />

Elbistan became then known as "[[Vilayet|vilayet-i]] Türkmân" in the Ottoman documents.<ref>[https://www.kulturportali.gov.tr/medya/dokuman/dokumandetay/3790 Akdeniz'in Altın Kenti Kahramanmaraş]. Kahramanmaraş Valiliği İl Kültür ve Turizm Müdürlüğü. p.33 {{ISBN|978-605-149-657-3}} ([https://www.kulturportali.gov.tr/Common/DownloadFile.aspx?f=AkdenizinAltnKentiKahramanmara_20160111135249039.pdf PDF])</ref> [[Evliya Çelebi]]'s [[Seyahatnâme]] from the 17th century gives information about the region that in the mountains and towns mostly reside [[Turkoman (ethnonym)|Turkmens]] who originally migrated from [[Bukhara]].<ref>Derviş Mehmet Zılli bin, Evliya Çelebi (2006). "Mar-iş (Maraş)". Seyahatname. Yeni Şafak Gazetesi. p. 297. {{ISBN|975-363-798-5}}.</ref><ref>[http://www.kahramanmaras.gov.tr/elbistan "Elbistan"] - Official website of the Kahramanmaras Governor (Kahramanmaraş Valiliği)</ref> It seems that some local chiefdoms were given varying degrees of autonomy, notably around the localities of [[Haticepınar]] and [[Kasanlı]].

== Demographics ==
[[Evliya Çelebi]] noted that the majority of the town's population was Turkoman in his [[seyahatname]].<ref>{{cite book |title=Evliyâ Çelebi Seyahatnâmesi: III |page=201 |url=https://archive.org/details/EvliyelebiSeyahatnmesiIII/page/n201/mode/2up |access-date=17 October 2022}}</ref> Currently, the majority of the population of the district is [[Sunni Islam|Sunni]] [[Turkish people|Turkish]]<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.pirha.net/marasta-alevi-kurtlerin-nufusu-yuzde-10lara-dustu-66179.html/15/06/2017/|title = 'Maraş'ta Alevi Kürtlerin nüfusu yüzde 10'lara düştü'|date = 15 June 2017|access-date = 27 August 2021|archive-date = 27 August 2021|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20210827152752/https://www.pirha.net/marasta-alevi-kurtlerin-nufusu-yuzde-10lara-dustu-66179.html/15/06/2017/|url-status = dead}}</ref> with a significant [[Kurdish Alevism|Alevi]] and Sunni [[Kurds|Kurdish]] population.<ref>{{Cite web|last=McDowall|first=Donald|date=November 2002|title=Asylum seekers from Turkey II|url=https://www.refworld.org/pdfid/478e3c990.pdf|page=64}}</ref><ref>{{citation |title=Aşiretler Raporu |author=Turkish state|date=2014 |publisher=Kaynak Yayınları |isbn=978-975-343-220-7|edition=3 |language=tr|page=205}}</ref> Turkish [[Alevism|Alevis]] are also present.<ref>Dursun, Mehmet E.; Kirik, Esra. [https://turkishstudies.net/turkishstudies?mod=makale_tr_ozet&makale_id=15518 Elbistan Ağzı (Kahramanmaraş Ağızları - II)]. ==Climate==
Elbistan has a fairly dry climate with cold winters and hot, dry summers. Elbistan's climate is classified as a dry-summer [[continental climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification|Köppen]]: ''Dsa'').<ref>{{Cite journal |title=Table 1 Overview of the Köppen-Geiger climate classes including the defining criteria. |url=https://www.nature.com/articles/s41597-023-02549-6/tables/1 |journal=Nature: Scientific Data |language=en}}</ref>

== Economy ==

{{See also|Coal in Turkey}}

The [[Elbistan coalfield]] supplies [[lignite]] to the nearby [[Afşin-Elbistan power stations]] in [[Afşin]].

== Environment ==
It is said that [[air pollution in Turkey]] from the nearby [[coal-fired power station]]s also affects Elbistan, as well as smoke from landfill.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Sesi|first=Elbistanın|title=Dumanı hem boğuyor hem zehirliyor, kokusu da cabası!|url=http://www.elbistaninsesi.com/guncel/dumani-hem-boguyor-hem-zehirliyor-kokusu-da-cabasi-h67733.html|access-date=2020-10-05|website=Elbistanın Sesi|language=tr}}</ref> In late 2020 the oldest plant Afşin-Elbistan A, was said by opposition MP Ali Öztunç to be still operating without filters.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Akdemir -|first=Özer|date=2020-09-11|title=Afşin Elbistan Termik Santrali A Ünitesi hâlâ filtresiz çalışıyor! {{!}} Özer Akdemir|url=https://ekolojibirligi.org/afsin-elbistan-termik-santrali-a-unitesi-hala-filtresiz-calisiyor-ozer-akdemir/|access-date=2020-10-05|website=Ekoloji Birliği|language=tr}}</ref>
