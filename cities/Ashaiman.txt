'''Ashaiman''' (often wrongly spelled as "Ashiaman") is a large town and the capital of the [[Ashaiman Municipal District]] in the [[Greater Accra Region]] of [[Ghana]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2021-04-19 |title=Afienya, Ashaiman, Katamanso and other areas in Accra to have interrupted supply - Water Company - MyJoyOnline.com |url=https://www.myjoyonline.com/afienya-ashaiman-katamanso-and-other-areas-in-accra-to-have-interrupted-supply-water-company/ |access-date=2022-05-25 |website=www.myjoyonline.com |language=en-US}}</ref> According to the 2021 census, the town, along with the district, has a population of 208,060 people.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Ghana Districts: A repository of all Local Assemblies in Ghana |url=https://www.ghanadistricts.com/Home/District/108 |access-date=2023-11-24 |website=www.ghanadistricts.com}}</ref> Ethnic groups who resided in Ashaiman are the [[Ga-Adangbe people|Ga-Adangbe]], [[Ga people|Ga]], [[Guan people|Guan]], [[Hausa people|Hausa]], [[Fante people]], [[Dagomba people|Dagomba]], [[Ewe people|Ewe]], [[ashanti people|Ashanti]], and [[Akuapem people|Akuapem]].<ref name="2010 census"/> The current [[Omanhene]] of the town is Nii Annang Adzor.<ref name="paramount chief">{{cite news|last=Neequaye|first=B.|url=https://www.graphic.com.gh/sports/boxing/ashaiman-chief-prepares-to-welcome-awinongya-jr-sunday.html|title=Ashaiman chief prepares to welcome Awinongya Jr Sunday|date=6 February 2022|at=para. 1|publisher=Graphic Online|access-date=18 August 2024}}</ref>

== Etymology ==
According to local belief, the name came from two Ga words: "Ashai", the first name of the man who had founded the town (Ibrahim Nii Ashai), and "''Mang''" which means "town". Therefore, it is abbreviated to "Ashaiman".<ref name="name origin"/>

== History ==
Ashaiman was founded by Nii Ashai after he had moved from [[Tema]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ghanaweb.com/GhanaHomePage/features/artikel.php?ID=152649|title = Spotlight on Ashaiman &#124; Opinions 2008-11-08|date=30 November 2001}}</ref> The town was expanded by squatters who were building Tema and others and had moved in to occupy government land.<ref name="Housing">{{cite journal |last1=Tipple |first1=Graham |last2=Korboe |first2=David |last3=Garrod |first3=Guy |last4=Willis |first4=Ken |title=Housing supply in Ghana: A study of Accra, Kumasi and Berekum |journal=Progress in Planning |date=May 1999 |volume=51 |issue=4 |pages=255–324 |doi=10.1016/S0305-9006(99)00002-1}}</ref>

== Demographics ==
Most of the popular is religious coming in with 96.9% while the other 3.1% aren't affiliated with any religions. The biggest religions in the town are Christianity (approx. 80%), Islam (16.5%), and traditional religions (0.4%).<ref name="2010 census"/>{{rp|29}} Major Ethnic groups who lived in Ashaiman are the [[Ga-Adangbe people|Ga-Adangbe]], [[Ga people|Ga]], [[Guan people|Guan]], [[Hausa people|Hausa]], [[Fante people]], [[Dagomba people|Dagomba]], [[Ewe people|Ewe]], [[ashanti people|Ashanti]], and [[Akuapem people|Akuapem]].<ref name="2010 census"/>{{rp|3}}

== Economy ==
The biggest economic sector in the town is [[agriculture]], followed by [[manufacturing]] and [[service (economics)|services]].<ref name="2010 census"/>{{rp|4}}

== Culture ==
Ashaiman, along with other parts of Ghana, has a paramount chief or [[Omanhene]] in which the current placeholder is Nii Annang Adzor.<ref name="paramount chief"/>

== Geography ==
Ashaiman is located in the [[Ashaiman Municipal District|Ashaiman Municipal]] which has an total land area of {{convert|45|sqkm|sqmi}}. The district bordered [[Kpone Katamanso Municipal District]] to the north and east and the [[Tema Metropolitan District]] towards the south and the west.<ref name="2010 census"/>{{rp|4}}

=== Climate ===
The town has a [[tropical savanna climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification]] ''Aw''), experiencing a wet season and a dry season and the temperature being hot year-round. The average annual rainfall ranges from {{convert|730|to|790|mm|ft|abbr=on}}. The wet season usually starts from April to June while the dry is from September to November.<ref name="2010 census"/>{{rp|1}}

== Human resources ==
=== Health ===
On 3 October 2024, It was announced that a 190-bed capacity district hospital was going to be built at Ashaiman as part of Ghana's Agenda 111, a project aim to improve access to convectional healthcare. The project has a budget of US$12 million and is expect to be completed in late-2024.<ref>{{cite news|last=Tetteh|first=I.|url=https://gna.org.gh/2023/10/agenda-111-ashaiman-to-benefit-from-12m-district-hospital/|title=Agenda 111: Ashaiman to benefit from $12m, district hospital|date=3 October 2024|publisher=[[Ghana News Agency]]|access-date=18 August 2024}}</ref>

=== Education ===
The most well-known educational institution in the town is the Ashaiman Senior High School. It was founded in September 1990 by the [[Provisional National Defence Council]] and it began with 39 students and two teachers. Currently the school has a population of about 1,400 students and 104 teachers. Courses offered in the school include General Arts, General Science, Agriculture, Business and Visual Arts.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.newsghana.com.gh/ashaiman-shs-25th-anniversary-launched/|title=Ashaiman SHS 25th Anniversary launched - News Ghana|date=31 October 2014|publisher=[[News Ghana]]|access-date=18 August 2024}}</ref>
