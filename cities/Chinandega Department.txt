'''Chinandega''' ({{IPA|es|tʃinanˈdeɣa}}) is a [[Departments of Nicaragua|department]] in [[Nicaragua]], located on the border with [[Honduras]]. It covers an area of 4,822&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> and has a population of 441,897 (2021 estimate). The indigenous inhabitants of Chinandega are the [[Chorotega people|Chorotegas]] and the [[Nahuas]], and was the location of the [[pre-Columbian]] Nahua kingdom of Teswatlan.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.territorioindigenaygobernanza.com/web/nnic_06/|title = Chorotegas}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://camjol.info/index.php/raices/article/view/6891/6559|title = Races of Chinandega}}</ref><ref>Fernández de Oviedo y Valdés, Gonzalo, ''Historia General y Natural de las Indias'', pag. 127., tomo XI, Editorial Guarania. Asunción, Paraguay, 1945.</ref> The capital is the city of [[Chinandega]].

Largely agricultural, it produces [[rum]] from [[sugar cane]]; other products are [[bananas]], [[peanut]]s, [[shrimp]] and [[salt]].

The port of [[Corinto, Nicaragua|Corinto]], in Chinandega, is the most important in Western Nicaragua.
