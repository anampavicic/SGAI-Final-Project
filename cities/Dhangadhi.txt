'''Dhangadhi''' ({{langx|ne|धनगढी }}) is a [[List of cities in Nepal|sub-metropolitan city]]<ref name="Kathmandupost.ekantipur.com">{{cite web|url=http://kathmandupost.ekantipur.com/news/2015-09-19/govt-declares-26-new-municipalities.html|title=The Kathmandu Post :: Govt declares 26 new municipalities|access-date=13 March 2016|archive-date=22 November 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171122232549/http://kathmandupost.ekantipur.com/news/2015-09-19/govt-declares-26-new-municipalities.html|url-status=live}}</ref> and the district headquarters of [[Kailali District]] in [[Sudurpashchim Province]] of Nepal. It shares a border with [[India]] in the south, [[Godawari, Seti|Godawari]] and Gauriganga Municipality in the North, Kailari Rural Municipality in the east and [[Kanchanpur District]] in the west. Dhangadhi is a sub-metropolis divided into 19 wards.<ref>{{cite web|title=Dhangadhi Sub-Metropolitan City|url=https://drive.google.com/open?id=1ohXM1efbgzTBn126vP0rlEulS_s&usp=sharing|access-date=1 May 2018}}</ref> It has an area of 271.74 Sq. KM. It is one of the major cities of Far - West Province of Nepal along with [[Bhimdatta|Mahendranagar]].<ref name="cbs.gov.np">{{cite web|url=http://cbs.gov.np/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/National%20Report.pdf|title=Archived copy|access-date=1 November 2012|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130418041642/http://cbs.gov.np/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/National%20Report.pdf|archive-date=18 April 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=PSU Dhangadhi {{!}} Local Governance and Community Development Programme (LGCDP) - II |url=http://lgcdp.gov.np/dhangadhi |website=lgcdp.gov.np |access-date=29 June 2019 |archive-date=29 June 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190629081845/http://lgcdp.gov.np/dhangadhi |url-status=live }}</ref> The city is connected with the [[Mahakali Highway]], about 750 kilometers west of [[Kathmandu]].

Dhangadhi was established in 1976 as a municipality.<ref>{{cite web|title=Brief Introduction|url=http://dhangadhimun.gov.np/en/node/40|publisher=Dhangadhi Sub-Metropolitan City|access-date=1 May 2018|archive-date=13 May 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180513145120/http://dhangadhimun.gov.np/en/node/40|url-status=live}}</ref> There is a fable. [[Rana Tharu people|Rana Tharu]] of Dhangadhi used to bury their wealth in the ground due to the fear of robbers. That is why the name of Dhangadhi remained Dhangadhi.<ref>{{cite web|title=26 new Municipalities announced|url=http://therisingnepal.org.np/news/6587|publisher=The Rising Nepal|access-date=1 May 2018|date=18 September 2015|archive-date=27 September 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150927235504/http://therisingnepal.org.np/news/6587|url-status=dead}}</ref>

198,792 population as per [[2021 Nepal census]]. It is the most densely populated city in the province.<ref>{{Cite web |title=population {{!}} national_population and housing_census_year results |url=https://censusnepal.cbs.gov.np/results/population?province=7&district=76&municipality=13 |access-date=2024-02-03 |website=censusnepal.cbs.gov.np}}</ref> It became the first sub-metropolitan city in the far-west after it was upgraded to a sub-metropolis from municipality status on 18 September 2015 as the [[Village development committee (Nepal)|village development committees]] Fulbari and [[Urma]] were [[Merger (politics)|merged]] into Dhangadhi and later wards 9 and 11 of former [[Attariya Municipality]] were also merged.

== Economy ==

[[File:Dhangadhi dt.jpg|thumb|[[Dhangadhi]]|300px]]

Dhangadhi serves as the primary commercial and industrial hub for [[Sudurpashchim Province]] and one of the fastest developing cities of Nepal.<ref>{{Cite web |last=CHHATYAL |first=DIL BAHADUR |title=Transforming Dhangadhi: Development Shaping the City’s Face |url=http://myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/140422/ |access-date=2023-08-10 |website=My Republica |language=en}}</ref> It got its first democratically elected mayor who was affiliated with the Nepali Congress Kishor Kumar Bam (1964-2014) who held his office for full term.

It is one of the major trading centres in [[Sudurpashchim Province]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=PSU Dhangadhi {{!}} Local Governance and Community Development Programme (LGCDP) - II |url=http://lgcdp.gov.np/dhangadhi |access-date=2023-04-24 |website=lgcdp.gov.np}}</ref> The city has health care facilities, a number of parks, private schools, colleges, and internet service providers. There are medical, engineering, and management colleges that are affiliated to [[Tribhuvan University]] and [[Far Western University]].

The city attracts migrants from the surrounding area, who move to the city for educational and health services.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Families struggle to care for their children |url=https://www.sos-childrensvillages.org/where-we-help/asia/nepal/dhangadhi}}</ref>

Dhangadhi is the gateway to nearby tourist places like [[Shuklaphanta National Park]], [[Ghodaghodi Tal|Ghodaghodi Lake]] and [[Khaptad National Park]]. It is the connecting city between nearby cities of [[Bhimdatta|Mahendranagar]], [[Attariya Municipality|Attariya]] and [[Dipayal Silgadhi]]. It is also a land route to enter Nepal from India. Thus, it is also involved in hospitality sector.

The city has many commercial banks such as regional office of [[Nepal Rastra Bank]], National Commercial Bank, Agricultural Development Bank, Everest Bank, Nabil Bank, Bank of Kathmandu, Nepal Credit and Commerce Bank, Siddharth Bank, Machhapuchhre Bank, Kumari Bank, Nepal Bangladesh Bank and Kanchan Development Bank.

A number of ''haat bazaars'' are held each week in different parts of the town, where farmers from the rural hinterland set up stalls to trade agricultural products, spices and handicrafts.

Every year, the Kailali Chamber of Commerce and Industry organizes Sudurpaschim Mahotsav in Dhangadhi on the month of [[Mangsir]].

Dhangadhi's economy is predominantly based on agriculture. The fertile plains surrounding the city make it an ideal location for cultivating crops such as rice, wheat, sugarcane, maize, and vegetables. The region's agricultural produce not only serves the local market but also contributes to the national supply chain. Additionally, Dhangadhi's proximity to the Indian border facilitates cross-border trade, making it a significant trading hub for goods and commodities.

In recent years, the city has witnessed the establishment of small-scale industries and businesses. These include manufacturing units, food processing plants, and trading enterprises, contributing to economic growth and employment opportunities.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Everything you need to know about Dhangadhi {{!}} Interesting things about Dhangadhi City |url=https://www.natureloverstrek.com/blog/everything-you-need-to-know-about-dhangadhi--interesting-things-about-dhangadhi-city.htm |access-date=2023-10-16 |website=www.natureloverstrek.com}}</ref>

==Climate==
Dhangadhi is located in the [[Terai]]. It has a monsoon-influenced [[humid subtropical climate]] (''Cwa'' according to the [[Köppen climate classification]]).The months of March and April are hot and dry. It is a cold winter from December to February. It is affected by the south-west monsoon from June to September which brings heavy rainfall. The weather in this period remains hot and humid. The highest temperature ever recorded in Dhangadhi was {{convert|46.4|°C}} on 16 June 1995, which is the highest temperature to have ever been recorded in Nepal.<ref name="NepalCPS2015">{{cite web |url=https://cbs.gov.np/download/5887/ |title=Climatic Extremities in Nepal |work=Compendium of Environmental Statistics Nepal 2015 |publisher=Central Bureau of Statistics |year=2016 |pages=43–44 |access-date=18 December 2019 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191219013149/https://cbs.gov.np/download/5887/ |archive-date=19 December 2019 }}</ref> The lowest temperature ever recorded was {{convert|0.0|°C}} on 11 January 1985.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.dhm.gov.np/uploads/climatic/1368567860Ext%20temp%20of%20November.pdf |title=Archived copy |access-date=27 February 2016 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304201452/http://www.dhm.gov.np/uploads/climatic/1368567860Ext%20temp%20of%20November.pdf |archive-date=4 March 2016 }}. == Transportation ==

===Road===
{{Unreferenced section|date=September 2020}}

Dhangadhi is well connected with other major cities of [[Nepal]] and the Indian state of [[Uttar Pradesh]]. Indian and Nepali nationals may cross the international border without restrictions, however there is a [[Geography of Nepal#Border crossing|customs checkpoint]] for goods and third country nationals. Frequent bus services operate between Dhangadhi and Nepalese cities. Local transport includes Cycle Rickshaws, Taxis and Public City Buses.[[Mahakali Highway]] connects Dhangadhi to different parts of Far western Nepal. The country's longest highway, [[Mahendra Highway]], runs through the town of [[Attariya Municipality|Attariya]], 6 kilometers from Dhangadhi.{{citation needed|date=May 2018}}[[File:Dhangadhi Airport.jpg|thumb|Dhangadhi Airport]]

===Air===
[[Dhangadhi Airport]] is located 10&nbsp;km from the administrative center and about 660 kilometers west of Kathmandu.<ref>[[Dhangadhi Airport]]</ref>  The runway has been extended to 1,524 metres (5,000&nbsp;ft) length and blacktopped to accommodate [[Fokker 100]] aircraft.<ref>{{cite web |title=Flights To Dhangadhi - Book Tickets Online To Dhangadhi {{!}} Buddha Air |url=https://www.buddhaair.com/flights-to-dhangadhi |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190419212208/https://www.buddhaair.com/flights-to-dhangadhi |archive-date=19 April 2019 |access-date=29 June 2019 |website=www.buddhaair.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |date=5 May 2019 |title=Dhangadhi airport closed for a month from today |url=https://thehimalayantimes.com/nepal/dhangadhi-airport-closed-for-a-month-from-today/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190629081835/https://thehimalayantimes.com/nepal/dhangadhi-airport-closed-for-a-month-from-today/ |archive-date=29 June 2019 |access-date=29 June 2019 |website=The Himalayan Times}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=CHHATYAL |first1=DIL BAHADUR |title=Dhangadhi airport shut for a month for upgrading |url=https://myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/amp/dhangadhi-airport-shut-for-a-month-for-upgrading/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190629081839/https://myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/amp/dhangadhi-airport-shut-for-a-month-for-upgrading/ |archive-date=29 June 2019 |access-date=29 June 2019 |website=My Republica |language=en}}</ref> It operated flights to [[Tribhuvan International Airport|Kathmandu]], [[Pokhara Airport|Pokhara]], [[Bajura Airport|Bajura]], [[Doti Airport|Dipayal Silgadhi]], [[Bajhang Airport|Bajhang]], [[Surkhet Airport|Surkhet]], [[Sanphebagar Airport|Achham]].
