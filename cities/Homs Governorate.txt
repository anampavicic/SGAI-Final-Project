'''Homs Governorate''' ({{langx|ar|مُحافظة حمص}} / [[ALA-LC]]: ''Muḥāfaẓat Ḥimṣ'') is one of the fourteen [[Governorates of Syria|governorates]] (provinces) of [[Syria]]. It is situated in central Syria. Its geography differs in various locations in the governorate, from {{convert|40,940|km2|0|abbr=on}}<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.statoids.com/usy.html|title=Syria Provinces}}</ref> to {{convert|42,223|km2|0|abbr=on}}.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.citypopulation.de/Syria.html|title=Syria: Governorates, Major Cities & Localities - Population Statistics in Maps and Charts}}</ref> It is geographically the largest governorate in Syria. Homs Governorate has a population of 1,763,000 as of 2010. The Homs governorate is divided into six administrative districts (''[[mantiqah]]''), with the city of Homs as a separate district. [[Homs]] is the [[capital city]] of the [[Homs District|district of Homs]]. Its governor is Namir Habib Makhlouf.<ref name="Zwaan">{{cite news|url=http://www.volkskrant.nl/vk/nl/13828/Burgeroorlog-in-Syrie/article/detail/3630277/2014/04/07/Pater-Frans-van-der-Lugt-doodgeschoten-in-Homs.dhtml|title=Pater Frans van der Lugt doodgeschoten in Homs - Burgeroorlog in Syrië - VK|last=Zwaan|first=Irene de|date=7 April 2014|work=[[de Volkskrant]]|language=nl|access-date=7 April 2014}}</ref>

A Homs Governorate also formed part of [[Ottoman Syria]], when it was also known as the Sanjak of Homs.

== Demographics == 
The [[Al-Mukharram District]] is predominantly inhabited by [[Alawites]]. The Taldou, Talkalakh, Homs and Al-Qusayr districts have a mixed [[Alawites|Alawite]], Sunni Muslim and [[Christianity in Syria|Christian]] population. The [[Al-Qusayr District]] is also home to a large number of [[Shia]] [[Muslims]].
