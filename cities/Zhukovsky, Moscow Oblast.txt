'''Zhukovsky''' ({{langx|ru|link=no|Жуковский}}, {{IPA|ru|ʐʊˈkofskʲɪj}}) is a [[types of inhabited localities in Russia|city]] in [[Moscow Oblast]], Russia, located on the [[Moskva River]], {{convert|40|km|mi|sp=us}} southeast of Moscow. Population: {{ru-census|p2010=104,736|p2002=101,328|p1989=100,609}}

==History==
The [[urban-type settlement]] of '''Stakhanovo''' was founded in 1935 from the [[dacha]] settlement '''Otdykh''' (literally, "Relaxation").{{citation needed|date=February 2024}} It was named after [[Alexey Stakhanov]], a Russian miner of the [[Stalinism|Stalinist]] era. On April&nbsp;23, 1947, the settlement was granted town status and renamed Zhukovsky, in honor of famous scientist [[Nikolay Yegorovich Zhukovsky|Nikolay Zhukovsky]].{{citation needed|date=February 2024}}

==Administrative and municipal status==
Within the [[subdivisions of Russia#Administrative divisions|framework of administrative divisions]], it is incorporated as '''Zhukovsky [[City of federal subject significance|City Under Oblast Jurisdiction]]'''—an administrative unit with the status equal to that of the [[administrative divisions of Moscow Oblast|districts]].<ref name="Ref1406">Law #11/2013-OZ</ref> As a [[subdivisions of Russia#Municipal divisions|municipal division]], Zhukovsky City Under Oblast Jurisdiction is incorporated as '''Zhukovsky Urban Okrug'''.<ref name="Ref804">Law #171/2004-OZ</ref>

==Research and economy==
{{See also|List of aerospace flight test centres}}

Zhukovsky is a home to the [[Gromov Flight Research Institute|M.&nbsp;M.&nbsp;Gromov Flight Research Institute]] and [[Zhukovsky Central Aerohydrodynamic Institute|N.&nbsp;Ye.&nbsp;Zhukovsky Central Aerohydrodynamic Institute]] — they are two major facilities involved in testing and designing aircraft. These facilities were employers for a great portion of the city's population before [[perestroika]]. Also, there is situated the [[FALT|Aeromechanics faculty]] of [[Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology|MIPT]].

There are a number smaller, but also important [[company|enterprises]], such as:
*[[Tikhomirov Scientific Research Institute of Instrument Design]] ({{lang|ru|НИИП}}, NIIP),
*[[:ru:Научно-исследовательский институт авиационного оборудования|Scientific Research Institute of Avionics]] ({{lang|ru|НИИАО}}, NIIAO),
*[[:ru:Московский научно-исследовательский институт «Агат»|Agat Moscow Scientific Research Institute]] ({{lang|ru|Агат}}, Agate),
*[[:ru:Экспериментальный машиностроительный завод имени В. М. Мясищева|Myasishchev Experimental Machine-building Plant]]  ({{lang|ru|ЭМЗ}}, EMZ).

The airline [[Aviastar-TU]] has its head office in Zhukovsky.<ref>"[http://www.aviastartu.ru/en/contacts/ Contact information] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160703075703/http://www.aviastartu.ru/en/contacts/ |date=July 3, 2016 }}." There are also a number of flight testing divisions of the aviation [[design bureau]]s ([[Sukhoy]], [[Ilyushin]], [[Tupolev]], etc.) located in the area as well as machine-building plants, paper-mill food industry - Inko, [[Nestlé]], local bread-baking plant.

In 2008, [[President of Russia|President]] [[Vladimir Putin]] signed a [[Decree of the President of Russia|presidential decree]] establishing the National Aircraft Industry Centre.<ref>[http://www.kommersant.com/p-12090/Zhukovsky_aircraft/ Kommersant-Putin Inked Ruling to Create National Aircraft Construction Center in Zhukovsky] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110606090049/http://www.kommersant.com/p-12090/Zhukovsky_aircraft/ |date=June 6, 2011 }}</ref>

==Sports==
Zhukovsky is the center of [[track and field]] athletics in Moscow Oblast. Most notable athletes born in Zhukovsky are [[Yuriy Borzakovskiy]], Yekaterina Podkopayeva, Andrey Yepishin, Dmitry Bogdanov, and others. In 2005, the [[Meteor Stadium (Zhukovsky)|Meteor]] international standard [[athletics stadium]] was opened.

There was also a [[bandy]] club Strela based in Zhukovsky.
