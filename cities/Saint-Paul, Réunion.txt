'''Saint-Paul''' ({{IPA|fr|sɛ̃ pɔl|-|LL-Q150 (fra)-Jules78120-Saint-Paul.wav}}) is the second-largest [[Commune in France|commune]] in the [[France|French]] [[Département d'outre-mer|overseas department]] of [[Réunion]]. It is located on the extreme west side of the island of Réunion.

Until 1999, near Saint Paul there was the {{convert|428|m|adj=on}} tall mast [[Omega Navigation System#Chabrier Omega Transmitter|OMEGA Chabrier transmitter]].

== Transport ==
Saint-Paul was to be the western terminus of the proposed [[Réunion Tram Train]]. However, the project was abandoned in May 2010 due to a lack of funds.

The traditional grave of French [[Piracy|pirate]] [[Olivier Levasseur]], nicknamed ''La Buse'' ("The Buzzard") or ''La Bouche'' ("The Mouth"), who was most famous for allegedly hiding one of the biggest [[Buried treasure|treasures]] in pirate history, estimated at over [[£]]1 billion, is located in Saint-Paul's [[Cimetière marin de Saint-Paul]] cemetery. Besides pirates, the cemetery also serves as the permanent resting place of poets [[Leconte de Lisle]] and [[Eugène Dayot]], as well as the painter [[Arthur Grimaud]].

=== Climate ===
Saint-Paul has a [[tropical savanna climate]] (Köppen ''Aw''), closely bordering on a [[hot semi-arid climate]] (''BSh''). The average annual temperature in Saint-Paul is {{cvt|25.3|C}}. The average annual rainfall is {{cvt|482.3|mm}} with January as the wettest month. The temperatures are highest on average in February, at around {{cvt|28.2|C}}, and lowest in July, at around {{cvt|22.3|C}}. The highest temperature ever recorded in Saint-Paul was {{cvt|37.0|C}} on 25 January 2019; the coldest temperature ever recorded was {{cvt|15.1|C}} on 14 August 1991.
