'''Central Hungary''' ({{langx|hu|Közép-Magyarország}} {{IPA-hu|ˈkøzeːp ˈmɒɟɒrorsaːɡ|pron}}) is one of the eight statistical regions in [[Hungary]] ([[Nomenclature of Territorial Units for Statistics|NUTS 1]] and NUTS 2). It includes [[{{Hungarian county name|Pest}}]] and since 2018 no longer includes [[{{Hungarian county name|Budapest}}]], the capital of the region.

==Tourism==
Central Hungary is part of the [[Budapest Central Transdanubia Tourist Region]].

===Budapest===

In 1987 a [[World Heritage Site]] was declared which includes [[Buda Castle]], the [[Danube]] Riverbank, the [[Andrássy út|Andrássy Avenue]] and its historic surroundings, the [[Line 1 (Budapest Metro)|Millennium Underground Railway]] and [[Hősök tere|Heroes' Square]].

Other important landmarks in [[Buda]] are the [[Gellért Hill]] and the tomb of [[Gül Baba (poet)|Gül Baba]] and [[Rudas Baths]] built during the [[Ottoman Hungary|Ottoman rule of Hungary]], ruins of [[Óbuda|Old Buda]], the Coliseum in Nagyszombat Street and the ruins of [[Aquincum]]. In the [[Buda hills|Buda Hills]] are the [[Chairlift]], the [[Children's railway]] and [[cave]]s with [[stalagmite]]s and [[stalactite]]s.

The most important landmarks in [[Pest, Hungary|Pest]] are the [[Hungarian Parliament Building]], the [[St. Stephen's Basilica]], the [[Inner City (Budapest)|Inner City]] Parish Church, the [[Hungarian Academy of Sciences]], the [[Vigadó Concert Hall]], the [[Hungarian National Museum]], the [[New York Palace (Budapest)|New York Palace]] on the [[Small Boulevard (Budapest)|Small Boulevard]], the [[Dohány Street Synagogue]], the [[Grand Boulevard (Budapest)|Grand Boulevard]], and the [[Museum of Applied Arts (Budapest)|Museum of Applied Arts]].

Other important displays are at the [[Museum of Fine Arts (Budapest)|Museum of Fine Arts]], the [[Hungarian National Gallery]],  the [[Ethnographic Museum (Budapest)|Ethnographic Museum]], the [[Budapest History Museum]] and [[Statue Park]].

Important landmarks are Budapest's oldest bridges, such as the Széchenyi Chain Bridge, the [[Margaret Bridge]], the [[Liberty Bridge (Budapest)|Liberty Bridge]]. The biggest parks are very popular, especially the [[City Park (Budapest)|City Park]] with [[Vajdahunyad Castle]], the [[Széchenyi thermal bath]], the [[Budapest Zoo & Botanical Garden|Budapest Zoo and Botanical Garden]] and the [[Margaret Island]]. Budapest is world-famous for its hot [[spa]]s too.

Budapest holds many perennial events, for example Budapest Fair, Fireworks and [[Budapest Parade]].

==={{Hungarian county name|Pest}}===

Places of interest in [[{{Hungarian county name|Pest}}]] are [[Gödöllő]]  (Royal Castle and [[Arboretum]]), [[Ráckeve]] ([[Serbian Orthodox Church|Serbian]] cathedral and [[Savoya Castle]]), [[Szentendre]] ([[Baroque architecture|Baroque]] town square, [[Margit Kovács]] Museum, Ethnographic Open Air Museum), [[Vác]] (cathedral, [[triumphal arch]]) and [[Visegrád]] (Visegrád Castle).

Other landmarks are the church (built in the [[Middle Ages]]) in [[Zsámbék]], the [[Reformed churches|Reformed church]] of the [[Holy Roman Empire]], the church in [[Fót]] in the [[Romanticism|Romantic style]], [[Apaj]] plain, ancient [[juniper]] fields in [[Tatárszentgyörgy]],  [[equestrianism]] in [[Pusztavacs]], [[Attila the Hun|Attila]] Rise in [[Tápiószentmárton]], the arboretum in [[Vácrátót]] and the bear sanctuary in [[Veresegyház]].

The [[Pilis Mountains]] offer plenty of opportunities for [[hiking]]. There are also the [[Visegrád Hills]], [[Ördögmalom]] [[waterfall]], [[Gödöllő National Park]] and [[Ócsa National Park]].
