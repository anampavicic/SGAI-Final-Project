'''Loikaw''' ({{MYname|MY=လွိုင်ကော်မြို့|MLCTS=lwuing kau mrui.}}, {{IPA-my|lwàɪ(ŋ)kɔ̀ mjo̰|pron}}) is the capital of [[Kayah State]], also known as Karenni State, in [[Myanmar]]. It is located in the [[Karen Hills]] area, near the State's northern tip, just above an embayment on the [[Pilu River]].<ref>Bitsch, Jørgen (1966) ''Why Buddha Smiles'' Taplinger Publishing, New York, pages 55–56, {{OCLC|421824}}</ref> The inhabitants are mostly [[Kayah people|Kayah]] ([[Karenni people|Karenni]]).  Myanmar's largest [[hydropower]] plant (built by the Japanese as [[war reparation]]) is located about {{cvt|20|km}} east of Loikaw at [[Lawpita Falls]].

The town of Loikaw comprises 13 [[Wards of Myanmar|urban wards]], namely Naungya, Daw-ukhu, Mainglon, Mingala, Dhammayon, Zaypaing, Shwetaung, Landama, Dawtanma, Dawnoeku, Shansu, and Minsu wards.<ref name=":0" />

==History==
[[image:Loikaw.jpg|thumb|left|345px|Landscape of Loikaw in 1922. The bridges appear flimsy, but were substantial enough that elephants could walk over them.]] 
Loikaw was the Headquarters of the Political Officer in Charge of the [[Karenni States]], part of the [[Princely States]] of British Burma, in 1922 during [[British rule in Burma]]. The town was located in the only flat part of the Karenni area. In 1892 it numbered four huts. As an [[Agencies of British India|Agent of the British government]] he was exercising control over the local Karenni Rulers, being supervised by the Superintendent at [[Taunggyi]].<ref name="IGI">[https://dsal.uchicago.edu/reference/gazetteer/pager.html?objectid=DS405.1.I34_V15_042.gif Imperial Gazetteer of India, v. 15, p. 36.]</ref> The headquarters of the [[American Baptist Mission]] to Hill Karens was also located in Loikaw.

As with the rest of Kayah State, outsider access to Loikaw has been restricted post-independence, with special permits required to secure entry.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2016-10-08|title=A guide to Loikaw, Kayah State (Myanmar) – Against the Compass|url=https://againstthecompass.com/en/loikaw-kayah-state-best-place-myanmar/|access-date=2022-01-14|language=en-US}}</ref> Since the 2021 coup, Loikaw has been the scene of intense fighting between the Burmese military and ethnic armed groups opposed to military control, forcing many of the city's residents to flee.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2022-01-14|title=Burmese flee bombardment as junta 'makes example' of city of Loikaw|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2022/jan/14/burmese-flee-bombardment-as-junta-makes-example-of-city-of-loikaw|access-date=2022-01-14|website=the Guardian|language=en}}</ref> On 7 November 2023, the [[Karenni National People's Liberation Front]], [[Karenni Army]], and [[Karenni Nationalities Defence Force]] launched a military offensive known as [[Operation 1107]] against the ruling [[State Administration Council]] junta. An additional offensive, known as [[Operation 1111]], started on 11 November 2023 with the aim of capturing Loikaw.<ref>{{cite news |title=ကရင်နီဒေသ ၁၁၀၇ စစ်ဆင်ရေး တိုက်ပွဲ ဆင်နွှဲ |url=https://www.bbc.com/burmese/articles/cyx2379qdz9o |access-date=10 November 2023 |work=[[BBC News]] မြန်မာ |date=9 November 2023 |language=my |archive-date=10 November 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231110124854/https://www.bbc.com/burmese/articles/cyx2379qdz9o |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=ကရင်နီဒေသ ၁၁၀၇ စစ်ဆင်ရေး စတင် |url=https://burmese.voanews.com/a/7346374.html |access-date=10 November 2023 |work=ဗွီအိုအေ |date=8 November 2023 |language=my|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20231110130401/https://burmese.voanews.com/a/7346374.html|archive-date=November 10, 2023}}</ref> By 18 December, anti-junta forces were reported to have control of 85% of the city.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://myanmar-now.org/en/news/resistance-forces-claim-control-of-85-percent-of-karenni-state-capital/ | title=Resistance forces claim control of '85 percent' of Karenni State capital | date=19 December 2023 }}</ref>

== Transport ==
[[File:Thiri Mingalar Taung Kwe Pagoda.jpg|thumb|370px|View of Taung Kwe Pagoda.]]

=== Air ===
[[Loikaw Airport|Loikaw]] is linked to [[Yangon International Airport|Yangon]] and [[Chanmyathazi Airport|Mandalay]] by air.
[[File:Loikaw Airport.jpg|thumb|Loikaw Airport]]

=== Rail ===
Loikaw is linked by the newly constructed [[Aungban|Aungpan]]-[[Pinlaung]]-Loikaw rail line.
[[File:Loikaw Station.jpg|thumb|Loikaw Station]]

=== Buses ===
There are express buses from Loikaw to Yangon, Mandalay and other towns.

== Education ==

The town is home to [[Loikaw University]], [[Technological University, Loikaw]], [[Computer University, Loikaw]]. Loikaw Education College

== Health care ==
Loikaw General Hospital serves not only locals in the state but also to those in the southern part of neighbouring [[Shan State]]. The existing buildings of the hospital were built in 1964. Due to increase demands, new two two-storey buildings were constructed with the 1.945 billion yen funding from [[Japan International Cooperation Agency|Japan International Cooperation Agency (JICA)]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.globalnewlightofmyanmar.com/loikaw-to-get-new-hospital-buildings-funded-by-jica/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150212121915/http://globalnewlightofmyanmar.com/loikaw-to-get-new-hospital-buildings-funded-by-jica/|url-status=usurped|archive-date=February 12, 2015|title=Loikaw to get new hospital buildings funded by JICA – Global New Light Of Myanmar|date=2015-02-11|work=Global New Light Of Myanmar|access-date=2017-10-08|language=en-US}}</ref>[[File:Naung Yah Lake.jpg|thumb|Naung Yah Lake]]
