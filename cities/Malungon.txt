'''Malungon''', officially the '''Municipality of Malungon''' ({{lang-ceb|Lungsod sa Malungon}}; {{lang-mdh|Inged nu Malungun}}, [[Jawi Alphabet|Jawi]]: ايڠد نوملوڠون; {{lang-tl|Bayan ng Malungon}}), is a 1st class [[municipality of the Philippines|municipality]] in the [[Philippine Province|province]] of Sarangani,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Official Website of the Provincial Government of Sarangani - Malungon|url=https://sarangani.gov.ph/index.php/features/secondistrict/malungon|access-date=2021-12-14|website=sarangani.gov.ph}}</ref> [[Philippines]]. According to the 2020 census, it has a population of 105,465 people.{{PH census|current}}

The only landlocked municipality in Sarangani, Malungon lies in the north-eastern part of the province. It is bounded on the west by [[Tupi, South Cotabato]], on the north by the province of [[Davao del Sur]], east by [[Malita, Davao Occidental|Malita]], capital of the newly created province of [[Davao Occidental]], on the south by [[Alabel, Sarangani|Alabel]] (the provincial capital) and [[General Santos]], and on the south-west by [[Polomolok, South Cotabato]].

==Economy==
{{PH poverty incidence}}
Malungon's economy is largely based on agriculture with a high level production of dried coconut meat. Animal husbandry is the second biggest income earner, notably cattle farming. Other agricultural products are coconuts, maize, sugarcane, bananas, pineapples, mangoes, pork, eggs, beef, and fish.

The economy has accelerated in the past decade driven by advances in global communication technology and the finishing of a modern highway that tremendously improved trade and transport.
