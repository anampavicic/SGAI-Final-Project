'''Bokaro''', officially known as '''Bokaro Steel City''' ({{Audio|Bokaro.ogg|pronunciation}}), is a large and [[Planned community|planned]] city in [[Jharkhand]], [[India]].<ref name=":0" /> It is the [[List of cities in Jharkhand by population|fourth most populous city in the state]]<ref name="UA"/> and one of the first planned cities of India.<ref>{{Cite web|title=About Bokaro, Introduction on Bokaro, All About Bokaro District|url=https://www.bokaroonline.in/city-guide/about-bokaro|access-date=17 January 2022|website=www.bokaroonline.in}}</ref> Bokaro is the administrative headquarters of [[Bokaro district]].<ref name="bokaro.nic.in">{{Cite web|title=History {{!}} District Bokaro, Government of Jharkhand {{!}} India|url=https://bokaro.nic.in/history/|access-date=17 January 2022}}</ref> The city is located on the banks of Garga River and on the fringes of [[Bokaro River|Bokaro river]] and is surrounded by hill ranges at [[Giridih]] and [[Ramgarh district]]s.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=Geography of Bokaro, Climate and Demography of Bokaro|url=https://www.bokaroonline.in/city-guide/geography-of-bokaro|website=www.bokaroonline.in|access-date=17 January 2022}}</ref> It is one of the most peaceful cities in India.<ref>{{Cite web|date=17 April 2016|title=Bokaro, Hazaribagh peaceful, a day after communal clashes on Ram Navmi|url=https://www.hindustantimes.com/ranchi/bokaro-hazaribagh-peaceful-a-day-after-communal-clashes-on-ram-navmi/story-bQzQHOpaEP0LekqlIgP3gM.html|access-date=17 January 2022|website=Hindustan Times|language=en}}</ref> As per 2011 census, the city's population was 563,417 and 1,100,000 of its metropolitan area.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|date=9 July 2017|title=Story of my hometown bokaro steel city|url=https://gouritravelblogindia.wordpress.com/2017/07/09/story-of-my-hometown-bokaro-steel-city/|access-date=17 January 2022|website=Every Corner a Story|language=en}}</ref> The city span across 183&nbsp;km in geographic area.<ref name=":1" />

Marafari is the oldest settlement in Bokaro which was just a village during the time of Mughals and Sultanates.<ref name="bokaro.nic.in"/> The nearby cities of [[Purulia]] was ruled by Maharaja of Kashipur.<ref name=":2" /> Manbhum region also covered Bokaro which was ruled by Mughal and Sultanates. After independence, it became a part of [[Bihar]] state. Bokaro Steel City is the headquarters of the [[Bokaro District]] as well as Koylanchal range ([[Bokaro district|Bokaro]], [[Dhanbad]] and [[Giridih]]) and is also one of the headquarters of the Police IG zone, apart from [[Ranchi]] and [[Dumka]], covering seven districts- [[Hazaribagh district|Hazaribagh]], [[Dhanbad district|Dhanbad]], [[Giridih district|Giridih]], [[Koderma district|Koderma]], [[Chatra district|Chatra]], [[Bokaro district|Bokaro]], and [[Ramgarh district|Ramgarh]] ([[North Chotanagpur Division]]).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.jhpolice.gov.in/organization-chart|title=Organization Chart &#124; Department of Police, State Government of Jharkhand, India|website=www.jhpolice.gov.in|access-date=29 December 2019|archive-date=16 August 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190816162408/https://jhpolice.gov.in/organization-chart|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Bokaro has emerged as a commercial, industrial, healthcare, sports, educational and startup hub. It is India's first global active city (GAC). [[Private property]] is absent in the city, with all property being [[Public housing|public-owned]]. Despite this, the city has a cosmopolitan culture with people belonging to different ethnicity from [[South India]] as well as [[North India]], representing its unique attributes, giving the city the character of Mini India while having heavy an influence of [[Bihari culture|Bihari]] and [[Culture of Bengal|Bengali]] culture.<ref>{{cite web |title=Lifestyle in Bokaro |url=https://www.bokaroonline.in/guide/lifestyle-in-bokaro |website=Bokaro Online |access-date=17 August 2024}}</ref> The city hosts tourist spots such as [[Jawaharlal Nehru Biological Park]], [[Pindrajora]], [[Tenughat Dam]] etc. It has multiple big corporations operating such as [[Steel Authority of India]], [[Bharat Coking Coal|Bharat Coking Coal Limited]], [[Bharat Petroleum Corporation Limited]], [[Damodar Valley Corporation]], [[Oil and Natural Gas Corporation]], [[Inox Air Products|INOX Group]], [[Jaypee Group|Jaypee Cement]], [[Orica]], [[Vedanta Resources]], [[Dalmia Group|Dalmia Cement]], [[Electrosteel Castings]] and various other industries. It lies on the mineral-rich [[Chota Nagpur Plateau#Mineral resources|Chota Nagpur Plateau]] and a Coal Bed Methane(Gas) reserves of 45 billion cubic metres.<ref>{{cite news |title=ONGC, partners to invest Rs 823 crore for gas in Bokaro block |url=https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/industry/energy/oil-gas/ongc-partners-to-invest-rs-823-crore-for-gas-in-bokaro-block/articleshow/53695330.cms |access-date=22 September 2023 |work=The Economic Times |agency=PTI |date=14 August 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite book |last1=History |first1=Kenneth Pletcher Senior Editor, Geography and |title=The Geography of India: Sacred and Historic Places |date=15 August 2010 |publisher=The Rosen Publishing Group, Inc |isbn=978-1-61530-142-3 |page=28 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Mjr0X-8jrLAC&dq=mineral+rich+chhota+nagpur+plateau&pg=PA28 |language=en}}</ref> It has one of the most important coalfields of India in nearby areas which are [[Jharia]], [[Ramnagar, Kulti|Kulti]], [[West Bokaro Coalfield|West Bokaro]], [[East Bokaro Coalfield|East Bokaro]] and [[Ramgarh Coalfield|Ramgarh]].

It has an average elevation of {{convert|200 to 540|m}} above mean sea level. The highest hill, Lugu Pahar, rises to a height of {{convert|1070| m}}. The [[East Bokaro Coalfield]] located in the [[Bermo]]-[[Phusro]] area and small intrusions of [[Jharia Coalfield]] make Bokaro a coal rich district.  In 1965, one of the largest steel manufacturing units in the country, [[Bokaro Steel Plant]], operated by [[Steel Authority of India Limited]], was set-up at Bokaro Steel City. The [[Damodar Valley Corporation]] established its first [[thermal power station]] at [[Bokaro (Thermal)]]. The {{convert|5|km}} long, {{convert|55|m}} high earthfill dam with composite masonry cum concrete spillway, [[Tenughat Dam]], across the Damodar River, is operated by the Government of Jharkhand. The average annual rainfall is {{convert|1291.2|mm}}. The soil is generally infertile and agriculture is mostly rain-fed.<ref>{{cite web|url = https://censusindia.gov.in/2011census/dchb/DCHB_A/20/2010_PART_A_DCHB_BOKARO.pdf | title =  District Census Handbook Bokaro 2011 |work=  Physical features, Mines and minerals, Indistires, p 7-8 |publisher = Directorate of Census Operations  | access-date = 30 May 2021 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url =http://www.india9.com/i9show/Tenughat-Dam-49898.htm  | title = Tenughat Dam  |publisher =india9   | access-date = 30 May 2021 }}</ref>

Bokaro Steel City is located at {{coord|23.67|N|86.15|E|display=inline}}. The city stands at an elevation of {{convert|210|m|abbr=off}} [[above sea level]] and has an urban area of {{convert|183|km2||lk=on|abbr=off}}. Bounded on the east by [[Dhanbad]] and [[Purulia]], on the west by [[Ramgarh district|Ramgarh]] and [[Hazaribagh]], on the north by [[Giridih]] and on the south by [[Ranchi]]. It is accessible through National Highway [[NH 23|NH 320]] & [[National Highway 18 (India)|NH-18]]. The city has the total area of 183&nbsp;km. It includes the suburban area of Chas Municipal Corporation. There are many national highways crossing the city of Bokaro. Each sector has shopping centres, playgrounds, schools, recreational areas and health centres as entire city is planned with avenues and residential areas as well as commercial areas. Bokaro was one of the first planned cities in the state of [[Jharkhand]].<ref>{{cite web|date=22 August 2016|title=Bokaro|url=http://bokaro.jharkhand.org.in/2009/05/about-bokaro-steel-city.html|publisher=jharkhand.org}}</ref>

According to the [[2011 Census of India]], Bokaro Steel City is the 86th largest urban agglomeration in India, and the 4th largest city in Jharkhand.<ref name="UA">{{cite web|title=View population details|url=http://www.censusindia.gov.in/PopulationFinder/View_Village_Population.aspx?pcaid=6162&category=U.A.|access-date=14 December 2015|publisher=Registrar General and Census Commissioner, India}}</ref>

According to the [[2011 Census of India]], Bokaro Steel City Urban Agglomeration had a total population of 563,417, of which males were 299,232 and females 264,185.<ref name="census2-2011">{{cite web|title=Provisional population totals, Census of India 2011|url=http://www.censusindia.gov.in/2011-prov-results/paper2/data_files/India2/Table_3_PR_UA_Citiees_1Lakh_and_Above.pdf|access-date=14 December 2015|work=Urban Agglomeration – Cities having population 1 lakh and above|publisher=Government of India}}</ref> Bokaro Steel City Urban Agglomeration is composed of Bokaro Steel City ([[Census Town]]), Chas ([[Nagar Nigam]]) and [[Bandhgora]] (CT).<ref name="Provisional">{{cite web|title=Provisional population totals, Census of India 2011|url=http://censusindia.gov.in/2011-prov-results/paper2/data_files/India2/Table_4_PR_UAs_1Lakh_and_Above_Appendix.pdf|access-date=14 December 2015|work=Constituents of Urban Agglomerations having population 1 lakh and above, Census 2011|publisher=Government of India}}</ref> The UA had an effective literacy rate (7+ population) of 84.87%, with male literacy of 92.27% and female literacy of 76.50%.<ref name="census2-2011" /> A new metropolitan area of Bokaro has been planned named Greater Bokaro and population is expected to be around 1,100,000.<ref name=":2" /><ref>{{Cite web|date=10 August 2016|title=Residents back Greater Bokaro vision |url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/jamshedpur/residents-back-greater-bokaro-vision/articleshow/53627157.cms|access-date=18 January 2022|website=The Times of India|language=en}}</ref>

Bokaro Steel City had a population of 413,934, of which males were 220,088 and females were 193,846. Population in the age range of 0-6 was 48,834. The effective literacy rate (7+ population) was 84.94%, with male literacy of 92.35% and female literacy of 76.54%.<ref name="CT_2011">{{cite web|title=Cities having population 1 lakh and above, Census 2011|url=https://www.censusindia.gov.in/2011-prov-results/paper2/data_files/India2/Table_2_PR_Cities_1Lakh_and_Above.pdf|access-date=2 September 2020|website=census.gov.in}}</ref>

The major languages spoken are [[Hindi]],  [[Khortha language|Khortha]],  [[Urdu]], [[Bengali language|Bengali]], [[Santali language|Santhali]] and [[Maithili language|Maithili]] with [[Hindi]] and [[Santali language|Santali]] have official status and [[Urdu]] as second official language.<ref name="lang">{{cite web|title=C-16 Population By Mother Tongue - Town level|url=https://censusindia.gov.in/2011census/C-16T/DDW-C16-TOWN-STMT-MDDS-2000.XLSX|access-date=2 September 2020|website=census.gov.in}}</ref> A ''Russian colony'' was located in the city housing [[Soviet]] communities.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mrs Kasalova's perestroika|url=https://www.telegraphindia.com/culture/heritage/mrs-kasalovas-perestroika-a-steel-city-the-iron-curtain-and-the-russians-within-a-dancing-girl-a-truant-boy-and-a-broken-betrothal/cid/1793126|access-date=18 January 2022|website=www.telegraphindia.com}}</ref> Kannada, Tamil, Malayalam and Telugu speaking and various other South Indian communities also settled in the city.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Soumya |first1=Savvy |title=Tamils dabble with modern culture |url=https://www.telegraphindia.com/jharkhand/tamils-dabble-with-modern-culture/cid/877930 |access-date=28 June 2022 |work=The Telegraph |date=15 May 2005}}</ref>

==Infrastructure==
According to the ''District Census Handbook 2011, Bokaro'', Bokaro Steel City  covered an area of 162.91&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>. Among the civic amenities, it had 415&nbsp;km roads with both open and closed drains, the protected water supply involved tapwater from treated and untreated sources, overhead tank, service reservoir. It had 67,083 domestic electric connections, 10,283 road lighting points. Among the medical facilities, it had 9 hospitals (with 1,200 beds), 2 dispensaries, 2 health centres, 1 family welfare centre, 1 maternity and child welfare centre, 1 maternity home, 5 nursing homes (with 120 beds), 30 medicine shops. It had 1 polytechnic, 2 recognised shorthand, typewriting and vocational training centres, 1 non-formal educational centre (Sarva Siksha Abhiyan). Among the social, recreational and cultural facilities it had 2 stadiums, 3 cinema theatres, 8 auditorium/ community halls, 9 public libraries, 9 reading rooms. An important commodity it produced was steel products. It had the branch offices of 30 nationalised banks, 12 private commercial banks, 1 cooperative bank.<ref name=":0">{{cite web | url = https://censusindia.gov.in/2011census/dchb/DCHB_A/20/2010_PART_A_DCHB_BOKARO.pdf| title =  District Census Handbook Bokaro, Census of India 2011, Series 20, Part XII A | work= Pages 384-397| publisher = Directorate of census Operations, Jharkhand  |accessdate = 2 March 2021}}</ref>

==Economy==
{{Main|Economy of Bokaro}}
[[File:Bokaro Sector 4 G.jpg|thumb|Sector 4G, part of Planned SAIL Township]]
[[File:Cooling_pond_-_Bokaro_Steel_Plant.jpg|thumb|Cooling pond 2 in Sector 4]]
[[File:BSL-SAIL.jpg|thumb|Bokaro Steel Plant main gate, commonly known as Steel Gate ay [[Bokaro Steel Plant]] - [[Steel Authority of India|SAIL]]]]
[[File:ONGC, Bokaro Steel City.JPG|thumb|[[Oil and Natural Gas Corporation|Oil and Natural Gas Corporation (ONGC)]] at [[Marafari]]]]
The economy of the city is primarily dependent on the integrated steel plant established by [[Steel Authority of India]]. The [[Bokaro Steel Plant]] was established with the collaboration of [[Soviet Union]] when the First Prime Minister Jawahar Lal Nehru desired to establish a steel plant in the region. Bokaro Steel Plant expansion to extend its capacity from 4.5 million metric tons to 7.5 million metric tonnes in 2010-2011.<ref>{{cite news |agency=PTI |title=SAIL to add 7MT of additional steel capacity at Bokaro: Paswan |url=https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/industry/indl-goods/svs/steel/sail-to-add-7mt-of-additional-steel-capacity-at-bokaro-paswan/articleshow/3527739.cms |access-date=14 August 2022 |work=The Economic Times |date=25 September 2008}}</ref>

[[Vedanta]] Electrosteel Castings Limited - A Kolkata-based water pipe manufacturer acquired {{convert|2500|acre|km2|lk=in|abbr=off}} of land {{convert|18|km|abbr=off}} from the city and has erected its 2.2 MTPA steel plant. The company has invested close to Rs 80 Bn (US$1.6 Bn) on this project which was operational from 2010.<ref>{{cite web|date=3 July 2007|title=Electrosteel in cash chase for Bokaro unit|url=http://www.telegraphindia.com/1070703/asp/business/story_8008216.asp|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080206114938/http://www.telegraphindia.com/1070703/asp/business/story_8008216.asp|url-status=dead|archive-date=6 February 2008|access-date=27 November 2013|work=The Telegraph|location=Kolkota}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|date=23 July 2009|title=Electrosteel-Bokaro|url=http://economictimes.indiatimes.com/News/News-By-Industry/Indl-Goods-Svs/Electrosteel-to-start-production-next-year/articleshow/4813053.cms|access-date=27 November 2013|work=The Economic Times}}</ref>

[[ONGC |ONGC Bokaro]] operates the Bokaro Coal Bed Methane (CBM) block BK-CBM-2001/1 with 80 per cent stake while the remaining 20 per cent is with Indian Oil Corp (IOC). It plans to invest ₨ 8.23&nbsp;billion from 2017 to 2018 to achieve a peak production of 0.9&nbsp;million standard cubic meters per day.<ref>{{cite news |agency=PTI |title=ONGC, partners to invest Rs 823 crore for gas in Bokaro block |url=https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/industry/energy/oil-gas/ongc-partners-to-invest-rs-823-crore-for-gas-in-bokaro-block/articleshow/53695330.cms |access-date=14 August 2022 |work=The Economic Times |date=14 August 2016}}</ref> [[Dalmia Group|Dalmia Cement Bharat Limited (DCBL)]] operates a 1.5 Million Tonne cement plant at Bokaro utilising slag supply from Bokaro Steel Plant.<ref>{{cite news |agency=IANS |title=Dalmia Cement fully acquires Bokaro Jaypee Cement plant |url=https://www.business-standard.com/article/news-ians/dalmia-cement-fully-acquires-bokaro-jaypee-cement-plant-114120400891_1.html |access-date=14 August 2022 |work=Business Standard India |date=4 December 2014}}</ref>

[[Inox Air Products]], India’s largest manufacturers of industrial and medical gases, has a Cryogenic Air Separation Unit (ASU) at the Steel Authority of India’s (SAIL) Bokaro plant.<ref>{{cite news |title=INOX to invest Rs 750 crore to set up Oxygen plant at SAIL Bokaro - ET EnergyWorld |url=https://energy.economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/oil-and-gas/inox-to-invest-rs-750-crore-to-set-up-oxygen-plant-at-sail-bokaro/90214283 |work=Economic Times |date=15 March 2022 |access-date=5 March 2024}}</ref>

==Transport==
===Air===
[[File:Bokaro Airport.jpg|thumb|[[Bokaro Airport]] Runway]]
The nearest commercial airport is at [[Ranchi]], named [[Birsa Munda Airport]], at a distance of 120&nbsp;km.
[[Bokaro Airport]] is an unserviced airport with no scheduled commercial flights. However, the [[Government of India]] has planned to connect Bokaro to the regional hub of [[Patna]] and an international airport in [[Kolkata]] through the [[UDAN]] regional airport development scheme, opening the airport up could see commercial flights by 2023.<ref>{{cite news  |url=https://www.livemint.com/news/india/bokaro-airport-will-be-connected-with-patna-kolkata-through-udan-flights-aai-11607070195906.html |title=Bokaro airport will be connected with Patna, Kolkata through Udan flights: AAI |work=Mint |date=4 December 2020 |access-date=18 July 2021}}</ref>

[[SpiceJet]] airline has been awarded under the second phase of the UDAN Scheme to cater unserved market of Bokaro.SpiceJet starts direct flight between Kolkata and Lilabari under UDAN scheme(however the airport is still not operable in 2024 )<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/industry/transportation/airlines-/-aviation/spicejet-starts-direct-flight-between-kolkata-and-lilabari-under-udan-scheme/articleshow/67261567.cms|title=SpiceJet starts direct flight between Kolkata and Lilabari under UDAN scheme|date=26 December 2018|work=The Economic Times|access-date=27 December 2018}}</ref>

===Railway===
[[File:Bokaro Steel City Railway Station Entrance.jpg|thumb|Bokaro Steel City Railway Station Entrance]]
[[Bokaro Steel City railway station]] lies at the edge of the Jharia Coalfield and serves the residents of Bokaro and the surrounding mining-industrial area. It is a A-category railway station, with amenities including escalators,<ref>{{cite news |last1=Choubey |first1=Pradhuman |title=Moving stairs at Bokaro station |url=https://www.telegraphindia.com/jharkhand/moving-stairs-at-bokaro-station/cid/1352958 |access-date=14 August 2022 |work=The Telegraph |date=24 May 2017}}</ref> A.C waiting rooms, a food court, charging points, a foot over-bridge, and computerised ticket reservation counters.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.telegraphindia.com/1160817/jsp/jharkhand/story_102736.jsp|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160822052846/http://www.telegraphindia.com/1160817/jsp/jharkhand/story_102736.jsp|url-status=dead|archive-date=22 August 2016|title=Bokaro eyes model station tag|date=17 August 2016|publisher=The Telegraph|author=Shashank Shekhar|access-date=21 September 2016}}</ref> The railway station is operated by the [[South Eastern Railway zone|South Eastern Railway]] of the [[Indian Railways]], and provides connectivity to neighbouring states and major metropolitan areas such as [[Delhi]], [[Kolkata]], [[Mumbai]] and [[Chennai]].
