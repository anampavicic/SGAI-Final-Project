'''Shchyolkovo''' ({{lang-rus|Щёлково|p=ˈɕːɵlkəvə}}) is a [[types of inhabited localities in Russia|city]] and the [[administrative center]] of [[Shchyolkovsky District]] in [[Moscow Oblast]], [[Russia]], located on the [[Klyazma River]] ([[Oka River|Oka]]'s [[tributary]]), {{convert|20|km|sp=us}} northeast of [[Moscow]]. Population: {{ru-census2021|134,211|;}} {{ru-census2010|110,411|;}} 112,865 ([[Russian Census (2002)|2002 Census]]);<ref name="2002Census">{{ru-pop-ref|2002Census}}</ref> 109,255 ([[Soviet Census (1989)|1989 Census]]);<ref name="1989Census">{{ru-pop-ref|1989Census}}</ref> 91,000 (1977).

==History==
It dates back to the 16th century.{{citation needed|date=May 2010}} Town status was granted to it in 1925.{{citation needed|date=May 2010}}

==Administrative and municipal status==
Within the [[subdivisions of Russia#Administrative divisions|framework of administrative divisions]], Shchyolkovo serves as the [[administrative center]] of [[Shchyolkovsky District]].<ref name="MoscowO_admlist">Resolution #123-PG</ref> As an administrative division, it is, together with seven [[types of inhabited localities in Russia|rural localities]], incorporated within Shchyolkovsky District as the '''[[Town of district significance|City]] of Shchyolkovo'''.<ref name="MoscowO_admlist" /> As a [[subdivisions of Russia#Municipal divisions|municipal division]], the City of Shchyolkovo is incorporated within Shchyolkovsky Municipal District as '''Shchyolkovo Urban Settlement'''.<ref name="MoscowOShchyolkovskyD_mun">Law #83/2005-OZ</ref>

==Transportation==
The city is reachable in about one hour by suburban trains from the [[Yaroslavsky railway station]] in [[Moscow]].
