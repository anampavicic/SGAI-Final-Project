'''Lagos de Moreno''' ({{IPA|es|ˈlaɣos ðe moˈɾeno|lang|Lagos de Moreno.ogg}}) is a city and [[Municipalities of Mexico|municipality]] in the [[Jalisco|State of Jalisco]], [[Mexico]]. Lagos is located in the region of [[Los Altos de Jalisco]], within the [[macroregion]] of the [[Bajío]], one of the most highly developed areas in [[Latin America]].<ref name=":4">{{cite web|url=http://t21.com.mx/opinion/bitacora/2013/08/16/bajio-nuevo-milagro-mexicano|title=Bajío, el nuevo milagro mexicano|first=Osiel|last=Cruz|date=16 August 2013}}</ref> Lagos de Moreno is occasionally known as the "Athens of Jalisco", owing to the numerous writers and poets who were born there.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hellotravel.com/mexico/lagos-de-moreno|title=Lagos de Moreno 2020, #52 places to visit in jalisco, top things to do, reviews, best tourist places to visit for 4 - 7 Days, photo gallery {{!}} HelloTravel Mexico|website=www.hellotravel.com|access-date=2020-04-03}}{{dead link|date=February 2021|fix-attempted=yes|comment=For Mexico destinations, hellotravel.com links appear to be very transient and thus unsuitable for use in citations unless one has manually archived the link.}}{{cbignore}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://laatenasdejalisco.blogspot.com/2011/06/lagos-de-moreno-la-atenas-de-jalisco.html|title=La Atenas de Jalisco: Lagos de Moreno: "La Atenas de Jalisco".|last=Luciérnaga|first=La|date=2011-06-25|website=La Atenas de Jalisco|access-date=2020-04-03}}</ref>

Lagos de Moreno was founded as Villa Santa Maria de los Lagos on March 31, 1563, by [[Hernando Martel|Don Hernando Martel]], an [[Andalusia]]n conquistador. The town was the economic and cultural center of a region that eventually attracted many ranchers. By 1600 there were over 20,000 cattle in the region. Santa Maria continued growing both physically and culturally, and by the 1800s, the town was elevated to a city and renamed to Lagos de Moreno in honor of [[Pedro Moreno (soldier)|Pedro Moreno]]. Lagos de Moreno was the site of the signing of several important political documents, the two identically named but unrelated ''Convenios de Lagos''. The arts flourished in the city during the latter half of the 19th century, but this literary boom decreased somewhat after the [[Mexican Revolution]], especially during the 1940s. Recently, Lagos de Moreno has become an important cultural and [[Tourism in Mexico|tourist destination]] thanks to its rich history and architecture, some of which still stands from the [[Spanish Golden Age|17th century]].

As of the 2020 census, the city had a population of 111,569, making it the [[List of largest Jalisco cities by population|6th largest city in the state of Jalisco]].<ref name="pop"/><ref name=":6">{{Cite web |last=INEGI |date=2016-01-01 |title=México en cifras |url=https://www.inegi.org.mx/app/areasgeograficas/ |access-date=2023-06-05 |website=www.inegi.org.mx |language=es}}</ref> The municipality had a population of 172,403 in 2020.<ref name="Citypopulation.de">[http://www.citypopulation.de/en/mexico/admin/jalisco/14053__lagos_de_moreno/ Citypopulation.de] Population of Lagos de Moreno municipality with localities</ref> It includes many other outlying small communities, the largest of which are Paso de Cuarenta (San Miguel de Cuarenta) and Los Azulitos.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://mexicanroutes.com/lagos-de-moreno/|title=Lagos de Moreno|website=mexicanroutes.com|access-date=2020-04-03}}</ref>

== Etymology ==
The conquistador [[Hernando Martel]] gave Lagos de Moreno its original name, Villa de Santa Maria de los Lagos (Town of Saint Mary of the Lakes). The town's name was derived from [[Mary, mother of Jesus|Saint Mary]], the townspeople's chosen patron saint, and the numerous lakes that used to be scattered across the valley. The town was renamed on April 9, 1829, as Lagos de Moreno in honor of Mexican independence hero [[Pedro Moreno (soldier)|Pedro Moreno]], who was born in the city.

==History==

=== Pre-hispanic era ===
The origins of Lagos de Moreno date to its founding as Pechichitlán or Teziziatlan by Ahnuvic VII in about 1028 BCE. Then the city served as a capital for the Chichimecatlalli fiefdom.<ref name=":0"/> In the 12th century, semi-nomadic [[Guachichil]]es occupied the valley and dominated an extensive area stretching from present-day [[San Juan de los Lagos]] to southern [[Coahuila]]. Before the [[Spanish conquest of the Aztec Empire|colonization of Mexico]] began, indigenous culture thrived in this area, with notable pre-Hispanic architecture being established at Rincón de Mesa, Sauceda, and El Maguey.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=http://www.elclima.com.mx/fundacion_e_historia_de_lagos_de_moreno.htm|title=Fundación e historia de Lagos de Moreno|website=www.elclima.com.mx|language=es|access-date=2020-04-03}}</ref>

=== Colonial era ===
After the fall of the Aztec Empire, explorers set out to explore the western frontier in the Bajio Region. The Spanish expansion in [[Nueva Galicia]] was slowed down by several tribes in the vicinity, such as the semi-nomadic [[Chichimeca]]s and the Guachichiles. To the east of Pechititan, the [[Guamare]]s inhabited the [[Guanajuato]] area, and to the west, the [[Zacateco]]s lived. The Zacateco [[caudillo]]s (chiefs), Xiconaqui and Custique, along with the Chichimecas, struck down multiple Spanish invasions from 1550 to 1590 in the well-known [[Chichimeca War]].

However, [[Encomienda|encomenderos]] gradually occupied the Bajio. The first Spaniard to build a hacienda in the ''Laguense'' valley was Don [[Diego de Ibarra]].<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=https://www.pueblosmexico.com.mx/lagos-de-moreno-pueblo-magico.html|title=Lagos de Moreno Pueblo Magico|last=Cana|first=Marco|website=www.pueblosmexico.com.mx|language=en|access-date=2020-04-03}}</ref> Even so, in 1551, a group of Chichimecas attacked his ranch (La Sauceda and San Antonio) and killed all his animals. The constant raids in the area made, for the time being, made settlement of the Lagos region impossible.<ref name=":2"/>

==== Founding of Lagos ====
[[File:Don Hernando de Martel.png|left|thumb|255x255px|[[Hernando Martel|Don Hernando Martel]] founded Villa Santa Maria de los Lagos in 1563. He and his family held considerable influence over the town in its first few decades.]]
After decades of constant combat and raids, the first Spanish authorities of the region were appointed; the priest of [[Teocaltiche]] and the Mayor of Teocaltiche had the region stretching from Teocaltiche to the Lagos valley as their jurisdiction. Later, the [[Royal Audiencia of Guadalajara|Royal Audiencia of Nueva Galicia]] ordered the Mayor of Teocaltiche, Don [[Hernando Martel]] from [[Seville]], to establish a town in the vicinity of Pechichitan. The new settlement was founded jointly with the first priest and vicar of the local parish, father [[Juan de Cuenca Virues]], who also participated in the official founding of the Real Comanja mine in 1561.<ref name=":2"/> As such, on March 31, 1563, ''Villa de Santa María de los Lagos'' was founded by Martel and Cuenca, along with around 100 Spanish noble families.<ref name=":0" />

'''Development'''

The residents of Santa Maria chose the [[Mary, mother of Jesus|Virgin Mary]] as the namesake of their town and [[Saint Sebastian]] as a minor patron saint. The settlement and its surroundings became quite appealing to ranchers; by 1600 around 20,000 head of cattle were active in the Lagos valley, facilitating the eventual creation of the [[Charreada|charreria]]-style rodeo.<ref name=":3" /> The increasing population of the area contributed to the naming of the first ordinary Mayor of Santa Maria de los Lagos, the miner [[Pedro Marfil]] from Guanajuato. Marfil would be succeeded by [[Gonzalo Vazquez Baladez]], the first and only clergyman to-date to serve as mayor of the town.

The town continued to grow steadily in the 17th century, benefiting from the general prosperity of the [[Spanish Golden Age|Golden Age of the Spanish Empire]]. In 1615, a new jail was inaugurated, and 1621 saw the construction of a third parish temple with a tower that would be completed by 1685. Religious buildings were created often during this period; the Convent of the Poor Capuchins of Saint Joseph was founded and the construction of the Parish Church of Our Lady of the Assumption and the Temple of Our Lady of the Rosary began during this time.<ref name=":2"/>

==== Mexican War of Independence ====
[[File:Pedro Moreno insurgente.jpg|left|thumb|upright|[[Pedro Moreno (soldier)|Pedro Moreno]], namesake of Lagos de Moreno and hero of the [[Mexican War of Independence]].]]Santa Maria de los Lagos, like the rest of New Spain, underwent rapid political change in the early 1800s. Local lawyer [[Francisco Primo de Verdad y Ramos]] spoke openly to the colonial authorities, advocating for Mexican independence. However, he soon died under mysterious circumstances in the dungeon of the Mexico City Trustee building. In the initial phase of the war, Father [[Miguel Hidalgo y Costilla]] visited Santa Maria de los Lagos and provided religious services there, and recruited some families for the fight against the forces of the [[List of viceroys of New Spain|Spanish Viceroy]].<ref name=":3" />

=== Independence to present ===
{{Infobox UNESCO World Heritage Site|WHS=Historic Centre of Lagos de Moreno|Criteria=ii, iv|ID=1351-017|image=Lagos de Moreno restos coloniales.jpg|caption=The historic Lagos Bridge with the Temple of Our Lady of the Light in the distance|Location=Lagos de Moreno, Jalisco, Mexico|Year=2010|Session=34th|child=}}
In 1823, the ''Convenio de Lagos'' (Agreement of Lagos) was signed in Santa Maria by [[Nicolás Bravo|Nicolas Bravo]], [[Pedro Celestino Negrete]], and [[Luis de Quintanar|Luis Quintanar]], establishing the [[Jalisco|Free and Sovereign State of Jalisco]]. On March 27, 1824, Santa Maria was given the title of city.<ref name=":2"/> On April 9, 1829, the city was renamed as Lagos de Moreno in memory of insurgent General [[Pedro Moreno (soldier)|Pedro Moreno]] (1775–1817), a founding father of Mexico who led forces against Spanish authorities in the [[Mexican War of Independence]].<ref name=":0"/><ref name=":2"/>

In 1855, a second and similarly titled ''Convenio de Lagos'' (Agreement of Lagos) was signed in Lagos de Moreno, in which [[Manuel Doblado]] and [[Antonio de Haro y Tamariz]] recognized the [[Plan of Ayutla]], thus joining the movement to topple conservative President [[Antonio López de Santa Anna|Antonio López de Santa Ana]]. During the [[Reform War]], Lagos de Moreno changed hands between liberal and conservative forces multiple times until an eventual liberal victory in 1861.<ref name=":2"/>

During the [[Mexican Revolution]] in the 1910s, residents of Lagos de Moreno like [[Mariano Azuela]] and [[Francisco Guerrero Ramirez]] joined the fighting.<ref name=":2"/><ref name=":3" /> In the subsequent [[Cristero War]], the nearby Mesa Redonda was the site of a battle between federal troops and rebel forces.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Contreras|first=Karla Zafiro Rizo|date=2015-12-16|title=Adiós a la mesa redonda. La segunda Cristiada en Lagos de Moreno|url=http://www.revistascientificas.udg.mx/index.php/DIV/article/view/4748|journal=Diversae. Revista del Seminario de Historia Mexicana|language=es|volume=1|issue=1}}</ref>

In 1963, Lagos de Moreno celebrated the 400th anniversary of its founding. In recent times, the city has received several important titles. It has been named an Area of Historical Monuments by the [[Instituto Nacional de Antropología e Historia|National Institute of Anthropology and History]] (INAH) in 1989, a [[World Heritage Site]] on the Camino Real "Tierra Adentro" by [[UNESCO]] on August 1, 2010, and "[[Pueblos Mágicos|Pueblo Mágico]]" by SECTUR on November 16, 2012.<ref name=":2"/><ref name=":3" />

==Geography==

=== Climate ===
Lagos de Moreno has a [[subtropical highland climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification]] ''Cwb'') with mild temperatures year round.<ref>{{cite journal| last = Kottek | first = M.|author2=J. Grieser |author3=C. Beck |author4=B. Rudolf |author5=F. Rubel | title =World Map of the Köppen-Geiger climate classification updated| journal =Meteorol. Z.| volume =15 | pages =259–263| url =http://www.schweizerbart.de/resources/downloads/paper_free/55034.pdf| doi =10.1127/0941-2948/2006/0130| access-date = December 22, 2012| year =2006| issue = 3}}</ref> Temperatures are generally similar for any given month, but daily high and low fluctuations can be very sharp in the winter due in part to the city's altitude and its uneven surroundings. Winters are mild with an average maximum temperature of {{convert|23|C|1}} in January although nighttime temperatures are often cold, with an average minimum temperature of {{convert|3|C|1}}.<ref name="SMN" /> Many days are sunny, averaging around 15-19 clear days per month and precipitation is low.<ref name="clima" /> Nighttime temperatures regularly fall below {{convert|0|C|1}} but extended periods of frost are rare.<ref name="clima" />

The [[Bajío|Bajio]]'s two main seasons are the rainy season and the dry season. The rainy season, which runs from June to October, sees an increase in precipitation and humidity, with July and August being the months with the most precipitation. === Topography ===
[[File:Vista de la 'Mesa redonda' (Jalisco) desde la carretera León-Aguascalientes 02.jpg|thumb|315x315px|A view of the Mesa Redonda from the nearby highway. Mesa Redonda was the site of a [[Cristero War]] battle.]]Lagos de Moreno is situated 1,942 meters (6,371 feet) above mean sea level.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thediggings.com/places/mex4002224|title=Lagos de Moreno, Jalisco Mines|website=The Diggings™|language=en|access-date=2020-04-04}}</ref> Lagos is located in the region of [[Los Altos de Jalisco]], within the [[macroregion]] of the [[Bajío]], one of the most highly developed areas in [[Latin America]].<ref name=":4" /> The city's vicinity is quite mountainous and hilly, with the nearby Mesa Redonda being a particularly notable formation.<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|url=http://udgtv.com/noticias/lagos-de-moreno-noticias/mesa-redonda-emblema-natural-lagos-moreno/|title=Mesa redonda, el emblema natural de Lagos de Moreno|last=Moreno|first=Radio UdeG Lagos de|date=2018-07-31|website=UDG TV|language=es-ES|access-date=2020-04-04}}</ref>

=== Flora and fauna ===
The nearby Mesa Redonda is home to many unique species of flora. There, two species of [[cactus]] [[Endemism|endemic]] only to Jalisco flourish, with one of these species only being endemic to the Mesa Redonda, particularly its very steep slopes, protecting it from predators.

Many animals call the Lagos valley home, with fauna including hares, rabbits, deers, and boars. Residents of nearby ranches have commented on the presence of coyotes and snakes in rural areas. Areas like the Mesa Redonda have been utilized by locals to gather vegetables straight from nature, particularly [[Opuntia|prickly pear cacti]].<ref name=":5" />

== Demographics ==
As of the 2020 Mexican census, the city had a population of 111,569, making it the [[List of largest Jalisco cities by population|6th largest city in the state of Jalisco]].<ref name="pop">[http://www.citypopulation.de/en/mexico/cities/jalisco/ Citypopulation.de] Population of cities & towns in Jalisco</ref><ref name=":6" /> The municipality as a whole had an estimated population of 164,981 in 2015.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web |title=Número de habitantes. Jalisco |url=http://cuentame.inegi.org.mx/monografias/informacion/jal/poblacion/ |access-date=2020-04-03 |website=cuentame.inegi.org.mx}}</ref> The municipality has a slightly higher female population, with 88,426 females compared to 83,977 males.<ref name=":6"/>

In Lagos de Moreno city, 38,024 dwellings were enumerated, with 38,002 of these being private households. Of the private residences, 30,020 were inhabited; and in turn, of the inhabited private residences: 29,817 had non-dirt flooring; 29,64 had electricity; 29,919 had sanitary facilities; and 29,941 had drainage.<ref name=":6"/>
