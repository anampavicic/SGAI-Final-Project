'''Funtua''' is a [[Local Government Areas of Nigeria|local government area]] in [[Katsina State]], [[Nigeria]], and is headquartered in the town of Funtua on the [[A126 highway (Nigeria)|A126 highway]].

It is one of the premier local governments in Nigeria created after the local government reforms in 1976. It is the headquarters of the Katsina South senatorial district, which comprises eleven local governments: [[Bakori]], [[Danja, Nigeria|Danja]], [[Dandume]], [[Faskari]], Sabuwa, [[Kankara]], [[Malumfashi]], [[Kafur, Nigeria|Kafur]], [[Musawa]], [[Matazu]] and [[Funtua]].

Funtua has a conducive weather condition as it lies at latitude and longitude 11°32'N, 7°19'E. The city has an average temperature of 32<sup>o</sup>C and humidity of 44%.

It has an area of {{convert|448|km2}} and a population of 225,571 at the 2006 census and 570,110 according to 2016 estimate. The chairman is the official head of local government. The inhabitants are predominantly Fulani, Their main occupations are trading, farming and animal rearing.

Funtua is in the southern extreme end of Katsina State and is the second largest city in the state after Katsina. It borders [[Giwa]] local government of [[Kaduna State]] to the south, [[Bakori]] to the east, [[Danja, Nigeria|Danja]] to the southeast, Faskari to the northwest and Dandume to the west.

The [[postal code]] of the area is 830.<ref>{{cite web |title=Post Offices- with map of LGA |publisher=NIPOST |url=http://www.nipost.gov.ng/PostCode.aspx |accessdate=2009-10-20 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20091007011423/http://www.nipost.gov.ng/PostCode.aspx |archivedate=2009-10-07 }}</ref>

The source of the [[Sokoto River]] is located near Funtua.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Funtua Local Government Area |url=https://www.manpower.com.ng/places/lga/464/funtua |access-date=2024-07-30 |website=www.manpower.com.ng}}</ref>

==Transport==
Funtua is served by a railway station on a branch on the western line of the national railway network and four major federal highways: Funtua-Birnin Gwari-Lagos Road, Funtua -[[Zamfara State|Zamfara]]-[[Sokoto]]-[[Kebbi State|Kebbi]] Road, Funtua-Yashe Road and Funtua-Zaria Road, Funtua-Bakori-malunfashi-Dayi Road.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Uwakmfon |date=2019-07-24 |title=A HISTORY OF THE INTRODUCTION, GROWTH AND DECLINE OF RAILWAY IN FUNTUA TOWN, 1927 – 1992 |url=https://www.modishproject.com/a-history-of-the-introduction-growth-and-decline-of-railway-in-funtua-town-1927-1992/ |access-date=2024-07-30 |website=Modish Project |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=A HISTORY OF THE INTRODUCTION, GROWTH AND DECLINE OF RAILWAY IN FUNTUA TOWN, 1927 – 1992 HISTORY & INTERNATIONAL RELATIONS Project Topics |url=https://eduproject.com.ng/history-and-international-relations/a-history-of-the-introduction-growth-and-decline-of-railway-in-funtua-town-1927-1992/index.html |access-date=2024-07-30 |website=eduproject.com.ng}}</ref>

==Educational institutions==
Funtua has higher education institutions which accommodate students from all over the country. One is a remedial school known as [[Ahmadu Bello University]] School of Basic and Remedial Studies (SBRS), Funtua, which accommodates students from all 19 northern states of Nigeria. It has three health technology colleges - the Muslim Community College of Health Science & Technology Funtua, the College of Health and Environmental Sciences Funtua and the Community School of Health Sciences and Technology Funtua Annex. Another diploma-awarding institution is the Abdullahi Aminchi College of Advanced Studies Funtua which is registered to award diploma certificates by its affiliated with ABU Zaria, Imam Saidu College of Education, which awards NCE and Isma'ila Isah College of Advanced Studies which also award a diploma certificate in collaboration with Haicas Tsafe. Funtua had a certificate-awarding institution now preparing to start awarding diplomas, known as the College of Administration, Funtua.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Educational Institutions in Funtua L.G.A, Katsina State {{!}} Nigeria Directory, A List of Nigerian Businesses, Organisations, Firms and Companies {{!}} Nigeria Yellow Pages |url=https://www.directory.org.ng/clga-educational_institution?lga=katsina_funtua |access-date=2024-07-30 |website=www.directory.org.ng}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Educational Institutions in Funtua, Katsina State |url=https://www.manpower.com.ng/lists/educational-institutions/lga/464/funtua |access-date=2024-07-30 |website=www.manpower.com.ng}}</ref>

==Electricity and water supply==
Funtua is served by 132kV transmission station of the National Grid that comes from Mando receiving station via [[Zaria]] and from there it extends to [[Gusau]] and terminates at Talata Mafara. The 132kV/33kV transmission station in Funtua serves nine local government areas out of the 11 area councils that make up of Funtua senatorial district. While erratic power supply is a nationwide phenomenon, Funtua does not often have such problem because the town enjoys electricity for a good 12–20 hours daily.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Carmen |date=2021-09-17 |title=Funtua - Zaria Line, Nigeria |url=https://www.power-technology.com/marketdata/funtua-zaria-line-nigeria/ |access-date=2024-07-30 |website=Power Technology |language=en-US}}</ref>

Funtua has two dams, Mairuwa and Gwagwaye, that serve the city and some parts of Faskari and Bakori area councils.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Olarinoye |first=Ajibola |date=2018-01-11 |title=$70m dam construction: Katsina partners Indian firm |url=https://infrastructurenews.ng/70m-dam-construction-katsina-partners-indian-firm/ |access-date=2024-07-30 |website=Infrastructure News |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal |last=Salawu |first=Abdulazeez Ozomari |date=May 2014 |title=THE REMAINING STORAGE AND RATE OF SILTATION OF MAIRUWA DAM IN FUNTUA, NORTH CENTRAL NIGERIA |url=http://hdl.handle.net/123456789/5433 |language=en}}</ref>

The two water bodies can be used for other things such as irrigation and hydro-power generation.

There is a Songhai farm which is used for training and the cultivation of some farm products.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2016-04-21 |title=Songhai Farm initiative suffers neglect in states - Daily Trust |url=https://dailytrust.com/songhai-farm-initiative-suffers-neglect-in-states/ |access-date=2024-07-30 |website=dailytrust.com/ |language=en-US}}</ref>

==Funtua Inland Container Dry Port==
Following the decision of the federal government to decongest Nigeria's seaports, six dry ports were established in the country by the Obasanjo administration in 2006, including Funtua. Work is in progress at the site and when completed, the dry port would provide job opportunities as well as revenue for the government.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Rapheal |date=2022-08-02 |title=How inland dry port projects'll boost FG's revenue, trade flows –Minister |url=https://sunnewsonline.com/how-inland-dry-port-projectsll-boost-fgs-revenue-trade-flows-minister/ |access-date=2023-10-22 |website=The Sun Nigeria |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Ports |first=Ships & |date=2024-05-10 |title=Tinubu commissions Funtua Inland Dry Port to support economic diversification |url=https://shipsandports.com.ng/tinubu-commissions-funtua-inland-dry-port-to-support-economic-diversification/ |access-date=2024-07-30 |website=Ships & Ports |language=en-US}}</ref>

== Climate ==
Funtua has a mean [[temperature]] of 30.26<sup>o</sup>C, a subtropical steppe climate, 50.56 inches of precipitation, and 53.52 wet days.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Funtua, Katsina, Nigeria Climate |url=https://tcktcktck.org/nigeria/katsina/funtua#:~:text=The%20district's%20yearly%20temperature%20is,%25%20of%20the%20time)%20annually. |access-date=2023-08-28 |website=Weather and Climate}}</ref> 

In Funtua, the year-round heat is accompanied by an uncomfortable, overcast wet season and a partially cloudy dry season. The average annual temperature fluctuates between 53°F and 97°F, seldom falling below 48°F or rising over 102°F.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Weather in Funtua - accurate and detailed weather forecast in Funtua for today, tomorrow and week. Funtua, Katsina, Nigeria |url=https://meteotrend.com/forecast/ng/funtua/ |access-date=2024-08-24 |website=meteotrend.com |language=en}}</ref><ref name=":0" />

=== Average Temperature ===
Temperature has an impact on Funtua's climate; a positive trend denotes rising annual temperatures, while a negative trend denotes falling temperatures.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |title=Climate Change Funtua |url=https://www.meteoblue.com/en/climate-change/funtua_nigeria_2342490 |access-date=2023-10-24 |website=meteoblue |language=en}}</ref> 

With an average daily high temperature of 94°F, the hot season spans 1.8 months, from March 8 to May 2. At 96°F on average for highs and 70°F for lows, April is the hottest month of the year in Funtua. With an average daily maximum temperature below 85°F, the cool season spans 2.9 months, from July 6 to October 2. At an average high temperature of 84°F and low temperature of 54°F, January is the coldest month of the year in Funtua.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Funtua Climate, Weather By Month, Average Temperature (Nigeria) - Weather Spark |url=https://weatherspark.com/y/55118/Average-Weather-in-Funtua-Nigeria-Year-Round#:~:text=In%20Funtua,%20the%20wet%20season,or%20above%20102%C2%B0F. |access-date=2024-08-24 |website=weatherspark.com |language=en}}</ref>
