'''Las Heras''' is a city in the [[provinces of Argentina|province]] of [[Mendoza Province|Mendoza]], Argentina, located in the north of the [[metropolitan area]] of the provincial capital (Greater [[Mendoza, Argentina|Mendoza]]). It has more than 180,000 inhabitants as per the {{census-ar|2001}} and is the head town of the department of the same name.

The city is immediately next to [[Mendoza, Argentina|Mendoza City]], going north down San Martín Avenue. The area includes the low mountain range of El Challao, home to [[hot spring]]s.

The name of the city is an homage to General [[Juan Gregorio de las Heras]], hero of the [[Argentine War of Independence]].

Las Heras was heavily damaged in the [[1927 Mendoza earthquake]].<ref name="Tello">{{Cite journal |url=http://www.saber.ula.ve/bitstream/123456789/24595/2/articulo44-1-6.pdf |title=Sismicidad histórica entre los 32° 30′ – 36° lat. sur y los 68° – 69° 30′ long. oeste, provincia de Mendoza, Argentina |first=Graciela E. |last=Tello |journal=Revista Geográfica Venezolana |volume=44 |issue=1 |year=2003 |pages=125–141|language=es}}</ref>
