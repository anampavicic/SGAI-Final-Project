'''Biên Hòa''' (Northern accent: {{Audio|Bien Hoa Northern.ogg|listen|help=no}}, Southern accent: {{Audio|Bien Hoa Southern.ogg|listen|help=no}}) is the capital city of [[Đồng Nai Province]], [[Vietnam]], and is part of the [[Ho Chi Minh City metropolitan area]]. Situated northeast of [[Ho Chi Minh City]] (also known as [[Saigon]]), Biên Hòa is connected to it via [[National Route 1 (Vietnam)|National Route 1]]. As a class-1 [[Provincial city (Vietnam)|provincial city]], it is the [[List of cities in Vietnam#Largest cities by population|sixth largest city in Vietnam by population]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=Báo Đồng Nai điện tử|title=Biên Hòa chính thức trở thành đô thị loại I|url=http://www.baodongnai.com.vn/tintuc/201601/bien-hoa-chinh-thuc-tro-thanh-do-thi-loai-i-2654518/}}</ref>

==Geography==
===Topography===
Biên Hòa spans 264 square kilometers of midland terrain in western Đồng Nai Province. The majority of the city is situated to the east of the [[Đồng Nai river|Đồng Nai River]].

*[[Bình Dương province|Bình Dương Province]] to the west
*[[Long Thành district]] and [[Ho Chi Minh City]] to the south
*[[Vĩnh Cửu district]] to the North

==Demographics==
In 1989, Biên Hòa's population was estimated at 273,879. By 1999, it had grown to 435,400 and reached 701,194 in 2009.<ref>[http://www.gso.gov.vn/Modules/Doc_Download.aspx?DocID=12724 Population module] gso.gov.vn {{dead link|date=March 2024}}</ref> In December 2012, the city's population surpassed one million.<ref>[http://www.bienhoa.gov.vn/tin_noi_bat/mlfolder.2013-01-04.2171417158/mlfolder.2013-11-01.3813962953/mlnews.2013-11-21.2704336110 Chào mừng Hội nghị hiệp hội các đô thị Việt Nam diễn ra tại thành phố Biên Hòa — Trang thông tin điện tử thành phố Biên Hòa <!-- Bot generated title -->] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131126051226/http://www.bienhoa.gov.vn/tin_noi_bat/mlfolder.2013-01-04.2171417158/mlfolder.2013-11-01.3813962953/mlnews.2013-11-21.2704336110 |date=26 November 2013 }}</ref> By 2019, it had increased to 1,055,414.<ref>{{cite web|page=34|publisher=Tổng cục Thống kê Việt Nam|title=Kết quả toàn bộ tổng điều tra dân số và nhà ở năm 2019|url=http://tongdieutradanso.vn/uploads/data/6/files/files/Ket%20qua%20toan%20bo%20Tong%20dieu%20tra%20dan%20so%20va%20nha%20o%202019_ca%20bia_compressed.pdf}}</ref> 

As of 2021, the city's population was 1,119,190.<ref name=baoxaydung>{{cite web|publisher=Báo Bộ Xây Dựng|title=Phát triển thành phố Biên Hòa, tỉnh Đồng Nai theo hướng hiện đại – xanh – thông minh|date=9 September 2022 |url=https://baoxaydung.com.vn/phat-trien-thanh-pho-bien-hoa-tinh-dong-nai-theo-huong-hien-dai-xanh-thong-minh-339705.html}}</ref>

==History==
[[File:Đền thờ Nguyễn Hữu Cảnh tại Cù lao Phố.jpg|thumb|right|Nguyễn Hữu Cảnh temple in Cù Lao Phố.]]
[[File:Nhà thờ chính Văn miếu Trấn Biên.jpg|thumb|right|[[Trấn Biên Literature Temple|Trấn Biên Temple of Literature]], a Vietnamese [[Confucian]] temple.]]

===Nguyễn dynasty===
[[File:Bản vẽ thành Biên Hòa.jpg|thumb|Drawing of Biên Hòa citadel in the Nguyễn dynasty]]
The [[capture of Biên Hòa]] on 16 December 1861, was an important allied victory in the [[Cochinchina Campaign]] (1858–62). This campaign, fought between the French and the Spanish on the one side and the Vietnamese (under the [[Nguyễn dynasty]]) on the other, began as a limited [[punitive expedition]] and ended as a French war of conquest. The war concluded with the establishment of the French colony of Cochinchina, a development that inaugurated nearly a century of French colonial dominance in Vietnam.{{fact|date=November 2024}}

===Republic of Vietnam===
Biên Hòa grew into a major suburb of Saigon as the capital city of the [[South Vietnam|Republic of (South) Vietnam]] grew. Following the [[First Indochina War]], tens of thousands of refugees from the northern and central regions of Vietnam&mdash;a large portion of whom were [[Roman Catholicism|Roman Catholics]]&mdash;resettled in Biên Hòa as part of [[Operation Passage to Freedom]]. During the [[Vietnam War]], the [[United States Air Force]] operated [[Biên Hòa Air Base]] near the city. [[Mortar (weapon)|Mortar]] attacks on U.S. and [[Army of the Republic of Vietnam|ARVN]] targets were frequently staged from residential districts in Biên Hòa. Two of the better-known attacks took place during Tết of 1968 as well as 1969.<ref>Pike, Thomas F., Operations & Intelligence, III Corps Reporting: Tet 1969, 2016, {{ISBN|978-1-534-79903-5}}, pp 91-103.</ref>

===Socialist Republic===
Because of its large population of former refugees and their descendants who fled [[North Vietnam]]'s communist government in the mid-1950s, Biên Hòa was a center of resistance against the communist government in the months immediately following [[fall of Saigon|the fall]] of the [[Republic of Vietnam]].{{fact|date=November 2024}}

Like much of Vietnam, [[Vietnam War|post-war]] Biên Hòa suffered a period of severe economic decline between 1975 and the second half of the 1980s. However, after the government of the Socialist Republic of Vietnam introduced [[Đổi Mới]], a series of economic and political reforms in 1986, Biên Hòa experienced significant economic growth. The city and its surrounding areas attracted substantial foreign investment, leading to industrialization and development.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=Nhân Dân Newspaper|title=Kỷ niệm 320 năm hình thành và phát triển Biên Hòa - Ðồng Nai|date=28 December 2018 |url=https://nhandan.vn/ky-niem-320-nam-hinh-thanh-va-phat-trien-bien-hoa-dong-nai-post345343.html}}</ref>

By 2005, Biên Hòa had become an industrial hub of southern Vietnam. Many factories and warehouses (often funded in collaboration with Japanese, Singaporean, American, Swiss and other foreign investors) operate in the areas surrounding the city.

With regard to entertainment, the city includes several amusement parks, nightclubs and restaurants lining the [[Đồng Nai River]]. Construction has increased rapidly, with many Western-style houses and villas under development. The real estate market has experienced a series of boom cycles since the mid-1990s.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=Tuổi Trẻ Newspaper|title=Bất động sản Biên Hoà bùng nổ trong năm 2018|date=16 March 2018 |url=https://tuoitre.vn/bat-dong-san-bien-hoa-bung-no-trong-nam-2018-20180315175311323.htm}}</ref>

Biên Hòa is home to [[Bình An Cemetery]] (also known as Biên Hòa Military Cemetery), a large national cemetery for fallen soldiers and military officials of the former [[Republic of Vietnam]] ([[ARVN]]). After the [[fall of Saigon]], the cemetery was ransacked and subsequently abandoned.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2012-03-01 |title=Excavations of Burial Sites at Vietnamese Re-Education Camps by The Returning Casualty |url=https://www.southeastasianarchaeology.com/2012/03/01/excavations-of-burial-sites-at-vietnamese-re-education-camps-by-the-returning-casualty/ |access-date=2024-10-11 |website=Southeast Asian Archaeology |language=en-GB}}</ref> The Vietnamese American Foundation, also known as The Returning Casualty, is working to restore the cemetery and excavate a nearby mass grave of approximately 200 ARVN soldiers.<ref name=martin>[http://www.southeastasianarchaeology.com/2012/03/01/excavations-of-burial-sites-at-vietnamese-re-education-camps-by-the-returning-casualty/ Excavations of Burial Sites at Vietnamese Re-Education Camps by The Returning Casualty], Julie Martin, MSc in Forensic Archaeology and Anthropology candidate, Cranfield University UK, from south-eastasianarchaeology.com</ref>

At the end of 2015, the [[Prime Minister of Vietnam]] issued Decision No.2488/QD-TTg recognizing Biên Hòa as a class-1 provincial city.<ref name="Thư viện pháp luật">{{cite web|publisher=Thư viện Pháp luật|title=Quyết định 2488/QĐ-TTg năm 2015 về việc công nhận thành phố Biên Hòa là đô thị loại I trực thuộc tỉnh Đồng Nai do Thủ tướng Chính phủ ban hành|url=https://thuvienphapluat.vn/van-ban/Xay-dung-Do-thi/Quyet-dinh-2488-QD-TTg-2015-cong-nhan-thanh-pho-Bien-Hoa-la-do-thi-loai-I-thuoc-tinh-Dong-Nai-394181.aspx}}</ref>

==Economy==
Biên Hòa is a key industrial hub in southern Vietnam. There are six industrial zones:
*Biên Hòa I Industrial Park. The country’s oldest industrial park, covering 340 hectares, is set to be converted into an urban, commercial, and service area by the end of 2025.<ref>{{cite web |title=A glimpse into Vietnam’s oldest industrial park before it’s gone |url=https://e.vnexpress.net/photo/news/a-glimpse-into-vietnams-oldest-industrial-park-before-its-gone-4721057.html |access-date=10 October 2024 |website=VnExpress}}
</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Vietnam’s oldest industrial park to be converted into urban area |url=https://tuoitrenews.vn/news/society/20240305/vietnams-oldest-industrial-park-to-be-converted-into-urban-area/78601.html |access-date=10 October 2024 |website=Tuoi Tre News}}</ref>
*Biên Hòa II Industrial Zone, 365 ha
*Amata Industrial Park, 674 ha. This is the first investment project of [[Amata Corporation]] in Vietnam.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=Vietnamnet|title=Amata to build two more industrial-urban complexes in Vietnam|url=https://vietnamnet.vn/en/amata-to-build-two-more-industrial-urban-complexes-in-vietnam-E149098.html}}</ref>
*Long Bình Industrial Zone Development
*Agtex Long Bình Industrial Park - AGTEX 28, 43 ha
*Tam Phước Industrial Park, 323 ha

[[Sanyang Motor]]'s Vietnam Manufacturing & Export Processing Co., Ltd. (VMEP) is located in Biên Hòa.

==Environment==

[[Bien Hoa Air Base|Biên Hòa Air Base]] served as the main storage and handling site for [[Agent Orange]] during the [[Vietnam War]] and remains the largest [[Dioxins and dioxin-like compounds|dioxin]] hotspot in Vietnam.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |date=2024-01-11 |title=Dioxin Remediation at Bien Hoa Air Base Area Project {{!}} Vietnam {{!}} Fact Sheet |url=https://www.usaid.gov/vietnam/fact-sheets/dioxin-remediation-bien-hoa-airbase-area |access-date=2024-10-10 |website=U.S. Agency for International Development |language=en}}</ref> U.S. and Vietnamese authorities are working to clean up the affected areas.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite news | title = Vietnam, US finish first part of dioxin cleanup at Bien Hoa airbase | url = https://e.vnexpress.net/news/news/vietnam-us-finish-first-part-of-dioxin-cleanup-at-bien-hoa-airbase-4224110.html | newspaper = VNExpress }}</ref>
