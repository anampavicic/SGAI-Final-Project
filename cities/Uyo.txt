'''Uyo''' is the [[capital city]] of [[Akwa Ibom]], a state in [[South South]] Nigeria.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Uyo {{!}} Location, Facts, & Population|url=https://www.britannica.com/place/Uyo|access-date=2021-09-12|website=Encyclopedia Britannica|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Uyo {{!}} Location, Facts, & Population {{!}} Britannica |url=https://www.britannica.com/place/Uyo |access-date=2022-11-14 |website=www.britannica.com |language=en}}</ref>

It became the capital on September 23, 1987 when Akwa Ibom was created from the former [[Cross River State]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=BusinessDay |date=2022-12-23 |title=Akwa Ibom at 35: Moving forward in business and leadership transformation |url=https://businessday.ng/opinion/article/akwa-ibom-at-35-moving-forward-in-business-and-leadership-transformation/ |access-date=2023-06-21 |website=Businessday NG |language=en-US}}</ref> According to the 2006 Nigerian [[Census]], the [[population]] of Uyo (including [[Itu, Nigeria|Itu]]) is 427,873,<ref>Summing the 2 LGAs Uyo and Itu LGA's as per:<br />{{cite web| url=http://www.nigerianstat.gov.ng/nbsapps/Connections/Pop2006.pdf| title=Legal Notice on Publication of the Details of the Breakdown of the National and State Provisional Totals 2006 Census| author=Federal Republic of Nigeria Official Gazette|date=15 May 2007|access-date=2007-05-19}}</ref> while the greater urban area, including [[Uruan]], has a population of 554,906.<ref>Summing the 2 LGAs Uyo and Itu LGA's as per:<br />{{cite web| url=http://www.nigerianstat.gov.ng/nbsapps/Connections/Pop2006.pdf| title=Legal Notice on Publication of the Details of the Breakdown of the National and State Provisional Totals 2006 Census| author=Federal Republic of Nigeria Official Gazette|date=15 May 2007|access-date=2007-05-19}}</ref>The population of Uyo is put at 1,393,000 as at 2024.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Uyo, Nigeria Metro Area Population 1950-2024 |url=https://www.macrotrends.net/global-metrics/cities/206379/uyo/population |access-date=2024-07-05 |website=www.macrotrends.net}}</ref>

[[Ibibio language|Ibibio]] is the primary indigenous language.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ibibio {{!}} people|url=https://www.britannica.com/topic/Ibibio|access-date=2021-09-10|website=Encyclopedia Britannica|language=en}}</ref> The main campus of the [[University of Uyo]] is in [[Nwaniba]], with satellites on Ikpa Road.<ref>{{Citation |title=LIST OF CONTRIBUTORS |date=1987 |work=Shallow Impurity Centers in Semiconductors |pages=304 |url=http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/b978-0-444-87087-2.50027-5 |access-date=2024-01-30 |publisher=Elsevier}}</ref>

== Climate ==

=== Wind ===
The average [[wind]] vector in Uyo, which is greatly influenced by terrain and other local characteristics, is discussed in this section. [[August]] is the windiest month, with an average wind speed of 6.4 miles per hour. The windier season lasts 4.4 months. The more tranquil period lasts 7.6 months, with [[December]] having the lowest average speed of 4.0 miles per hour.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /><ref name=":2" />
