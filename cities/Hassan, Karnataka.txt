'''Hassan''' (pronounced: Haasana) is a city in the southern part of the [[Indian state]] of [[Karnataka]]. It is the headquarters of [[Hassan district]]. The city is situated midway between [[Bangalore]] and [[Mangalore]] on [[National Highway 75 (India)|National Highway 75]].

==Etymology==
Hassan city gets its name from the Hindu goddess [[Hasanamba temple|Hassanamba Devi ]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.karnataka.com/hassan/|title=Hassan {{!}} Hassan Sightseeing {{!}} Hassan Temples {{!}} Hassanamba|website=Karnataka.com|language=en-US|access-date=8 December 2019}}</ref>

== History ==
Hassan dates from beginnings of the [[Hoysala Empire]] in the 11th century.<ref name=EB1911>{{cite EB1911 |wstitle=Hassan |volume=13 |page=51}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|last1=Muthanna|first1=I. M.|title=Karnataka, History, Administration & Culture|date=1962|page=21|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=uCYdAAAAMAAJ|language=en}}</ref> Hoysala Empire ruled this city for a long time and their influence can be seen in the art and inscriptions on the different monuments such as in [[Halebidu]], [[Belur, Karnataka|Belur]] and [[Shravanabelagola]].

==Geography==
The city is situated {{convert|950|m|abbr=on}} above sea level. The urban population in 2011 was 133,436.<ref name="Census"/> It is situated at a distance of {{convert|182|km|abbr=on}} from the state capital, [[Bangalore]], and {{convert|171|km|abbr=on}} from [[Mangalore]].

{{As of|alt=As of the 2011|2011}} [[2011 Census of India|Indian census]], the city of Hassan had an urban population of 177,484. Males were 49.5% of the population and females 50.5%. The average literacy rate was 80.8%. Male literacy was 82.7%, and female literacy was 78.9%. Of the population, 10.1% was under the age of 7.<ref name="Census">{{cite web|url=http://www.censusindia.gov.in/pca/SearchDetails.aspx?Id=701044|title=2011 Primary Census Abstract Data, Population Finder, Karnataka, Hassan, Hassan|access-date=1 July 2017|publisher=Office of the Registrar General & Census Commissioner, India}}</ref>

In 2020, the Karnataka Government upgraded Hassan's city municipal council area to {{convert|66.12|km2|abbr=on}} by including nearby villages to the panchayat and the population increased from 133,436 to 226,520.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Gazette Notification|url=https://erajyapatra.karnataka.gov.in/WriteReadData/2020/1047.pdf|website=karnataka.gov.in|date=24 July 2023|lang=kn|access-date=16 December 2020|archive-date=16 May 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220516102601/https://erajyapatra.karnataka.gov.in/WriteReadData/2020/1047.pdf|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Economy==
===Industries===
Hassan city is also an industrial hub in Karnataka. Major industries related to pharma, textiles, food processing sectors and also numerous MSMEs have set up their units in the KIADB Industrial Growth Centre, Hassan.

====HPCL POL Terminal====
The terminal is located on 103 acres of land in Hassan industrial area. It supplies diesel, petrol and ethanol to retail outlets across 14 districts of Karnataka.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thehindu.com/news/national/karnataka/drill-at-hpcl-terminal-in-hassan/article65698039.ece|title=HPCL POL TERMINAL| website=[[The Hindu]] |date=29 July 2022 }}</ref>
