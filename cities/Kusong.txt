'''Kusŏng''' ({{Korean|hangul=구성}}; {{IPA|ko|ku.sʌŋ}}) is a city in central [[North Pyongan]] province, [[North Korea]].

It borders [[Taegwan]] to the north, [[Taechon]] to the east, [[Kwaksan]] and [[Chongju (North Korea)|Chongju]] to the south, and [[Chonma County|Chonma]] to the west. The highest point is [[Chongryongsan]] (청룡산, 920 m).

Educational institutions located in Kusong include [[Kusong Mechanical College]] and Kusong Industrial College. Historical relics include the [[Goryeo|Koryo dynasty]]-era [[Kuju Castle]].

[[Panghyon-dong]], an [[exclave]] of [[Pyongyang]], was formerly part of Kusong.

== Industry ==
Kusong is also home to much of North Korea's military industry, with both munitions factories and uranium mines in the area.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Pike|first1=John|title=Kusong - North Korean Special Weapons Facilities|url=http://www.globalsecurity.org/wmd/world/dprk/kusong.htm|website=www.globalsecurity.org}}</ref> The No.112, also known as the January 12th Factory was the site of the first successful [[Hwasong-12]] launch, with a memorial dedicated to the successful launch nearby.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-06-20|title=Commemorating the 2017 Hwasong-12 IRBM Launch?|url=https://beyondparallel.csis.org/commemorating-the-2017-hwasong-12-irbm-launch/|access-date=2021-03-12|website=Beyond Parallel|language=en-US}}</ref>

The [[Panghyon Airport|Panghyon airfield]] is also located by Kusong,<ref name="NYT July 4, 2017" /> which is a site of missile test launches, including the first successful ICBM test launch by the DPRK.<ref name="NYT July 4, 2017" />

Kusong is home to the Machine Plant managed by Ho Chol Yong, a large factory that produces tracked vehicles and tanks. This factory saw multiple extensions to it in 2016 and 2020. The factories' expansion is reflected on the increasing use of tracked [[transporter erector launcher]]s.<ref>{{Cite web|title=New Construction Activity at the Kusong Tank Plant|url=https://www.armscontrolwonk.com/archive/1210381/construction-activity-at-kusong-tank-plant/|access-date=2021-03-12|website=www.armscontrolwonk.com}}</ref>
