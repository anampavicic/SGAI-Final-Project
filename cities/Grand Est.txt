'''Grand Est'''<ref>{{Cite web |title="Grand Est": les élus valident le nom de région |url=http://www.lefigaro.fr/flash-actu/2016/04/29/97001-20160429FILWWW00180-grand-est-les-elus-valident-le-nom-de-region.php |access-date=29 April 2016 |website=Le Figaro |date=29 April 2016 |language=french}}</ref> ({{IPA|fr|ɡʁɑ̃t‿ɛst|lang|LL-Q150 (fra)-WikiLucas00-Grand Est.wav}};<ref> {{langx|gsw-FR|Grossa Oschta}}; [[Moselle Franconian]]/{{langx|lb|Grouss Osten}};
[[Rhine Franconian]]: ''Groß Oschte''; {{langx|de|Großer Osten}} {{IPA|de|ˈɡʁoːsɐ ˈʔɔstn̩|}};</ref> {{langx|en|"Great East"}}) is an [[Regions of France|administrative region]] in northeastern [[France]]. It superseded three former administrative regions, [[Alsace]], [[Champagne-Ardenne]] and [[Lorraine]], on 1 January 2016 under the provisional name of '''Alsace-Champagne-Ardenne-Lorraine''' ({{IPA|fr|alzas ʃɑ̃paɲ aʁdɛn lɔʁɛn|pron}}; '''ACAL''' or, less commonly, '''ALCALIA'''),<ref>{{Cite web |last=Dupuis-Remond |first=Dupuis-Remond |date=18 December 2014 |title=Débat d'orientation budgétaire : la Grande Région ALCA dans tous les esprits – France 3 Lorraine |url=http://france3-regions.francetvinfo.fr/lorraine/2014/12/18/debat-d-orientation-budgetaire-la-grande-region-alca-dans-tous-les-esprits-616060.html |access-date=13 January 2015 |website=[[France 3]] |language=French}}</ref> as a result of territorial reform which had been passed by the [[French Parliament]] in 2014.<ref>{{Cite web |date=17 December 2014 |title=La carte à 13 régions définitivement adoptée |url=http://www.lemonde.fr/politique/article/2014/12/17/la-carte-a-13-regions-definitivement-adoptee_4542278_823448.html |access-date=13 January 2015 |website=[[Le Monde]] |language=French |agency=[[Agence France-Presse]]}}</ref><ref name="Region reform law: article about name and capital" />{{efn|The name ''Alsace-Champagne-Ardenne-Lorraine'' was created by hyphenating the merged regions' names in alphabetical order; the [[Regional council (France)|regional council]] had to approve a new name for the region by 1 July 2016.<ref name="Region reform law: article about name and capital" /> France's {{lang|fr|[[Conseil d'État (France)|Conseil d'État]]|italic=no}} approved Grand Est as the new name of the region on 28 September 2016, effective 30 September 2016.<ref name="Decree confirming new region name">{{cite French decree|number or usual name=n° 2016-1262|date in French=28 septembre 2016 |full name=portant fixation du nom de la région Grand Est |article= |articles= |language=French |lower case= |URL=https://www.legifrance.gouv.fr/affichTexte.do?cidTexte=JORFTEXT000033161418&dateTexte=&categorieLien=id}}</ref>}}

The region sits astride three water basins ([[Seine]], [[Meuse]] and [[Rhine]]), spanning an area of {{cvt|57,433|km2}}, the fifth largest in France; it includes two mountain ranges ([[Vosges]] and [[Ardennes]]). It shares borders with [[Belgium]], [[Luxembourg]], [[Germany]] and [[Switzerland]]. As of 2021, it had a population of 5,561,287 inhabitants. The [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]] and largest city is [[Strasbourg]].

The East of France has a rich and diverse culture, being situated at a crossroads between the [[Gallo-Romance languages|Gallic-Latin]] and [[Germanic languages|Germanic]] worlds. This history is reflected in the variety of languages spoken there ([[Alsatian language|Alsatian]], [[Champenois language|Champenois]], [[Lorrain language|Lorrain]] and [[Lorraine Franconian]]). Most of today's Grand Est region was considered "Eastern" as early as the 8th century, when it constituted the southern part of the [[Francia]]n territory of [[Austrasia]]. The city of [[Reims]] (in Champagne), where Frankish king [[Clovis I]] had been baptized in 496 AD, would later play a prominent ceremonial role in French monarchical history as the traditional site of the coronation of the kings of France. The [[Champagne fairs]] played a significant role in the economy of [[medieval Europe]] as well. Alsace and Lorraine thrived in the sphere of influence of the [[Holy Roman Empire]] for most of the Middle Ages and Renaissance, and subject to competing claims by France and Germany over the centuries. 

The region has distinctive traditions such as the celebration of [[Saint Nicholas Day]], [[Christmas market]]s, or traditions involving the [[Easter Hare|Easter hare]] in Alsace and Lorraine. Alsace-Moselle are furthermore subject to [[Local law in Alsace-Moselle|local law]] for historical reasons. With a long industrial history and strong agriculture and tourism (arts, gastronomy, sightseeing), the East of France is one of the top economic producing regions in the country.

== Toponymy ==
===Provisional name===<!-- after a permanent name is chosen, this should be moved to the "Formation" section  -->
The provisional name of the region was Alsace-Champagne-Ardenne-Lorraine, formed by combining the names of the three former regions—[[Alsace]], [[Champagne-Ardenne]] and [[Lorraine]]—in alphabetical order with [[hyphen]]s.<!-- <ref name="Region reform law: article about name and capital" /> --> The formula for the provisional name of the region was established by the territorial reform law and applied to all but one<!-- Upper Normandy and Lower Normandy was to be called Normandy --> of the provisional names for new regions.<ref name="Region reform law: article about name and capital">{{cite French law|number or usual name=n° 2015–29|date in French=16 janvier 2015 |full name=relative à la délimitation des régions, aux élections régionales et départementales et modifiant le calendrier électoral |article=2(I) |language=French |lower case= |URL=http://www.legifrance.gouv.fr/affichTexte.do;jsessionid=9FECBA9D9314D1D2C093CF793C886ED5.tpdila21v_1?idSectionTA=JORFSCTA000030109623&cidTexte=JORFTEXT000030109622&dateTexte=29990101}}</ref> The ACAL regional council, which was elected in December 2015, was given the task of choosing a name for the region and submitting it to the {{Lang|fr|[[Conseil d'État (France)|Conseil d'État]]|italic=no}}—France's highest authority for [[administrative law]]—by 1 July 2016 for approval.<ref name="Region reform law: article about name and capital" /><ref>[http://www.sudouest.fr/2014/12/04/quel-nom-pour-la-nouvelle-region-vous-avez-choisi-1755244-6058.php Quel nom pour la nouvelle région ? Vous avez choisi...], [[Sud-Ouest (newspaper)|Sud-Ouest]], 4 December 2014. Retrieved 2 January 2015</ref> The provisional name of the region was retired on 30 September 2016, when the new name of the region, ''Grand Est'', took effect.<ref name="Decree confirming new region name" />

In Alsace and in Lorraine, the new region has frequently been called ALCA, for Alsace-Lorraine-Champagne-Ardennes, on the internet.<ref name="DNA">{{Cite news |date=7 December 2014 |title=Cette région que l'Alsace ne veut pas baptiser |language=French |work=Dernières Nouvelles d'Alsace |url=http://www.dna.fr/politique/2014/12/07/cette-region-que-l-alsace-ne-veut-pas-baptiser |access-date=25 August 2015}}</ref>

Like the name ''[[Hauts-de-France|Région Hauts-de-France]]'' (and, until 2015, the name ''[[Région Centre]]''), the name ''Région Grand Est'' contains no reference whatsoever to the area's history or identity, but merely describes its geographical location within metropolitan France.

===Permanent name===<!-- after a permanent name is chosen, the subheading should be deleted; information about polling and naming process should be moved to the "Formation" section, not deleted  -->
In a poll conducted in November 2014 by [[France 3]] in Champagne-Ardenne, ''Grand Est'' (29.16%) and ''Austrasie'' (22.65%) were the top two names among 25 candidates and 4,701 votes.<ref name="F3 names">{{Cite news |date=24 November 2014 |title=Votez pour le nom de la future grande région Champagne-Ardenne – Lorraine – Alsace |language=French |work=[[France 3]] Champagne-Ardenne |publisher=France Télévisions |url=http://france3-regions.francetvinfo.fr/champagne-ardenne/2014/11/24/votez-pour-le-nom-de-la-future-grande-region-champagne-ardenne-lorraine-alsace-599160.html |access-date=25 August 2015}}</ref> Grand Est also topped a poll the following month conducted by [[L'Est Républicain]], receiving 42% of 3,324 votes.<ref name="ER: Names">{{Cite news |date=2 December 2014 |title=Choisissez un nom pour la Grande Région |language=French |work=L'Est Républicain |url=http://www.estrepublicain.fr/actualite/2014/12/02/reforme-territoriale-alsace-lorraine-champagne-ardenne-proposez-un-nom-pour-la-grande-region |access-date=25 August 2015}}</ref>

The names which received a moderate amount of discussion were:

* {{Interlanguage link multi|Grand Est français|fr}}, a term used to refer to the northeast quarter of [[Metropolitan France]], although this term refers to a geographic region larger than just ACAL. The term has been commonly used and topped the polls mentioned above.
* ''Grand Est Europe'' (Great East Europe), a variant of Grand Est that alludes to the region being a gateway to Europe both through trade and since Strasbourg is [[European institutions in Strasbourg|home to several European institutions]] (which makes it one of the three [[Institutional seats of the European Union#"Capital"|unofficial capitals]] of the [[European Union]]).<ref name="DNA: name July 2015">{{Cite news |last=Bach |first=Christian |date=21 June 2015 |title=Région Alsace-Lorraine-Champagne-Ardenne : le nom de la chose... |language=French |work=Derniers nouvelles d'Alsace |url=http://www.dna.fr/actualite/2015/07/21/politique |access-date=25 August 2015}}</ref> However, the name was mocked for how it could suggest that the region is in [[Eastern Europe]].<ref>{{Cite news |last=Baldit |first=Etienne |date=21 July 2015 |title=Philippot refuse le nom 'Grand Est Europe' pour sa région : "Et pourquoi pas 'Roumanie' ?" |language=French |work=Europe 1 |url=http://lelab.europe1.fr/philippot-refuse-le-nom-grand-est-europe-pour-sa-region-et-pourquoi-pas-roumanie-1369616 |access-date=25 August 2015}}</ref>
* ''Austrasie'' ([[Austrasia]]), which refers to an [[historical region]] spanning parts of present-day northeast France, the [[Benelux]], and northwest Germany.<ref name="F3 names" /><ref name="ER: Names" />
* ''Quatre frontières'' (Four Frontiers), which refers to the region's border with four countries.<ref name="F3 names" />

== Geography ==
Grand Est covers {{convert|57433|km2}} of land and is the sixth-largest of the regions of France. Grand Est borders four countries—[[Belgium]] ([[Wallonia]] region) and [[Luxembourg]] (Cantons of [[Esch-sur-Alzette (canton)|Esch-sur-Alzette]] and [[Remich (canton)|Remich]]) on the north, [[Germany]] on the east and northeast,<ref name="German states">[[Baden-Württemberg]], [[Rhineland-Palatinate]] and [[Saarland]]</ref> and [[Switzerland]]<ref name="Swiss cantons">Cantons of [[Basel-Landschaft]], [[Basel-Stadt]], [[Canton of Jura|Jura]] and [[Canton of Solothurn|Solothurn]]</ref> on the southeast. It is the only French region to border more than two countries, or more countries than French regions. Its neighbors within France are [[Bourgogne-Franche-Comté]] on the south, [[Île-de-France]] on the west, and [[Hauts-de-France]] on the northwest.

=== Departments ===
Grand Est contains ten [[Departments of France|departments]]: [[Ardennes (department)|Ardennes]], [[Aube]], [[Bas-Rhin]], [[Marne (department)|Marne]], [[Haute-Marne]], [[Haut-Rhin]], [[Meurthe-et-Moselle]], [[Meuse (department)|Meuse]], [[Moselle (department)|Moselle]], [[Vosges (department)|Vosges]].<!-- this should be expanded into a table including the current region and key figures -->

=== Topography ===
The main ranges in the region include the [[Vosges]] to the east and the [[Ardennes]] to the north.

=== Hydrology ===
The region is bordered on the east by the [[Rhine]], which forms about half of the border with Germany. Other major rivers which flow through the region include the [[Meuse]], [[Moselle]], [[Marne (river)|Marne]], and [[Saône]].

Lakes in the region include [[lac de Gérardmer]], [[lac de Longemer]], [[lac de Retournemer]], [[lac des Corbeaux]], [[Lac de Bouzey]], [[lac de Madine]], étang du Stock and [[lac de Pierre-Percée]].

=== Climate ===

Grand Est climate depends on the proximity of the sea. In [[Champagne]] and [[Lorraine|Western Lorraine]], the climate is [[oceanic climate|oceanic]] ([[Köppen climate classification|Köppen]] : [[Oceanic climate|Cfb]]), with cool to mild winters and warm summers. But [[Ardennes]], [[Moselle]] and [[Alsace]] climates are borderline [[humid continental climate|humid continental]] ([[Köppen climate classification|Köppen]] : [[Humid continental climate|Dfb]]) - [[oceanic climate|oceanic]] ([[Köppen climate classification|Köppen]] : [[Oceanic climate|Cfb]]), characterized by cold winters with frequent days below the freezing point, and hot summers, with many days with temperatures up to 32&nbsp;°C.

=== Formation ===
[[File:Strasbourg manifestation contre la fusion des régions 23 novembre 2014.jpg|thumb|Protesters of the [[Alsace independence movement]] holding a banner saying "No to merger" (''Non a la fusion''), 2014 in Strasbourg.]]
Grand Est is the result of [[Regions of France#Reform and mergers of regions|territorial reform legislation]] passed in 2014 by the French Parliament to reduce the number of regions in Metropolitan France—the part of France in continental Europe—from 22 to 13.<ref>{{Cite news |date=17 December 2014 |title=La carte à 13 régions définitivement adoptée |language=French |work=[[Le Monde]] |url=http://www.lemonde.fr/politique/article/2014/12/17/la-carte-a-13-regions-definitivement-adoptee_4542278_823448.html |access-date=27 October 2015}}</ref> Grand Est is the merger of three regions: [[Alsace]], [[Champagne-Ardenne]], and [[Lorraine]].

=== Opposition ===
{{See also|Alsace independence movement}}
The merger has been, and still is, strongly opposed by some groups in Alsace, and a large majority of Alsatians.<ref>{{Cite news |date=21 February 2018 |title=Le malaise Alsacien ne décroit pas |language=French |work=[[L'Alsace]] |url=https://c.lalsace.fr/actualite/2018/02/21/le-malaise-alsacien-ne-decroit-pas |access-date=21 February 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |date=30 September 2017 |title=Grand Est: Philippe Richert, le "fossoyeur" de l'Alsace, démissionne |language=French |work=[[L'Express]] |url=https://www.lexpress.fr/actualite/politique/philippe-richert-le-fossoyeur-de-l-alsace-demissionne_1948113.html |access-date=27 November 2017}}</ref> The territorial reform law allows new regions to choose the seat of the regional councils, but specifically made Strasbourg the seat of the Grand Est regional council—a move to appease the region's politicians.<ref>{{Cite news |date=20 November 2014 |title=Strasbourg sera la capitale de la future région Alsace-Champagne-Ardenne-Lorraine |language=French |work=[[Le Monde]] |url=http://www.lemonde.fr/politique/article/2014/11/20/strasbourg-sera-la-capitale-de-la-future-region-alsace-champagne-ardenne-lorraine_4526695_823448.html |access-date=25 August 2015 |quote=[Members of the National Assembly] decided Thursday, 20 November to designate in advance Strasbourg as the capital of the future region Alsace-Champagne-Ardenne-Lorraine in a gesture to appease the Alsatian politicians. (From French: Les députés ont décidé jeudi 20 novembre de désigner par avance Strasbourg comme capitale de la future grande région Alsace-Champagne-Ardenne-Lorraine dans un geste d'apaisement vis-à-vis des élus alsaciens.)}}</ref>

The creation of the new region was unpopular among [[Alsatians (people)|Alsatians]]. In response, the Government created the [[European Collectivity of Alsace]] merging the departments of [[Bas Rhin]] and [[Haut Rhin]], to take effect in 2021.

== Government ==

=== Regional council ===
[[File:Séance plénière inaugurale conseil région ACAL 4 janvier 2016.jpg|thumb|Inaugural session of the new Regional council on 4 January 2016]]
[[File:Alsace Regional Council headquarters in Strasbourg January 2013.jpg|thumb|The current headquarters of the [[Alsace Regional Council]], which serves as the headquarters of Grand Est's regional council]]
{{further|Regional council (France)}}
The regional council has limited administrative authority, mostly concerning the promotion of the region's economy and financing educational and cultural activities. The regional council has no legislative authority. The seat of the regional council will be [[Strasbourg]]. The regional council, [[2015 French regional elections|elected in December 2015]], is controlled by [[The Republicans (France)|The Republicans]].<ref name="Dec 2015 election results">{{Cite web |title=Alsace – Champagne-Ardenne – Lorraine: Nouveau Conseil Régional |url=http://elections.interieur.gouv.fr/regionales-2015/44/44.html |access-date=14 December 2015 |website=Élections régionales et des assemblées de Corse, Guyane et Martinique 2015 |publisher=Ministre de l’Intérieur}}</ref> The elected inaugural president of the Grand Est Regional Council is [[Philippe Richert]], who was previously the President of the Alsace Regional Council.<ref name="Dec 2015 election results" /> The current president is [[Jean Rottner]].

== Economy ==
The [[gross domestic product]] (GDP) of the region was 159.9 billion euros in 2018, accounting for 6.7% of French economic output. GDP per capita adjusted for purchasing power was 25,400 euros or 84% of the EU27 average in the same year. The GDP per employee was 101% of the EU average.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Regional GDP per capita ranged from 30% to 263% of the EU average in 2018 |url=https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/documents/2995521/10474907/1-05032020-AP-EN.pdf/81807e19-e4c8-2e53-c98a-933f5bf30f58 |website=Eurostat}}</ref>

== Heritage ==
{{expand section|date=January 2016}}
[[File:Thann Saint-Thiébaut Westportal 372.jpg|upright|thumbnail|West portal of St Theobald's Church of Thann, a masterpiece of late 14th-century sculpture and architecture.]]
Grand Est is rich with architectural monuments from the [[Roman Empire]] to the early 21st century.

[[Gothic architecture]] is particularly conspicuous, with many famous [[cathedral]]s, [[basilica]]s and [[Church (building)|churches]], such as [[Reims Cathedral]], [[Strasbourg Cathedral]], [[Metz Cathedral]], [[Troyes Cathedral]], [[Châlons Cathedral]], [[Toul Cathedral]], the [[Notre-Dame de l'Épine|Basilica of L'Épine]], the [[Saint-Nicolas, Saint-Nicolas-de-Port|Basilica of Saint-Nicolas-de-Port]], the {{Interlanguage link multi|Basilica of Notre-Dame, Avioth|fr|3=Basilique Notre-Dame d'Avioth}}, the [[Basilique Saint-Urbain de Troyes|Basilica of St. Urbain in Troyes]], [[St Theobald's Church, Thann|Thann Church]], [[Niederhaslach Church]], [[Notre-Dame-en-Vaux]], [[St. George's Church, Sélestat]] and [[St. Peter and St. Paul's Church, Wissembourg]].
