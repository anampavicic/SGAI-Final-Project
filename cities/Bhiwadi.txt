'''Bhiwadi''' is a [[Planned community|planned city]] in [[Khairthal-Tijara district|Khairthal- Tijara]] district of the [[India]]n state of [[Rajasthan]] and a Part of National Capital Region of Delhi. It is located 85&nbsp;km away from the city of [[Alwar]] and situated on the [[Rajasthan]]-[[Haryana]] border. Bhiwadi is considered to be a hub for Taiwanese and Japanese companies.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hindustantimes.com/cities/delhi-news/bhiwadi-the-emerging-industrial-hub-of-ncr-with-affordable-housing-connectivity-woes-and-crime-concerns-101685904287423.html|title = Bhiwadi: NCR’s low-cost industry, housing hub|access-date=2023-08-26|publisher=Hindustan Times}}</ref> The city is divided into three separate zones, which are the residential, industrial and commercial zones. In recent years, it also became one of the most polluted cities in the world in terms of air pollution.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/jaipur/rajasthans-bhiwadi-most-polluted-city-in-the-world/articleshow/90386442.cms|title = Rajasthan's Bhiwadi most polluted city in the world|access-date=2023-08-25|publisher=The Times of India}}</ref>

==Location and Infrastructure==
Bhiwadi is situated at 28.21°N, 76.87°E. It is 60&nbsp;km away from [[New Delhi]], 200&nbsp;km from [[Jaipur]], 90&nbsp;km from Alwar, 40&nbsp;km from [[Gurgaon]] and 50&nbsp;km from [[Faridabad]] (PROP Touch India Sec-49). It can be reached through the [[National Highway 48 (India)|National Highway NH48]] (Delhi-Jaipur highway) via [[Dharuhera]], in 50 min from Hero Honda Chowk (Gurgaon) and in 70-90 min from [[IGI Airport]], [[New Delhi]].  It can also be reached by the [[Gurgaon]]-[[Sohna]]-[[Taoru|Tauru]]-[[Dharuhera]] road.

The nearest railway station is [[Rewari Junction railway station|Rewari Junction]], 26&nbsp;km south of city.

The nearest Airport is [[Indira Gandhi International Airport]], 55&nbsp;km north of the city.

==Demographics and population==
As of the [[2021 census of India]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.censusindia.net/results/town.php?stad=A&state5=999|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20040616075334/http://www.censusindia.net/results/town.php?stad=A&state5=999|archive-date=2004-06-16|title= Census of India 2001: Data from the 2001 Census, including cities, villages and towns (Provisional)|access-date=2008-11-01|publisher= Census Commission of India}}</ref> Bhiwadi had a 
population of 6,04,883. However, it is estimated that the Bhiwadi Municipality's population is approximately 6,44,000 people, and will grow in the following years - by 2030, it is expected that more than 10,73,000 people will live in the city. Males and females constitute 57% and 43% of the population respectively, with 59,712 males and 45,209 females. That means that there are 800 females per 1000 males. According to the [[2011 census of India]], children under the age of 6 make 16.29% of the city's total population. 79.84% of Bhiwadi's population is literate, which is almost 15% higher than the average literacy in [[Rajasthan]], the state were the city is located. Male's literacy rate is almost 20% higher than female's, with 87.2% of men having the ability to read and write compared to women's 69.87%.

According to the census, 87.94% of the population follows the religion of [[Hinduism]], 10% recognise themselves as [[Islam|Muslim]], 1.34% are [[Sikh]]. 0.68% follow other religions, and 0.03% do not believe in any.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.census2011.co.in/data/town/800487-bhiwadi-rajasthan.html#:~:text=Bhiwadi%202023%20Population,in%202023%20is%20approximately%20144%2C000|title = Bhiwadi Town Population Census 2011 - 2023|access-date=2023-08-26|publisher=Census Commission of India}}</ref>

==Economy==
The town is spread over nearly 5,300 acres with houses covering around 2,700. The town contains a range of large, medium and small scale industries, from steel mills and furnaces to automobile and electronics manufacturing.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://articles.economictimes.indiatimes.com/2012-09-01/news/33535417_1_avalon-group-manesar-real-estate|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130708105916/http://articles.economictimes.indiatimes.com/2012-09-01/news/33535417_1_avalon-group-manesar-real-estate|url-status=dead|archive-date=8 July 2013|title = Business News Today: Read Latest Business news, India Business News Live, Share Market & Economy News}}</ref>

==Climate and geography==
During the summer, the weather in the city gets hot, while temperatures in winter are fairly cool. The maximum and minimum temperatures ever recorded are ~51&nbsp;°C and ~3&nbsp;°C respectively. The average wind velocity is 5&nbsp;km/h, while the humidity is around 70%. Bhawadi is located 270 m above sea level. The Arawali mountain ([[Aravalli Range]]) is located 95&nbsp;km South-East of the city. The [[Sariska Tiger Reserve]], which is also a popular tourist destination, is located 120&nbsp;km south of the city.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bhiwadionline.in/city-guide/geography-of-bhiwadi|title = Georgraphy of Bhiwadi|access-date=2023-08-25|publisher=Bhiwadionline.in}}</ref>

In 2021 and 2022 Bhiwadi was deemed one of the most polluted cities in the world by IQAir, a Swiss air quality technology company. In 2022, Bhiwadi ranked 3rd in the worst air quality list by IQAir in the world after [[Lahore]] and [[Hotan]], with an annual average PM2.5 concentration of 92.7 μg/m³. In some months, such as April, the monthly average [[PM2.5]] concentration becomes as high as 150 μg/m³, which exceeds the [[WHO]] guideline by 30 times.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.iqair.com/world-most-polluted-cities|title= World's most polluted cities|access-date=2023-08-26|publisher=IQAir}}</ref>
