'''Cabo Frio''' ({{IPA|pt|ˈkabu ˈfɾi.u}}, ''Cold Cape'') is a tourist destination located in the state of [[Rio de Janeiro (state)|Rio de Janeiro]].

The Brazilian coast runs east from Rio de Janeiro to Cabo Frio where it turns sharply north. North of Cabo Frio is [[Cabo de São Tomé]].

It was named after the geography of its location, a [[Cape (geography)|cape]], and because the water temperature is colder than in nearby cities (''[[wikt:frio#Portuguese|frio]]'' is Portuguese for "cold"). This city features beaches with white fine sand. Due to a lack of [[mica]], the sand stays cool in the sun.

{{as of|2020}}, Cabo Frio's estimated population is 230,378 and its area is 410&nbsp;km².

== Infrastructure ==

Cabo Frio is served by [[Cabo Frio International Airport]].
