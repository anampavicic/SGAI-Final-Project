'''Naga''', officially the '''City of Naga''' ({{lang-ceb|Dakbayan sa Naga}}; {{lang-fil|Lungsod ng Naga}}), is a 3rd class [[Cities of the Philippines#Legal classification|component city]] in the [[Provinces of the Philippines|province]] of [[Cebu]], [[Philippines]]. According to the 2020 census, it has a population of 133,184 people.{{PH census|current}}

Naga City is bordered to the north by the town of [[Minglanilla, Cebu|Minglanilla]], to the west is the city of [[Toledo City, Cebu|Toledo]], to the east is the [[Cebu Strait]], and to the south is the town of [[San Fernando, Cebu|San Fernando]]. It is {{convert|21|km}} from [[Cebu City]].

It lies within the [[Metro Cebu|Cebu metropolitan area]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=sparksph |date=March 8, 2022 |title=Pasilong sa Naga: Boardwalk in Naga City |url=https://suroy.ph/boardwalk-in-naga-city/ |access-date=July 20, 2022 |website=Suroy.ph |language=en-US}}</ref>

It is one of the two Philippine cities named Naga, the other being [[Naga, Camarines Sur]] in [[Luzon]].

== History ==

Naga was previously named by the first settlers as "Narra" due to the abundance of trees called "narra". The name eventually became "Naga". Naga became a municipality on June 12, 1829.

===Cityhood===
{{main|Cities of the Philippines}}
{{Hatnote|For further information, see [[League of Cities of the Philippines v. COMELEC]].}}
Cityhood was ratified in a plebiscite on September 2, 2007. The [[Supreme Court of the Philippines|Supreme Court]] declared the cityhood law of Naga and 15 other cities unconstitutional after a petition filed by the [[League of Cities of the Philippines]] in its ruling on November 18, 2008. On December 22, 2009, the cityhood law of Naga and 15 other municipalities regain its status as cities again after the court reversed its ruling November 18, 2008 ruling.

On August 23, 2010, the court reinstated its ruling on November 18, 2008, making Naga and 15 other cities regular municipalities. Finally, on February 15, 2011, Naga and the other 15 municipalities declared that the conversion to cityhood met all legal requirements.
In 2013, after six years of legal battle, in its board resolution the [[League of Cities of the Philippines]] acknowledged and recognized the cityhood of Naga and 15 other cities on July 19, 2013.<ref>{{cite news|title=16 new cities recognized as LCP members (Archived)|url=http://archive.sunstar.com.ph/tacloban/local-news/2013/07/19/16-new-cities-recognized-lcp-members-293321|accessdate=March 6, 2018|publisher=Sun Star News|date=July 19, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=League finally recognizes 16 'unqualified' cities|url=https://www.rappler.com/nation/34247-league-cities-philippines-recognizes-new-cities|accessdate=March 6, 2018|work=Rappler|date=July 19, 2013}}</ref>

==Economy==
{{PH poverty incidence}}
Naga is home to several [[heavy industries]], and as such, the city bills itself as the ''Industrial City of the South''. Among the industries in Naga are the Apo Cement Corporation, the largest factory in the country, producing {{convert|4,000|MT|LT}} per day; [[FSP Group]]; the 290-megawatt [[Korea Electric Power Corporation#Overseas activities|KEPCO Philippines Corporation]] power plant; [[MRC Allied|MRC Allied Industries]]; Pryce Gases, Inc.; Rikio Southeast Asia;  the 147-megawatt coal-fired Salcon Power Corporation plant;{{sfn|Philippine Global|2012}} Asian Grains Corporation, a flour milling company; Sugbo ACS Food Manufacturing Corporation, the maker of Ichipan bread; UNAHCO Feeds, Inc.; and Provera Nutritional Solutions Corp. (feedmill), among other industries.

The Naga Valley Industrial Park (NAVA) is a {{convert|36|ha|acre|abbr=on}} [[Philippine Economic Zone Authority]] (PEZA)-registered [[industrial park]] in Barangay Cantao-an. Locators in NAVA include Cebu Mitsumi, Inc., [[Kyocera|Kyocera Kinseki Philippines, Inc.]], and Tokyo Microshaft Corporation. The industrial park was a part of the {{convert|250|ha|acre|abbr=on}} New Cebu Township One (NCTO) of MRC Allied before it was acquired by [[Cebu City]]-based developer Primary Properties Corporation.<ref>{{ cite news |date=August 2, 2015 |title='Renewed interest' in Naga industrial park |url=https://www.sunstar.com.ph/article/23557/renewed-interest-in-naga-industrial-park |work=[[SunStar Cebu]] |accessdate=January 8, 2023 }}</ref>

==Sports venues==
In December 2015, the Naga City government officially opened the Teodoro Mendiola Sports Field and Oval, located along North Poblacion. Its inauguration was in time for its usage as the main venue for the 2016 Central Visayas Regional Athletic Association (CVIRAA) games in February. The ₱68 million project comprises a track and field oval, swimming pool (Olympic-sized), and basketball, tennis, and volleyball courts.{{sfn|Sun.Star|2015}}
