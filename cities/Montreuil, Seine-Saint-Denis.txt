'''Montreuil''' ({{IPA|fr|mɔ̃tʁœj|-|LL-Q150 (fra)-Harmonia Amanda-Montreuil.wav}}), also known unofficially as '''Montreuil-sous-Bois''' ({{IPA|fr|mɔ̃tʁœj su bwa|pron}}), is a [[Communes of France|commune]] in the eastern suburbs of [[Paris]], [[France]]. It is located {{convert|6.6|km|mi|abbr=on}} from the [[Kilometre zero|centre of Paris]], in the [[Seine-Saint-Denis]] department and in the [[Métropole du Grand Paris]]. With a population of 111,367 as of 2020, Montreuil is the third most populous suburb of Paris after [[Boulogne-Billancourt]] and [[Saint-Denis, Seine-Saint-Denis|Saint-Denis]]. It is located north of Paris's [[Bois de Vincennes]] (in the [[12th arrondissement of Paris|12th arrondissement]]), on the border with [[Val-de-Marne]].

==Name==
The name Montreuil was recorded for the first time in a royal edict of 722 as {{lang|la|Monasteriolum}}, meaning "little [[monastery]]" in [[Medieval Latin]]. The settlement of Montreuil started as a group of houses built around a small [[Merovingian dynasty|Merovingian]] monastery.

==History==
A small monastery was recorded on the site during the [[Merovingian dynasty|Merovingian]] period on a hill that overlooked [[Vincennes]], which is most likely where Montreuil gained its name.<ref name=tourisme93>{{cite web |language=fr|url=https://www.tourisme93.com/document.php?pagendx=93&engine_zoom=PcuIDFSRV0001007|title=Église saint Pierre-saint Paul|website=tourisme93.com|access-date=November 16, 2019}}</ref>

Under the reigns of [[Louis XIV of France|Louis XIV]] and [[Louis XVI of France|Louis XVI]] the "Peach Walls" which provided the royal court with the fruits were located in Montreuil. It was also later home to the [[Lumière brothers]] and [[George Méliès]] whose workshops were located in lower Montreuil.

On 1 January 1860, the city of Paris was enlarged by annexing neighboring communes. On that occasion, the commune of [[Charonne]] was disbanded and divided between the city of Paris, Montreuil, and [[Bagnolet]]. Montreuil received a small part of the territory of Charonne.

Today Montreuil is divided into several districts:

* Le bas Montreuil (which joins together the old workshops (bordering on [[Saint-Mandé]]), the ''[[marché aux puces]]'' (bordering on Paris, carries of Montreuil),
*The Mairie (town hall) (the malls, la croix de [[Chavaux]], the ''mairie'', and the [[Church of St. Peter and St. Paul (Montreuil, Seine-Saint-Denis)|Church of St Peter and St Paul]]),
* La Noue (parc des [[Guilands]], city of du Val, Robespierre, bordering on [[Bagnolet]]),
* Le Bel Air ([[Jean Moulin]] housing estate, park des Beaumonts, city of Bel Air, city of Grand Pechers),
* La Boissière (all the north of Montreuil including parc Montreau and parc des Beaumonts, bordering on [[Romainville]], [[Noisy-le-Sec]], [[Rosny-sous-Bois]] and [[Fontenay-sous-Bois]]).

===Main artistic heritage===
* Decorations (ceramics and frescoes) in the state school "[[Voltaire]]" by [[Maurice Boitel]] (1954).
* Many definitive street art murals on several buildings of the city, like the tribute to Frantz Fanon, French psychiatrist and philosopher from the French overseas department of Martinique (boulevard Théophile Sueur). 
* "Au temps d'harmonie", pointillist painting from famous 19th century French painter Paul Signac, in the town hall.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://histoire-image.org/de/etudes/visions-bonheur|title = Visions du bonheur &#124; Histoire et analyse d'images et oeuvres}}</ref>
* Polyptych (enamels), in the cathedral entrance of the [[Georges Méliès]] student residence by [[Guillaume Bottazzi]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://openagenda.com/jep-2021-ile-de-france/events/un-polyptyque-de-guillaume-bottazzi-illumine-lentree-cathedrale-de-la-residence-etudiante-georges-melies-a-montreuil?lang=en|title=A polyptych by Guillaume Bottazzi illuminates the cathedral entrance of the Georges Méliès student residence in Montreuil}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.in-interiors.fr/2021/03/16/le-polyptyque-de-guillaume-bottazzi-illumine-la-nouvelle-residence-etudiante-georges-melies-a-montreuil/|title = Le polyptyque de Guillaume Bottazzi illumine la nouvelle résidence étudiante Georges Méliès à Montreuil – in interiors}}</ref>

==Geography==
===Climate===

Montreuil has an [[oceanic climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification]] ''Cfb''). The average annual temperature in Montreuil is {{cvt|12.0|C}}. The average annual rainfall is {{cvt|669.9|mm}} with May as the wettest month. The temperatures are highest on average in July, at around {{cvt|20.2|C}}, and lowest in January, at around {{cvt|4.6|C}}. The highest temperature ever recorded in Montreuil was {{cvt|40.0|C}} on 25 July 2019 and 12 August 2003; the coldest temperature ever recorded was {{cvt|-17.7|C}} on 17 January 1985.

Montreuil's inhabitants often exaggeratedly nickname the town the "second Malian town after [[Bamako]]", or sometimes "Mali-sous-Bois"<ref name="Lexpress mali sous bois">{{cite news |last=Bordier |first=Julien |date=2005-10-06 |title=Mali-sous-Bois |url=http://www.lexpress.fr/region/mali-sous-bois_484282.html?xtmc=mali_sous_bois |language=fr |newspaper=L'Express |access-date=2013-11-01 }}</ref> or "Bamako-sur-Seine" even though the [[Seine]] does not cross the town. Montreuil has indeed a very important Malian population: more than {{formatnum:2000}} inhabitants according to the [[INSEE]] in 1999, between {{formatnum:6000}} and {{formatnum:10000}} people according to the ''mairie'',<ref>{{in lang|fr}} [http://www.montreuil.fr/1-8894-Yelimane---Mali.php Le cercle de Yelimané sur le site de la mairie de Montreuil] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131102183342/http://www.montreuil.fr/1-8894-Yelimane---Mali.php |date=2013-11-02 }}</ref> which estimates that Montreuil has the largest Malian community in France.<ref name="Lexpress mali sous bois" /> 10% of the population is Malian or has Malian origins.<ref>{{in lang|fr}} c dans l'air émission du 04/04/2012 http://www.france5.fr/c-dans-l-air/international/aqmi-main-basse-sur-le-mali-35834</ref>

==Administration==
The mayor of Montreuil is the member of [[Parti communiste français]] [[Patrice Bessac]], who was elected on the second round of [[2014 French municipal elections|2014 municipal elections]], defeating the former ex-Communist mayor [[Jean-Pierre Brard]] in a four-way second round. He was re-elected in the first round of the [[2020 French municipal elections|2020 elections]] with 51.34% votes, though with 33.67% participation (down from 53.11% in the 2014 election) due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic in France|COVID-19 crisis]] (which also caused a delay of the 2nd turn in other cities).

The city is divided into two cantons: [[canton of Montreuil-1]] and [[canton of Montreuil-2]].

==Transport==
Montreuil is served by three stations on [[Paris Métro Line 9]]: [[Robespierre (Paris Métro)|Robespierre]], [[Croix de Chavaux (Paris Métro)|Croix de Chavaux]], and [[Mairie de Montreuil (Paris Métro)|Mairie de Montreuil]].

There are several new Métro (Line 11 - Montreuil-Hôpital<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://prolongementligne11est.fr/le-prolongement/|title = Le prolongement &#124; Prolongement Ligne 11 Est}}</ref>) and tramway (extension of the T1 tramway<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.t1bobigny-valdefontenay.fr/ |title=Home |website=t1bobigny-valdefontenay.fr}}</ref>) stations under construction. The region is also working on the extension of the Métro Line 1 to Val-de-Fontenay, with a possible new station in the East of Montreuil (Grands Pêchers).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.iledefrance-mobilites.fr/le-reseau/projets/metro-1-prolongement-chateau-de-vincennes-val-de-fontenay|title = Projet Métro ligne 1 Prolongement Château de Vincennes > Val de Fontenay| date=10 March 2022 }}</ref>
