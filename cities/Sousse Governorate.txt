'''Sousse Governorate''' ({{Langx|ar|ولاية سوسة}} ''{{transl|ar|Wilāyat Sūsah}}'' {{IPA|ar|ˈsuː.sə|pron}}; {{langx|fr|Gouvernorat de Sousse}}) is one of the twenty-four governorates (provinces) of [[Tunisia]]. It is beside the eastern coast of Tunisia in the north-east of the country and covers an area of 2,621&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> and has a population of 674,971 (2014 census).<ref>{{in lang|fr}} [http://rgph2014.ins.tn/fr/resultats Census 2014 (National Institute of Statistics)] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141029170649/http://rgph2014.ins.tn/fr/resultats |date=2014-10-29 }}</ref> The capital is [[Sousse]].

==Geography==
The area compasses most of the broad eastern coastal plain (which has a hot Mediterranean climate) featuring salt and fresh water lakes, fed by winter rains. A narrow strip of forest, the Foret Nationale de Tunisie, adjoins part of the beach swathe between Sousse and Hammamet to the far north, the beach forming most of the coastline.  These cities are on the [[Gulf of Hammamet]] which is a gently-curved bay.  The largest lake is the Sebkhet de [[Sidi El Hani]] which is shared with two other areas but is mostly in the Sousse Governorate.<ref>[http://www.ezilon.com/maps/africa/tunisia-physical-maps.html Physical Map of Tunisia]</ref>

Elevations are pronounced in the second national park in the area, which is mainly in [[Nabeul Governorate]], is on all of the seaward sides of [[Hammam Bent Djadidi]] adjoining the northern border.

===Transport===
Linked by the widest road in the country and railways to [[Tunis]], the area has roads and railways leading further into the country and towards Libya as well as an airport beside the main port city of Sousse, [[Monastir Habib Bourguiba International Airport]] which is beyond its short eastern border in much smaller [[Monastir Governorate]]. There is a Metro line that goes from Sousse Beb Djedid through Monastir to Mahdia.
