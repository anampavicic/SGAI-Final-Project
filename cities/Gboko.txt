'''Gboko''' is a [[Local government areas of Nigeria|Local Government Area]]  in [[Benue State|Benue state]], North-central [[Nigeria]]. It is headquartered in the town of Gboko.

Gboko LGA was created on the 11th of May, 1970 with a landmass of 2,264 Sqr Km. Wielding a population of 358,936 according to 2006 census, it is the largest of the twenty-three local government by population in Benue State.

[[Tiv people|Tiv]], [[Hausa language|Hausa]] and English are the languages most widely spoken in Gboko.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Gboko Local Government Area ‚Äì I am Benue |url=http://www.iambenue.com/benue-state/local-governments-areas/gboko-local-government-area/ |access-date=2023-08-26 |language=en-GB}}</ref>

The town of Gboko also doubles as the ancestral headquarters of the Tiv people. The palace of the '''[[Tor Tiv]]''', who is the supreme traditional leader, is situated right in the heart of the town. The current Tor Tiv honorifically called '''Begha U Tiv''' (Lion of Tiv people) is His Royal Majesty, Begha U Tiv, Orchivirigh, Prof Ortese Iorzua James Ayatse.    

[[Gboko]] is also host to former Benue Cement Company now [[Dangote Cement]], and also home to the defunct [[BCC Lions F.C.|BCC Lions Football Club]], who were the 1990 winners of the [[African Cup Winners' Cup|African Cup Winners's cup]]. They played host at the [[Joseph Tarka|J.S Tarkar Stadium]] in the Gboko township.

== Economy ==
[[Mineral]] reserves including [[limestone]], [[granite]], [[Baryte|barite]], and [[Alluvium|alluvial clay]] are abundant in the Gboko LGA. The majority of Gboko LGA residents work in [[agriculture]], and the region is well-known for [[Crop rotation|cultivating crops]] like [[rice]], yams, [[maize]], and [[cassava]] in significant quantities. Several marketplaces, including the Ortese, Ikpa, and Tarukpe markets, where locals can buy and sell a wide range of goods, are located in the Gboko LGA, where trade is also thriving.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |title=Gboko Local Government Area |url=https://www.manpower.com.ng/places/lga/160/gboko |access-date=2023-08-26 |website=www.manpower.com.ng}}</ref>  

==Geography ==

=== Climate ===
Gboko LGA is located in the Savannah region and has a total area of 2,264 square [[Kilometre|kilometers]]. The dry and wet seasons are the two main ones that the LGA experiences. The local average temperature is 28 degrees [[Celsius]], and the local average relative [[humidity]] is 49 percent.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Gboko weather and climate ‚òÄÔ∏è Best time to visit üå°Ô∏è Temperature |url=https://www.besttravelmonths.com/nigeria/gboko-2887774/ |access-date=2023-08-26 |website=www.besttravelmonths.com |language=en}}</ref><ref name=":0" />

Gboko has a tropical wet and dry or [[savanna]] climate (Classification: Aw) and is zero [[Metre|meters]] (0 feet) above sea level. The district has an average annual temperature of 29.69¬∞C (85.44¬∞F), which is 0.23% higher than Nigeria's national average. 136.61 [[Millimetre|millimeters]] (5.38 inches) of precipitation and 161.68 wet days (44.3% of the time) are typical annual totals for Gboko.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Gboko, Benue, NG Climate Zone, Monthly Averages, Historical Weather Data |url=https://tcktcktck.org/nigeria/benue/gboko |access-date=2023-08-26 |website=tcktcktck.org}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Gboko, Benue, NG {{!}} Local and National Hourly Weather Forecasts |url=https://www.weatherbug.com/weather-forecast/hourly/gboko-benue-ni |access-date=2023-08-26 |website=WeatherBug}}</ref> 

In Gboko, the dry season is hot, [[Humidity|muggy]], and partly cloudy whereas the wet season is warm, [[Oppression|oppressive]], and [[Cloud|cloudy]]. The temperature rarely falls below 56¬∞F or rises over 94¬∞F throughout the year, often fluctuating between 62¬∞F and 90¬∞F.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Gboko Climate, Weather By Month, Average Temperature (Nigeria) - Weather Spark |url=https://weatherspark.com/y/61839/Average-Weather-in-Gboko-Nigeria-Year-Round#google_vignette |access-date=2023-08-26 |website=weatherspark.com |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Besttimetovisit.com.au - The best time to visit every destination |url=https://www.besttimetovisit.com.au/ |access-date=2023-08-26 |website=www.besttimetovisit.com.au |language=en}}</ref>

With an average daily high [[temperature]] of 88¬∞F, the hot season lasts for 2.3 months, from February 5 to April 16. With an average high of 88¬∞F and low of 74¬∞F, April is the hottest month of the year in Gboko.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Atlas |first=Weather |title=Yearly & Monthly weather - Gboko, Nigeria |url=https://www.weather-atlas.com/en/nigeria/gboko-climate |access-date=2023-08-26 |website=Weather Atlas |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Gboko weather forecast for today |url=https://justweather.org/Nigeria/Benue/Gboko/ |access-date=2023-08-26 |website=justweather.org |language=en}}</ref>

With an average daily maximum temperature below 83¬∞F, the chilly season lasts for 3.9 months, from June 23 to October 21. In Gboko, December is the [[Cold|coldest]] month of the year, with average lows of 64¬∞F and highs of 84¬∞F.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Simulated historical climate & weather data for Gboko |url=https://www.meteoblue.com/en/weather/historyclimate/climatemodelled/gboko_nigeria_2341374 |access-date=2023-08-26 |website=meteoblue |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Gboko Weather Forecast (Mkar, Benue, Nigeria) |url=https://www.tititudorancea.com/z/weather_forecast_gboko.htm |access-date=2023-08-26 |website=www.tititudorancea.com}}</ref>
