'''Manfalut''' ({{langx|ar|منفلوط}} ''{{transl|ar|Manfalūṭ}}'', {{IPA|arz|mɑnfɑˈluːtˤ|IPA}}; {{Langx|cop|ⲙⲁⲛⲃⲁⲗⲟⲧ|lit=place of pelts, skins}}<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |last=Peust |first=Carsten |title=Die Toponyme vorarabischen Ursprungs im modernen Ägypten |url=http://www.peust.de/ortsnamen_original.pdf |page=59}}</ref>) is a [[List of cities and towns in Egypt|city]] in [[Egypt]]. It is located on the west bank of the [[Nile]], in the [[Asyut Governorate]]. The city is at 350&nbsp;km (230 miles) south of [[Cairo]].<ref name=bbc>{{cite news|title=Egypt bus crash kills 50 children near Manfalut|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-middle-east-20375395|accessdate=17 November 2012|newspaper=BBC|date=17 November 2012}}</ref> In 2006, it had a population of 82,585 people.

Local agriculture includes [[cotton]] production.<ref>[[The Manchester Guardian]] ''EGYPTIAN COTTON CROP''; 5 August 1939</ref>

[[Egyptians|Egyptian]] writer and poet [[Mustafa Lutfi al-Manfaluti]] was born in Manfalut. By 1993, the area was considered an Islamic stronghold.<ref>[[Reuters]], ''Britons escape death on the Nile as gunman opens fire'', [[The Guardian]]; 17 August 1993</ref>

On 17 November 2012, [[Manfalut railway accident|a bus-train collision near Manfalut]] caused the deaths of 51 people.<ref name=bbc/>
